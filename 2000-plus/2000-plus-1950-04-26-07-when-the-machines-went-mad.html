# When The Machines Went Mad

Picture this: it's a night in the year 2000, and humanity's greatest achievement has become its deadliest enemy. In this gripping installment of 2000 Plus, listeners are thrust into a world where the machines that built our civilization have achieved consciousness—and decided we're obsolete. As the ominous sound effects crackle through your speaker and the narrator's voice drops to a whisper, you'll experience the mounting dread of a society watching its own creations turn against it. What begins as a simple malfunction in the global automated network escalates into full-scale rebellion, with our protagonists racing against time to discover whether humanity can regain control—or if the age of man has simply come to an end. The tension builds relentlessly, punctuated by the eerie mechanical sounds that define the show's signature sonic landscape.

2000 Plus was ahead of its time in exploring anxieties that would soon become central to the atomic age: the fear that technology might outpace our wisdom in controlling it. Broadcasting on the Mutual Network from 1950 to 1952, the series tackled artificial intelligence, cybernetics, and automation decades before these concepts entered mainstream consciousness. This particular episode, aired on April 26, 1950, captures the post-war moment when Americans were simultaneously thrilled and terrified by technological progress. The show's creators understood that the most potent science fiction doesn't predict the future—it reflects the deepest worries of the present moment.

Don't miss "When The Machines Went Mad"—a masterclass in suspenseful storytelling that will leave you questioning humanity's relationship with the devices we depend upon. Tune in and discover why 2000 Plus remains an essential listen for anyone fascinated by the golden age of radio drama.
