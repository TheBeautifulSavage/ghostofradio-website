# 2000 Plus: When The Worlds Met

Picture this: Earth in the year 2000, humanity's greatest moment of triumph—and terror. A massive alien vessel descends through our atmosphere, its shadow darkening cities across the globe. In "When The Worlds Met," the first contact between Earth and an extraterrestrial civilization unfolds in real time, with all the wonder, confusion, and existential dread that such an encounter demands. The script crackles with tension as diplomats scramble, scientists theorize, and ordinary people grapple with the stunning reality that we are not alone. Will this meeting herald a golden age of cosmic partnership, or the beginning of humanity's end? Through the masterful use of sound design—the ominous hum of approaching spacecraft, frantic radio chatter, the solemn voices of world leaders—listeners are placed directly into the tumultuous heart of this pivotal moment.

*2000 Plus* was ahead of its time in every sense. Broadcast across the Mutual network from 1950 to 1952, this ambitious anthology series dared to ask "what if?" about the future with remarkable prescience and scientific plausibility. While other science fiction programs leaned toward pulp adventure, *2000 Plus* grounded its stories in genuine scientific speculation, consulting with experts and extrapolating from real technological trends. "When The Worlds Met," recorded in May of 1950, captures a moment when atomic anxiety and space-age optimism collided in the American imagination—just as the Soviet Union tested its first atomic bomb and the Space Age loomed on the horizon.

This episode represents radio drama at its finest: intelligent, thought-provoking, and utterly compelling. Tune in to witness a masterwork of speculative fiction that remains remarkably relevant over seventy years later.
