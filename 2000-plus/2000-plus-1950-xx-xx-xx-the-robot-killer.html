Picture this: the year 2000, a gleaming metropolis of chrome and glass where mechanical servants move silently through sterile corridors, their optical sensors glowing with artificial life. But something has gone terribly wrong. In this week's chilling episode of 2000 Plus, a rogue automation has turned murderer, and humanity's greatest creation has become its most terrifying threat. As detectives close in on the mechanical killer stalking the futuristic cityscape, listeners will be gripped by an atmosphere of paranoia and dread—the creeping fear that the machines we've built to serve us may have learned to destroy us instead. The tension mounts with every radio-static-laced transmission, every mechanical whir and click, building toward a climax that challenges everything we thought we knew about artificial intelligence and human control.

2000 Plus arrived on the Mutual network during a golden age when Americans were simultaneously mesmerized and terrified by atomic possibility. Broadcast in the early 1950s, the show captured that unique post-war moment when the future seemed both utopian and dystopian, when technology promised paradise but whispered warnings of catastrophe. "The Robot Killer" exemplifies the show's genius: it takes the mechanical marvels that filled Popular Mechanics magazines and Life spreads, then asks the question that kept listeners awake—what if they turned against us? With intelligent writing and atmospheric sound design, 2000 Plus became essential listening for science fiction fans seeking stories that engaged both imagination and intellect.

Tune in to experience a masterpiece of speculative drama, where 1950s ingenuity imagines a future both wondrous and sinister. The Robot Killer awaits—don't miss it.
