# The Temple of the Pharaohs

As the familiar theremin wail of *2000 Plus* pierces the static, listeners are transported across fifty centuries to a Cairo archaeological dig where an American expedition has uncovered something that defies rational explanation. Within the sealed inner sanctum of a pharaoh's tomb, scientists discover an artifact of impossible origin—technology that predates human civilization by millennia. What follows is a taut drama of wonder and dread, as the team grapples with the terrifying implications of their find. Could the ancient Egyptians have possessed knowledge beyond their time? Was divinity itself merely advanced science misunderstood? The episode crackles with that golden-age sci-fi tension: measured scientific dialogue punctuated by moments of genuine horror, all underscored by orchestral swells that transform a tomb into a cathedral of mystery.

*2000 Plus* emerged during science fiction's renaissance on American radio, a period when audiences hungered for thoughtful speculation about humanity's future and past. The Mutual network's anthology format allowed writers to probe the boundaries of the possible—from time travel paradoxes to first contact scenarios to archaeological impossibilities like "The Temple of the Pharaohs." Aired during an era when atomic optimism collided with Cold War anxiety, these episodes captured a unique cultural moment when tomorrow seemed simultaneously wondrous and terrifying. Each installment promised something genuinely surprising, crafted by writers who treated speculative scenarios with intellectual rigor.

For anyone seeking authentic science fiction from radio's finest hour, this episode exemplifies why *2000 Plus* remains essential listening. Switch your dial to the mysterious frequencies of the Mutual network and prepare yourself for a journey that questions everything we think we know about human history—and our place in the cosmos.
