Step right up, step right up! When Abbott and Costello arrive at the big top on this March 16th broadcast, chaos erupts under the canvas in the most delightful way imaginable. Picture the sounds of calliope music, neighing horses, and the crowd's roaring laughter as our comedic duo tangles with circus performers, wild animals, and the inevitable confusion that follows them everywhere. With special guest Alan Hale bringing his gruff charm to the mayhem, listeners are in for a whirlwind of slapstick comedy, rapid-fire wordplay, and those masterful "Who's on First?" style routines that have made Abbott and Costello radio royalty. You can almost smell the sawdust and popcorn through your speaker as the comedy builds to hilarious crescendos.

By the early 1940s, Abbott and Costello had already conquered vaudeville and were rapidly becoming America's most beloved comedic team. Their radio show became a cultural phenomenon, drawing millions of listeners every week who eagerly anticipated their next misadventure. This circus episode exemplifies what made the show so special—the writers understood that radio comedy thrived on verbal dexterity and physical comedy rendered through sound effects and vocal performance. While their film careers would eventually overshadow their radio work, these broadcasts represent the golden age of American comedy, when a microphone and two brilliant minds could entertain an entire nation.

Don't miss your chance to experience radio comedy at its finest. Settle into your favorite chair, adjust the dial to the familiar voices of Bud and Lou, and let yourself be transported to the circus. This is entertainment the way audiences have enjoyed it for nearly a century—immediate, inventive, and absolutely hilarious.
