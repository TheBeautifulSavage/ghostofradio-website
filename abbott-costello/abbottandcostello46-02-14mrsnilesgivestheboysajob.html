Step into the bustling office of Mrs. Niles' employment agency on this February 14th broadcast, where Cupid's arrows fly as wildly as Abbott's exasperation and Costello's bewilderment. When the boys find themselves in need of work—and Mrs. Niles in need of her usual dose of slapstick suffering—the stage is set for romantic chaos and bureaucratic mayhem. Listen as Bud Abbott's crisp, rapid-fire patter collides with Lou Costello's innocent confusion, their voices crackling through your radio with the unmistakable chemistry that made them America's favorite comedy duo. You'll hear the studio audience roar with laughter, feel the tension of a job interview gone completely sideways, and experience the warm, intimate magic that only live radio comedy could deliver. Mrs. Niles' exasperation becomes your own as the boys bungle every detail, turning what should be a simple employment placement into a delightful disaster.

This episode captures Abbott and Costello at the height of their popularity, when radio was the supreme entertainment medium and their rapid-fire routines dominated the airwaves. The duo had perfected their craft through years of vaudeville, translating slapstick into pure vocal comedy—no sight gags needed when you have perfect timing and the right words. Broadcasting live to millions of listeners, they improvised, played to the audience reaction, and created moments that could never be perfectly replicated. Each episode was a cultural event, heard simultaneously in living rooms across the nation, binding strangers together in shared laughter during an uncertain decade.

Don't miss this gem of comedic timing and manic energy. Settle in, turn up your dial, and let Abbott and Costello transport you to a simpler time when a radio program could fill an entire evening with genuine joy and hilarity.
