# The Abbott and Costello Show - June 16, 1948

Tune in as Bud Abbott and Lou Costello welcome special guest Benny Rubin to the microphone for an evening of rapid-fire comedy and elaborate musical numbers that crackle with the energy of live radio broadcast. You'll hear the familiar patter of Abbott's smooth, controlled delivery playing against Costello's frantic, high-pitched responses as the boys launch into their classic routines—likely featuring the wordplay and physical comedy that translates so brilliantly through the magic of radio. With Rubin in the mix, the energy shifts into high gear; expect overlapping jokes, quick comebacks, and that wonderful live-wire feeling of comedians feeding off each other's timing. The studio audience's laughter and applause provide the perfect backdrop to this golden age showcase, transporting you directly into the NBC studios where comedy legends performed without a safety net, every flub and brilliant improvisation captured forever on acetate disc.

By 1948, Abbott and Costello had become America's most bankable comedy team, having conquered vaudeville, film, and now radio. Their NBC variety program represented the pinnacle of radio comedy—a sophisticated blend of slapstick, wordplay, and celebrity guest appearances that kept listeners glued to their sets twice weekly. Benny Rubin, a seasoned vaudeville and film performer himself, understood this world intimately, making him the perfect foil for the boys' well-honed routines. This era of radio was comedy's last golden gasp before television would reshape entertainment forever.

Don't miss this sterling example of classic radio at its finest—where laughter was live, timing was everything, and the greatest comedians in America performed for the love of the craft and the roar of a studio audience.
