Tune in as young Henry Aldrich stumbles headlong into his latest scheme, this time involving a prized model airplane that's sure to send the entire household into delightful chaos. What begins as an innocent afternoon of tinkering in the garage quickly spirals into a comedy of errors, complete with irate neighbors, a disapproving mother, and Henry's trademark cry of "Say, Mom!" echoing through living rooms across America. The tension crackles with suburban anxiety—will this flying contraption soar majestically, or will it crash spectacularly through Mrs. Henderson's prize-winning rose garden? Listen as the Aldrich family navigates the thin line between youthful ambition and domestic disaster, their good-natured squabbles and quick-witted banter capturing the heart of American family life on the eve of a transforming decade.

The Aldrich Family was appointment radio for millions, a show that perfectly crystallized the anxieties and joys of middle-class American households. Starring Ezra Stone as the perpetually well-meaning but hapless Henry, the program debuted in 1939 and became NBC's crown jewel of family entertainment. What set it apart was its authenticity—rather than talking down to young listeners, the show respected their intelligence while delivering genuine laughs for parents navigating the bewildering world of teenage ambition. This 1940 episode captures the show at its peak, before Henry Aldrich's antics would eventually spawn films and later television adaptations.

Whether you're a devoted fan of golden age radio or discovering The Aldrich Family for the first time, this charming episode reminds us why America couldn't wait to hear what Henry would get into next. Switch on your dial and settle in for fifteen minutes of warmth, humor, and timeless family comedy that still resonates today.
