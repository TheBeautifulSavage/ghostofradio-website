As autumn leaves crackle and jack-o'-lanterns flicker on neighborhood stoops, young Henry Aldrich and his scheming friends concoct what they're certain is the perfect Halloween prank. But in this delightfully spirited episode from October 31st, 1940, their elaborate trick spins wildly out of control, transforming an innocent evening of mischief into a hilarious cascade of misunderstandings that threatens to embarrass the entire Aldrich household. Listen as panicked phone calls stack up, worried parents descend upon Maple Street, and Henry desperately attempts damage control—only to find each frantic explanation makes matters worse. The familiar, affectionate voice of Ezra Stone as Henry crackles through your radio speaker with all the exasperation only a teenager caught red-handed can muster, while the supporting cast delivers perfectly timed reactions that build the comedic tension to an irresistible crescendo.

The Aldrich Family became America's favorite fictional household during the Golden Age of Radio, capturing the authentic rhythms of middle-class suburban life with warmth and wit that resonated across the nation. This 1940 Halloween episode exemplifies why the show endured for fourteen years—it taps into universal family dynamics that remain timeless: the well-meaning teenager, the bewildered parents, the spiraling consequences of one small choice. Rather than relying on slapstick or mean-spirited humor, the show drew its comedy from genuine character and the recognizable chaos of family life that listeners encountered in their own homes.
