# The Aldrich Family – October 21, 1948

Step into the Aldrich home on an ordinary October evening, where young Henry faces an impossible choice: slip away to the picture show with his pals, or stay behind to babysit his troublesome nine-year-old cousin Willie. It's a dilemma as old as adolescence itself, yet rendered with the perfect comic timing that made America fall in love with this family week after week. As Henry weighs his options, Willie proves to be more than a handful—a pint-sized tornado of mischief who transforms the living room into his personal playground. The tension between duty and desire crackles with genuine humor, punctuated by the sound effects that transport you directly into that suburban household: the creak of the front door, the distant call of friends outside, and Willie's persistent wheedling. You can almost feel Henry's frustration mounting as his evening spirals further from his control.

For nearly a decade, *The Aldrich Family* had captured the American imagination by holding up a mirror to the modern household—nothing too serious, nothing too saccharine, just the everyday victories and defeats of family life. Created by Clifford Goldsmith from his successful play, the show became a cultural touchstone, spawning a feature film and proving that audiences hungered for wholesome yet genuinely funny entertainment. The 1948 season found the show at its peak, with veteran actor Ezra Stone bringing impeccable comic sensibility to Henry's perpetual predicament of being caught between childhood and the wider world.

Tune in now and rediscover why millions of listeners gathered around their sets for this timeless tale of family obligation meeting teenage ambition. Some battles never change—and some comedies never grow old.
