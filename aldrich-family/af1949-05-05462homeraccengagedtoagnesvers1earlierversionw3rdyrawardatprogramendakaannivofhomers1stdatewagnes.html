Homer Aldrich's heart is racing, and listeners will feel every beat of it in this delightful May afternoon broadcast. The gentle patriarch has made a momentous decision—he's ready to propose to Agnes, and the whole household is in an uproar over the news. What unfolds is a perfectly timed comedy of errors, brimming with Henry Aldrich's well-meaning interference, Mrs. Aldrich's knowing asides, and all the fumbling charm that comes when a bashful suitor tries to orchestrate the perfect moment. This special anniversary episode celebrates Homer's first date with Agnes, and as the plot tumbles forward, listeners will discover whether his carefully-laid plans survive contact with the Aldrich family's characteristic chaos. The episode crackles with that warm, intimate quality that made radio comedy so irresistible—you can almost feel the living room setting, smell the evening dinner preparations, and hear the genuine affection beneath every joke.

For a decade, The Aldrich Family had perfected the art of domestic humor, capturing America's middle-class anxieties and joys with surprising tenderness. By 1949, the show had become a beloved institution, beloved enough that NBC kept it on the air through the early 1950s. This particular episode is a treasure: a variant recording that shows how radio writers and performers continually refined their material, reworking scenes and timing to achieve maximum impact. It's a fascinating glimpse into the craft behind the microphone, where comedy was as much about instinct and live performance as it was about the script.

Tune your dial to this charming piece of Americana and rediscover why millions of families gathered around their radios for the Aldriches' adventures. It's wholesome without being saccharine, funny without cruelty, and utterly captivating.
