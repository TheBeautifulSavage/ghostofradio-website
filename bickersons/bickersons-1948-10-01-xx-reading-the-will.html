# The Bickersons: "Reading The Will" (October 1, 1948)

Picture yourself settling into your favorite armchair on a crisp autumn evening, the amber glow of your radio dial illuminating the darkened parlor as Don and Blanche Ickerson's unmistakable bickering crackles through the speaker. In this uproarious installment, a death in the family sets the stage for marital mayhem when the reading of a will threatens to expose old grievances and buried resentments. What begins as a solemn legal proceeding devolves into a masterclass of comic insult and affectionate sparring, as the Bickersons' razor-sharp wit cuts through the formality of the lawyer's office. You'll find yourself laughing at lines that sting, caught between the couple's genuine affection and their compulsive need to have the last word—even when discussing inheritance and family legacy.

The Bickersons represented something revolutionary for late 1940s radio audiences: a married couple whose relationship thrived not on saccharine sweetness but on intelligent, rapid-fire repartee. Phil Harris and Alice Faye's chemistry was legendary, their timing impeccable, and their verbal sparring reflected a more honest portrayal of matrimony than most domestic comedies dared attempt. By 1948, the show had become essential listening, proving that Americans were hungry for humor that acknowledged marriage's complications without surrendering its fundamental warmth. This particular episode captures the show at its peak, when the writers had perfected the delicate balance between meanness and tenderness.

Step back in time and experience the golden age of radio comedy at its finest. The Bickersons remind us that laughter—especially when shared by two people who genuinely love each other—remains timeless. Tune in now.
