EPISODE SUMMARY:

"Solid Gold Soldiers," the 72nd episode of CBS Radio Mystery Theater, is a tense and intriguing tale that whisks listeners away to a small town shrouded in mystery and deception. The story centers around a series of bizarre occurrences at a local bank, where employees claim to have found mysterious gold bars hidden among their vault's contents. As the protagonist, Detective Frank Stevens, delves deeper into the case, he uncovers a web of deceit and corruption that threatens to destroy the very fabric of the community. With its intricate plot, complex characters, and masterful pacing, "Solid Gold Soldiers" is a classic example of radio drama at its finest.

At the heart of this episode lies the character of Ben Taylor, a charismatic but troubled individual with secrets to hide. Played by renowned actor Paul Frees, Taylor's performance is both captivating and unnerving, adding depth and complexity to an already suspenseful narrative. Meanwhile, Detective Stevens, played by Richard McGonagle, serves as the perfect foil to Taylor's enigmatic presence, embodying a sense of determination and skepticism that drives the investigation forward.

As the episode unfolds, listeners are treated to a rich tapestry of atmospheric sound design, clever plot twists, and unexpected surprises, all expertly woven together by writer/producer Robert Hamner. With its blend of mystery, intrigue, and social commentary, "Solid Gold Soldiers" is an engaging and thought-provoking listen that will keep even the most seasoned radio enthusiasts on the edge of their seats.
