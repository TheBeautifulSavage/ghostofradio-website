# Bold Venture: "Sailor Framed for Murder"

Picture yourself in a dimly lit Havana nightclub, the air thick with cigarette smoke and the distant sound of a jazz quartet, when suddenly gunshots shatter the tropical night. In this gripping episode, our hero Slate Shannon finds himself racing against time to prove that an innocent merchant sailor has been framed for a murder he didn't commit. With the local authorities ready to hang the man and a web of double-crosses leading deeper into the Caribbean underworld, listeners will be drawn into a labyrinth of false alibis, planted evidence, and dangerous enemies who'll stop at nothing to keep the truth buried. The tension crackles through every scene—from tense interrogation rooms to shadowy dockside confrontations—as Slate navigates a criminal conspiracy that reaches far beyond one dead man in a back alley.

*Bold Venture* arrived on syndicated airwaves in 1951 as a masterclass in adventure noir, starring Adventure Comics icon Will Cowan as the roguish, quick-witted Slate Shannon operating out of the Caribbean with his sardonic partner Sailor Duval. The show captured that golden age of radio storytelling when exotic locales and morally gray protagonists thrilled American audiences through their speakers. "Sailor Framed for Murder" exemplifies the series' signature blend of hard-boiled dialogue, international intrigue, and genuine mystery—proof that syndicated radio could compete with the networks' biggest productions in both ambition and execution.

Don't miss this classic adventure as the stakes climb higher and the noose tightens around an innocent man's neck. Tune in and discover why *Bold Venture* remains a treasure of the golden age of radio.
