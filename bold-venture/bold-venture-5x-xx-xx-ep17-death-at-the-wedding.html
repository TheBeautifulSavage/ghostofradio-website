# Death at the Wedding

Picture this: the humid Caribbean night hangs heavy as a velvet curtain as our intrepid adventurers, Slate Shannon and Wandy Thorne, find themselves unexpectedly invited to the society wedding of the season in the islands. But beneath the white orchids and champagne toasts lurks something far more sinister—a murder most calculated, orchestrated to perfection amid the swirl of dancing couples and whispered secrets. When the bride's uncle is found dead in the garden gazebo, Slate must navigate a treacherous maze of suspects, jealous relatives, and dangerous liaisons, all while keeping Wandy from becoming the killer's next victim. The clock ticks relentlessly as our heroes race against time to unmasking a murderer who strikes with cold precision, turning romance into requiem.

*Bold Venture* stands as a remarkable artifact of post-war adventure radio, capturing that golden moment when syndicated programming could match the sophistication of major network productions. Starring Adventurer Slate Shannon and the ever-capable Wandy Thorne, the show transported listeners to exotic locales where danger lurked behind every palm tree and promise. "Death at the Wedding" exemplifies the show's masterful blend of hard-boiled noir atmosphere with tropical exoticism—a distinctly American fantasy of sophisticated danger and romance that resonated deeply with audiences seeking escape during uncertain times.

If you've never experienced the crackling immediacy of *Bold Venture*—the sound design that places you directly in that sweltering Caribbean night, the voice work that makes every character live and breathe—now is your chance. Tune in for this gripping episode and discover why radio's golden age deserves its legendary status. Some stories are meant to be heard, not read.
