Picture yourself in a smoke-filled Havana nightclub as the orchestra fades to a sultry saxophone solo. Slate Shannon and Ito Suki find themselves tangled in a web of old debts and dangerous memories when a mysterious woman from Shannon's past walks through the door—and with her comes a promise made over a decade ago that could cost them everything. In this nail-biting episode, the line between loyalty and survival blurs as our heroes must decide whether to honor an oath sworn in desperation or protect the precarious empire they've built in Cuba's criminal underworld. With betrayal lurking in every shadow and the clock ticking toward a fateful midnight rendezvous, listeners will grip their radio dials as the stakes climb higher than ever before.

Bold Venture captured audiences nationwide with its intoxicating blend of post-war noir cynicism and tropical intrigue, starring Adventurous adventurer Slate Shannon and his wisecracking partner Ito Suki navigating the steamy streets and back alleys of Havana. The show's 1951-1952 syndicated run became a cult favorite precisely because it refused to offer easy morality—its heroes were flawed, its victories pyrrhic, and its world felt authentically dangerous. "A Twelve Year Promise" exemplifies why the show developed such a devoted following: it mines genuine dramatic tension from character history rather than mere plot mechanics, exploring what it means to be bound by your past in a place where the present can kill you in a heartbeat.
