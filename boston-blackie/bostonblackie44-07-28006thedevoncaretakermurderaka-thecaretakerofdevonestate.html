When the lights dimmed on July 28th, 1944, Boston Blackie's radio audience settled in to a particularly sinister mystery: a murder at the stately Devon Estate, committed by someone who knew every shadow and secret passage of the grand house. The caretaker lies dead, and suspicion falls upon those who move through the manor's dimly lit corridors—the servants, the guests, the hidden figures lurking in the darkness. Blackie, that reformed jewel thief with an uncanny knack for reading human nature, must navigate the treacherous waters of class, motive, and deception to uncover the truth. Expect tense interrogation scenes, the crackle of period ambiance, and Blackie's signature blend of wisecracks and hard-boiled wisdom as he circles ever closer to a killer.

Boston Blackie occupied a unique place in detective radio history. Unlike the more straightforward heroics of The Lone Ranger or The Shadow, Blackie was a man who lived between worlds—forever marked by his criminal past, yet driven by a dogged moral compass that made him invaluable to both the law and the desperate innocents he championed. The show thrived during the golden age of network radio, when millions of Americans gathered around their receivers for weekly doses of mystery and intrigue. This particular episode exemplifies the show's strength: intimate human drama wrapped in procedural intrigue, all delivered through the expert voice acting and sound design that made 1940s radio a genuine theater of the mind.

The Devon Estate mystery awaits, a mystery solved only through Blackie's unique perspective and relentless determination. Tune in to discover whether justice can truly be served when murder hides behind the respectable façade of an old family's home.
