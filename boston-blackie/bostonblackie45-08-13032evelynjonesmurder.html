# The Evelyn Jones Murder

On a fog-shrouded August evening in 1945, Boston Blackie finds himself ensnared in the most perplexing case of his career—the murder of the elegant Evelyn Jones. When the body of the socialite is discovered in her Beacon Hill mansion, all evidence points to Blackie himself, leaving the reformed jewel thief to prove his innocence while hunting the true killer through Boston's shadowy back alleys and glittering high society drawing rooms. With Inspector Farraday breathing down his neck and time running short, Blackie must navigate a labyrinth of lies, blackmail, and long-buried secrets. The tension crackles through every scene as you'll hear the click of a revolver's hammer, the sharp dialogue of suspects under pressure, and the unmistakable rasp of suspicion in every voice.

What made Boston Blackie a phenomenon across America's radio dials was precisely this—the show's ability to transform Chester Morris's protagonist from a two-bit criminal into a folk hero whose cunning and charm made audiences root for him against the establishment. Premiering in 1944 and dominating airwaves through 1950, the program captured post-war America's fascination with morally complex antiheroes who lived by their own code. The "Evelyn Jones Murder" episode is quintessential Blackie: fast-paced, brilliantly plotted, and featuring that electric chemistry between our hero and the long-suffering Inspector Farraday, whose exasperation barely conceals his grudging respect.

Step into this golden age of mystery radio and experience the unfolding drama as it was meant to be heard—live, immediate, and utterly gripping. Tune in to Boston Blackie's desperate race against the clock to clear his name and unmask a murderer before justice claims an innocent man.
