Step into the shadowy streets of Boston on a sultry summer evening as amateur sleuth Boston Blackie and his trusty sidekick Farraday find themselves entangled in a most peculiar case—a murder weapon disguised as an innocent timepiece. When a prominent banker is found dead beneath a seemingly ordinary cuckoo clock, Blackie must navigate a web of suspects, hidden mechanisms, and murderous ingenuity. What appears to be a crime of passion slowly reveals itself as a masterwork of premeditation, where every tick and every chime brings our hero closer to danger. Listen as the clock's mechanical heart becomes the pulse of suspense, each mechanical whirring amplified by the masterful sound design that made radio dramas the entertainment of choice for millions.

Boston Blackie, a reformed jewel thief turned crime-solver, represented a uniquely American hero during the golden age of radio. Unlike the buttoned-up detectives dominating the airwaves, Blackie operated in the grey zones between law and lawlessness, his checkered past lending authenticity to his understanding of the criminal underworld. This 1947 episode exemplifies the show's remarkable talent for transforming everyday objects into instruments of intrigue—a trademark that kept listeners guessing and gasping throughout its six-year run. The program thrived during an era when families huddled around their radios seeking escape from post-war reality, and "The Murdering Cuckoo Clock" delivers exactly that: mystery, humor, and the promise that even a man of Blackie's questionable background could serve justice.

Don't miss this sterling example of radio mystery done right. Tune in to hear Boston Blackie unwind the secrets hidden within a killer's timepiece—where every second counts and murder keeps perfect time.
