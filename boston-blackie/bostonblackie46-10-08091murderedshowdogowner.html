When the doorbell chimes at Blackie's apartment on this October evening, he expects anything but a case involving the ritzy world of dog shows—yet that's precisely where danger has struck. A prized show dog owner lies dead under mysterious circumstances, and the trail leads through a maze of envious competitors, shady breeding operations, and dark secrets buried beneath ribbons and trophies. As Blackie and his faithful sidekick the Runt navigate the sophisticated circles of Boston's canine elite, the usual suspects emerge: the rival handler nursing a grudge, the mysterious bidder from out of town, the wife with something to hide. The tension builds as our hero uncovers a motive far more sinister than simple competition—one that reaches into the very heart of greed and obsession. With Mary the police lieutenant growing increasingly impatient and danger closing in from unexpected quarters, Blackie must stay several steps ahead to unmask the killer before another victim falls.

Boston Blackie occupied a unique place in the golden age of radio detective fiction. Unlike the harder-boiled operatives dominating the airwaves, Blackie was a reformed thief with underworld connections—a figure straddling both sides of the law. This delightful moral ambiguity, combined with the rapid-fire dialogue and genuine chemistry between cast members, made the show essential listening from 1944 through 1950. The production values were first-rate, the scripts consistently clever, and the show's portrayal of Boston itself added authentic local flavor that distinguished it from countless Manhattan-based mysteries.

Don't miss "Murdered Show Dog Owner"—a case that proves even in the polished world of prize-winning pedigrees, murder plays no favorites. Tune in tonight for a mystery as cunning as Boston Blackie himself.
