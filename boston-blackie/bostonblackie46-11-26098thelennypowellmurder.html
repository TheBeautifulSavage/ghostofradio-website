Picture this: a cold November night in Boston, 1946, when the city's most shadowy streets echo with danger. As our program opens, you'll find yourself thrust into the gritty underworld where Boston Blackie—that reformed jewel thief turned amateur detective—must navigate a labyrinth of lies and corruption to solve the murder of Lenny Powell. Who was Powell, and why was he worth killing? Every suspect has motive, every alibi crumbles under scrutiny, and only Blackie's razor-sharp mind and his trusty sidekick Farraday stand between justice and a killer who walks free. The tension crackles through your loudspeaker as our protagonist unravels this deadly mystery, where the line between the criminal world and the legitimate one blurs into shades of grey.

Boston Blackie emerged from pulp fiction pages directly into radio's golden age, becoming one of the most beloved detective programs of the 1940s. What set Blackie apart from his contemporaries was his unique moral complexity—he wasn't a by-the-book cop or a straightforward hero, but rather a man caught between two worlds, using his intimate knowledge of both underworld and upperworld to crack cases others couldn't touch. This 1946 episode exemplifies the show's brilliance: tight plotting, naturalistic dialogue, and that distinctive Boston atmosphere that made listeners feel they were wandering the same dangerous streets as their hero.

Don't miss "The Lenny Powell Murder"—a masterclass in radio mystery craft that showcases why Boston Blackie remained must-listen programming for millions. Tune in and discover why this reformed crook with a conscience became radio's most compelling detective.
