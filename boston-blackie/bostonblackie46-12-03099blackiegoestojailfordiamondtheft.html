When the cell doors slam shut on Boston Blackie, listeners knew they were in for something special. In this thrilling December 1949 episode, our wisecracking rogue finds himself on the wrong side of the law—framed for a diamond heist he didn't commit. With the police convinced of his guilt and time running out before a wrongful conviction, Blackie must use every ounce of cunning and street smarts to uncover the real culprit from behind bars. The tension crackles through your radio speaker as Blackie navigates the treacherous prison underworld, where trust is a luxury and one misstep could seal his fate forever. Will his trademark charm and detective instincts save him, or will the innocent man finally find himself truly trapped?

Boston Blackie was radio's most enduring gentleman thief, a character who captivated audiences for nearly a decade across multiple networks. Unlike typical hard-boiled detectives of the era, Blackie occupied a unique space in the criminal underworld—a reformed jewel thief with an impeccable moral compass and an uncanny ability to outsmart both crooks and cops alike. The show's brilliance lay in this paradox: audiences rooted for a criminal protagonist because his heart always proved true. By 1949, the series had become a beloved fixture in American homes, with creator Jack Boyle's character transcending the radio medium to become a film and television icon.
