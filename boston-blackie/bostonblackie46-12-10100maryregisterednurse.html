Picture this: the rain hammers against wet Boston streets as our hero Boston Blackie receives a desperate telephone call in the dead of night. A nurse—Mary, competent and composed in her crisp white uniform—has disappeared from City Hospital under the most sinister circumstances, and the police are ready to pin the blame on the one man in town who might actually find her: the reformed jewel thief with connections in every shadowy corner of the city. What begins as a missing persons case quickly spirals into a web of blackmail, addiction, and hospital corruption that reaches far higher than anyone suspected. With his sardonic wit and street-smart instincts, Blackie must navigate the sterile corridors of medicine and the murky underworld of narcotics trafficking to save an innocent woman's life—all while staying one step ahead of both the criminals and the cops who'd rather see him in handcuffs than solving crimes.

By the mid-1940s, Boston Blackie had become radio's most unlikely hero, embodying the postwar American fascination with the antihero who plays by his own rules. Unlike the rigid morality of earlier detective programs, Blackie operated in shades of gray, his criminal past giving him unique access to cases that stumped conventional law enforcement. This episode exemplifies the show's brilliance: examining a timely social issue—hospital crime and the growing drug crisis—while delivering the snappy dialogue and genuine danger that kept millions of listeners glued to their sets each week.

Don't miss this gripping tale of corruption and courage, where a nurse's fate rests in the hands of Boston's most famous reformed crook. Tune in now to discover whether Blackie can crack the case before another victim disappears into the darkness.
