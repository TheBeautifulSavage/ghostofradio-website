# Millicent Bromley Kidnapped

When the telephone rings in Boston Blackie's shabby office on this fateful April evening, it brings news that will plunge our reformed jewel thief into the darkest case of his career. A young society girl—the lovely Millicent Bromley—has vanished without a trace, and the ransom note that arrives hours later carries all the hallmarks of a ruthless criminal operation. As Blackie and his faithful sidekick the Inspector race through Boston's fog-shrouded streets, from the genteel drawing rooms of Beacon Hill to the dangerous dockside warehouses where anything can happen, listeners will find themselves suspended in mounting tension. Every shadow conceals a suspect; every alibi crumbles under scrutiny. The clock ticks relentlessly, and with each passing moment, Millicent's fate grows more precarious.

*Boston Blackie* captured America's imagination throughout the 1940s by offering something audiences craved during wartime—a charming antihero who operated by his own code of honor. Unlike the hard-boiled detectives dominating the airwaves, Boston Blackie was a gentleman criminal reformed into a gentleman detective, played with tremendous charm by Chester Morris (who originated the role on screen). The show's rapid-fire dialogue, clever plotting, and the genuine chemistry between Blackie and his Inspector friend created compelling listening that kept families huddled around their radios. This particular episode exemplifies why the series earned such devoted followers.

If you've never experienced the wit and suspense of Boston Blackie's world, this kidnapping case is the perfect entry point. Tune in now and discover why this 1947 broadcast remains a standout in the golden age of radio mystery. The game is afoot, and only Boston Blackie can uncover the truth hidden in Boston's shadowy underworld.
