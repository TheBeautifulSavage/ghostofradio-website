The fog rolls thick through the Boston night as Boston Blackie finds himself in the crosshairs of circumstance once again. When a valuable shipment goes missing and a night watchman lies wounded, all fingers point to the city's most notorious—and most misunderstood—reformed crook. But Blackie knows the truth: he's been framed, and worse, someone's using his name to cover their own felonies. With his sardonic wit as his only shield and his unwavering sense of justice as his compass, Blackie must navigate a treacherous web of corruption and deceit to clear his name before the police close in. This electrifying installment crackles with the uncertainty that made the show essential listening—you'll never know if Blackie's latest scheme will save him or condemn him further.

Boston Blackie captivated millions during radio's golden age by daring to make a criminal the hero. Debuting in 1944, the series defied convention by centering on a protagonist who operated in moral gray zones, challenging listeners' assumptions about good and evil in a post-war America grappling with its own complexities. The show's smart dialogue and intricate plots attracted devoted audiences across NBC, CBS, and Mutual networks, spawning successful film adaptations and cementing Blackie as an enduring archetype of the anti-hero detective.

Step back into a time when suspense was delivered through the spoken word alone, when a creaking door or a well-timed pause could send shivers down your spine. Tune in to "Blackie Shoots a Watchman" and discover why this program held listeners on the edge of their seats nearly eighty years ago.
