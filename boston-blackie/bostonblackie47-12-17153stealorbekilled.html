Picture this: a fog-shrouded Boston street corner, the distant wail of a police siren cutting through the December night. Boston Blackie receives an urgent midnight call—a desperate voice warns him that someone will die before dawn unless a fortune in jewels surfaces by daybreak. But here's the twist: Blackie himself is being framed for the theft. With Inspector Farraday already convinced of his guilt and a killer closing in from the shadows, our reformed ex-convict detective must navigate a razor-thin line between clearing his name and becoming the very criminal he's worked so hard to prove he isn't. This is the kind of high-stakes thriller that kept millions of listeners glued to their sets, hearts pounding as the clock ticked toward an inevitable showdown.

Boston Blackie was that rare radio phenomenon—a character beloved despite his checkered past, or perhaps because of it. Throughout the late 1940s, audiences tuned in faithfully to follow Richard Kollmar's quick-witted, fast-talking protagonist as he outwitted both criminals and cops alike, always operating in that thrilling gray area between the law and the underworld. The show's genius lay in its fast-paced scripts, snappy dialogue, and genuine affection for a antihero who genuinely wanted to go straight, even when circumstances kept pulling him back into danger. Each episode was a masterclass in radio drama—no elaborate sets needed, just smart writing and superb sound design to transport you directly into Blackie's dangerous world.

This December episode perfectly captures what made the series essential listening for detective fiction fans. Whether you're a devoted aficionado of classic radio mysteries or discovering Boston Blackie for the first time, "Steal or Be Killed" delivers exactly what the medium does best: suspense, wit, and the voice of a rogue with a conscience. Tune in—you won't want to miss how Blackie escapes this one.
