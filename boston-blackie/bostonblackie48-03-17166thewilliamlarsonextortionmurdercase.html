As the glow of your radio dial flickers to life on this March evening in 1948, Boston Blackie materializes from the shadows of the city streets with his faithful sidekick The Runt, drawn into a web of blackmail and murder that threatens to unravel the carefully constructed life of William Larson. Listen closely as anonymous threats creep across telephone wires, as Larson's darkest secrets become currency for a killer, and as our reformed crook-turned-detective races against time to separate desperate extortion from premeditated murder. The tension mounts with each ticking second—is this a case of vengeance, greed, or something far more sinister? Every clue matters, every suspect could be the killer, and Blackie's razor-sharp intellect is the only thing standing between justice and a perfect crime.

Boston Blackie had captured the American imagination since the 1920s, but it was this broadcast era—these vital years from 1944 to 1950—that cemented the character's legacy in the hearts of millions of radio listeners. Played with irresistible charm and cunning by Chester Morris, Blackie embodied a uniquely American mythology: the clever rogue who uses his underworld knowledge for righteous ends, walking the razor's edge between law and lawlessness. These tightly crafted thirty-minute mysteries, honed by top-tier writers working for NBC, CBS, and Mutual, delivered intelligent crime drama when the genre was at its absolute peak, each episode a masterclass in suspense and narrative economy.

Don't miss "The William Larson Extortion Murder Case"—a case that will keep you guessing right up to the final, shocking revelation. Tune in and discover why Boston Blackie remains radio's most captivating detective.
