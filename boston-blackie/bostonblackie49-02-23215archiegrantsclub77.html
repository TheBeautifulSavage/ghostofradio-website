When Boston Blackie accepts an invitation to the exclusive Archie Grant's Club on this February evening in 1949, he walks straight into a web of blackmail, stolen diamonds, and a murder that won't stay solved. The velvet-cushioned rooms of Boston's most prestigious establishment become a pressure cooker of secrets as Blackie and his faithful sidekick the Runt navigate between acid-tongued society figures, nervous club employees, and a corpse that raises more questions than answers. With Lieutenant Farraday breathing down his neck and a killer still prowling the mahogany-paneled halls, Blackie must use every ounce of his criminal cunning to clear his name and unmask the guilty party—all while keeping one eye on a cache of gems that may be worth killing for.

Boston Blackie thrived during radio's golden age because audiences couldn't get enough of the charming reformed crook who operated in that tantalizing gray area between law and lawlessness. Unlike the Boy Scout detectives populating the airwaves, Blackie used his underworld connections and street smarts to solve mysteries that stumped the police, making him a kind of folk hero to Depression and postwar listeners who appreciated his clever shortcuts and sharp patter. The show's writers crafted increasingly complex plots during these later seasons, moving beyond simple mysteries into exploring the psychology of greed and the dangerous allure of high society's hidden vices.

Settle into your favorite chair, tune the dial to the frequency of intrigue, and let yourself be transported back to an era when a smooth voice and a clever mind could talk their way out of almost anything. Boston Blackie awaits.
