Picture this: the telephone rings in the dead of night at Boston Blackie's apartment, and on the other end is the desperate, trembling voice of Dolly Preston—a woman who has nowhere else to turn. What begins as a seemingly simple cry for assistance spirals into a labyrinth of blackmail, murder, and dangerous secrets that reach far beyond what Blackie bargained for. As our reformed jewel thief navigates the shadowy streets of 1940s Boston, he finds himself caught between the police, who still view him with suspicion, and a web of criminals who would see him silenced forever. With only his quick wit, street smarts, and loyal sidekick the Major at his side, Blackie must unravel Dolly's tangled story before it's too late. The tension mounts with every clue, every false lead, and every sinister encounter—will our hero solve the mystery in time, or will he become the next victim?

Boston Blackie represented something unique in the golden age of radio: the anti-hero detective who'd walked both sides of the law. Unlike the clean-cut G-men of other programs, Blackie's ambiguous past made him relatable and unpredictable, appealing to audiences weary of one-dimensional heroes. Running from 1944 to 1950, the show captured post-war America's fascination with redemption and second chances, while the stellar writing kept listeners on the edge of their seats week after week. "Dolly Preston Calls for Help" exemplifies the show's signature blend of gritty realism and breathless mystery.
