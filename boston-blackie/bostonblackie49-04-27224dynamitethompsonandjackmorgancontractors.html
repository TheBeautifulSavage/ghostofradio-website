# Boston Blackie: "Dynamite Thompson and Jack Morgan, Contractors"

Picture this: a fog-choked Boston waterfront, the salt air thick with danger and deceit. It's April 27th, 1949, and Boston Blackie—that reformed jewel thief with a nose for trouble—finds himself entangled with a pair of contractors whose construction project masks something far more sinister. When dynamite goes missing from a job site and a man turns up dead, Blackie must navigate a maze of suspects, shifting allegiances, and double-crosses to uncover the truth. Will his razor-sharp wit and street smarts be enough to outsmart genuine killers? Tune in to discover how Blackie and his faithful sidekick The Runt talk their way through danger, bluffing and clever-talking across the city's shadowy underbelly with nothing but their wits and Blackie's uncanny ability to stay one step ahead of both the police and the criminals.

Boston Blackie occupied a unique niche in radio's golden age—a sympathetic criminal protagonist when such characters were still daring and unusual. The show thrived on NBC, CBS, and Mutual networks throughout the mid-to-late 1940s, offering listeners a sophisticated blend of mystery, humor, and moral ambiguity that appealed to both hardboiled detective fans and those seeking lighter entertainment. Richard Kollmar's portrayal of Blackie captured something essential about postwar American sensibilities: the rogue with a code, the outsider who knew both sides of the law and could exploit that knowledge for justice.

This particular episode exemplifies why Boston Blackie endured as a fan favorite—combining industrial-age detail with timeless noir atmosphere. Don't miss this glimpse into a vanished Boston, heard through the crackle and hum of authentic 1940s radio transmission.
