# Nobody at the Door

When the knock comes at midnight, Boston Blackie knows better than to answer it. But in this gripping May 1949 episode, our favorite reformed jewel thief finds himself caught between the law and the lawless once again, as a mysterious visitor at his door sets off a chain of events that will test every ounce of his street smarts and underworld connections. With his faithful sidekick the Runt and the ever-skeptical Inspector Farraday breathing down his neck, Blackie must unravel a twisted tale of double-crossing and deception that threatens to frame him for a crime he didn't commit. The tension crackles through your radio speaker as shadowy figures move through Boston's dimly-lit streets, and you'll find yourself leaning closer to the speaker, wondering who—or what—lies on the other side of that fateful door.

Boston Blackie's enduring appeal lay in the character's unique position as an anti-hero audiences could root for—a criminal with a heart of gold operating in the moral gray zones of post-war America. By 1949, the show had already become a beloved institution across three major networks, with veteran actor Chester Morris bringing a sophisticated charm and quick wit to the role that made listeners forget they were rooting for a former jewel thief. These episodes represent radio's golden age, crafted during a time when sound design and tight scripts could transport millions of Americans into shadowy worlds of mystery and intrigue.

Tune in to experience the masterful storytelling that made Boston Blackie essential listening for mystery fans across the nation. This episode exemplifies everything that made the series a classic—snappy dialogue, genuine suspense, and a protagonist you can't help but believe in, even when the chips are down.
