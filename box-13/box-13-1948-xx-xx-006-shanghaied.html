# Box 13: "Shanghaied"

When Dan Holliday opens the mysterious Box 13 on his desk this fateful evening, he discovers a cryptic message that will plunge him into the shadowy world of Shanghai's waterfront—a realm of corrupt ship captains, desperate stowaways, and men willing to kill for passage to the Orient. Our hero finds himself caught between the tongs and international smugglers, navigating treacherous alleys where trust is a luxury and every shadow conceals danger. The crackle of a ship's horn, the slap of water against the dock, and the menacing whispers of men conducting business in the darkness transport you to 1948's most dangerous corners of the globe. With only his wits and determination to guide him, Holliday must unravel a web of deception before he himself becomes cargo bound for parts unknown.

Box 13 represents the golden age of syndicated radio adventure, when resourceful protagonists and mysterious challenges captivated American audiences during the postwar boom. Created by and starring veteran actor Robert Mitchum, the series perfectly captured that era's fascination with international intrigue and self-made men who could solve any puzzle. Each week, Holliday's encounters with the contents of Box 13 revealed cases that the police couldn't—or wouldn't—touch, crafted with snappy dialogue and genuine suspense that kept listeners on the edge of their seats.

If you've ever wondered what drew millions of Americans to their radio sets night after night, "Shanghaied" is an essential listen. The episode crackles with authentic period atmosphere and the kind of breakneck pacing that made Box 13 a must-hear among adventure enthusiasts. Tune in and discover why this forgotten gem deserves rediscovery.
