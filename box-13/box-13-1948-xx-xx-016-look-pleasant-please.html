# Box 13: Look Pleasant, Please

When Dan Holliday opens that mysterious Box 13 on this evening's broadcast, he discovers a photograph—a simple snapshot that will plunge him into a labyrinth of blackmail, desperation, and hidden identities. A desperate woman arrives at his office with a terrible choice: retrieve a damning image before it destroys everything she holds dear, or watch her carefully constructed life crumble into scandal. As our resourceful protagonist races through the shadowy streets to intercept the photograph before it reaches its intended recipient, the tension crackles with each ticking clock and muffled footstep. "Look Pleasant, Please" captures the essence of Box 13's formula: a taut, fast-paced mystery where ordinary people harbor extraordinary secrets, and where Dan Holliday must navigate the murky space between right and wrong to help those who've nowhere else to turn.

Box 13 arrived in 1948 as a beacon of intelligent adventure radio, syndicated across America at a moment when the medium was reaching its golden zenith. Starring Alan Ladd—already gaining prominence in Hollywood—the series became a showcase for tightly plotted scripts and morally complex storytelling that elevated it above typical detective fare. Each episode begins with Holliday receiving an anonymous clue from Box 13, setting him on courses through urban underworlds where blackmailers, con artists, and desperate innocents collide. The show's brilliance lay in its recognition that mysteries often involve people worth saving, even when the law might not see it that way.

Whether you're a devoted fan of classic radio or newly discovering the pleasures of this overlooked gem, "Look Pleasant, Please" exemplifies why audiences huddled around their sets each week. Tune in for an evening of genuine mystery, authentic suspense, and the golden voice of Alan Ladd navigating the shadows of Box 13.
