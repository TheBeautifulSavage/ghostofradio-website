# Box 13: Episode 113.5

When postal inspector Dan Holliday opens the mysterious Box 13 on this autumn evening in 1948, he finds himself entangled in a web of deception that stretches from the seedy underbelly of the city to the gleaming penthouses of the wealthy elite. As Dan's gruff voice crackles through the airwaves, listeners are transported into a shadowy world where a single clue—a photograph, a matchbook, a hastily scrawled note—becomes the thread that unravels an elaborate criminal conspiracy. The atmospheric sound design of creaking hinges, distant sirens, and ominous musical stings sets the perfect backdrop for a mystery that demands your complete attention. With each new discovery, the stakes grow higher and the danger more palpable, leaving you wondering whether Dan will solve this case before those who left their secrets in Box 13 decide to silence him permanently.

Box 13 was a trailblazer of the adventure-mystery genre during radio's golden age, pioneering the anthology format that would influence countless shows to follow. Starring the gravelly-voiced Alan Ladd in his final radio role before transitioning fully to Hollywood stardom, the series captured the postwar American zeitgeist—a time when ordinary people stumbled upon extraordinary secrets and had to navigate moral ambiguity and corruption. The show's brilliance lay in its premise: every episode a complete story born from the contents of a single mailbox, each one a gateway into someone's hidden truth. This particular episode, number 113.5, represents the show at its creative peak, combining tight plotting with the kind of character work that made listeners feel they truly knew Dan Holliday's world.
