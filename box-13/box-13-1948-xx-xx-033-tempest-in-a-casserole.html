# Box 13: Tempest In A Casserole

When Dan Hollis opens his mysterious mailbox numbered 13 on this fateful evening, he discovers a plea wrapped in culinary intrigue—a case so deliciously deceptive that it threatens to boil over into scandal, betrayal, and perhaps murder. What begins as an innocent dinner party invitation spirals into a web of poisoned accusations and simmering resentments as our hero must navigate the treacherous terrain of small-town secrets, where a simple casserole becomes the centerpiece of a crime that could destroy innocent lives. The tension crackles like a sizzling stovetop as Hollis peels back layers of deception, each reveal more shocking than the last, all while his trusted contacts work the angles from their corners of the underworld. You'll find yourself gripping your radio dial as the mystery deepens, wondering whether the culprit is motivated by jealousy, greed, or something far more sinister lurking beneath the surface of respectability.

*Box 13*, syndicated across America in 1948, represented the golden age of radio mystery adventure—that sweet spot where intelligent writing met the intimate immediacy of sound. Starring the suave Dan Hollis, each episode delivered a complete, satisfying mystery wrapped in atmospheric sound design and clever dialogue, creating a show that demanded rapt attention and rewarded devoted listeners with genuine surprises. This particular episode exemplifies the show's genius for mining drama from everyday life, proving that danger and intrigue could emerge from the most mundane circumstances.

Tune in now and discover why thousands of listeners made *Box 13* their appointment with mystery each week. In thirty-three episodes, this show earned its cult status through sheer storytelling excellence and imagination. "Tempest In A Casserole" awaits—a perfect entry point into a classic thriller that still captivates audiences three-quarters of a century later.
