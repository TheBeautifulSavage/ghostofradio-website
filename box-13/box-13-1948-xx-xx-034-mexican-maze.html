# Mexican Maze

When Dan Holliday answers the cryptic summons from Box 13, he finds himself plunged into the shadowy streets of Mexico City—a labyrinth of colonial architecture, dangerous strangers, and secrets buried deeper than any tourist could imagine. This week's adventure crackles with the exotic intrigue of south-of-the-border noir as our resourceful hero must navigate language barriers, corrupt officials, and a conspiracy that grows more deadly with each wrong turn. You'll hear the atmospheric street sounds of a foreign city—the distant mariachi, the urgent Spanish whispers—as Holliday races against time to solve a puzzle that could mean the difference between freedom and a nameless grave in a foreign land. Actor Dan Duryea's distinctive voice carries you through every tense moment, from tense alley confrontations to nail-biting close calls that will keep you gripped until the final revelation.

*Box 13* arrived on American radios during the golden age of syndicated adventure programming, when networks hungry for fresh content gave writers freedom to experiment beyond the restrictive formulas of network radio. This 1948 episode exemplifies what made the series a cult favorite among radio enthusiasts: intelligent scripts, international settings, and a protagonist clever enough to outwit his adversaries through wit rather than mere brute force. Unlike the police procedurals and superhero serials dominating the airwaves, *Box 13* offered genuine mystery and globe-trotting adventure for the discerning listener.

If you've never experienced Dan Holliday's world of danger and intrigue, *Mexican Maze* is the perfect entry point—a self-contained story that delivers everything vintage radio adventure promised: exotic atmosphere, genuine suspense, and the kind of storytelling that made families gather around their sets every week. Tune in now and discover why *Box 13* remains unforgettable.
