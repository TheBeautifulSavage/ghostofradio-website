# Box 13: The Clay Pigeon

When Dan Holliday receives a cryptic message in his mysterious Box 13, he finds himself ensnared in a deadly game where he himself becomes the hunted quarry. In "The Clay Pigeon," our quick-witted adventurer discovers that someone has placed an enormous bounty on his head—and now every two-bit criminal and desperate fortune-seeker in the city is closing in. With danger lurking around every corner and trust becoming his scarcest commodity, Holliday must outmaneuver a growing conspiracy while racing against time to uncover who's pulling the strings. The tension crackles across the airwaves as the protagonist finds himself cornered, outnumbered, and forced to use every ounce of cunning to survive until dawn. This is Box 13 at its finest: a taut, breathless thriller that leaves listeners gripping their armrests.

Box 13 arrived in 1948 as a breath of fresh air in the adventure serial format, built around a deceptively simple premise that yielded endless dramatic possibilities. Starring Alan Ladd in his only major radio role, the show proved that motion picture talent could command the intimate medium of radio with the same magnetism they commanded the silver screen. Each episode's central conceit—anonymous messages delivered to a mysterious box—allowed writers to craft self-contained mysteries while maintaining serialized appeal. The show's syndicated distribution meant it reached audiences coast-to-coast during radio's golden twilight, cementing its place in adventure broadcasting history.

"The Clay Pigeon" showcases everything that made Box 13 a must-listen for fans of intelligent, fast-paced mystery. Tune in and experience the master class in suspense that kept millions of Americans on the edge of their seats during that golden age of broadcasting.
