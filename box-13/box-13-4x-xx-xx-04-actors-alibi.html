When the lights dim and that haunting theme music swells across your radio speaker, you know you're about to step into the shadowy world of Dan Hawkins and his mysterious Box 13. In this fourth episode, "Actor's Alibi," a prominent stage actor arrives at Hawkins's office with a problem that only grows more sinister with each passing moment. Someone has framed him for a jewel theft that occurred during his opening night performance—but his alibi is locked tighter than a vault. As Hawkins peels back the layers of the theater world's glittering facade, he uncovers a web of jealousy, desperation, and calculated deception. The clock ticks mercilessly as evidence piles against our desperate client, and Hawkins must navigate the treacherous corridors of backstage intrigue to find the real culprit before an innocent man takes the fall.

Box 13 stands as a masterpiece of syndicated radio entertainment, representing that golden era when mystery and adventure commanded America's living rooms. The show's ingenious premise—a problem solver who works for those who send their cases to a mysterious box numbered 13—gave writers endless narrative possibilities while maintaining an episodic, accessible format. With Alan Ladd's commanding voice bringing Dan Hawkins to life and expertly crafted scripts that balanced clever plotting with genuine suspense, Box 13 delivered consistent, high-quality entertainment across its 1948-1949 run.

If you've never experienced the particular thrill of old-time radio drama—where everything lives in the realm of suggestion and sound—"Actor's Alibi" is the perfect entry point. Settle in, let the crackling broadcast transport you to another era, and prepare to be thoroughly entertained.
