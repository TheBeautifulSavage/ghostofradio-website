When Dan Holliday opens his mail on a foggy San Francisco evening, he finds an anonymous clipping about a murdered journalist stuffed inside the mysterious Box 13—and with it comes an urgent plea for help that will plunge him into the cutthroat world of newspaper corruption, blackmail, and dangerous secrets. As our hero races through the city's newspaper district, following cryptic leads and dodging shadowy figures who seem determined to keep the truth buried, listeners will be drawn into a labyrinthine mystery where every headline conceals a darker story. The episode crackles with the authentic snap of 1940s journalism—telephones ringing in crowded newsrooms, printing presses thundering in the night, and the unmistakable tension of a man caught between competing truths. Who murdered the reporter? Why was Box 13 chosen as the messenger? The answers lie somewhere in the gray zone between journalistic integrity and criminal conspiracy, and Holliday must navigate both to survive.

Box 13*, which aired in syndication from 1948 to 1949, captured the post-war fascination with pulp adventure and urban mystery, starring the magnetic Alan Ladd as Dan Holliday—a man forever bound by fate and the contents of an enigmatic box that arrives with impossible cases. Each episode showcased the show's signature blend of hard-boiled detective work and the theatrical energy that defined the golden age of radio drama, when sound effects and voice acting transported millions into worlds of intrigue and danger.
