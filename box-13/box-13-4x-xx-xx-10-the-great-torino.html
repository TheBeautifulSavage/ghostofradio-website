# Box 13: The Great Torino

When Dan Holliday opens the mysterious box that arrives at his Chicago apartment each week, he never knows what perilous assignment awaits—but nothing quite prepares him for the elaborate con that unfolds in "The Great Torino." A master illusionist has vanished during his most famous trick, leaving behind a trail of forged documents, missing jewels, and a network of accomplices who seem to anticipate Holliday's every move. As our intrepid adventurer pieces together the puzzle, the line between illusion and reality blurs dangerously. Is Torino truly gone, or has he orchestrated an even more elaborate deception? With each clue uncovered, the danger mounts—and Holliday finds himself trapped in a labyrinth of misdirection where the greatest magic trick of all might be his own survival.

*Box 13* captured the imagination of post-war American audiences hungry for intelligent adventure that didn't insult their intelligence. Starring Alan Ladd in his only radio series, the show distinguished itself through clever plotting and psychological depth absent from more pulpy competitors. Each episode turned on a genuine mystery rather than mere action, with Holliday relying on wit and deduction as much as courage. "The Great Torino" exemplifies the show's sophistication—a locked-room puzzle translated into the modern world, where the criminal mind proves as fascinating as any detective's.

Radio listeners in 1948 would have thrilled to Ladd's distinctive voice—already famous from *This Gun for Hire*—lending gravitas to Holliday's internal monologues and tense confrontations. The sound design creates an atmospheric Chicago noir, complete with the ambient bustle of the city and the intimate silence of Holliday's apartment where each mysterious envelope arrives.

Tune in and experience why *Box 13* remains one of adventure radio's most treasured gems. What's inside the box this time?
