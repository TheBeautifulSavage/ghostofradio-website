# Box 13: "Damsel in Distress"

When the midnight hour strikes and an urgent telephone call pierces the darkness of Dan Holliday's apartment, listeners know they're about to be thrust into another mystery. In this pulse-pounding episode, a woman's terrified voice crackles through the receiver—her sister has vanished without a trace, and she has nowhere else to turn. With nothing but a cryptic note and the contents of Box 13 to guide him, our resourceful hero must navigate a treacherous web of deception and danger to uncover the truth before it's too late. The orchestra swells with foreboding as Holliday begins his investigation, each clue drawing him deeper into a labyrinth where trust is a luxury and every shadow conceals a potential threat. Expect breathtaking chases through fog-shrouded streets, narrow escapes, and revelations that will keep you riveted to your radio.

Box 13 captured the imagination of post-war America with its ingenious premise: a mystery columnist who solves real crimes by consulting the anonymous tips and mysterious objects that arrive in his titular mailbox. Alan Ladd's crisp narration brought sophistication to the role, while the show's tight thirty-minute format and rapid-fire dialogue set the standard for adventure serials in the 1948-1949 broadcasting season. This particular episode exemplifies why the program became syndicated throughout the nation—combining genuine emotional stakes with clever plotting and the irresistible allure of an everyman hero thrust into extraordinary circumstances.

Don't miss "Damsel in Distress"—tune in as Dan Holliday races against time to save an innocent life. In the world of Box 13, danger waits around every corner, and only wits and determination can light the way through the darkness.
