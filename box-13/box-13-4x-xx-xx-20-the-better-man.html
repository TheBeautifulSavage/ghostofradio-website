# Box 13: The Better Man

Dan Holliday, mysterious operator of the advice column "Box 13," finds himself entangled in a case of mistaken identity with deadly consequences in this taut episode of intrigue. When a desperate letter arrives claiming that two men cannot occupy the same life, Holliday is drawn into a shadowy world where reputation becomes currency and a single lie can unravel a man's existence. As the tension mounts, listeners will find themselves caught between two compelling claims, each man convinced he is the rightful one—and neither willing to concede. The episode crackles with the kind of moral ambiguity that made *Box 13* a must-listen for adventure fans, where the final answer arrives not with a bang, but with a whisper that reframes everything that came before.

Starring John Dehner as the quick-witted Dan Holliday, *Box 13* was a syndicated gem that thrived in the post-war golden age of radio drama. Each week, Holliday would wade through the moral quicksand of his readers' dilemmas, turning letter-writing into high-stakes detective work. The show's genius lay in its formula: the mundane became dangerous, and the drawing room became a minefield of secrets. Unlike the spectacular crime fighters and masked heroes of radio's roster, Holliday operated in gray zones where right and wrong were never quite certain. "The Better Man" exemplifies this approach, presenting a puzzle that resists easy solution.

For fans of sophisticated mystery and those who appreciate the understated tension that only radio could create, this episode remains a masterclass in dramatic storytelling. Tune in to *Box 13: The Better Man* and discover why advice columnists made for the most unlikely—and most compelling—radio detectives.
