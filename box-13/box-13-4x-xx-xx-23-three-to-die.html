# Three to Die

The clock strikes midnight in a Manhattan hotel room, and DanHolliday receives a cryptic message slipped under his door—three names, three deaths, and a mystery locked away in Box 13. As the smooth-voiced adventurer races against time through the neon-lit streets of the city, listeners will find themselves caught in a web of blackmail, betrayal, and desperation where every shadow conceals a killer. This episode crackles with the noir sensibility of late-1940s radio drama: tense interrogations punctuated by the screech of tires, ominous musical stings that signal approaching danger, and the relentless ticking of a clock counting down to tragedy. With only his wits and a mysterious box of secrets at his disposal, Holliday must determine which of three suspects will meet their fatal end—or if he can prevent the murders altogether.

*Box 13*, starring Alan Ladd as the irrepressible Dan Holliday, represented the golden age of syndicated radio drama when local stations across America could deliver big-budget adventure tales to millions of eager ears. Created during the post-war boom when radio remained entertainment's undisputed king, the show perfectly captured the restless energy and moral ambiguity of the late 1940s. Each episode turned on Holliday's discovery of mysterious items in an abandoned mailbox—ordinary objects that somehow held the power to unravel extraordinary secrets. It was serialized suspense crafted for an audience that craved intelligent, fast-paced storytelling night after night.

Whether you're a devoted fan of classic radio or discovering this lost treasure for the first time, "Three to Die" exemplifies everything that made *Box 13* essential listening for a generation. Tune in and let the darkness pull you in.
