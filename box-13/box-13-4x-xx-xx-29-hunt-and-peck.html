# Box 13: Hunt and Peck

When a mysterious message arrives at the offices of Dan Holliday, radio's most compelling amateur detective, it sets in motion a thrilling game of cat and mouse that will test every ounce of his wits and courage. "Hunt and Peck" crackles with the tension of a man who has stumbled upon secrets someone desperately wants to keep buried. As typewriter keys clatter in darkened rooms and shadowy figures move through city streets, Holliday must decode cryptic clues and outmaneuver dangerous adversaries who'll stop at nothing to silence him. The episode builds with remarkable momentum—each revelation pulling our hero deeper into a web of deception where the line between hunter and hunted blurs dangerously. You'll find yourself leaning closer to the speaker, heart pounding, as Holliday races against time to uncover the truth hidden in plain sight.

Box 13 stands as a remarkable achievement in the golden age of radio drama, offering something refreshingly different from the typical detective fare that dominated the airwaves of 1948-1949. Created around the concept of a mysterious mailbox that brings adventure to its discoverer, the show showcased the brilliant voice and improvisational talents of Alan Ladd, who brought charismatic realism to Holliday's character. Unlike the formulaic mysteries of the era, Box 13 emphasized clever plotting and genuine suspense, with each episode presenting puzzles that felt genuinely challenging rather than conveniently resolved. The show's syndicated nature allowed it to reach audiences across the country who craved intelligent, adult-oriented entertainment.

If you've never experienced the unique thrill of Box 13, or if you're a devoted fan seeking to revisit this gem, "Hunt and Peck" represents the show at its finest—proof that great radio drama transcends the decades. Tune in now and discover why audiences in 1949 simply couldn't miss what Box 13 would deliver next.
