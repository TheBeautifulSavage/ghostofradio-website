# Box 13: Dan and the Wonderful Lamp

Step into the shadowy world of Dan Holiday, mystery reporter extraordinaire, as he pursues a tantalizing lead that arrives in the mail—another cryptic assignment from the anonymous Box 13. This time, our quick-thinking hero finds himself entangled in a case involving an ancient Arabian artifact, a lamp that promises untold riches to whoever possesses it. As Dan investigates deeper into the murky underworld of collectors and thieves, he discovers that some desires are deadly enough to kill for. With danger lurking in every corner and his wits his only weapon, Dan must separate fact from fancy to uncover who's pulling the strings behind this modern-day treasure hunt. Expect sharp dialogue, tense confrontations, and plot twists that will keep you guessing until the final revelation—all delivered with the crackle and authenticity that made radio's golden age truly golden.

Box 13 remains a masterpiece of syndicated adventure radio, representing that unique sweet spot of the late 1940s when radio drama was reaching its creative zenith. Created by and starring Robert Readick, the show capitalized on the public's insatiable appetite for mystery and intrigue just as television was beginning to steal audiences away. These standalone episodes, each triggered by a new assignment from the mysterious Box 13, offered perfect thirty-minute packages of entertainment—complete stories with memorable characters, moral complexity, and genuine suspense. The show's influence on later mystery programming cannot be overstated, and it remains a treasure for fans of classic radio craftsmanship.

Don't miss this essential chapter in Dan Holiday's chronicle of investigations and intrigue. Tune in as Box 13 delivers another unforgettable mystery from radio's finest hour.
