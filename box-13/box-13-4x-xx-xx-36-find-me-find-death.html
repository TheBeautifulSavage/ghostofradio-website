In this chilling installment of *Box 13*, our protagonist Dan Holliday finds himself ensnared in a labyrinth of blackmail and murder most foul. When a desperate woman's cryptic message arrives at his office—scrawled on a scrap of paper and sealed with terror—Holliday is drawn into a web where every shadow conceals a suspect and every clue leads deeper into darkness. The killer's taunt echoes through the script: "Find me, find death." As Holliday races against time through fog-shrouded streets and dimly-lit rooms, listeners will experience the genuine dread of a man one step behind a cunning murderer, unsure whether his next move brings him closer to justice or into the killer's waiting trap.

Box 13* distinguished itself in the golden age of radio mystery as a show of remarkable sophistication and stylistic flair. Created by and starring Alan Ladd in his pre-Hollywood glory days, the syndicated series captured the hardboiled noir sensibility perfectly suited to the post-war American imagination—gritty, psychologically complex, and unafraid to linger in moral ambiguity. Though the show's run was brief (1948-1949), its influence on the detective radio genre was substantial, paving the way for the psychological thrillers that would dominate the medium's final years. Each episode crackled with genuine menace, atmospheric sound design, and Ladd's distinctive, world-weary delivery.

Tune in now to experience *Box 13: Find Me, Find Death*—a masterclass in suspense that proves radio's golden age produced mysteries as compelling as anything committed to film. Will Holliday solve the case, or will he become another victim of this elaborate, deadly game?
