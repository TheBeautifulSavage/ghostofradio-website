# Box 13: "Daytime Nightmare"

When Dan Holliday reaches into his mysterious mailbox to find Box 13's latest assignment, he discovers something far more disturbing than the usual cryptic case—a photograph that shouldn't exist, and a desperate plea from a woman whose nightmare has somehow bled into waking life. What begins as a simple daytime investigation spirals into a hallucinatory descent where reality fractures and every shadow conceals a sinister truth. The peculiar genius of this episode lies in how it subverts the show's familiar formula, transforming the bright daylight hours into something far more unsettling than any midnight mystery. As Holliday peels back layer after layer of deception, listeners must grapple with whether they're following a genuine investigation or descending into madness alongside him—a masterful radio trick that plays with sound design and pacing to create an almost Kafkaesque sense of creeping dread.

Box 13 thrived on the postwar appetite for intelligent adventure mysteries, with each episode featuring the cunning insurance investigator unraveling impossible cases triggered by anonymous requests. Aired during syndication's golden age of 1948-1949, the show distinguished itself through crisp writing and the confident performance of its lead, avoiding the supernatural explanations that lesser programs relied upon. "Daytime Nightmare" exemplifies why the series earned such devoted listeners—it understands that true terror often emerges from the rational world turned sideways, from the everyday made strange.

For anyone seeking that rare blend of psychological thriller and detective intrigue, this episode represents Box 13 at its finest. Tune in and experience what thousands discovered nearly seventy-five years ago: sometimes the most dangerous mysteries are the ones that make you question your own mind.
