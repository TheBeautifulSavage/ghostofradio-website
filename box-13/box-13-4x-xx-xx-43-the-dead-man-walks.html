# Box 13: The Dead Man Walks

When a corpse sits up in the county morgue and walks out into the fog-shrouded night, private investigator Dan Holiday knows he's stumbled into something far darker than the usual case of blackmail and extortion that arrived in his mysterious Box 13. This chilling episode plunges listeners into a realm where the line between the living and the dead blurs dangerously, and every shadow conceals a new threat. As Holiday pursues the impossible—a man who should be six feet under—the tension mounts with each clue, each encounter, building toward a revelation that will shake even the most seasoned detective. The atmospheric sound design crackles with authenticity: the slap of wet shoes on pavement, the creak of the morgue door, whispered conversations thick with dread. You'll hear the genuine fear in the voices of those who've witnessed something that defies explanation, and the steely determination of a man who won't rest until he uncovers the truth, no matter how bizarre.

Box 13 itself was a marvel of postwar radio storytelling, syndicated across America when mystery-hungry audiences were hungry for cerebral thrills without the supernatural trappings of horror shows. Starring Alan Ladd—already a rising Hollywood name—the series distinguished itself through intelligent plotting and a protagonist who solved crimes through deduction and grit rather than gimmickry. "The Dead Man Walks" exemplifies why the show developed such a devoted following: it asks "what if?" with theatrical flair while maintaining the grounded detective procedural that made Box 13 appointment listening for millions.

Tune in now to discover what secrets lie beneath this impossible case. Does a man truly return from death, or is something far more sinister at work? Box 13 awaits your investigation.
