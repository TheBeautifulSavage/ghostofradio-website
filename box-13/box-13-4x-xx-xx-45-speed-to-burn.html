Picture yourself huddled near the radio dial on a tense evening in 1948, the amber glow of the tube casting dancing shadows across your living room. In "Speed to Burn," our intrepid host Dan Hallowell finds himself caught in a deadly race against time when a cryptic message arrives at the office—a plea for help from someone fleeing across the country in a stolen automobile. What begins as a simple mystery deepens into a labyrinth of double-crosses, desperate men, and the question of whether justice can catch up with a fugitive who may—or may not—deserve his fate. The rhythmic percussion of tires on pavement mingles with staccato dialogue and the ever-present threat lurking just beyond the next mile marker. As Dan pursues the truth, listeners are whisked along dangerous highways where trust is a liability and the road ahead conceals both salvation and ruin.

Box 13, the syndicated gem that captivated audiences from 1948 to 1949, represented the golden age of radio adventure at its finest. The show's premise—Dan Hallowell, a newspaper man who solves mysteries triggered by messages left in a mysterious mailbox—gave each episode complete narrative freedom while maintaining an overarching sense of mystery and intrigue. With only sixty-five episodes produced, Box 13 became a cult favorite among radio enthusiasts, remembered for its sophisticated storytelling and the chemistry between the cast members who brought Dan's world vividly to life.

Don't miss "Speed to Burn"—a masterclass in suspense that will keep you guessing until the final fade-out. Adjust your dial and prepare for an evening of genuine mystery and thrills. Box 13 awaits.
