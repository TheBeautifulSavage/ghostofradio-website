Detective Danny Halloran hits the pavement on a cold New York morning, his fedora pulled low against the March wind cutting through the canyons of Broadway. When society figure Andrew Jenkins turns up dead in his swanky Manhattan apartment—the victim of a carefully staged suicide that smells decidedly like murder—Halloran finds himself navigating a treacherous web of theater world intrigue, blackmail, and secrets that reach into the highest echelons of New York society. As he interviews nervous actors, intimidated servants, and evasive socialites, the detective pieces together a puzzle where every suspect has motive, means, and a practiced smile. The tension mounts with each scene, each revelation, each contradiction that brings Halloran closer to the truth—and closer to danger.

Broadway Is My Beat captured something essential about post-war New York that resonated deeply with 1949 audiences. Created during the golden age of CBS radio drama, the show grounded listeners in the authentic grit and glamour of Manhattan's theater district, where art and crime intersected nightly. Detective Halloran became the everyman's guide through a world of sophistication and deception, his hard-boiled pragmatism cutting through the pretense that money and status could buy. Each episode offered what radio audiences craved: compelling procedural storytelling, believable character work, and the comforting knowledge that justice—however complicated—would prevail.

Tune in now to follow Detective Halloran as he unravels The Andrew Jenkins Case. With sharp dialogue, authentic New York atmosphere, and a mystery that will keep you guessing until the final commercial break, this is radio drama at its finest.
