# Broadway Is My Beat: "A Fixed Prize Fight"

Step into the smoky underbelly of 1940s Manhattan as Detective Danny Halloran cracks down on the seamier side of the boxing world. When a championship bout at Madison Square Garden becomes the epicenter of a corruption scandal, our hardened NYPD detective must navigate a treacherous maze of crooked promoters, desperate fighters, and mob connections that reach into the highest echelons of the sport. With only his wits, his badge, and an unwavering sense of justice, Halloran pursues the truth through back-alley gyms and high-stakes gambling dens, where one wrong move could mean a one-way ticket to the East River. This March 1949 episode captures the gritty authenticity that made *Broadway Is My Beat* essential listening for crime drama enthusiasts—every interrogation crackles with tension, every revelation peels back another layer of depravity in the glittering, corrupt heart of the city that never sleeps.

Created by Larry Marks and produced by Herb Klynn, *Broadway Is My Beat* stands as one of radio's most authentic urban crime dramas, largely because its writers understood the Broadway they depicted. Unlike more stylized detective shows, Halloran's cases drew inspiration from genuine New York Police Department files and real criminal cases, lending the series an air of documentary realism that audiences craved in the post-war years. The show's success—running five seasons on CBS with star Larry Thor bringing gravelly authenticity to Halloran—reflected America's fascination with big-city crime and the ordinary officers who battled it. Boxing, with its rich tradition of mob involvement and match-fixing scandals, provided perfect material for exploring the show's central theme: corruption lurking beneath the glamorous surface of entertainment.

Don't miss this thrilling installment of *Broadway Is My Beat*. Tune in and experience the golden age of radio crime drama, where every episode promised danger, mystery, and the unyielding determination of a cop who refused to let the streets of New York beat him down.
