# The Jimmy Dorn Murder Case

Picture it: a sweltering July night in Manhattan, the neon signs casting their garish glow across rain-slicked pavement, and somewhere in the theater district, a body has been found. *Broadway Is My Beat* opens with the unmistakable voice of Detective Danny Barr cutting through the urban chaos, his weary authority commanding attention as he steps into yet another case that will expose the underbelly of show business glamour. In "The Jimmy Dorn Murder Case," listeners are plunged into a world of backstage jealousies, blackmail, and backstabbers—where a promising young performer's dreams end in murder, and the truth lies buried beneath layers of lies, motive, and the kind of desperation that only the Great White Way can breed. The crisp sound effects of snapping fingers, slamming doors, and that iconic police station ambiance transport you directly to 1949 New York, where every shadow holds a suspect and every clue unravels another tangled thread.

The show's creator, Vincent Pelletier, crafted *Broadway Is My Beat* as a love letter to New York City itself, treating the metropolis not merely as a setting but as a living, breathing character in every mystery. Broadcasting from CBS's Studio 57 in Manhattan, the production captured something authentically New York—the sharp-tongued dialogue, the rapid-fire revelations, and the moral complexity that only a city of millions could generate. This premiere episode exemplifies everything that made the series a favorite among radio enthusiasts: tight storytelling, genuine character work, and the intoxicating atmosphere of theatrical corruption.

If you've never experienced the electric thrill of old-time radio drama, or if you're a devoted collector seeking out rare early broadcasts, "The Jimmy Dorn Murder Case" deserves a place in your collection. Tune in and let Detective Barr guide you through the glittering, dangerous world of Broadway—where talent meets treachery, and justice must navigate the most complicated stage of all.
