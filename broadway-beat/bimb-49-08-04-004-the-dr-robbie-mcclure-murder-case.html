# The Dr Robbie McClure Murder Case

Step into the neon-soaked streets of Manhattan on a sweltering August evening in 1949, where Broadway's glittering marquees cast long shadows over secrets far darker than any stage production. Detective Danny Barton must unravel the puzzling death of Dr. Robbie McClure, a man who seemed to have everything—wealth, prestige, a thriving medical practice—yet somehow found himself at the wrong end of a killer's designs. As our hard-boiled investigator works the case from the glamorous hotel suites of Times Square to the murky back alleys behind the theaters, listeners will experience the crackling tension of a metropolis where fame and fortune can vanish as quickly as a curtain call. The clues lead everywhere and nowhere; the suspects multiply with each interrogation; and the clock ticks relentlessly toward a revelation that will shock even the most seasoned crime drama enthusiast.

*Broadway Is My Beat* arrived on CBS radio precisely when America's love affair with urban crime stories was reaching its fever pitch. Created by Louis Pelletier and starring Grant Richards as the everyman detective hero, the show captured the authentic voice of postwar New York with remarkable specificity—the real geography, the genuine police procedures, the true atmosphere of a city still adjusting to peace. Unlike the theatrical melodrama of earlier radio crimes, this series grounded itself in procedural realism while maintaining the atmospheric sizzle that made radio the dominant entertainment medium of the era.

Tune in now and experience the golden age of detective radio at its finest—where every shadow conceals a motive, every alibi crumbles under scrutiny, and justice emerges not from certainty, but from Barton's relentless pursuit of truth. The streets of Broadway are calling.
