# Broadway Is My Beat: The Eugene Bullock Murder Case

Step into the neon-soaked streets of Manhattan on a crisp November evening, where Detective Danny Halloran finds himself entangled in a baffling murder case that threatens to unravel the glittering façade of Broadway's elite. When the body of Eugene Bullock—a theater impresario with powerful enemies and dangerous secrets—turns up in a backstage dressing room, Halloran must navigate through a maze of false alibis, scorned actresses, and shadowy underworld figures who all had reason to want Bullock silenced. As the detective peels back the layers of deception, listeners will find themselves suspended in that golden age of radio drama, where every creaking door and distant siren crackles with genuine menace, and the line between Broadway's glittering promise and its dark underbelly becomes inescapably thin.

*Broadway Is My Beat* captured the imagination of postwar America during its five-year run, presenting New York City not as the romantic backdrop of musicals, but as the gritty, morally complex stage where real crimes unfolded against the backdrop of the theater world. This November 1949 episode exemplifies the show's strength—combining hard-boiled detective work with insider knowledge of Broadway's unique culture, where ambition, passion, and desperation created perfect conditions for murder. The show's authenticity came from writer Larry Auerbach's intimate familiarity with both police procedure and theatrical life, making each case feel plausible and urgent.

Don't miss this gripping installment of one of radio's finest crime dramas. Tune in as Detective Halloran confronts the murderer behind the curtain, in *Broadway Is My Beat*.
