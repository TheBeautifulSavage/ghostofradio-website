# Broadway Is My Beat: Nick Norman And Santa Claus

Picture this: Christmas Eve on the Great White Way, 1949. The streets glitter with tinsel and holiday cheer, but underneath the festive veneer lurks something sinister. Detective Nick Norman, CBS's gritty protector of Broadway's darkest corners, finds himself tangled in a case where Saint Nick himself becomes suspect number one. In this yuletide episode, the warmth of the season collides with the cold reality of crime as our hero navigates shadowy stage doors, interrogates suspicious characters, and uncovers a mystery that threatens to shatter the holiday spirit of New York's theatrical district. The tension crackles—will Norman catch his man before Christmas morning arrives?

*Broadway Is My Beat* captured something uniquely American during the post-war years: the noir-drenched glamour of Manhattan's theater world, where dreams and danger danced together under the marquee lights. Created by Len Heaton and starring Ed Begley as the unflinching Detective Nick Norman, the series became CBS's answer to urban crime drama, rivaling even the legendary *Dragnet* for authentic police procedural authenticity. Each episode unfolded like a backstage thriller, with the show's writer consulting actual NYPD detectives to ensure every procedural detail rang true. The series' five-year run solidified its place in radio history as a masterclass in dramatic storytelling, combining the glitz of show business with hard-boiled investigation.

This particular episode—broadcast during the magic and melancholy of December 24th—stands as a perfect example of the show's ability to weave seasonal atmosphere into compelling crime drama. Whether you're a devoted fan of classic radio mysteries or discovering this gem for the first time, *Nick Norman And Santa Claus* promises the perfect crime-solving companion for your holiday listening. Tune in and experience the golden age of broadcast drama.
