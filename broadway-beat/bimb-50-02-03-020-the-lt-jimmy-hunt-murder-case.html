# The Lt Jimmy Hunt Murder Case

Picture the rain-slicked streets of New York's theater district on a cold winter night—the neon signs of Broadway blurred behind sheets of precipitation as Detective Danny Halloran arrives at a scene that will test everything he knows about justice and corruption. In this gripping episode, the murder of Lieutenant Jimmy Hunt, a man with connections that reach into the highest echelons of the NYPD, threatens to unravel the department itself. As Halloran navigates a labyrinth of false leads, political pressure, and witnesses who fear speaking the truth more than they fear death, listeners will find themselves caught in the tightening noose of a mystery where nobody is quite what they seem—not the victim, not the suspects, and certainly not the system meant to protect them.

*Broadway Is My Beat* stands as one of radio's finest crime dramas, capturing the authentic grit of 1949 New York with a procedural realism that predates television's police dramas by years. Detective Danny Halloran, portrayed with world-weary conviction, became the voice of postwar urban America—a man trying to do right in a city where right and wrong had become dangerously blurred. The show's writers mined the real headlines and street-level rumors of Manhattan, creating episodes that felt ripped from the morning papers, while the dense orchestral scores and sound design transported listeners directly into smoky precinct rooms and shadowy alleyways.

Don't miss this essential episode from one of CBS Radio's most acclaimed dramatic series. Tune in and let the unmistakable strains of the show's theme pull you into a murder investigation where the truth is as elusive as a shadow on a foggy night, and every question only leads to darker answers.
