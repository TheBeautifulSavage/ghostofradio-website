# The Dion Hartley Murder Case

When the curtain falls on a Broadway starlet's final performance, Detective Danny Halloran finds himself threading through the glittering underworld of New York's theater district—where jealousy, ambition, and passion collide with deadly consequences. In this gripping episode, the mysterious death of Dion Hartley sends shockwaves through the backstage world of bright lights and darker secrets. With each interview, each contradiction, and each carefully planted clue, listeners are drawn deeper into a web of suspicion where the show must go on, but a killer walks free. The tension crackles through your radio speaker as Halloran peels back the layers of motive and deception, racing against time to unmask a murderer hiding in plain sight among the marquees and speakeasies of Manhattan.

*Broadway Is My Beat* captured the golden age of radio crime drama by anchoring its stories in the authentic grit of post-war New York City. Unlike police procedurals set in anonymous American towns, this CBS series plunged listeners directly into the roaring heart of Broadway itself—a world that felt simultaneously glamorous and dangerous, where stardom and criminality danced together under the same neon signs. Detective Danny Halloran became the audience's guide through this world of theatrical intrigue, his sharp dialogue and dogged determination reflecting the hard-boiled detective fiction that inspired the series during its five-year run from 1949 to 1954.

Don't miss this masterclass in classic radio mystery. Tune in as Danny Halloran investigates *The Dion Hartley Murder Case*—where the bright lights of Broadway cast the darkest shadows. This is crime drama as it was meant to be heard: intimate, immediate, and utterly compelling.
