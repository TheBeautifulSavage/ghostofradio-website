# The Ted Forstek and Ruth Ballard Murder Case

Picture the smoky Manhattan night of June 9th, 1950—the kind of evening when danger lurks behind every theater marquee and backstage door. Detective Danny Barron returns to the glittering chaos of Broadway to untangle the brutal murder of Ted Forstek and Ruth Ballard, two names that will soon become synonymous with the kind of sordid crime that makes headlines scream and audiences gasp. In this episode, listeners will navigate the dim corridors of Broadway's underbelly, where showbiz glamour masks ruthless ambition and betrayal. The tension crackles through every commercial break as clues emerge from the mouths of chorus girls, stage hands, and shady characters operating in the shadows between Fifth and Eighth Avenues. With each revelation, the web tightens, and listeners find themselves locked in a mystery as intricate as the city itself.

*Broadway Is My Beat* arrived on CBS radio at precisely the right moment—when post-war America craved gritty, urban storytelling delivered with the sophistication of live performance. The show thrived on authenticity, with writer-producer Vincent Pelletier crafting scripts that captured the real vernacular of New York's criminal underworld and its intersection with the theater world. Unlike sanitized mysteries, these episodes embraced the moral complexity of a city where corruption was woven into the very fabric of success. The show became essential listening for those wanting their crime drama served New York-style: sharp, cynical, and utterly uncompromising.

Don't let this case go unsolved. Tune in to hear Detective Barron work his magic through the jungle of Broadway's dark side, where every witness has a motive and every performance conceals a secret. The truth about Ted Forstek and Ruth Ballard awaits.
