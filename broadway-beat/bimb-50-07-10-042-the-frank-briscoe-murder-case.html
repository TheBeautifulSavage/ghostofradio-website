Bimb 50 07 10 (042) The Frank Briscoe Murder Case

Step into the neon-soaked streets of Manhattan as Detective Danny Halloran takes on one of Broadway's most baffling homicides. The Frank Briscoe Murder Case opens with the body of a well-connected theatrical producer sprawled across the marble floor of an exclusive nightclub, his killer vanished like smoke into the city's shadowy underworld. What unfolds is a masterclass in mid-century crime detection—a taut, breathless investigation that winds through backstage dressing rooms, swanky speakeasy lounges, and the grimy tenement apartments where Broadway's forgotten chorus girls tell their desperate stories. As Halloran peels back layers of motive and alibi, listeners are drawn deeper into a web of jealousy, blackmail, and revenge where everyone on the Great White Way has something to hide. The crackling dialogue and vivid sound design transport you to a New York that exists only in the amber glow of this golden age medium.

Broadway Is My Beat stands as one of CBS Radio's defining achievements—a show that captured the authentic rhythm and danger of postwar New York with unprecedented grit and sophistication. Created for adult audiences hungry for intelligent crime drama, the series paired hardboiled scripts with the incomparable performance of veteran actor Craig Stevens as Halloran, a detective who moved through Broadway's world with the weariness of a man who'd seen too much and the wisdom of one who understood that the theater was just another stage for human nature's darkest impulses. Between 1949 and 1954, episodes like "The Frank Briscoe Murder Case" became cultural touchstones, proving radio drama could rival any medium for suspense and artistry.

Tune in now and discover why critics called Broadway Is My Beat the finest crime drama ever broadcast. Let the city's midnight bells and the distant cry of a lonely saxophone pull you back to an era when radio ruled the night.
