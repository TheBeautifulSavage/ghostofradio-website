The neon-soaked streets of Manhattan come alive once more as Detective Danny Barr finds himself entangled in a case that cuts to the heart of Broadway's glittering underbelly. When the body of Amelia Ramirez, a chorus girl with dangerous connections, turns up in a darkened theater alley, Barr must navigate a treacherous web of jealousy, blackmail, and betrayal that winds through the neighborhood's jazz clubs, backstage dressing rooms, and dimly lit hotel lobbies. With only cryptic clues and the contradicting testimonies of performers, stagehands, and small-time crooks, the detective works methodically through the night, his fedora-brimmed silhouette becoming as much a fixture of the Manhattan landscape as the Empire State Building itself. Every shadow hides a suspect; every alibi conceals a secret.

Broadway Is My Beat* stands as one of the finest achievements in radio crime drama, capturing the authentic pulse of New York's theater district during the golden age of entertainment. Starring Edmond O'Brien as the unflappable Detective Barr, the series distinguished itself through meticulous attention to Broadway vernacular and the real corruption that lurked beneath the footlights. Rather than relying on sensationalism, the show built its reputation on smart writing, authentic police procedure, and characters who felt like genuine New Yorkers. Each episode peels back another layer of the city's complex social fabric, where talent, luck, and desperation collide nightly.

Tune in now and step back into the smoky theaters and dangerous streets of 1940s New York. *Broadway Is My Beat* awaits—where every case tells the story of the city that never sleeps, and justice operates on its own schedule.
