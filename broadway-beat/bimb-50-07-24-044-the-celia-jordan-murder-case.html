# The Celia Jordan Murder Case

Step into the neon-soaked streets of Manhattan as Detective Danny Halloran tackles one of his most perplexing cases yet. When a beautiful nightclub singer named Celia Jordan is found dead in her dressing room, the investigation spirals through backstage intrigues, jealous rivals, and the kind of double-crosses that make Broadway notorious. With only cigarette smoke and shadows to guide him, Halloran must navigate the glittering underworld of theatrical dressing rooms and jazz joints, where everyone has secrets and nobody tells the truth. The tension crackles through every line of dialogue as witnesses contradict each other, clues lead nowhere, and the killer remains hidden in plain sight among the Broadway elite.

*Broadway Is My Beat* captured something uniquely American during its run from 1949 to 1954—the gritty, glamorous pulse of post-war New York City filtered through the voice and determination of its protagonist. Detective Halloran became an icon of the golden age of radio drama, embodying the street-smart cop who understood that the glitziest venues in America could hide the darkest crimes. The show's writers drew inspiration from real New York cases while crafting a world where high society and criminal underworld collided nightly. This particular episode, broadcast in 1950, represents the series at its peak, blending authentic police procedure with the theatrical melodrama that made listeners lean closer to their sets night after night.

Don't miss "The Celia Jordan Murder Case"—a masterclass in radio mystery storytelling where every shadow hides a suspect and the truth waits in the wings. Tune in and discover why *Broadway Is My Beat* remains the gold standard of crime drama radio.
