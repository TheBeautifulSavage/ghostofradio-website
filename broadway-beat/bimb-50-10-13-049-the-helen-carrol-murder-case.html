# The Helen Carrol Murder Case

Picture yourself in a Manhattan penthouse on a fog-shrouded October evening in 1950, where the glittering facade of Broadway's elite conceals a darker truth. Detective Danny Halloran steps into the Helen Carrol murder case—a seemingly open-and-shut matter of passion and betrayal that unravels into something far more sinister. As cigarette smoke curls through dimly lit nightclubs and rain hammers against rain-slicked streets, Halloran peels back layers of motive, contradiction, and carefully constructed alibis. The victim's life was intricately woven into the fabric of show business itself, and everyone from stagehands to society figures becomes a suspect. With each revelation, the case grows more complex, more personal, and more urgent—can Halloran separate truth from theater before the killer strikes again?

*Broadway Is My Beat* captured the golden age of New York crime drama with an authenticity that resonated deeply with post-war radio audiences. Created by the legendary production team that knew Broadway's streets intimately, the show aired during a time when real crimes regularly made headlines and New Yorkers hung on every detail. The series was groundbreaking for its gritty realism and nuanced characterization—Halloran wasn't a caricature, but a weary, intelligent detective navigating a city of eight million stories. By 1950, when this episode aired, the show had perfected the formula: metropolitan atmosphere, methodical police work, and human drama that transcended simple whodunit conventions.

So tune your dial to this classic broadcast and join Detective Halloran as he navigates the treacherous intersection of art, ambition, and murder on the Great White Way. The Helen Carrol case awaits—and in the world of *Broadway Is My Beat*, nothing is ever quite what it seems.
