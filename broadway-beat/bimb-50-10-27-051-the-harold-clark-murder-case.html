# The Harold Clark Murder Case

Picture yourself settled into your favorite chair on a crisp October evening in 1950, the amber glow of your radio dial casting dancing shadows across the room as Detective Danny Halloran steps onto the rain-slicked streets of Broadway. In "The Harold Clark Murder Case," our hard-boiled investigator finds himself ensnared in a web of deception that reaches from the glittering marquees of the Theater District into the shadowy corners of Manhattan's underworld. When a prominent theatrical figure turns up dead, Halloran must navigate a treacherous landscape of false alibis, bitter rivalries, and dangerous secrets—all while the clock ticks relentlessly toward a killer who walks freely among the bright lights and dark intentions of the Great White Way. The snap of dialogue crackles with authentic New York grit, the foley artists' feet pounding pavement and car doors slamming punctuating every twist of the investigation.

*Broadway Is My Beat* captured the post-war American imagination by bringing the electricity and danger of New York's theatrical world directly into listeners' living rooms. Running from 1949 to 1954 on CBS, the show became celebrated for its unflinching depiction of urban crime and its meticulously researched scripts that reflected the genuine perils lurking beneath Broadway's glittering facade. This particular episode exemplifies the series' strength—combining police procedural authenticity with the soap-opera intrigue that made theater life endlessly fascinating to audiences across the country.

Don't miss this gripping chapter in Detective Halloran's casebook. Whether you're a devoted fan of the series or a newcomer to classic radio drama, "The Harold Clark Murder Case" promises the perfect blend of mystery, atmosphere, and that unmistakable 1950s sensibility that made Broadway Is My Beat an essential appointment for radio listeners everywhere.
