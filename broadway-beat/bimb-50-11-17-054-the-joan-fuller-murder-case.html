# The Joan Fuller Murder Case

Step into the rain-slicked streets of Manhattan as Detective Danny Halloran pursues one of his most perplexing cases yet. When the body of Joan Fuller, a chorus girl with connections to Broadway's seediest corners, is discovered in a shabby hotel room off Times Square, Halloran must navigate a labyrinth of false alibis, jealous rivals, and dangerous secrets. The usual suspects materialize quickly—the spurned lover, the vengeful stage manager, the mysterious out-of-town visitor—but nothing quite adds up. As our detective digs deeper into Joan's final hours, the case spirals into unexpected territory, touching on blackmail, betrayal, and the dark underbelly of show business that the bright stage lights work so hard to conceal. Expect the signature tension that makes Broadway Is My Beat essential listening: sharp dialogue crackles with period authenticity, while the atmospheric sound design of crowded theaters, police precincts, and dimly lit nightclubs transports you directly to 1949 New York.

For five seasons, Broadway Is My Beat captured something vital about postwar urban crime drama—it wasn't the gritty realism of later television, but rather a heightened, theatrical approach to murder and detection that mirrored the world it depicted. Star William Gargan's portrayal of Halloran became iconic among radio listeners, embodying the smart, world-weary detective who understood that in Manhattan, appearance often masks terrible truths. This particular episode, recorded in November 1950, exemplifies the show's mastery of the procedural mystery format while maintaining the romantic, noir-tinged atmosphere that made the series legendary.

Don't miss this fascinating window into classic American radio drama. Tune in and let yourself be captivated by the mystery of Joan Fuller—a case that will keep you guessing until the very final moments.
