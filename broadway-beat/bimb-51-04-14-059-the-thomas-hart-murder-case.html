# The Thomas Hart Murder Case

Detective Danny Fortunato steps into the Manhattan night where the neon signs blur through rain-slicked streets, and a prominent businessman lies dead in his elegant penthouse—a locked-room mystery that will lead our hardboiled detective through the smoky back rooms of Broadway nightclubs and the gleaming corridors of high society. In "The Thomas Hart Murder Case," the line between glamour and corruption dissolves as Danny uncovers a web of blackmail, betrayal, and dangerous secrets that someone was willing to kill to protect. With only a cryptic note and conflicting testimony from the city's most deceptive residents, Danny must navigate the treacherous underworld that exists in the shadows of Times Square, where every witness has an alibi and every suspect has a motive. The clock ticks as he races to solve the crime before the murderer strikes again.

*Broadway Is My Beat* captured something authentic about post-war New York—that electric, dangerous pulse of a city still reinventing itself in the late 1940s. The show's creator, Morton Fine, grounded his crime stories in the real geography and genuine moral ambiguity of mid-century Manhattan, making Danny Fortunato not a glamorous hero, but a weary, persistent cop who understands that in New York, nobody's hands stay clean for long. Each episode was a expertly crafted mystery that respected listeners' intelligence while delivering genuine suspense and atmosphere, anchored by the naturalistic performances that made CBS's drama programming legendary.

Don't miss this masterpiece of old-time radio crime drama. Tune in to experience the sights, sounds, and shadows of Broadway's underbelly, where one detective searches for truth in a city built on lies.
