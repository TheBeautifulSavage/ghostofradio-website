# The Frank Dunn Murder Case

Picture this: a rain-soaked Manhattan street corner, a body cooling on the pavement, and Detective Danny Halloran walking into the kind of case that makes a cop's stomach turn. In this compelling episode of *Broadway Is My Beat*, our hardened NYPD detective finds himself tangled in the murder of Frank Dunn, a small-time operator whose connections run deeper than anyone suspected. The case pulls Halloran through dimly-lit hotel rooms, interrogation chambers thick with cigarette smoke, and the shadowy underbelly of Times Square itself. With only fragments of testimony and a ticking clock, Halloran must separate truth from deception in a world where everyone's got an angle and nobody talks straight. This is classic New York noir—the kind of tight, tension-filled drama that made audiences lean closer to their radio sets each week.

*Broadway Is My Beat* was one of CBS's crown jewels of crime entertainment, running from 1949 to 1954 with Detective Danny Halloran as the beating heart of the series. What set the show apart was its gritty authenticity; creator and writer Herb Philbrook drew directly from real NYPD case files, lending the series a documentary-like credibility that listeners craved in those post-war years. The June 16th broadcast falls right in the show's golden period, when the writing was sharp, the sound design immersive, and William Gargan's portrayal of Halloran had reached its naturalistic peak. Each episode captured the exhausted determination of a detective trying to maintain order in a city that never sleeps.

Don your fedora and step into the rain-soaked streets of 1950s New York. *The Frank Dunn Murder Case* awaits—where justice moves at the speed of a detective's footsteps on cold pavement.
