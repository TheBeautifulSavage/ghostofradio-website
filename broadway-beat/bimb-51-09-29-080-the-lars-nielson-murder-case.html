Bimb 51 09 29 (080) The Lars Nielson Murder Case

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

Detective Danny Halloran's shoes echo against rain-slicked pavement as he navigates the shadowy intersection of Broadway glamour and Manhattan violence. When the body of Lars Nielson—a Swedish stage electrician—is discovered backstage at a glittering theater, the case threatens to expose corruption that reaches from the dressing rooms to the penthouse suites. Halloran must unravel a tangled web of jealousy, stolen spotlight, and desperation, where every witness has something to hide and the bright lights of the Great White Way cast equally dark shadows. With his cigarette-rough voice and street-smart intuition, our detective presses deeper into a world where ambition can turn deadly and theater rivalries become murder plots.

Broadway Is My Beat captured the essence of late 1940s New York crime drama with gritty authenticity and cinematic storytelling. The show's creator, Morton Fine, grounded his scripts in the real geography and psychology of Manhattan, making every episode feel like an insider's glimpse into the city's beating heart. This September 1949 episode typifies the series' strength—weaving the theatrical world's glamour with hard-boiled detective work, where the mystery is never simple and human nature is endlessly complex. CBS's commitment to the series demonstrated radio's continued appeal even as television loomed on the horizon, and Broadway Is My Beat stands as one of the medium's finest crime dramas.

Tune in to hear Detective Halloran crack the case wide open. Who killed Lars Nielson? What secrets lie buried beneath the footlights? The answer awaits in the electric darkness of Broadway.
