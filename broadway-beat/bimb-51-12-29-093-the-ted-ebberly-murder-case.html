Detective Danny Clover plunges into the glittering underworld of 1940s Broadway where champagne flows as freely as blood spills in the shadows. When Ted Ebberly, a rising stage star with connections to the city's most dangerous players, is found dead in his dressing room, Clover must navigate a labyrinth of jealous rivals, spurned lovers, and theatrical phonies who all had reason to want him silenced. The taut script crackles with the rapid-fire dialogue and cynical wisdom that made this series a weekly ritual for millions, as Clover's gravelly voice cuts through the smoky atmosphere of nightclubs, rehearsal halls, and police precincts to expose the sordid truth beneath the Broadway marquees.

Broadway Is My Beat captured the imagination of postwar America by bringing the gritty realism of New York crime procedurals into living rooms across the nation. Running from 1949 to 1954 on CBS, the show pioneered a style that would later influence countless television dramas, grounding its storytelling in authentic police work while never losing sight of Broadway's unique allure as a world of ambition, talent, and moral compromise. Each episode pulled listeners deeper into Danny Clover's world, where the footlights concealed as many secrets as they illuminated. The Ebberly case exemplifies the show's genius for weaving together elements of showbiz glamour with the cold machinery of criminal investigation, creating something that felt both exotic and urgently real.

Join Detective Clover as he untangles the twisted motives behind Ted Ebberly's murder, where every suspect has a soliloquy prepared and the truth plays to a packed house. Tune in to experience radio drama at its finest—where Broadway truly is the beat.
