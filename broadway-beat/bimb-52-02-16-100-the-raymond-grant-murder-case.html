# The Raymond Grant Murder Case

Detective Danny Barr hits the pavement on a frigid Manhattan night in pursuit of justice—and a killer who's left nothing but questions in his wake. When wealthy socialite Raymond Grant turns up dead in his Park Avenue penthouse, the case bears all the hallmarks of a crime of passion: a locked room, a missing diamond pin, and a trail of suspicious characters whose motives are as murky as the smoke-filled jazz clubs of Times Square. As Barr conducts his methodical investigation through the glittering yet sinister underbelly of New York's theater district, listeners will find themselves drawn into a web of blackmail, infidelity, and long-buried secrets. The writing crackles with authenticity—you can almost hear the clip-clop of taxi horns echoing through the Manhattan streets and smell the whiskey-soaked breath of informants in dimly lit back rooms.

*Broadway Is My Beat* represents the golden age of crime radio drama, a show that captured the pulse of postwar New York with unflinching realism and moral complexity. Airing live from CBS's New York studios, the program was anchored by the gravelly voice and keen intelligence of star Barrett Robbins as Detective Danny Barr, a cop who understood that the city's glittering surface concealed endless darkness. Each episode was a love letter to the noir-drenched streets that viewers could only imagine, making radio the perfect medium for tales of urban crime and existential struggle. The show's creators drew inspiration from genuine police files and the real geography of Manhattan, lending the stories an air of authenticity that kept audiences riveted.

Don't miss this classic episode of *Broadway Is My Beat*. Tune in as Detective Danny Barr unravels the Raymond Grant murder case, where every suspect has something to hide and the truth lies buried beneath layers of deception and desire. It's crime drama at its finest—visceral, compelling, and absolutely unmissable.
