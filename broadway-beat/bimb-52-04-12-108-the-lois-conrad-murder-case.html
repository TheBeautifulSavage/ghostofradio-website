Bimb 52 04 12 (108) The Lois Conrad Murder Case

When Broadway's glittering marquees cast their neon shadows on the pavement below, Detective Danny Halloran knows that murder doesn't discriminate between the famous and the forgotten. In this taut installment, a beautiful actress named Lois Conrad lies dead in her apartment, and the detective must navigate the tangled web of ambition, jealousy, and secrets that defines the theater world. As Halloran pieces together conflicting testimonies from stagehands, fellow performers, and society figures, listeners will find themselves drawn into a Manhattan night thick with suspicion and danger. The score builds with each revelation, the city itself becoming a character—restless, unforgiving, and utterly captivating. This is crime drama at its finest: intimate yet expansive, gritty yet sophisticated.

Broadway Is My Beat captured something essential about post-war American consciousness during its five-year run on CBS. Created in 1949, just as the Golden Age of radio was reaching its twilight, the show presented New York City not as the sanitized backdrop of musicals, but as a genuine urban jungle where theaters stood alongside speakeasies and murder investigations. Detective Halloran, played with world-weary authenticity by Anthony Caruso, became the listener's trustworthy guide through a world most Americans knew only through cinema and imagination. Each episode crackled with authentic New York atmosphere while exploring the moral complexities that fascinated the post-war audience.

Tune in to experience the sights, sounds, and sinister undertones of Broadway at its darkest hour. Follow Detective Halloran as he closes in on the truth about Lois Conrad's fate, and discover why Broadway Is My Beat remains essential listening for anyone who loves classic crime radio.
