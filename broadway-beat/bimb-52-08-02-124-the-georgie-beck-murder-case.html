# The Georgie Beck Murder Case

Picture this: it's a sultry August night in 1952, and the neon-soaked streets of Broadway are about to yield their darkest secrets. Detective Danny Halloran, that world-weary gumshoe with a heart as hard as the pavement he walks, finds himself entangled in the twisted case of Georgie Beck's murder—a case that will pull him through smoky jazz clubs, dingy hotel rooms, and into the dangerous underworld where Broadway glamour collides with raw criminal desperation. As the case unfolds across thirty thrilling minutes, you'll hear the clatter of typewriters in police precincts, the whispered confessions of witnesses, and the constant tension of a detective racing against time to separate truth from the lies that line these famous streets. This episode crackles with the authentic grit of post-war Manhattan crime drama, where every suspect has motive, means, and a carefully constructed alibi ready to crumble under interrogation.

*Broadway Is My Beat* stands as one of radio's most authentic crime dramas, eschewing melodrama for the procedural realism that made it a favorite among police departments and crime writers alike. Created by writer Mark Hellinger's vision of New York as a character itself, the show ran from 1949 to 1954 on CBS, becoming essential listening for anyone who wanted to understand the real machinery of metropolitan detective work. The writing is sharp and economical, wasting no words, and the performances—particularly that of Ed Begley as Danny Halloran—bring a weary competence that feels lived-in and genuine, transporting listeners directly into the detective's battered office and onto the rain-slicked avenues of the city.

Don your fedora and step into the neon-lit darkness of the city that never sleeps. Tune in to "The Georgie Beck Murder Case" and discover why this program earned its reputation as radio's most gripping portrait of urban crime and detection.
