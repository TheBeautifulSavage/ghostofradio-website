Bimb 52 10 04 (133) The Marty Connell Murder Case

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

The neon glow of Times Square dims to shadow as Detective Danny Halloran faces his most twisted case yet—the brutal murder of small-time hustler Marty Connell, a man who knew too many secrets in a city built on them. When the body turns up in a midtown hotel room, the investigation spirals through Broadway's glittering underworld: the chorus girls and doormen, the crooked promoters and two-bit con artists who populate the Great White Way after dark. As Halloran peels back the layers of Connell's double life, he discovers that in the theater district, everyone has motive, everyone has means, and the truth is always more dangerous than the crime. This episode crackles with the authenticity of a New York detective truly beaten down by the job—exhausted, cynical, but never quite able to abandon his search for justice.

Broadway Is My Beat captured something essential about postwar America that few programs could match: the gritty reality of urban crime drama told by someone who understood the streets. Created by and starring future Gunsmoke legend James Arness as Halloran, the series brought hard-boiled detective fiction to radio with a documentary-like precision that made listeners feel they were riding along with an actual NYPD man. Each episode, drawn from real cases and New York police files, presented the unglamorous truth behind the Broadway marquees—the petty criminals, the corrupted cops, the ordinary people pushed into extraordinary circumstances. It was crime drama stripped of sentimentality, set against the backdrop of a city that never slept.

Tune in now to experience radio drama at its finest, when the microphone could transport you into the rain-slicked streets of 1950s Manhattan, where murder is just another part of doing business on Broadway.
