# The Mary Trevor Murder Case

Picture yourself in a Manhattan penthouse as autumn rain hammers against the windows, the streets of Broadway glittering with neon forty stories below. Detective Danny Halloran, NYPD, stands over the body of Mary Trevor—a rising starlet found sprawled across her dressing room floor, her golden future snuffed out in an instant. The case that unfolds is a tangled web of backstage jealousy, midnight rendezvous, and secrets that powerful people would kill to keep buried. As Halloran navigates the glittering yet treacherous world of theater people and their dangerous entanglements, listeners will find themselves caught in the noir-soaked mystery of who wanted Mary Trevor dead—and more importantly, why they were willing to risk everything to silence her forever.

*Broadway Is My Beat* arrived in 1949 when radio's golden age was in its final, brilliant chapter. Created by Irving Vendig and starring veteran actor Larry Thor as the dogged Detective Halloran, the show became CBS's answer to the crime drama craze that had captivated American audiences for nearly a decade. What set it apart was its unflinching New York City setting—not the stylized fantasy of Hollywood productions, but the gritty, authentic world of real Manhattan streets, real theaters, and real crimes. Each episode crackled with the electricity of Broadway itself, where art and criminality danced an uneasy tango. The show ran for five successful seasons, attracting listeners who craved both the glamour and grit that only New York could provide.

Don your overcoat, step into the shadows, and join Detective Halloran as he unravels the mystery of the Mary Trevor murder. Your local radio station awaits—tune in tonight.
