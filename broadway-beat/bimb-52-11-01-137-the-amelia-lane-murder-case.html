# The Amelia Lane Murder Case

Picture yourself in a dimly lit Manhattan apartment on a fog-thick November evening, the city's neon glow bleeding through venetian blinds as Detective Danny Clover arrives at a crime scene that will pull him into the depths of Broadway's most dangerous underworld. *Broadway Is My Beat* delivers one of its most gripping cases yet with "The Amelia Lane Murder Case"—a labyrinthine mystery involving a glamorous actress, a web of jealousies and secrets, and a killer prowling the theatre district's glittering facade. As Clover methodically pieces together alibis and motives, listeners will be drawn deeper into the shadowy world behind the marquee lights, where ambition curdles into obsession and passion becomes lethal. The crackling dialogue and pulse-quickening sound design—the screech of car tires, the hollow echo of footsteps in an empty dressing room—create an atmosphere thick with danger and moral ambiguity.

*Broadway Is My Beat* stands as one of radio's most authentic crime dramas, created by and starring Vincent Price as the shrewd, cynical Detective Clover. The show's strength lies in its unflinching portrayal of New York during the late 1940s—a city where theatrical glamour masks corruption, where the streets of Times Square breed violence, and where detectives like Clover must navigate both high society and criminal networks. Rather than relying on sensationalism, the series grounds its stories in procedural realism and psychological depth, earning its reputation as essential radio drama of the Golden Age.

Don't miss this taut, engrossing episode. Tune in and let the rain-slicked streets of Broadway pull you into a mystery that still captivates listeners decades after its original broadcast. *Broadway Is My Beat* awaits.
