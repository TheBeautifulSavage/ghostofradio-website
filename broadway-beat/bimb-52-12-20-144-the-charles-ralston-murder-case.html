# The Charles Ralston Murder Case

When Detective Danny Halloran steps onto the rain-slicked streets of Broadway on this December evening in 1952, he's about to untangle one of the most bewildering cases of his career. The murder of Charles Ralston—a man with secrets buried deeper than the subway tunnels beneath Times Square—pulls our intrepid detective through the glittering theaters, seedy nightclubs, and shadowy backstage corridors where ambition and desperation collide with deadly consequences. With only cigarette smoke and sharp questioning as his tools, Halloran must navigate a web of theatrical intrigue, blackmail, and passion to unmask a killer hiding in plain sight. Listen as the city itself becomes a character in this taut drama, where every clue is a crucial thread and every suspect harbors a motive.

*Broadway Is My Beat* emerged during radio's golden age when listeners craved the gritty realism of urban crime stories, and this show delivered authenticity with every broadcast. Created by and starring Art Carney as the methodical Detective Halloran, the series captured New York's theatrical world with remarkable specificity—drawing from actual police procedures and the genuine color of Broadway's underworld. These weren't farfetched melodramas but rather intelligent mysteries rooted in the real struggles of 1950s Manhattan, where showbiz glamour masked corruption, vice, and murder. The show became a CBS flagship program precisely because it understood that the greatest drama wasn't invented—it was waiting on every street corner.

Don't miss this gripping installment of *Broadway Is My Beat*. Tune in now to hear Danny Halloran pursue justice through the neon-lit nights of New York City, where nothing is ever quite what it seems and the truth lies waiting in the shadows.
