Step into the neon-soaked streets of Manhattan on a cold March evening in 1953, where Detective Danny Barton finds himself entangled in a case that cuts straight to the heart of Broadway's glittering façade. When prominent theater impresario John Stewart is found dead under circumstances far murkier than they first appear, Barton must navigate the shadowy world of backstage intrigue, ambitious starlets, and powerful men who prefer their secrets kept in the dark. This episode crackles with the kind of urban tension that defined the show—the snap of smart dialogue, the underlying menace of a city that never sleeps, and the relentless pressure of a detective determined to uncover the truth beneath the spotlight and scandal.

Broadway Is My Beat remains one of the era's most distinctive crime dramas, capturing post-war New York with an authenticity that few shows could match. Running from 1949 to 1954 on CBS, the series starred character actor Larry Thor as the dogged, world-weary Detective Barton, and its scripts crackled with the kind of procedural realism that would later influence television's golden age. Rather than Hollywood glamour, the show grounded its mysteries in the actual Broadway theatrical world—the ambitious producers, the struggling performers, the mob connections that lurked beneath the marquee lights. Each episode delivered not just a puzzle to solve, but a vivid slice of Manhattan street life.

Whether you're a devoted fan of classic detective radio or discovering this gem for the first time, "The John Stewart Murder Case" exemplifies everything the show did best: compelling mystery, atmospheric storytelling, and the gritty authenticity of a great American city. Tune in and let Detective Barton guide you through the case—you won't find the answers where you expect them.
