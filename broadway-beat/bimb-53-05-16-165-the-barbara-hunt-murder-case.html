# The Barbara Hunt Murder Case

Detective Danny Halloran walks the rain-slicked streets of Manhattan once more, his fedora pulled low against the May night as the neon signs of Broadway blur in the wet pavement. A woman lies dead—Barbara Hunt, a chorus girl with secrets darker than the alley where she was found—and Halloran must navigate the glittering theaters, smoky nightclubs, and shadowy underworld of Times Square to uncover the truth. As the city's jazz trumpets wail in the background and suspects emerge from the fog of theater marquees and backstage dressing rooms, listeners will be drawn into a mystery where every witness has motive, opportunity, and something to hide. This is Broadway at its most dangerous—glamorous on the surface, rotten underneath.

*Broadway Is My Beat* captured the essence of post-war New York like few shows could, bringing the authentic grit and glamour of the Great White Way directly into living rooms across America. Starring veteran actor Larry Thor as the world-weary but determined Halloran, the show aired during the golden age of radio drama when listeners craved stories rooted in real urban geography and contemporary crime. The series' genius lay in its specificity: real Broadway locations, genuine theatrical culture, and the moral ambiguity of a city where show business and criminal enterprise occupied uncomfortably close quarters. Each episode became a love letter to New York itself—dangerous, exciting, and endlessly fascinating.

Don't miss this classic installment of detective radio drama. Join Detective Halloran as he unravels the Barbara Hunt murder case and discovers that on Broadway, the brightest lights can hide the darkest secrets. Tune in now to experience the crackling tension, sophisticated dialogue, and atmospheric storytelling that made *Broadway Is My Beat* an essential part of American radio history.
