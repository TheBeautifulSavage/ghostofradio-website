# The Ted Lawrence Murder Case

Detective Danny Halloran steps into the neon-soaked Manhattan night, collar turned against the chill, the city's pulse throbbing beneath his feet as another body turns cold on the pavement. *The Ted Lawrence Murder Case* plunges listeners directly into the grimy underbelly of postwar Broadway, where showbiz glamour masks a den of jealousy, betrayal, and blood. As our hard-boiled protagonist unravels the tangled web of clues surrounding Lawrence's death, you'll hear the clash of Broadway sophistication against street-level brutality—the jazz saxophone wails in smoke-filled clubs, the bark of angry voices in dingy back rooms, and always, always the ticking clock of justice working against time. This episode crackles with the authentic tension that made *Broadway Is My Beat* a Thursday night essential for crime drama devotees across America.

What distinguished this series from countless other noir mysteries was its obsessive authenticity. Created by and featuring Vincent Price as the sardonic Detective Halloran, the show benefited from real NYPD consultation and meticulous attention to Broadway's actual geography and culture. Each episode transplanted listeners directly into New York's theater district—that intoxicating blend of art, commerce, and crime that defined the late 1940s entertainment world. By 1949, when the show debuted on CBS, postwar America craved sophisticated urban storytelling, and *Broadway Is My Beat* delivered exactly that: procedural realism wrapped in the glamorous darkness of New York at night.

Don't miss your chance to experience this extraordinary moment in radio history. Tune in to *The Ted Lawrence Murder Case* and let the sights, sounds, and shadows of old Broadway transport you back to a golden age of dramatic storytelling—when mystery and danger lurked behind every velvet curtain and the city itself became a character in every crime.
