It's 1939, and America's appetite for the fantastic knows no bounds. Buck Rogers has become the gold standard of science fiction radio, captivating millions of listeners who gather around their Philco and RCA sets each week. This episode, broadcast during the height of pre-war optimism, showcases the show's remarkable technical innovation—sound engineer Al Brackett's ingenious use of theremin tones and rubber sheets create effects that seem genuinely otherworldly. The writing crackles with contemporary anxieties about scientific progress, nuclear potential, and the moral responsibilities of brilliant minds.
