# Captain Midnight: Episode 1 (September 30, 1940)

Step into the cockpit of adventure as Captain Midnight roars to life in this thrilling premiere broadcast! On this autumn evening in 1940, listeners across America tuned their dials to NBC expecting entertainment—but what they discovered was pure, high-octane excitement. Captain Midnight, the heroic aviator and engineer, springs into action when enemy agents threaten to steal a revolutionary new aircraft design. With nothing but his wits, his loyal sidekick Sergeant, and the purr of a powerful engine, he must outmaneuver saboteurs and international spies across rooftops, airfields, and under cover of darkness. The crackling sound effects of revving propellers and screeching tires pulse through your speaker, while the urgent orchestral score builds as danger closes in. This is radio drama at its most electric—where imagination and sound create a world of unbounded possibility.

Captain Midnight arrived at precisely the moment America needed him. With Europe engulfed in war and American anxieties about industrial espionage running high, the show captured the zeitgeist of a nation preparing for an uncertain future. The character himself—a mysterious airman operating in the shadows of night—embodied the resourceful American hero: intelligent, moral, and utterly fearless. What made the series exceptional was its grounding in actual aviation technology and aeronautical knowledge, lending credibility that younger listeners found intoxicating and older audiences found reassuring.

Don't miss this historic debut that launched a nine-year phenomenon. Captain Midnight would become one of radio's most beloved series, spawning comic books, serials, and a devoted national audience of aviation enthusiasts and adventure seekers. Experience where it all began—when Captain Midnight first answered the call to justice.
