# Captain Midnight: Episode Four (October 20, 1939)

As the opening theme swells with its urgent, propeller-driven urgency, listeners are whisked away to the hangar of Captain Midnight's secret base, where danger lurks in the shadows of international intrigue. In this October 1939 episode, our daring aviation hero and his loyal crew must confront a sinister plot that threatens not just their lives, but the very security of the nation. With war spreading across Europe and uncertainty gripping America, Captain Midnight faces his most cunning adversary yet—a master spy with connections to foreign powers who will stop at nothing to steal the secrets of Midnight's revolutionary aircraft. The tension crackles through your radio speaker as coded messages are intercepted, midnight rendezvous are arranged, and the clock ticks down to a climactic confrontation high above the clouds. Every sound effect—the roar of aircraft engines, the sharp crack of gunfire, the ominous whisper of conspiracy—pulls you deeper into a world of high-stakes adventure.

This episode arrived at a pivotal moment in American consciousness. Broadcast just weeks after Nazi Germany's invasion of Poland, listeners tuned in to Captain Midnight seeking not mere entertainment but reassurance—a vision of American ingenuity and heroism standing resolute against shadowy forces of darkness. The show, created by radio's master of suspense, became a cultural phenomenon, spawning merchandise, comic strips, and a devoted following of young listeners who idolized the captain's courage and quick thinking. Jack Johnstone's voice brought Captain Midnight to vivid life, making him the embodiment of the American hero for a generation growing up in uncertain times.

Don't miss this thrilling chapter in aviation adventure history. Adjust your dial and prepare yourself for an evening of genuine suspense, daring exploits, and the kind of storytelling that made radio the heartbeat of American homes. Captain Midnight awaits!
