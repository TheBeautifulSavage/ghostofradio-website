# Captain Midnight: Episode 6 (October 24, 1939)

The skies darken as Captain Midnight throttles back his twin-engine fighter, banking hard over an uncharted island where mystery and danger lurk beneath the tropical canopy. In this electrifying installment, our intrepid aviator discovers evidence of a clandestine operation—one that threatens national security and demands his signature brand of aerial heroics. With only his trusty crew and his wits to guide him through a web of espionage and sabotage, Captain Midnight must navigate both the treacherous clouds and an even more sinister plot unfolding on the ground below. Listeners will find their hearts racing as each commercial break looms, wondering what fresh peril awaits when the program resumes.

This episode arrives at a pivotal moment in American history, broadcast just as Europe spirals into World War II and the nation grapples with questions of national defense and foreign intrigue. Captain Midnight, created by Robert Burtt and Wilfred G. Moore, captured the zeitgeist perfectly—a hero for an anxious age, representing American ingenuity, courage, and technological prowess at a time when such virtues felt urgently necessary. The show's success was phenomenal, spawning a vast merchandising empire of decoder rings, comic books, and model airplanes that turned children into devoted followers of the good Captain's exploits.

If you've never experienced the pulse-pounding adventure of Captain Midnight, or if you're a devoted listener eager to revisit these golden-age broadcasts, this October 1939 episode offers the perfect entry point.
