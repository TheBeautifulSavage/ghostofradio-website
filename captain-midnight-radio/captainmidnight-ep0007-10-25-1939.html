The roar of aircraft engines cuts through the static as Captain Midnight faces his most treacherous challenge yet—a sabotage plot that threatens to ground America's finest pilots and cripple national defense. From his secret headquarters, the Captain and his trusted flight crew must race against time to unmask a spy network operating in the heart of the aviation industry. Listen as tension crackles through every scene: coded radio messages, narrow escapes from enemy operatives, and the thunderous drama of pursuit across moonlit skies. This October evening brings danger lurking in unexpected places, where trust becomes a dangerous luxury and every shadow might conceal a threat to the nation itself.

Captain Midnight arrived on the airwaves at a pivotal moment in American history—1938—when fascism was spreading across Europe and the skies above were becoming the new frontier of global conflict. The show captured the imagination of millions of listeners with its blend of authentic aviation detail, pulp-adventure excitement, and patriotic fervor that resonated deeply with a nation anxious about its future. Created as a vehicle for aviation enthusiast and stunt pilot Russell Keeslar (who performed many of the show's sound effects personally), the series distinguished itself through meticulous attention to flying procedure and aircraft specifications, earning it respect from actual pilots while thrilling younger listeners who dreamed of the skies. By 1939, Captain Midnight had become essential listening for adventure seekers hungry for escapism during uncertain times.

Tune in to hear why America's youth made Captain Midnight their most anticipated appointment at the radio set. The adventure, the danger, the heroism—it's all waiting in the darkness.
