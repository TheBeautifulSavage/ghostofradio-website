# Captain Midnight: Episode 22 — November 15, 1939

Danger stalks the skies once more as Captain Midnight and his elite squadron of flying detectives race against time to thwart a sinister plot threatening America's aviation corridors. In this electrifying installment, our valiant hero discovers that enemy agents have infiltrated a commercial airfield, and with war drums beating ominously across the Atlantic, the stakes have never been higher. Listen as the roar of powerful aircraft engines mingles with whispered conspiracies in shadowy hangars, as coded radio transmissions crackle through the night, and as Captain Midnight must outmatch cunning adversaries in a deadly game of aerial cat-and-mouse. With loyal Patty Donovan and the technical genius of Major Steel at his side, will our hero uncover the saboteurs before catastrophe strikes? The tension builds with each passing moment as listeners are transported into a world of propellers and peril, where quick thinking and faster reflexes mean the difference between heroism and disaster.

Airing at the height of America's pre-war anxiety, *Captain Midnight* captured the nation's fascination with aviation adventure while quietly reflecting deeper concerns about espionage and national security. Created by writer George Lowther and sponsored by Ovaltine (the drink of champions), the program had already become a cultural phenomenon by late 1939, with listeners tuning in religiously to follow the Captain's exploits. The show's technical authenticity—drawing from real aviation knowledge—lent credibility that made the thrills feel disturbingly plausible to Depression-era audiences newly aware of global instability.
