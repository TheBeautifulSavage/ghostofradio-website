# The Reunion - A Casey, Crime Photographer Mystery

Picture this: a crowded high school gymnasium decorated with crepe paper and worn memories, where Casey arrives to cover what should be a simple nostalgia piece—the twenty-year reunion of his old graduating class. But when the evening's nostalgic toasts turn to screams, and a prominent banker is found dead in the darkened coat room, our hard-boiled photographer discovers that some old grudges never really die. With nothing but his camera, his wits, and the grainy photographs developing in his makeshift darkroom, Casey must navigate the minefield of rekindled friendships and buried secrets to expose the killer before the night ends. The tension crackles through every commercial break as innocent reminiscences become sinister alibis, and Casey realizes that his own past may be more intertwined with the crime than he bargained for.

What makes *Casey, Crime Photographer* essential listening is its perfect marriage of newspaper procedural authenticity with genuinely suspenseful plotting. Throughout its twelve-year run on CBS, the show captivated millions by grounding its mysteries in the workings of a real newshound's day—the clacking of typewriters, the smell of developer fluid, and the moral ambiguity of photographing tragedy. This particular episode exemplifies the show's genius for using confined spaces and interconnected characters to build claustrophobic dread, a technique that would later influence television detective shows for decades to come.

Settle in with the static and crackle of history as Casey's voice cuts through the darkness, camera in hand and danger close behind. Whether you're a devoted fan revisiting an old favorite or discovering this golden-age gem for the first time, "The Reunion" delivers exactly what made radio's greatest crime dramas unforgettable: mystery, atmosphere, and the unmistakable sound of a city's secrets being exposed one photograph at a time.
