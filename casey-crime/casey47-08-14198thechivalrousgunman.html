When a gentleman thief targets the city's wealthiest socialites, Casey Burton finds himself entangled in a case far more complex than simple robbery. Our intrepid crime photographer picks up the scent of a story that reeks of honor among thieves and desperation wrapped in a velvet glove—but as the bodies begin to pile up, it becomes chillingly clear that chivalry has its price in blood. This episode crackles with the sophisticated tension of mid-1940s urban noir, where every shadow harbors secrets and Casey's camera becomes as much a weapon as the criminals' guns. You'll hear the distinctive sounds of 1940s New York City—the screech of tires, the bark of police sirens, the whispered confessions of witnesses—all masterfully woven into a narrative that keeps you guessing whether our mysterious gunman is a Robin Hood figure or something far more sinister.

Casey, Crime Photographer thrived on CBS's airwaves as the perfect intersection of hard-boiled detective fiction and the golden age of radio drama. The show's genius lay in its central conceit: Casey wasn't merely an observer but an active participant in the criminal underworld, using his camera and quick wits to outpace both crooks and cops. This 1940s episode exemplifies the show's hallmark blend of journalistic grit and theatrical suspense, reflecting post-war America's fascination with moral ambiguity and the figure of the resourceful everyman pitted against shadowy forces.

Don't miss your chance to step into Casey's fedora and experience one of radio's most compelling mysteries. "The Chivalrous Gunman" awaits—tune in and discover why listeners kept their dials locked to CBS for over a decade.
