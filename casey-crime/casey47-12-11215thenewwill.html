# Casey, Crime Photographer – "The New Will"

Picture this: a dimly lit newspaper office where the telephone never stops ringing, the typewriter keys clatter like machine-gun fire, and danger lurks just beyond the next deadline. In this week's gripping installment, Casey receives a frantic call about a wealthy industrialist found dead in his study—and the mysterious reappearance of a will that vanished twenty years ago. As Casey rushes to the crime scene with camera in hand, he discovers that someone is willing to kill to keep the old document buried. With witnesses contradicting each other, a family divided by greed, and a deadline bearing down like a freight train, Casey must navigate a treacherous web of lies to uncover the truth before the killer strikes again. The tension mounts as our intrepid photojournalist pieces together clues that will either send an innocent person to the gallows or expose a calculated murderer.

During the golden age of radio drama, *Casey, Crime Photographer* stood apart as a show that married hard-boiled journalism with detective work—a perfect fit for post-war American audiences hungry for intelligent crime stories with real stakes. Broadcast live from CBS studios, the show's success lay in its authentic portrayal of newspaper life, its snappy dialogue, and its gritty urban atmosphere. Casey wasn't a superhero or a wealthy amateur sleuth; he was an everyman with a camera and a nose for news, solving crimes the way real reporters might—through persistence, brains, and old-fashioned legwork.

Don't miss "The New Will"—a masterclass in suspense that proves radio drama's power to captivate, unsettle, and thrill. Tune in and discover why *Casey, Crime Photographer* earned its place in broadcasting history.
