Picture this: a rain-slicked Manhattan street corner at midnight, and our intrepid news photographer Casey Jones stumbles upon a murder scene that the police would rather he forget. But Casey can't forget—not when a terrified witness emerges from the shadows, trembling with secrets that could either solve the crime or get them both killed. In this gripping episode, "Key Witness," Casey must race against time to protect his crucial informant from a killer who'll stop at nothing to keep the truth buried. The tension crackles through every scene as Casey photographs the evidence, follows dangerous leads through the city's underbelly, and realizes that the person he's protecting may be his only chance to expose a corruption ring that reaches far higher than anyone suspected. Every tick of the clock brings Casey closer to either breaking the case wide open or becoming another unsolved mystery himself.

Casey, Crime Photographer captured something uniquely American about the 1940s: the image of the newspaperman as hero, armed with nothing but wit, determination, and a camera. The show's brilliance lay in its documentary-style realism—writers drew from actual crime cases and journalistic investigations, giving listeners the illusion they were eavesdropping on real police work. Starring Darren McGavin in the lead role, the series became a beloved fixture on CBS radio, proving that listeners craved intelligent drama grounded in procedural authenticity rather than melodrama.

Don't miss this taut, expertly-crafted episode that exemplifies everything the show did best. Settle in with the lights dimmed low, let the sound effects transport you to the streets of postwar New York, and experience radio drama at its finest. "Key Witness" awaits.
