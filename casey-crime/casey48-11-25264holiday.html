As the holiday season draws near and snow begins to dust the streets of New York City, Casey Burton finds himself entangled in a case far more sinister than the usual fare that crosses the desk of a crime photographer. When a glamorous socialite is found dead in her Park Avenue apartment on Thanksgiving Eve—surrounded by unopened Christmas gifts and champagne glasses—Casey must navigate a web of jealousy, inheritance disputes, and carefully constructed alibis to uncover the truth. The cozy trappings of the season serve only to heighten the darkness of human nature, as Casey's camera flashbulb illuminates secrets that the city's elite would prefer remained buried. With his trusted contact at the police department and his own sharp instincts, Casey races against time to capture the killer before another holiday passes in mourning.

Since its debut in 1943, Casey, Crime Photographer has captivated audiences by grounding its mysteries in the authentic world of tabloid journalism and crime scene investigation. The show distinguished itself through its meticulous attention to procedural detail and the chemistry between Casey and his supporting cast, while maintaining the visceral excitement of danger lurking just beneath New York's glittering surface. By 1948, the program had become essential listening for those seeking intelligent crime drama with real stakes and believable characters.

Tune in to this compelling November broadcast and discover why listeners across America made Casey, Crime Photographer appointment radio. In this special holiday episode, murder proves to be the most unwelcome guest at the season's most sacred celebrations.
