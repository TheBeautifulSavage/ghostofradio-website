As the familiar orchestral theme swells through your home receiver, Cavalcade of America transports you to the gaslit streets of nineteenth-century America, where brave women defy convention and law alike in their desperate struggle for the right to vote. This gripping episode pulses with the tension of a nation divided, following courageous suffragettes as they navigate hostile crowds, indifferent legislatures, and their own inner doubts. You'll hear the crack of police batons, the roar of determined crowds, and the intimate moments of doubt whispered in darkened parlors—all brought vividly to life by the stellar cast and the masterful sound design that has made this program a national institution. The drama crackles with relevance and urgency, reminding listeners in 1940 that the freedoms they take for granted were won through sacrifice and unrelenting conviction.

Cavalcade of America stands apart from mere entertainment; it is a carefully crafted historical sermon, sponsored by DuPont, designed to remind Americans of their shared heritage and the pioneers who shaped the nation. Each week, the show resurrects pivotal moments from American history—not to peddle nostalgia, but to illuminate the values upon which the republic was founded. This particular episode, examining women's emancipation, arrives at a moment when those very freedoms feel newly precious and under threat. The producers spare no effort in historical authenticity, consulting scholars and primary documents to ensure that listeners experience these moments not as sanitized lessons, but as living, breathing dramas of real human struggle.

Tune in tonight and witness history come alive. Hear the voices of suffragettes and abolitionists echo through your radio, reminding you that America's greatest achievements were born from courage. Cavalcade of America airs Tuesday evenings—don't miss this powerful chapter in our nation's story.
