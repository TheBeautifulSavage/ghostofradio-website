As the orchestra swells and Du Pont's golden announcer introduces tonight's presentation, listeners are transported to the chambers of power where one courageous woman dares to challenge the status quo. "Woman in Public Service" unfolds the gripping true story of a pioneering female leader who broke through the marble halls of government, proving that patriotism and public dedication know no gender. Through carefully crafted dialogue and superb character work, this episode captures the tension, skepticism, and ultimate triumph of a woman determined to serve her nation when the very notion seemed scandalous. The sound design—from the rustling of important documents to the murmured conversations of doubting colleagues—creates an immersive portrait of institutional resistance and personal resolve that crackles with dramatic electricity.

Cavalcade of America stood as network radio's most ambitious historical drama, commissioning original stories that transformed American achievement into intimate human moments. Sponsored by Du Pont, the series premiered in 1935 with a noble mission: to celebrate the nation's progress and inspire listeners with tales of ordinary Americans accomplishing extraordinary things. By the 1940s, as the country mobilized for war, episodes like "Woman in Public Service" took on urgent relevance, highlighting the vital contributions women were making to American democracy at home and abroad. The show's writers drew from actual historical records, ensuring authenticity while crafting narrative tension that rivaled any fictional drama.

Tune in tonight for a stirring half-hour that reminds us why character and conviction matter more than convention. Cavalcade of America awaits—where history lives and breathes in the golden age of radio.
