As the opening fanfare swells and the announcer's resonant voice declares "Cavalcade of America," you're transported to a bustling American factory floor in the early twentieth century. In this gripping episode, "Safety First," listeners will witness the dramatic true story of industrial reform and human courage. A mill worker's crusade to implement basic safety measures meets fierce resistance from factory owners more concerned with profit margins than human lives. Through tense dialogue and carefully crafted sound effects—the roar of machinery, the sharp crack of an accident, the murmur of concerned voices—you'll experience the moral struggle of a man determined to prevent tragedy, even when the cost to himself seems unbearable.

What makes Cavalcade of America so essential listening is its remarkable ability to dramatize the unsung heroes who shaped the nation's character. Throughout its eighteen-year run, the series celebrated ordinary Americans whose extraordinary convictions built the democratic and industrial foundations we often take for granted. "Safety First" exemplifies this mission, drawing from the real history of workplace safety advocacy in America's gilded industrial age. Rather than presenting sanitized history, the program captures the genuine tension between progress and greed, between compassion and commerce, reminding listeners that modern conveniences and protections were hard-won through sacrifice and persistence.

Tune in to this captivating episode and hear how one person's dedication to human dignity sparked a movement that would forever change American labor. Cavalcade of America awaits—where history comes alive through the power of radio drama.
