# The Story of Christmas Seals

As the winter of 1940 descends across America, *Cavalcade of America* invites you into a tale of compassion born from desperation. The familiar opening fanfare gives way to a poignant narrative: the true story of how a humble Danish postman's brilliant idea transformed into a nationwide crusade against tuberculosis. Through crackling dialogue and the show's signature orchestral swells, listeners will discover how colorful little seals—adorable designs meant only to decorate holiday mail—became the lifeblood of a movement that saved thousands of lives. The episode captures both the quiet determination of those battling the era's most dreaded disease and the grassroots momentum of ordinary Americans who rallied to a cause through nothing more than the simple act of purchasing a Christmas seal for a few pennies.

*Cavalcade of America* had established itself as radio's most trusted vehicle for patriotic storytelling, transforming genuine American history into intimate, human dramas that resonated in living rooms across the nation. By the 1940s, the program had perfected its formula: taking pivotal moments and forgotten heroes from the national chronicle and rendering them with documentary authenticity and theatrical power. The Christmas Seals episode exemplifies this mission perfectly, celebrating not generals or presidents, but the everyman spirit that defined American progress—how innovation and generosity could merge to combat suffering.

Don't miss this stirring chapter in *Cavalcade of America*. Tune in to hear how one small idea sparked a revolution in public health and charitable giving, and how America's heart proved as boundless as its ambitions. A program that reminds us why history matters—and why it still speaks to us today.
