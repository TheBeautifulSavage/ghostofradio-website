Step into a lamplit parlor of the 1890s where a young composer struggles against poverty and ridicule to immortalize the spirit of a vanishing era through music. As mandolins echo and bicycle bells chime in the distance, you'll witness the true story behind some of America's most enduring melodies—the songs that defined a generation and nearly died with their creator. The drama unfolds with genuine warmth and period authenticity, capturing both the romantic nostalgia and the harsh realities of that gilded age, as our protagonist races against time and circumstance to prove that simple, heartfelt music can transcend the very poverty that threatens to silence him forever.

Cavalcade of America earned its place as one of radio's most respected historical dramas by refusing to sanitize the past. Rather than offering mere patriotic pageantry, the series—which aired during the turbulent 1930s and 1940s—examined the struggles, failures, and human dimensions of American achievement. "Songs of the Gay Nineties" exemplifies this philosophy, using a beloved cultural artifact (those very melodies listeners hummed daily) to explore themes of artistic persistence and social class that resonated deeply with Depression-era audiences. The show's commitment to historical accuracy, combined with its dramatic flair, transformed obscure biographical details into gripping human stories.

Don't miss this essential episode of Cavalcade of America, where the familiar suddenly becomes extraordinary and you'll hear the untold story of music you thought you knew. Tune in and rediscover why America's past continues to inspire its present.
