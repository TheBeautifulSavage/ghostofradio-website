As the familiar orchestral fanfare fades into the crackling evening air, you're transported to the glittering world of Broadway in the 1920s, where a young composer named Cole Porter is fighting against the odds to prove that popular song can be high art. This episode captures the precise moment when ambition collides with circumstance—Porter's determination to break free from his wealthy family's expectations and create something genuinely original, even as the theater world threatens to chew him up and spit him out. You'll hear the wit, the near-despair, and the brilliant defiance that defines his character, all woven through musical interludes that showcase the very songs that would eventually define an era. The tension builds steadily as Porter battles both external skepticism and his own self-doubt, creating that unmistakable tension that made Cavalcade indispensable listening for millions of Americans.

Cavalcade of America was America's favorite historical sermon, airing during the Depression and war years when ordinary people desperately needed reminders that American genius had always triumphed before. This 1940s episode about Cole Porter fit perfectly into the show's mission: celebrating the unconventional dreamers who refused to accept limitations. In bringing Porter's story to life, the episode captures not just a biography but a philosophy—that American greatness came from individuals bold enough to reject convention in pursuit of excellence. For radio audiences enduring wartime uncertainty, such stories were more than entertainment; they were proof that persistence and talent could reshape the world.

Tune in to hear how one man's refusal to compromise transformed American popular culture forever. This is Cavalcade of America—where history comes alive.
