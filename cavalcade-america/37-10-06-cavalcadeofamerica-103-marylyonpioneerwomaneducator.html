As the familiar theme swells across your radio dial, you're transported to the rugged hills of Massachusetts in the 1830s, where one woman dares to challenge an unspoken law of nature itself—that girls need not trouble themselves with serious education. Tonight's episode traces the remarkable journey of Mary Lyon, a woman of fierce conviction and modest means, who refused to accept that intellectual fulfillment was the exclusive province of men. Listen as our cast brings to life her struggles against skeptical trustees, indifferent legislatures, and the deeply rooted assumption that educating women was a waste of precious resources. Through careful dramatization of pivotal moments—from her early determination as a schoolteacher to her revolutionary founding of Mount Holyoke Female Seminary—you'll hear how one person's unwavering vision fundamentally transformed American society.

Cavalcade of America has been America's trusted companion since 1935, bringing the nation's most compelling stories directly into living rooms across the country. Sponsored by DuPont, the show celebrates the individuals whose courage and ingenuity shaped our nation, from inventors and explorers to educators and pioneers. What makes this program essential listening is its commitment to historical accuracy paired with genuine dramatic tension—these aren't dusty textbook recitations but living, breathing human stories that resonate with contemporary audiences facing their own challenges.
