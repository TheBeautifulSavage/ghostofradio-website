As the opening theme swells with patriotic fervor, Cavalcade of America transports listeners to the early American frontier, where the struggle for industrial independence becomes a gripping human drama. In "The Search for Iron," you'll follow the determined efforts of pioneering industrialists racing against time and geography to unlock America's hidden mineral wealth. The episode crackles with tension as engineers survey treacherous terrain, investors gamble their fortunes, and workers risk their lives in primitive mines and foundries. Thunder rumbles overhead; picks strike stone in echoing darkness; furnace fires roar to life. Through masterful sound design and compelling dialogue, the program captures both the romance and the raw desperation of men who believed that iron—that most fundamental element of industrial might—held the key to national greatness.

First broadcast in the 1940s when America was grappling with its role on the world stage, this episode resonates with particular urgency. Cavalcade of America distinguished itself by dramatizing not merely the famous figures of history, but the overlooked entrepreneurs, engineers, and everyday citizens whose ingenuity built the nation. DuPont's sponsorship brought considerable resources to the production, ensuring authentic period detail and stellar performances. The show's formula—celebrating American enterprise and self-reliance through historical narrative—struck a chord with Depression and wartime audiences hungry for stories of national achievement and hope.

Tune in to relive a moment when Americans first discovered that beneath their feet lay the resources to build an industrial powerhouse. "The Search for Iron" reminds us why DuPont proudly presented Cavalcade of America: to honor the dreamers and doers who shaped our nation's destiny.
