Step into the candlelit study of colonial Philadelphia as this compelling episode brings to life one of America's most ingenious minds at a pivotal moment of personal and national consequence. As thunder crackles in the distance and the fate of a young nation hangs in the balance, we witness Benjamin Franklin navigating the treacherous waters between loyalty and revolution. The drama unfolds through intimate conversations and tense confrontations, capturing the intellectual fervor and moral complexity that defined this remarkable statesman. With superb sound design—the scratch of quill on parchment, the crackle of the hearth, the ambient bustle of colonial streets—listeners will find themselves transported directly into Franklin's world, experiencing the very crossroads where ambition, duty, and patriotism collided.

Cavalcade of America stands as one of radio's most prestigious dramatic anthologies, a weekly celebration of American achievement that transformed the nation's history into compelling human drama for millions of listeners. Rather than presenting dry historical facts, the show understood that history lives in the hearts and choices of real people facing impossible decisions. This particular episode exemplifies the program's commitment to exploring not just what happened, but why it mattered—examining Franklin not as a marble bust in a museum, but as a living, breathing figure grappling with doubt and conviction. Throughout its eighteen-year run, the series earned legions of devoted listeners and critical acclaim for bringing authenticity and emotional depth to America's past.

Don't miss this remarkable portrait of one of our nation's founding architects. Tune in to experience Benjamin Franklin as you've never heard him before—a man of science and principle, caught between two worlds. Cavalcade of America awaits you.
