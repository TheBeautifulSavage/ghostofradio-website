Picture yourself settling into your favorite chair on a quiet evening, the amber glow of your radio dial cutting through the darkness as DuPont's Cavalcade of America transports you to the Mississippi riverbanks of the 1840s. In this compelling dramatization, you'll walk alongside young Samuel Clemens as he pursues his dream of becoming a riverboat pilot—a vision as vast and untamed as the great river itself. Watch as ambition and circumstance collide, as the boy who would become Mark Twain navigates the treacherous waters of his own destiny, learning that sometimes the most important journeys are the ones that shape our souls. The lively sounds of the riverboat, the urgent voices of pilots and crew members, the haunting whistle of steam through the night—all come alive through the golden age of radio drama, pulling you into a pivotal moment that would define an American literary legend.

For over a decade, Cavalcade of America has been America's premier historical drama, celebrating the courage and ingenuity of ordinary people who shaped a nation. Broadcast nationwide, the show has earned its reputation for meticulous historical accuracy wrapped in gripping human storytelling. Each episode transforms dusty history into living, breathing entertainment—whether recounting the exploits of pioneers, inventors, or writers—proving that American history is not mere dates and names, but profound human drama worthy of our attention and wonder.

Join us for this unforgettable evening as we chronicle the formative years of one of America's greatest writers. Tune in to Cavalcade of America and discover the man behind the legend. This is radio drama at its finest.
