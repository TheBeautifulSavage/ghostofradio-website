As the familiar fanfare swells and Du Pont's proud sponsorship echoes through your living room, you're transported to the glittering jazz age of 1920s New York. In this stirring installment, Cavalcade of America captures the electric life of George Gershwin—a young man from Brooklyn with an insatiable hunger to bridge the gap between tin pan alley and the concert hall. Hear the story of how Gershwin's fingers first danced across piano keys, how his melodies captivated Broadway audiences, and how his audacious dream to compose a rhapsody that would define a generation nearly consumed him. The drama crackles with creative tension as Gershwin races against time and doubt, driven by the conviction that American music could be both populist and profound. You'll witness the pivotal moments leading to that fateful night when Rhapsody in Blue premiered—a composition that would change music forever.

Cavalcade of America distinguished itself among radio's historical dramas by celebrating not just presidents and generals, but the architects of American culture who shaped the nation's soul. In the 1940s, with the world convulsed by war, these episodes reminded listeners of the enduring power of American creativity and ambition. Gershwin's story—a tale of artistic vision transcending class and convention—resonated deeply with an audience hungry for inspiration. The show's stellar cast and meticulous historical recreations made each episode feel like eavesdropping on history itself, while Du Pont's sponsorship ensured production values befitting the grandeur of these narratives.

Don't miss this luminous portrait of genius in motion. Tune in and experience how one composer's relentless pursuit of beauty gave voice to the American spirit itself.
