# Cavalcade of America - Patrick Henry

Picture yourself in the warm glow of the radio dial on a crisp evening in the 1940s as the opening fanfare of *Cavalcade of America* swells through your living room. Tonight's episode transports you to colonial Virginia, where the fate of a nascent nation hangs in the balance. You'll witness Patrick Henry's transformation from an unlikely orator into the voice of revolution, as he stands before the Virginia House of Burgesses with words that would ignite the spark of independence. The tension crackles through your speaker—can this fiery patriot convince reluctant delegates that armed rebellion is the only path forward? The radio actors bring authentic passion to this pivotal moment, their voices capturing both the idealism and the terrible stakes of defying the British Crown.

*Cavalcade of America* carved out a unique place in broadcasting history by dramatizing the pivotal moments that shaped the nation, from the workplace of Du Pont laboratories to the battlefields and legislatures where history was forged. Unlike mere recitation, these episodes breathed life into founding figures, showing them not as marble monuments but as flesh-and-blood humans wrestling with impossible decisions. In this Patrick Henry episode, listeners encounter not just the famous words "Give me liberty or give me death," but the very heart of a man called to greatness by circumstance and conviction.

Whether you're a devoted follower of America's storied past or simply seeking an evening of first-rate dramatic radio, this episode delivers both education and entertainment in equal measure. Tune in and experience why millions of Americans made *Cavalcade of America* appointment listening for nearly two decades—it's history as it was meant to be heard.
