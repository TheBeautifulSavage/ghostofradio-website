Picture yourself settling into your living room on a crisp evening, the amber glow of the radio dial warming your face as the familiar orchestral fanfare of Cavalcade of America swells through the speakers. Tonight's drama ventures into one of our nation's most troubling chapters—the story of General Benedict Arnold, the patriot whose name became synonymous with treachery. As the talented cast brings Arnold's conflicted inner world to life, listeners will find themselves drawn into the psychological portrait of an American general torn between loyalty and resentment, ambition and disillusionment. The production crackles with tension as Arnold's secret correspondence with the British unfolds, his motivations laid bare in dialogue that reveals how personal grievance can corrode even the most decorated soldier's conviction.

This program represents the very best of Cavalcade of America's mission: to dramatize the pivotal moments that shaped our republic, neither whitewashing our heroes nor demonizing our villains, but instead revealing them as complex human beings navigating impossible choices. During the 1940s, when the nation once again faced its own trials and uncertainties, such stories carried profound relevance. Cavalcade became an institution precisely because it understood that American history was not a simple morality play, but a rich tapestry of ambition, principle, and consequence. By examining figures like Arnold with nuance and psychological depth, the show helped listeners understand that patriotism itself was fragile, earned, and worth defending.

Tune in to witness this compelling examination of ambition's dark shadow—a masterfully crafted drama that reminds us why loyalty, once broken, echoes through the centuries. This is Cavalcade of America, where history comes alive.
