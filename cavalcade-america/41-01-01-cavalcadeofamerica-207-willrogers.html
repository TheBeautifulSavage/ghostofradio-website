Step into the spotlight of American showmanship as Cavalcade of America presents the remarkable life of Will Rogers, the Oklahoma humorist who captured the nation's heart with his wit, rope tricks, and homespun philosophy. This episode traces Rogers' improbable journey from cowboy performer to international celebrity, from the dusty ranches of Indian Territory to the glittering stages of Broadway and Hollywood. Listeners will experience the crackling energy of the Ziegfeld Follies, the intimate charm of his radio broadcasts, and the penetrating wisdom beneath his seemingly casual humor. With every quip and observation, Rogers became the conscience of a country grappling with Depression and uncertainty—a man who could make Americans laugh while speaking uncomfortable truths about politics, wealth, and human nature. This broadcast captures that singular magic, the voice and spirit of a performer who proved that authenticity and humor could move audiences far more than pretense ever could.

Cavalcade of America itself stands as a monument to the radio drama's golden age, having entertained millions of Americans since its 1935 debut with dramatizations of the nation's most pivotal moments and remarkable citizens. Sponsored by DuPont, the series brought history alive in living rooms across the country, transforming historical figures into vital, breathing characters whose struggles and triumphs resonated with contemporary listeners. The Will Rogers episode exemplifies the show's mission: to celebrate the everyday Americans and unconventional heroes who shaped the national character through courage, cleverness, and unwavering principle.

Tune in for a portrait of an American original—a man whose lariat and laughter lassoed a generation's affections. Don't miss this unforgettable evening with Will Rogers.
