As the orchestra swells with triumphant brass, listeners are transported to the wind-scoured decks of a Viking longship, cutting through the dark waters of the North Atlantic nearly a thousand years before Columbus ever set sail. In this thrilling installment of Cavalcade of America, you'll experience the courage and determination of Leif Erikson, the Norse explorer who dared venture beyond the known world to discover a vast, mysterious land he called Vinland. The drama unfolds with authentic period detail—the creak of wooden hulls, the cry of seafaring men facing unimaginable dangers, and the mounting tension as Erikson and his crew encounter hostile natives and treacherous terrain. This is American history as DuPont intended it to be told: as a grand narrative of bold individuals who shaped a nation's destiny through sheer willpower and vision.

Cavalcade of America distinguished itself by celebrating lesser-known heroes alongside household names, presenting dramatized historical moments that affirmed American exceptionalism and pioneering spirit during an era when such narratives provided comfort and inspiration. By the 1940s, the show had become a cultural institution, commanding millions of listeners each week with its Hollywood-quality production values and stellar cast. The Leif Erikson episode exemplifies the program's commitment to historical scholarship wrapped in compelling entertainment—reclaiming Viking achievements often overshadowed by later European exploration narratives.

Don't miss this extraordinary voyage into America's pre-Columbian past. Tune in to hear Leif Erikson's saga brought vividly to life through the magic of radio drama, where imagination and history intertwine to remind us that the discovery of America began long before we thought it did.
