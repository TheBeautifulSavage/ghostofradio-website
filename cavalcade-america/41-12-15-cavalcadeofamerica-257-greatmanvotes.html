Step into the voting booth on election day, 1940, as Cavalcade of America presents a timely examination of democracy in action. In "The Great Man Votes," listeners will discover how one citizen's ballot becomes a battleground for the ideals that built a nation. As political operatives and civic leaders converge on an ordinary American, the drama crackles with urgency—will principle triumph over pressure? The episode captures the tension of a pivotal election year, when radio itself became the nation's town square, and every broadcast carried the weight of the moment. With period-perfect dialogue and the orchestral sweep that made DuPont's sponsorship a household name, this installment reminds us that the greatest dramas aren't always found on distant battlefields, but in the quiet courage required to vote one's conscience.

Cavalcade of America spent nearly two decades celebrating the stories that shaped the republic, transforming American history into compelling human drama. Broadcast live across the nation, these episodes arrived in homes during unprecedented times—the Depression giving way to global conflict, uncertainty yielding to determination. The show's genius lay in finding the universal in the particular, whether dramatizing the Wright brothers' first flight or an everyday citizen's moral reckoning. "The Great Man Votes" exemplifies this approach, using a single election to explore the machinery of democracy and the individual's power within it. In the 1940s, when fascism threatened democracy itself, such reminders of civic duty held special resonance for American listeners.

Don't miss this sterling example of radio's golden age, where history comes alive through skilled actors, evocative sound design, and stories that still resonate today. Tune in and hear what moved a nation.
