# The Giant in the Meadow

When the needle drops on this gripping installment of *Cavalcade of America*, listeners are transported to the rolling farmlands of early America, where a humble meadow becomes the unlikely stage for an extraordinary confrontation. "The Giant in the Meadow" weaves together folklore, historical authenticity, and genuine dramatic tension as ordinary citizens face an extraordinary circumstance that will test their courage and forever alter their community. The episode crackles with that distinctive *Cavalcade* magic—the orchestral swells, the naturalistic performances, the careful sound design that places you squarely in the dust and tall grass of frontier America. What begins as a simple day in the fields transforms into something far more momentous, something that children will hear about for generations to come.

Throughout its eighteen-year run, *Cavalcade of America* distinguished itself by celebrating the forgotten heroes and pivotal moments that shaped the nation, each week drawing from the vast archives of American history to find stories of triumph, ingenuity, and human resilience. Du Pont, the show's sponsor, championed these narratives as part of its mission to inspire Americans during the turbulent 1930s and 1940s. Every episode was meticulously researched, every detail authentic, creating a vibrant portrait of the American experience that resonated in living rooms across the country. The show's rotating cast of superb actors and its commitment to historical truth set it apart from mere entertainment—this was education wrapped in compelling drama.

Don't miss this remarkable episode. Tune in tonight and discover why millions of Americans made *Cavalcade of America* an essential part of their week, and experience the power of radio to transport us through time and connect us to the valor of those who came before.
