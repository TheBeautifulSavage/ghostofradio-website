Step into the parlor of American ambition as Cavalcade of America unfolds "In the Best Tradition," a stirring tale of ordinary citizens called upon to do extraordinary things. As the familiar orchestral theme swells through your radio speaker on this evening in the 1940s, you'll find yourself transported to a pivotal moment when conscience collides with convenience, and one person's decision ripples across their community. The intimate sound design—creaking floorboards, the rustle of correspondence, whispered conversations—draws you into the intimate lives of those who shaped this nation, not through grand gestures, but through the quiet courage of everyday choices. This is radio drama at its finest: a story that begins in whispers and builds to a crescendo of moral clarity.

Cavalcade of America commanded the respect of millions during its eighteen-year run precisely because it understood what made this nation worth celebrating. Rather than glorify distant historical figures gathering dust in textbooks, host Du Pont's remarkable anthology series illuminated the forgotten heroes—the shopkeeper, the schoolteacher, the immigrant family—whose decency became the bedrock of American progress. Each episode served as both entertainment and civics lesson, reminding listeners during Depression and wartime that greatness isn't confined to marble monuments or history books. "In the Best Tradition" exemplifies this mission, weaving together period authenticity, fine character acting, and the kind of storytelling that makes democracy feel not like abstract principle, but like lived experience.

Tune in tonight and discover why millions of Americans made Cavalcade of America an appointment with their radios. Let the voices and sound effects transport you, remind you, and inspire you—just as they have for generations of listeners seeking connection to something larger than themselves.
