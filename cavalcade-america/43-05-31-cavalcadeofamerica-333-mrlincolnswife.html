Step into the candlelit parlors of 19th-century America as Cavalcade of America presents "Mr. Lincoln's Wife," a poignant exploration of Mary Todd Lincoln's devotion and sacrifice during the nation's darkest hours. This episode ventures beyond the marble monuments and history books to reveal the intimate struggles of a woman standing beside the most burdened president in American history. As war tears the nation asunder and the weight of command threatens to shatter Abraham Lincoln's spirit, it falls to Mary to hold together not just a marriage, but a man—and perhaps, the Union itself. Listen as the intimate conversations, the whispered doubts, and the steadfast love between husband and wife illuminate a relationship too often overlooked by history's glare.

Since its debut in 1935, Cavalcade of America has distinguished itself as NBC and later CBS's premier historical drama series, bringing the untold stories and forgotten heroes of American history to life with meticulous attention and theatrical brilliance. Rather than celebrating triumphant dates and decisive battles, the program has always found its greatest power in the human moments—the decisions made in private rooms, the courage found in ordinary people facing extraordinary circumstance. "Mr. Lincoln's Wife" exemplifies this noble mission, reminding listeners in the turbulent 1940s that even the greatest among us draw strength from love, family, and the quiet dignity of those who stand beside them.

Tune in tonight as Cavalcade of America reminds us that history is written not only by great men, but by the remarkable women who sustain them through their trials. This is radio drama at its finest—gripping, emotionally resonant, and deeply human.
