Picture this: it's a crisp evening in the 1940s, and you've settled into your favorite chair as the familiar overture swells from your radio speaker. Tonight on Cavalcade of America, listeners are transported to Revolutionary Boston, where the fate of a fledgling nation hangs in the balance. In "Prologue to Glory," we witness the intimate, turbulent moments before the shots heard 'round the world—the secret meetings, the heated debates, the agonizing personal sacrifices of ordinary citizens becoming extraordinary patriots. The tension crackles through the airwaves as you hear the footsteps of militiamen, the whispered conversations of conspirators, and the quiet resolve of men choosing liberty over security. It's history brought to vivid, pulsating life through the masterful performances of radio's finest actors and the evocative sound design that made Cavalcade of America appointment listening for millions.

What made Cavalcade of America such a phenomenon was its unique ability to dramatize the lesser-known human stories behind history's great moments. Sponsored by DuPont and running from 1935 to 1953, the show believed that understanding America meant understanding its people—not just its presidents and generals. Each week, listeners discovered the courage of inventors, the moral quandaries facing everyday citizens, and the unsung heroes whose names never made the history books. "Prologue to Glory" exemplifies this approach, finding profound drama in the ordinary anxieties of extraordinary times.

Don't miss this remarkable journey into the crucible of American independence. Tune in and let the golden age of radio transport you back to that pivotal moment when ordinary citizens dared to dream of a new nation. Your speakers await.
