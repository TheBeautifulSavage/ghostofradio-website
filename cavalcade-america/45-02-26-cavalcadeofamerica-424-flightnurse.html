# Cavalcade of America: "Flight Nurse"

As static crackles and the familiar strains of "Cavalcade of America" bloom through your radio speaker, you're transported to the clouds themselves—to the mercy missions that defined a generation of American heroism. "Flight Nurse" presents an intimate portrait of a woman of courage, soaring above war-torn landscapes where every descent could be her last. Listen as the roar of aircraft engines gives way to hushed conversations in cramped cargo holds, where a dedicated nurse administers comfort to the wounded while the plane shudders through anti-aircraft fire. The drama unfolds with authentic tension: will she reach the base in time? Can she save the soldier bleeding in the darkness? Director's touches of ambient sound—groaning metal, labored breathing, distant explosions—pull you into that narrow, terrifying passage between earth and sky, where duty and danger become indistinguishable.

"Cavalcade of America" arrived in 1935 as radio's most ambitious historical anthology, transforming dusty textbook moments into living, breathing human experiences. During the 1940s, as the nation mobilized for war, these episodes became something more than entertainment—they were affirmations of American character under pressure, reminding listeners that extraordinary people walked among them. "Flight Nurse" exemplifies this mission perfectly, celebrating the unsung women who answered their country's call, their contributions often relegated to footnotes while male soldiers dominated headlines. The show's commitment to historical accuracy, paired with superb supporting actors and expert sound design, made these half-hour programs essential listening in households across America.

Tune in tonight and discover the story of one remarkable woman whose quiet valor at thirty thousand feet reminds us that heroism wears many faces. "Flight Nurse" awaits—a timeless testament to American resolve.
