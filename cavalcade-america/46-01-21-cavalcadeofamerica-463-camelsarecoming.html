Picture yourself tuning your dial on a warm evening in the 1940s—the satisfying click of the knob, the gentle hum of the vacuum tubes warming up—as the distinctive Cavalcade of America orchestral fanfare swells from your speaker. Tonight's drama unfolds on the American frontier, where commerce, culture, and adventure collide in unexpected ways. A caravan approaches across the desert landscape, bearing exotic cargo and even more exotic promises. As the narrator's authoritative voice guides us through this peculiar chapter of American enterprise, you'll discover how one ambitious businessman's audacious scheme to introduce camels into the American Southwest nearly rewrote the history of westward expansion. The tension builds as skeptical ranchers, curious townsfolk, and determined entrepreneurs clash over this imported beast of burden—part business venture, part circus spectacle, wholly American in its bold ambition.

Cavalcade of America earned its reputation as radio's premier historical drama by dramatizing the true stories that shaped our nation, and this episode exemplifies that mission perfectly. The show's writers had an remarkable gift for finding those forgotten moments—the roads not taken, the experiments abandoned—that reveal the character and imagination of earlier generations. Each week from 1935 through the early 1950s, millions of listeners discovered that real American history was far more colorful and unpredictable than schoolbooks suggested. These weren't tales of presidents and generals alone, but of ordinary people wrestling with extraordinary circumstances.

Whether you're a devoted radio historian or simply curious about a forgotten chapter of American enterprise, "The Camels Are Coming" offers an evening of superb storytelling, period-perfect performances, and that ineffable magic only old-time radio can conjure. Settle into your chair, dim the lights, and let this tale transport you across time and desert alike.
