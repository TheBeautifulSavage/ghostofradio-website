Step into the gleaming corridors of Victorian ambition as Cavalcade of America presents the remarkable story of George Pullman and his revolutionary palace car. Picture the rumble of steel wheels beneath you, the soft glow of oil lamps illuminating mahogany interiors, and the clash between a visionary industrialist's dreams and the human cost of progress. This episode pulses with the tension of a man determined to transform American rail travel—but at what price? As Pullman's porters and workers move through the shadows of his meticulously designed cars, listeners will witness the collision of innovation and conscience, luxury and labor, that defined an era of explosive industrial growth.

Cavalcade of America was radio's most ambitious historical drama series, and episodes like this showcase why millions of Americans tuned in each week from 1935 through 1953. Sponsored by DuPont and committed to celebrating American achievement, the show didn't shy away from complexity. Rather than simple hagiography, it explored the contradictions embedded in the nation's rise—the brilliance alongside the moral questions it raised. The Pullman Palace Car Company genuinely revolutionized train travel, introducing sleeping berths and creature comforts that seemed almost impossibly luxurious to Depression-era listeners. Yet the company town Pullman created became a symbol of labor unrest, and this episode captures that fascinating, troubling duality with remarkable sophistication for its time.

Settle in with your radio dial tuned to the frequency of history itself. Whether you're a devoted fan of vintage drama or discovering this golden-age gem for the first time, this episode offers both rousing entertainment and genuine insight into the American character.
