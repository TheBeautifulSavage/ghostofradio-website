As your radio crackles to life on this autumn evening, you'll find yourself transported to the windswept decks of the Mayflower, where desperate colonists cling to hope amid the howling Atlantic gales. This gripping installment of Cavalcade of America brings the legendary voyage of 1620 into your living room with vivid sound design—the groan of wooden timbers, the wail of the wind, the hushed prayers of frightened passengers. You'll witness the human struggles behind the Thanksgiving myth: the fever that claimed half their number, the bitter conflicts between "Saints" and "Strangers," and the quiet determination of men and women who risked everything for religious freedom and a new life in an untamed wilderness. The drama unfolds through intimate character moments and rousing ensemble scenes, capturing both the mundane hardships and the transcendent faith that sustained these pioneers as they faced the unknown.

Cavalcade of America achieved something remarkable during its 18-year run—it transformed American history from dusty textbook pages into immediate, breathing drama. Sponsored by DuPont, the program reached millions of listeners weekly, presenting historical episodes alongside contemporary stories of American achievement and innovation. The show's creators understood that history wasn't merely facts and dates; it was the personal triumph and tragedy of ordinary people in extraordinary circumstances. This particular episode exemplifies the program's gift for dramatizing pivotal moments while keeping the focus on human courage and conviction rather than mere pageantry.

Tune in tonight and experience why families across America made Cavalcade of America appointment listening. You'll emerge with a deeper appreciation for the extraordinary sacrifices that founded our nation—and perhaps a renewed sense of the pioneer spirit that still flows through American veins.
