# The House Near Little Dock Street

Picture yourself settling into your favorite chair on a quiet evening, the amber glow of your radio dial warming the darkened parlor, as the familiar fanfare of *Cavalcade of America* swells through the speaker. Tonight's episode, "The House Near Little Dock Street," transports you to a pivotal moment in American maritime history, where ordinary citizens grappled with extraordinary circumstances that would ripple across generations. You'll hear the creak of wooden docks, the salt-tinged voices of harbor workers, and the intimate conversations that unfolded within the modest walls of a house that became far more significant than its humble location suggested. This is a story of ambition, sacrifice, and the quiet heroism that shaped our nation's commerce and character—told with the vivid immediacy that made radio drama America's heartbeat.

*Cavalcade of America* distinguished itself throughout its impressive eighteen-year run by dramatizing authentic moments from American history, transforming dusty textbook entries into living, breathing human experiences. Each week, the program's stellar cast brought to life the inventors, merchants, activists, and ordinary folk whose decisions altered the nation's trajectory. The show's commitment to historical accuracy, combined with Du Pont's sponsorship and NBC's resources, created a broadcast experience that educated millions while entertaining them—a golden age achievement when radio could simultaneously delight and inform an entire nation gathered around their sets.

As the clock strikes the hour, don't miss this compelling glimpse into America's storied past. Tune in to *Cavalcade of America* and discover why listeners across the nation made this program an essential part of their weekly ritual. History awaits at the house near Little Dock Street.
