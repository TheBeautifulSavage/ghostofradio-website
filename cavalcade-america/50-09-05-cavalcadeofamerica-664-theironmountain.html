Travel back to the rugged heart of America's industrial awakening as Cavalcade of America presents "The Iron Mountain," a gripping tale of ambition, engineering prowess, and the human cost of progress. From the moment the opening theme swells across your loudspeaker, you'll be transported to the windswept peaks and bustling mining operations of the American frontier, where ordinary men undertake extraordinary feats of determination. Listen as the drama unfolds: ambitious engineers, desperate workers, and stubborn visionaries clash against impossible odds and unforgiving terrain, all racing against time and nature itself. The script crackles with period authenticity—the clang of pickaxes, the hiss of steam engines, the urgent voices calling out across mountain passes—creating an atmosphere as solid and immovable as the iron mountain itself.

Cavalcade of America was more than mere entertainment; it was a cultural institution that brought the nation's heritage directly into living rooms across the country. Sponsored by Du Pont and broadcast on both NBC and CBS throughout its eighteen-year run, the show celebrated American achievement at a crucial moment when the nation needed reminders of its pioneering spirit. Each episode dramatized a true historical event, employing professional actors and meticulous research to capture not just the facts, but the human drama behind them. "The Iron Mountain" exemplifies this mission perfectly—transforming an overlooked chapter of industrial history into compelling radio theater that educated listeners while entertaining them.

Don't miss this chance to experience why Cavalcade of America commanded audiences of millions each week. Tune in now to hear how vision, courage, and American determination transformed mountains of ore into the sinews of a mighty nation. These stories shaped how Americans understood themselves—and they still resonate today.
