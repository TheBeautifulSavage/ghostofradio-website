Picture this: it's a sweltering summer evening in 1940s America, and families across the nation are gathered around their radios as the familiar stirring fanfare of the Cavalcade of America theme swells through the speaker. Tonight's episode transports listeners to the heart of industrial ingenuity—a tale of ambition, desperation, and the sheer audacity of the American entrepreneur. "700 Boiled Shirts" tells the true story of a struggling businessman whose gamble on a simple laundry innovation becomes an unexpected turning point in his fortunes. As the drama unfolds, you'll hear the clash of conflicting dreams, the weight of financial ruin hanging over an honest man's shoulders, and the crackling tension of a make-or-break business deal. The sound design pulls you into cramped factory floors and bustling boardrooms, where every decision echoes with consequence.

Cavalcade of America had become appointment listening for millions during its eighteen-year run, showcasing the lives of ordinary Americans and forgotten heroes whose contributions shaped the nation. Rather than focusing on presidents and generals, the show's producers understood that real American history lived in the workshops, trading posts, and determined hearts of everyday people. This particular episode exemplifies the program's gift for finding drama not in bloodshed but in the struggle for dignity and success—themes that resonated deeply during wartime America.
