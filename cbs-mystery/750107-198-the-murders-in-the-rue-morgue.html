Step into the fog-shrouded streets of Paris as CBS Radio Mystery Theater brings Edgar Allan Poe's masterwork to vivid life through the crackling speakers of your radio. This haunting adaptation plunges listeners into a maze of locked doors, baffling evidence, and a mystery so bizarre that even the authorities stand bewildered. You'll hear the gaslit atmosphere of 19th-century Paris rendered in sterling sound design—the creak of floorboards, the whisper of silk gowns, the frantic questioning of witnesses whose testimonies contradict all logic. As the body count rises and the investigation deepens, you're drawn inexorably toward a solution so startling, so perfectly strange, that it redefines what a murder mystery can be.

CBS Radio Mystery Theater, which thrived during the 1974-1982 revival period, represented a nostalgic return to radio drama's golden age, when millions gathered around their sets for spine-tingling entertainment. By adapting classic literature alongside original scripts, the show honored radio's storytelling tradition while introducing new generations to enduring tales of suspense and the supernatural. This particular episode exemplifies the program's skill at transforming beloved source material into an intimate, immersive experience—one where your imagination becomes the ultimate special effect, conjuring every horrific detail far more vividly than any visual medium could achieve.

For anyone who cherishes genuine atmospheric drama, who understands that the best scares bloom in the darkness of one's own mind, this episode remains essential listening. Turn down the lights, adjust your dial, and prepare yourself for the inexplicable horror waiting in the Rue Morgue.
