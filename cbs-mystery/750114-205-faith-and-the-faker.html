Faith And The Faker

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

When the mysterious Reverend Cray rolls into the small town of Millbrook with promises of miracles and salvation, the desperate townspeople welcome him with open hearts—but one woman's faith begins to crack under the weight of suspicious circumstances. As the reverend's "healings" multiply and his influence deepens, a schoolteacher discovers troubling connections between his arrivals and a string of convenient deaths among the town's wealthiest citizens. In this gripping tale of deception and moral reckoning, listeners will find themselves drawn into a web of small-town secrets where the line between spiritual conviction and calculated fraud becomes terrifyingly thin. The atmospheric production captures the claustrophobic dread of a community held hostage by charisma, while the reverend's silken voice—promising redemption—grows more sinister with every revelation.

CBS Radio Mystery Theater* arrived during a golden resurgence of radio drama in the 1970s, proving that audiences still craved the intimate terror that only the spoken word could deliver. Airing five nights a week for nearly a decade, the series became a masterclass in psychological suspense, attracting talented writers and performers who crafted stories that lingered long after the final fade-out. "Faith and the Faker," with its exploration of manipulation and blind belief, exemplifies why the show developed such a devoted following—its mysteries never relied on cheap scares, but rather on the human capacity for self-deception and the chilling realization that evil often wears the most trustworthy of faces.

Don't miss this haunting exploration of faith, fraud, and the terrible price of truth. Tune in to *CBS Radio Mystery Theater* and discover why this episode remains a listener favorite—a masterwork of suspense that proves sometimes the most dangerous mysteries lurk beneath a smile.
