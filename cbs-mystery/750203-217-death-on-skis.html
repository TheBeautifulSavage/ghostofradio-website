Death On Skis

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

Bundle up, lock your doors, and prepare yourself for a chilling descent into mystery as our tale unfolds high in the snow-covered peaks of a remote mountain lodge. *Death On Skis* traps you with a party of wealthy vacationers whose winter getaway becomes a nightmare of suspicion and danger. When one of their party plummets down an icy slope under mysterious circumstances, the question isn't whether it was an accident—it's who among them had the motive and opportunity to commit murder. As the lodge becomes snowbound and isolated from the outside world, paranoia spreads faster than the evening chill. The creaking of the lodge, the wind howling through the passes, and the steady ticking of the grandfather clock create an atmosphere so palpable you'll feel the frost on your breath as the mystery deepens.

CBS Radio Mystery Theater* mastered the art of transforming everyday scenarios into nightmarish puzzles during its golden run from 1974 to 1982. Though this episode carries the spirit of 1940s mystery fiction, the show's writers understood that the greatest terror lies not in the supernatural, but in the sinister capabilities of ordinary people confined in extraordinary circumstances. This episode exemplifies that craftsmanship—sophisticated plotting, stellar voice acting, and sound design that makes a mountain lodge feel like a prison of snow and secrets. The show's commitment to intelligent storytelling made it a beacon of quality drama during radio's twilight years.

Don't miss this masterclass in atmospheric mystery. Settle into your favorite chair, dim the lights, and let the voices and effects transport you to a world where one wrong turn on the slopes might be no accident at all. *Death On Skis* awaits—if you dare to listen.
