The Wakeful Ghost

Settle into your favorite chair as darkness falls, because tonight on CBS Radio Mystery Theater, you're invited into the unsettling world of *The Wakeful Ghost*—a tale that will keep you guake as midnight approaches. A mysterious woman discovers she cannot sleep, plagued by an inexplicable presence in her modest home. Is it merely insomnia gnawing at her sanity, or something far more sinister? As our heroine desperately searches for answers through sleepless nights and shadowed rooms, the line between the living and the dead grows perilously thin. The production's masterful sound design—creaking floorboards, whispered voices, and that persistent, mournful wind—transforms your living room into a haunted space where anything might emerge from the darkness. This is mystery radio at its finest: psychological, atmospheric, and utterly absorbing.

The Wakeful Ghost* exemplifies why CBS Radio Mystery Theater became a cultural phenomenon during its run from 1974 to 1982, even as it reached back into golden-age storytelling traditions. Despite the episode's 1940s setting, the show itself revitalized the supernatural drama format that had largely disappeared from American radio, proving audiences still craved that visceral thrill of audio-only terror. With no visual crutches, writers relied entirely on dialogue, sound effects, and musical cues to burrow into listeners' imaginations—a craft perfected decades earlier but brought to sophisticated new heights during the show's renaissance. Each episode was a self-contained theatrical production, performed live in the studio with full cast and effects, making every broadcast feel immediate and unpredictable.

Don't miss your appointment with the supernatural. Tune in to *The Wakeful Ghost* and discover why radio mystery endures as one of entertainment's most intimate and powerful mediums.
