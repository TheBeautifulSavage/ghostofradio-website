The Velvet Claws

Picture yourself in a dimly lit parlor on a winter's evening, the radiator hissing softly in the corner, when suddenly you hear the unmistakable click of a woman's high heels on hardwood floors. In *The Velvet Claws*, a calculating femme fatale enters the life of an unsuspecting businessman, her motives as mysterious as the shadows that seem to follow her every movement. As the drama unfolds across thirty tense minutes, you'll find yourself drawn deeper into a web of deception and danger—where every whispered confession and every accusation could be a lie, and trust becomes the most dangerous commodity of all. The orchestral score swells and fades with each revelation, each twist of the knife, as our protagonist realizes too late that he's been ensnared by someone far more cunning than he ever imagined.

CBS Radio Mystery Theater* stands as one of the finest achievements in American broadcasting, a late-blooming masterpiece that proved radio drama could thrive even in the television age. Drawing inspiration from the golden age of radio while maintaining a modern sensibility, the show crafted stories that lingered long after the final fade-out. Though the episode is attributed to the 1940s in spirit and setting, this particular tale was produced during the show's acclaimed run in the mid-1970s, when creator Ester Phillips and her talented roster of writers understood that the human voice, paired with sound effects and music, could create terror and suspense more effectively than any visual medium.

If you've never experienced the peculiar magic of radio drama—that intimate darkness where your imagination becomes the cinematographer—*The Velvet Claws* is an ideal entry point. Tune in and discover why millions of listeners made this show appointment radio.
