Killer Of The Year

When the clock strikes midnight on December 31st, a prestigious Manhattan hotel hosts its most exclusive gala—where the city's elite gather to toast the arrival of a new decade. But this New Year's Eve celebration will become something far more sinister. As champagne flows and orchestras swell, a guest is found dead in a locked room, and Detective Harry Madsen discovers himself trapped in a web of secrets, jealousies, and lies so tangled that the killer could be standing mere feet away. The CBS Radio Mystery Theater pulls you into this glittering world of Art Deco glamour and creeping dread, where every shadow conceals a motive and every alibi dissolves under scrutiny. You'll hear the clinking of glasses give way to screams, the band's music transform into an instrument of tension, and the countdown to midnight become a countdown to murder.

This 1977 episode stands as a masterclass in what made the CBS Radio Mystery Theater a cultural phenomenon during its nine-year run—sophisticated storytelling that refused to condescend to its audience, paired with impeccable sound design that transformed your living room into a crime scene. The show's creators understood that radio's greatest power lay not in showing violence, but in *suggesting* it through expert use of atmosphere, dialogue, and Foley artistry. "Killer Of The Year" showcases this brilliance, with a script that plays fair with listeners while maintaining breathless momentum, and a cast of character actors who bring nuance and menace to every line.

Step into this glittering, deadly world. Settle into your favorite chair, dim the lights, and let the opening announcements transport you back to an era when mysteries unfolded in the darkness, painted only by words and imagination. The truth awaits—if you're brave enough to listen.
