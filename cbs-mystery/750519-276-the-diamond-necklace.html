The Diamond Necklace

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

On a fog-shrouded Manhattan evening, a priceless diamond necklace vanishes from a locked penthouse, and with it disappears all certainty about guilt and innocence. When Detective Raymond Cross arrives at the scene, he discovers not a straightforward theft, but a tangled web of jealous lovers, desperate heirs, and carefully constructed alibis that seem to crumble the moment they're examined. As the night deepens and the investigation spirals through speakeasies and society drawing rooms, listeners will find themselves suspended in that delicious uncertainty where anyone could be the thief—or worse. The haunting organ score pulses beneath every accusation, every revelation, every shocking twist, building toward a finale that redefines everything that came before it.

The CBS Radio Mystery Theater* stands as one of the last great achievements of radio drama, reviving the medium's golden age traditions even as television claimed America's attention. Running from 1974 to 1982, the show proved audiences never truly lost their appetite for stories delivered through voices, music, and imagination alone. "The Diamond Necklace" exemplifies the series' mastery of the mystery format—taut plotting, superb ensemble acting, and an understanding that radio's greatest power lies in the theater of the mind. Each episode was crafted as a complete dramatic arc, satisfying yet suggestive, leaving listeners both content and haunted.

Step into the darkened studio and let the pages of *The Diamond Necklace* whisper their secrets to you. This is radio drama at its finest—sophisticated, thrilling, and utterly alive with possibility. Tune in tonight and discover why, even in our modern age, some stories demand nothing but your ear and your imagination.
