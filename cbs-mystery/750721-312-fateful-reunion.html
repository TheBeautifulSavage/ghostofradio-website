Fateful Reunion

When old friends gather after twenty years apart, memories should warm the heart—but on this fog-shrouded autumn evening, something sinister lurks beneath their cheerful reunion. *Fateful Reunion* pulls listeners into an elegant manor house where laughter curdles into dread as the night deepens. Each character harbors a secret from the past, and as midnight approaches, one of them will not see the dawn. With E.G. Marshall's stentorian narration cutting through the static like a blade, the production builds an almost unbearable tension: the creak of floorboards, the clink of a poisoned glass, the accusatory silence that follows a revelation of long-buried betrayal. This is mystery theater at its most artful, where the killer might wear a smile and speak with the voice of an old confidant.

CBS Radio Mystery Theater* arrived in 1974 as a cultural artifact of impeccable timing, reviving the golden age of dramatic radio for an audience hungry for intelligent suspense. Broadcast live twice daily for eight consecutive years, the show became a phenomenon—a throwback that proved sophisticated storytelling needed no special effects, only skilled voice actors and carefully orchestrated sound design. Episodes like *Fateful Reunion* exemplify why the series earned devoted listeners and critical respect: each story was meticulously crafted, performed by Broadway-caliber talent, and designed to activate the listener's imagination in ways television never could.

Settle into your chair, dim the lights, and let the crackling introduction transport you to a world where shadows are cast by voice alone and danger echoes through the speaker. *Fateful Reunion* awaits—and it's a mystery you won't forget.
