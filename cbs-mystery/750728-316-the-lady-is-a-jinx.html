The Lady Is A Jinx

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

When Patricia Ashford boards the luxury ocean liner *Mauritania*, she carries more than luggage in her gilded cabin—she carries a curse. Every man who has ever loved her has met with sudden, inexplicable disaster: one drowned, another struck by lightning, a third consumed by a mysterious fever. Now, as the ship cuts through moonlit waters with its glittering passenger list, an ambitious shipping magnate becomes dangerously infatuated with the beautiful, haunted woman. Is Patricia truly jinxed by some malevolent force, or does she harbor darker secrets of her own? The drama unfolds with delicious ambiguity as whispered accusations echo through the ship's corridors, crew members grow suspicious, and a final twist awaits that will leave you questioning everything you've heard.

CBS Radio Mystery Theater brilliantly revived the golden age of mystery broadcasting in the 1970s, bringing sophisticated, adult-oriented suspense to an era of television dominance. "The Lady Is a Jinx" exemplifies the show's mastery of period atmosphere and psychological tension—a masterclass in how radio creates dread through voice alone. The sterling cast delivers every innuendo and accusation with theatrical precision, while the sound design conjures the elegant interiors and storm-tossed decks of a bygone ocean voyage. These episodes represent a nostalgic return to what made radio thrilling: pure storytelling, ensemble acting, and the listener's own imagination doing the heavy lifting.

Tune in tonight for an evening of maritime mystery and romantic entanglement where nothing—and no one—is quite as they seem. Settle into your favorite chair, dim the lights, and let the opening theme transport you to a world where danger lurks behind every sophisticated smile.
