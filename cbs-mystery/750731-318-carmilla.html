Carmilla

When the sun sets over the Carpathian countryside in this classic adaptation, listeners are drawn into a world of creeping dread and sensual menace. *Carmilla* unfolds as a masterwork of psychological terror, following a mysterious noblewoman who arrives at an isolated manor with secrets as dark as the shadows that surround her. As the young mistress of the house grows inexplicably drawn to her exotic guest, strange afflictions begin to plague the household—pallor, weakness, and an unsettling fascination that defies reason. Through expertly layered sound design, the CBS Radio Mystery Theater transforms Joseph Sheridan Le Fanu's seminal 1872 novella into an experience that prowls through the listener's imagination, each creak of floorboards and whispered confession heightening the sense that something preternatural lurks beneath the veneer of Victorian civility.

This episode represents the golden age of radio drama, when CBS's pioneering anthology series commanded the nation's attention during prime evening hours. The Radio Mystery Theater, which ran from 1974 to 1982, revived the tradition of serialized mystery storytelling at a moment when television seemed poised to render radio obsolete. By choosing timeless horror literature as source material, the producers created something uniquely powerful—stories that relied entirely on voice, sound, and imagination to terrify audiences. *Carmilla*, in particular, stands as an early vampire tale that predates Dracula and introduced themes of forbidden desire and predatory seduction that would echo through horror for generations.

Step through the manor doors and into an evening of genuine mystery and suspense. Let your mind's eye construct the shadows while the voices of talented actors paint the psychological landscape. *Carmilla* awaits—and she is very interested in making your acquaintance.
