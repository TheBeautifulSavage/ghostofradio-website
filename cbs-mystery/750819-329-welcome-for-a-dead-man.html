Welcome For A Dead Man

On a fog-choked evening in a crumbling Victorian mansion, a mysterious stranger arrives at the door—but no one knows who he is, or why the household seems to recognize him instantly. In "Welcome For A Dead Man," the CBS Radio Mystery Theater weaves a tale of uncanny presences and impossible identities that will keep listeners suspended between the rational and the supernatural. As the night deepens and secrets unravel, you'll find yourself gripped by the central mystery: Is this visitor a ghost returning to settle old scores, or something far more sinister? The program unfolds with masterful sound design—creaking floorboards, howling wind, and hushed, urgent dialogue—that transforms your living room into that very mansion, where every shadow seems to conceal a threat and every revelation raises darker questions.

This episode epitomizes what made the CBS Radio Mystery Theater such a cultural phenomenon during its eight-year run. While most radio drama had faded by the 1970s, this series proved that audiences still craved the intimate terror that only radio could deliver—where imagination completes the picture and the unknown becomes truly frightening. Produced during an era when television dominated entertainment, the show's commitment to intelligent, atmospheric storytelling attracted millions of devoted listeners who understood that the most effective horror exists not on screen, but in the mind's eye.

If you've never experienced the golden age of radio drama, or if you're a devoted fan seeking to revisit a classic mystery, "Welcome For A Dead Man" is an essential listen. Dim the lights, settle in, and prepare yourself for an evening of genuine suspense—the kind that lingers long after the final dramatic chord fades into static. This is radio mystery at its finest.
