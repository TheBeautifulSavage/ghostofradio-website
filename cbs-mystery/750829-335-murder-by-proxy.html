When the curtain rises on this chilling tale, you'll find yourself in the shadowy corridors of a Manhattan penthouse where a wealthy industrialist receives an unexpected visitor bearing an impossible proposition: commit a murder you'll never witness, pull a trigger you'll never touch, and walk away with clean hands and a guilty conscience. As the plot unfolds with meticulous precision, our protagonist discovers that some crimes are engineered with such cunning that the perpetrator becomes merely a puppet in another's deadly game. The drama crackles with paranoia and moral complexity, as each revelation peels away another layer of deception—will our hero realize too late that he's been playing a role written by a master criminal, or can he turn the tables before blood is spilled?

CBS Radio Mystery Theater stands as a beacon of broadcasting brilliance during an era when television had already begun stealing listeners away from their beloved radio serials. Premiering in 1974, the show was a deliberate throwback to the golden age of radio drama, yet it brought contemporary storytelling sensibilities and top-tier voice talent to the format. "Murder By Proxy" exemplifies the show's genius for psychological thriller narratives—plots where the greatest danger lies not in jump scares or monsters, but in the labyrinthine machinations of human ambition and revenge. Each episode was a self-contained mystery, allowing listeners to surrender themselves completely to a new world of intrigue every broadcast.

Settle into your favorite chair, dim the lights, and prepare yourself for an evening of superb storytelling and stellar performances. "Murder By Proxy" awaits—a masterclass in suspense that proves mystery and murder need only the power of imagination to truly terrify.
