When the screams stop, the real terror begins. In this chilling installment of CBS Radio Mystery Theater, a seemingly genteel neighborhood is gripped by fear as elderly women fall victim to a phantom killer whose identity remains shrouded in shadow. As detectives close in, the murderer grows bolder, more cunning—striking when no one expects it, vanishing without a trace. The apartment doors lock from the inside. The windows remain sealed. Yet somehow, inexplicably, death finds its way in. With each passing moment, the tension mounts: Is the killer someone we've already met? A trusted neighbor? A family member hiding in plain sight? The haunting score swells as red herrings multiply and suspicion spreads like poison through the community, turning neighbors into suspects and friends into enemies.

CBS Radio Mystery Theater arrived in 1974 as a glorious resurrection of radio's golden age, bringing theatrical mystery and suspense back to millions of listeners who remembered when radio was America's heartbeat. For nearly a decade, the show became an institution, with its distinctive opening narration—"*Submitted for your approval...*"—becoming as iconic as the programs themselves. "The Little Old Lady Killer" represents the show at its finest, expertly blending classic whodunit conventions with the psychological sophistication that made the revival series so compelling. The production showcases the remarkable talents of professional voice actors and sound designers who transformed simple microphones into portals to shadowy, dangerous worlds—proof that radio's power to unsettle and captivate had lost none of its potency.

Tune in tonight and discover why CBS Radio Mystery Theater captivated over 9 million listeners weekly. In the dark of your room, with only your imagination and a speaker before you, prepare to meet a killer who may be closer than you think.
