The Coffin With The Golden Nails

As the CBS Radio Mystery Theater's opening theme swells into your living room, you settle into darkness for a tale that defies all rational explanation. In "The Coffin With The Golden Nails," an ordinary antique dealer stumbles upon a centuries-old coffin adorned with mysterious golden fastenings—each one inscribed with a date, a name, and a chilling prophecy. When he unwisely decides to restore the macabre piece, he discovers that the final nail remains empty, its space waiting to be filled. The subsequent nights bring strange visitors, cryptic warnings, and the horrifying realization that the coffin's curse may not be confined to history. With expert sound design—the creak of mahogany, the whisper of unseen presences, the tolling of distant bells—this episode wraps you in an atmosphere of creeping dread that builds to an unforgettable climax.

The CBS Radio Mystery Theater represented the golden age of sophisticated horror entertainment, bringing theatrical production values and literary ambition to American homes during the 1970s and early 1980s. This particular episode exemplifies the show's genius for blending genuine historical curiosity with supernatural terror, tapping into listeners' fascination with antiques and the dangerous secrets they might harbor. The era's post-Twilight Zone appetite for thoughtful, twist-ending mysteries found perfect expression in stories like this one, where greed, curiosity, and fate intertwine with elegant inevitability.

"The Coffin With The Golden Nails" awaits you in the archives—a masterclass in radio suspense that proves some mysteries are better left undisturbed. Dim the lights, tune in, and discover why this episode remains a listener favorite nearly fifty years later. Some things, after all, were never meant to be restored.
