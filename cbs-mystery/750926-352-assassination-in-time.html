As the CBS Radio Mystery Theater's signature theremin wails through your speaker, you're transported to a shadowy corridor of power where time itself becomes the perfect weapon. *Assassination In Time* pulls listeners into a labyrinthine tale of espionage and temporal paradox set against the backdrop of the 1940s—a period when the world trembled on the edge of catastrophe. Our protagonist, a brilliant but desperate physicist, discovers he can manipulate the fabric of history itself, and he's determined to alter the course of World War II by eliminating a key figure before they can shape the future. But as past and present collide in delicious dramatic irony, listeners will find themselves caught in a tightening web of consequences, where every action echoes across decades and the line between salvation and damnation blurs into shadow.

The CBS Radio Mystery Theater emerged in 1974 as a triumphant resurrection of a dying art form, proving that radio drama could still captivate audiences in the age of television. This particular episode exemplifies the show's masterful blend of genuine historical atmosphere with speculative science fiction—a combination that allowed writers to explore the moral complexities of the war years through an imaginative lens. The cast delivers performances crackling with tension, supported by the show's legendary sound design team who conjured the echoing footsteps, creaking doors, and ominous orchestrations that made listeners' hearts race.

If you've never experienced the visceral thrill of radio drama, or if you're a seasoned devotee of the mysterious and macabre, *Assassination In Time* deserves a place in your listening queue. Dim the lights, settle into your favorite chair, and prepare for thirty minutes of expertly crafted suspense. Some mysteries, after all, transcend time itself.
