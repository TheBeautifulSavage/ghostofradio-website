Picture this: the year is 1940, and you're settling into your favorite chair as the CBS Radio Mystery Theater's signature organ music swells through your home. Tonight's offering is "Million Dollar Murder"—a tale of greed, deception, and cold-blooded revenge that unfolds in the shadowy corridors of high finance. A wealthy businessman lies dead under suspicious circumstances, and a million-dollar fortune hangs in the balance. As the investigation deepens, you'll find yourself navigating a labyrinth of suspects: the ambitious nephew, the estranged wife, the disgruntled business partner. Each voice crackling through the speaker becomes a window into motive and opportunity. The sound effects—the creak of a floorboard, the whisper of turning pages, the sharp report of a gunshot—pull you deeper into the mystery, and just when you think you've solved it, a stunning revelation will leave you gasping.

CBS Radio Mystery Theater arrived in 1974 as a triumphant revival of a golden age that had nearly vanished. In an era increasingly dominated by television, the show proved that radio's power to terrify and enthrall remained undiminished. "Million Dollar Murder" exemplifies why the program became a phenomenon, beloved by both nostalgic listeners and a new generation discovering the magic of audio drama. These episodes, with their clever plotting and superb voice acting, harken back to a time when imagination was the only special effect you needed.

This is radio drama at its finest—crafted for listeners who know that the most frightening monsters exist in the mind. Tune in and let the mystery unfold.
