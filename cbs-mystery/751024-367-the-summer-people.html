The Summer People

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

As the opening organ notes fade into the gathering darkness, you'll find yourself drawn into a fog-shrouded New England seaside town where nothing is quite as it seems. When a young couple inherits a charming Victorian cottage for the season, they discover their quiet refuge harbors a sinister secret—the locals speak in hushed tones about "the summer people," visitors who arrive each July and vanish without a trace before autumn comes. What begins as idle gossip transforms into creeping dread as our protagonists uncover a pattern spanning decades, each disappearance meticulously recorded in a leather-bound ledger hidden in the cottage's walls. The tension mounts exquisitely as they realize the townspeople's smiles grow stranger, their invitations more insistent, and the calendar pages turn toward the fateful final night of their lease.

CBS Radio Mystery Theater* thrived during the 1970s revival of classic radio drama, capturing the golden age's mastery of suspense while embracing contemporary storytelling sensibilities. "The Summer People" exemplifies the show's genius for transforming intimate, relatable scenarios into tales of creeping horror—it's not monsters or mayhem, but the unsettling notion that evil wears a neighborly face. The episode's 1940s setting grounds the supernatural unease in post-war American anxieties about community, belonging, and the darkness lurking beneath small-town normalcy. This was radio drama at its finest: using only voice, sound effects, and your own vivid imagination to construct terror.

If you've never experienced the particular magic of *CBS Radio Mystery Theater*, "The Summer People" is the perfect entry point—a masterclass in atmospheric storytelling that will have you checking your locks long after the final commercial break. Tune in tonight and rediscover why millions of listeners once gathered around their radios, surrendering to the dark.
