As the opening theme swells with its signature organ notes, listeners are drawn into the glittering but treacherous world of Manhattan's high society in the 1940s. *Party Girl* presents a mystery wrapped in silk and champagne, where a beautiful woman's fatal plunge from a penthouse balcony may be far more sinister than a simple suicide. Was it an accident? Murder? Or something darker still lurking beneath the surface of cocktail hour confessions? The cast delivers performances dripping with period authenticity—the clipped dialogue, the knowing innuendo, the barely concealed desperation of characters clawing their way through New York's elite circles. As the investigation unfolds, listeners will find themselves navigating a maze of suspects, each with compelling motives and equally compelling secrets to protect.

CBS Radio Mystery Theater, which ran from 1974 to 1982, brought the golden age of radio drama roaring back to life for a new generation of devoted fans. By recreating stories set in earlier decades, the show captured the elegance and shadow of bygone eras while employing modern production techniques and storytelling sensibilities. *Party Girl*, with its 1940s setting, exemplifies the show's masterful ability to evoke the atmosphere of noir-influenced mysteries—those tales of femmes fatales and morally ambiguous characters that defined an entire era of American popular culture. The production's attention to period detail, from the ambient sounds of a busy Manhattan nightclub to the subtle inflections of mid-century speech patterns, transports listeners directly into a world they might recognize from classic films and literature.

Tune in now to experience *Party Girl* and discover whether the truth behind this high-society death will surprise you. In the hands of CBS Radio Mystery Theater's expert storytellers, every shadow could conceal a clue, and every guest at the party could harbor a deadly secret.
