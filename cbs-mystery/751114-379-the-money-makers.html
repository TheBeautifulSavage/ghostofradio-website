The Money Makers

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

When a struggling printer discovers a mysterious customer willing to pay handsomely for a peculiar job, he thinks his financial troubles are finally over. But the late-night delivery of blank paper and ink quickly spirals into a nightmare of moral compromise and criminal entanglement. In this taut thriller, the line between necessity and culpability blurs as our protagonist finds himself caught between a ruthless counterfeiting ring and the federal agents closing in. With each pressing of the plate, each stack of perfect bills, he descends deeper into a world where desperation becomes complicity. The creeping dread builds masterfully as our hero realizes too late that some debts can only be paid in blood—and that the men running this operation will stop at nothing to silence anyone who knows too much.

The CBS Radio Mystery Theater premiered in 1974 as a deliberate resurrection of a golden age many feared had passed forever. In an era dominated by television, creator Himan Brown proved that radio drama could still captivate and terrify audiences with nothing but skilled voice acting, sound design, and an expertly crafted script. "The Money Makers" exemplifies the show's particular genius: anchoring supernatural and criminal tales in gritty, recognizable worlds where ordinary people face extraordinary temptations. The series ran for over a thousand episodes across eight seasons, becoming a cultural touchstone for those who remembered radio's heyday while introducing younger listeners to the medium's unmatched intimacy and power.

Settle into your favorite chair, dim the lights, and let the familiar CBS opening theme transport you to a world where danger lurks in the shadows and a single wrong decision can seal your fate. "The Money Makers" awaits—a masterclass in suspense that proves some of the greatest thrills come not from what you see, but from what you imagine.
