Step into the shadowed drawing room of an aristocratic estate where secrets writhe beneath the surface like serpents in tall grass. In this chilling installment of CBS Radio Mystery Theater, a wealthy family gathers to read the will of their recently departed patriarch—only to discover that someone among them harbors a murderous grudge. As night falls and the house becomes an island of suspicion, listeners will find themselves caught between competing confessions, hidden motives, and the gnawing sense that the killer may strike again before dawn breaks. The crackle of tension builds with each revelation, enhanced by the masterful sound design that transforms a simple room into a claustrophobic maze of danger. Who inherits the fortune, and at what cost?

CBS Radio Mystery Theater emerged in 1974 as a triumphant revival of old-time radio drama, bringing sophisticated mystery and horror storytelling back to audiences who remembered the golden age of broadcast entertainment. Though the episode "The Serpent's Tooth" carries the atmospheric hallmarks of 1940s suspense fiction, the series itself became a cultural bridge—introducing new generations to the lost art of radio drama while honoring listeners who had grown up with shows like The Shadow and Inner Sanctum. The program's commitment to tight plotting, stellar voice acting, and psychological terror rather than cheap jump scares made it a critical darling throughout its eight-year run.

Tune in now and experience the singular thrill of mystery told through sound alone—where your imagination becomes the screen and every creak of a floorboard might signal doom. "The Serpent's Tooth" awaits, and it bites deeper than you'd expect.
