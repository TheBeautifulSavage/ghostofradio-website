The Frammis

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

On a moonless October evening, a traveling salesman's car breaks down on a desolate country road, and his desperate knock on a farmhouse door sets in motion a nightmare that defies explanation. What begins as a simple request for help spirals into something far more sinister when the farmhand produces a peculiar wooden object—the titular "Frammis"—and explains, with chilling matter-of-factness, that certain things must be fed. As shadows creep across the isolated property and the salesman's rational mind struggles against the mounting evidence of something genuinely evil, the sound design of the show becomes your only guide: the creak of floorboards, the whisper of wind through broken shutters, the wet, unsettling sounds that suggest the Frammis has developed a taste for its nightly meal. The mystery deepens with each revelation, leading to a conclusion that redefines what horror truly means.

The CBS Radio Mystery Theater remains one of the finest examples of dramatic storytelling ever broadcast, delivering 1,399 episodes of terror and intrigue across its nine-year run. Premiering in 1974, the show captured the golden age of radio drama's essence while speaking to a modern audience hungry for intelligent, literary scares. "The Frammis" exemplifies the series' genius—it takes the familiar and mundane, then twists it into something that challenges our understanding of reality itself. With a stellar cast and E.G. Marshall's authoritative narration anchoring each tale, the show proved that radio drama could be every bit as sophisticated and terrifying as the great films of the era.

Tune in now and discover why generations of listeners have found their sleep disturbed by CBS Radio Mystery Theater. "The Frammis" awaits in the dark—and it's hungry.
