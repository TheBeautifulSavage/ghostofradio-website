Stitch In Time

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

In this chilling episode of CBS Radio Mystery Theater, listeners are drawn into the shadowy world of a small-town tailor shop where a seemingly innocent garment holds secrets that refuse to stay buried. When a mysterious bolt of fabric arrives at the shop with no return address, the proprietor begins to notice something profoundly unsettling: the threads seem to move of their own accord, and those who wear clothing sewn from the mysterious cloth experience visions of events they could not possibly have witnessed. As the tension builds, our protagonist must unravel the supernatural mystery before the garment claims its next victim. The production crackles with period authenticity, from the ambient sounds of the sewing machine's rhythmic clacking to the whispered warnings of suspicious neighbors, creating an atmosphere of creeping dread that tightens around the listener like a well-fitted seam.

CBS Radio Mystery Theater stands as one of the golden age's final and finest achievements, reviving the traditions of suspenseful storytelling during an era when television threatened to silence the radio drama entirely. Running from 1974 to 1982, the show demonstrated that audiences still craved the intimate terror that only radio could deliver—where the mind's eye conjures horrors far more effective than any visual medium. "Stitch In Time" exemplifies the show's mastery of taking the mundane and transforming it into nightmare fuel, turning everyday objects into vessels of supernatural menace through clever writing and superb voice acting.

If you're seeking a story that will keep you glancing nervously at your sewing basket, "Stitch In Time" awaits. Tune in and discover why millions of listeners made CBS Radio Mystery Theater an essential part of their evenings, proving that some of the most terrifying stories are those we hear in the dark.
