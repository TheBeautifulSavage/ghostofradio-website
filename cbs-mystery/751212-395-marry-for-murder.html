As the clock strikes midnight and the organ music swells with sinister intent, listeners are drawn into the shadowy world of a marriage built on deception and desire. In "Marry For Murder," a young woman accepts a proposal from a mysterious suitor whose affections seem almost too perfect, too calculated to be genuine. But is he a devoted husband-to-be, or does he harbor darker intentions beneath his charming exterior? As the wedding day approaches, whispered conversations and suspicious encounters begin to unravel the truth—and the bride discovers that the man she's promised to marry may have promised the same vows to others. The tension mounts as she races against time to uncover his terrible secret before she walks down the aisle toward a fate far more sinister than she ever imagined. With stellar performances from the ensemble cast and masterful sound design that transforms an ordinary parlor into a prison of dread, this episode captures everything that made CBS Radio Mystery Theater a beloved institution of American broadcasting.

Running from 1974 to 1982, the CBS Radio Mystery Theater revived the golden age of radio drama at a time when television seemed to have claimed victory over the medium. Yet millions of devoted listeners tuned in nightly, proving that the human imagination, guided by expert storytelling and atmospheric sound, remains the most powerful tool in entertainment. "Marry For Murder" exemplifies the show's genius for transforming intimate human drama into tales of psychological terror—where the greatest monsters are not lurking in the shadows, but sleeping beside you.

Don't miss this classic descent into romantic nightmare. Settle into your favorite chair, dim the lights, and let the words and sounds transport you to a world where true love and true evil wear remarkably similar masks.
