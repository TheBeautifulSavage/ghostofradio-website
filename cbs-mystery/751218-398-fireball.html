On a moonless night in a remote mountain town, a peculiar phenomenon begins to terrorize the locals—a mysterious ball of fire that streaks across the sky, leaving destruction and madness in its wake. When the inexplicable inferno claims its first victim, suspicion falls upon a drifter newly arrived in town, yet the evidence becomes increasingly strange and supernatural. As investigators close in, listeners will find themselves suspended in an atmosphere thick with dread and paranoia, where rational explanations crumble against mounting supernatural evidence. The masterful sound design—crackling flames, wind howling through mountain passes, and the hushed whispers of frightened townsfolk—pulls you directly into the heart of this otherworldly terror. What is the fireball? A weapon? A curse? A creature of pure elemental fury? The answer lies waiting in the shadows.

The CBS Radio Mystery Theater stands as a testament to radio drama's remarkable resurgence in the 1970s, proving that this golden-age medium could captivate audiences even in the television era. This particular episode exemplifies the show's genius for blending science fiction and horror elements with deeply human psychology. Airing during the show's peak years, "Fireball" showcases the production team's commitment to atmospheric storytelling and the kind of imaginative worldbuilding that required listeners to paint their own vivid mental pictures—far more powerful than any visual could achieve.

Don't miss this gripping tale of inexplicable terror and small-town suspicion. Dim the lights, settle into your seat, and prepare yourself for an evening of genuine mystery and dread.
