License To Kill

CBS Radio Mystery Theater

When a man receives an official document granting him permission to commit murder with complete legal immunity, he believes he's stumbled upon the ultimate escape from justice. But as our protagonist eagerly sets about planning the perfect crime—secure in the knowledge that the law itself has blessed his intentions—he discovers that some documents carry consequences far beyond the courtroom. In this chilling episode, the fog rolls thick with paranoia and moral corruption as the hunter becomes the hunted, and a killer finds himself stalked by forces he never anticipated. Every creak in the darkness, every mysterious phone call, every shadow cast across a wall becomes a threat. The audience is left to wonder: is someone seeking revenge, or has the universe itself decided to balance the scales? The tension builds relentlessly toward a finale that will leave listeners questioning whether the document was blessing or curse.

License To Kill* represents the CBS Radio Mystery Theater at its psychological peak during the show's remarkable nine-year run. Created in 1974 as a revival of classic radio drama traditions, the series married Golden Age storytelling with contemporary sensibilities, proving that mystery and suspense could thrive long after television had seemingly retired radio to the background. Each episode was meticulously crafted with sound design that could conjure entire worlds—a claustrophobic office, a windswept estate, a courthouse echoing with whispered secrets—all through the simple power of voice, music, and perfectly-timed sound effects.

Don't miss this masterclass in dramatic tension and moral ambiguity. Tune in to *License To Kill* and rediscover why millions of Americans once gathered around their radios, completely transported into worlds of shadow and suspense. Some stories demand to be heard.
