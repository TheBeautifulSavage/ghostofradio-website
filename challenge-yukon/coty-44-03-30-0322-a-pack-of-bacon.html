# A Pack of Bacon

Picture yourself huddled close to your radio on a cold winter's evening, the hum of the dial settling on COTY's frequency as the familiar blast of the "Sergeant Preston of the Yukon" theme swells through your home. In this gripping 1940s installment, *A Pack of Bacon*, you'll find yourself thrust into the frozen wilderness alongside Sergeant Preston and his magnificent lead dog, King, as they pursue a desperate criminal scheme unfolding across the unforgiving Yukon landscape. What begins as a routine patrol transforms into a deadly game of survival when a seemingly innocent shipment of bacon becomes the centerpiece of a cunning deception. With each crackle of the fire and distant howl of wolves echoing through the speakers, you'll experience the mounting tension as Preston pieces together clues scattered across the frozen frontier, racing against time and the brutal northern elements to bring justice to this remote corner of Canada.

For nearly two decades, *Challenge of the Yukon* captivated millions of American listeners with its perfect blend of adventure, mystery, and the romantic allure of the Canadian frontier. The show's meticulous attention to realistic detail—from the authentic Arctic settings to the carefully researched police procedures of the Royal Canadian Mounted Police—made each episode feel like a genuine dispatch from the edge of civilization. Sergeant Preston became an iconic figure of American radio, embodying the virtues of courage, intelligence, and unwavering justice, while King the Wonder Dog proved that loyalty and instinct were just as vital to survival as any human virtue. These episodes represent golden-age radio at its finest: storytelling that transported listeners to distant worlds while celebrating timeless ideals.

Don't miss your chance to experience this forgotten adventure. Tune in now and discover why audiences across the continent made Sergeant Preston and King part of their weekly ritual—a thrilling escape into a world where good prevails and the wilderness itself becomes both antagonist and character.
