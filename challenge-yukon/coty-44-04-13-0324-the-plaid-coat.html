(0324) The Plaid Coat

· GHOST OF RADIO ·

As the haunting strains of "Specific Gravity" fade and the crackle of the northern wilderness fills your parlor, Sergeant Preston and his magnificent dog King mount their dog sled across the frozen expanses of the Yukon Territory. Tonight, a simple plaid coat becomes the thread of a mystery that could condemn an innocent man—or expose a calculated murderer hiding behind a frontier veneer. The snow swirls thick and treacherous as Preston questions hardened prospectors and townspeople, his steady voice cutting through the arctic wind. With only the coat as evidence and danger lurking at every cabin and trading post, listeners will find themselves gripping their radio dials, wondering whether justice can prevail when the truth lies buried beneath layers of deception and distance.

Challenge of the Yukon captured American imaginations throughout the late 1930s and 1940s with its irresistible blend of action, integrity, and untamed wilderness. Created during an era when audiences hungered for heroes of unshakeable moral fiber, Sergeant Preston embodied the best of frontier justice—clever, compassionate, and uncompromising. The show's popularity was so immense that it spawned merchandise, a television series in the 1950s, and an indelible place in broadcasting history. Each episode, meticulously scripted and performed before a live audience, transported listeners north to a realm where good and evil were starkly drawn against the backdrop of savage, beautiful nature.

Tune in now to "The Plaid Coat" and discover why millions of devoted listeners made this program an essential part of their evening ritual. Whether you're a devoted fan revisiting a cherished memory or a newcomer to Preston's adventures, this episode exemplifies everything that made old-time radio golden.
