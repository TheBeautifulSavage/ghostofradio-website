# Coty 45 05 29 — Ambush

Deep in the frozen wilderness where the northern lights dance across endless snow, Sergeant Preston and his trusty husky King find themselves caught in a deadly trap. A ruthless gang of claim jumpers has set their sights on a valuable gold strike, and they're willing to do anything—including ambush a Mounted Police officer—to keep their secret hidden. As Preston investigates strange activity near a remote mining camp, the tension crackles like ice breaking on the Yukon River. Will he discover the trap before it's sprung? Can King's keen senses alert his master to danger lurking in the white silence? This episode delivers everything listeners crave: the howl of the wilderness, quick thinking under pressure, and the unwavering bond between man and dog against impossible odds.

*Challenge of the Yukon* became a beloved institution precisely because it captured the romance of frontier justice with authenticity and heart. Airing throughout the Golden Age of radio, the series followed the real code of the Royal Canadian Mounted Police, blending historical accuracy with serialized adventure that kept audiences returning night after night. Sergeant Preston, brought to vivid life by various actors across the show's seventeen-year run, embodied an ideal: honest, courageous, and utterly incorruptible. By the 1940s, when this episode aired, the show had perfected its formula, and King the dog had become as much a character as Preston himself—a reminder that heroism comes in many forms.

Don't miss this classic encounter between law and lawlessness in Canada's untamed north. Tune in and let your imagination transport you to those frozen frontiers where right triumphs over might, and loyalty never falters.
