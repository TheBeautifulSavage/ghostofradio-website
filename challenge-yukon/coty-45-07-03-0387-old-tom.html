# Old Tom

As the northern lights flicker across the frozen Yukon wilderness and King, Sergeant Preston's loyal husky, lets out a mournful howl, listeners are transported once again to the remote outposts of Canada's unforgiving frontier. In "Old Tom," a grizzled prospector stumbles into the trading post with a cryptic story of a lost partner and a fortune in gold dust that has mysteriously vanished into the snow. What begins as a simple missing-persons inquiry quickly spirals into a tense cat-and-mouse game across treacherous ice fields, where nothing is quite as it seems and every shadow conceals potential danger. With King leading the way and Preston's steady determination as their guide, the stakes climb higher as dark secrets from the past begin to surface—secrets that someone in this harsh land would kill to keep buried.

*Sergeant Preston of the Yukon* captured American imaginations for nearly two decades by combining the rugged appeal of the frontier with the orderly justice of the Mounted Police. Premiering in 1938 and running through the 1950s, the show became one of radio's most beloved adventure serials, earning its place alongside classics like *The Lone Ranger* and *Tales of the Texas Rangers*. The program's authentic Yukon setting, meticulous sound design—from howling winds to crackling fires—and Preston's unwavering moral code created an immersive escape from Depression-era anxieties. Episodes like "Old Tom" showcased the show's signature blend of mystery, outdoor adventure, and character-driven drama that kept listeners tuning in night after night.

Step into the frozen reaches of the North and experience the tension, intrigue, and adventure that made this Golden Age classic essential listening. Old Tom's secret awaits.
