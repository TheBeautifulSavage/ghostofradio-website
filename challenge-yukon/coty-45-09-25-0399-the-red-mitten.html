# The Red Mitten

As the opening notes of "Donna Diana" crackle through your speaker, you're transported to the frozen vastness of the Yukon Territory, where Sergeant Preston of the Royal Canadian Mounted Police faces a mystery as crimson as fresh blood on snow. When a valuable red mitten goes missing from a remote trading post, nothing is quite as it seems—and the investigation will lead Preston and his loyal sled dog King through treacherous mountain passes and into the hearts of desperate men. This episode captures everything that made the series a national obsession: the authentic danger of the frontier, the moral clarity of a man devoted to justice, and the kind of suspenseful storytelling that had families huddled around their radios, breathless until the final resolution.

*Challenge of the Yukon* (later retitled *Sergeant Preston of the Yukon*) became a phenomenon during radio's golden age precisely because it blended historical authenticity with pure adventure fantasy. Produced during the late 1930s and 1940s, the show tapped into America's enduring romance with the frontier—even as the nation faced the realities of industrialization and global conflict. The RCMP setting provided a veneer of international legitimacy, while the Yukon offered a landscape that felt genuinely exotic to American listeners. Each episode balanced mystery, action, and character-driven drama in a formula that proved irresistible, running for seventeen years and spawning a television series, comic strips, and merchandise.

Tune in to "The Red Mitten" and discover why millions of listeners made Sergeant Preston their appointment listening. In an era before television, this was cinematic entertainment delivered through pure storytelling—let your imagination do the heavy lifting as Preston and King race toward justice across an unforgiving wilderness.
