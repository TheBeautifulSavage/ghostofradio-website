# The Pup That Ate Too Much

In the frozen vastness of the Canadian north, Sergeant Preston and his faithful King face an unexpected crisis when the legendary sled dog's insatiable appetite threatens to jeopardize their most critical mission yet. What begins as an amusing domestic problem in the barracks quickly escalates into genuine danger as the resourceful Mountie must puzzle out whether simple gluttony or something far more sinister is behind King's behavior. As snowstorms gather and the trail beckons, Preston races against time to restore his noble companion to health while pursuing a cunning fugitive through treacherous mountain passes. The bond between man and dog—that essential cornerstone of survival in the Yukon—hangs in the balance, and listeners will grip their radio dials as Preston employs both his legendary lawman's cunning and his deeper understanding of the wilderness itself to save the day.

Challenge of the Yukon became a cultural phenomenon throughout the 1940s, capturing America's imagination during wartime with tales of heroism, loyalty, and frontier justice. Sergeant Preston—played with authentic gravitas and tireless dedication—embodied the idealized Canadian Mountie, while King became arguably radio's most beloved canine character, rivaling even Rin Tin Tin in the hearts of listeners. The show's writers masterfully balanced action-packed drama with surprisingly tender moments of humor and genuine emotion, proving that adventure stories need not sacrifice heart for excitement. These episodes represent a golden age when families gathered together, lights dimmed, and the static-laced airwaves transported them thousands of miles north to adventure.

Don't miss this enchanting tale that perfectly captures why audiences kept tuning in week after week. Whether you're a longtime devotee of the Yukon or discovering Sergeant Preston for the first time, this episode demonstrates why the show remained a beloved fixture on radios throughout the decade.
