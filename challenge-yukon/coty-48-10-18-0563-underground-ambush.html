# Underground Ambush

Deep beneath the frozen Yukon wilderness, Sergeant Preston of the Royal Canadian Mounted Police faces his most claustrophobic challenge yet. When a dangerous criminal escapes into an abandoned mining tunnel system to evade capture, Preston must pursue him into the pitch-black depths where every sound echoes with menace and every shadow conceals a potential ambush. The steady thrum of his sled dogs fades above as Preston descends into the earth, armed only with his wits, his service revolver, and the unwavering determination that has made him a legend throughout Canada's frozen frontier. As the tension mounts and the tunnels twist into a labyrinth of danger, listeners will find themselves trapped alongside the Sergeant in this gripping tale of survival and justice against impossible odds.

*Challenge of the Yukon* became a cultural phenomenon precisely because it captured the mythic spirit of Canada's last great wilderness with such authentic detail and genuine suspense. Premiering in 1938 and running through the 1950s, the show's meticulous attention to geography, weather, and the actual dangers faced by mounted police gave weight to even the most dramatic scenarios. Sergeant Preston—played with quiet, unflappable heroism—embodied the ideal of law and order advancing into untamed territory, while his remarkable sled dog King became as beloved to listeners as any human character on radio. Episodes like "Underground Ambush" showcase why the program earned millions of devoted fans who tuned in faithfully for each new adventure.
