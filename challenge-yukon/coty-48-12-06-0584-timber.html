# Coty 48 12 06 — "Timber"

High in the frozen territories where the snow lies thick upon the spruce forests, Sergeant Preston of the Yukon faces a mystery as dark and tangled as the timber itself. When a ruthless lumber baron's camp falls silent in the dead of winter, Preston must navigate treacherous logging operations, suspicious characters, and the unforgiving wilderness to uncover what happened in the isolated woods. With only his loyal dog King at his side and his wits to guide him, the sergeant pursues leads through blinding snowstorms and into the shadowy bunkhouses where rough men keep dangerous secrets. The tension builds as Preston closes in on the truth—but in the Yukon, justice operates under its own harsh rules, and not everyone wants the facts to come to light.

Since its debut in 1938, *Challenge of the Yukon* captured the imaginations of millions of listeners seeking genuine adventure in an age of uncertainty. The show's meticulous attention to authentic frontier detail, combined with Paul Sutherland's commanding performance as Preston and the stirring theme music that opened each episode, established it as a genuine classic of adventure radio. The series drew inspiration from real RCMP history and the gold rush era, grounding its thrilling narratives in a world listeners recognized as plausible and vivid. "Timber" exemplifies the show's formula at its finest—a compelling mystery nested within the broader struggle of law and order in Canada's untamed North.

If you crave the crackle of authentic adventure radio, the snap of frontier justice, and stories that transport you to another era, tune in to this gripping episode. *Challenge of the Yukon* awaits—where the law of the North prevails and Sergeant Preston always gets his man.
