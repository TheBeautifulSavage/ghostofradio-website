Challenge of the Yukon / Sergeant Preston ABC/Mutual · December 10, 1948

(0586) The Trap In Cabin Four

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

Picture this: a howling Arctic blizzard batters the windows of a remote trading post while Sergeant Preston and his loyal sled dog King stand accused of a crime they didn't commit. "The Trap In Cabin Four" plunges listeners into a claustrophobic mystery where trust dissolves like snow in spring, and every shadow cast by the flickering oil lamp could conceal a murderer. As the temperature drops and tempers rise, Preston must unravel a tangled web of deception and hidden motives before the guilty party strikes again—or before the accusers lynch an innocent man. This is vintage Challenge of the Yukon at its finest: frontier justice hanging by a thread, danger closing in from both man and nature, and our hero's wit and integrity his only weapons.

For fifteen thrilling years, Sergeant Preston of the Yukon captured the imagination of American families huddled around their radios, offering escape to the frozen frontier during depression and war. These weren't simple good-versus-evil adventures—they were complex tales of human nature tested by isolation and hardship, where the Canadian North served as both setting and character. The show's success hinged on its authenticity; creator George W. Trendle grounded each episode in genuine accounts of Mounted Police work, lending credibility to Preston's moral authority. King, the magnificent sled dog, became as beloved as any co-star in broadcasting, his loyal presence a reminder that civilization's restraints slip easily in the wilderness.

Join Sergeant Preston in Cabin Four, where an innocent man faces the mob, a mystery awaits solving, and King's instincts may prove sharper than any human suspicion. It's adventure, intrigue, and the unshakeable code of the North—waiting for you in the archives of radio's golden age.
