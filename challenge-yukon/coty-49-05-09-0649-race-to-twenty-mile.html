# Race to Twenty Mile

Picture yourself huddled close to the radio on a winter's night as the immortal bugle call of the Royal Canadian Mounted Police pierces the static, followed by that unforgettable cry: "King, you are a beautiful dog!" In this thrilling installment, Sergeant Preston and his magnificent husky King must race across the frozen Yukon wilderness to Twenty Mile in a desperate bid to prevent a criminal from escaping into the vast Canadian frontier. With temperatures plummeting and danger lurking in every snowdrift, Preston must push both himself and his faithful companion to their absolute limits. Every dog team, every treacherous trail, and every moment counts as the Sergeant faces one of his most perilous challenges yet—a breathless pursuit that will test not only his courage and cunning but his unwavering dedication to the law and order of the frozen North.

What made *Challenge of the Yukon* endure for nearly two decades was its perfect blend of authentic frontier danger and the unshakeable moral certainty of its hero. Drawing from real Mounted Police legends and the actual geography of the gold rush territories, the show transported millions of listeners to a world where justice was personal, heroism was earned through sacrifice, and the bond between man and dog transcended the everyday. Preston became an icon of American radio adventure—a lawman whose pursuit of criminals often seemed secondary to his pursuit of doing what was right, regardless of the cost.

If you've never experienced the peculiar thrill of old-time radio adventure, or if you're a devoted fan seeking a classic episode you may have missed, "Race to Twenty Mile" remains an exemplary adventure: tense, well-crafted, and enduringly entertaining. Tune in and rediscover why audiences across North America made this program appointment listening for seventeen years.
