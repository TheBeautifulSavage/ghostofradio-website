# The Uphill Sled

Picture yourself huddled near the radio on a dark evening as Sergeant Preston of the Yukon cracks his whip and cries "On, King, on!"—the galloping hooves of his magnificent husky blend with the howling Arctic wind as our stalwart Mountie faces his most grueling challenge yet. In "The Uphill Sled," Preston must navigate a treacherous mountain pass during the brutal Yukon winter, where the snow piles higher than a man is tall and every breath feels like inhaling daggers of ice. An innocent family's survival hangs in the balance, and only Preston's unwavering courage, his faithful companion King, and the sheer force of his will to uphold justice can see them through. Listeners will find themselves gripping their armrests as natural obstacles prove as formidable as any outlaw, and Preston discovers that sometimes the greatest enemy isn't man—it's the unforgiving wilderness itself.

*Challenge of the Yukon*, which aired under the more familiar title *Sergeant Preston* during much of its remarkable run from 1938 to 1955, became one of radio's most enduring adventure series, captivating millions of North American listeners with tales of the Canadian frontier. Unlike typical crime dramas of the era, the show's genius lay in pitting its hero not just against villainous criminals but against the raw, indifferent power of nature itself. This particular episode exemplifies the show's trademark blend of physical danger and moral clarity—Preston never compromises his duty, and the wilderness becomes both obstacle and teacher.

Don't miss this thrilling chapter in radio's golden age. Tune in to *The Uphill Sled* and experience why listeners made *Sergeant Preston* an appointment with adventure for nearly two decades.
