# Ambush Near Selkirk

The howling wind cuts across the frozen wilderness as Sergeant Preston and his faithful husky King approach the settlement near Selkirk, unaware that danger lurks behind every snow-laden pine. In this thrilling episode, our hero rides directly into a carefully laid trap—a scheme born from desperation and vengeance by men who have lost everything to the harsh northern frontier. As Preston's loyal companion senses the peril first, listeners will find themselves gripping their radio dials, wondering if even the Royal Canadian Mounted Police's finest can escape the crossfire of desperate fugitives who have nothing left to lose. The tension mounts with every crackle of the transmitter, every distant gunshot echoing across the Yukon, building to a climax that will leave you breathless.

*Challenge of the Yukon* commanded the imaginations of millions during its golden age, serving as the definitive adventure serial for Americans hungry to escape the mundane realities of wartime life. The program's meticulous attention to authentic Canadian geography and Mountie procedure lent credibility to its most outlandish plots, while announcer Dick Wesson's gravelly narration became the voice of rugged frontier justice itself. This particular episode exemplifies the show's mastery of sustained suspense—building its narrative with careful pacing, genuine character stakes, and the ever-present threat of the unforgiving wilderness itself.

Settle in with a warm drink and prepare yourself for forty minutes of pure adventure radio. *Ambush Near Selkirk* awaits, where heroism faces its greatest test and the frozen north demands everything its visitors possess. This is classic radio drama at its finest—don't miss it.
