# Bear Trap

Picture the vast, unforgiving wilderness of Canada's frozen north—where the howling wind cuts through dense pine forests and danger lurks at every turn. In this gripping episode, Sergeant Preston and his faithful husky King must navigate a treacherous landscape where a cunning grizzly has been terrorizing remote settlements, leaving destruction and fear in its wake. But this is no ordinary hunt. The bear has been injured, making it more desperate and vicious than ever, and the sergeant must use all his wilderness expertise and quick thinking to outsmart the beast before it strikes again. As the tension mounts and the trap is carefully laid, listeners will find themselves breathless with anticipation—wondering if Preston's courage and King's uncanny instincts will be enough to vanquish this primal threat.

*Challenge of the Yukon* stands as one of radio's most enduring adventure serials, captivating audiences for nearly two decades with tales of law and order in Canada's untamed frontier. Beginning in 1938, the show became a cultural phenomenon, blending authentic wilderness atmosphere with compelling moral storytelling—each episode delivered a self-contained adventure while building a larger narrative of Sergeant Preston's tireless commitment to justice. The show's remarkable staying power came from its perfect recipe: a hero of unwavering principle, a canine sidekick whose loyalty became legendary, and scripts that honored both the rugged landscape and the human spirit's triumph over adversity.

Don't miss this thrilling encounter with danger in the frozen wilderness. Tune in to experience why millions of listeners made *Challenge of the Yukon* an essential part of their evenings, and discover the adventure that made Sergeant Preston a timeless radio hero.
