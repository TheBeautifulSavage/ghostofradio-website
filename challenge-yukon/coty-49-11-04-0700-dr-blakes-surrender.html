Challenge of the Yukon / Sergeant Preston ABC/Mutual · November 4, 1949

(0700) Dr Blake's Surrender

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

The frozen wastes of the Canadian Yukon Territory crackle with tension as Sergeant Preston and King, his magnificent husky, close in on Dr. Blake—a fugitive whose crimes have cast a long shadow across the north. In this taut episode, listeners will experience the mounting dread as pursuit becomes inescapable; Blake faces the brutal choice between surrender and the merciless wilderness. With the sergeant drawing nearer and winter's teeth snapping at every heel, the drama unfolds across a landscape of unforgiving ice and isolation. You'll hear the howling winds, the sled dogs' urgent barking, and Preston's steady, determined voice as justice tracks its quarry through endless white expanses. This is storytelling where geography itself becomes a character—where the Yukon's indifference to human suffering makes every decision matter, every moment count.

Challenge of the Yukon, known to millions as Sergeant Preston of the Yukon, stands as one of radio's most enduring adventure series, captivating audiences from 1938 through the 1950s with its authentic frontier atmosphere and moral clarity. Created against the backdrop of Depression-era radio, the show offered listeners an escape into a world of clear good and evil, where law and order triumphed and nature tested the human spirit. The show's meticulous attention to detail—accurate depictions of Mounted Police procedures, Yukon geography, and sled dog culture—gave it legitimacy that set it apart from routine westerns. King the husky became as beloved as Preston himself, a sidekick whose instincts often proved as valuable as the sergeant's cunning.

Don your parka and step into the howling darkness of the Yukon. "Dr. Blake's Surrender" awaits—a masterclass in radio suspense where one man's reckoning echoes across the frozen north.
