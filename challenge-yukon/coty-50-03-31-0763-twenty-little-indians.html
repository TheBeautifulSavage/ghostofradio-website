# Challenge of the Yukon: "Twenty Little Indians"

The howling Arctic winds cut through the frozen silence of the Canadian frontier as Sergeant Preston of the Mounted Police faces his most chilling mystery yet. When a remote trading post falls mysteriously silent, Preston and his faithful husky King must navigate a treacherous landscape to uncover the truth behind an unsettling disappearance. This episode crackles with the tension of isolation and danger—listeners will find themselves gripped by the sergeant's determined investigation as clues unfold with deliberate, mounting dread. The sparse sound design captures every footstep in the snow, every creak of timber, every distant wolf cry, drawing audiences deep into the unforgiving Yukon wilderness where civilization feels impossibly far away.

*Challenge of the Yukon* became a cultural phenomenon precisely because it captured America's fascination with adventure, law and order, and the romantic frontier during a period when modern life felt increasingly routine and confined. Premiering in 1938 during the Depression, the show offered listeners weekly escapes into exotic northern territories while celebrating the virtues of duty and justice embodied by its stalwart protagonist. Sergeant Preston's unwavering moral compass and innovative detective work made him a hero for audiences seeking certainty in an uncertain world. The program's popularity spawned merchandise, comic books, and eventually a television series, cementing Preston's place in the pantheon of beloved radio characters.

Tune in to experience the golden age of radio drama at its finest. "Twenty Little Indians" showcases everything that made *Challenge of the Yukon* essential listening for millions of families huddled around their receivers. The mystery awaits—can you solve it before Preston does?
