# Six Gun Clue

As the northern lights dance across the frozen Yukon wilderness, Sergeant Preston of the Royal Canadian Mounted Police faces his most perplexing case yet. A murder has shattered the fragile peace of a remote trading post, and the only evidence is a cryptic message etched into a spent rifle cartridge. With his faithful wolf-dog King at his side and the vast, unforgiving tundra stretching endlessly in all directions, Preston must navigate treacherous snow passes, interrogate desperate frontier characters, and unravel a conspiracy that reaches far beyond the isolated settlement. The tension mounts as clues point in conflicting directions, each suspect harboring dark secrets that could explain the tragedy—but which thread, when pulled, will unravel the truth before the killer strikes again?

*Challenge of the Yukon* captured the imagination of millions during radio's golden age by offering something genuinely different from the crowded western landscape: an authentically Canadian hero operating under the rule of law, backed by the authority of the Mounted Police. Rather than the gunslinger mythology dominating American radio, Sergeant Preston embodied justice tempered with compassion, intelligence, and respect for the harsh land itself. The show's 1940s episodes represent the height of the series' creative power, when writers had perfected the balance between action-packed adventure and atmospheric storytelling, transforming each episode into a journey through an exotic, dangerous world that felt utterly real to listeners huddled around their receivers.

Don't miss "Six Gun Clue"—a masterclass in mystery and suspense from radio's most thrilling adventure series. Tune in and discover why audiences made Preston and King household names.
