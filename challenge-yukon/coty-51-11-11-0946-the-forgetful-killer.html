# The Forgetful Killer

Picture yourself huddled near the warm glow of your radio on a crisp November evening, when Sergeant Preston of the Royal Canadian Mounted Police crackles to life through your speaker. In "The Forgetful Killer," a cunning fugitive has escaped into the frozen Yukon wilderness with murder on his conscience and amnesia clouding his mind—a dangerous combination that threatens an entire remote settlement. As Preston and his loyal dog King brave blizzards and treacherous terrain, they must piece together the fragmented memories of a man who cannot remember his own crime. The tension mounts with each discovery, each false lead, each moment when the killer draws closer to striking again, all while the relentless Canadian winter tightens its grip on friend and foe alike.

What made *Challenge of the Yukon* endure for seventeen glorious years was precisely this: the marriage of authentic frontier adventure with the intimate drama of human conflict, all delivered with impeccable production values and unforgettable sound design. Created when radio was the dominant entertainment medium, the show benefited from a stellar cast, particularly the commanding presence of Sergeant Preston himself, while the crackling gunshots, howling winds, and King's distinctive barks transported listeners to a world that felt utterly real. This 1940s episode represents the show at its peak, when writers had perfected the formula of moral clarity wrapped in genuine suspense.

Tune in now to experience radio's golden age—where imagination and artistry combined to create unforgettable adventure. "The Forgetful Killer" awaits, with danger lurking in every shadow cast by the northern lights.
