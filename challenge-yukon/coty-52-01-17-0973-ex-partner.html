Picture yourself huddled close to the radio on a bitter winter's night, the crackling of the hearth mixing with the opening strains of that unforgettable theme—Due north, up in the Yukon! Sergeant Preston and his magnificent dog King are back on the case, but this time the past has come calling with a vengeance. An old companion from Preston's early days surfaces in the frozen wilderness, bringing with him a tangle of secrets, betrayal, and unfinished business that threatens to shatter everything Preston has built as the Dominion's most trusted lawman. With danger lurking behind every snowdrift and trust hanging by a thread as thin as ice in spring, listeners will be gripped by moral questions as complex as the Yukon landscape itself: Can a man truly escape his past? And what is the price of loyalty when survival is at stake?

From 1938 through the mid-1950s, Challenge of the Yukon captivated millions with its potent blend of adventure, justice, and the romance of the frozen North. The show, starring Burt Lytell as Preston, became the template for the mounted lawman archetype that would dominate American imagination. Each episode's thundering hoofbeats and King's faithful bark weren't mere sound effects—they were the heartbeat of a nation hungry for tales of honor and order on the frontier. The writing was sharp, the drama authentic, and the atmosphere so immersive that listeners forgot their own living rooms entirely.

This particular episode, "Ex Partner," showcases everything that made the series legendary. Don't miss your chance to experience radio drama at its finest—tune in and discover why Sergeant Preston and King remain unforgettable icons of the Golden Age.
