# Death By Proxy

The frozen wastes of the Canadian north have never felt more treacherous than when a killer operates from the shadows, striking through unsuspecting hands. In this gripping episode of *Challenge of the Yukon*, Sergeant Preston faces his most diabolical mystery yet: a series of deaths with no apparent murderer. As the snow piles high and the temperature plummets, Preston must untangle a web of proxy murder—where an innocent party becomes an unwitting instrument of death itself. The steady voice of Preston crackles through the static, narrating his dogged pursuit of justice, while his faithful dog King stands ready to track a scent that leads through dangerous moral terrain. Listeners will find themselves caught between suspense and the haunting question: can one be guilty of murder without ever intending harm?

*Challenge of the Yukon* stood as one of radio's most enduring adventure serials, captivating audiences for seventeen years with tales of frontier justice and wilderness survival. Premiering in 1938, the show became a cultural phenomenon, cementing Sergeant Preston as the quintessential lawman of the North. The program thrived on authenticity—incorporating real Yukon geography, authentic police procedures, and the omnipresent threat of nature itself. Episodes like "Death By Proxy" showcased the show's sophisticated storytelling, moving beyond simple shoot-outs to explore complex moral dilemmas. The serial format kept listeners returning week after week, eager to discover how Preston would solve the seemingly impossible.

Don your heaviest coat and settle into the crackling warmth of your radio speaker. *Challenge of the Yukon* awaits—where justice is as unforgiving as the Yukon itself, and no mystery is too dark for Sergeant Preston and King to uncover. Tune in now for "Death By Proxy" and discover why this timeless adventure captivated a nation.
