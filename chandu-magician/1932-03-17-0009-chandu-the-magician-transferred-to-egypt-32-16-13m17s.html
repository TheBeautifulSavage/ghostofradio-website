Picture yourself huddled around the radio on a winter evening in 1932, the amber glow of the dial your only light. As the mysterious Eastern theme music swells and fades, you hear the voice of announcer Don Dailey ushering you back into the shadowy world of Chandu—and this time, the master magician finds himself whisked away to the ancient sands of Egypt. What sinister forces have orchestrated his transfer to that land of pharaohs and forgotten secrets? With each crackle of static and expertly timed sound effect, danger seems to materialize from the darkness. Frank Chandler's quick wit and supernatural abilities will be tested as exotic locations materialize in your imagination: bustling Cairo bazaars, moonlit tombs, and laboratories hidden beneath shifting dunes. The stakes have never felt higher as Chandu navigates a web of intrigue that reaches across continents.

For thirteen minutes, listeners were transported far beyond their living rooms—a feat that made Chandu the Magician one of radio's most beloved fantasy adventures during the early 1930s. Created by Harry A. Earnshaw and Wyllis Cooper, the show pioneered the serialized adventure format while maintaining an aura of plausibility through Chandu's pseudo-scientific approach to magic. This episode, broadcast early in the original ABC run, demonstrates why audiences tuned in religiously, eager to follow Chandu's exploits through exotic locales and increasingly elaborate mysteries.

Don't miss this gateway into radio's golden age. Whether you're a longtime Chandu devotee seeking to revisit these treasured broadcasts or a newcomer curious about the origins of adventure radio, this episode captures the essence of 1930s escapism at its finest. Turn down the lights, tune in, and let your imagination do what no screen ever could.
