Broadcast during the golden age of radio adventure, Chandu the Magician captured the American imagination with its intoxicating blend of Harry Houdini-inspired illusions and serialized mystery plotting. This 1932 episode represents the show at its genesis, when audiences were still discovering the character's mythology and the magic system that would define the series for sixteen years. The program's success on ABC and the Mutual network demonstrated radio's unique power to transform the ordinary living room into an exotic tableau of wonder and danger, where a character's abilities existed somewhere between stage magic and genuine supernatural intervention.
