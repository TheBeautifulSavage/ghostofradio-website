Step into the exotic mystery of the East as Chandu the Magician faces a critical turning point in his battle against the forces of darkness. In this thrilling 1932 episode, the stage is set for an unexpected alliance when Abdallah—a figure shrouded in intrigue—crosses paths with our hero. As the eerie organ music swells and sound effects transport you to shadowy temples and moonlit streets, listeners will discover whether this newcomer can be trusted or if he represents a sinister trap designed to ensnare the world's greatest magician. The tension crackles through your radio speaker as Chandu must rely on both his supernatural gifts and his wits to navigate this dangerous new development.

Chandu the Magician stands as one of broadcasting's most imaginative achievements, capturing America's golden age fascination with the mystical and the mysterious. Premiering in 1932 and enjoying a remarkable sixteen-year run, the show pioneered the fantasy-adventure serial format, predating later superhero programs by nearly a decade. With its evocative sound design, atmospheric scripts, and the compelling presence of its cast, Chandu offered Depression-era listeners an escape into a world where magic was real and one man could challenge ancient evil. This early episode, recorded just months into the show's debut season, represents the show at its most vibrant and inventive.
