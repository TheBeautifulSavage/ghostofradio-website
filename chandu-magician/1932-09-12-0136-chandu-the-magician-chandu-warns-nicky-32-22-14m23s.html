Picture yourself huddled around the radio on a crisp evening in 1932, the amber glow of the dial your only light as an otherworldly organ swells and a hypnotic voice announces the master of the mystic arts. In this episode, the mysterious Chandu discovers that danger lurks closer than anyone suspects—young Nicky, the street urchin who has become his unlikely protégé, is being hunted by forces that even Chandu's supernatural abilities may struggle to overcome. What warning will the magician deliver? Will his arcane knowledge and Eastern mysticism prove sufficient against enemies both mundane and sinister? The tension builds as Chandu races against time, his measured tones cutting through crackling static to reveal a plot that threatens everything he holds dear.

Chandu the Magician emerged during radio's golden age as one of the most inventive fantasy adventures ever broadcast, captivating millions with tales of a white magician who wielded supernatural powers to combat evil across exotic locales. Frank Chandler—Chandu—represented something uniquely American: a dashing, educated hero steeped in Eastern philosophy and occult wisdom, battling shadowy criminal organizations and supernatural threats. The show's meticulous sound design, elaborate organ scores, and imaginative storytelling set it apart from the straightforward detective and adventure programs flooding the airwaves, creating immersive worlds that inspired listeners to believe that mystery and magic still existed in the modern age.
