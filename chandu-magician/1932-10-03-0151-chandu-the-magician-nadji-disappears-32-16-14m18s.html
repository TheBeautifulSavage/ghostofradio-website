Step into the shadowed temples of the Far East as Chandu the Magician confronts his first great mystery. When the beautiful Nadji vanishes without a trace, our hero must draw upon every mystical power at his command to pierce the veil between worlds and uncover the sinister forces at work. In this premiere broadcast, listeners will experience the crackle of suspense that defined early 1930s radio—the eerie organ music swelling beneath whispered dialogue, the breathless narration that transforms a humble studio into an exotic realm of danger and enchantment. What began as an ordinary day in Chandu's life spirals into an otherworldly ordeal, promising the kind of thrilling cliffhanger that had America glued to their radio dials.

Chandu the Magician emerged during radio's golden age as one of the medium's most ambitious fantasy programs. Premiering in 1932, the series represented a bold departure from the detective and adventure shows that dominated the airwaves, instead offering listeners a gateway to mysticism, international intrigue, and supernatural wonder. With its talented cast, innovative sound design, and Orson Welles-caliber dramatic flair, Chandu captured the era's fascination with Eastern spirituality and occult traditions while maintaining the fast-paced storytelling that kept families gathered around their receivers night after night.

This is where it all began—the inaugural episode that launched one of radio's most enduring franchises, spawning stage productions, films, and over a decade of continued broadcasts. Whether you're a devoted collector of vintage radio or discovering this classic for the first time, "Nadji Disappears" offers an unmissable glimpse into the imagination and artistry of radio's greatest era. Tune in and let Chandu transport you to a world where magic is real and mystery lurks around every corner.
