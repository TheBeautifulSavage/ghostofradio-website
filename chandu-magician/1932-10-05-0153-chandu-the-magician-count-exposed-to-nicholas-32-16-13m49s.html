Picture this: the year is 1932, and across America, families are gathering around their radios on this autumn evening. The familiar, hypnotic announcer's voice crackles through the speaker, promising mystery and supernatural thrills. In this third episode, Chandu finds himself entangled with a sinister Count whose dark intentions have attracted the attention of the mysterious Nicholas. As the plot unravels, listeners will be drawn into a world of hidden identities, magical exposés, and breathless confrontations—all conjured vividly through sound effects, dramatic pauses, and the masterful voice acting that made Chandu irresistible to millions. The tension builds as secrets are revealed and the Count's true nature is laid bare before Nicholas himself. This is radio drama at its most compelling, where the listener's imagination becomes the stage.

Chandu the Magician arrived at precisely the moment America needed escapism. Debuting in 1932 during the depths of the Great Depression, this fantasy-adventure series offered audiences a thrilling respite from economic hardship. Unlike other adventure serials of the era, Chandu blended the exotic mysticism of the Far East with genuine magical spectacle—all rendered impossible to achieve on stage or screen. The show's success spawned films, comic books, and countless imitators, but none captured the enchantment of the original broadcast quite like these early episodes. This particular installment represents Chandu at its creative peak, when the show was still fresh and audiences were discovering the character for the first time.

If you've never experienced the golden age of radio drama, this is your invitation. Tune in and let yourself be transported back nearly a century, to a time when storytelling meant everything and the human voice was enough to conjure genuine wonder. Once Chandu casts his spell, you won't want to miss a single moment.
