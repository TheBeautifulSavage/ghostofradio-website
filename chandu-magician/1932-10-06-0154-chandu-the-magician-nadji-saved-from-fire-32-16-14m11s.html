# Chandu the Magician: "Nadji Saved From Fire"

Picture yourself huddled around the crackling radio dial on an autumn evening in 1932, the copper glow of the vacuum tubes casting shadows across your living room. As the mystical theme music swells and the announcer's voice intones those immortal words—"Chandu the Magician!"—you're transported to the glamorous yet perilous world of Frank Chandler, master of the occult arts. In this episode, the stakes have never been higher: Nadji, Chandu's loyal companion, finds himself ensnared in a raging inferno, the flames consuming everything in their path. Will Chandu's supernatural powers prove sufficient to penetrate the wall of fire? Can he bend the very laws of nature itself to save his devoted friend? Only the mysterious magician's command of the unseen forces stands between Nadji and a fiery doom. Your heart races as sound effects—the roar of hungry flames, the crash of burning timbers—create an almost unbearable tension.

Chandu the Magician represents the golden age of radio adventure, when imagination reigned supreme and a skilled cast could conjure entire worlds from nothing but their voices and the sound technician's clever props. This 1932 episode was broadcast during the show's inaugural year, when the phenomenon was still fresh, when millions of Americans discovered they could travel beyond their neighborhoods through nothing more than electromagnetic waves. The series ran for sixteen years across multiple networks, spawning films, comic books, and a cultural phenomenon—proof that in the depths of economic depression, listeners craved escape and wonder.

Don't miss this rare surviving episode from Chandu's earliest broadcasts. Here is radio adventure in its purest form—where magic was real, danger was genuine, and a magician's wits were his greatest weapon. Tune in and discover why an entire nation couldn't wait to hear what Frank Chandler would accomplish next.
