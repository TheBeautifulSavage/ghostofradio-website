# Cisco Kid: "Berserk"

The desert night closes in like a fist as our beloved caballero finds himself trapped in a nightmare of his own making. When a trusted friend succumbs to fever-induced madness, Cisco must navigate treacherous terrain—both physical and moral—to save a man no longer in control of his own actions. The crackling static of the broadcast seems to mirror the feverish chaos unfolding across the airwaves as gunshots ring out, horses thunder across rocky ground, and Cisco's quick wit and quicker draw become his only allies against an unpredictable enemy. This isn't a simple tale of good versus evil; it's a study in compassion under pressure, where every decision carries weight and consequences ripple through the dusty frontier towns of the Southwest.

The Cisco Kid endured for over a decade as one of radio's most beloved adventure serials, capturing audiences' imaginations with its perfect blend of swashbuckling action and genuine human drama. By the early 1950s, when this episode aired, the show had become an institution—a reliable weekly escape for millions of listeners who tuned in to hear Duncan Renaldo's charming Spanish-accented English and the iconic galloping sound effects that announced Cisco's arrival. What set this program apart was its refusal to reduce its Mexican protagonist to mere stereotype; instead, Cisco emerged as a thoughtful, cultured hero whose intelligence matched his bravery, appealing equally to children and adults seeking quality entertainment.

If you've never experienced the particular magic of old-time radio drama, "Berserk" offers the perfect entry point—a tense, human story wrapped in the romance of the Old West. Settle into your chair, dim the lights, and let the words paint vivid pictures across your imagination. *Cisco!*
