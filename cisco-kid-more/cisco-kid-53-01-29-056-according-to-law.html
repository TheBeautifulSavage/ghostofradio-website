# According to Law

As twilight descends over the dusty borderlands between Texas and Old Mexico, The Cisco Kid finds himself ensnared in a web of frontier justice far more complicated than a simple outlaw's bullet could ever be. When a seemingly innocent traveler is found murdered near a remote mission, all evidence points toward Cisco himself—and the local magistrate, hungry for a conviction that will cement his authority, is determined to hang the notorious caballero before sundown. But Cisco knows something the law doesn't: the real killer is someone the town respects, someone with far more to lose. In this gripping tale of deception and redemption, listeners will ride alongside the Kid as he races against time to uncover the truth, all while evading capture and outwitting those who would see him swing from the gallows.

What made *The Cisco Kid* such a beloved fixture of American radio for over a decade was precisely this kind of moral complexity wrapped in genuine action and adventure. Unlike the typical good-versus-evil westerns that dominated the airwaves, the show's writers understood that the frontier was murky terrain where the law itself could be corrupt, where a fugitive might possess more honor than the authorities sworn to catch him. Cisco, as portrayed by Jackson Beck and others, was never a simple hero—he was a thinking man's outlaw, charming and clever, whose exploits served not his own enrichment but justice beyond the reach of official channels.

Don your spurs and saddle up for an evening of authentic Old West drama that crackles with tension and wit. *The Cisco Kid* awaits—where the law isn't always right, and the right man doesn't always follow the law.
