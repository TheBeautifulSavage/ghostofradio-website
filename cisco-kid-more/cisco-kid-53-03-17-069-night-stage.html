# Night Stage

When darkness falls across the desert badlands and the wind carries whispers of danger, there's only one man the honest folk trust to keep the night stage safe from banditos and brigands. In this thrilling installment, *The Cisco Kid* faces one of his most perilous adventures yet as a heavily laden stagecoach rumbles through the treacherous mountain passes after sundown. Somewhere in the shadows, outlaws plot to intercept the precious cargo—and it's up to Cisco's quick wits, faster draw, and noble heart to outwit them. Will his faithful companion Pancho keep up with the action? What secrets does the mysterious passenger aboard the stage harbor? Tune in as the hoofbeats grow louder and the tension tightens with every passing mile.

Throughout the 1940s and into the 1950s, *The Cisco Kid* captured America's imagination as one of radio's most beloved adventure serials. Created from O. Henry's original literary characters, this show transformed the Mexican rogue into a champion of the common people—a folk hero who championed justice and righteousness with a roguish charm that made him irresistible to audiences nationwide. The Mutual Broadcasting System's episodic format allowed listeners to anticipate each new installment with genuine excitement, making Cisco and Pancho household names during radio's golden age.

The beauty of "Night Stage" lies in everything that made *The Cisco Kid* essential listening: authentic desert atmosphere, genuine suspense, and a hero defined not by his origins but by his integrity. Whether you're a devoted admirer of vintage westerns or discovering this classic for the first time, this episode represents radio adventure at its finest—when storytelling prowess and sound effects conjured entire worlds from the speaker of your radio set.
