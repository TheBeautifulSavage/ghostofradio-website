# The Cisco Kid: "Tenderfoot"

Picture yourself settling into your favorite armchair on a warm spring evening in 1942, the glow of your radio dial warming the darkened parlor as static crackles to life. The familiar theme music swells—that irresistible Spanish guitar melody—and you're transported to the dusty borderlands between California and Mexico, where the legendary Cisco Kid and his faithful companion Pancho ride toward trouble once again. In this thrilling installment, a naive young greenhorn fresh from back East stumbles into a web of deception and danger, and only the quick wit and nimble fingers of the Cisco Kid stand between the innocent tenderfoot and a gang of ruthless cattle rustlers. The stakes rise with every moment as Cisco must outwit, outsmart, and outgun his adversaries, all while teaching this city slicker the true meaning of frontier justice—delivered with charm, cunning, and that signature roguish laugh.

The Cisco Kid brought romance and adventure into living rooms across America during the golden age of radio, when a well-crafted voice and expertly orchestrated sound effects could paint vivid pictures in the listener's imagination. Created in the 1930s and flourishing throughout the 1940s, the show capitalized on the enduring American fascination with the Old West, even as the country grappled with the realities of global war. Unlike the brutal gunslinger tales that would later dominate television, Cisco represented a gentler breed of hero—a bandit with a code of honor, a man who used his brain as readily as his revolver, and who never harmed the innocent.

Don't miss this classic encounter with one of radio's most charming rogues. Tune in and discover why millions of listeners tuned in each week to follow the Cisco Kid's adventures—where courage meets cunning, and honor prevails against all odds.
