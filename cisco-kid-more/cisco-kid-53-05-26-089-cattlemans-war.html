# Cisco Kid: Cattleman's War

The dusty rangeland erupts into chaos as two feuding cattle barons lock horns over water rights and pride in this thrilling May 26th installment of The Cisco Kid. When honest ranchers find themselves caught in the crossfire of a brewing conflict that threatens to ignite into open warfare, it falls to the dashing caballero and his faithful companion Pancho to expose the real culprit behind the escalating tensions. With bullets flying, cattle stampeding across parched Texas grassland, and a mystery that runs deeper than mere greed, listeners will find themselves on the edge of their seats as our heroes navigate treacherous alliances and narrow escapes. The chemistry between Cisco and Pancho crackles with witty banter even as danger closes in from all sides—their clever minds proving mightier than any gunslinger's draw.

The Cisco Kid, one of radio's most beloved westerns, brought Spanish-speaking sophistication and roguish charm to the frontier narrative during an era when such representation was virtually unheard of in American entertainment. Running from 1942 through the mid-1950s, the show featured Jackson Beck as the quick-thinking Cisco, whose intelligence and honor made him a hero for audiences nationwide, transcending the typical gunslinger archetype. These episodes captured the post-war imagination of listeners hungry for adventure beyond their living rooms, offering escapism wrapped in genuine storytelling that respected both the complexity of the Old West and its multicultural reality.

Don't miss this classic tale of deception and justice served with style. Cattle's War reminds us why The Cisco Kid remains an unforgettable chapter in radio's Golden Age—where adventure, wit, and moral clarity ruled the airwaves.
