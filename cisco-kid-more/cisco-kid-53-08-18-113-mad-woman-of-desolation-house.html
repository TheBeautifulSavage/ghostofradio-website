# The Cisco Kid - "Mad Woman of Desolation House"

Picture yourself in a dusty Arizona saloon in 1953, tuning your radio dial to catch the latest exploits of the legendary outlaw with a heart of gold. Tonight's adventure promises to be particularly chilling: deep in the badlands, where civilization surrenders to desolation, stands an abandoned hacienda rumored to be haunted by a woman driven to madness. When Cisco and his faithful Pancho stumble upon a murder most foul, they must navigate treacherous terrain—both physical and psychological—to separate truth from legend, and to unmask a cunning killer hiding behind superstition and shadow. The desert night grows thick with danger as our heroes close in on a mystery that seems to blur the line between the supernatural and the sinister.

For over a decade, *The Cisco Kid* captured American imaginations with tales of a Robin Hood figure who robbed from the wealthy to aid the downtrodden, all while maintaining the charm and wit of a rogue with principles. Created during Hollywood's golden age when audiences craved adventure and moral complexity in equal measure, the show became a cultural phenomenon—later inspiring films and television—because it offered something beyond mere gunfights and chases. It delivered a folk hero for the common man, a character who questioned authority and injustice while remaining thoroughly entertaining. These recordings represent a golden age of radio drama when sound effects, music, and talented voice actors could transport listeners across the country to the lawless frontier.

Don't miss this gripping installment as Cisco faces madness, murder, and mystery in the heart of desolate country. Tune in now and discover why *The Cisco Kid* remains an unforgettable piece of American entertainment history.
