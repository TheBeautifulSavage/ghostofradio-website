# His Honor Killer

When the gavel falls in a dusty frontier courtroom, justice itself becomes the victim. In this gripping episode of The Cisco Kid, our charming caballero finds himself entangled in a web of corruption where the very man sworn to uphold the law has become its greatest transgressor. The judge—a figure of authority gone terribly wrong—has turned his bench into an instrument of vengeance, condemning innocent men with a smile and a flourish of his gavel. As tension crackles through the airwaves, listeners will hear Cisco's quick wit battle against an adversary wielding power far more dangerous than any six-shooter. Can he expose the truth before an innocent man swings at dawn? The answer lies in a maze of deception, courtroom drama, and the kind of clever detective work that made this show an American institution.

The Cisco Kid's enduring popularity throughout the 1940s and beyond lay in its perfect alchemy of adventure and charm. While westerns dominated radio waves, Cisco himself—a character born from pulp fiction and refined through radio—offered something different: a sophisticated, Spanish-accented hero who preferred cunning to brutality, whose smile disarmed enemies as readily as his sword. During an era when radio dramas demanded absolute commitment from their audiences, The Cisco Kid delivered swiftly paced stories that never insulted listeners' intelligence, combining action, moral complexity, and genuine humor into twenty-minute packages of pure entertainment.

Don't miss "His Honor Killer"—a masterclass in dramatic irony where authority becomes corruption's closest accomplice. Tune in and discover why millions of Americans made this show part of their daily ritual, and why those who hear it today understand that some stories, like some heroes, never truly fade from memory.
