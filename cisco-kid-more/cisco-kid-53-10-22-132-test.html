# The Cisco Kid: "Test" (October 22, 1940s)

When the crackling opening notes of the Cisco Kid theme burst through your radio speaker, you're transported instantly to the dusty borderlands between California and old Mexico, where justice doesn't always wear a badge and a clever tongue can be mightier than a six-shooter. In this episode, "Test," listeners will find themselves caught in a tense game of wits and wills as the Cisco Kid faces a situation where his legendary charm and quick thinking must prove themselves under pressure. Is someone testing his honor? His courage? His loyalty to the innocent? As the dramatic scenes unfold—punctuated by the authentic snap of gunfire and the thundering hoofbeats of Pancho's faithful horse—you'll discover that sometimes the greatest dangers aren't the ones you can see coming.

The Cisco Kid captured America's imagination during the golden age of radio by presenting a hero entirely unlike the typical lone gunslinger of the era. Here was a charming, intelligent outlaw who used his wits as often as his Colt .45, a man devoted to protecting the downtrodden and exposing corruption. The show's popularity spanned fourteen years across multiple networks, making it one of the most enduring western adventure series of radio's greatest decade. The chemistry between the Kid and his loyal companion Pancho crackled with genuine warmth and humor, grounding these exciting adventures in real human relationships.

Settle back in your chair, dim the lights, and prepare yourself for high stakes and higher adventure. "Test" awaits—a reminder of radio's power to thrill, to inspire, and to transport listeners to a world where courage and cunning triumph. Tune in and discover why The Cisco Kid remained America's favorite masked hero for generations.
