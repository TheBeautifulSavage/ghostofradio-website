# Cisco Kid – Episode 133: "Frontier Justice"

When the broadcast crackles to life on this October evening in 1942, listeners are thrust into the dusty heart of Old California, where quick wit and quicker justice reign supreme. "Frontier Justice" finds the charming caballero Cisco Kid and his faithful companion Pancho facing a moral reckoning in a remote frontier town where the law has become the instrument of the corrupt rather than the defender of the innocent. A local rancher stands accused of a crime he didn't commit, with powerful enemies determined to see him hanged before sundown. As tension mounts and the lynch mob gathers, Cisco must navigate a treacherous landscape of false testimony and purchased silence, using cunning and courage to expose the truth. The episode pulses with the urgency of a ticking clock, punctuated by the familiar strumming of Spanish guitar and Pancho's good-natured interjections, building toward a climax that will demand everything Cisco has learned about reading men's hearts.

For nearly a decade, *The Cisco Kid* has enchanted millions of Americans with its thrilling blend of adventure and roguish charm, offering an alternative vision of the West where a Mexican rogue became America's favorite outlaw-hero. Created during wartime, the series provided escapism while subtly challenging the era's racial attitudes through Cisco's dignity, intelligence, and unwavering moral code. Each episode balances action with character, proving that heroism isn't merely about drawing faster or shooting straighter—it's about standing for what's right when doing so costs you everything.

Step into the California twilight and discover why *The Cisco Kid* remains radio's most beloved western adventure. Tune in now and experience the episode that proves justice, true justice, cannot be bought or bullied—only earned.
