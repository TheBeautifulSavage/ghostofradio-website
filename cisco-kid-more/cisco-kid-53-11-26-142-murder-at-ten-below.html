# Murder At Ten Below

When the winter wind howls across the frozen frontier and the mercury plummets to ten below, even the legendary Cisco Kid finds himself tested in ways that gunplay alone cannot solve. In this gripping installment, our caballero finds himself stranded in an isolated mountain community where a body lies cold in the snow and suspicion falls like fresh-fallen flakes upon every inhabitant. With the passes blocked and help days away, Cisco must navigate treacherous human nature in addition to the treacherous landscape. As the hours tick away and body heat becomes as precious as gold, only the Kid's quick wit and sharper intuition stand between justice and a murderer escaping into the white wilderness. The tension crackles through every scene—will Cisco uncover the killer before another victim joins the first in the frozen dark?

The Cisco Kid radio series captured something essential about the Golden Age of radio: the ability to create genuine suspense through voice, sound effect, and storytelling alone. Airing throughout the 1940s and early 1950s on the Mutual network, the show elevated the western adventure format beyond simple shoot-em-up tales, incorporating mystery, humor, and genuine character development. This particular episode, recorded in that prolific late-war period, showcases the writers' skill at transforming a confined setting and harsh weather into psychological pressure that rivals any gunfight. The show's popularity endured because listeners sensed something authentic beneath the adventure—a charming rogue with a code, navigating a world far more complex than legend suggested.

Settle in by the fire, turn your dial, and experience radio storytelling at its finest. *Murder At Ten Below* awaits, with danger lurking in every shadow cast by the storm.
