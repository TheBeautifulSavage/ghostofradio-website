# Cisco Kid Episode Page

Step into the dusty streets of the Old West as The Cisco Kid faces his most cunning adversary yet—not with bullets, but with words. In "Cross Examination," our charming caballero must clear his name in a courtroom where justice hangs by a thread, and a clever opponent seeks to twist the truth into a noose. The crackling static of the radio crackles with tension as witnesses take the stand, each testimony a potential betrayal. Will Cisco's quick wit and silver tongue prove mightier than the prosecution's circumstantial evidence? Listen as the famous outlaw-with-a-heart-of-gold navigates legal treachery, all while maintaining his trademark charm and humor. The courtroom drama unfolds with period-perfect sound effects—the gavel's sharp crack, the shuffle of papers, the murmur of the gallery—transporting listeners to a frontier town where fate itself seems to be on trial.

The Cisco Kid radio series represents the golden age of adventure radio, when actors like Jackson Beck brought the legendary outlaw to life with charisma and warmth that made listeners root for a wanted man. Airing throughout the 1940s and into the 1950s, these episodes captured America's romantic fascination with the Southwest and its mythologized bandits who robbed from the corrupt and corrupt only. "Cross Examination" showcases the show's unique approach: unlike simple shoot-'em-up westerns, this series often placed Cisco in situations requiring intelligence and cunning, making him a hero for the thinking listener.
