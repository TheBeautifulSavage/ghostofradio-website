# Cisco Kid 54 04 20 183 Mistaken Identity

When a stranger arrives in town bearing an uncanny resemblance to the legendary outlaw Cisco Kid, the dusty borderlands erupt into confusion and danger. Our hero finds himself pursued by bounty hunters who cannot distinguish the imposter from the genuine article, while Pancho struggles to convince the authorities of the terrible mix-up before bullets start flying in earnest. In this thrilling installment, Cisco must outwit not only the criminals who would claim the reward on his head, but also the well-meaning lawmen who've been given faulty descriptions and itchy trigger fingers. The tension builds masterfully as mistaken identity becomes the perfect cover for a cunning crime, and only Cisco's quick wit and resourcefulness can unravel the mystery before an innocent man—or Cisco himself—pays the ultimate price.

The Cisco Kid series, which entertained millions from the early 1940s through the mid-1950s, represented something uniquely American: a positive portrayal of a Mexican folk hero on network radio. Rather than the villainous bandito archetype common in lesser westerns, Cisco was depicted as a charming, intelligent rogue with a strong moral code—a gentleman outlaw who championed the underdog and outwitted corrupt officials. The show's popularity across the Southwest and beyond demonstrated audiences' hunger for adventurous heroes who defied simple categorization, paving the way for more nuanced characters in popular entertainment.

Tune in now to experience the crackling excitement that captivated listeners during radio's golden age, where a case of mistaken identity becomes a matter of life and death in the lawless territories south of the border.
