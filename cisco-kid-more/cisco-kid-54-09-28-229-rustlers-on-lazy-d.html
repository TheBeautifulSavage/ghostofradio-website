# Rustlers On Lazy D

As the static crackles and fades, you're transported to the dusty ranchlands of the Southwest, where the Lazy D Ranch faces its darkest hour. Cisco Kid and Pancho arrive to find honest rancher Tom Bradley robbed of his entire herd—and worse, the local sheriff has already made up his mind about who's guilty. With suspicion falling on the wrong man and ruthless rustlers disappearing into the desert with stolen cattle, our caballero must unravel a web of deceit before an innocent man swings from a noose. The tension mounts as Cisco and Pancho trace false leads through saloons and hideouts, racing against time and prejudice to expose the real culprits. You'll hear the thunder of hoofbeats, the sharp crack of gunfire, and Pancho's faithful comic relief punctuating every narrow escape—all building to a climactic confrontation where justice prevails only through Cisco's quick thinking and quicker draw.

For over a decade, *The Cisco Kid* captivated millions of American listeners with tales of a gentleman outlaw whose honor and cunning made him a hero to the underdog and a nightmare for villains. Unlike the violent gunfighters of pulp fiction, Cisco embodied a romantic ideal: the charming rogue who righted wrongs through cleverness and courage, never needless bloodshed. Broadcast across the Mutual network and independent stations from 1942 onward, the show became essential evening entertainment, with the dynamic partnership between Cisco's suave intelligence and Pancho's lovable bumbling creating comedy and camaraderie that appealed to families across America.

Tune in now to experience this thrilling 1949 adventure—a perfect snapshot of radio's Golden Age when imagination, not screens, brought the Old West to vivid, unforgettable life.
