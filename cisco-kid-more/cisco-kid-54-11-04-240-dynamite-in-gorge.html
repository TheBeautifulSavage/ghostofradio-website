# Dynamite in the Gorge

Picture this: it's a moonless night in the Sierra Madre, and Cisco Kid finds himself trapped in a treacherous canyon with nothing but his wits, his loyal companion Pancho, and a ticking crate of dynamite that could bring the entire gorge crashing down around them. When a band of ruthless miners plants explosives to seal off a secret passage—and the only escape route for our heroes—Cisco must race against time to defuse the charges before they detonate. The tension crackles through every moment of this episode, complete with the thundering hoofbeats of pursued horses, the ominous hiss of burning fuses, and dialogue crackling with barely contained desperation. Listeners will grip their radio sets as Cisco's quick thinking and fearless nature are put to their ultimate test, wondering if even the legendary outlaw's luck can save him this time.

For over a decade, *The Cisco Kid* captivated millions of Americans with tales of a noble bandit operating in the Old West, a figure both heroic and mysterious. Airing on the Mutual network and later syndicated across the country, the show became a cultural phenomenon during radio's golden age, introducing listeners to a romanticized Southwest filled with honor among thieves and justice dispensed outside the law. This particular episode exemplifies why the series endured—it combines genuine suspense with character-driven storytelling, while the chemistry between Cisco (voiced with suave charm) and his faithful Pancho never fails to entertain.

Don't miss your chance to experience classic adventure radio at its finest. Tune in to "Dynamite in the Gorge" and discover why audiences huddled around their receivers each week, transported to a world of danger, daring, and the swift justice of the Cisco Kid.
