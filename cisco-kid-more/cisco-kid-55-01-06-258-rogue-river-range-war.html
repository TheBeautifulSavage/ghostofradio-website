# Cisco Kid: Rogue River Range War

Saddle up for an evening of high-stakes frontier justice as Cisco Kid and his faithful companion Pancho ride into the dangerous territory of Rogue River, where the greed of ruthless cattlemen threatens to ignite a powder keg of violence. When honest ranchers find their water rights stolen and their livelihoods destroyed by a corrupt land baron and his hired gunslicks, only the quick wit and faster draw of the legendary outlaw-hero can restore peace to the troubled range. Listen as gunfire cracks across the Oregon badlands, as Cisco outwits his enemies with cunning and charm, and as Pancho provides both comic relief and steadfast loyalty. This episode crackles with the authentic tension of the American West—the desperation of settlers, the moral complexity of a hero who operates outside the law, and the thrilling certainty that justice will prevail against impossible odds.

The Cisco Kid radio series, which dominated the airways throughout the 1940s and 1950s, represented the golden age of adventure programming, bringing the romance of the Old West into living rooms across America. With its blend of action, humor, and surprisingly nuanced characterization, the show transcended typical western fare, presenting Cisco as a noble rogue whose methods were unconventional but whose heart remained pure. The chemistry between the smooth-talking Cisco and the good-natured Pancho created an enduring dynamic that influenced countless adventure duos that followed, while the scripts themselves tackled themes of injustice and corruption with remarkable sophistication for the era.

Don't miss "Rogue River Range War," an exemplary episode that showcases everything that made The Cisco Kid essential listening for adventure seekers everywhere. Tune in and discover why audiences made this show a staple of their radio schedules for fourteen unforgettable years.
