# Murder at Pine Creek

Picture this: a dusty frontier town where trust is as scarce as water, and danger lurks in every shadow of the saloon. In "Murder at Pine Creek," our charming caballero Cisco Kid finds himself tangled in a web of deception and vengeance when a prominent rancher turns up dead under mysterious circumstances. Suspicion falls on an innocent man—a drifter with the bad luck of being in the wrong place at the wrong time. As the local lawmen sharpen their nooses, Cisco must use his quick wit, lightning-fast draw, and his uncanny ability to read a man's character to uncover the real killer before justice becomes merely another name for mob rule. The tension crackles through every scene as Cisco navigates saloon confrontations, tense interrogations, and the ever-present threat of lead flying faster than words.

For over a decade, *The Cisco Kid* captured the imaginations of millions of radio listeners by offering something different from the typical western fare—a charming, clever hero of Spanish descent who relied on brains as much as brawn, operating in a moral gray area where the law wasn't always just. This 1940s adventure, part of the show's golden years under the Mutual network, exemplifies what made Cisco enduringly popular: authentic frontier atmosphere married to clever plotting, with a protagonist who championed the underdog and the wrongfully accused. Actor Jackson Beck's quick-tongued delivery of Cisco's dialogue and the authentic sound design transported listeners directly into the pine-scented hills and volatile saloons of the Old West.

Don your spurs and join us for this classic tale of frontier justice gone wrong. "Murder at Pine Creek" awaits—where one man's integrity becomes everyone's only hope.
