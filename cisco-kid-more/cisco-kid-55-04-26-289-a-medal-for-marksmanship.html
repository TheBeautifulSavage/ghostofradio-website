# Cisco Kid 55 04 26 289 – A Medal For Marksmanship

Picture yourself in the dusty borderlands of the Old West as our dashing caballero, the Cisco Kid, finds himself drawn into a competition that's far more dangerous than it appears. When a mysterious sharpshooter arrives in town claiming to be the fastest draw in the territory, Cisco's reputation—and his pride—are put to the test. But there's more at stake than simple bragging rights. As the episode unfolds across the crackling airwaves, you'll discover that this contest conceals a sinister plot, and our hero must use not just his legendary marksmanship, but his quick wit and honor to expose the truth. The tension builds with each crack of a gunshot and each breathless revelation, keeping listeners riveted to every word.

The Cisco Kid stands as one of radio's most enduring adventures, a show that perfectly captured post-war America's fascination with the romanticized West. Unlike the gritty anti-heroes that would later dominate westerns, Cisco remained the chivalrous rogue—clever, charming, and devoted to justice with a distinctly Latin flavor that made him unique in the genre. Produced during the 1940s and 1950s when radio drama was reaching its artistic peak, the show benefited from talented writers who crafted plots that balanced genuine danger with moments of humor and humanity. This particular episode exemplifies what made Cisco endure: a simple premise elevated through skilled acting and storytelling into something genuinely compelling.

If you've never experienced the thrill of classic radio adventure, or if you're a devoted fan seeking a cherished favorite, this episode awaits you.
