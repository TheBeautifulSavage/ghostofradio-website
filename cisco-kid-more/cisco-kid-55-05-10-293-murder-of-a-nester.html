# Cisco Kid Episode Page

Picture this: the dusty frontier town of Red Gulch, where tensions simmer hotter than a branding iron in July. A humble nester—a small-time farmer just trying to scratch out a living on disputed land—lies dead under mysterious circumstances, and suspicion falls like a vulture on the nearest stranger in town. When the local law proves more trigger-happy than thorough, it falls to the cunning Cisco Kid and his faithful companion Pancho to unearth the truth before an innocent man swings from the nearest rope. This pulse-pounding adventure, "Murder of a Nester," traps our heroes in a web of greed, jealousy, and frontier justice gone wrong, where every shadow hides a potential killer and time is running out.

The Cisco Kid series, which galloped across America's airwaves throughout the 1940s and 1950s, represented something rare in radio's golden age: a hero of Mexican descent who outsmarted rather than outgunned his opponents, who used charm and quick wit as readily as his famous pistols. Created during an era when such nuanced portrayals were uncommon, the show offered listeners an alternative to the typical hard-boiled lawman formula—here was a gentleman bandit with a code of honor, fighting on behalf of those the system forgot. Each episode captured the spirit of the Old West while exploring themes of justice, loyalty, and the tensions between frontier settlement and established law.

Don't miss your chance to ride alongside Cisco and Pancho as they navigate the treacherous underworld of small-town murder and corruption.
