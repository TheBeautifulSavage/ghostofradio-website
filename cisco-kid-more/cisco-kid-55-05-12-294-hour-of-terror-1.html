# The Hour of Terror

Step into the desert darkness with the Cisco Kid as a mysterious terror grips a small frontier town, and only the legendary caballero stands between innocent citizens and a shadowy evil that strikes without warning. In this spine-tingling adventure, Cisco and his faithful companion Pancho find themselves caught in a web of fear and suspicion where the danger is as invisible as it is deadly. As night falls across the dusty streets, townspeople barricade themselves behind locked doors while whispered rumors spread of something—or someone—moving through the shadows with malevolent purpose. The Kid must use all his cunning, quick wit, and legendary skill to unravel the mystery before panic consumes the town entirely. Every creak of a floorboard, every distant hoofbeat, every sinister laugh carries the weight of impending doom in this unforgettable tale of suspense and western justice.

What made *The Cisco Kid* such an enduring fixture of American radio entertainment throughout the 1940s and 1950s was its perfect balance of swashbuckling adventure and genuine character depth. Unlike many westerns of the era, the show presented Cisco not merely as a bandit, but as a folk hero operating under his own moral code—a charming rogue who always championed the downtrodden and righteously punished the truly guilty. The chemistry between Cisco and Pancho became the backbone of the series, their witty banter and genuine affection providing warmth and humor even in the darkest episodes. This particular installment showcases that masterful blend, combining atmospheric horror with the show's trademark charm and adventure.

Don't miss "Hour of Terror"—a masterclass in old-time radio suspense that will keep you on the edge of your seat long after the final broadcast has faded into static. Tune in tonight and discover why audiences made *The Cisco Kid* legendary.
