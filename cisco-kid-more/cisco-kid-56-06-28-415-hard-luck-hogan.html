# Cisco Kid – "Hard Luck Hogan" (June 28, 1956)

Picture yourself in a dusty Arizona cantina as the sun dips below the desert horizon. Tonight's adventure brings Cisco and Pancho face to face with Hard Luck Hogan, a prospector down on his fortune who's willing to cross dangerous lines to strike it rich. When a scheme to frame an innocent man for a mine theft unfolds, our caballero hero must ride hard through moonlit arroyos and navigate the treacherous politics of frontier justice. Will Cisco's quick wit and quicker trigger finger save the day, or will Hogan's desperation prove too powerful? The crackling tension, punctuated by gunfire and galloping horses, promises a thrilling thirty minutes that captures everything listeners adored about this golden-age series.

For nearly two decades, *The Cisco Kid* captivated millions of radio devotees with tales of the Robin Hood of the Old West—a charming Mexican caballero with a heart for justice and a code all his own. What made the show remarkable was its delicate cultural balance; rather than relying on tired stereotypes, the program presented Cisco as intelligent, dignified, and genuinely heroic, with his loyal companion Pancho offering comic relief without compromise. The Mutual network's syndication meant the show reached listeners from coast to coast, making Cisco as recognizable to Kansas farmers as to Hollywood starlets.

Don't miss this week's episode as Cisco grapples with the fine line between compassion and law. Tune in and experience the craft of classic radio storytelling—where sound effects and stellar voice acting transport you straight to the American West. *The Cisco Kid* awaits.
