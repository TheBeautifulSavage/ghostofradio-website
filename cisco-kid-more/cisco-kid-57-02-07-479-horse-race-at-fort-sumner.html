# Cisco Kid Episode: Horse Race at Fort Sumner

As the opening guitar flourish crackles through the static, listeners are transported to the dusty plaza of Fort Sumner, where high stakes and higher tempers collide under the New Mexico sun. In this thrilling installment, Cisco and his faithful companion Pancho find themselves caught between a crooked cavalry officer and a desperate rancher whose only hope of saving his land rests on the outcome of a seemingly innocent horse race. But nothing is innocent in Fort Sumner—the officer has rigged the competition, and our masked hero must use his wits, charm, and quick reflexes to expose the scheme before an honest man loses everything. With danger lurking at every turn and a climactic race sequence that will have you gripping your seat, this episode delivers the perfect blend of adventure and roguish justice that made The Cisco Kid a beloved fixture in American homes.

What distinguished The Cisco Kid from countless other westerns of the era was its sophisticated portrayal of its hero—not as a brutish lawman, but as a gentlemanly outlaw with an unwavering moral compass and a quick tongue as sharp as his sword. The show's popularity exploded during World War II, when audiences craved escapism alongside redemption, and Cisco's Robin Hood-like exploits offered both in equal measure. This particular episode, recorded in the early 1940s, captures the series at its creative peak, with snappy dialogue and a supporting cast of colorful characters brought to vivid life by an exceptional ensemble cast.

Don't miss this masterpiece of old-time radio adventure. Settle back with the lights dimmed low, and let the golden age of broadcasting transport you to a frontier where honor still matters and one clever caballero can outwit the corrupt and protect the innocent—all before sundown.
