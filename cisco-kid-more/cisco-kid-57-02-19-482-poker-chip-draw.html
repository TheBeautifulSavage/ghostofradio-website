# The Cisco Kid: "Poker Chip Draw"

The desert night closes in as our noble rogue Cisco and his faithful Pancho find themselves caught between honor and survival in a high-stakes game that could cost them everything. When a mysterious stranger arrives in town with a fortune in poker chips—and a dangerous secret—Cisco must decide whether to trust his wits or his gun. The tension crackles through every scene: the clink of chips on weathered tables, the whispered Spanish asides between our heroes, the mounting suspicion that someone in that smoke-filled room is playing with marked cards and marked intentions. This is the Cisco Kid at his finest—charming his way through danger, outsmarting greed-blind opponents, and always, *always* staying one step ahead of the law that pursues him.

The Cisco Kid represented a golden age of American adventure radio, where the clever outlaw with a code of honor captured the nation's imagination during the uncertain 1940s. Created from Johnston McCulley's literary character and adapted brilliantly for the airwaves, the show starred Jackson Beck's warm, charismatic Cisco opposite Mel Blanc's comedic Pancho—a pairing that became legendary. Unlike typical westerns of the era that glorified lawmen, this series dared to make listeners root for the bandit, the man outside society who righted wrongs and protected the innocent. "Poker Chip Draw" exemplifies this formula perfectly, combining mystery, humor, and heart in just thirty minutes of pure radio magic.
