# The Cisco Kid: "Phineas Boys"

When the opening trumpet fanfare pierces the evening air and that unmistakable Spanish guitar melody swells, listeners are transported once more to the dusty borderlands where justice wears a smile and honor rides a swift horse. In "Phineas Boys," the legendary Cisco Kid and his faithful companion Pancho find themselves tangled in the web of a notorious outlaw family whose reputation for brutality has made even hardened lawmen think twice. What begins as a routine patrol takes a dangerous turn when Cisco stumbles upon evidence of a sinister plot—one that will test not only his quick wits and quicker draw, but his unwavering code of chivalry. The tension crackles through every line of dialogue as Cisco must navigate the treacherous divide between law and mercy, between justice and vengeance. With gunfire erupting across desert canyons and double-crosses around every corner, this episode delivers the perfect blend of swashbuckling adventure and genuine peril that made *The Cisco Kid* a must-listen for millions.

*The Cisco Kid* stands as one of radio's most enduring adventures, running continuously from 1942 through the mid-1950s across the Mutual network and numerous stations. The show's genius lay in its appeal across all ages and backgrounds—children thrilled to the action, while adults appreciated the moral complexity beneath the surface. Cisco himself, that charming Mexican caballero with impeccable manners and a robin-hood-like sense of justice, represented something uniquely appealing in postwar America: a hero who fought for the underdog without sacrificing his dignity or his honor.

So settle into your favorite chair this evening and dial in to join the Kid and Pancho as they face the Phineas Boys. You won't want to miss a moment of this thrilling adventure—*The Cisco Kid* waits for no one!
