# Cisco Kid Episode: "Westward From St. Joe"

As our cunning caballero prepares to depart from the bustling riverfront of St. Joseph, Missouri, the stakes have never been higher. Cisco finds himself entangled in a desperate race against time—a shipment of stolen gold bullion, a band of ruthless claim jumpers, and an innocent family caught in the crossfire all converge on the western trail. With only his wits, his lightning-quick draw, and the faithful Pancho at his side, Cisco must navigate the treacherous landscape between civilization and the untamed frontier. Listen as thunder cracks across the plains, hoofbeats pound the dusty trail, and danger lurks at every bend in the road. This is vintage Cisco Kid adventure: part mystery, part morality play, all heart-pounding suspense.

For over a decade, *The Cisco Kid* captivated millions of radio listeners with its perfect blend of swashbuckling heroics and social conscience. Created by O. Henry's classic character, the show distinguished itself by presenting its Mexican protagonist not as a bandito villain, but as a charming, intelligent Robin Hood figure who protected the downtrodden and outwitted corrupt authorities. In the early 1940s, when this episode aired, the show represented something rare on American radio: a lead character of Hispanic descent portrayed with dignity and sophistication. Cisco's catchphrase—"Hello, Señorita!"—became iconic, while his partnership with the loyal, often bumbling Pancho established one of radio's most beloved duos.

Don't miss "Westward From St. Joe"—a masterclass in Old West storytelling where honor, justice, and quick reflexes triumph over greed and villainy. Tune in today and discover why *The Cisco Kid* remains an essential piece of American radio history.
