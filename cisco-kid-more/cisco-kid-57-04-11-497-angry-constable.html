# Cisco Kid Episode: "Angry Constable"

The dusty trails of the Southwest grow dangerous when a lawman's pride becomes as volatile as a desert thunderstorm. In this thrilling episode, Cisco finds himself caught between a furious constable determined to make an arrest and an innocent man facing the noose for a crime he didn't commit. With only his wits, his quick draw, and the loyal companionship of Pancho at his side, the caballero must outmaneuver both the letter of the law and a man's wounded ego to uncover the truth. Will Cisco's charm and cunning be enough to expose the real culprit before justice miscarries? Settle in as the hoofbeats pound across the badlands and tensions crackle like lightning in the desert air.

The Cisco Kid radio program, which captivated millions of listeners throughout the 1940s and 1950s, represented a unique American mythology—the charming outlaw with a code of honor, fighting injustice in the lawless frontier. Drawing from earlier dime novels and the 1945 film that sparked the radio series' tremendous popularity, the show struck an unusual balance between action-packed adventure and surprisingly progressive storytelling, often portraying Cisco as a champion of the downtrodden and wrongly accused. The chemistry between Cisco and his comic relief partner Pancho became legendary, their rapid-fire Spanish phrases and playful banter endearing them to audiences of all ages across America's living rooms.

Don't miss "Angry Constable"—a masterful episode that exemplifies why The Cisco Kid remained a staple of American radio entertainment for over a decade. Tune in and experience the golden age of adventure radio.
