# Johnson County Raid

Picture yourself huddled around the radio on a warm evening in 1944, the static crackling like a distant campfire as the familiar guitar riff announces the arrival of the Cisco Kid and Pancho. In "Johnson County Raid," our masked hero finds himself caught between feuding cattlemen and a band of desperate rustlers who will stop at nothing to claim the vast herds of Wyoming's most contentious valley. The tension builds from the opening gunshot—is Cisco truly the outlaw he's accused of being, or is he once again the only man clever and honorable enough to uncover the real culprits? With Pancho's loyal comic relief providing momentary respite from the mounting danger, listeners are swept into a thrilling tale of mistaken identity, frontier justice, and the razor-thin line between law and lawlessness.

The Cisco Kid radio series captured something essential about American mythology that kept millions tuned in throughout the 1940s and '50s. Created as a vehicle to showcase the charm and wit of the character popularized in films and pulp fiction, the show became a masterclass in economical storytelling—the entire landscape of conflict painted with nothing but a skilled actor's voice, sound effects, and swelling orchestral cues. "Johnson County Raid" exemplifies the program's particular genius, drawing on real territorial conflicts from the American West while crafting a morality tale perfectly suited to wartime audiences hungry for heroes who fought not with brutality, but with intelligence and integrity.

Tune in to experience why The Cisco Kid remained one of radio's most beloved adventures, where the hero never fires first and always knows that the fastest gun is no match for a quick mind.
