# Nightriders of Redrock

When darkness falls over the Arizona badlands, danger rides with it—and so does Cisco! In this thrilling installment, "Nightriders of Redrock," our caballero hero and his faithful companion Pancho face a band of ruthless outlaws who strike under cover of moonlight, terrorizing honest ranchers and absconding with payroll shipments meant for hardworking settlers. The tension crackles through every scene as Cisco must outwit a clever gang operating in the treacherous shadows of Redrock Canyon, where every narrow passage could hide an ambush and every whispered conversation might be Cisco's last. With the lawmen stumped and desperation mounting among the ranching community, only Cisco's quick wit, expert swordsmanship, and unwavering sense of justice stand between civilization and chaos. Listen as the hoofbeats pound, the gunshots ring out, and the mystery of the Nightriders' true identities slowly unravels.

The Cisco Kid brought authentic Spanish-California adventure to millions of American homes during radio's golden age, transforming a Mexican folk legend into a beloved cultural icon. Unlike the crude villains of lesser westerns, Cisco was a sophisticated hero—intelligent, charming, and fighting for the common people against corrupt officials and genuine bad men alike. J. Carrillo's distinctive voice and the show's snappy scripts made Cisco more than just another trigger-happy desperado; he was a modern Robin Hood, and audiences couldn't get enough. This particular episode, from the show's strong mid-1940s run, showcases everything that made the series endure for fourteen years across radio networks nationwide.
