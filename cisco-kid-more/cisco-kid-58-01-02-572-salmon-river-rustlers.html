# Salmon River Rustlers

Picture this: the crackling static fades, and you're transported to the rugged wilderness of the Pacific Northwest, where the swift Salmon River cuts through towering pines and rocky gorges. Our hero, the legendary Cisco Kid, finds himself ensnared in a web of cattle theft and frontier justice far from his usual haunts in the Southwest. As rustlers drive stolen herds through treacherous mountain passes under cover of darkness, Cisco must use all his cunning and quick draw to outwit criminals who know this unforgiving terrain far better than he does. With his faithful companion Pancho beside him—offering both comic relief and steadfast loyalty—Cisco races against time to recover the stolen livestock and expose the mastermind pulling the strings. The tension builds as our hero realizes this operation runs deeper than simple rustling; powerful men with even darker intentions lurk in the shadows.

The Cisco Kid had become a cultural phenomenon by the 1940s, transcending pulp magazines and dime novels to become radio's most beloved gunslinger. This syndicated series, airing across the Mutual network, brought a refreshing sophistication to the western adventure format—Cisco wasn't a trigger-happy lawman but rather a Robin Hood figure who championed the underdog and fought injustice with wit as sharp as his blade. Actor Jackson Beck's resonant voice and masterful delivery made Cisco irresistibly charming, while the show's spirited scripts elevated it beyond typical shoot-em-up fare into genuine dramatic storytelling.

Don't miss "Salmon River Rustlers," where danger lurks around every bend and trust becomes the rarest commodity of all. Tune in and discover why millions of listeners made the Cisco Kid an essential part of their evening radio ritual—where adventure, honor, and justice galloped across the airwaves every single week.
