# The Cisco Kid: "Six Gun Law"

Picture this: the Arizona frontier, dust devils spinning across red rock country as night falls over a sleepy border town. Our legendary caballero, the Cisco Kid, rides into trouble once again—this time tangled in a web of false accusations and crooked lawmen who've decided justice is whatever they say it is. With his faithful companion Pancho at his side, Cisco must outthink a corrupt marshal who's twisted the law into a weapon against the innocent. Expect sharp dialogue crackles through your speaker, the thunder of hoofbeats across hardpan, and that distinctive guitar flourish that signals our hero's clever escape. This episode captures everything listeners loved about the Kid: wit sharper than any blade, a code of honor that transcends mere legality, and the kind of high-stakes adventure that had families gathering around their radios in rapt attention.

The Cisco Kid arrived at radio's golden age when Americans craved heroes who operated by their own moral compass. Unlike the rigid lawmen of other westerns, Cisco represented a roguish Robin Hood figure—charming, intelligent, and unapologetically outside the system. These 1942-1956 broadcasts, featuring the smooth-voiced Vorgel, became syndication gold, thriving on stations across America because they offered something deeper than shoot-outs: they explored justice itself, questioning who truly wore the white hat.

"Six Gun Law" stands as a perfect example of the show's enduring appeal—where action serves character, and principle drives the plot. Tune in and discover why audiences couldn't resist the Cisco Kid's particular brand of frontier justice, where a clever mind and a good heart proved mightier than any six-shooter.
