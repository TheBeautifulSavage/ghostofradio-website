When the hoofbeats thunder across the desert and Cisco's distinctive laugh echoes through your living room, you know justice rides tonight—but this time, the law itself has turned rotten. In this November 1941 adventure, our masked caballero finds himself hunted not by common outlaws, but by a corrupt posse of hired killers who'll stop at nothing to silence him. As danger closes in from all sides, Cisco must use his wits, his charm, and his lightning-fast draw to expose the conspiracy before sunset brings the final reckoning. The tension builds relentlessly as the noose tightens, with Pancho's loyal voice urging our hero onward and innocent lives hanging in the balance.

The Cisco Kid represented something revolutionary for 1940s radio audiences: a Spanish-language hero who commanded respect, outwitted villains with cunning rather than mere gunplay, and proved that adventure serials needn't rely on tired stereotypes. Created by Johnston McCulley and brought vividly to life by actor Jackson Beck's distinctive delivery, the show became a cultural phenomenon that transcended its genre, airing on stations nationwide and capturing the imagination of millions who tuned in for tales of a hero who championed the underdog and outwitted corrupt authority. These episodes, preserved now as a window into post-war entertainment, showcase the artistry of early radio drama at its finest.

Don't miss "Posse of Killers"—it's a masterclass in suspense, heroism, and the golden age of radio adventure. Settle into your chair, dim the lights, and let the desert night transport you back to an era when a single voice and sound effects could hold an entire nation spellbound. Cisco's fate awaits.
