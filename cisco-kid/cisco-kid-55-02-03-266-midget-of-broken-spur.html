When the sun dips low over the dusty trails of Broken Spur, listeners in 1940s living rooms lean closer to their radio dials for this thrilling tale of the legendary outlaw with a conscience. In "Midget of Broken Spur," Cisco and his faithful companion Pancho find themselves caught between a small man with an outsized grudge and a town teetering on the edge of violence. As gunshots punctuate the desert night and voices crackle with tension through the speaker, the question looms: can Cisco's quick wit and faster gun hand untangle a mystery where nothing—and no one—is quite what it seems? The episode crackles with the authentic atmosphere of the Old West, complete with the clip-clop of horses' hooves, the jingle of spurs, and the unmistakable twang of frontier justice about to be served.

The Cisco Kid radio program stands as a masterpiece of adventure broadcasting, bringing the romantic outlaw legend to millions of Americans during the 1940s and 1950s. Far from the villainous bandit of dime novels, this Cisco was a Robin Hood figure—charming, honorable, and always on the side of the downtrodden. The show's syndicated reach across the Mutual network made it a fixture in households nationwide, with listeners captivated by the chemistry between Cisco and Pancho, their witty banter providing comic relief alongside genuine peril. These episodes represent a golden age of radio drama when sound effects artists and voice actors created entire worlds through imagination and ingenuity.
