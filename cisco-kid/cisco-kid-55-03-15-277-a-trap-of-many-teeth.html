When the Cisco Kid and Pancho stumble upon a remote canyon settlement plagued by a deadly predator, they discover that the real danger lurking in the shadows is far more sinister than any wild beast. In this gripping installment, mysterious disappearances and cryptic warnings of something with "many teeth" transform an ordinary rescue mission into a desperate race against time. As night falls over the desert, our heroes must untangle a web of secrets and deception while the unseen menace draws ever closer. The tension crackles through every scene—will Cisco's quick wit and cunning be enough to save the townspeople, or has he finally met a threat too dangerous even for the swiftest blade in the West?

The Cisco Kid represents a remarkable chapter in American broadcasting history, bringing the beloved Mexican caballero to millions of American homes during the golden age of radio drama. Created by Johnston McCulley and adapted brilliantly for audio by a talented cast and production team, the show became a syndication powerhouse, captivating audiences with its perfect blend of action, humor, and moral clarity. Cisco embodied an increasingly rare character type for the era—a noble outsider whose heroism transcended racial boundaries at a time when such representation was far from common. The show's success lay not merely in its thrilling plots, but in its fundamental decency and charm, making it a family favorite that could hold its own against any of radio's premiere adventure programs.

Step into a world of desert mystery and frontier courage. Tune in now and experience why generations of listeners were captivated by the legend of the Cisco Kid—where danger lurks with many teeth, but where justice and honor always find their mark.
