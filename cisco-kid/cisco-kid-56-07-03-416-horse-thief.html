As the familiar strumains of the Spanish guitar theme fade into the Arizona night, listeners are drawn into a tale of cunning, justice, and the code of the Old West. In "Horse Thief," the legendary Cisco Kid and his faithful companion Pancho find themselves tracking a ruthless band of rustlers who have stolen the prized mustangs of an honest rancher. What begins as a straightforward case of theft quickly spirals into danger when Cisco discovers the thieves are working for a corrupt territorial official with deep pockets and deeper connections. With nothing but their wits, their horses, and an unwavering commitment to frontier justice, our heroes must navigate a treacherous web of deception and violence to recover the stolen animals and expose the corruption at the heart of the scheme. The episode crackles with tension—you can almost hear the hoofbeats pounding across the desert floor and feel the weight of a loaded pistol at Cisco's hip.

For over a decade, *The Cisco Kid* captured American imaginations as one of radio's most beloved adventure programs, bringing the mythology of the Southwest into living rooms across the nation. This particular episode exemplifies the show's masterful blend of action, moral complexity, and character charm that made it a Mutual Broadcasting staple. Unlike the typical "good versus evil" westerns of the era, the Cisco Kid series often revealed that villainy wore respectable faces and that true heroism meant protecting the vulnerable against those who abused their authority.

Whether you're a devoted fan of classic radio westerns or a newcomer to Cisco's exploits, "Horse Thief" delivers everything that made this series a cultural phenomenon. Tune in and discover why audiences lined up by their radios each week for another adventure with radio's most charismatic outlaw hero.
