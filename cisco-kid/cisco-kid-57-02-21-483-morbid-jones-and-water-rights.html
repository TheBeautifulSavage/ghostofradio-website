As twilight descends over the dusty Arizona territories, the urgent strains of the Cisco Kid's theme song crackle through your radio speaker, and you're transported to a landscape of sagebrush, scorching heat, and looming danger. In this gripping episode, our Mexican Robin Hood and his faithful companion Pancho find themselves embroiled in a desperate conflict when the sinister Morbid Jones—a water baron with a heart as parched as the desert itself—schemes to steal vital water rights from honest ranchers and farming families. What begins as a property dispute quickly escalates into a thrilling game of wits and courage, with Cisco forced to outwit a ruthless antagonist determined to control the lifeblood of the territory. As the noose of Jones's malevolent plot tightens, listeners will hang on every word, wondering whether Cisco's cunning and quick draw will be enough to save the innocent from ruin and restore justice to the arid wasteland.

The Cisco Kid radio program stands as a remarkable testament to adventure entertainment's golden age, transforming Johnston McCulley's original pulp character into a beloved fixture in American living rooms throughout the 1940s and '50s. Unlike many radio westerns that relied solely on gunplay and rough-and-tumble heroics, *The Cisco Kid* distinguished itself through clever plotting, witty banter between Cisco and Pancho, and surprisingly nuanced explorations of frontier justice—all wrapped in the distinctive sound design that made radio drama an art form unto itself.

Don't miss this classic tale of greed, heroism, and the eternal struggle for justice in the Old West. Tune in for "Morbid Jones and Water Rights" and experience the timeless appeal of adventure radio that captivated millions.
