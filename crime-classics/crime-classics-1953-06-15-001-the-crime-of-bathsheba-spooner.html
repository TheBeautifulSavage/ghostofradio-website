Crime Classics 1953 06 15 (001) The Crime Of Bathsheba Spooner

As the CBS orchestra swells into that unmistakable Crime Classics theme, listeners are transported to colonial Massachusetts in the grip of a tale as dark and twisted as any modern crime could be. In this premiere episode, narrator Allen Dulles guides you through the shocking case of Bathsheba Spooner, a woman whose desperate circumstances led her to orchestrate a murder that would shake the young American republic to its core. Against the backdrop of post-Revolutionary uncertainty, you'll hear the voices of desperate characters—a woman trapped in a loveless marriage, soldiers passing through her tavern, and the machinery of colonial justice grinding toward an irreversible verdict. The production crackles with authenticity and tension, each scene meticulously reconstructed from court records and historical documents. This is no fictional crime—this is what actually happened in 1777, and the moral ambiguities will linger with you long after the final fade-out.

Crime Classics represents a bold departure from the fictional detectives and imagined crimes that have dominated radio drama. Producer-host Allen Dulles—himself a figure of historical significance—pioneered this format of dramatizing actual historical crimes, treating each case with the gravitas of a courtroom proceeding while maintaining the gripping pace of theatrical drama. By focusing on real crimes from America's own past rather than contemporary headlines or pure fantasy, the show offers listeners something richer: a window into how justice worked in different eras, how ordinary people became entangled in extraordinary circumstances, and how the past remains perpetually relevant.

Don't miss this inaugural episode of Crime Classics, premiering Monday evening on CBS. The truth, as they say, is stranger than fiction—and far more compelling. Tune in at 8:30 PM and discover why this new series is already generating conversation across the nation.
