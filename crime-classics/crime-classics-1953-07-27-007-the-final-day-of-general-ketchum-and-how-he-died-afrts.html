On July 27th, 1953, millions of Americans gathered around their radio sets as Crime Classics peeled back the layers of one of the military's most shocking scandals. In this episode, listeners are transported to the tension-filled corridors of power where General Ketchum's meteoric rise came to an abrupt and violent end. What unfolds is a masterfully crafted drama of ambition, betrayal, and the dark secrets that lurked beneath the polished brass and dress uniforms of high command. The episode's title itself—"How He Died"—promises that elusive truth, as narrator and sound effects work in concert to reconstruct the final hours before tragedy struck. You'll hear the measured tones of period-appropriate dialogue, the sharp crack of period-authentic sound design, and the inexorable march toward a conclusion that shocked the nation.

Crime Classics distinguished itself among the crowded field of radio crime dramas by grounding each episode in meticulously researched fact rather than pure sensationalism. Broadcast during the height of the Korean War when military authority loomed large in American consciousness, this particular episode struck at the heart of public curiosity about what really happened behind closed doors in our armed forces. The show's writers took genuine historical events and crafted them into compelling radio theater, lending legitimacy to every dramatic pause and reconstructed conversation.

For fans of vintage radio drama and true crime alike, this episode represents Crime Classics at its finest—where historical documentation meets theatrical presentation. Settle in with the lights dimmed and let this 1953 broadcast transport you back to an era when radio was America's window into its own mysteries.
