# Crime Classics: The Axe And The Droot Family

On a sweltering August evening in 1953, listeners across America huddled near their radio speakers to witness one of the most disturbing domestic tragedies ever committed. *Crime Classics* presents the gruesome true story of a family destroyed by violence—a tale of axes, blood-soaked bedsheets, and the terrible question of whether any member of the Droot household could have survived the other's murderous rage. As the narrator's measured voice cuts through the static, you'll hear the meticulous reconstruction of that fateful night: the sounds of struggle in a darkened home, the chilling discovery that would shock the small community, and the relentless investigation that followed. This is not fiction embellished for effect—every detail draws from actual case files, court records, and witness testimonies that expose the raw, unvarnished brutality lurking behind closed doors.

*Crime Classics*, now in its second season on CBS, has established itself as America's premier documentary-style crime program, steering clear of sensationalism in favor of journalistic rigor. Where competitors relied on melodrama and speculation, host and creator Elliot Lewis demanded authenticity, consulting with law enforcement and legal experts to ensure each episode reflected the true complexity of real criminal cases. The show's measured pacing and precise narration create an almost forensic atmosphere—this is crime investigation rendered as serious drama, worthy of the attention of thoughtful listeners who wanted more than mere entertainment from their nighttime broadcasts.

So join us now as we examine the evidence, interview those involved, and uncover exactly what happened the night the Droot family's world ended in violence. *Crime Classics* airs tonight at 10 o'clock, Eastern Standard Time. Don't miss it.
