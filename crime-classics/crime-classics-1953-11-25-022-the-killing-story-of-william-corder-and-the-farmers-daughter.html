# Crime Classics: The Killing Story Of William Corder And The Farmer's Daughter

When the curtain rises on this chilling November broadcast, you'll find yourself transported to the misty English countryside of 1827, where a respectable gentleman harbors the darkest of secrets. William Corder's tale unfolds with the grim precision that *Crime Classics* has made its trademark—each revelation meticulously presented, each detail carefully placed to draw you deeper into a murder that shocked Victorian England. Listen as the announcer's grave voice guides you through the fateful encounters between Corder and Maria Marten, the farmer's daughter whose disappearance would ignite one of the era's most sensational trials. The Red Barn looms large in this narrative, a place of rural innocence that became synonymous with unspeakable tragedy. Every word carries weight; every pause builds unbearable tension.

*Crime Classics*, now in its triumphant run on CBS, has distinguished itself by dramatizing the most compelling true crimes from history's darker chapters, stripped of sensationalism yet laden with authentic human drama. Rather than manufacturing suspense, these writers find it already woven into the historical record—cases where real people made fatal choices that reverberated through generations. The Corder murder exemplifies this perfectly: a case that influenced British law enforcement, captured the public imagination through early true-crime pamphlets and broadsides, and remains a touchstone of criminal history. The show's commitment to accuracy, paired with first-rate dramatic performances, has made it essential listening for anyone fascinated by how justice and mystery intersect.

So dim the lights this Wednesday evening and prepare yourself for a journey back 126 years to a crime that scandalized a nation. *Crime Classics* awaits with the story of a murder that would echo through the ages—a tale of deception, betrayal, and the inexorable march of justice. Tune in and discover why history's greatest crimes continue to haunt us.
