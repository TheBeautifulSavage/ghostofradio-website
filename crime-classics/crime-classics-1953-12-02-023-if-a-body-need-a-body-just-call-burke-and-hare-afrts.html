# Crime Classics: "If A Body Need A Body, Just Call Burke And Hare"

Settle into your favorite chair and prepare yourself for a descent into the darkest corners of nineteenth-century Edinburgh, where two seemingly ordinary men orchestrated one of history's most chilling murder schemes. "If A Body Need A Body, Just Call Burke and Hare" brings listeners face-to-face with the true story of William Burke and William Hare—grave robbers turned cold-blooded killers who murdered sixteen innocent souls to supply the insatiable appetite of anatomists hungry for cadavers. This December 1953 broadcast strips away the gothic embellishments to reveal the horrifying reality: how desperation, poverty, and the booming demand for medical specimens created the perfect conditions for unthinkable evil. As the CBS actors bring these historical horrors to life, you'll hear the claustrophobic alleyways of Edinburgh, the whispered negotiations, and the tragic fates of victims whose only misfortune was crossing paths with these two monsters.

Crime Classics distinguished itself from sensationalized pulp by combining meticulous historical research with dramatic authenticity. Drawing from court records and contemporaneous accounts, the show's writers crafted narratives that educated as much as they thrilled, presenting true crime not as mere entertainment but as a window into the darker chapters of human civilization. The Burke and Hare case particularly resonated with 1950s audiences—a reminder that horror need not be fictional to chill the spine, and that society's most respectable institutions were sometimes complicit in its darkest crimes.

Don't miss this unforgettable journey into one of history's most notorious murder cases. Tune in to Crime Classics and discover why listeners across America made this CBS gem essential listening for anyone fascinated by the true stories that lurk beneath the surface of civilized society.
