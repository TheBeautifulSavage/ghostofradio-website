# Crime Classics: "Madeline Smith, Maid Or Murderess"

Picture yourself settling into your favorite chair on a winter evening in 1954, the crackling warmth of the radio filling your living room as the familiar opening theme fades into darkness. Tonight, Crime Classics brings you a tale of passion, poison, and Scottish scandal that captivated the Victorian world—the case of Madeline Smith, a respectable young woman whose love letters and a mysterious death would tear apart the fabric of Glasgow society. Was she a devoted maid caught in circumstance, or a calculating murderer who used arsenic to eliminate an inconvenient lover? As the drama unfolds through period testimony and expert reconstruction, you'll hear the voices of Victorian Scotland spring to life, the tension mounting with each revelation. The evidence is damning; the emotions are raw. By the program's end, you may find yourself as conflicted as the jury that heard her case.

Crime Classics has become essential listening for those who crave historical accuracy wrapped in the theatrical power of radio drama. Unlike sensational crime programs of the day, this CBS series treats each case with scholarly precision, drawing directly from court records, trial transcripts, and historical archives. The Smith episode exemplifies the show's commitment to complexity—presenting both prosecution and defense arguments fairly, allowing listeners to wrestle with genuine ambiguity rather than spoon-feeding convenient conclusions. This approach reflects a postwar sophistication, an audience eager to engage with moral questions that defy simple answers.

Don't miss this masterpiece of true crime broadcasting. Tune in to Crime Classics for "Madeline Smith, Maid Or Murderess"—a haunting journey into a case that remained unsolved in the hearts and minds of Victorians. Some mysteries, after all, were never meant to be forgotten.
