# Crime Classics: Twenty Three Knives Against Caesar

On the Ides of March—that fateful date forever etched into history—one of the ancient world's most powerful men was felled by the daggers of his own senators. "Twenty Three Knives Against Caesar" reconstructs the assassination of Julius Caesar in chilling detail, drawing listeners into the marble halls of Rome where ambition and paranoia collided in 44 B.C. You'll hear the whispered conspiracies in shadowed corridors, the final moments of a man who conquered nations but could not escape the blade, and the dramatic unraveling of a conspiracy that would reshape an empire. The program's skilled narrator guides you through the evidence—historical documents, eyewitness accounts, and the complex web of motives that bound together the conspirators, including Caesar's trusted friend Brutus. Every footstep echoes with tension; every dialogue crackles with the electric dread of impending violence.

Crime Classics has established itself as the gold standard of true crime radio drama since its 1953 debut on CBS, bringing listeners authentic historical crimes presented with the narrative drive of great fiction. Unlike sensationalized programs of the era, Crime Classics scrupulously researches each episode, consulting historical sources and primary accounts to ensure accuracy while maintaining gripping dramatic entertainment. This approach transforms real crimes into compelling human stories—tales of passion, greed, betrayal, and justice that resonate across centuries.

Don't miss this remarkable journey into ancient history's most infamous murder. Tune in to Crime Classics on Wednesday evening and discover how a single act of violence altered the course of Western civilization. History has never sounded so immediate, so dangerous, so utterly compelling.
