# Crime Classics: John Baptiste Troppmann, Killer of Many

Step into the shadowy world of mid-nineteenth-century France with this gripping installment of *Crime Classics*, where the chilling case of John Baptiste Troppmann unfolds with all the dark intrigue of a serialized murder spree. On this February evening in 1954, CBS brings you the methodical dismantling of one man's elaborate deceptions—a charming swindler who preyed upon the desperate hopes of working families, only to transform into something far more sinister. Listen as the drama builds from promises of fortune to unspeakable violence, tracking a killer whose callous brutality shocked even the crime-hardened authorities of the Belle Époque era. The tension crackles through your speaker as evidence mounts, motives become clear, and justice—slow and inexorable—finally catches up with a monster hiding behind a genteel façade.

*Crime Classics*, which premiered in 1953 as CBS's venture into historical true crime, distinguished itself by treating these dark chapters of criminal history with scholarly gravitas rather than exploitation. Each episode, narrated with measured authority, transported listeners across continents and centuries to examine the cases that defined criminology itself. The Troppmann case particularly exemplified the show's strength: drawing from actual court records and contemporary accounts to resurrect not merely sensational murders, but the forensic and investigative breakthroughs they inspired. This was serious drama rooted in historical reality—the golden age of radio refusing to shy away from humanity's darkest impulses.

Don't miss this extraordinary window into criminal history. Tune in to hear how one man's trail of destruction led to innovations in detective work that still echo through police procedures today. *Crime Classics* awaits those brave enough to venture into the archives of human tragedy.
