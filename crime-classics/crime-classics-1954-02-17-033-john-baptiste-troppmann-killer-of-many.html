# Crime Classics: John Baptiste Troppmann, Killer Of Many

Step into the shadowed world of 1870s France as Crime Classics reconstructs the chilling case of John Baptiste Troppmann, a cunning predator whose methodical murders shocked a nation still reeling from war. On this February evening in 1954, listeners will hear the meticulous dramatization of how Troppmann's greed and depravity led him to systematically eliminate an entire family—the Kinck household—along with numerous other victims whose bodies were discovered in shallow graves across the French countryside. The narrator's measured, authoritative voice guides you through the mounting evidence, the desperate manhunt, and the psychological portrait of a killer who believed himself cleverly above the law, all while period sound effects and understated dramatic music create an atmosphere thick with dread and the inexorable march toward justice.

Crime Classics, which aired on CBS during 1953 and 1954, distinguished itself among the crowded landscape of radio crime dramas by focusing exclusively on historical murders and their investigations. Rather than inventing sensational scenarios, each episode drew from actual case files, court records, and newspaper archives, lending an air of documentary authenticity to the proceedings. This particular episode examines a case from the so-called "Golden Age of Murder," a time when forensic techniques were primitive yet determined investigators still managed to piece together the truth. The show's commitment to historical accuracy made it educational as well as entertaining—listeners felt they were genuinely learning about criminology and the evolution of detective work.

Don't miss this haunting account of one of Europe's most prolific and calculating murderers. Tune in to Crime Classics on February 17th for a masterfully told true crime narrative that reminds us justice, though sometimes delayed, ultimately prevails.
