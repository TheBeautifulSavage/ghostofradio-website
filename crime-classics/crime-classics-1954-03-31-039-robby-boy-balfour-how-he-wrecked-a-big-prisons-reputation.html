The year is 1954, and CBS brings you deep into the shadowy corridors of American penal history with "Robby Boy Balfour: How He Wrecked A Big Prison's Reputation." Picture yourself hunched over your radio set, the amber dial glowing softly in the darkness, as narrator and creator Ellery Queen guides you through the true story of a cunning inmate whose schemes and manipulation threatened to topple the very foundation of institutional authority. This is no sanitized prison picture show—this is the raw, documented account of how one man's intelligence and ruthlessness exposed the vulnerabilities of the American correctional system, turning guards against administrators, sparking riots, and transforming a model facility into a house divided against itself. The tension builds with each revelation, as contemporary interviews blend with dramatic reenactment to reconstruct the events that shook the prison world.

Crime Classics stands apart in the golden age of radio as perhaps the most meticulously researched true crime program ever broadcast, treating each episode with the gravity of a courtroom proceeding while maintaining the narrative drive of genuine drama. In 1953-1954, CBS delivered these stories when America's fascination with crime had matured beyond pulp sensationalism—these were real cases, real criminals, and real consequences, presented by one of mystery fiction's greatest minds. Each episode peels back layers of American social history, revealing not just the crimes themselves, but the institutional failures and human frailties that allowed them to flourish.

Don't miss this unforgettable journey into the real underworld of American justice. Tune in to experience how one criminal mind exploited a system built to contain criminals—a story that remains as relevant today as it was when it first shattered the headlines.
