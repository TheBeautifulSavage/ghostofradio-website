# The Kid with a Gun

Step into the shadowed streets of post-war America as *Crime Does Not Pay* presents "The Kid with a Gun"—a harrowing descent into juvenile delinquency that will grip your radio speaker with the intensity of a detective's interrogation lamp. This October 1949 broadcast peels back the veneer of suburban innocence to expose how a single firearm and a moment of recklessness can shatter a young life forever. Listeners will find themselves in the tense company of a troubled teenager whose desperate choice echoes with tragic finality, while the show's masterful sound design—the crack of gunfire, the wail of sirens, the hollow footsteps of regret—transports you directly into this crime scene. The performances crackle with authenticity, each voice a thread in the dark tapestry of consequences that unfolds across thirty electric minutes.

During the late 1940s, *Crime Does Not Pay* occupied a unique position in American radio, transforming actual case files into moral instruction wrapped in thrilling drama. The show's creators believed deeply that real crimes, faithfully adapted, could serve as cautionary tales for listeners navigating an increasingly complex post-war society. "The Kid with a Gun" exemplifies this noble mission, drawing from genuine law enforcement records to examine how circumstance and choice intersect in the lives of America's youth. Unlike sensationalized crime programs, this series prioritized documentary accuracy and social responsibility, earning it respect from educators, law enforcement, and families alike.

Don't miss this unforgettable episode—a sobering reminder that crime's true cost cannot be measured in dollars, but in shattered potential and irreversible harm. Tune in to hear why *Crime Does Not Pay* became must-listen radio for a generation determined to learn from America's darkest moments.
