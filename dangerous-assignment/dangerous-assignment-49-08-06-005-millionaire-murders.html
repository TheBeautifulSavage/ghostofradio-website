# Dangerous Assignment – "Millionaire Murders" (August 6, 1949)

Picture the glittering lobbies of Manhattan's most exclusive penthouses, where old money whispers dangerous secrets behind mahogany doors. In this week's episode of *Dangerous Assignment*, Steve Mitchell—the globe-trotting troubleshooter for the mysterious Operator Five—finds himself ensnared in a baffling mystery where the wealthy are dropping like autumn leaves. Each victim a millionaire, each death seemingly impossible to solve. As Mitchell digs deeper into the shadowy world of high society's dark underbelly, the noose tightens around him. Was it murder or suicide? Is there a mastermind orchestrating these peculiar deaths, or is something far more sinister at play? The snapping of a lighter, the crackle of a telephone connection, and Brian Donlevy's cool, measured voice guide you through a labyrinth of clues, red herrings, and nail-biting suspense.

*Dangerous Assignment* captured post-war America's hunger for international intrigue at precisely the moment the Iron Curtain descended across Europe. Broadcasting from 1949 to 1953, the show's formula of globe-trotting mystery and Cold War paranoia proved irresistible to listeners seeking escapism without leaving their living rooms. Each episode sent Mitchell careening from Shanghai to Rio, from Cairo to Vienna—places that felt both exotic and ominously real to audiences watching tensions escalate worldwide. The show's lean, efficient storytelling and Donlevy's commanding presence made it a favorite among adventure enthusiasts, proving that sophisticated thrills didn't require extravagant production—only crisp writing and keen dramatic timing.

Don't miss "Millionaire Murders" as *Dangerous Assignment* delivers one of its most intricate puzzles, combining locked-room mystery with international espionage. Tune in now and discover why this 1949 classic still captivates fans of vintage radio drama.
