# Dangerous Assignment: Find Hired Killer Lupac

The streets of Paris shimmer with intrigue in this nail-biting installment as secret agent Steve Mitchell accepts his most perilous assignment yet—tracking down the elusive assassin known only as Lupac. With the City of Light as his hunting ground, Mitchell must navigate the shadowy world of international espionage, where a wrong turn could mean death and every shadow conceals a potential enemy. Listen as the pursuit intensifies through fog-shrouded boulevards and clandestine meeting places, where a dangerous killer moves silently between the cafe society, always one step ahead of our hero. The tension crackles through every scene as Mitchell closes in on his quarry, employing cunning and nerve in equal measure against an opponent who has evaded capture across three continents.

*Dangerous Assignment* stood apart in the crowded field of postwar adventure radio by offering listeners a sophisticated spy thriller rooted in the genuine anxieties of the emerging Cold War era. Premiering in 1949, just as international tensions began to simmer, the show tapped into America's fascination with global intrigue while maintaining a grittier, more realistic tone than its pulp adventure competitors. Host Brian Donlevy's measured narration lent gravitas to each episode, while the globe-trotting format—with assignments taking Mitchell from Paris to Prague to Havana—reflected the dawning realization that danger now lurked in every corner of the world. This particular episode exemplifies the show's formula at its finest: authentic European atmosphere, high stakes, and the constant reminder that in the shadowy world of espionage, trust is the rarest commodity of all.

If you're ready to step into the shadowed streets of post-war Paris and experience the cat-and-mouse game between Mitchell and his deadliest adversary, tune in now. *Dangerous Assignment* awaits—where every broadcast delivers pure, heart-pounding suspense.
