# Operation Fish Hook

Picture yourself huddled around the radio on a winter evening, as the dramatic orchestral overture fades and Steve Mitchell's commanding voice pulls you into the steaming jungles of Haiti. In "Operation Fish Hook," our intrepid secret agent finds himself embroiled in a dangerous plot involving stolen naval blueprints and a shadowy network of saboteurs operating in the Caribbean. The stakes have never been higher—with American security hanging in the balance, Mitchell must navigate treacherous mountain terrain, interrogate suspects in dimly lit colonial mansions, and outwit operatives who will stop at nothing to silence him. Every creaking door hinge, every whispered conversation in Creole patois, every close call with discovery will keep you suspended in delicious tension until the final, breathless reveal.

*Dangerous Assignment* captured the Cold War imagination of post-war America like few programs could, transforming the globe into a chessboard where good and evil clashed in exotic locales. Premiering in 1949, the show rode the crest of geopolitical paranoia and adventure-hungry audiences desperate to make sense of America's new role as a world power. Brian Donlevy's portrayal of Steve Mitchell—cool under pressure, resourceful, and unflappably American—became the template for secret agents that would dominate television and film for decades. Each episode whisked listeners away from suburban living rooms to actual hotspots of international intrigue: Havana, Monaco, Hong Kong, and here, the mystical island nation of Haiti, with its complex history and strategic importance to American interests.

Join us for this thrilling chapter in Steve Mitchell's ongoing mission to protect democracy abroad. "Operation Fish Hook" is waiting—and danger never announces itself before it strikes.
