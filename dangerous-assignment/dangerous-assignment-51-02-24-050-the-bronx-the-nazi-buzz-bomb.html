Picture this: New York City, 1945. The war against Nazi Germany rages overseas, yet somehow, inexplicably, a German V-1 flying bomb—a "buzz bomb"—has materialized in the Bronx. As our intrepid adventurer Brian Cameron arrives to investigate, the city teeters on the edge of panic. What sinister Nazi plot could possibly explain this weapon's presence in America's own backyard? Is sabotage afoot? A daring spy operation? The ticking clock of danger crescendos as Cameron must race against time, navigating the grimy streets and shadowy docks of the outer boroughs, interviewing suspects and uncovering a conspiracy that threatens the very safety of the nation's largest city. The atmosphere crackles with Cold War paranoia mixed with the raw urgency of wartime America.

Dangerous Assignment was the vehicle that made Brian Cameron—and his portrayer, Brian Donlevy—household names during radio's golden age. The show's genius lay in its authentic blend of globe-trotting espionage and homegrown American peril, never letting listeners forget that danger lurked both abroad and in their own neighborhoods. This particular episode exemplifies the program's genius: rooted in genuine wartime anxieties about Nazi technology and sabotage, yet thrilling enough to transport audiences far beyond their living rooms. With its snappy dialogue, tense sound design, and Donlevy's steely delivery, Dangerous Assignment captured the post-war paranoia and sense of adventure that defined late-1940s America.

Tune in today to experience the very essence of classic adventure radio—where peril knows no borders, and one man's courage stands between civilization and chaos. Will Cameron solve the mystery of the Bronx buzz bomb before disaster strikes?
