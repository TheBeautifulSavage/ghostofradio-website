# Dangerous Assignment: "Epidemic Needle In A Haystack"

Picture this: a remote village gripped by an invisible killer, and Steve Mitchell—that intrepid globe-trotting troubleshooter—racing against time to locate the single vial of vaccine that stands between civilization and catastrophe. As your radio crackles to life on this May evening in 1951, you'll be transported to a world where disease spreads faster than answers, where every shadow harbors suspicion, and where one man's determination to find a needle in an epidemiological haystack becomes humanity's last hope. The tension builds with each tick of the clock, each false lead, each agonizing interview with villagers who may—or may not—know something crucial. This is radio drama at its finest: intimate, urgent, and utterly gripping.

*Dangerous Assignment* thrived during that golden age when Americans hungered for tales of heroism and international intrigue. The show's genius lay in its ability to blend contemporary Cold War anxieties with timeless adventure, featuring real locations and plausible crises that could keep listeners awake long into the night. Brian Donlevy's portrayal of Mitchell—cool under pressure, resourceful, incorruptible—embodied the American ideal of the immediate postwar era. These weren't supernatural mysteries or masked vigilantes; they were real-world problems solved through ingenuity and courage. This particular episode, expertly crafted by writer Landsburg, exemplifies the show's trademark blend of globe-trotting action and genuine human stakes.

Tune in now and experience the crackling authenticity of 1950s network radio. Feel the desperation, the danger, the determination. Let Steve Mitchell guide you through a world where one man truly can make a difference—if he survives long enough to find the answers. *Dangerous Assignment* awaits.
