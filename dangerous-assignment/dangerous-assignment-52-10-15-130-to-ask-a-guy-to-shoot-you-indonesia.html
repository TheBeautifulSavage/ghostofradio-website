# Dangerous Assignment: "To Ask A Guy To Shoot You"

Picture yourself huddled around the radio on that October evening in 1952, the crackle of static giving way to an ominous orchestral swell. Somewhere in the steaming jungles of Indonesia, international operative Steve Mitchell finds himself in an impossible position—one that demands he ask a trusted ally to do the unthinkable. As rebels close in and escape routes vanish into the mist-shrouded landscape, Mitchell must convince someone to pull the trigger, making a desperate gambit that tests the bonds of loyalty and survival itself. This is espionage stripped of glamour, reduced to raw human choice in the face of impossible odds. The humid jungle atmosphere practically seeps through the speaker as our protagonist navigates a web of intrigue where trust is the most dangerous currency of all.

*Dangerous Assignment* represented the golden age of international adventure radio—a time when audiences were captivated by tales of American ingenuity operating beyond the reach of law and comfort. Broadcast during the height of Cold War anxieties, the series tapped into postwar fascination with global intrigue and exotic locales, while reflecting the era's confidence in American problem-solvers operating in uncertain foreign territories. Brian Donlevy's portrayal of Steve Mitchell became iconic precisely because he played a man forced to improvise, to negotiate, to survive through cunning rather than conventional heroics. Each episode, meticulously crafted for maximum dramatic tension, transported listeners to corners of the world most would never see.

Tune in now and experience the moment when moral certainty collapses under pressure, when the only way out requires asking the impossible of another person. *Dangerous Assignment* awaits—where every decision carries the weight of life and death, and desperation makes philosophers of us all.
