# Dangerous Assignment: A Nursery Rhyme

Picture yourself huddled around the radio on a November evening in 1951, the winter darkness pressing against your windows as exotic strings swell and that unmistakable voice cuts through the static: "Dangerous Assignment!" This week's adventure carries you to the frozen streets of Stockholm, where American operative Steve Mitchell must unravel a sinister mystery hidden within the innocent verses of a children's nursery rhyme. What begins as a routine intelligence gathering mission transforms into a deadly game of espionage, where nothing—not even a child's bedtime poem—can be trusted. As ominous Nordic shadows close in and shadowy figures move through snow-laden alleyways, you'll find yourself gripping your armrest, wondering whether Mitchell can decode the cryptic message before time runs out.

*Dangerous Assignment* arrived at the dawn of the Cold War, when American audiences hungered for tales of brave operatives navigating an increasingly complex global stage. Produced by Frederic W. Ziv and starring Brian Donlevy as the resourceful Steve Mitchell, the show became NBC's answer to growing public fascination with international intrigue and espionage. Each episode whisked listeners to a different corner of the world—from Scandinavia to the Far East—where danger lurked behind every corner and cultural mysteries demanded solving. The show's appeal lay not merely in action, but in its sophisticated blend of genuine geographic detail and high-stakes Cold War tension, all delivered with the polish and craftsmanship that defined the golden age of radio drama.

Tune in this Friday evening at 8 o'clock for "A Nursery Rhyme." Bring your wits, your nerve, and your imagination. You won't need anything else.
