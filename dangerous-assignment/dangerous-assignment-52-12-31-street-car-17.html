# Dangerous Assignment: Street Car #17

Picture this: the screech of steel wheels on wet rails cutting through a fog-laden European night, while our intrepid troubleshooter finds himself trapped aboard a mysterious streetcar with secrets as dangerous as the shadowy figures pursuing him. In "Street Car #17," the tension mounts with every stop, every passenger a potential threat, every moment a chance for deadly revelation. As the tram lurches through cobblestone streets toward an unknown destination, our hero must navigate a web of espionage, blackmail, and betrayal—all while the conductor's bell tolls like a countdown to catastrophe. Will he uncover the truth before the final stop, or will this assignment prove to be his last?

*Dangerous Assignment* captured the imagination of post-war American audiences who craved excitement and international intrigue during an era when the world felt simultaneously smaller and more threatening. Broadcasting from 1949 to 1953, the show epitomized the golden age of radio adventure, featuring the resourceful Brian Cameron—a troubleshooter for the State Department—solving international crises with quick wit and quicker reflexes. This particular episode, recorded in the early 1950s, exemplifies the show's masterful use of sound design and pacing, leveraging radio's unique power to create claustrophobic tension and vivid atmosphere through nothing but voices, music, and effects. The confined setting of a moving streetcar becomes a pressure cooker of suspense, a microcosm of Cold War paranoia.

Join us as the wheels keep turning and danger lurks around every corner. Tune in to "Street Car #17" and discover why *Dangerous Assignment* remains a thrilling testament to radio's golden age—where imagination transforms a simple journey into an unforgettable adventure.
