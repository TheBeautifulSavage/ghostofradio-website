# Dangerous Assignment: Colonel Chan (New Guinea)

Deep in the steaming jungles of New Guinea, where civilization surrenders to primordial wilderness, our intrepid assignment editor faces a mission unlike any before. A rogue military officer—the mysterious Colonel Chan—has gone silent, and his disappearance threatens to unravel delicate post-war alliances in the Pacific. As our hero ventures into uncharted territory where cannibals roam and Japanese stragglers still lurk in hidden compounds, each shadow could conceal either salvation or death. The humid night air throbs with danger, radio static crackles with incomplete intelligence reports, and time is running out. This January 7th episode crackles with the authentic tension of frontier espionage, where every decision could mean the difference between exposing a conspiracy and vanishing without a trace into the jungle's waiting darkness.

*Dangerous Assignment* thrived on this intoxicating blend of real-world geopolitics and pulp adventure, launching in 1949 when the world was still reeling from the Second World War's conclusion. The Cold War tensions were mounting, and American audiences hungered for stories that reflected genuine global intrigue—the show delivered exactly that, though filtered through the thrilling lens of international derring-do. Brian Donlevy's gravelly narration became iconic, guiding listeners through locations that dominated postwar headlines: Berlin, Bangkok, Cairo, and now the contested territories of New Guinea. The writing crackled with verisimilitude, often drawing from actual geopolitical situations while maintaining the breathless excitement that made listeners lean closer to their sets.

Don't miss this electrifying adventure into colonial mystery and jungle intrigue. Tune in to *Dangerous Assignment* and discover why this program captivated millions of listeners, proving that the world's most dangerous assignments made for radio's most thrilling drama.
