# The Hired Assassin

Picture yourself settling into your favorite chair as the opening theme swells—urgent, mysterious, tinged with Latin percussion—and you're transported to the sultry streets of Havana, where danger lurks in every shadow. In this May 27th broadcast, our globe-trotting protagonist finds himself ensnared in a deadly game of cat and mouse. An assassin has been hired to silence him, and in the Cuban capital's labyrinth of casinos, cantinas, and corruption, he must stay one step ahead of a killer he's never seen. The tension mounts with each scene: coded messages, double-crosses, and the ticking clock of impending doom. Will our hero survive the night, or will Havana become his final resting place?

*Dangerous Assignment* captured the imagination of post-war America during its four-year run, offering listeners the perfect escape into exotic locales and international intrigue. The show's strength lay in its authenticity—detailed research into real cities and genuine political tensions gave each episode a documentary-like credibility that separated it from pure fantasy adventures. Set in the volatile 1940s, when Cold War anxieties simmered just beneath the surface and espionage seemed to lurk around every corner, episodes like "The Hired Assassin" tapped into very real contemporary fears. The Cuba setting is particularly poignant, broadcasting just a decade before Castro's revolution would transform the island nation forever.

Whether you're a devoted fan of classic radio drama or new to the medium's golden age, this episode offers everything that made *Dangerous Assignment* unmissable appointment listening: superb sound design, crackling dialogue, and genuine stakes. Tune in to experience radio adventure at its finest—where your imagination remains the most powerful special effect.
