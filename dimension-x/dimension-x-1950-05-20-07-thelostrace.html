# Dimension X: "The Lost Race"

On a distant world shrouded in eternal twilight, humanity discovers the crumbling remnants of a civilization that should not exist. As your radio crackles to life on this May evening in 1950, you'll find yourself transported across the vast gulfs of space to witness a haunting encounter with the unknown. "The Lost Race" unfolds with mounting dread as our explorers uncover the terrible truth behind an ancient, abandoned city—and confront the possibility that they themselves may be the instruments of their own extinction. The sound design pulls you into claustrophobic corridors and echo-filled chambers where every shadow harbors mystery, while the cast delivers performances tinged with genuine fear and philosophical wonder. This is science fiction at its most intimate and unsettling, not content merely to astonish but determined to make you *think*.

*Dimension X* arrived at a peculiar moment in American radio history, when the golden age of the medium was already dimming under the glow of television sets spreading through suburban living rooms. Yet NBC's ambitious anthology series refused to surrender to the new technology, instead offering listeners something television could barely attempt in 1950—unlimited imagination. Each episode represented a collaboration between some of science fiction's finest minds and radio's most talented craftspeople, creating soundscapes of impossible worlds that remain unmatched in their ability to conjure vivid futures and alien vistas. "The Lost Race" exemplifies the series' commitment to intelligent, thought-provoking storytelling, exploring themes of cyclical history and cosmic irony.

Settle into your chair, dim the lights, and prepare yourself for a journey beyond the known dimensions. *Dimension X* awaits—where the impossible becomes unforgettably real.
