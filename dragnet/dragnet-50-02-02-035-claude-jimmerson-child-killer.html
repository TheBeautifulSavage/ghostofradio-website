Dragnet 50 02 02 035 Claude Jimmerson Child Killer

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

The streets of Los Angeles grow cold as Sergeant Joe Friday and Officer Ben Romero pursue one of the most harrowing cases in the annals of the LAPD—the hunt for Claude Jimmerson, a predator who has targeted the city's most vulnerable. This episode strips away any romantic notions of detective work, plunging listeners into the methodical, often grim reality of homicide investigation. You'll follow Friday's clipped, matter-of-fact narration as he and Romero canvas neighborhoods, interview witnesses, and piece together evidence with the inexorable patience of men who know that every hour lost might mean another child in danger. The tension builds not through histrionics or manufactured suspense, but through the quiet, terrifying competence of professional lawmen closing in on a monster.

What made Dragnet revolutionary was creator Jack Webb's commitment to authenticity. Working directly with LAPD consultants and drawing from actual case files, Webb crafted a procedural that felt disturbingly real to 1949 audiences—a stark contrast to the glamorized detectives and far-fetched plots of earlier radio crime shows. This episode exemplifies that approach: there are no convenient coincidences, no dramatic confrontations, only the unglamorous work of police procedure. The show became a cultural phenomenon precisely because it honored the everyday heroism of police work while confronting listeners with the dark realities that officers faced on city streets.

This is essential radio drama—gripping, unflinching, and historically significant. Tune in to experience why Dragnet became one of the most acclaimed shows of radio's golden age and later dominated television. It's police work as it really was.
