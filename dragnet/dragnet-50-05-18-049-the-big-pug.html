# Dragnet 50 05 18 049 The Big Pug

The streets of Los Angeles are dark and rain-slicked as Sergeant Joe Friday and Officer Ben Romero answer the call. A small-time boxer known only as "The Big Pug" has vanished, and the case leads our detectives through the shadowy underbelly of the city's fight game—a world of fixed matches, gambling debts, and desperate men. What begins as a routine missing persons report evolves into something far more sinister, with each interview peeling back another layer of deception. Listeners will experience the methodical, unflinching interrogation technique that made Dragnet legendary: the patient questioning, the careful note-taking, the relentless pursuit of fact over assumption. Every clue matters. Every contradiction counts.

This 1950 episode exemplifies why Dragnet became a cultural phenomenon that shaped American law enforcement for generations. Created by and starring Jack Webb, the show abandoned the sensationalism that dominated radio crime dramas, instead presenting police work as it actually was—tedious, procedural, and grounded in the mundane details that crack cases. Working directly with the Los Angeles Police Department, Webb achieved unprecedented authenticity, broadcasting actual case files and police methods to millions of Americans. By the time "The Big Pug" aired, Dragnet had already become must-listen radio, pioneering a documentary realism that influenced everything from television to literature.

For the modern listener seeking to understand the golden age of radio drama, this episode remains essential listening. Experience the crackling tension of a real procedural investigation, hear the legendary rapport between Friday and his partner, and discover why America fell in love with this unglamorous portrait of police work. Tune in to Dragnet 50-05-18-049 and step into a Los Angeles frozen in time—where facts alone drive the narrative, and justice waits at the end of thorough, determined detective work.
