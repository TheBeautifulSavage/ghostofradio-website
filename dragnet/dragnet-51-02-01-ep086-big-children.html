# Dragnet: Big Children

On a cold Los Angeles night in 1951, Sergeant Joe Friday and Officer Ben Romero face a case that strikes at the heart of the city's most vulnerable. "Big Children" follows the relentless detectives as they investigate a crime that shatters the innocence of youth, moving methodically through interviews, evidence, and the grim realities of post-war urban America. Jack Webb's deadpan narration cuts through the darkness with surgical precision, each clue meticulously catalogued, each witness statement weighed carefully in the balance of justice. Listeners will feel the weight of the Los Angeles Police Department's determination to protect those who cannot protect themselves, as the investigation unfolds with the documentary-like authenticity that made Dragnet appointment radio for millions across the nation.

By 1951, Dragnet had become far more than entertainment—it was a cultural institution that shaped America's understanding of law enforcement itself. Webb, who served as technical advisor with LAPD blessing, pioneered a revolutionary approach that abandoned melodrama for stark procedural realism. The show's influence extended beyond the radio waves; it legitimized police work as heroic and essential, presented the LAPD as professional and trustworthy, and established the template for decades of crime fiction to follow. "Big Children" represents the show at the height of its powers, when listeners tuned in not for easy answers but for a meditation on duty, loss, and the thin line separating order from chaos in the modern city.

Step into the squad room and join Sergeant Friday on a case that demands your full attention. The evidence is waiting, the mystery is unfolding, and justice—as always—hangs in the balance. This is Dragnet.
