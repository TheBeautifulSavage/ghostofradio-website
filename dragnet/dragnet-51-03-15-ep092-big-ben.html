# Dragnet: "Big Ben" (March 15, 1951)

The fog rolls thick over Los Angeles tonight as Sergeant Joe Friday and Officer Ben Romero answer the call that will test their detective instincts to the breaking point. A seemingly routine missing persons case spirals into a labyrinth of false leads and mounting desperation when a prominent businessman vanishes without a trace. As the hours tick away with methodical precision, listeners will experience the grinding procedural work that defines real police investigation—the unglamorous interviews, the tedious cross-references, the nagging hunches that separate amateur guessing from professional detective work. Jack Webb's matter-of-fact narration cuts through the Los Angeles night like a searchlight, illuminating the dark corners where crime hides and justice waits to be served.

This 1951 episode exemplifies what made *Dragnet* revolutionary for American radio. While most crime dramas of the era relied on melodrama and sensationalism, Webb's creation pioneered a documentary-style realism that captivated post-war audiences hungry for authenticity. Born from Webb's own experience as a reserve Los Angeles police officer, each episode was meticulously researched and approved by the LAPD itself, grounding the show's narrative in procedural truth. "Big Ben" showcases the show's signature formula at its finest—real police work, real consequences, and real moral clarity delivered with unflinching directness.

Pull up a chair, darken the room, and let the crackle of the radio transport you to 1951 Los Angeles. This is police work as it actually happens: methodical, measured, and utterly compelling. Tune in and discover why *Dragnet* became the gold standard for police procedurals across every medium that followed.
