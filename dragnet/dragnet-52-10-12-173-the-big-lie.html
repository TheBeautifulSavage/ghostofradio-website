# The Big Lie

As the unmistakable Dragnet theme punctuates the evening air—"Dum-da-dum-dum!"—Sergeant Joe Friday and Officer Ben Romero find themselves entangled in a web of deception that strikes at the very heart of Los Angeles. In "The Big Lie," a seemingly straightforward investigation spirals into something far more sinister when a single falsehood threatens to derail justice itself. Listeners will hear the crisp snap of detective work as Friday methodically peels back layers of misdirection, his relentless pursuit of truth echoing through the streets of the city. The tension builds with each interview, each contradiction, as two dedicated men race against the clock to separate fact from fiction before an innocent person pays the price.

This program exemplifies Dragnet at its peak—a show that transformed police procedural drama from mere entertainment into a cultural institution. Created by and starring Jack Webb, Dragnet pioneered the documentary-style approach to crime drama, working directly with the Los Angeles Police Department to ensure authenticity. Every detail matters: the case numbers, the names changed to protect the innocent, the methodical police work that America had come to admire during an era when law and order symbolized societal stability. These weren't Hollywood fabrications—they were the real stories of real cops doing the unglamorous work of detective investigation, broadcast to millions of eager listeners who tuned in to understand the world around them.

If you've never experienced the taut drama of classic Dragnet, "The Big Lie" is the perfect entry point. Settle in, dim the lights, and let yourself be transported back to 1950s Los Angeles. You'll understand immediately why this show captivated a generation.
