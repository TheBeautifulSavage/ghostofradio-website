# Dragnet: "The Big Gun, Part 2"

The Los Angeles night is thick with danger as Sergeant Joe Friday and Officer Ben Romero close in on a killer armed with a stolen .45 caliber pistol. In part two of "The Big Gun," the investigation intensifies with each passing hour—fingerprint evidence, witness statements, and dogged police work converge to reveal the identity of a dangerous criminal still loose on the city streets. Listeners will find themselves riding alongside the LAPD's finest as they navigate the gritty underbelly of 1950s Los Angeles, where a single firearm can shatter lives and test the limits of detective work. The tension mounts with each clue uncovered, each suspect questioned, and each false lead followed to its bitter end. This is police work as it actually happened—methodical, unglamorous, and absolutely riveting.

*Dragnet* revolutionized radio and later television by stripping away melodrama to present crime investigation with documentary-like authenticity. Creator and star Jack Webb worked directly with the LAPD, drawing cases from actual department files with names and details changed only to protect the innocent. When *Dragnet* premiered in 1949, audiences had never heard anything quite like it—the signature theme, those stark sound effects, and Webb's deadpan narration created an entirely new standard for police procedurals. By 1953, when "The Big Gun" aired, the show had become a cultural phenomenon that fundamentally shaped how Americans viewed law enforcement.

Don't miss the electrifying conclusion of this two-part investigation. Tune in to experience *Dragnet* at its finest: authentic police procedure, compelling human drama, and the sound of a city's finest working through the night to bring a killer to justice. This is the real Los Angeles Police Department—this is *Dragnet*.
