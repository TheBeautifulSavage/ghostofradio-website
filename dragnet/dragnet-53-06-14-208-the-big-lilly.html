In the neon-soaked streets of Los Angeles, Sergeant Joe Friday and Officer Ben Romero face their most dangerous assignment yet—tracking down the elusive "Big Lilly," a ruthless operator whose criminal enterprise has left a trail of victims across the city. With clipped dialogue and methodical precision, this June 1949 episode unfolds like a genuine police investigation, pulling listeners into smoke-filled interrogation rooms and through the shadowy underworld of mid-century crime. The atmosphere crackles with tension as the detectives follow lead after lead, each clue bringing them closer to a confrontation with one of Los Angeles' most wanted criminals. You'll experience the grinding, unglamorous work of real police procedure—no heroic shootouts, just detective work, determination, and the relentless pursuit of justice that defined Jack Webb's groundbreaking vision.

Dragnet revolutionized radio entertainment by abandoning the melodrama and sensationalism of earlier crime shows. Webb, a technical consultant with the LAPD, insisted on absolute authenticity—every case was based on actual police files, every procedure verified, every detail authentic. This revolutionary approach transformed the genre and made Dragnet not merely entertainment but a window into the actual machinery of law enforcement. The show became a cultural phenomenon, spawning a television series and motion pictures, all while maintaining Webb's uncompromising commitment to realism. Episodes like "The Big Lilly" showcase why audiences embraced the show's documentary-style approach, finding genuine drama in procedure rather than fiction.

Tune in now to experience Dragnet as millions did in 1949—where justice isn't glamorous, but it's relentless, and every case tells a story ripped from the case files of the Los Angeles Police Department itself.
