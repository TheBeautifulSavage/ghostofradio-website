# The Big Try

On this September evening in 1949, Sergeant Joe Friday and Officer Frank Smith answer a call that will test the very limits of police procedure and human persistence. *The Big Try* follows our detectives as they navigate a case that refuses to yield its secrets easily—a methodical, sometimes grinding investigation that showcases the real work behind the badge, where patience and procedure often matter more than dramatic breakthroughs. The Los Angeles streets are dark and unforgiving, and somewhere in the city, answers wait to be uncovered through nothing but dogged determination and careful police work. Listeners will experience the authentic rhythm of an actual investigation, complete with dead ends, procedural details, and the mounting tension that comes when a case hangs in the balance.

*Dragnet* revolutionized American radio and television by stripping away the melodrama and presenting police work as it truly existed—unglamorous, methodical, and bound by regulation. Created by and starring Jack Webb, the show aired with the official cooperation of the Los Angeles Police Department, lending it an unprecedented authenticity. Each episode was a masterclass in procedural storytelling, the kind that would influence law enforcement dramas for decades to come. Webb's flat, matter-of-fact delivery became iconic, and the show's commitment to realistic detail set a new standard for the crime drama genre. This particular episode represents that golden age of radio when millions of Americans gathered around their sets to experience the unglamorous truth of police work.

Don't miss *The Big Try*—tune in and discover why *Dragnet* captivated a nation and became the template for every police procedural that followed. Just the facts await.
