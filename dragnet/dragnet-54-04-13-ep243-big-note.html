# Dragnet: "Big Note" (April 13, 1954)

On a fog-shrouded Los Angeles night, Sergeant Joe Friday walks the beat with methodical precision, his fedora pulled low against the damp chill. A counterfeiting operation is spreading false currency through the city's underworld, and Friday—that tireless LAPD detective whose monotone delivery masks an unrelenting pursuit of justice—is determined to follow the evidence wherever it leads. This episode crackles with the authentic procedural tension that made *Dragnet* appointment radio: the stakeouts, the interrogations, the painstaking cross-examination of suspects who crack under Friday's patient, implacable questioning. Listeners will experience the gritty reality of police work stripped of Hollywood glamour, each clue methodically catalogued, each detail recorded in Friday's mental case file like entries in an evidence log.

Since its 1949 debut, *Dragnet* has captivated millions by offering something revolutionary for its time—a show that treated law enforcement with documentary realism. Created by and starring Jack Webb, the series was groundbreaking in its collaboration with the LAPD itself, lending the program an air of absolute authenticity that competitors could only imitate. Webb's sparse, just-the-facts approach became the template for police procedurals that would dominate television and radio for decades to come. By 1954, when "Big Note" aired, the show had already become a cultural institution, influencing public perception of police work and establishing the framework for an entirely new genre.

Don't miss your chance to experience the brilliance of classic crime radio at its zenith. Tune in to "Big Note" and let Sergeant Friday's unflinching investigation pull you into the shadowy streets of 1950s Los Angeles, where justice is pursued one fact at a time.
