# The Big Heel

Step into the fog-laden streets of Los Angeles on a sweltering July night in 1954 as Sergeant Joe Friday and Officer Frank Smith pursue a case that cuts straight to the heart of post-war corruption. "The Big Heel" plunges listeners into a world where a seemingly minor incident spirals into a web of shakedowns, intimidation, and the kind of criminal arrogance that preys on ordinary citizens trying to make an honest living. With each clue methodically uncovered and each witness carefully interrogated, you'll experience the exacting police work that made *Dragnet* the most authentic crime drama of the era—no glamour, no shortcuts, just the facts and the relentless pursuit of justice.

*Dragnet* revolutionized American radio by stripping away the melodrama that defined earlier detective serials. Jack Webb's brainchild, drawn from actual LAPD case files and consulted on by real detectives, brought unprecedented realism to the airwaves. By 1954, the show had become a cultural touchstone, influencing everything from television to police training itself. Each episode's matter-of-fact narration, sparse sound design, and procedural rigor created an almost documentary quality that made listeners feel they were riding along with the LAPD. "The Big Heel" exemplifies this approach—a crime story told with the precision of an official report, yet compelling enough to keep millions glued to their sets.

Don't miss this opportunity to experience radio drama at its finest. Tune in to "The Big Heel" and discover why *Dragnet* remains the gold standard of police procedurals. Just the facts, and nothing but.
