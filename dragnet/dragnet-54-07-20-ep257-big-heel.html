# Dragnet: "The Big Heel"

As the distinctive four-note theme strikes through your radio speaker—*dum-dum-dum-dum*—you're transported back to the neon-soaked streets of Los Angeles, where Sergeant Joe Friday and Officer Bill Gannon are about to unravel a case of blackmail, betrayal, and a criminal who thinks he's untouchable. In "The Big Heel," the LAPD's most methodical investigators follow a cold trail of evidence through the city's shadowy underworld, where one man's arrogance becomes his undoing. The sharp dialogue crackles with authenticity, each clue meticulously documented, each interview stripped bare of emotion—just the facts, delivered with Friday's trademark deadpan precision. You'll hear the authentic sounds of 1950s Los Angeles police work: the squawk of radio dispatch, the measured footsteps through interrogation rooms, the quiet click of a case finally clicking into place.

*Dragnet* revolutionized crime radio by pioneering a documentary-style approach that was virtually unheard of in 1949. Creator-star Jack Webb brought unprecedented realism to the format, working directly with the LAPD to ensure procedural accuracy, earning the department's official cooperation and technical consultants. Rather than sensationalism, Webb delivered methodology—showing listeners exactly how real detectives solved crimes through patient investigation and careful observation. This episode, from the show's peak years on NBC, represents *Dragnet* at its finest: intelligent, gripping, and utterly authentic.

Don't miss your chance to experience radio's most influential crime drama when it aired at the height of post-war America's fascination with law and order. Tune in to "The Big Heel" and discover why millions of listeners made *Dragnet* appointment radio.
