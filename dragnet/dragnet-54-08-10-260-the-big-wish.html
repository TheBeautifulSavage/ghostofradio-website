# The Big Wish

When the opening strains of *Dragnet*'s iconic theme pierce the static on your dial, Sergeant Joe Friday steps into the smog-choked streets of Los Angeles with nothing but a notepad and an unshakeable determination to get the facts—just the facts. In "The Big Wish," that methodical approach to justice collides with the desperate dreams of ordinary people pushed to the breaking point. A simple desire becomes a dangerous obsession, and before the night is through, Friday and his partner must untangle a web of lies, desperation, and consequences that ripple through the city's underbelly. The tension builds with each question, each clue, each step closer to the truth, as the LAPD's finest piece together what really happened.

*Dragnet* fundamentally transformed American radio and television by stripping away melodrama and replacing it with stark realism. Jack Webb, who created and starred as Friday, revolutionized police procedurals by working directly with the LAPD, basing storylines on real cases and bringing authentic police terminology and procedure to living rooms across the nation. These weren't tales of heroic showdowns—they were methodical, sometimes mundane investigations that revealed the human cost of crime. The show's documentary-style approach made listeners feel like they were riding along in an actual patrol car, and the 1949-1957 NBC run represents the golden age when radio drama could still captivate millions.

Settle in with the lights dimmed low and prepare for an evening of masterful storytelling. *Dragnet* delivers the kind of compelling, character-driven drama that made audiences return week after week, eager to discover what mysteries awaited them in the City of Angels.
