# Dragnet: "Big Coins" — November 9, 1954

Picture this: it's late evening in Los Angeles, and Sergeant Joe Friday is back on the beat, his gravelly voice cutting through the darkness with just the facts—nothing more, nothing less. In "Big Coins," Friday and Officer Bill Gannon find themselves tracking a counterfeiting ring that threatens to flood the city with bogus currency. What begins as a routine investigation spirals into a web of criminal deception, where every lead matters, every detail could break the case, and danger lurks in the shadows of seemingly ordinary locations. You'll hear the methodical footsteps of law enforcement, the crackling police radio, and the tension mounting as our detectives close in on their suspects. This isn't Hollywood theatrics—it's the unglamorous, meticulous work of real detective work brought vividly to life through sound.

Dragnet revolutionized American radio by dispensing with the romantic notions of crime-fighting that had dominated earlier detective programs. Created by and starring Jack Webb, the show pioneered a documentary-style realism that would influence television, film, and the very way America viewed its police force. With technical cooperation from the Los Angeles Police Department and scripts rooted in actual case files, Dragnet lent unprecedented authenticity to the procedural genre. By 1954, the show had become a cultural institution, and episodes like "Big Coins" exemplified why audiences trusted Webb's unflinching, no-nonsense approach to crime drama.

Don't miss this gripping chapter in Dragnet's legendary run. Tune in to experience the craftsmanship, the tension, and the unvarnished truth of detective work that made this show an American classic. Just the facts—and they're waiting for you.
