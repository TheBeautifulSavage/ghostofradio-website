# Duffy's Tavern – September 22, 1944

Step behind the mahogany bar of America's favorite gin mill as Teixeira's establishment welcomes the radiant presence of Hollywood starlet Gene Tierney on this September evening in 1944. With the war raging across two oceans and home front tensions running high, listeners tune in for their weekly dose of ribald humor and the warm camaraderie of a New York City tavern where romance, intrigue, and comedic mishaps flow as freely as the drinks. As the bell above the door chimes and that unforgettable opening line crackles through the speaker—"Hello, Duffy!"—audiences are transported to a sanctuary where servicemen, working girls, and neighborhood characters intersect in scenarios both hilarious and oddly touching. The presence of Tierney, fresh from her starring role in *Laura*, adds an electric glamour to the proceedings, promising an evening of sophisticated banter between the Hollywood elite and Duffy's regulars.

What makes *Duffy's Tavern* a touchstone of American radio comedy is its singular alchemy: it's simultaneously a saloon and a mirror held up to wartime American society. Created by Ed Gardner, who voiced the fast-talking Duffy, the show pioneered the sitcom format on radio while never forgetting its roots in genuine human vulnerability. Celebrity guests appeared regularly, but they were never treated as untouchable; they were fellow customers to be needled, befriended, and drawn into the tavern's peculiar logic. This AFRS broadcast—recorded for Armed Forces Radio Service distribution to troops worldwide—represents radio at its most democratic, bringing world-class entertainment and a slice of home to GIs fighting far from shore.

Don't miss this opportunity to experience a lost golden age of entertainment, when a celebrity guest star meant something profound: a moment of shared laughter in uncertain times. Tune in and discover why *Duffy's Tavern* remained America's most beloved radio comedy for over a decade.
