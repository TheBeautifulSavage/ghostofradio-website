Duffy's Tavern 49 03 16 Archie Opens A Bank Account With $10

Step inside Duffy's Tavern on this particular Tuesday evening and you'll find Archie Andrews, that fast-talking, ever-scheming manager, on the verge of what he's convinced himself is his greatest financial endeavor yet—opening a bank account with a mere ten dollars. What could possibly go wrong? Listeners in 1949 knew that Archie's grandiose plans inevitably collided with reality in the most hilarious ways, and this episode promises the trademark verbal slapstick and rapid-fire one-liners that made the show essential appointment radio. As Archie navigates the intimidating marble halls of high finance with nothing but his wits and a crumpled bill, the supporting cast—from the perpetually missing Duffy himself to the bartenders and oddball patrons who populated the tavern—weaves a comedy tapestry of misunderstandings, mixed signals, and magnificently mangled logic.

For over a decade, Duffy's Tavern remained one of radio's most consistently inventive comedies, thriving on the natural chemistry between its regular cast and the cavalcade of celebrity guests who would pop in for cameos. The show captured the democratic spirit of the American tavern itself—a space where millionaires and dock workers, con artists and innocents, collided in equal measure. Archie's character, brought to life with theatrical relish, embodied the hustler's eternal optimism, that American belief that fortune was always just one scheme away. By 1949, the show had already secured its place in radio history, and episodes like this one showcased why audiences couldn't resist returning week after week.

Tune in and witness Archie's folly unfold in real time—where ten dollars becomes an odyssey, and banking becomes an adventure. This is classic radio comedy at its finest.
