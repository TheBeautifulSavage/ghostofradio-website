# Duffy's Tavern - April 13, 1949

Step up to the mahogany bar of this Greenwich Village institution and prepare yourself for an evening of mistaken identity and comedic chaos. When a mysterious wealthy gentleman arrives at Duffy's Tavern claiming to be the humble J. Everett Poindexter, the regulars can scarcely believe their ears. But there's a catch—Poindexter is actually a millionaire in disguise, and his elaborate masquerade sets off a chain reaction of confusion that will have you clutching your radio set in delighted laughter. Watch as Archie, the fast-talking manager with his finger perpetually on the pulse of New York street vernacular, attempts to navigate this bewildering situation, while the tavern's colorful cast of characters—from lovelorn bartenders to scheming patrons—scramble to determine who this mysterious stranger really is and what he wants from their beloved watering hole.

For a full decade, *Duffy's Tavern* captivated millions of Americans with its rapid-fire wit and ensemble cast of unforgettable characters. Created by Ed Gardner, who performed the iconic role of Archie, the show became a cultural touchstone of 1940s radio comedy, offering listeners a window into the authentic humor and dialect of New York's working-class establishments. Each episode crackled with improvisation and genuine chemistry between cast members, creating a warm, intimate atmosphere that made listeners feel like regulars themselves. The show's success spawned a 1945 film and spawned countless imitations, yet none captured the spontaneous brilliance of the original broadcasts.

Don't miss this classic tale of wealth, deception, and small-tavern shrewdness. Tune in and discover why *Duffy's Tavern* remains one of radio's most cherished comedies, where every night promised laughter, surprise, and the peculiar wisdom only a Greenwich Village bar could provide.
