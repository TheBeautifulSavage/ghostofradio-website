# It's Time To Smile: October 30, 1940 - "Off to Washington"

Step into the NBC studio on this autumn evening as Eddie Cantor, the inimitable "Banjo Eyes," whisks his audience away to the nation's capital with his signature blend of rapid-fire comedy and infectious charm. As political tensions simmer across the Atlantic and Americans contemplate their future, Cantor offers the perfect escape—a rollicking jaunt through Washington filled with mistaken identities, comedic misadventures with government officials, and the kind of musical interludes that made this show appointment radio for millions of families huddled around their sets. The orchestra swells, the studio audience erupts in laughter, and you can almost smell the autumn leaves as Cantor's unmistakable voice leads you down Pennsylvania Avenue into a world of pure entertainment.

By 1940, *It's Time To Smile* had become one of American radio's most cherished institutions, a weekly refuge where Eddie Cantor's boundless energy and everyman persona provided solace during uncertain times. Cantor himself was more than just a comedian—he was a showman of the first order, a vaudeville legend whose career spanned decades, and a genuine innovator in bringing theatrical variety to the intimacy of radio. This episode arrives at a particularly resonant moment in the show's run, when America stood at a crossroads between isolationism and international involvement, making a comedic visit to Washington feel both timely and timeless.

Tune in now and discover why millions of listeners considered Eddie Cantor essential listening. You'll hear the polished professionalism of classic variety radio, the spontaneous energy of a live broadcast, and the warmth of an entertainer who understood that laughter was perhaps America's greatest defense against despair.
