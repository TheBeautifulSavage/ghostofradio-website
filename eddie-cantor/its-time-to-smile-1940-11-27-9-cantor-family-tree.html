# The Eddie Cantor Show: It's Time To Smile (November 27, 1940)

Picture the American home on a crisp November evening in 1940—the console radio glowing warmly as families gather around for their weekly dose of laughter and music. Eddie Cantor's infectious energy bursts through the speakers with characteristic enthusiasm as he launches into "It's Time To Smile," a special episode devoted to tracing the Cantor family tree. What unfolds is a rollicking comedic journey through generations, complete with Cantor's animated storytelling, surprise guest appearances, and the kind of punchy one-liners that made him a household name. You can practically hear the live studio audience roaring as Eddie weaves tales of his relatives—some real, some hilariously embellished—creating a warm, intimate portrait of Jewish-American family life that resonates deeply with the show's devoted listeners.

By 1940, Eddie Cantor had become one of America's most beloved entertainers, and his radio show represented the golden age of variety entertainment when families gathered around the wireless for comedy, song, and connection. Cantor's unique ability to blend sophisticated humor with heart-felt sentiment made him a bridge between vaudeville traditions and the modern radio era. This episode, with its focus on family heritage during an increasingly uncertain year, carries particular poignancy—it celebrates immigrant roots and domestic bonds at a moment when American families were beginning to feel the weight of impending global conflict.

The Cantor Family Tree episode showcases everything that made The Eddie Cantor Show an institution of American entertainment: stellar musicianship, sharp comedy writing, and an undeniable charm that transcends eight decades. Whether you're a devoted fan of classic radio or discovering Cantor for the first time, this November broadcast captures lightning in a bottle—a perfect snapshot of radio's brightest era.
