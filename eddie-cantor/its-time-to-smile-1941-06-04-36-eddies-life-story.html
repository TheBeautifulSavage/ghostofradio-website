# It's Time To Smile: Eddie's Life Story

Step into the warm glow of your radio on this June evening in 1941, and prepare yourself for an intimate evening with Eddie Cantor himself. In "Eddie's Life Story," the beloved entertainer pulls back the curtain on the journey that transformed a scrappy kid from the Lower East Side into one of America's most celebrated comedians. With his trademark rapid-fire delivery and infectious energy, Eddie guides listeners through the pivotal moments that shaped his remarkable rise—the vaudeville circuits, the theatrical triumphs, the moments of doubt and triumph that defined his path. The studio audience roars with laughter and gasps with genuine emotion as Eddie relives these stories with characteristic charm, supported by the brilliant NBC orchestra that punctuates each memory with musical flourishes. This is more than comedy; it's autobiography rendered as art, a celebration of the American dream told by a man who lived it.

By 1941, Eddie Cantor had already earned his place as one of radio's most magnetic personalities, a vaudeville legend who understood the intimate power of the medium in a way few could match. The early 1940s marked a golden age for variety radio, and Cantor's weekly program was essential listening for millions of Americans seeking escape and laughter during uncertain times. His willingness to share his personal story spoke to audiences yearning for connection and authenticity—a reminder that even the brightest stars had humble beginnings and faced real struggles.

Don't miss this extraordinary glimpse into the life of Eddie Cantor, as he shares the stories behind the smile. Tune in for an evening of unforgettable entertainment that proves the greatest comedy always comes from truth.
