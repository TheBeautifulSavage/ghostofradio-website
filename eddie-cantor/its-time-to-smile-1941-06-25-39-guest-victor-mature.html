# The Eddie Cantor Show: "It's Time to Smile"

**June 25, 1941**

Step into the glittering world of 1941 as Eddie Cantor himself welcomes you into Studio 8-H at Radio City, where the unmistakable sound of the orchestra swells and that familiar, infectious voice crackles through your speaker with unbridled energy. Tonight's broadcast promises all the zaniness you've come to expect—rapid-fire jokes, elaborate comedic sketches, and those signature Cantor ad-libs that keep audiences on their toes—but there's something extra special in store. Hollywood's latest heartthrob, the dashingly handsome Victor Mature, fresh from his triumphant roles in the picture shows, joins Eddie for a night of sparkling banter and musical interludes. As war clouds gather over Europe and America teeters on the brink of involvement, Cantor's message rings true in his show's very title: it's time to smile, to forget troubles for an evening, and to revel in the pure, unadulterated joy of live entertainment at its finest.

What made The Eddie Cantor Show an institution during the golden age of radio was its perfect alchemy of vaudeville tradition and modern showmanship. Cantor, a veteran of the Ziegfeld Follies, brought a theatrical vitality to the airwaves that few could match, commanding the microphone with the same authority he once commanded the stage. By 1941, as the nation shifted from Depression recovery to wartime footing, Cantor's relentless optimism and star-studded guest appearances became a cherished ritual for millions seeking respite from an uncertain world.

Don't miss this sparkling half-hour of comedy, music, and Hollywood glamour—a time capsule of American entertainment when radio was king and laughter was the greatest luxury of all.
