# The Eddie Cantor Show: It's Time To Smile (June 14, 1944)

Step into the warm glow of a 1944 broadcast studio as Eddie Cantor, that infectious vaudevillian virtuoso, welcomes the remarkable child star Jackie Coogan to the microphone. With the nation still deep in World War II, this episode crackles with the particular magic that made America's living rooms the heart of family entertainment during wartime. Expect Cantor's signature rapid-fire comedy—his perfectly timed jokes, impeccable timing, and that unmistakable nasal delivery that could make millions laugh in unison. The banter between the seasoned showman and young Coogan promises genuine warmth and hilarity, punctuated by the lush sounds of the orchestra and the responsive audience that helped define the golden age of radio. There's a palpable sense of escapism here, that precious commodity during a dark chapter of history.

What makes this episode particularly resonant is how it captures the very essence of the Eddie Cantor Show's mission: to deliver smiles when the world desperately needed them. Cantor was no mere entertainer—he was a cultural institution, a bridge between vaudeville's rough charm and modern radio sophistication. His Thursday night broadcasts on NBC and CBS had become ritual listening for American families since 1931, and by 1944, he'd perfected the art of balancing comedy with genuine warmth. Jackie Coogan's appearance is equally significant; the former child sensation of Charlie Chaplin's *The Kid* represented entertainment's ability to span generations and eras.

Don your headphones and join millions of listeners as they tune in for an evening of timeless laughter and stellar variety entertainment. This is radio at its most vital—live, spontaneous, and deeply connected to its audience's need for joy during uncertain times.
