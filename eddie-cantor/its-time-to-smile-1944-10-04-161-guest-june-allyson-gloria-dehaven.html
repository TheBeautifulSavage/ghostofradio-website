# The Eddie Cantor Show: It's Time To Smile (October 4, 1944)

Picture yourself settling into your favorite chair on a crisp autumn evening, tuning the dial to catch the warm glow of Eddie Cantor's infectious energy crackling through your radio speaker. On this October broadcast, the master of ceremonies welcomes the enchanting June Allyson and vivacious Gloria DeHaven to the studio, promising an evening of sophisticated comedy sketches, musical interludes, and the kind of spontaneous wit that made Cantor America's favorite entertainer. You can almost hear the studio audience's anticipation as the orchestra swells—there's a palpable electricity in the air, that unmistakable magic of live performance captured for millions tuning in across the nation.

By 1944, The Eddie Cantor Show had become an American institution, a Thursday night appointment for families hungry for escapism during wartime. Cantor's variety format—mixing comedy, song, and celebrity guests—represented the golden age of radio entertainment at its finest, when a comedian's timing and a guest star's charm could captivate an entire nation without a single visual aid. His support for the war effort and ability to balance current events with comic relief made him essential listening, a voice of hope and laughter when Americans needed it most. The presence of Hollywood starlets like Allyson and DeHaven, both ascending to prominence in Metro's musicals, brought that glamorous MGM sheen to the intimate medium of radio.

Don't miss this perfectly preserved window into 1944's entertainment landscape. Tune in to experience Eddie Cantor at his comedic peak, surrounded by beauty, music, and the unbridled joy of live radio in its greatest era.
