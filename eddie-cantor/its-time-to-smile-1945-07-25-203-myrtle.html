# The Eddie Cantor Show: "It's Time To Smile" (July 25, 1945)

Picture yourself settling into your favorite chair on a sweltering July evening in 1945, the radio dial warming to life as Eddie Cantor's unmistakable voice crackles through the speaker with characteristic energy and charm. Tonight's broadcast promises the perfect escape from wartime anxieties—a rollicking comedy-variety hour where Cantor's rubber-faced humor and infectious laugh will carry you away from the headlines. Featured prominently is the delightful character of Myrtle, whose interactions with Cantor promise the kind of rapid-fire comedy and surprising musical numbers that have made this program appointment listening for millions across America. With a live orchestra punctuating every laugh and a supporting cast ready to deliver perfectly-timed comic bits, listeners can expect to be transported to that magical space where entertainment provided not just laughter, but genuine solace during uncertain times.

What makes this particular episode significant is its place in radio's golden age, broadcast just weeks after V-E Day while the nation still rallied behind troops in the Pacific. The Eddie Cantor Show had become an institution by 1945—fourteen years of consistent excellence had earned Cantor his reputation as one of broadcasting's finest entertainers. His show's blend of humor, musical talent, and emotional intelligence resonated especially deeply with audiences seeking reassurance and normalcy. Cantor himself, with his earnest patriotism and genuine warmth, embodied the spirit of American entertainment at its most vital.

Don't miss your chance to experience a half-hour of timeless entertainment preserved in its original broadcast form. Tune in to "It's Time To Smile" and discover why Eddie Cantor remained America's beloved entertainer for over two decades on the radio.
