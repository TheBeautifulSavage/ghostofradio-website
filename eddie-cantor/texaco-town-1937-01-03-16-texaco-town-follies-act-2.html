# Texaco Town 1937 01 03 (16) Texaco Town Follies Act 2

Step into the glittering world of Texaco Town on this January evening in 1937, where Eddie Cantor takes the stage with his trademark energy and infectious charm. In this second act of the Follies, you'll experience the virtuoso comedic timing that made Cantor radio's most beloved entertainer—rapid-fire jokes punctuated by his signature falsetto laugh, clever wordplay that captures the very spirit of Depression-era humor, and musical interludes that shimmer with sophistication. The orchestra swells behind him as he navigates sketches and banter with a supporting cast of talented performers, each moment crackling with the spontaneity and genuine warmth that only live radio could deliver. His audience's laughter becomes your laughter, shared across invisible airwaves in one of broadcasting's most intimate and magical experiences.

This episode arrives at a pivotal moment for both Cantor and American radio. By 1937, Cantor had already established himself as a vaudeville legend and film star, yet radio remained his truest home—a medium where his manic, kinetic personality could flourish without the constraints of the silver screen. The Texaco sponsorship reflected the show's mainstream appeal; what began as a vehicle for selling gasoline became America's living room entertainment. This particular broadcast captures radio at its zenith, before television would reshape the landscape forever. Cantor's ability to blend social commentary with slapstick, to make audiences forget their economic anxieties through pure entertainment, made him an essential part of the national fabric during these uncertain times.

Don your finest evening attire and join the studio audience as Eddie Cantor reminds us why radio's Golden Age earned its name. This is comedy, variety, and American culture at their finest—a genuine piece of our shared past waiting to charm you all over again.
