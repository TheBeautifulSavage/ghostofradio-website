# The Edgar Bergen & Charlie McCarthy Show: Chase and Sanborn 100th Anniversary

Step into the warm glow of your radio dial as Edgar Bergen and his impudent wooden companion Charlie McCarthy celebrate a century of Chase and Sanborn coffee—the very sponsor that made this legendary program possible. In this special anniversary broadcast, the chemistry between ventriloquist and dummy crackles with an electricity that transcends the medium itself; you can almost see Charlie's painted eyebrows arch with mischief as he delivers his razor-sharp wisecracks, while Bergen's barely-suppressed laughter threatens to break character entirely. The show bursts with guest stars, musical numbers, and the kind of rapid-fire comedy that defined an era, all wrapped in the genuine warmth of celebrating a partnership that survived and thrived through three decades of American broadcasting.

This 1964 episode captures something poignant: a show that had already begun its sunset years, yet remained vital and beloved. Bergen and McCarthy pioneered the art of radio comedy during the Depression, proving that audiences didn't need to see a wooden dummy to fall in love with him—they only needed to hear the interplay between creator and creation, the barely-restrained laughter, the perfect timing that could only come from a performer and his instrument working as one. Chase and Sanborn coffee had been their presenting sponsor since the beginning, making this centennial a celebration not just of a beverage, but of American entertainment itself.

Tune in for a glimpse of radio's golden age at its most reflective and joyful—a reminder of when families gathered around the set and the simple magic of voices and imagination could captivate an entire nation. This is broadcasting history made intimate and personal, caught at a moment when nostalgia and present-day vitality collide.
