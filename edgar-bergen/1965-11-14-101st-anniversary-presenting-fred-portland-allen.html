# The Edgar Bergen & Charlie McCarthy Show

## St Anniversary Special: Fred & Portland Allen

Step into the gleaming NBC studios of 1965 as America's most beloved ventriloquist takes the microphone with his impudent wooden sidekick for a landmark anniversary celebration. This special edition features the legendary Fred Allen and his wife Portland—comedic royalty from radio's golden age—joining Bergen and Charlie for an evening of razor-sharp wit and nostalgic camaraderie. Listeners will experience the crackle of live performance energy as four comedy titans engage in rapid-fire banter, each trying to outmaneuver the others with increasingly absurd jokes and ironic asides. There's an electricity in the air that only comes when seasoned professionals, secure in their craft, push each other toward spontaneous brilliance. Charlie McCarthy's wooden interjections—delivered with Bergen's masterful vocal precision—puncture the adult sophistication of the Allen's worldly humor, creating moments of pure comedic alchemy.

Bergen's show had been a fixture of American radio for nearly three decades by this point, evolving from a curiosity—how does a ventriloquist even *work* on radio?—into an institution that proved the power of personality and timing could transcend any medium. Fred Allen, perhaps radio's wittiest writer-performer, brought a different comedic sensibility: more acerbic, more literary, with jokes that rewarded the intelligent listener. When these worlds collided, the result was appointment listening for millions of Americans who understood they were witnessing masters at their craft.

To hear this special is to eavesdrop on an era when radio comedy was theater of the mind, when virtuosity meant something beyond simple entertainment. Tune in and discover why these performers remained relevant across decades—and why their influences echo through comedy even today.
