# Edgar Bergen & Charlie McCarthy Show – May 23, 1937

Settle into your favorite chair and prepare for an evening of unparalleled entertainment as Edgar Bergen brings his celebrated dummy Charlie McCarthy to life for NBC audiences on this spring evening in 1937. The irrepressible wooden imp is in rare form, trading barbs with his creator while legendary actress Mary Boland graces the stage with her sophisticated charm and impeccable comic timing. But the real thrill comes with the appearance of Norwegian ice skating sensation Sonja Henie, fresh from her triumphant Olympic victories and her new Hollywood career. As Bergen orchestrates the chaos between Charlie's saucy wisecracks, Boland's witty repartee, and Henie's glamorous presence, listeners are transported to a world of sparkling sophistication where anything might happen in the next moment—a world that only live radio could deliver.

This episode captures the Edgar Bergen & Charlie McCarthy Show at the apex of American radio comedy, just as the program was establishing itself as a phenomenon that would captivate the nation for nearly two decades. Bergen's ventriloquism—already legendary on the vaudeville circuit—found its perfect medium in radio, where the dummy's personality could flourish without visual distraction, allowing audiences to project their own imaginations onto the wooden curmudgeon's voice and character. The show's format of celebrity guests, musical interludes, and Charlie's relentless insults became the template for variety radio entertainment throughout the Golden Age. These were the shows that defined an era, drawing millions of Americans together in their living rooms for shared laughter and wonder.

Don't miss this sparkling May evening from nearly a century ago—a window into the wit, glamour, and spontaneous joy that made radio the dominant entertainment medium of its time.
