# The Edgar Bergen & Charlie McCarthy Show - June 27, 1937

Settle into your favorite chair and prepare yourself for an evening of sophisticated comedy and musical elegance as Edgar Bergen welcomes the radiant Norwegian skating sensation Sonja Henie to the broadcast. You'll hear the unmistakable wooden-headed sass of Charlie McCarthy—that wiseguy dummy with more personality than most humans—trading rapid-fire quips with his master, while guest vocalist Ray Middleton lends his smooth baritone to the evening's musical interludes. The chemistry between Bergen and his puppet creation crackles with genuine wit, as Charlie throws barbs about everything from Henie's glamorous skating career to the absurdities of show business itself. This is comedy that demands your full attention; there's no laugh track to tell you when to respond, just the roar of a live studio audience and the crackle of genuine spontaneous humor across the airwaves.

By the summer of 1937, Edgar Bergen had become a nationwide sensation—a ventriloquist whose dummy had somehow become more famous than its operator, a paradox that delighted millions of Americans. The Edgar Bergen & Charlie McCarthy Show represented the very pinnacle of American radio entertainment, where vaudeville traditions met modern broadcasting technology. Bergen's impeccable timing and the celebrity guests who eagerly competed for airtime on his program reflected radio's golden age, when this medium was the undisputed center of American popular culture. Sonja Henie, at the height of her Olympic fame, represents the aspirational glamour audiences craved during the Depression era.

This is essential listening for anyone seeking to understand the magic of classic radio and the particular genius of Edgar Bergen's artistry. The verbal sparring, the musical numbers, the audience laughter—it's all here, preserved from over eight decades ago. Tune in and discover why millions of listeners made this their unmissable weekly appointment with entertainment.
