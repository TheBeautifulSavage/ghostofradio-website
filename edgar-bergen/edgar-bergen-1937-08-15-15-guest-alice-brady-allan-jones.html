# Edgar Bergen & Charlie McCarthy Show – August 15, 1937

Step into the golden age of American entertainment as Edgar Bergen settles into the NBC studio with his irreverent wooden companion Charlie McCarthy for an evening of unscripted hilarity and musical sophistication. This August broadcast crackles with the anticipation of live radio—you can almost hear the audience's expectant murmur as Bergen exchanges rapid-fire quips with his wisecracking dummy, their chemistry as electric as the vacuum tubes powering the broadcast. With elegant stage actress Alice Brady and crooning tenor Allan Jones joining the festivities, the evening promises a delightful collision of sophisticated comedy, romantic ballads, and the kind of spontaneous moments that only live radio could deliver. Charlie, as always, proves more irreverent than his creator, steering conversations into mischievous territory while Bergen struggles to maintain propriety before the assembled guests and the invisible millions tuning in across America.

The Edgar Bergen & Charlie McCarthy Show represents the apex of radio's variety format—a brilliant synthesis of ventriloquism, comedy, and live music that somehow translated perfectly to the audio medium. Bergen's dummy had become a national phenomenon, and by 1937, Charlie McCarthy was commanding top dollar and guest stars of considerable stature. This was the era when radio dominated American living rooms, and Bergen's peculiar genius—making an audience believe in the personality and independence of a wooden figure—captivated listeners in ways that today's visual media cannot quite replicate. The show's success lay in Bergen's impeccable timing and the sheer charm of the illusion itself.

Join us as we eavesdrop on a night of sparkling wit and genuine show business magic. Tune in to hear why, in 1937, America couldn't get enough of Charlie McCarthy—and why his impudent charm still enchants today.
