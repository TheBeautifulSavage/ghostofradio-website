# Edgar Bergen & Charlie McCarthy Show - August 28, 1938

Picture yourself huddled around a wooden radio cabinet on a warm summer evening in 1938, the glow of the dial illuminating eager faces in darkened living rooms across America. As the orchestra swells and Edgar Bergen's smooth voice introduces tonight's program, you can practically feel Charlie McCarthy's wooden presence—that irrepressible dummy whose impudent wisecracks have made him the most famous dummy in the world. Tonight's show promises an electric mix of comedy and musical brilliance, with distinguished guest Ralph Bellamy bringing Hollywood glamour and the golden-voiced Nelson Eddy delivering the operatic sophistication that audiences crave. Bergen's ventriloquism will keep you guessing throughout—who's really speaking, and more importantly, who will get the last laugh? Charlie's caustic wit invariably targets his master, while Mortimer Snerd and Effie Klinker add their own layers of comedy chaos.

By 1938, Edgar Bergen had already revolutionized radio comedy, transforming ventriloquism from a stage novelty into intimate household entertainment. What makes this show's format revolutionary is its seamless blend: comedy sketches punctuated by world-class musical performances, all held together by Bergen's subtle talent and Charlie's magnetic personality. The dummy had become so beloved that he received fan mail by the sackful and appeared alongside Hollywood's biggest stars. This was radio's golden age, when talented performers could command entire networks and millions of Americans gathered nightly to be entertained, informed, and transported beyond their everyday lives.

Tune in now and experience the magic that made this show essential listening for over two decades—the perfect blend of humor, music, and the peculiar intimacy that only radio could provide.
