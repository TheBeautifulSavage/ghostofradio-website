# The Edgar Bergen & Charlie McCarthy Show: November 6, 1938

Picture this: it's a Sunday evening in 1938, and across America families are gathered around their radio consoles, the warm glow of the dial illuminating eager faces. Edgar Bergen takes the stage with his dummy partner Charlie McCarthy—that impossibly sophisticated wooden ventriloquist's figure with his monocle and top hat—ready to deliver an evening of impeccable comedy timing and clever repartee that has audiences roaring with laughter. Tonight, the charm is doubled with the arrival of Hollywood's radiant Jean Arthur, whose breathy voice and impeccable comedic timing translate beautifully to radio. Listen as Bergen orchestrates the perfect storm of witty banter between Charlie's impertinent one-liners and Miss Arthur's quick-witted responses, with Bergen himself the genial conductor of this carefully choreographed mayhem. You'll find yourself swept up in the infectious energy, the live audience's delighted reactions, and that unmistakable crackle of chemistry that only live performance could produce.

This episode captures the show at its absolute zenith—a phenomenon that defied all logical explanation yet commanded an astounding 40 million listeners weekly. Bergen's ventriloquism translated mysteriously but perfectly through radio waves, with Charlie becoming as real to listeners as any flesh-and-blood actor. The puppet's outrageous flirtations and impudent wisecracks became national conversation, while Bergen's deadpan responses and the guest stars' willingness to play along created an almost theatrical magic. This wasn't merely entertainment; it was appointment listening that shaped American humor and proved radio's unparalleled power to captivate the imagination.

Tune in now to experience this November evening when comedy, charm, and the incomparable Charlie McCarthy held a nation spellbound.
