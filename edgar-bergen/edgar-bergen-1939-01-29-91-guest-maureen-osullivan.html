# Edgar Bergen & Charlie McCarthy Show — January 29, 1939

Step into the glittering world of 1939 as Edgar Bergen settles into the microphone with his wooden-faced companion Charlie McCarthy perched faithfully on his knee. This evening's program sparkles with the arrival of Hollywood starlet Maureen O'Sullivan, fresh from her success in the Tarzan pictures, bringing genuine glamour to the NBC studios. Listeners will delight in the rapid-fire banter between Bergen's velvet baritone and Charlie's impudent wisecracks, while the sultry Irish charm of Miss O'Sullivan adds a new dimension to the comedy. The chemistry between seasoned ventriloquist and celebrated actress crackles with an electricity that only live radio could capture—you'll hear the studio audience roar with laughter, gasp at the clever ripostes, and feel the authentic spontaneity of performance that transcends the medium itself.

What made Bergen and Charlie McCarthy a phenomenon of the Depression era was their unique ability to blur the line between reality and artifice. Bergen's dummy became a genuine personality in the minds of millions, a wooden confidant who could say the outrageous things listeners only dared whisper. This show, at the height of their popularity, represents radio at its most sophisticated and entertaining—a polished variety program that seamlessly wove comedy sketches, musical interludes, and guest stars into an evening of pure escapism. O'Sullivan's appearance was no small feat; bringing major Hollywood names to radio broadcasts was a carefully orchestrated affair that demonstrated the medium's continuing cultural clout.

Don't miss this glimpse into radio's Golden Age, when an invisible comedian and his wooden partner could captivate an entire nation week after week. Tune in and discover why Americans couldn't resist Charlie McCarthy's impudent charm and Edgar Bergen's masterful showmanship.
