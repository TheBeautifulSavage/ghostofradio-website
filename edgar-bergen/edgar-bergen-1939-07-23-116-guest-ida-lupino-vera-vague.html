# Edgar Bergen & Charlie McCarthy Show - July 23, 1939

Settle into your favorite chair and prepare for an evening of impeccable timing and wooden wit as Edgar Bergen brings his wise-cracking dummy Charlie McCarthy to life once again. On this sweltering July night in 1939, the master ventriloquist welcomes the sultry, sophisticated Ida Lupino—fresh from her Hollywood triumphs—who finds herself caught in Charlie's relentless verbal sparring and Edgar's vaudeville-trained comedy. Watch out for Vera Vague too, whose scatter-brained charm will keep the studio audience roaring. There's music, there's mayhem, and there's that distinctive magic only possible in live radio, where the invisible becomes unforgettable through sheer force of performance.

This was the golden age of American entertainment, when ventriloquism on radio seemed almost miraculous—how could a wooden dummy with a painted smile steal every scene? Yet that was precisely Bergen's genius. From 1937 onward, he and Charlie became America's most beloved comedy duo, drawing audiences of millions to their Sunday evening broadcast. Charlie wasn't merely a prop but a fully formed personality: sarcastic, brash, a ladies' man with an edge that Edgar's gentlemanly persona could never quite manage alone. Their chemistry created something revolutionary—proof that radio's greatest asset wasn't what you could see, but what you could imagine.

For anyone seeking authentic American entertainment from radio's finest hour, this episode captures the spirit of pre-war optimism, vaudeville tradition meeting modern broadcasting, and star power in its purest form. Ida Lupino's presence adds glamour and unpredictability to the usual Bergen-McCarthy dynamic. Don't miss it—some pleasures are timeless, and this is one of them.
