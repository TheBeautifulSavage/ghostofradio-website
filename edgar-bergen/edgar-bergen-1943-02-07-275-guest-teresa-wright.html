# The Edgar Bergen & Charlie McCarthy Show: February 7, 1943

Settle into your favorite chair and prepare yourself for an evening of pure enchantment as Edgar Bergen and his irreverent wooden sidekick Charlie McCarthy welcome the luminous Teresa Wright to the broadcast. It's wartime America, and families gathering around their radio sets are hungry for laughter and distraction from the anxieties of 1943. Wright, fresh from her acclaimed performances on the silver screen, brings a graceful charm to the microphone as Bergen and Charlie launch into their perfectly timed comedy bits—the dummy's impudent one-liners landing with practiced precision while Bergen maintains his straight-faced composure. You'll hear the studio audience roar with laughter, the orchestra's bright arrangements punctuating the comedy, and that unmistakable ventriloquism magic that somehow seems even more wondrous when you *can't* see the act. The interplay between the sophisticated guest star and the wooden troublemaker creates a delightful tension, as Wright plays along gamely with Charlie's flirtations and asides.

This episode captures the golden age of radio variety entertainment at its peak. The Bergen-McCarthy partnership had already become a cultural phenomenon, with Charlie McCarthy commanding fan mail rivaling that of living actors—a testament to Bergen's virtuosic skill and the dummy's perfectly calibrated personality. By 1943, this program was appointment listening, a cherished weekly escape during wartime that brought together diverse entertainment elements: comedy sketches, musical numbers, celebrity guests, and the genuine warmth of a performer at the height of his powers.

Don't miss this beautifully preserved glimpse into radio's greatest era. Tune in to hear Teresa Wright and Charlie McCarthy spar with wit and charm, and experience the artistry that made Edgar Bergen a household name.
