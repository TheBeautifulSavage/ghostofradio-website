# The Edgar Bergen & Charlie McCarthy Show: October 31, 1943

On this Halloween evening in 1943, listeners tuning into their radio sets are in for a treat as Edgar Bergen brings his impudent dummy Charlie McCarthy to life once more, this time joined by the sultry, exotic presence of Dorothy Lamour. The chemistry between Bergen's masterful ventriloquism and the starlet's quick wit crackles through the airwaves as Charlie wastes no time in his trademark cheekiness, launching into his most audacious flirtations while Bergen attempts—with varying degrees of success—to maintain some semblance of propriety. The studio audience roars with laughter as Lamour playfully parries each of Charlie's wooden advances, her movie-star glamour providing the perfect foil to the dummy's impudent charm. With the nation deep in the throes of World War II, this hour of escapist entertainment offers Americans something precious: a chance to forget their worries and simply laugh at the timeless interplay between a ventriloquist, his mischievous creation, and a leading lady who can hold her own against both.

The Bergen-McCarthy partnership had become an American institution by 1943, a phenomenon that transcended the limitations of radio itself. Charlie McCarthy was no mere puppet—he was a personality, complete with his own sponsor, his own feuds with fellow cast members, and a devoted following that rivaled that of flesh-and-blood actors. Bergen's uncanny ability to make audiences forget they were watching wood and paint speak volumes about his artistry, while guest stars like Lamour understood that trading barbs with Charlie was a badge of honor in Hollywood.

Don't miss this delightful Halloween episode—it's a window into an evening when America gathered around the radio for laughter, wonder, and pure entertainment magic.
