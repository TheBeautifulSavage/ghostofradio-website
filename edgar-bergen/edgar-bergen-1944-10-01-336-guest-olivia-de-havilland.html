# Edgar Bergen & Charlie McCarthy Show - October 1, 1944

Picture yourself huddled around the radio on this crisp autumn evening in 1944, the glow of the dial illuminating eager faces in living rooms across America. Edgar Bergen settles into the studio with his impudent wooden companion Charlie McCarthy, whose painted grin seems to shimmer with mischief. Tonight brings a special guest: the luminous Olivia de Havilland, fresh from her triumphant role in *The Strawberry Blonde*. As Bergen's orchestra swells, you know what's coming—the witty banter, Charlie's caustic asides about Hollywood starlets, and the incomparable chemistry between puppet and master that has America utterly enchanted. De Havilland's arrival sparks a cascade of innuendos and clever repartee, with Charlie alternating between fawning admiration and cutting remarks that somehow escape censure. Bergen's ventriloquism reaches its peak as he manages to seem genuinely flustered by his own creation's impudence.

This episode represents the golden zenith of *The Edgar Bergen & Charlie McCarthy Show*, a phenomenon that transcended the boundaries of radio itself. Bergen had created something revolutionary—a ventriloquist act perfectly suited to an audio medium where the audience's imagination completes the magic. By 1944, the show commanded millions of listeners weekly, and guest stars of de Havilland's caliber competed for appearances. The program seamlessly blended variety entertainment, comedy, and music, reflecting the escapist appetite of a nation at war.

Tune in and experience why America abandoned its screens to listen to a man and his dummy become the most electrifying entertainment of the decade. Charlie McCarthy awaits, ready with a quip that will have you laughing aloud—and wondering how on earth Bergen keeps a straight face.
