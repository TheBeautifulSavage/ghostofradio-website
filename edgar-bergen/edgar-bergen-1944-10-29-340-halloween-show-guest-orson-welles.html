# Edgar Bergen & Charlie McCarthy Show - Halloween Special, October 29, 1944

Step into the flickering glow of a radio studio on a moonlit October evening in 1944, where ventriloquist Edgar Bergen and his impudent wooden dummy Charlie McCarthy prepare for their most deliciously spooky broadcast yet. With Orson Welles as guest—the very man who terrified the nation with his infamous *War of the Worlds* broadcast just six years prior—the stage is set for a Halloween extravaganza brimming with supernatural hijinks, witty repartee, and the kind of star power that had America huddled around their receivers in anticipation. Expect Charlie's razor-sharp wisecracks to puncture any pretense, Bergen's masterful control of the irascible dummy to showcase why millions found him endlessly entertaining, and Welles' theatrical gravitas to provide the perfect counterpoint to the evening's carefully orchestrated mayhem. The atmosphere crackles with the promise of genuine spontaneity—always the hallmark of live radio—as three masters of their craft navigate a script that surely teems with ghostly gags and autumn mischief.

For nearly a decade, *The Edgar Bergen & Charlie McCarthy Show* had dominated American radio, transforming a ventriloquist act that should have been impossible for audio into an irresistible national phenomenon. Bergen's ability to create the illusion of a living, breathing character through voice alone, combined with Charlie's bratty personality and Bergen's straight-man responses, made for comedy gold. By 1944, the show was in its prime, and adding Welles—already a legendary figure despite his youth—elevated this particular broadcast into the realm of event radio. It's a unique moment where mid-century entertainment's brightest talents converged.

Tune in now and experience the artistry, spontaneity, and sheer charm of radio's golden age. This is broadcasting as it was meant to be—live, unpredictable, and utterly spellbinding.
