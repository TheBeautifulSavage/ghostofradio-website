# The Edgar Bergen & Charlie McCarthy Show — November 26, 1944

Step into the warm glow of your radio dial this Sunday evening as Edgar Bergen settles into the studio with his impudent wooden sidekick Charlie McCarthy, ready to deliver an evening of wit, charm, and musical splendor. Tonight's special guest is none other than Lauritz Melchior, the legendary Wagnerian tenor whose golden voice has captivated audiences from the Metropolitan Opera House to concert halls across the nation. Watch as the quick-tongued dummy takes gentle jabs at the distinguished baritone while Bergen guides the conversation with practiced charm, and expect moments of genuine musical magic when Melchior showcases his magnificent operatic gifts. The contrast between Charlie's brash wisecracks and the dignity of America's premiere opera singer creates an electric tension that only this incomparable ensemble can generate.

In 1944, with the nation deep in wartime sacrifice, Bergen's show has become essential Sunday evening entertainment—a refuge where millions of Americans gathered around their sets to forget the anxieties of a world at war. The Bergen-McCarthy partnership had already achieved cultural phenomenon status by this point, having conquered radio, vaudeville, and film. What made this act endure across nearly two decades was Bergen's seemingly effortless ventriloquism combined with genuinely clever writing; Charlie wasn't merely a puppet mouthing insults, but a fully realized character with his own mordant personality. Adding a world-class vocalist like Melchior to the mix transformed the variety hour into something transcendent—high culture meeting popular entertainment in a uniquely American way.

Don't miss this extraordinary meeting of minds and voices. Tune in as Charlie McCarthy proves that no guest, however celebrated, is safe from his razor-sharp one-liners, while Melchior demonstrates why he remains opera's most beloved tenor. This is radio at its finest.
