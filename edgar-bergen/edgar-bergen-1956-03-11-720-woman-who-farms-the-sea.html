# Edgar Bergen & Charlie McCarthy Show – March 11, 1956

Step into the glittering world of 1950s entertainment as Edgar Bergen takes the microphone for what promises to be an unforgettable evening of wit, wonder, and wooden charm. In this week's installment, listeners are introduced to a remarkable woman who has devoted her life to an extraordinary calling—farming the sea itself. As Bergen's impeccable timing and his irreverent dummy Charlie McCarthy probe the mysteries of this unconventional livelihood, the studio audience erupts with laughter and astonishment. What begins as a seemingly straightforward interview transforms into a delightful exploration of human ingenuity, complete with McCarthy's trademark impertinence and Bergen's masterful ability to draw out the most captivating stories. The chemistry between ventriloquist and dummy crackles through the airwaves, reminding listeners why this show has remained America's favorite for nearly two decades.

The Edgar Bergen & Charlie McCarthy Show represents the golden age of radio entertainment, a time when millions gathered around their sets to experience live comedy, remarkable guests, and the undeniable magic of a man and his wooden partner. Bergen's ventriloquism—invisible to radio audiences yet somehow perfectly conveyed through his artistry—became a phenomenon that transcended the medium itself. By 1956, the show had already secured its place in American cultural history, having survived the transition from NBC to CBS and countless changes in the entertainment landscape. Each episode showcases Bergen's remarkable ability to create genuine moments of spontaneity and human connection, whether through McCarthy's sardonic observations or the compelling narratives of his diverse guests.

Don't miss this captivating window into postwar American curiosity and ingenuity. Tune in for an evening that celebrates the extraordinary people who dare to pursue unconventional dreams—and the entertainer who knew just how to let their stories shine.
