The Episode

EPISODE SUMMARY:

"Wild Oranges" is a tense and suspenseful episode of Escape, starring William Conrad as Peter Gunn, an American diplomat stranded in Berlin after the war. The story revolves around Peter's investigation into a mysterious fruit smuggler, known only as "The Professor," who is secretly shipping wild oranges to East Germany to fuel the Soviet Union's economy. As Peter delves deeper into the mystery, he becomes entangled in a web of espionage and betrayal that threatens his own life.

As the episode unfolds, Conrad masterfully conveys Peter's growing unease and paranoia, as he navigates the treacherous world of Cold War Berlin. The atmospheric soundscapes, expertly crafted by sound designer William LeBaron, create an unsettling sense of tension and foreboding, drawing the listener into Peter's desperate quest for answers. Meanwhile, Conrad's co-star, Jean Hersholt, brings a sense of dry wit and authority to his portrayal of Colonel von Krause, a skeptical German officer who becomes Peter's unlikely ally.

As the episode hurtles towards its climax, the stakes grow increasingly high, with Peter facing off against a ruthless foe in a heart-pumping game of cat-and-mouse. The finale is both shocking and thought-provoking, leaving the listener questioning the true nature of loyalty, duty, and survival in the dark days of post-war Europe.

HISTORICAL CONTEXT:

It was 1949, two years after the end of World War II, and America was grappling with the consequences of its emergence as a global superpower. The Cold War was in full swing, with tensions between the United States and the Soviet Union reaching a fever pitch. The Berlin Blockade, which had begun just weeks before this episode aired, had brought the city to the brink of chaos, as Allied forces struggled to maintain supply lines while the Soviet Union tightened its grip on the eastern sector.

In this climate of fear and uncertainty, radio drama resonated with audiences who were desperate for entertainment and escapism. As newsreels and newspaper headlines filled the airwaves with stories of espionage, sabotage, and atomic threats, Escape provided a welcome respite from the dire circumstances outside the studio. Listeners tuned in to find themselves transported into worlds of danger and intrigue, where heroes like Peter Gunn fought against the forces of darkness.

The episode's themes of loyalty, duty, and survival also tapped into the deep-seated fears of an America on edge. As the nation struggled to come to terms with its new role in the world, "Wild Oranges" offered a gripping exploration of what it meant to be American – and how that identity might be tested by the pressures of global politics.
