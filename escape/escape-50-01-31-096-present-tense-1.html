"Present Tense - 1" is a gripping episode of Escape, one of the most iconic radio dramas of its era. The story revolves around a young American, Robert, who finds himself trapped in an Austrian concentration camp during World War II. As Robert navigates the treacherous landscape of the camp, he must confront his own demons and make impossible choices to survive. The atmosphere is tense and foreboding, with the sound design expertly evoking the sense of claustrophobia and desperation that defines life in a concentration camp. The characters are well-developed and complex, with Robert's desperation and determination driving the plot forward.

As Robert navigates the camp, he encounters a range of characters, each with their own motivations and secrets. There's Maria, the mysterious and alluring woman who may hold the key to his survival; Hermann, the ruthless commandant of the camp; and Frau Klein, the kind-hearted old woman who becomes Robert's ally. The cast is superb, with each actor bringing depth and nuance to their characters. The dialogue is naturalistic and intelligent, conveying the emotional complexity of the situation through subtle suggestion rather than overt statement.

As the episode hurtles towards its climax, the tension builds to a fever pitch. Will Robert find a way out of the camp, or will he succumb to the harsh realities of his surroundings? The ending is both shocking and heartbreaking, leaving the listener breathless and disturbed.

Radio drama like Escape resonated deeply with audiences in this year because it provided a safe space to explore complex emotions and moral dilemmas. The rise of radio drama also coincided with a growing middle class in America, with more people than ever having access to radios and entertainment options. This made radio a popular choice for families and individuals looking for a low-cost form of entertainment during a time of economic uncertainty.
