Picture this: May 11th, 1945—just days after V-E Day, when America still thrums with the electricity of victory. But in the darkened living rooms of post-war America, listeners lean close to their Philco radios to hear a different kind of battle unfold. This episode exposes the sinister machinations of Andrew Rockland, a war profiteer whose greed threatened the very fabric of the nation's defense effort. While brave soldiers fought and died on foreign shores, Rockland sat in mahogany-paneled offices, padding contracts and siphoning government funds meant for weapons and supplies. The FBI's relentless agents follow the paper trail—falsified invoices, kickbacks, inflated prices—each revelation more damning than the last. Tension crackles through every scene as the investigation tightens around a man who profited from patriotism while honest Americans bled.

This Is Your FBI captured America's imagination precisely because it spoke to the anxieties of its moment. Broadcast during the twilight of the war and its immediate aftermath, the show presented real FBI cases with documentary-like authenticity, transforming the agency into a symbol of federal justice and moral order. These episodes weren't mere entertainment—they were reassurance that while corruption festered in the shadows, dedicated agents were working tirelessly to root it out. The production's meticulous attention to procedural detail and its stirring patriotic undertones made listeners feel they were witnessing justice in action, week after week.

Join us as the FBI closes its net around Andrew Rockland. Discover how dedication to duty and tireless investigative work expose those who would profit from their country's peril. Tune in to This Is Your FBI and experience the golden age of radio drama—where every case mattered, and justice always prevailed.
