# This Is Your FBI: Grand Larceny

Picture yourself in the spring of 1945, adjusting your radio dial as the FBI's distinctive theme music crackles through the speaker—that stirring march that promised America's most thrilling crime stories, told straight from the Bureau's own files. Tonight, you're about to hear the cunning tale of Daniel Holly, a master thief whose audacious schemes have made him one of the most wanted men in the nation. As the narrator's authoritative voice guides you through the investigation, you'll follow federal agents as they methodically piece together evidence, track Holly's movements across state lines, and close in on a criminal whose glib charm and meticulous planning have outsmarted law enforcement at every turn. The tension builds as each clue draws the noose tighter—a fingerprint here, a witness account there—until the dramatic confrontation that brings this grand larceny case to its inevitable conclusion.

*This Is Your FBI* stood as one of radio's most prestigious crime dramas, operating with the full cooperation and consultation of J. Edgar Hoover's Bureau itself. Unlike the sensationalized crime stories that populated the airwaves, this program presented cases drawn from actual FBI investigations, lending it an air of authenticity that audiences craved during the postwar years. By 1945, when this episode aired, Americans were hungry for stories celebrating their government's competence and moral authority—and what better way to reinforce faith in the system than through dramatized accounts of real FBI triumphs?

Don't miss this gripping chapter in law enforcement history. Tune in to hear how federal agents brought a dangerous criminal to justice, and discover why *This Is Your FBI* became must-listen radio for millions of Americans seeking truth, justice, and the unmistakable voice of American authority.
