When the opening fanfare of This Is Your FBI crackles through your speaker on this May evening in 1945, you're about to witness one of the Bureau's most cunning cases—a manhunt for a respected physician who's abandoned his oath to serve his country. Dr. Woodbury seemed the picture of American respectability: a successful medical practice, a sterling reputation in his community, and yet beneath that veneer lurked a desperate criminal willing to exploit America's wartime vulnerabilities. As federal agents close in through a web of false identities, forged documents, and carefully orchestrated deceptions, you'll experience the tense cat-and-mouse game that defined the FBI's work during the Second World War. This episode captures that uniquely 1940s anxiety—the fear that danger could be lurking behind any respectable front door, that patriotic duty could divide families, and that the men and women of law enforcement stood as the only bulwark against chaos.

This Is Your FBI was more than entertainment; it was government-sanctioned propaganda that gave Americans a weekly glimpse into actual Bureau operations while subtly reinforcing values of loyalty, obedience, and respect for federal authority. Hosted by the authoritative Westbrook Van Voorhis, each episode drew from real case files, lending an undeniable authenticity that thrilled audiences for eight seasons. During wartime, such broadcasts served a dual purpose—they entertained while they reassured a nervous public that the FBI was vigilantly protecting the home front from saboteurs, draft dodgers, and worse.

Tune in now and step into 1945 America—where patriotism wasn't abstract, where the FBI's reach seemed infinite, and where one man's cowardice became the nation's business.
