Step into the shadows of wartime America as the FBI's finest pursue one of the most dangerous threats to national security: a woman operating in plain sight. In this June 1945 episode, listeners will experience the tense cat-and-mouse game between federal agents and Cora Lee Williamson, a cunning spy whose carefully constructed identity threatens to unravel the nation's most closely guarded secrets. As the investigation unfolds, the tension builds with each clue—a dead drop in a crowded marketplace, coded messages hidden in plain correspondence, the methodical work of fingerprint analysis and informant testimony. You'll hear the determined voices of FBI agents closing in on their quarry, the crackling radio dispatches, and the ever-present sense that in this war against espionage, one misstep could cost countless lives.

This Is Your FBI arrived on ABC radio during America's darkest hours, offering listeners a reassuring glimpse into the real work of J. Edgar Hoover's bureau. Broadcast from 1945 to 1953, the show drew directly from actual FBI case files, lending an authenticity that audiences craved during the anxious postwar period. Espionage stories like the Williamson case were particularly compelling—they transformed the abstract threat of enemy agents into concrete, dramatic narratives. The show became appointment listening for Americans seeking both entertainment and confidence that their government's defenders were vigilant and capable.

Tune in to hear authentic crime drama rooted in genuine FBI investigations. This Is Your FBI brings you the real stories of those who stood between America and those who would betray her.
