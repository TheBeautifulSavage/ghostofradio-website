# This Is Your FBI: "The Surplus Swindle"

Picture this: it's a sweltering summer evening in 1946, and you've just settled into your favorite chair with a cold drink as the familiar opening fanfare of *This Is Your FBI* crackles through your radio speaker. Tonight's case plunges you into the shadowy world of black market schemes and wartime corruption—where unscrupulous criminals are systematically looting government surplus stockpiles meant for honest Americans. As the narrator's authoritative voice guides you through the investigation, you'll follow dedicated FBI agents as they methodically unravel a complex swindle involving falsified documents, bribed warehouse workers, and enough stolen military equipment to supply a small army. The tension builds as our investigators close in on the masterminds, each clue drawing the noose tighter around the criminals' necks. You'll hear the authentic sounds of the 1940s FBI at work: the rustle of case files, the sharp ring of telephones, and the determined footsteps of justice in pursuit.

*This Is Your FBI* arrived on ABC just as America was transitioning from wartime to peace, capturing the public's fascination with federal law enforcement and their role in combating the emerging crime wave of the postwar era. These episodes were often based on actual Bureau cases, lending them a documentary-like authenticity that audiences craved. By 1946, the show had become essential listening for crime drama aficionados, offering a thrilling window into real detective work stripped of Hollywood embellishment.

Whether you're a devoted fan of classic radio or discovering this cornerstone of crime drama for the first time, "The Surplus Swindle" exemplifies why millions tuned in faithfully each week. Don't miss your chance to experience this gripping tale of federal investigation and criminal justice—adjust your dial and prepare yourself for an evening of authentic American suspense.
