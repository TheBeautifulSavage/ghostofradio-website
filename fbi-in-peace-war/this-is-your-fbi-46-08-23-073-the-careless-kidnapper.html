# This Is Your FBI: "The Careless Kidnapper" (August 23, 1946)

Picture this: the crackling static of your radio set settling into focus as the FBI's official seal materializes in sound—that unmistakable badge of federal authority. Tonight's case centers on a kidnapper whose arrogance becomes his undoing, a criminal who believes himself clever enough to outwit the Bureau's vast investigative machinery. As Narrator Phillips H. Lord guides you through the facts, you'll follow the meticulous work of Special Agents as they piece together seemingly trivial details—a carelessly chosen word, a distinctive handwriting, a small-town postmaster's suspicious observation—into an airtight case. The tension builds with each clue, each narrowing circle of suspicion, until the careless criminal's own pride delivers him directly into federal custody. This is not melodrama; this is documented truth from the files of J. Edgar Hoover's FBI.

*This Is Your FBI* stands as one of radio's most unique hybrids, granted unprecedented access to actual Bureau cases with official sanction. Beginning in 1945, the show transformed real investigative work into gripping drama, using authentic details and genuine procedural methods to showcase the FBI's capabilities during a pivotal era. Where other crime shows relied on fabrication and sensationalism, *This Is Your FBI* offered something more powerful: the assurance that these weren't invented tales, but actual triumphs of American law enforcement. Each episode became a subtle instrument of civic confidence, reminding listeners that federal agents worked tirelessly against the criminal element threatening their communities.

Tune in now to witness how one kidnapper's fatal mistake became a masterclass in criminal investigation. The evidence awaits in the official records—and in your speaker tonight.
