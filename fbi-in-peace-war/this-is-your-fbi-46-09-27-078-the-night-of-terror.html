# The Night of Terror

As darkness falls across the city on this September evening in 1946, terror stalks the streets in ways both mysterious and deadly. When a series of brutal assaults leaves the city gripped by fear, FBI Agent Jim Taylor must navigate the shadows to uncover whether these crimes are the work of a single madman or something far more sinister. With each new lead taking him deeper into the underworld's hidden corners, listeners will find themselves suspended in that classic radio tension where danger lurks in every commercial break. The sound design crackles with authenticity—footsteps on wet pavement, the slam of interrogation room doors, whispered confessions—pulling you directly into the investigation with all its urgency and uncertainty.

"This Is Your FBI" stands as one of the most authoritative crime dramas of the Golden Age of Radio, grounded in actual case files and blessed with FBI cooperation that gave the series an unmatched air of legitimacy. Premiering in 1945, the show became a cultural phenomenon by presenting dramatizations of real FBI cases, narrated with procedural precision that made listeners feel they were witnessing actual law enforcement work. By 1946, when "The Night of Terror" aired, the show had perfected its formula—combining the meticulous detective work that FBI agents actually performed with storytelling that kept America on the edge of its seat. The series not only entertained but also educated listeners about modern criminal investigation techniques, making heroes of the faceless federal agents who protected the nation.
