# This Is Your FBI - "The Divorced Child"

When the opening fanfare of "This Is Your FBI" crackles through your radio on this March evening in 1947, you're transported directly into the austere offices of J. Edgar Hoover's Federal Bureau of Investigation. Tonight's case carries an especially poignant weight: a young child caught in the machinery of a custody dispute, thrust into danger when her estranged parents become entangled with dangerous criminals. As the narrator's measured voice lays out the facts with characteristic precision, the tension builds through carefully crafted sound effects—the distant ring of a telephone, footsteps echoing down marble corridors, the soft weeping of a confused child. This episode explores the tragedy that unfolds when federal agents must intervene in a family's darkest hour, where the nation's law enforcement stands as the only shield between an innocent and those who would exploit her vulnerability for profit or revenge.

"This Is Your FBI" occupied a unique position in radio's golden age, offering listeners an insider's perspective on actual FBI cases with the Bureau's full cooperation and blessing. Premiering on ABC in 1945, the show became a cultural phenomenon, blending documentary realism with compelling drama. Each episode drew from the FBI's own case files, giving audiences an unprecedented sense of authenticity. The program ran for nine seasons, capturing the post-war anxieties of Americans reassured by federal authority and professional law enforcement, while the divorce episode particularly resonates with the era's social upheaval as returning servicemen reclaimed civilian life.

Whether you're a devoted fan of classic crime drama or a newcomer to radio's golden age, "The Divorced Child" exemplifies why this program remained must-listen broadcasting throughout the 1940s. Tune in and discover why millions of Americans trusted this show to bring them closer to truth, justice, and the tireless work of the Federal Bureau of Investigation.
