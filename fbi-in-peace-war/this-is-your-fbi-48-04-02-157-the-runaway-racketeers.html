Picture yourself huddled around the radio on a crisp April evening in 1948, the amber glow of the dial casting shadows across the living room. Tonight's episode of This Is Your FBI plunges you into the dangerous underworld of organized crime, where a network of racketeers has fled federal jurisdiction, scattering across state lines like roaches when the lights come on. With narrator Westbrook Van Voorhis's authoritative voice cutting through the static, you'll follow federal agents as they piece together a criminal conspiracy that reaches from the streets of major cities into small towns where nobody thought such corruption could take root. The tension builds as phone lines crackle with urgent leads, as investigators race against time, and as each new clue brings them closer to dangerous men who'll stop at nothing to stay free. This is real crime, ripped from actual FBI case files—the kind of story that made listeners check their locks at night.

This Is Your FBI held a unique position in the radio drama landscape: it wasn't pure fiction, nor was it a simple documentary. Produced with FBI cooperation and based on genuine cases, each episode lent an air of authenticity that competitors couldn't match. The show ran from 1945 to 1953 during the Golden Age of radio, capturing post-war America's fascination with law and order at a time when organized crime seemed to be everywhere. These weren't melodramatic fantasies—they were stories of real federal agents doing real work, adapted for broadcast entertainment.

Tune in now and experience the crackling excitement of classic radio crime drama, where every footstep could be a break in the case, and justice always—always—prevails.
