When a spiritualist's séance turns sinister and a prominent businessman vanishes without a trace, the FBI must pierce the veil between the material and the supernatural—or at least expose the con artist lurking behind it. In "The Unhappy Medium," listeners will find themselves in a fog-shrouded parlor where flickering candlelight, creaking floorboards, and disembodied voices create an atmosphere thick with menace. Is the medium genuinely channeling the dead, or is she orchestrating an elaborate scheme to bilk the city's wealthy elite? As Special Agent in Charge narrates the Bureau's meticulous investigation, the tension mounts: coded messages, forged spiritualist documents, and witnesses who may be willing accomplices all point toward a conspiracy far more earthly—and far more dangerous—than any ghost.

"This Is Your FBI" distinguished itself from the typical pulp crime dramas of the mid-1940s by grounding its stories in actual FBI case files, lending an air of documentary authenticity that audiences craved during the post-war years. The show premiered in 1945 as America's faith in law enforcement institutions reached new heights, and each episode served as both entertainment and subtle propaganda for J. Edgar Hoover's increasingly powerful Bureau. Episodes like "The Unhappy Medium" cleverly exploited American fascination with spiritualism and the occult—a cultural phenomenon that had surged during the Depression and wartime, when grieving families sought contact with lost loved ones—while simultaneously debunking pseudoscience and celebrating rational, scientific detective work.

Don't miss this masterclass in old-time radio suspense, where the real mystery isn't supernatural at all, but the very human capacity for deception. Tune in to discover how the FBI separates fact from fraud, and how one woman's spiritualist scheme leads to a criminal conspiracy that stretches far beyond the séance room.
