When the starting bell rings at the racetrack, fortunes change in seconds—but on this August evening in 1951, a different kind of fortune vanishes entirely. A bold daylight robbery at the betting windows leaves investigators scrambling and listeners on the edge of their seats as "This Is Your FBI" unravels The Post Time Stick Up. This tense episode captures the Bureau's methodical pursuit of criminals who prey on the racing crowd, where large sums exchange hands and split-second timing is everything. Expect breathless narration, the authentic procedural detail that made this program a national phenomenon, and the mounting pressure as federal agents close in on their quarry through fingerprints, witness statements, and old-fashioned detective work.

"This Is Your FBI" held a unique place in American radio during its eight-year run, operating with unprecedented access to actual FBI case files and official Bureau cooperation—a partnership that lent it documentary authenticity few crime programs could claim. By 1951, when this episode aired, listeners had grown accustomed to the show's unflinching realism and its celebration of federal law enforcement during an era when the FBI loomed large in the American imagination. The program's opening announcement—"The Federal Bureau of Investigation, an agency so vast that its files contain the stories of thousands of cases"—promised real crime stories dramatized for radio, blurring the line between entertainment and official record in a way that captivated millions.

If you've never experienced the crackling tension of classic FBI radio drama, or if you're already a devoted fan of procedural crime fiction, this episode delivers the goods: swift action, genuine suspense, and the satisfaction of seeing justice served by America's premier investigative agency. Tune in and discover why this show remained a fixture in American living rooms for nearly a decade.
