# Fibber McGee and Molly: April 16, 1935

Step into the cozy, chaos-filled home at 79 Maple Street as Fibber McGee spins yet another tall tale that spirals hilariously out of control, while his patient wife Molly delivers her trademark deadpan reactions with impeccable timing. In this inaugural broadcast, listeners are introduced to the lovable schemer and his sharp-tongued better half as they navigate the comic minefield of domestic life—a place where Fibber's grandiose exaggerations collide spectacularly with Molly's unflappable common sense. The warm, intimate studio audience laughter and the crisp clarity of the original NBC broadcast capture a moment when radio comedy was becoming America's favorite evening entertainment.

This historic premiere represents the birth of what would become one of the longest-running and most beloved programs in radio history, pioneering the domestic comedy format that would define an era. Jim and Marian Jordan's creation broke new ground with its emphasis on character-driven humor and genuine affection between its leads—no slapstick needed when the chemistry was this authentic. The show's success lay in its accessible humor that appealed equally to housewives and businessmen, children and grandparents, making Fibber and Molly household names across America for nearly a quarter-century. This first episode captures the spark of a phenomenon in its infancy, before the running gags and beloved supporting characters became legendary.

If you've never experienced the magic of Fibber McGee and Molly, this is your invitation to witness radio comedy at its finest. Settle in, imagine yourself in that living room alongside generations of Americans who gathered around their sets, and discover why this show earned its place in broadcasting immortality. One listen to Fibber's convoluted storytelling and Molly's knowing chuckle, and you'll understand why listeners kept tuning in, night after night, for over two decades.
