# Fibber McGee and Molly - Crime Expert McGee (36-02-03)

When the doorbell rings at 79 Maple Street and a mysterious visitor arrives with tales of criminal intrigue, Fibber McGee springs into action with characteristic bravado—convinced that his years of reading detective magazines have made him an expert criminologist. What follows is a delightful cascade of mishaps and misunderstandings as our hero attempts to crack a case, each "brilliant deduction" more ridiculous than the last, while the long-suffering Molly watches with bemused patience and sharp-tongued commentary. The familiar warmth of their living room becomes a comedy stage where every creaking floorboard, every interruptive telephone call, and every entrance by their eccentric neighbors contributes to a perfect storm of comedic timing. Listeners will thrill to the rapid-fire dialogue and expertly-timed sound effects that made this show an institution in American homes.

Fibber McGee & Molly was radio's longest-running situation comedy, and episodes like "Crime Expert McGee" exemplify why audiences tuned in faithfully for over two decades. Jim and Marian Jordan created a household as relatable as your own—cluttered, chaotic, yet deeply affectionate—where blue-collar aspirations and white-collar pretensions collided in hilarious ways. The show's success lay not in slapstick alone but in genuine character development; audiences loved Fibber not despite his endless schemes and tall tales, but because his heart was always in the right place, even when his judgment was decidedly elsewhere.

Settle into your favorite chair and prepare to visit one of radio's most beloved homes. "Crime Expert McGee" is classic Golden Age radio at its finest—proof that the best comedy comes from characters we recognize, situations we understand, and the timeless human desire to be something just a little grander than we actually are. Tune in to hear why America couldn't get enough of Fibber and Molly!
