# Fibber McGee And Molly - Judge Of Court Of Domestic Relations

Step into the cozy living room at 79 Wistful Vista as Judge McGee presides over the most delicate cases imaginable—marital disputes brought right to his humble home. In this rollicking installment, Fibber has somehow convinced himself (and is desperately trying to convince Molly) that he possesses the wisdom and fairness to settle domestic quarrels between their neighbors. What follows is a masterclass in comedic chaos as real couples present their grievances, Fibber's advice grows increasingly absurd, and Molly punctures every pretense with her sharp tongue and sharper wit. The studio audience roars with recognition as Fibber spins elaborate justifications for his rulings, each one more ridiculous than the last, while our ever-patient Molly delivers devastating one-liners that cut straight through the nonsense. It's the kind of homespun humor that made millions of Americans tune in faithfully each Tuesday night.

For nearly a quarter-century, *Fibber McGee & Molly* dominated the airwaves by mining comedy from the everyday frustrations of married life—a revolutionary concept in the early days of broadcasting. Created by Don Quinn, the show's genius lay in its perfect comic timing and the palpable chemistry between stars Jim and Marian Jordan, who played the titular couple with such authenticity that listeners felt like family friends eavesdropping on their lives. These weren't caricatures but recognizable people, and whether Fibber was fabricating tall tales or attempting some harebrained scheme, his heart remained true.

Don't miss this glorious example of radio comedy at its finest. Tune in to hear why Judge Fibber McGee's court of domestic relations is the most entertaining—if least reliable—arbiter of marital justice ever broadcast into American homes.
