# Fibber McGee and Molly: Winning the Scandinavian Sweepstakes

Picture this: it's a crisp November evening in 1940, and Fibber McGee bursts through the front door of 79 Wistful Vista with news that will upend the entire McGee household. He's won the Scandinavian Sweepstakes—a fortune beyond imagining! But as Molly will soon discover, Fibber's version of the facts bears only the faintest resemblance to reality. What follows is a delicious carousel of schemes, misunderstandings, and the kind of rapid-fire banter that made America tune in religiously every Tuesday night. Fibber's lies spiral with comic precision, each whopper building on the last, while Molly delivers cutting remarks with the timing of a seasoned vaudeville performer. The supporting cast—from the perpetually skeptical Mayor La Trivia to the voice of doom himself, Old Timer—adds layer upon layer of comedic chaos. By the time the episode reaches its inevitable unraveling, listeners will be gasping between fits of laughter.

Fibber McGee and Molly stands as the longest-running comedy program in radio history, and episodes like this showcase exactly why. The show thrived on a simple but brilliant formula: an ordinary American couple living in an imaginary small town, with Fibber's fantastical fabrications serving as the engine for escalating comic mayhem. By the early 1940s, the show had become a cultural institution, with "getting the door" a national catchphrase referring to the famous closet gag that spilled sound effects for nearly a minute straight.

If you've never experienced the McGees, this is the perfect episode to start with—a masterclass in comedic timing and ensemble work that captures radio's golden age at its most enchanting. Tune in and discover why forty million Americans couldn't miss a single broadcast.
