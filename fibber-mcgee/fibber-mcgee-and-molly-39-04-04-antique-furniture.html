# Fibber McGee And Molly - April 4, 1939: Antique Furniture

Step into the McGee household on this delightful spring evening and witness the chaos that ensues when Fibber decides their modest home needs refinishing with "authentic antiques." What begins as an innocent Saturday afternoon quickly spirals into Molly's exasperated attempts to stop her well-meaning but hopelessly misguided husband from bringing home every dusty, questionable relic he encounters at the local auction house. As Fibber spins increasingly elaborate tales about the pedigree of his "treasures"—naturally each one more wildly implausible than the last—the sound effects team conjures the crash, bang, and splinter of furniture being hauled through doorways it was never meant to fit. Molly's withering one-liners cut through Fibber's bluster like a knife through butter, and you can almost hear the live studio audience roaring with approval at every perfectly timed comeback.

For over two decades, *Fibber McGee & Molly* remained one of America's most beloved radio programs, earning its place as a weekly ritual in millions of households. The show's genius lay in its portrait of marriage itself—not saccharine or sentimentalized, but genuine, witty, and deeply affectionate. Jim and Marian Jordan, the husband-and-wife team behind Fibber and Molly, brought an authenticity to their characters that resonated across every demographic, making the show a cultural touchstone through the Depression, World War II, and beyond. Their rapid-fire banter and impeccable timing set the standard for domestic comedy that would influence television for generations to come.

Tune in now to experience the warmth, laughter, and unmistakable charm of classic radio entertainment—where the best comedy emerged not from spectacle, but from the timeless battle between romantic optimism and domestic reality.
