# Fibber McGee & Molly: The Hundred Dollar Bill

Picture yourself huddled close to the radio dial on a winter evening in the early 1940s, ready for the kind of domestic chaos that made America laugh during uncertain times. In this hilarious episode, Fibber McGee stumbles upon a mysterious hundred-dollar bill—a small fortune in Depression-era money—and the discovery sets off a comedic chain reaction of schemes, half-truths, and escalating misunderstandings that only Fibber's quick tongue can manufacture. Will he confess where he found it? Will Molly see through his increasingly elaborate fabrications? The magic of this episode lies in how the writers extract maximum comedy from a simple premise, as Fibber weaves tale after tale, each more outlandish than the last, while his ever-patient wife Molly delivers the perfect counterpoint of exasperation and affection that made their partnership legendary.

For nearly twenty-five years, *Fibber McGee & Molly* was appointment radio—the show that defined the domestic comedy format and influenced everything that would come after, from television sitcoms to modern humor itself. Jim and Marian Jordan's improvisational genius and chemistry transformed their characters from vaudeville acts into beloved neighbors, and their 678 episodes became a cultural touchstone for millions of listeners seeking escape and laughter. This particular episode captures the show at its peak, when the writers had perfected the formula: a small-town premise, relatable characters, and jokes that landed on multiple levels for children and adults alike.

These are the moments that radio was born to deliver—genuine laughter shared across the airwaves, binding a nation together in real time. Tune in and discover why Fibber McGee & Molly endured as one of broadcasting's greatest treasures.
