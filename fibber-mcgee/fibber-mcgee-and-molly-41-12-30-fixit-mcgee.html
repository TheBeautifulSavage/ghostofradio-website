# Fibber McGee and Molly: "Fix-It McGee" (December 30, 1941)

When Fibber McGee decides he's a master handyman, mayhem is sure to follow—and this December evening broadcast proves no exception. As the new year approaches and Molly watches with barely concealed exasperation, her husband sets out to tackle a household repair that spirals hilariously out of control. Listen as the McGees' cozy 79 Wistful Vista home becomes ground zero for comedic catastrophe, complete with the show's legendary sound effects and the palpable tension between Fibber's boundless confidence and Molly's perfectly-timed retorts. Whether he's mangling tools, misinterpreting instructions, or simply making matters exponentially worse, Fibber's "helpful" nature transforms a simple fix into an evening of domestic chaos that will have you laughing into your radio.

By the early 1940s, Fibber McGee and Molly had become America's favorite married couple—a refreshing departure from previous radio comedies that relied on slapstick or broad humor. This episode, broadcast during America's first Christmas season as a nation at war, captures the show's signature blend of witty dialogue, genuine affection between spouses, and working-class authenticity. The chemistry between Jim and Marian Jordan, who played the titular roles, was unmatched; their improvisation and timing made even the simplest domestic squabble feel like witnessing real marital navigation filtered through comedy. The show's influence on American humor and radio comedy cannot be overstated—it set the standard for situation comedy that would eventually define television.

Don't miss this timeless slice of Americana. Settle in, dial into the warmth and humor of the McGees' home, and discover why this show captivated millions of listeners for nearly a quarter-century. Some laughs, like fine wine, only improve with age.
