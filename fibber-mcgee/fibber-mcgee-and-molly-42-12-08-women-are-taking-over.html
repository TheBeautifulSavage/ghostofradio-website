# Fibber McGee and Molly: "Women Are Taking Over" (December 8, 1942)

Step into the cozy, cluttered living room of 79 Wistful Vista as Fibber concocts yet another scheme that's guaranteed to backfire spectacularly. In this December 1942 episode, the battle of the sexes reaches comic fever pitch when Molly and her female friends decide to take control—and poor Fibber finds himself outmaneuvered at every turn. Expect the rapid-fire banter that made this show a national institution, the clever wordplay from announcer Harlow Wilcox, and of course, the legendary sound effect of Fibber opening that closet to release an avalanche of mysterious contents. The women prove themselves more than a match for Fibber's elaborate fabrications, turning the tables with their own quick wit and unshakeable common sense.

This episode arrives at a particularly poignant moment in American history. With millions of men serving overseas in World War II, women had stepped into unprecedented roles in factories, offices, and communities—and "Women Are Taking Over" playfully reflects this seismic cultural shift. Yet Fibber McGee & Molly transcended mere topical comedy; it was appointment radio for over two decades, drawing 40 million listeners weekly with its portrayal of an ordinary marriage that felt authentically lived-in, bickering, loving, and hilarious.

Tune in to witness Jim and Marian Jordan—radio's most beloved husband-and-wife team—at their comedic peak, as they navigate the joyful chaos of mid-century domestic life. This is radio entertainment at its finest: intelligent, warm, and impossibly funny.
