Picture yourself settling into your favorite chair on a spring evening in the 1940s, radio dial glowing warm amber as you tune in to hear Jim and Marian Jordan—the real-life married couple behind Fibber McGee and Molly—launch into another hilarious misadventure. In this episode, Fibber has gotten his hands on a horoscope predicting catastrophe, and what begins as innocent superstition spirals into comic pandemonium as he attempts to outwit fate itself. Will his schemes protect him from the prophesied doom, or will his legendary fibbing finally catch up with him? You'll encounter the show's beloved supporting cast—the wise-cracking Mayor La Trivia, the Doctor, and the hall closet that's become legendary for its explosive contents—all woven into a plot that builds to perfectly-timed comedic chaos.

This episode exemplifies why Fibber McGee and Molly dominated American radio for over two decades, becoming one of the most beloved and influential comedy programs in broadcasting history. The Jordans' genuine chemistry and impeccable timing created a show that transcended simple gags; their portrayal of an ordinary married couple navigating everyday situations with extraordinary humor resonated deeply with Depression and wartime audiences seeking comfort and laughter. The program pioneered many techniques that would define American sitcoms for generations: recurring characters, a familiar setting, and most importantly, the warm domestic humor that made listeners feel like part of the family.

Don't miss this opportunity to experience the golden age of radio comedy when millions gathered around their sets for genuine, live entertainment. "Fibber's Horoscope" captures everything that made this show unforgettable—wit, warmth, and the incomparable timing of two masters of their craft. Tune in and discover why America couldn't get enough of Fibber McGee and Molly.
