# Fibber McGee & Molly: "WAAC Commission for Nitney's Daughter"

Picture yourself settling into your favorite chair on a crisp evening, the warm glow of your radio set casting shadows across the parlor as Fibber McGee's unmistakable voice crackles through the speaker with characteristic bluster and scheme. In this episode, the irrepressible schemer dreams up yet another elaborate plot to secure a Women's Army Auxiliary Corps commission for Nitney's daughter—and naturally, what could possibly go wrong? Listeners will delight in the rapid-fire comic exchanges, the masterful sound effects that bring the McGee household to vivid life, and the inevitable cascade of complications that unfolds as Fibber's "foolproof" plan spirals spectacularly out of control. Molly's dry, knowing commentary provides the perfect counterweight to her husband's manic energy, and you'll find yourself laughing aloud as the full cast weaves their comedy magic around this timely wartime scenario.

During the 1940s, when America's daughters were stepping boldly into uniform, *Fibber McGee & Molly* remained America's most beloved domestic comedy, proving that laughter was as essential as patriotic duty. The show's enduring appeal lay in its perfect formula: a hapless but lovable protagonist, a sensible spouse who served as comedy's straight man, and a supporting cast of unforgettable characters from the neighborhood. These weren't merely jokes—they were windows into American life, touching on real concerns while maintaining the escapist magic that kept millions tuning in twice weekly.

Tonight, join Fibber and Molly as they navigate wartime enthusiasm, neighborhood complications, and the hilariously unpredictable consequences of one man's good intentions. This is vintage radio comedy at its finest—the kind of wholesome, intelligent humor that built an empire and captured the heart of a nation.
