# Fibber McGee and Molly - "Etiquette"

Picture this: it's a crisp autumn evening in 1940s America, and the McGees of Wistful Vista are preparing for their most ambitious social undertaking yet. Fibber, ever the schemer with grandiose dreams, has convinced Molly that they simply *must* host an elegant dinner party befitting high society—despite their modest means and Fibber's catastrophic track record with anything resembling proper decorum. As the evening unfolds, listeners will delight in Fibber's increasingly desperate attempts to navigate the minefield of etiquette while Molly watches with knowing exasperation. The supporting cast of Wistful Vista's beloved characters—the Old Timer, Doc Gamble, and countless others—collide hilariously with Fibber's schemes, building to the kind of comedic crescendo that made this show an institution in American living rooms.

What set *Fibber McGee and Molly* apart from other comedies of the era was its grounding in recognizable domestic life. Here were characters that Americans knew intimately, living in a town that felt like their own, dealing with very real social anxieties about propriety and fitting in—yet filtered through Jim and Marian Jordan's brilliant comic sensibility. The show's longevity from 1935 through 1959 wasn't accident; it was the result of genuine warmth between the characters and a writing team that understood what made ordinary life extraordinary.

If you've never experienced the magic of Fibber McGee and Molly in action, "Etiquette" is a perfect entry point into this golden age treasure. Let yourself be transported back to a simpler time when families gathered around the radio, and comedy came from the heart. Tune in and discover why millions of listeners made this show an essential part of their weekly routine.
