# Fibber McGee and Molly: Treasure Map (January 15, 1946)

When Fibber McGee stumbles upon a mysterious treasure map tucked away in his cluttered attic, the stage is set for one of the season's most delightfully chaotic adventures. As Molly wisely counsels caution, Fibber's eyes gleam with visions of buried riches, and soon the McGees' modest Wisteria Lane home becomes the nerve center of a hilariously misguided treasure hunt. Bill Thompson's return to the show injects fresh comedic electricity into the proceedings, his distinctive voice and impeccable timing amplifying every mishap and misunderstanding. Listeners will delight in the increasingly absurd schemes hatched by Fibber and his confederates as they navigate false clues, suspicious neighbors, and the ever-present threat that Molly's common sense might actually derail the whole enterprise before disaster strikes.

For nearly a decade by 1946, Fibber McGee and Molly had become the heartbeat of American radio comedy, captivating millions of listeners with their perfect chemistry and razor-sharp writing. The show thrived on a simple but potent formula: Fibber's elaborate fabrications and get-rich-quick schemes perpetually colliding with Molly's grounded wisdom and the colorful cast of Wisteria Lane regulars, each a finely-drawn character in their own right. This episode exemplifies the show's golden age, when the McGees were at the height of their popularity and the writers crafted scenarios that felt both intimately domestic and wildly fantastical.

Don your headphones and prepare for an evening of genuine laughter as the McGees chase fortune and folly in equal measure. This is radio comedy at its finest—swift, clever, and utterly infectious. Tune in as Fibber's latest scheme unfolds, and discover why millions tuned in faithfully to follow the exploits of radio's most beloved couple.
