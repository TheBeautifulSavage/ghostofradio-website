Fibber Mcgee And Molly 46 03 26 Bullets Branigan

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

Tune in as Fibber's latest scheme spirals gloriously out of control when a mysterious visitor arrives at 79 Wistful Vista with a name that sounds plucked straight from a dime novel. "Bullets" Branigan becomes the unwitting catalyst for a comedy of errors that builds from Fibber's initial fabrication into an elaborate house of cards, complete with mistaken identities, escalating complications, and Molly's withering one-liners cutting through the chaos like a knife. Listeners will delight in the rapid-fire banter, perfectly-timed sound effects (including the now-legendary sound of their overcrowded closet door bursting open), and the mounting exasperation in Molly's voice as her husband digs himself deeper into deception. This is Fibber McGee at his scheming finest, with danger lurking in every misunderstanding.

By the mid-1940s, Fibber McGee and Molly had become America's most beloved domestic comedy, a fifteen-minute sanctuary of laughter that aired every Tuesday night in millions of homes. Created by Don Quinn and starring Jim and Marian Jordan, the show's magic lay in its perfect storm of genuine affection between the titular couple, razor-sharp writing, and an ensemble of recurring characters—from the gossipy Mrs. Uppington to the long-suffering Old Timer—that made Wistful Vista feel like a real neighborhood. The closet gag alone became a national institution, with listeners anticipating that eruption of sound and tumbling objects week after week.

Don't miss "Bullets" Branigan—a prime example of why this show remained at the top of America's radio ratings for over two decades. Settle in and experience the comedy that defined an era.
