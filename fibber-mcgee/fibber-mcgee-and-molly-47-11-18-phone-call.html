Settle into your favorite chair and prepare for an evening of delightful domestic chaos. In this November 1947 installment, a simple telephone call threatens to unravel Fibber McGee's carefully constructed web of tall tales. When an unexpected voice crackles through the receiver, our lovable fibber finds himself caught between his exaggerated stories and the uncomfortable truth—with Molly's sharp wit and the bustling sounds of their Wisteria Street neighborhood colliding in a comedy of mounting desperation. What begins as an innocent moment transforms into a masterclass of comedic timing, complete with overlapping dialogue, urgent knocks at the door, and the signature sound effects that made listeners lean closer to their radios in anticipation.

Fibber McGee & Molly stands as one of radio's most enduring treasures, having earned its place in American popular culture through fifteen years of weekly laughter and remarkably sophisticated humor. Unlike many comedies of the era that relied on slapstick or crude jokes, the show's genius lay in its character-driven narrative—Fibber's compulsive dishonesty played against Molly's patient exasperation created a marital dynamic that audiences found both hilarious and oddly touching. By the late 1940s, when this episode aired, the program had transcended mere entertainment to become a genuine cultural institution, with listeners across the nation tuning in religiously to hear what predicament Fibber would stumble into next.

Don't miss this sparkling example of radio comedy at its finest. Join Fibber and Molly as they navigate the treacherous waters of a telephone call, where one wrong word could expose everything. This is the Golden Age of radio preserved in amber—immediate, intimate, and utterly irresistible.
