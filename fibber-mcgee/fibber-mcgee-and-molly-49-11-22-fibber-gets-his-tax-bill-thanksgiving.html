# Fibber McGee and Molly: "Fibber Gets His Tax Bill"

Picture it: the week before Thanksgiving, 1949, and poor Fibber McGee has just opened an official-looking envelope from the tax collector. What follows is a masterclass in domestic comedy as this lovable schemer attempts to explain away his creative accounting to his ever-patient wife Molly, all while their 79 Wistful Vista neighbors—including the Old Timer, Doc Gamble, and the unforgettable Mayor LaTrivia—drop by with their own holiday troubles. The tension between mounting financial anxiety and the approaching holiday feast crackles through every carefully timed gag, as Fibber's increasingly elaborate excuses collide spectacularly with the simple truth. Will he face the music before Thanksgiving dinner, or will another of his wild schemes save the day? The laughter builds as expertly as the chaos that always seems to swirl around that famous hallway closet.

For nearly twenty-five years, Fibber McGee and Molly represented the beating heart of American radio comedy—a show that understood how to balance genuine affection between characters with razor-sharp comedic timing. Set in the fictional small town of Wistful Vista, the series captured the spirit of ordinary Americans navigating extraordinary situations, their troubles always punctuated by warm neighborly spirit and quick-witted repartee. This November 1949 episode perfectly encapsulates what made the show an institution, combining timely post-war concerns about taxation with timeless themes of marriage, community, and the holidays—all wrapped in comedy gold.

Settle in with your own cup of coffee and experience the golden age of radio comedy when humor was clever, characters were beloved, and a well-timed sound effect could bring down the house. Fibber McGee and Molly await.
