# Fibber McGee and Molly: "Sleighride" (January 31, 1950)

Bundle up and hitch your team to the runners, because this winter wonderland of an episode finds Fibber McGee hatching yet another scheme that's sure to send Molly into exasperated fits of laughter. When a sudden snowfall blankets Wistful Vista, Fibber decides he's the man to organize the neighborhood's first grand sleighride—never mind that his track record with community events rivals his talent for telling the truth. As the plans spiral delightfully out of control, listeners will be treated to the signature sound effects that made the show legendary: the crack of whips, the jingling of sleigh bells, and the unmistakable sound of Fibber's overconfidence meeting the real world. Molly's dry, cutting observations are perfectly timed to puncture his ego at every turn, while the supporting cast—including the unforgettable Doc Gamble and Mayor La Trivia—add layers of comic chaos to the proceedings.

By 1950, *Fibber McGee and Molly* had become an American institution, a show that families gathered around their radios to hear every Tuesday night for nearly two decades. Jim and Marian Jordan's chemistry was unparalleled in radio comedy; their banter felt genuine and warm, grounded in a real marriage that informed every joke and hesitation in their dialogue. The show's genius lay in its ability to capture small-town American life with affection and humor, transforming the mundane into comedy gold while maintaining a sweetness that audiences craved during uncertain times.

Don't miss this delightful winter episode that captures everything audiences loved about radio's greatest comedy team. Tune in and discover why millions of listeners made room in their hearts—and their radio schedules—for the McGees.
