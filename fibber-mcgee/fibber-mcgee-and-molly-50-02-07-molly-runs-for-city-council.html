# Fibber McGee and Molly: Molly Runs for City Council

When gentle Molly McGee decides to throw her hat into the ring for city council, she sets the stage for delicious domestic comedy and civic confusion. As listeners tune in on this winter evening in 1940, they'll find themselves in the cozy McGee household on Wistful Vista—a place where Molly's earnest ambitions clash hilariously with Fibber's schemes to help (and inevitably complicate) her campaign. Expect the usual parade of Wistful Vista characters who drop by to offer assistance: the well-meaning but bumbling friends, the bewildered neighbors, and of course, Fibber's tall tales that threaten to derail everything. The tension between Molly's genuine decency and Fibber's fibbing nature creates a perfect storm of small-town politics and domestic warmth that makes this episode quintessential McGee material.

By the 1940s, *Fibber McGee and Molly* had become America's favorite radio couple, their fifteen-minute episodes commanding millions of listeners each week. Jim and Marian Jordan created something revolutionary: a show where the husband wasn't the comedic hero, but rather his patient, clever wife who often outsmarted his schemes. When Molly ventures into politics—even at the local level—the show tackles the timely question of women's voices and civic participation with surprising sophistication wrapped in genuine humor. This episode represents the show at its peak popularity, when their blend of slapstick, character acting, and real emotional stakes had perfected the formula.

Step back into 1940 and join millions of Americans gathered around their radio sets for an evening of laughter. Molly's campaign for city council promises all the complications, misunderstandings, and heart that made *Fibber McGee and Molly* an institution in American homes.
