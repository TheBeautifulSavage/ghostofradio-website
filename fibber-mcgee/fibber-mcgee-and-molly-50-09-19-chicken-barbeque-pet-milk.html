# Fibber McGee and Molly: The Chicken Barbeque

Picture this: it's a warm evening in the McGee household on Wistful Vista, and Fibber has gotten it into his head to throw the neighborhood's most spectacular chicken barbeque anyone's ever seen. Of course, his grand plans collide spectacularly with reality in the way only Fibber knows how—his "foolproof" grilling contraption threatens to set the patio ablaze, Molly's sensible preparations are constantly undermined by her husband's increasingly wild improvisations, and the neighbors begin arriving with their own culinary disasters in tow. What unfolds is pure comedic chaos, punctuated by the kind of physical humor and snappy patter that made America tune in faithfully each Tuesday night, eager to hear what calamity would befall the McGees next.

For nearly a quarter-century, *Fibber McGee and Molly* reigned as one of radio's most beloved programs, and episodes like this one showcase exactly why. Jim and Marian Jordan, who originated and starred as the titular couple, perfected the art of the domestic comedy—real enough to feel relatable, absurd enough to provide genuine escape from the everyday troubles of the 1940s.
