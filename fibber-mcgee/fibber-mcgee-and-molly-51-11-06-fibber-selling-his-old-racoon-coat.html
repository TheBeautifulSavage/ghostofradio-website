# Fibber McGee & Molly: "Fibber Selling His Old Raccoon Coat"

Step into the McGee household on a crisp November evening as Fibber hatches yet another scheme to turn his threadbare raccoon coat into cold, hard cash. What begins as a simple transaction spirals into magnificent chaos when Molly catches wind of his plans—and her razor-sharp wit is more than a match for her husband's elaborate fabrications. Listeners will delight in the familiar sound of Fibber's increasingly desperate tall tales as he tries to convince everyone from Old Senator Claghorn to the unsuspecting neighbors that his shabby fur is a coveted treasure. The timing is impeccable, the banter sparkles with genuine affection beneath the squabbling, and you can practically hear the studio audience's delighted laughter echoing through your loudspeaker.

By 1940, *Fibber McGee & Molly* had become appointment radio—the show families gathered around their sets to hear, week after week, without fail. Jim and Marian Jordan's characters had evolved from simple caricatures into beloved friends, their 79 Wistful Vista address more familiar to millions than their own neighborhoods. The show's genius lay in its groundedness; beneath the slapstick and wild exaggeration was a marriage built on genuine partnership and affection. These episodes captured the economic anxieties of Depression-era America filtered through comedy, making Fibber's schemes to earn an extra dollar resonate with audiences struggling through hard times. The raccoon coat itself—that symbol of 1920s excess—had become almost quaint by the 1940s, making Fibber's desperation to sell it all the more amusing.

Don't miss this sparkling example of radio comedy at its finest. Tune in and discover why *Fibber McGee & Molly* remains unmatched in its ability to blend clever wordplay, physical comedy translated to sound, and genuine human warmth.
