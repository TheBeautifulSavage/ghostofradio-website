# Fibber McGee and Molly: Founder's Day (May 19, 1953)

When the town of Wistful Vista declares a Founder's Day celebration, Fibber McGee sees an opportunity too good to pass up—a dusty old municipal ordinance that might just save him from paying his taxes. What follows is a hilarious romp through red tape and bureaucratic absurdity as Fibber attempts to convince the authorities that an obscure nineteenth-century law exempts him from his civic obligations. Of course, his dear wife Molly watches from the wings with equal measures of exasperation and amusement as her husband's scheming spirals into increasingly elaborate territory. Meanwhile, the usual cast of Wistful Vista residents—from the Old Timer to Mayor LaTrivia—becomes entangled in Fibber's machinations, each convinced they're witnessing either genius or catastrophe, usually both at once. The tension between Fibber's optimistic cunning and the forces of law and order builds to a climax that will have listeners roaring with laughter.

For over two decades, *Fibber McGee and Molly* reigned as one of America's most beloved comedy programs, a show where masterful writing met perfectly-timed performances. Jim and Marian Jordan, the husband-and-wife team who created and starred as the titular characters, brought authentic domestic humor to millions of living rooms across the nation. The show's strength lay in its blend of slapstick, wordplay, and genuine warmth—you laughed *with* Fibber and Molly, not at them. This 1953 episode exemplifies the show's golden era, when writers mined timeless comedic gold from the clash between small-town dreams and small-town reality.

Tune in to experience radio comedy at its finest, when timing was everything and the human voice could conjure entire worlds of hilarity in thirty minutes.
