# Fibber McGee and Molly: Taking Train to Aunt Sara's (October 23, 1953)

All aboard for mayhem! When Fibber McGee and his long-suffering wife Molly announce their plans to catch the afternoon train to visit Aunt Sara, listeners know they're in for a wild ride—both metaphorical and literal. What should be a simple journey becomes a cascade of characteristic McGee mishaps: missed connections, confused luggage, chance encounters with the usual cast of Wistful Vista characters, and Fibber's elaborate tall tales that somehow manage to complicate matters at every turn. The familiar soundscape of the McGee household—that iconic creaking closet door and the ambient bustle of small-town America—gives way to the rhythmic clatter of the station platform, where Molly's exasperated common sense collides head-on with Fibber's infectious optimism and creative interpretations of the truth. By the time the whistle blows, you'll be thoroughly entangled in the comedic chaos only Fibber can generate.

For nearly two decades by the early 1950s, *Fibber McGee and Molly* had established itself as radio's gold standard of domestic comedy, proving that the medium thrived on authentic character chemistry and well-timed, clever writing. Jim and Marian Jordan's portrayal of America's favorite married couple captured something deeply relatable beneath the slapstick—genuine affection wrapped in the gentle ribbing of partners who truly knew each other. This episode exemplifies the show's enduring formula: the ordinary made hilarious through Fibber's irrepressible nature and Molly's steadying wisdom.
