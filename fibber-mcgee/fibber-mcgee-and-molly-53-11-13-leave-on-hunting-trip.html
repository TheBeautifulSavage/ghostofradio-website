# Fibber McGee & Molly: Leave on Hunting Trip (November 13, 1953)

Picture this: it's a crisp autumn evening, and Fibber McGee is practically vibrating with excitement about his upcoming hunting trip—the grandest adventure he's ever planned, naturally. Of course, his long-suffering wife Molly has her doubts, and with good reason. As the episode unfolds, listeners will delight in the familiar chaos that erupts when Fibber's elaborate schemes collide with reality. What could possibly go wrong when Fibber is in charge of preparations? Everything, naturally. From comically exaggerated hunting tales to the inevitable misunderstandings with their neighbors, this episode captures the show at its finest, with Molly's dry wisdom providing the perfect counterpoint to her husband's boisterous blundering. The famous McGee closet makes its inevitable appearance, and the sound effects—creaking doors, clattering equipment, and slamming metal—create a vividly chaotic backdrop that makes radio comedy an art form.

For nearly two decades, *Fibber McGee & Molly* had been America's favorite married couple, and by the 1950s, the show's formula was perfected. Jim and Marian Jordan, the real-life husband-and-wife team behind the characters, brought authentic affection to their bickering spouses, while the stellar supporting cast and ingenious sound design created an entire world listeners felt they truly inhabited. The show's humor transcended slapstick—it was rooted in the everyday frustrations and tenderness of marriage, resonating with audiences navigating their own domestic lives.

Settle into your favorite chair and tune in for this delightful slice of golden-age radio comedy. Whether you're a longtime fan or discovering Fibber and Molly for the first time, this episode reminds us why families gathered around the radio for decades to laugh with these unforgettable characters.
