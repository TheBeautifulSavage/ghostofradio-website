# Fibber McGee and Molly – November 17, 1953

Settle into your favorite armchair and prepare for an evening of domestic hilarity as Fibber McGee finally convinces Molly that this time—*this time*—he's genuinely ready for a hunting trip. The autumn air practically crackles through your radio speaker as Fibber begins his elaborate preparations, each exaggeration more preposterous than the last. You can almost hear the knowing exasperation in Molly's voice as she watches her husband don ridiculous hunting gear and spin increasingly tall tales about the mighty game he intends to bag. What could possibly go wrong? Everything, of course, in the most wonderfully entertaining fashion, as Fibber's grandiose plans collide spectacularly with reality, and the McGees' cozy Wisteria Avenue home transforms into a comedy of errors that will have you laughing out loud.

For nearly two decades, *Fibber McGee & Molly* reigned as one of America's most beloved radio comedies, pioneering the domestic sitcom format that would later dominate television. The show's genius lay in its effortless chemistry between Jim and Marian Jordan, who brought authentic married-couple banter to the airwaves with remarkable timing and warmth. By the early 1950s, the program was still hitting its stride, blending physical comedy (brought to life through brilliant sound effects), gentle satire of small-town American life, and genuine affection between characters. Episodes like this hunting adventure showcase why audiences tuned in religiously—these weren't just jokes, but miniature plays about recognizable human foibles.

Join millions of nostalgic listeners and experience the golden age of radio comedy. Tune in now to hear Fibber McGee stumble toward his hunting expedition with all the comedic grace of a bull in a china shop—and Molly standing by, ready with the perfect witty comeback.
