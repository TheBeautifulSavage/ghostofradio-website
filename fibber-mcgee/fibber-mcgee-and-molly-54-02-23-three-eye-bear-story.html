Picture this: it's a winter evening in 1942, and across America, families huddle around their radios as Fibber McGee launches into yet another tall tale that will have Molly rolling her eyes and the studio audience roaring with laughter. In this episode, our lovable schemer spins a wild yarn about a mysterious three-eyed bear, a tale so outrageously absurd that even his long-suffering wife knows better than to believe a word of it. As Fibber elaborates with increasingly ridiculous details, the comedy builds through the rapid-fire banter between the McGees—sharp-tongued Molly deflating each of his embellishments with her characteristic wit and practicality. The supporting cast of Wistful Vista's finest jump in and out of the chaos: the Mayor, the ever-gossipy Mrs. Uppington, and neighborhood regulars who've learned to take everything Fibber says with a grain of salt. It's vaudeville brought to life through the magic of sound effects, timing, and gifted comedians who know precisely how to milk a joke.

Fibber McGee and Molly was radio's longest-running comedy series, and episodes like "Three Eye Bear Story" showcase exactly why audiences couldn't get enough. The show's genius lay in its simplicity: a married couple trading witty insults and elaborate lies in a small-town setting that felt real to everyone listening, whether they lived in Manhattan or rural Montana. The chemistry between Jim and Marian Jordan, the real-life husband-and-wife team behind the McGees, gave the program an authenticity that couldn't be faked. In the 1940s, this was appointment listening—the moment the theme song hit, dinner conversations stopped and the nation tuned in for twenty-four minutes of guaranteed laughs.

Don't miss this chance to experience vintage comedy at its finest. Settle in with "Three Eye Bear Story" and discover why millions of listeners made the McGees part of their family.
