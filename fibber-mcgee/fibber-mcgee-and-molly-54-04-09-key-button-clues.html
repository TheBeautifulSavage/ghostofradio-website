# Fibber McGee and Molly: "Key & Button Clues" (April 9, 1954)

Step into the cozy clutter of 79 Wistful Vista as Fibber McGee faces his most perplexing predicament yet—a mystery that hinges on nothing more than a lost key and a stray button. What begins as a simple household annoyance spirals into classic McGee mayhem, complete with wild theories, suspicious neighbors poking their noses into the affair, and Molly's patient exasperation at her husband's increasingly elaborate explanations. As the evening unfolds, you'll hear the familiar crack of that legendary hall closet threatening to burst open, the timely arrival of the Old Timer with his rambling wisdom, and Mrs. Uppington's genteel judgment. The stakes may seem trivial—a key here, a button there—but in Fibber's hands, they become the centerpiece of an evening's worth of tangled dialogue, sly wordplay, and situations that spiral deliciously beyond anyone's control.

For nearly two decades, *Fibber McGee and Molly* had dominated NBC's Thursday night schedule, establishing itself as America's favorite domestic comedy and proving that humor thrived not in exotic settings but in the everyday frustrations of married life. Jim and Marian Jordan's chemically perfect portrayal of the McGees—Fibber's bluster and inventive dishonesty balanced against Molly's sharp-tongued wisdom—made the show a national institution by the 1940s. This 1954 episode exemplifies the show's enduring formula: simple domestic problems transformed through Fibber's irrepressible personality into elaborate comedic constructions.

Tune in now to experience the golden age of radio comedy, where a missing key and a button become gateways to unforgettable entertainment. *Fibber McGee and Molly* awaits—bring your appetite for laughter and clever writing.
