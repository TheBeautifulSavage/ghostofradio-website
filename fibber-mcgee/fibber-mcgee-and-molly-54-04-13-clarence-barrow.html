# Fibber McGee and Molly: April 13, 1954

Step into the cozy chaos of 79 Maple Street as Fibber spins another tall tale, this time involving the legendary defense attorney Clarence Darrow. When Fibber gets wind that the great courtroom orator might be coming to town, he simply cannot resist embellishing the story—claiming a personal connection that doesn't quite exist. As Molly rolls her eyes and their neighbors begin to arrive, the tension mounts between Fibber's fantastic fabrications and the looming possibility that someone might actually verify his claims. The timing couldn't be more delicious, with guests arriving at inopportune moments and the old closet door perpetually threatening to spill its legendary avalanche of household junk. You'll hear the familiar crackle of that golden-age comedy timing, the bursts of the studio audience's laughter, and Molly's patient-but-exasperated reactions to her husband's schemes—all the ingredients that made this show an American institution.

For nearly two decades, Fibber McGee and Molly had been America's favorite domestic comedy, broadcasting the weekly adventures of a lovable fibber and his clever, level-headed wife from their humble Midwestern home. By the 1950s, when this episode aired, the show had already become a cultural phenomenon, influencing countless sitcoms and establishing the template for radio comedy itself. The chemistry between Jim and Marian Jordan—the real-life husband-and-wife team behind the characters—was utterly natural, their improvisational gifts sharpening the scripts into comedy gold.

Don your headphones and prepare for the timeless entertainment that kept millions of Americans gathered around their radios night after night. This is comedy in its purest form—no laugh track, no studio antics, just masterful storytelling and impeccable timing that still captivates audiences today.
