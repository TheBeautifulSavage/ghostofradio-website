# Fibber McGee & Molly: "Baseball Tale"

Step into the cozy living room of 79 Wistful Vista on a warm spring evening as Fibber McGee spins one of his tall tales about baseball—a yarn so tall and so wildly entertaining that even his long-suffering wife Molly can barely contain her exasperation. What begins as an innocent anecdote spirals into an absurd adventure involving impossible plays, miraculous comebacks, and Fibber's characteristic embellishments that stretch the bounds of credibility (and good taste). The Smackout regulars—those wonderfully recurring characters who haunt the McGee household and neighborhood—weave in and out of the narrative with perfectly-timed interruptions and reactions that build the comedy to its inevitable, hilarious crescendo. In just eight minutes of pure radio magic, you'll experience the kind of genuine, spontaneous laughter that made America tune in religiously every Tuesday night.

Fibber McGee & Molly stood as the gold standard of domestic comedy throughout radio's golden age, and episodes like this one showcase why the show remained a top-ten phenomenon for nearly a quarter-century. Created by Don Quinn and starring the real-life married couple Jim and Marian Jordan, the show captured the rhythms of American middle-class life with remarkable warmth and authenticity, all while maintaining an edge of sophisticated wit. Baseball, being America's pastime, was an irresistible subject for Fibber's brand of humor—his fabrications about the game allowed Quinn's writers to lampoon both the sport's excesses and the American tendency toward boastful storytelling.

This particular episode preserves a vanished world of entertainment: live actors performing to studio audiences, split-second timing refined through hundreds of broadcasts, and the unique intimacy of voices filling living rooms across the nation. Slip on your headphones and discover why listeners made Fibber McGee & Molly appointment radio.
