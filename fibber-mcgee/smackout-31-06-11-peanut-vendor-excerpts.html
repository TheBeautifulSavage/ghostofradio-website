# Fibber McGee & Molly: "The Peanut Vendor"

Step into the cluttered living room at 79 Wistful Vista on a warm evening in the 1940s, where Fibber McGee is scheming once again—this time with dreams of striking it rich as a peanut vendor. What could possibly go wrong? Everything, naturally. In this hilarious excerpt from *Smackout*, the show's beloved running gag where Fibber attempts to peddle whatever odd merchandise he can cook up, we find our lovable huckster hawking roasted peanuts with all the bombast of a carnival barker. Molly watches with her characteristically patient exasperation as the plan spirals into chaos, while supporting characters tumble through the McGee household with perfectly timed comedic interruptions. The audience's laughter practically crackles through the airwaves as Fibber's grand schemes inevitably crumble into slapstick absurdity.

This episode captures what made *Fibber McGee & Molly* the longest-running situation comedy in radio history. Created by Don Quinn and starring real-life married couple Jim and Marian Jordan, the show defined American comedy for over two decades with its sharp writing, impeccable timing, and that legendary creaking closet door that became an iconic sound effect. Set in the fictional small town of Wistful Vista, the series proved that genuine character development and warm domestic humor could compete with vaudeville spectacle—and win. The *Smackout* segments especially showcased Fibber's indomitable optimism and Molly's grounded wisdom, a dynamic that resonated with Depression and wartime audiences seeking both escape and recognizable truth.

Tune in now to hear why millions tuned in every Tuesday night to watch Fibber McGee get in over his head one more time, with Molly standing by—always ready with a wisecrack and unconditional love.
