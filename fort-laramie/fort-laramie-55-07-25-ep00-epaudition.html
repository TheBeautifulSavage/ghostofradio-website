# Fort Laramie: "Audition" (July 25, 1955)

Step through the gates of Fort Laramie as the sun beats down on the Wyoming frontier, and prepare yourself for a tale of frontier justice gone awry. In this audition episode, listeners are thrust into the heart of military protocol and moral ambiguity when a soldier's questionable actions force Commander Hayden to navigate between loyalty to his men and duty to the law. The crackling campfire sounds and distant horse neighs transport you instantly to the 1870s, while tense dialogue crackles through your radio speaker like electricity. As accusations fly and secrets unravel within the fort's adobe walls, you'll find yourself gripped by the same uncertainty that grips the officers themselves—who deserves justice, and at what cost?

Fort Laramie represents the golden age of adult-oriented Western drama, when radio could explore the genuine complexities of frontier life without resorting to simple good-versus-evil narratives. This audition episode, broadcast in 1955, showcases why CBS's commitment to the series would span decades: authentic military procedure, nuanced character development, and scripts that treated the American West as a place where civilization and wilderness collide in morally complicated ways. The show's attention to historical detail—from the authentic sound design to the period-appropriate dialogue—set it apart from more melodramatic contemporaries.

If you've never experienced Fort Laramie, this audition episode is the perfect entry point into one of radio's most enduring dramas. Whether you're a devoted fan of vintage Westerns or simply curious about the golden age of radio, these voices from 1955 offer an unforgettable glimpse into America's frontier past, preserved in amber for over seventy years.
