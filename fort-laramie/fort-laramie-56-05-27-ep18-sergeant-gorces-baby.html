# Fort Laramie: "Sergeant Gorce's Baby"

When Sergeant Gorce's unexpected bundle of joy arrives at the remote Wyoming outpost, the hardened soldiers of Fort Laramie face an unprecedented crisis that threatens to unravel the strict military discipline holding the frontier garrison together. In this tender yet explosive episode, listeners will discover how a newborn child becomes the unexpected catalyst for conflict between duty and humanity, as the men must navigate the treacherous waters of compassion within the rigid framework of Army protocol. The crackling tension is palpable as gruff commanders clash with sympathetic soldiers, and the fate of an innocent life hangs in the balance. With authentic sound design—the wailing of an infant piercing through the wind-swept plains, boots marching across wooden barracks floors, and the measured tones of officers wrestling with impossible decisions—this episode captures the humanity that persisted even in the harshest outposts of the American West.

*Fort Laramie*, CBS's premier adult western drama, distinguished itself by refusing to traffic in simple good-versus-evil narratives. Instead, the show presented the frontier as a complex moral landscape where honor, survival, and compassion frequently collided. Set at the actual historic Fort Laramie, a crucial military installation during the post-Civil War era, each episode grounded its drama in authentic military procedure and genuine period challenges. The show's writers understood that soldiers were men first, and that the isolated prairie demanded both steel and sentiment from those who inhabited it.

Don't miss this gripping installment as Fort Laramie explores what it truly means to be a soldier when the rules of war meet the demands of the heart. Tune in to hear how one sergeant's personal crisis becomes the entire regiment's moral reckoning.
