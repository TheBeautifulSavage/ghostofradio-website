# Fort Laramie: "Old Enemy"

When the dust settles on the parade ground at Fort Laramie, Captain Lee Quince finds himself face to face with a ghost from his past—a man he believed dead, a rival whose very presence threatens to unravel the careful order he's maintained at the frontier post. In this tense episode, old wounds run deeper than the Wyoming territory is wide, and the Captain must confront not just a returning enemy, but the darker chapters of his own history that refuse to stay buried. As tensions escalate within the fort's walls, listeners will find themselves caught between loyalties, honor, and the question of whether some enemies deserve a second chance—or a bullet.

Fort Laramie stands as one of CBS Radio's most underrated achievements, a program that brought the authentic grit of the American West into living rooms across the nation. Unlike the more melodramatic westerns that cluttered the airwaves, Fort Laramie grounded its storytelling in the genuine complexities of frontier life: the rigid military hierarchy, the moral ambiguities of westward expansion, and the deeply personal conflicts that erupted when civilization clashed with the untamed frontier. The show's 1956 revival found eager audiences hungry for adult-oriented drama with substance beneath its six-gun exterior.

"Old Enemy" exemplifies why Fort Laramie earned its devoted following—it's a masterclass in building psychological tension through superb writing and performances that capture the weight of unresolved conflict. Whether you're a longtime devotee of classic radio drama or discovering Fort Laramie for the first time, this episode delivers the compelling character study and frontier atmosphere that made the show essential listening. Tune in and discover why audiences kept their radios tuned to Fort Laramie, night after night.
