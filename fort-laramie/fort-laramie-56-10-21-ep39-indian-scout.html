# Fort Laramie: "Indian Scout"

When the evening bells of Fort Laramie ring out across the Nebraska plains in this gripping October broadcast, listeners will find themselves in the shadow of impossible choices. Captain Lee Fossett faces a crisis that cuts to the very heart of frontier justice and loyalty when a trusted Indian scout stands accused of a crime that could ignite the powder keg of racial tension simmering beneath the fort's wooden palisades. As darkness falls and the wind howls across the high prairie, the captain must navigate treacherous political waters, questioning whether the evidence before him tells the true story—or merely the one his soldiers want to believe. The stakes have never been higher, and one man's fate hangs in the balance between military law and human conscience.

Fort Laramie stands as CBS's most authentic examination of the American West, stripping away the romanticized gunslinger mythology to present the complex, morally ambiguous reality of frontier life in the 1860s. Rather than celebrate manifest destiny with simple tales of good versus evil, the show's writers craft nuanced dramas where honorable men must confront their own prejudices, where duty and compassion collide, and where the real frontier struggle emerges not from savage encounters but from the difficult work of maintaining civilization in an untamed land. Fort Laramie treats its Native American characters with unusual dignity for the era, refusing to reduce them to one-dimensional obstacles in the white man's progress.
