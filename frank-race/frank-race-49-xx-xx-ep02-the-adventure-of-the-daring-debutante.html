# The Adventure of the Daring Debutante

Picture this: Frank Race, globe-trotting adventurer and master of intrigue, finds himself navigating the glittering yet treacherous waters of high society when a mysterious young woman crashes into his world with a dangerous secret. In this thrilling second episode, listeners are whisked away to an opulent Manhattan ballroom where beneath the champagne toasts and orchestra melodies lurks a sinister plot that only Frank's quick wits and steady nerve can unravel. When the debutante vanishes mid-waltz, leaving behind only a cryptic warning, Frank must race against time through fog-shrouded streets and shadowy speakeasies to discover the truth before it's too late. The stakes have never been higher, and neither have the odds.

The Adventures of Frank Race arrived at a golden moment in radio history, when post-war America hungered for excitement and escapism. Unlike the more domestic comedies and soap operas dominating the airwaves, this syndicated gem offered something deliciously different: a cosmopolitan hero for a generation discovering a wider world. Produced with crisp sound design and snappy dialogue that crackles with intelligence, Frank Race embodied the urbane sophistication listeners craved in 1949—equal parts Casablanca intrigue and modern noir sensibility. Each episode was crafted as a complete adventure, allowing audiences to tune in fresh while rewarding loyal fans with an ever-deepening sense of Frank's character.

Don't miss "The Adventure of the Daring Debutante"—a masterclass in suspense that proves radio's golden age was truly golden. Whether you're a devoted follower of Frank Race or discovering him for the first time, this episode captures everything that made this show a must-listen phenomenon. Tune in, settle back, and let the mystery unfold.
