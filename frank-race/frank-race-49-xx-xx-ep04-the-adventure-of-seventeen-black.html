# The Adventure of Seventeen Black

Picture yourself in a smoke-filled parlor on a foggy evening as Frank Race, that quick-witted globe-trotting investigator, finds himself ensnared in a labyrinth of deception centered around a mysterious racetrack syndicate. In "The Adventure of Seventeen Black," our hero must unravel the secrets surrounding a seemingly ordinary thoroughbred whose appearance has set off a chain reaction of blackmail, betrayal, and danger. With the clock ticking and shadowy figures closing in from all directions, Frank must rely on his razor-sharp intellect and street smarts to distinguish truth from elaborate lies. The episode crackles with that distinctive late-1940s energy—rapid-fire dialogue, unexpected plot twists, and the constant threat that danger lurks around every corner. You'll hear the authentic sound effects of a racing stable, the tension-building orchestral swells, and the voice of Frank Race cutting through the darkness with cool determination.

*The Adventures of Frank Race* arrived during radio's golden twilight, when the medium was beginning its slow fade as television rose on the horizon. Yet this syndicated series possessed all the hallmarks of adventure radio's finest hour—sophisticated writing that balanced humor with genuine suspense, and a protagonist who felt refreshingly modern for his era. Rather than relying on supernatural elements or pulp excess, Frank Race stories drew their power from clever plotting and character-driven drama, making them accessible to both seasoned radio enthusiasts and newcomers alike. The show's 1949-1950 run proved that audiences still hungered for quality adventure mystery, even as the medium entered its final chapter.

Settle in with static and shadows for "The Adventure of Seventeen Black." Whether you're a devoted Frank Race devotee or discovering this gem for the first time, this episode exemplifies everything that made old-time radio the theater of the mind. Tune in and let the drama wash over you.
