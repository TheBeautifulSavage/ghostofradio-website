When Frank Race boards a midnight express bound for Montreal, he expects another routine investigation—until a mysterious young stowaway turns his world upside down. As the locomotive cuts through the black Canadian night, the boy's cryptic warnings about danger aboard the train prove disturbingly accurate. Within hours, a passenger vanishes without a trace, and Frank finds himself racing against time through narrow corridor cars and cramped compartments, where every shadow conceals a potential suspect and every stop brings the train closer to a sinister revelation. The episode crackles with mounting tension as Race's trademark blend of quick thinking and harder-edged detective work collides with the vulnerability of protecting an innocent child in a confined, moving world where escape is impossible.

The Adventures of Frank Race emerged in 1949 as part of the last golden wave of syndicated adventure programming, capturing American listeners at the precise moment when radio's dominance was beginning to slip away to television. The show's appeal lay in its grounded realism—Race wasn't a caped crusader or a gentleman detective, but a seasoned operative who relied on street smarts, improvisation, and old-fashioned gumshoe determination. This tenth episode exemplifies why the series found devoted listeners across the country: it takes the familiar radio thriller machinery of a locked-room mystery and transplants it into the claustrophobic intimacy of a speeding train, where claustrophobia becomes as much a character as any of the cast.

Tune in tonight as Frank Race confronts his most challenging case yet—one where solving the mystery means protecting a life. In the golden age of radio drama, few shows captured the genuine unpredictability of danger quite like this.
