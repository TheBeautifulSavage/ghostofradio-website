Deep in the shadowed corridors of a Manhattan penthouse, Frank Race finds himself entangled in a murder most peculiar—one where the victim's heart has literally stopped beating, yet no poison graces the lips, no blade marks the flesh. As rain hammers against the windows and thunder splits the Manhattan sky, our intrepid investigator must navigate the treacherous waters of high society deceit, where every whispered confession masks a darker truth. The suspect list reads like a society page: a jilted lover, a business rival nursing old wounds, a devoted servant with secrets of her own. With only forty-eight hours before the police seal the case, Frank must employ both his razor-sharp intellect and his street-honed instincts to uncover whether this death was indeed medical mystery or carefully orchestrated murder. The tension crackles through every scene, punctuated by the eerie silence that gives this adventure its haunting title.

The Adventures of Frank Race emerged from the golden age of syndicated radio, when independent production companies competed fiercely for station time and listener loyalty. This 1949-1950 series distinguished itself through its psychological depth and atmospheric storytelling—eschewing the pulpier tropes of its contemporaries in favor of genuine mystery and character-driven drama. Created during the twilight of radio's dominance, Frank Race represented the medium's sophisticated evolution, appealing equally to urban commuters and small-town families huddled around their sets.

Don't miss this masterwork of suspenseful storytelling. Tune in as Frank Race peels back the layers of deception surrounding "The Adventure of the Silent Heart." The truth awaits in the darkness—if you're brave enough to listen.
