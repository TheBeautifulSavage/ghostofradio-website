# The Adventures of Frank Race: Episode 13 - The Adventure of the Garrulous Bartender

Picture yourself settling into your favorite chair on a crisp evening in 1949, the glow of your radio dial casting shadows across the room as that familiar dramatic sting announces Frank Race's latest peril. In this episode, our intrepid adventurer finds himself in a dimly-lit speakeasy—not as a patron, but as a desperate man searching for answers. A seemingly harmless bartender with a gift for rambling anecdotes becomes the unexpected key to unraveling a dangerous smuggling operation, but separating truth from tall tales proves more treacherous than any fistfight. As the bartender's endless stories weave in and out of genuine clues, Frank must navigate a minefield of misdirection, all while unseen eyes watch from the shadows. The tension mounts with each passing minute—will Frank extract the truth before the criminals realize he's onto them?

The Adventures of Frank Race carved out a unique niche in the golden age of radio, thriving during syndication's boom when shows could be tailored for local markets while maintaining broad appeal. Created for audiences hungry for sophisticated mystery-adventure that didn't talk down to them, the series balanced genuine danger with clever plotting and sharp dialogue. This particular episode exemplifies the show's strength: the ability to build suspense from deceptively ordinary situations, transforming a barroom chat into a chess match between wits and wills.

For those who appreciate storytelling that respects the listener's intelligence and imagination, this episode of Frank Race stands as a masterclass in building intrigue through conversation and implication.
