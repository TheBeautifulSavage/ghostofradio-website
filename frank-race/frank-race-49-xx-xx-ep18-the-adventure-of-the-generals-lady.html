When the curtain rises on this thrilling installment, Frank Race finds himself ensnared in a web of intrigue that reaches into the highest echelons of military power. A general's wife has vanished from a locked hotel room on the eve of a classified briefing, and all evidence points to an inside job—but Frank knows the truth is far more sinister. As orchestral swells punctuate each revelation and footsteps echo ominously through shadowed corridors, our intrepid investigator must navigate a treacherous landscape of blackmail, espionage, and betrayal where the wrong move could cost him everything. The clock ticks relentlessly as Frank races against time to uncover whether this disappearance is a crime of passion, an act of sabotage, or something darker still. Every clue leads deeper into danger; every suspect harbors secrets that could shake the very foundations of national security.

Frank Race emerged during that golden post-war era when America's appetite for intrigue seemed insatiable. This 1949 syndicated gem captured the paranoia and glamour of the emerging Cold War, offering audiences escapist thrills wrapped in contemporary anxieties about spies, corruption, and the corrupting influence of power. The show's strength lay in its refusal to paint mysteries in black and white—no criminal here is purely evil, no hero entirely spotless. "The General's Lady" exemplifies this moral complexity while delivering the breakneck pacing and clever plot mechanics that made Frank Race essential listening in living rooms across America.

Don't miss this masterclass in suspenseful storytelling. Tune in as Frank Race peels back the layers of deception to reveal the truth lurking beneath this military scandal. Mystery awaits!
