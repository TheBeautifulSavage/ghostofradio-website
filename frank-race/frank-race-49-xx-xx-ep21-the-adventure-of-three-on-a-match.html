Picture yourself settling into your favorite armchair on a Thursday evening, the living room bathed in lamplight as Frank Race finds himself ensnared in one of his most sinister predicaments yet. When a seemingly innocent superstition—three cigarettes lit on a single match—becomes the calling card of a criminal mastermind operating in the shadowy underbelly of postwar Manhattan, our intrepid investigator must navigate a labyrinth of double-crosses, blackmail, and murder. With only his wits, a .38 revolver, and his trusted associate's counsel to guide him, Race races against time to unmask the killer before another victim falls prey to the ancient curse. The crackling sound effects of rain-slicked streets, the ominous musical stings punctuating each revelation, and the steady-voiced narration will transport you directly into the noir-soaked world where danger lurks around every corner.

The Adventures of Frank Race embodied everything audiences craved from syndicated radio in 1949—sophisticated mystery, globe-trotting intrigue, and a protagonist whose moral compass remained true despite the moral murk surrounding him. This episode particularly showcases why the series earned devoted listeners across America, blending genuine superstition lore with hard-boiled detective fiction. The writing captures that fleeting moment when radio adventure was reaching its artistic zenith, before television would forever change entertainment consumption.

Don't miss "The Adventure of Three on a Match," where superstition becomes conspiracy and one wrong move could spell curtains for Frank Race himself. Tune in and discover why audiences kept their dials tuned to this classic adventure series—you'll understand immediately.
