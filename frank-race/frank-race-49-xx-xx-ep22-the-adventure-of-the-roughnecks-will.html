When the oil derricks of Texas cast long shadows across the badlands, trouble always follows—and this week, Frank Race stumbles into a conspiracy that reaches from the oil fields to the courthouse steps. A mysterious will surfaces, claimed by a hardened roughneck with calloused hands and a shadier past, but fortune-hungry relatives circle like vultures, and someone is willing to kill to keep the truth buried. As Frank peels back the layers of deception, the stakes mount higher with each discovery: forged documents, midnight rendezvous at the drilling site, and a final confrontation that will test his wits against a web of greed and violence. The rumble of oil pumps provides an ominous backdrop to this tale of inheritance and betrayal.

The Adventures of Frank Race captured audiences throughout 1949-1950 with its refreshingly modern take on the adventure-mystery formula. Unlike the more pulp-driven serials of the early radio era, Frank Race stories were grounded in real American settings and contemporary dilemmas—oil barons, business fraud, and small-town corruption—that resonated with post-war listeners rebuilding their nation. This particular episode exemplifies the show's strength: tight plotting, authentic period dialogue, and a protagonist who solved crimes through cunning and persistence rather than superhuman abilities. The supporting cast delivers memorably crooked characters, each with their own motives and secrets.
