# The Adventure Of The Green Doubloon

Picture this: it's late evening, the house is dark save for the warm glow of your radio dial, and Frank Race's distinctive voice crackles through the speaker with urgent purpose. In this thrilling installment, our globe-trotting investigator finds himself aboard a fog-shrouded ship in the Caribbean, pursuing a centuries-old Spanish treasure—a single, impossibly valuable green doubloon that has become the focal point of murder, betrayal, and nautical intrigue. As waves crash against the hull and unseen deck hands move through the mist, Race must distinguish between friend and foe while solving an ingenious puzzle: who among the ship's passengers would kill for a coin, and what sinister secret does its emerald hue conceal? The tension mounts with each passing moment, punctuated by the atmospheric sound effects that made this series the envy of every competing adventure program on the airwaves.

The Adventures of Frank Race occupied a unique niche in the golden age of radio, arriving just as the medium's influence was reaching its zenith in American households. Syndicated across stations nationwide from 1949 to 1950, it represented a distinctly post-war sensibility—a restless, sophisticated protagonist navigating an increasingly complex world with wit and determination. This episode exemplifies everything that made the series compelling: exotic locales, genuine mystery construction, and the kind of snappy dialogue that kept families gathered around the receiver into the late hours.

Don't miss "The Adventure Of The Green Doubloon." Settle into your favorite chair, dim the lights, and let the sterling voice work of Frank Race transport you across treacherous seas to a mystery only he can solve. This is radio adventure at its finest.
