Picture this: a shadowy penthouse high above the glittering streets of Manhattan, where a ruthless industrialist lies dead in his locked study, and the only person with the key to his murder is a hardened bodyguard—now reduced to tears, unable to speak. When Frank Race arrives on the scene, his sharp mind and sharper instincts must untangle a web of blackmail, betrayal, and blood. Is the bodyguard's grief genuine remorse, or an Oscar-worthy performance? As rain hammers against the windows and tension crackles through every room, Race navigates the treacherous world of high society where money buys silence and secrets cost lives. With a femme fatale lurking in the shadows and a police lieutenant breathing down his neck, Frank must decode the mystery before another body hits the floor.

The Adventures of Frank Race emerged during the golden age of syndicated radio when local stations hungered for affordable programming that could compete with network giants like The Shadow and The Saint. Starring smooth-voiced actor Bill Johnstone as the quick-witted investigator, the series captured the postwar appetite for sophisticated mystery—grounded not in supernatural terrors but in very human crimes of passion and profit. Each episode delivered tight plotting and atmospheric storytelling that proved you didn't need a major network's budget to captivate millions of listeners.

This particular installment showcases everything that made Frank Race a listener favorite: a morally complex protagonist, a puzzle-box plot that keeps you guessing, and performances that convey entire emotional landscapes through vocal nuance alone. Whether you're a devoted fan of classic radio mysteries or discovering this gem for the first time, "The Adventure of the Sobbing Bodyguard" promises an evening of genuine suspense and golden-age storytelling at its finest.
