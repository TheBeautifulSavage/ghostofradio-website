Picture this: a moonlit golf course shrouded in fog, where a priceless diamond has vanished and a beautiful woman lies unconscious in the rough. Frank Race, that quick-thinking adventurer with nerves of steel, must navigate a treacherous maze of lies, blackmail, and deception before dawn breaks over the greens. In this gripping thirty-ninth episode, our hero finds himself caught between a desperate heiress, a vengeful caddie, and a mysterious figure lurking in the shadows—someone who'll stop at nothing to keep a dark secret buried. As Race pieces together cryptic clues and confronts suspects in the clubhouse, the stakes escalate with each revelation. Will he recover the gem before the real culprit strikes again?

The Adventures of Frank Race arrived during the golden age of syndicated radio, when adventure serials could be heard in living rooms across America. This 1949 episode exemplifies what made the show a favorite among listeners: tightly plotted mysteries, snappy dialogue, and a hero who relied as much on charm and quick thinking as on his fists. The show's success lay in its ability to transport audiences to exotic and everyday locales alike—from Monte Carlo to small-town America—making danger feel both thrillingly distant and intimately real. The writing crackled with authenticity, and the sound design created atmospheres so vivid you could almost feel the sea spray or hear the rustle of evening clothes.

Don't miss The Adventure of the Fairway Beauty—a perfect example of why Frank Race became a beloved fixture in American homes. Tune in as this mystery unfolds with all the sophistication and suspense that made old-time radio unforgettable.
