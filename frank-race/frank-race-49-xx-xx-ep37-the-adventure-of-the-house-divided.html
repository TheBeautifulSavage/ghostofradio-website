When Frank Race pushes open the creaking door of the sprawling Ashworth mansion on a fog-shrouded evening, he finds far more than the missing heir he's been hired to locate. Behind the gothic facade and oil-painted portraits lies a family torn asunder by suspicion, greed, and dark secrets that someone would kill to keep buried. As Race navigates the labyrinthine corridors of wealth and deception, he uncovers a conspiracy that reaches into the very foundations of the estate—and discovers that the house's division runs deeper than mere architectural walls. With danger lurking around every corner and trust as rare as honesty in high society, our intrepid detective must use all his wits to expose the truth before another body turns cold.

The Adventures of Frank Race captured the imagination of post-war audiences with its perfect blend of hard-boiled detective work and genuine mystery. Airing during the final golden years of radio drama, the show showcased the talents of voice actors who could convey entire worlds through inflection and timing alone. "The House Divided" exemplifies why the series earned its loyal syndicated following—it's a masterclass in atmospheric storytelling, complete with authentic period details, expertly orchestrated sound effects, and a protagonist whose moral compass never wavers, even when the stakes become personal. The writing crackles with the cynicism and style that defined late-1940s adventure radio.
