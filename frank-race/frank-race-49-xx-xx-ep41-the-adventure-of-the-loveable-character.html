When Frank Race's plane touches down on the tarmac of a fog-shrouded Caribbean port, he finds himself drawn into a web of deception far more tangled than any he's encountered before. A seemingly harmless drifter—charming, quick-witted, and impossibly likeable—has insinuated himself into the lives of wealthy colonists, and Race suspects the man's disarming nature masks something far more sinister. As the mystery deepens through dimly-lit hotel corridors and shadowy waterfront encounters, listeners will find themselves questioning whether anyone can truly be trusted, no matter how warm their smile. The episode crackles with tension and intrigue, capturing that golden-age sensibility of moral ambiguity and the danger lurking beneath an affable exterior.

The Adventures of Frank Race emerged in 1949 as syndicated radio was experiencing its final golden years, just as television began its inexorable rise. The show's appeal lay in its sophisticated plotting and willingness to explore complex human motivations—a stark contrast to the straightforward heroics of earlier adventure serials. Frank Race himself became an archetype of the post-war adventurer: intelligent, world-weary, and deeply skeptical of surface appearances. This particular episode exemplifies the show's strength, presenting a protagonist who must navigate not through physical danger alone, but through the murky terrain of human psychology and deception.

Tune in to hear an adventure that reminds us why radio mystery thrived in an era before television's visual solutions could betray a character's true nature. In the dark of your living room, with only voices and sound effects to guide your imagination, Frank Race delivers an unforgettable journey into suspicion and intrigue.
