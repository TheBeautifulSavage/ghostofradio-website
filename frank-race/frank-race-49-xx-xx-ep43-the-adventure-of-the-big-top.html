Step right up, step right up—but watch your wallet and keep your wits about you, because danger lurks beneath the glittering canvas of Castellano's Traveling Circus. When a priceless emerald vanishes from the ringmaster's safe and a star performer plummets from the trapeze under suspicious circumstances, globe-trotting investigator Frank Race finds himself juggling murder, mystery, and mayhem under the big top. With only the evasive testimony of acrobats, clowns, and con artists to guide him, Race must navigate a labyrinth of secrets hidden behind painted smiles and sequined costumes. The stakes rise as each suspect proves more duplicitous than the last, and our hero discovers that in this world of illusion, nothing—and no one—is quite what they seem.

The Adventures of Frank Race captured the imagination of American listeners during radio's golden age, delivering the kind of globe-trotting thrills that made listeners forget their Depression-era worries and post-war anxieties. The show's syndicated run in 1949-1950 made it accessible across the nation, and episodes like "The Adventure of the Big Top" showcase why audiences found Race so compelling—a clever, resourceful detective with a knack for unmasking elaborate schemes and outsmarting criminals in exotic locales. The writing crackles with period authenticity, the sound effects create wonderfully tactile atmospheres, and the ensemble cast brings vivid life to every carnival barker and suspicious strongman.

Don't miss this thrilling installment as Frank Race puts on his detective's hat and steps into a world of sawdust, secrets, and sudden violence. Tune in and discover why this 1949 gem remains a testament to radio's unparalleled power to transport listeners into danger and intrigue—where mystery unfolds one expertly-crafted scene at a time.
