# Judge Allen's Cut Rate Court Incomplete

Step into the courtroom of Judge Allen's Cut Rate Court, where justice is served with a side of pandemonium and the proceedings are about to unravel in the most hilarious fashion imaginable. In this 1934 episode, Fred Allen presides over his court with characteristic deadpan authority, only to find himself utterly powerless as plaintiffs, defendants, and witnesses descend into chaos. The orchestra punctuates each absurd ruling with stabs of music, sound effects crackle and pop as evidence is presented—a broken alarm clock, a disputed laundry bill, perhaps a chicken that's wandered in off the street—and Allen's rapid-fire wisecracks fly faster than anyone can properly object. This incomplete surviving episode captures that precious moment when you can almost hear the studio audience losing control, their laughter rising above the carefully crafted gags, as Allen's sardonic commentary cuts through the mayhem like a verbal scalpel.

*The Fred Allen Show* revolutionized radio comedy by proving that intelligent, fast-paced wordplay and satirical sketches could captivate millions of listeners as effectively as sentimental drama or dance music. Allen himself was radio's supreme improviser and social commentator, whose show became a weekly appointment for nearly eighteen million Americans during its peak. The recurring Judge Allen's Court sketch became legendary, showcasing Allen's gift for constructing elaborate comic scenarios that felt spontaneous and dangerous—where anything might happen next. These episodes represent a golden age of radio when comedy relied on timing, linguistic precision, and the skill of a live ensemble performing before a studio audience, all transmitted invisibly into American living rooms.

Don't miss this incomparable slice of 1934 radio history. Tune in to experience Fred Allen at his improvisational peak, where laughter erupts organically from genuine human confusion and comedic brilliance collides with the beautiful unpredictability of live performance.
