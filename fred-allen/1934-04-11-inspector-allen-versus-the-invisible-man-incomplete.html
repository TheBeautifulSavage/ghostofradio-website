When an invisible criminal strikes in the dead of night, leaving only chaos and confusion in his wake, Inspector Allen must navigate a bewildering investigation where evidence vanishes as mysteriously as the culprit himself. The sketch spirals into delicious absurdity as Allen's deadpan reactions collide with the mounting impossibilities of apprehending a suspect no one can see. The audience's laughter erupts like fireworks, punctuating every clever quip and exasperated aside.

The Fred Allen Show represented the golden pinnacle of radio comedy, a weekly variety program that blended sketch comedy, music, and topical humor into an unmissable event. Allen's distinctive persona—equal parts sage, satirist, and self-deprecating everyman—set the template for generations of comedians to follow. His willingness to lampoon celebrities, bureaucrats, and the very medium broadcasting him created a refreshingly irreverent tone that audiences craved during the Depression era. This particular sketch showcases Allen at his finest, transforming a fantastic premise into commentary on the futility of law enforcement and the absurdity of modern life.
