# Once An Amateur, Always An Amateur

## The Fred Allen Show | December 8, 1935

Settle into your parlor chair and prepare yourself for an evening of uproarious comedy as Fred Allen takes the microphone for "Once An Amateur, Always An Amateur"—a rollicking installment that captures the vaudeville spirit at its most infectious. In this episode, Allen and his wife Portland Hoffa navigate the chaos of a local talent competition gone magnificently wrong, complete with earnest but hopelessly untalented performers, backstage mishaps, and Allen's razor-sharp commentary on American ambition. You can practically hear the nervous energy crackling through the airwaves as sketch follows sketch, each more absurd than the last, building to a crescendo of laughter punctuated by the tight precision of the studio orchestra. Allen's gift for rapid-fire wit and his ability to shift between deadpan delivery and manic energy create an atmosphere both intimate and grand—this is comedy for the entire family, yet sophisticated enough to delight discerning listeners.

By 1935, The Fred Allen Show had established itself as network radio's most intelligent variety program, a weekly showcase where genuine musical talent shared airtime with satirical sketches that slyly commented on contemporary American life. Unlike his competitors, Allen refused to rely on tired gags or sentimentality, instead crafting humor that rewarded listeners' intelligence while remaining universally entertaining. His genuine partnership with Portland Hoffa added warmth to his acerbic persona, and his stable of recurring characters—from the folksy to the absurd—gave the program a theatrical richness rare in broadcasting.

This December evening offers the perfect escape from the economic uncertainties of the Depression era, a reminder that laughter and cleverness were commodities no recession could diminish. Tune in and discover why millions of Americans made Allen's show an essential part of their weekly ritual—you won't find entertainment this satisfying anywhere else on your dial.
