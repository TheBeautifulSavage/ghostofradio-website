The Fred Allen Show NBC/CBS · 1941

The Quiz Nippers

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

Settle into your armchair and prepare for mayhem as Fred Allen takes on one of his most audacious episodes yet. When a group of precocious child contestants arrive at the studio for what should be a straightforward quiz program, the carefully laid plans of Allen and his cast dissolve into absolute pandemonium. Allen's razor-sharp wit clashes magnificently with the unpredictability of youth, creating moments of genuine hilarity punctuated by musical interludes and the unmistakable sound of studio audience laughter. As the "Quiz Nippers" bombard Fred with impossible questions and impudent answers, you'll hear the master comedian improvise his way through one crisis after another, his timing impeccable even as everything spirals wonderfully out of control. The episode crackles with the spontaneous energy that made live radio the most exciting medium of its era—there's a palpable sense that anything could happen at any moment.

By 1941, The Fred Allen Show had become one of radio's most celebrated programs, celebrated for Allen's intelligent humor and his willingness to mock both the medium itself and the commercial sponsors who bankrolled it. Unlike the gentler variety shows that dominated the airwaves, Allen's program was sophisticated and irreverent, appealing to listeners who appreciated satire alongside slapstick. "The Quiz Nippers" exemplifies this balance perfectly, combining the period's fascination with quiz shows (which would soon consume American radio) with Allen's signature comedic anarchy. His supporting cast, including the inimitable Portland Hoffa, adds layers of comedic brilliance throughout.

This is live radio at its finest—unpredictable, witty, and utterly captivating. Don't miss your chance to experience why Fred Allen was considered by many to be the greatest comic mind radio ever produced.
