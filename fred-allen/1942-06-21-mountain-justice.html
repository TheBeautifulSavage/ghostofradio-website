The Fred Allen Show NBC/CBS · 1942

Mountain Justice

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

As your radio crackles to life on this autumn evening, you're transported to the dusty backwoods of Appalachia, where frontier justice meets Fred Allen's razor-sharp wit. In "Mountain Justice," our quick-tongued comedian finds himself entangled in a backwoods feud, complete with ornery hillbillies, moonshine, and all the comedic chaos that only Allen could orchestrate. Expect rapid-fire puns, a parade of eccentric characters brought to life by Allen's unmatched cast of supporting players, and that signature blend of slapstick absurdity and clever wordplay that made Sunday nights unmissable for millions of Americans. The episode crackles with energy—you'll hear Allen's timing at its peak, the studio audience roaring with laughter, and sound effects that transport you directly into the heart of rural mayhem.

By 1942, The Fred Allen Show had become an American institution, a variety program that proved radio comedy could be sophisticated and wildly entertaining simultaneously. Allen, a vaudeville veteran with a gift for ad-libbing, created a fictional universe populated by recurring characters and places—like Senator Claghorn and Allen's Alley—that listeners cherished like old friends. During wartime, when Americans huddled around their radios for comfort and escape, Allen's irreverent humor provided both laughter and a subtle critique of authority. "Mountain Justice" exemplifies the show's fearless approach to comedy, blending topical satire with timeless character work.

Step back nearly eighty years and experience the golden age of radio comedy. Fred Allen's gift for language, his impeccable comedic timing, and his ability to create entire worlds in the listener's imagination remain undiminished by time. Tune in to "Mountain Justice" and discover why millions gathered faithfully each week—this is broadcast entertainment at its finest.
