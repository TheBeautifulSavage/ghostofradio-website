Step back into a November evening in 1940, when static crackles to life and the unmistakable police siren wails across your living room speaker. Tonight's installment picks up the manhunt for Dwight Beard with the tension wound tight as a clock spring—the fugitive is still at large, the leads are growing cold, and desperation hangs heavy in the air. You'll hear the authentic voices of real lawmen, the clack of typewriters in squad rooms, the screech of tires on wet streets. This is the second half of Beard's story, where the investigation tightens like a noose and every phone call, every tip, every stakeout could be the break that cracks the case wide open. The writing crackles with the urgency of a real police procedural, dragging listeners deeper into the machinery of American justice.

Gang Busters stood apart as radio's most meticulously researched crime drama, drawing directly from the case files of the FBI, local police departments, and district attorneys across the nation. Created by Phillips H. Lord, the show earned its reputation by broadcasting actual unsolved cases and real criminal methodology—cases that often remained open, inviting listeners themselves to contact the authorities if they recognized a suspect. By 1940, the show had already become a cultural phenomenon, simultaneously entertaining millions while serving as an authentic window into law enforcement work. The Beard case represents the show at its documentary peak, balancing genuine investigative procedure with the dramatic storytelling that made listeners feel like junior G-men themselves.

Don't miss this concluding chapter of a case that gripped the nation. Tune in and discover whether law and order prevail—or if Dwight Beard slips through their fingers once more.
