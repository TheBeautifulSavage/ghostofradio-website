# The Case of the Wolverine - Gang Busters

As the familiar siren wails and the machine-gun staccato of police teletype clatter fills your parlor on this autumn evening in 1942, you're pulled directly into the headquarters of the Federal Bureau of Investigation. Tonight's case concerns a criminal so elusive, so cunning, that law enforcement from three states has joined forces to bring him to justice. "The Wolverine"—a nickname earned through his vicious attacks and predatory nature—has left a trail of terror across the Midwest, and the clock is ticking. Expect rapid-fire dialogue, authentic police procedure, and the kind of mounting tension that had millions of Americans huddled around their radio sets, hanging on every word as real detectives pursued a real menace.

*Gang Busters* stood apart from mere entertainment in 1942; it was a civic institution, a weekly bulletin from the front lines of American law enforcement. With full cooperation from J. Edgar Hoover's FBI and local police departments, creator Phillips H. Lord crafted episodes ripped directly from case files, transforming actual crimes into gripping radio drama. During wartime, when Americans sought both reassurance and engagement with their nation's institutions, *Gang Busters* delivered both—proof that order and justice could prevail even as darkness threatened from abroad. The show's influence was so profound that it actually aided real law enforcement; wanted criminals were sometimes captured after radio appeals reached the public ear.

Settle in with the lights dimmed low and prepare yourself for authentic crime-fighting drama as only *Gang Busters* could deliver it. This is more than entertainment—it's a window into 1942 America, where ordinary citizens became vigilant partners with federal agents in the endless war against crime. Tune in tonight for *The Case of the Wolverine*.
