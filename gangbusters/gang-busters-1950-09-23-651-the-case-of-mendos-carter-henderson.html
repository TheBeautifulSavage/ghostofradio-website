On the evening of September 23rd, 1950, listeners across America tuned their dials to Gang Busters expecting another thrilling installment of crime-fighting justice—and they were not disappointed. This episode plunges headlong into a complex case of criminal conspiracy, where the line between petty crooks and dangerous masterminds blurs dangerously. As Detective-Sergeant Ed Farley narrates the proceedings with his characteristic gravitas, the drama unfolds through crackling police frequencies, urgent footsteps, and the unmistakable tension of men closing in on their quarry. The case of Mendos, Carter, and Henderson is no simple heist; it's a tangled web of deception, betrayal, and desperation that forces law enforcement to think several moves ahead of the criminals themselves. Every clue matters. Every moment counts.

Gang Busters had established itself as America's premier true crime radio drama by 1950, drawing directly from actual case files submitted by law enforcement agencies across the nation. Unlike the fictional detectives who dominated the airwaves, this show prided itself on authenticity and educational value—listeners weren't just being entertained; they were witnessing real criminal methodology and real investigative technique. The show's partnership with police departments nationwide gave it unprecedented access and credibility, making each episode feel urgent and immediate. By this point in the show's fourteen-year run, it had become required listening for anyone interested in understanding the cat-and-mouse games played between criminals and the men sworn to stop them.

Don't miss this gripping account of law enforcement at work. Tune in and discover how three criminals' schemes unraveled against the methodical precision of modern police work. Gang Busters brings you the facts—nothing embellished, nothing hidden.
