# The Great Gildersleeve - May 16, 1941

Step into the parlor of Throckmorton P. Gildersleeve on this enchanting spring evening as the genial bachelor prepares for the most consequential moment of his life—an audition that could change everything. The nervous energy crackles through every scene as our hero, that portly pillar of Summerfield propriety, rehearses his lines with the fretful intensity of a man who knows his dreams hang in the balance. Will his carefully cultivated charm and impeccable diction win over the judges? Listen as Gildersleeve fumbles through his preparations, his household in delightful chaos, while his niece Marjorie and nephew Leroy offer their own brand of dubious encouragement. The comedy flows thick as molasses—expect pratfalls, misunderstandings, and the particular brand of genteel humor that made families gather around their radio sets in rapt attention.

This audition episode represents a pivotal moment in broadcasting history, marking the character's leap from supporting player on *The Fred Allen Show* to headliner of his own series, a transition that would ultimately span sixteen glorious years. Hal Peary's creation would become a masterpiece of American radio comedy, proving that audiences hungered for the exploits of a well-meaning, somewhat self-important small-town pillar of society. The show's success established the template for domestic sitcoms that would later dominate television.

For modern listeners, this episode offers a rare glimpse into the audition process that launched a thousand broadcasts, capturing the hopeful energy and live-wire spontaneity that defined radio's golden age.
