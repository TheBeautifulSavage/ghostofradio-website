# The Great Gildersleeve: Fortune Teller

Step into the mysterious parlor of Madame Zara as Throckmorton P. Gildersleeve stumbles into an evening of comic chaos and supernatural shenanigans. In this delightfully absurd installment, our mustachioed hero becomes entangled with a fortune teller whose predictions prove far more troublesome than enlightening. What begins as innocent curiosity about his romantic prospects spirals into a series of increasingly ridiculous misadventures, complete with the kind of slapstick confusion that made audiences roar with laughter. Harold Peary's impeccable comedic timing transforms Gildy's mounting panic into pure gold, while the supporting cast weaves an intricate web of misunderstandings that only a master of situation comedy could untangle—or hopelessly complicate further.

The Great Gildersleeve represented a revolutionary shift in radio comedy during the 1940s, moving away from simple vaudeville-style humor toward character-driven narratives set in the fictional small town of Summerfield. Gildy himself—a wealthy, self-important bachelor perpetually entangled with his niece and nephew—became America's beloved everyman buffoon, his exaggerated sophistication forever undone by circumstance and his own bumbling nature. This episode exemplifies the show's golden formula: a premise ripe for misinterpretation, a cast of colorful locals ready to compound the confusion, and one man's futile attempts to maintain dignity in an increasingly bewildering situation.

If you've never experienced the madcap brilliance of classic radio comedy, this is your invitation. Settle in, let your imagination paint the scene, and discover why millions of Americans gathered around their sets week after week. The Great Gildersleeve awaits—and Madame Zara's fortunes are far more amusing than you could possibly predict.
