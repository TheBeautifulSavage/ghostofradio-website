# The Great Gildersleeve: "Who's Dating Who"

Step into the parlor of Throckmorton P. Gildersleeve on this delightful winter evening as romantic entanglements threaten to upend the social calendar of Summerfield. When word spreads about who's courting whom, the town's most eligible and most meddlesome bachelor finds himself caught in the crossfire of gossip, misunderstandings, and comic mishaps. Harold Peary's masterful vocal performance captures every shade of bewilderment and exasperation as the great man himself must navigate the treacherous waters of small-town romance—everyone else's, naturally. Expect the crisp sound design that transports you straight to the Gildersleeve mansion, where telephones ring at inopportune moments, doors slam with perfect comedic timing, and the supporting cast delivers rapid-fire banter that keeps the laughter rolling throughout this 1940s gem.

The Great Gildersleeve stands as one of radio's most enduring comedies, a show that captured the essence of American small-town life during an era when the radio was the nation's hearth. Premiering in 1941, this NBC mainstay became the first spinoff in radio history, plucking the title character from *Fibber McGee and Molly* to create his own universe. The show's genius lay in its character-driven humor—Gildersleeve's pompous yet oddly loveable nature, his complicated guardianship of nephew Marlin and niece Leroy, and the parade of colorful townspeople all contributed to its fifteen-year run. By the 1940s, when this episode aired, the formula had perfected itself into pure comedic gold.

Settle in and discover why America tuned in faithfully each week to hear what ridiculous predicament the Great Gildersleeve would find himself in next. This episode is a perfect entry point into the warm, witty world of 1940s radio comedy.
