# The Great Gildersleeve: Aunt Hattie Interferes

Step into Summerfield on a winter's evening, where the crackling warmth of your radio brings forth one of broadcasting's most beloved voices—that of Throckmorton P. Gildersleeve himself. In this delightful installment, "Aunt Hattie Interferes," our titular character finds himself caught between a rock and a hard place when his formidable aunt descends upon the household with her own ideas about how things ought to be run. What begins as a seemingly innocent visit spirals into comedic chaos as Aunt Hattie's well-intentioned meddling throws the orderly world of the Great Gildersleeve into delightful disarray. Listeners will find themselves transported to a world of misunderstandings, witty repartee, and that peculiar brand of American family comedy that defined the golden age of radio—where relationships are tested, pride is humorously deflated, and everything is resolved in time for the closing theme.

Harold Peary's creation stands as a masterpiece of character-driven comedy, having evolved from a supporting role on *The Fibber McGee and Molly Show* into its own wildly popular program. The Great Gildersleeve became NBC's answer to the domestic comedy craze, running for sixteen years and captivating millions of Americans who tuned in weekly to follow the exploits of this small-town Lothario and his misadventures. The show's genius lay in its perfect balance of satirical social commentary wrapped in genuine warmth, offering audiences both laughter and genuine affection for its characters.

Don't miss this opportunity to experience radio entertainment at its finest. Settle into your favorite chair, adjust the dial, and prepare yourself for an evening of laughter and charm that only the Golden Age of Radio could deliver. The Great Gildersleeve awaits.
