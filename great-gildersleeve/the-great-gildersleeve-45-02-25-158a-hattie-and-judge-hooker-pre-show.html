# The Great Gildersleeve: Hattie and Judge Hooker

Settle in by the radio for an evening of romantic scheming and small-town intrigue as Throckmorton P. Gildersleeve finds himself caught between two formidable personalities. When the sharp-tongued Hattie Harkins sets her sights on the esteemed Judge Hooker, our beloved protagonist must navigate the treacherous waters of matchmaking with all the charm and mishap his considerable talents can muster. The result is pure comedy gold—complete with misunderstandings, slapstick situations, and the kind of witty banter that made America fall in love with radio in the first place. As Gildersleeve schemes to bring these two unlikely prospects together, listeners will find themselves laughing at the parade of comedic disasters that inevitably unfold in the quirky town of Summerfield.

The Great Gildersleeve stands as one of radio's most enduring and beloved comedies, having already established itself as a cultural phenomenon by the early 1940s. Born from the supporting character of the same name in *The Fred Allen Show*, Gildersleeve became so popular that he earned his own program—a rare achievement in broadcasting history. This particular episode showcases the show's signature blend of physical comedy adapted brilliantly for audio, character-driven humor, and gentle satire of American small-town life. Hal Peary's masterful performance in the title role brings unparalleled energy and timing to every scene, while the supporting cast creates a living, breathing universe of eccentric townspeople.

If you've never experienced the magic of Gildersleeve and his Summerfield adventures, this episode offers the perfect entry point into a world where every day promises unexpected comedy and genuine warmth. Tune in and discover why audiences tuned their dials to this show week after week for over fifteen years.
