# The Great Gildersleeve: Leroy Wants A Motor Scooter

Step into Summerfield on this winter evening as Throckmorton P. Gildersleeve faces his greatest domestic challenge yet—the relentless pestering of his ward Leroy, who has become absolutely determined to own a motor scooter. What begins as innocent boyish enthusiasm quickly spirals into comic chaos as the guileless Gildersleeve attempts to manage his nephew's desires while navigating the treacherous waters of parental responsibility. Will the great man cave to Leroy's increasingly creative appeals? Can Aunt Margie and the rest of Summerfield's colorful cast keep the household from descending into complete mayhem? Expect the kind of gentle domestic comedy that had families gathering around their sets in living rooms across America—where the real humor lies not in zingers and punchlines, but in the universal truths of family life rendered absurd and endearing.

Since its debut on NBC in 1941, *The Great Gildersleeve* became America's beloved portrait of small-town life, with Harold Peary's magnificent baritone voice bringing Gildersleeve to vivid life week after week. The show's genius lay in its warmth; here was no curmudgeon or tyrant, but a genuinely well-meaning bachelor trying his bumbling best to raise a nephew and niece while serving as water commissioner. Each episode offered listeners a comforting escape into a timeless world where problems could be solved through good humor and neighborly decency.

If you long for an America that existed partly in fact and partly in the golden dreams of radio writers, this is your show. Tune in and let Gildersleeve's booming laugh and Leroy's persistent whining transport you back to an era when families made time for comedy that celebrated human kindness over cynicism.
