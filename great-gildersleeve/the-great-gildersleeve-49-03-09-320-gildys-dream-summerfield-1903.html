# The Great Gildersleeve: Gildy's Dream Summerfield 1903

Step into the parlor with Throckmorton P. Gildersleeve as he drifts into a peculiar slumber, only to awaken in the Summerfield of forty years past. In this whimsical journey through time, listeners will discover a younger, more idealistic version of their beloved curmudgeon navigating the turn-of-the-century world he once knew. What unfolds is both hilarious and strangely poignant—Gildy's encounters with old acquaintances, the quaint customs of 1903, and his own youthful misadventures create a tapestry of comedy laced with genuine nostalgia. The writing crackles with clever wordplay and physical humor perfectly suited to the radio format, while the supporting cast brings the ghost-town of memories vividly to life. You'll find yourself chuckling at Gildy's fumbling attempts to navigate a world he thought he understood, only to discover how much he's forgotten—and how much he's gained.

*The Great Gildersleeve* stands as one of radio's most enduring comedies, having spun off from *Fibber McGee and Molly* to become NBC's flagship sitcom. Hal Peary's volcanic vocal performance and impeccable timing made Gildy a household name throughout the 1940s and '50s, while the show's sharp scripts and ensemble cast created something revolutionary for the medium—a character-driven comedy that could mine both slapstick and subtle emotional beats from the simple act of listening. This particular episode showcases the show's willingness to experiment with narrative structure and sentiment, proving that radio comedy could be both frivolously entertaining and surprisingly reflective.

Tune in for a broadcast that captures the magic of radio's golden age: expertly crafted storytelling, stellar performances, and a premise that will transport you as surely as it transports Gildy himself.
