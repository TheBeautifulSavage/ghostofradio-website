# The Great Gildersleeve: "Gildy Considers Marriage"

Picture yourself settling into your favorite chair on a March evening in 1950, the warm glow of your radio dial beckoning you into the prosperous world of Summerfield. Tonight, the incomparable Throckmorton P. Gildersleeve faces his most consequential dilemma yet—matrimony. As this delightfully rotund bachelor contemplates abandoning his cherished bachelorhood, the stage is set for comedy both hilarious and surprisingly tender. Will Gildy take the plunge, or will his legendary self-interest and comic misadventures once again save him from domesticity? Listeners will treasure every stumble, every romantic miscalculation, and every perfectly timed aside as America's favorite fumbling romantic navigates the treacherous waters of courtship with his characteristic blend of pomposity and pratfall.

The Great Gildersleeve stands as a remarkable achievement in radio comedy—a show that transcended its origins as a supporting character on "Fibber McGee and Molly" to become its own phenomenon, running sixteen glorious years on NBC. This episode arrives during the show's golden age, when Hal Peary's voice had become synonymous with the character's sophisticated yet perpetually befuddled charm. What makes Gildersleeve endure is his fundamental humanity beneath the bombast; he's a man of genuine affection struggling against his own nature, a portrait of American middle-class aspiration rendered in comedy that never punches down.

If you've never experienced the infectious warmth of this classic program, "Gildy Considers Marriage" offers the perfect entry point—a showcase of everything that made radio's golden age truly golden. Tune in for an evening of laughter that crackles with authenticity, wit, and the unmistakable warmth of a simpler era.
