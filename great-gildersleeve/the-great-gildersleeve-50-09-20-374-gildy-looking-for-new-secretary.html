# The Great Gildersleeve: "Gildy Looking For New Secretary"

Step into the mahogany-paneled offices of Summerfield as the Great Gildersleeve embarks on one of his most comedically fraught endeavors—finding a replacement secretary. What could possibly go wrong? Everything, naturally. Listeners will delight as Harold Peary's magnificent baritone navigates the minefield of interviews, misunderstandings, and matrimonial misadventures that invariably follow in his wake. From the moment the Help Wanted sign goes up, chaos erupts in the most delightfully predictable way: unsuitable candidates parade through his office, each more bewildering than the last, while the surrounding cast of Summerfield's finest react with their trademark exasperation and barely-concealed amusement. The chemistry between Peary and his supporting players crackles with the kind of spontaneous wit that made this show an institution in American living rooms.

By 1950, when this episode aired, *The Great Gildersleeve* had already become one of radio's most beloved fixtures, spawning from its origins as a spin-off character on *The Fred Allen Show*. What sets this program apart is its perfect balance of broad physical comedy—translated ingeniously into sound effects and vocal performance—with genuine warmth and character development. Gildersleeve, that boisterous bachelor with his prominent nose for trouble, had evolved into something more than a mere buffoon; he was a relatable everyman, perpetually at odds with propriety yet fundamentally good-hearted.

For fans of vintage comedy and those discovering the golden age of radio for the first time, this episode exemplifies why millions tuned in faithfully each week. It's pure escapism wrapped in laughter, a perfect twenty-three minutes of entertainment that demands nothing but your attention and rewards it generously.
