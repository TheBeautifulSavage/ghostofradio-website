Settle into your favorite chair as Throckmorton P. Gildersleeve faces his greatest challenge yet—becoming an uncle to not one, but two newborns! When word arrives that his niece Marjorie has delivered twins, the good-natured, bumbling guardian of Summerfield throws the entire household into delightful chaos. Will he rise to the occasion with characteristic bluster and good humor, or will the crying, the diapers, and the sheer responsibility reduce him to flustered desperation? Expect the unmistakable warmth of Harold Peary's distinctive voice cracking with paternal pride and comic panic in equal measure, as the supporting cast—including the ever-present Birdie Winslow and the household staff—join in the mayhem with perfectly timed reactions and ad-libbed exchanges that capture the genuine affection beneath the show's comedic surface.

This episode arrives during the show's golden age, when The Great Gildersleeve had already become one of radio's most beloved fixtures. Spun off from the enormously popular Fibber McGee and Molly, Gildersleeve proved the character so captivating that he commanded his own program by 1941. The show's particular genius lay in balancing broad slapstick humor with genuine sentiment—Gildersleeve's exaggerated Southern belle mannerisms and ridiculous predicaments never overshadowed his authentic love for his family and community. These domestic episodes, focusing on life's milestones, showcased exactly why audiences tuned in faithfully week after week.
