# The Great Gildersleeve: Bullard's Boat Date

Picture this: it's a warm spring evening in 1941, and Throckmorton Gildersleeve is about to embark on what promises to be the most calamitous nautical adventure Summerfield has ever witnessed. When the pompous and perpetually scheming Judge Bullard extends an invitation for a boat outing, our bumbling hero sees only romance and adventure on the horizon—blissfully unaware that disaster lurks in every wake. As the engine sputters to life and the amateur sailors push away from the dock, listeners are treated to that unmistakable blend of slapstick mishap and verbal comedy that made this show an absolute phenomenon. The creaking of the vessel, the frantic dialogue, and the inevitable catastrophes unfold with the precision of a Swiss timepiece—or rather, the chaos of a jalopy with three blown tires.

The Great Gildersleeve represents something remarkable in American broadcasting: the successful spinoff that eclipsed its parent show. Born from *Fibber McGee and Molly*, Gildersleeve's booming voice, malapropisms, and irresistible charm captured audiences so thoroughly that NBC gave him his own program. By the early 1940s, he had become must-listen radio, a character so vivid that listeners felt they knew him personally. This episode showcases why—it's pure comedic gold, written for an audience that understood the rhythms of small-town life and craved that perfect balance of heart and hilarity.

So dim the lights, settle into your favorite chair, and prepare yourself for an evening of mayhem and merriment. *The Great Gildersleeve* awaits, ready to transport you back to an era when comedy came through the airwaves and fired the imagination. Tune in to hear how Gildersleeve navigates the treacherous waters of Judge Bullard's boat—you won't want to miss a single, glorious moment.
