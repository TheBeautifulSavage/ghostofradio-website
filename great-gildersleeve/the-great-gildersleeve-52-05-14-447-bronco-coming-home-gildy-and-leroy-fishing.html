# The Great Gildersleeve: "Bronco Coming Home / Gildy and Leroy Fishing"

Settle into your favorite chair as Throckmorton P. Gildersleeve faces double trouble on this delightful spring evening. With Bronco the horse making an unexpected homecoming, chaos erupts throughout the Gildersleeve estate—and that's before Gildy and his young charge Leroy head down to the fishing hole for what promises to be anything but a peaceful afternoon. Hal Peary's masterful comedic timing carries listeners through a whirlwind of misadventures, from slapstick barnyard antics to the innocent wisdom that emerges when a guardian and his ward share a quiet moment by the water. The sound effects team conjures up everything from the clip-clop of hooves to the gentle splash of fishing lines hitting the stream, transporting listeners directly to this small-town American haven where every day brings fresh laughter and unexpected heart.

*The Great Gildersleeve* stands as one of radio's most beloved comedies, born from the character's popularity on *The Fred Allen Show* before striking out to build his own empire. By the 1940s, audiences couldn't get enough of the rotund, verbose bachelor who ruled his Summerfield estate with infectious good humor, managing a household of colorful characters while dispensing dubious wisdom to anyone within earshot. The show's genius lay in balancing broad comedy with genuine warmth—Gildy's relationship with Leroy transcended typical sitcom formula, offering viewers touching moments alongside the pratfalls.

Join millions of listeners who tuned in faithfully to hear how Gildy would bumble his way through the day's crises, always landing on his feet through sheer force of personality and dumb luck. This episode exemplifies everything that made *The Great Gildersleeve* essential American entertainment.
