# The Great Gildersleeve: Katie Lee Visits Summerfield

Step into the parlor of Throckmorton P. Gildersleeve's stately home as the charming Katie Lee arrives in Summerfield for an unexpected visit, setting the stage for a delightful comedy of manners and romantic mishaps. Hal Peary's magnificently bombastic baritone carries us through a day of social upheaval as the great man himself scrambles to maintain his carefully cultivated reputation as the town's most eligible bachelor and pillar of respectability. What begins as a cordial afternoon of hospitality devolves into the kind of farcical entanglement that made this show a Tuesday night institution—misunderstandings multiply like rabbits, the telephone becomes an instrument of comic chaos, and poor Gildersleeve finds himself caught between his vanity and his conscience, desperately attempting to juggle multiple social obligations while preserving his dignity.

Spun off from *Fibber McGee and Molly* in 1941, *The Great Gildersleeve* became one of radio's most enduring comedies, a sophisticated urban fantasy set in the fictional small town of Summerfield where everyone knew everyone else's business—and cared deeply about their own reputations. Peary's creation was the perfect American archetype: a man of means and pretension, forever trying to impress the ladies and the townsfolk while constantly thwarted by circumstance, his own schemes, and an impeccably timed supporting cast. The show thrived on rapid-fire dialogue, physical comedy translated brilliantly to audio, and the kind of wholesome yet subtly knowing humor that appealed equally to children and their parents.

Settle in with this timeless episode and discover why America tuned in faithfully for sixteen glorious years of Gildersleeve's romantic fumbles and social triumphs. You'll understand immediately why this charming charlatan became a beloved fixture in millions of homes, one perfectly-timed comedic beat at a time.
