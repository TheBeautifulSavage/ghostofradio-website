Step into the comfortable chaos of Summerfield as Throckmorton P. Gildersleeve finds himself in another predicament of his own making—this time involving a mysterious timepiece and the kind of trouble that only the Great Gildersleeve can stumble into with such spectacular flair. What begins as an innocent matter quickly spirals into a comedy of errors filled with mistaken identities, escalating misunderstandings, and the perfectly timed entrance of his perpetually exasperated nephew Leroy and the indispensable Birdie Forrest. Hal Peary's masterful comedic timing carries listeners through every twist and turn, his trademark giggle punctuating the mayhem at precisely the right moments. The supporting cast delivers their lines with the crisp professionalism that made radio comedy an art form, and the sound effects team conjures the bustling world of small-town America with remarkable authenticity.

The Great Gildersleeve represents the golden age of radio comedy at its finest—a show that transcended its origins as a Fibber McGee and Molly spinoff to become NBC's flagship comedy program. By the 1940s, Gildersleeve had become a cultural phenomenon, with audiences tuning in weekly to follow the misadventures of this insufferable yet oddly endearing Southern gentleman. The show's brilliance lay in its balance between physical comedy translated through sound and clever writing that rewarded attentive listeners with layers of humor.

This episode captures everything that made the program a beloved fixture in American living rooms: warmth, wit, and the gentle reminder that even the most troublesome among us are usually just trying their best. Whether you're a devoted fan of classic radio or discovering the Great Gildersleeve for the first time, Watch Trouble offers the perfect opportunity to experience radio comedy the way millions experienced it—live, immediate, and utterly delightful.
