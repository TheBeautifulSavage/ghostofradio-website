When Throckmorton P. Gildersleeve decides to take on the responsibility of caring for a massive Great Dane, he sets in motion a chain of events that will test the patience of every soul in Summerfield—most notably the long-suffering Peavey. What begins as an innocent gesture of affection quickly spirals into comedic chaos as the enormous hound becomes an unstoppable force of mischief, destruction, and hilariously inconvenient timing. Listeners can expect the kind of physical comedy that only radio can conjure through masterful sound effects and perfectly timed dialogue, as furniture crashes, pedestrians scatter, and poor Peavey finds himself perpetually in the wrong place at the wrong time. The warmth and exasperation in the ensemble cast's voices captures the authentic friction between well-meaning citizens and their own best-laid plans gone awry.

The Great Gildersleeve stood as one of radio's most beloved comedies during its sixteen-year run, and this episode exemplifies why. Broadcasting from NBC's studios, the show thrived on the chemistry between Hal Peary's irrepressible Gildy and a supporting cast of richly drawn characters whose complaints, reactions, and schemes formed the backbone of Summerfield's small-town charm. By the mid-1940s, Gildersleeve had become an American institution—a voice that families gathered around, offering escape from wartime worries through the timeless trials of neighborly feuds and domestic disasters.

Tune in to experience radio comedy at its finest, where a single Great Dane can unravel an entire community's peace of mind, and where the simple act of pet ownership becomes the stuff of legend. This is the golden age of radio entertainment, preserved forever in sound and memory.
