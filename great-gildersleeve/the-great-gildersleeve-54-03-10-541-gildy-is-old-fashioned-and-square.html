# The Great Gildersleeve: "Gildy Is Old Fashioned And Square"

Picture this: it's a crisp October evening in 1941, and across America, families are gathering around their radios as Throckmorton P. Gildersleeve—the irrepressible town treasurer of Summerfield—finds himself the target of good-natured ribbing. In this delightful comedy, the rotund and perpetually befuddled Gildy discovers that the younger generation considers him hopelessly out of touch, a relic of yesterday's values in a rapidly modernizing world. Watch as his comic indignation builds, his voice rising in those familiar, exasperated tones while his nephew Leroy and the other youngsters tease him mercilessly about his old-fashioned ways. The situation spirals wonderfully from there, with misunderstandings piling upon misunderstandings, creating the kind of wholesome mayhem that made this show a national treasure.

*The Great Gildersleeve* represented something remarkable in American broadcasting—a spin-off that transcended its parent show, *Fibber McGee and Molly*, to become a phenomenon in its own right. With Harold Peary's masterful vocal performance bringing Gildy to life, the program struck gold by centering an entire universe around this single character: his romantic mishaps, his financial schemes, his conflicts with the school board, and his fumbling attempts to navigate a world that seemed intent on proving him irrelevant. The show's gentle humor and relatable small-town setting made it essential listening for millions during the 1940s and 1950s.

Whether you're a longtime devotee of classic radio or discovering the wonder of old-time comedy for the first time, this episode perfectly captures why *The Great Gildersleeve* endured for sixteen glorious years. Tune in and let Harold Peary's incomparable timing and comedic genius transport you to Summerfield—where confusion is always just around the corner!
