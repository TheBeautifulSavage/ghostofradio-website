# The Great Gildersleeve: Gildy Causes Water Outage

Picture this: it's a crisp evening in early March, 1957, and America's favorite bachelor is about to create absolute chaos in the quiet town of Summerfield. When Throckmorton P. Gildersleeve decides to tinker with something he shouldn't—in this case, the municipal water system—the results are predictably catastrophic and hilariously relatable. Listeners will delight as the pompous, well-meaning Gildy stumbles from one disaster to the next, leaving the entire town's water supply in jeopardy. Whether he's trying to impress someone, save money, or simply demonstrate his superior intellect, his schemes inevitably backfire in ways that showcase why audiences loved this character for nearly two decades. Expect exasperated reactions from his niece Marjorie, long-suffering patience from Leroy, and the kind of physical comedy that only radio's sound effects team could deliver with perfect comic timing.

The Great Gildersleeve holds a unique place in broadcasting history as one of radio's most successful spinoff shows, having originated from *Fibber McGee and Molly* in 1941. Harold Peary's voice work brought Gildy to vivid life—a character so popular he sustained a sixteen-year run with a devoted national audience. This episode exemplifies what made the show endure: gentle, intelligent humor grounded in small-town American life, where ordinary situations spiral into extraordinary predicaments through human folly and misunderstanding. The show's writers understood perfectly the comedy inherent in a man of inflated self-regard meeting the immovable object of reality.

If you've never experienced the magic of Gildersleeve's particular brand of mayhem, or if you're a devoted fan wanting to revisit a classic installment, this episode captures the essence of golden-age radio comedy. Tune in and discover why millions tuned in each week for the misadventures of Throckmorton P. Gildersleeve.
