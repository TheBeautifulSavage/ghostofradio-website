EPISODE SUMMARY:

"The Green Hornet: Justice Wears A Blindfold" (June 15, 1939) is a gripping episode that masterfully weaves together themes of justice, deception, and loyalty. The story centers around the Hornet's (Bruce Thomas) investigation into a series of mysterious thefts targeting wealthy socialites in Los Angeles. As he delves deeper into the case, Kato (Ken Cobb) begins to suspect that one of their closest allies may be behind the sinister plot. Meanwhile, the villainous Black Mask orchestrates chaos and destruction throughout the city, leaving a trail of clues that only the Hornet's keen mind can decipher.

The atmosphere in this episode is tense and foreboding, with an undercurrent of suspicion and mistrust among the characters. The Green Hornet's unwavering dedication to justice is put to the test as he navigates the complex web of deceit spun by Black Mask. From the eerie sound effects to the masterful dialogue, every element in this episode works together to create a sense of urgency and danger that propels the listener forward.

As the Hornet inches closer to uncovering the truth, the stakes grow higher, and the outcome is far from certain. Will justice prevail, or will Black Mask's cunning plans succeed? This episode expertly balances action, suspense, and wit, making it an unforgettable listen for fans of old-time radio.

June 15, 1939, marked a tumultuous time in America. Radio dramas like "The Green Hornet" provided a thrilling distraction for listeners, who could immerse themselves in exciting stories while staying safe in their own homes.

By doing so, these dramas helped shape public opinion and contributed to the growing desire for change in America.
