"The Second Choice" is a haunting episode of Gunsmoke, with an atmosphere as unforgiving as the Kansas prairie itself. Marshal Matt Dillon and his deputies are dealing with a particularly intractable case: the disappearance of a young farmhand who has left behind only a cryptic note hinting at a tragic love affair gone wrong. As the investigation unfolds, tensions run high between the townspeople, each with their own theories about what might have happened to the missing boy. Meanwhile, the character of Kitty Russell continues to simmer as a ticking time bomb. In 1955, the world was still reeling from the aftermath of World War II. At home, Americans were grappling with the social upheaval of post-war prosperity: racial tensions were on the rise, while women's liberation was gaining momentum.

As the United States struggled to find its footing in a rapidly changing world, people turned to familiar sources of comfort – like Gunsmoke. This Western drama taprooted into the collective psyche of a nation still reeling from the trauma of war and economic hardship, offering a fleeting respite from the anxieties that plagued everyday life.
