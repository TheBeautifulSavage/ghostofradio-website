# Gunsmoke 56 06 24 - "Sunday Supplement"

When Marshal Matt Dillon strides into the Long Branch Saloon on this June evening, trouble wears an unexpected face—that of a traveling newspaper correspondent whose innocent questions threaten to unravel the delicate order that keeps Dodge City civil. As William Conrad's gravelly voice guides us through the dusty streets of the frontier, we're drawn into a tale where the pen proves mightier than the six-shooter, and where the marshal must navigate not bullets and outlaws, but the slippery terrain of public opinion and journalistic ambition. The supporting cast of Chester, Festus, and Miss Kitty weave their way through a narrative that crackles with the particular tension of a small town facing scrutiny from the outside world. You'll hear the authentic saloon ambiance—the clink of glasses, the murmur of conversation—as the episode builds toward a climactic confrontation that questions what's worth protecting: a town's reputation or its truth.

*Gunsmoke* stands as one of radio's most enduring dramas, having evolved from a simple western into a nuanced examination of frontier justice and human nature. By 1956, the show had perfected the art of blending action with character study, and this particular episode exemplifies why it captivated millions of listeners across America. The writers understood that the real conflicts of the Old West weren't always shootouts—they were moral quandaries that required wisdom as much as courage.

Step back in time and experience the golden age of radio drama. "Sunday Supplement" awaits, ready to remind you why families gathered around their sets, hanging on every word of Marshal Dillon's steady voice. Tune in and discover what made *Gunsmoke* an American classic.
