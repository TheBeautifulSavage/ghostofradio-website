# Gunsmoke: Passive Resistance

When Marshal Matt Dillon faces a crisis that won't yield to gunplay or lawman's authority, Dodge City itself becomes a powder keg of tension in this riveting episode of *Gunsmoke*. In "Passive Resistance," a principled stranger arrives in town preaching nonviolence and civil disobedience, challenging the very foundation of frontier justice that Dillon has built his career upon. As the marshal watches his authority questioned and violence threatening to erupt from unexpected quarters, he must grapple with a mystery more troubling than any outlaw standoff: how do you maintain order when someone refuses to fight back? The episode crackles with philosophical debate wrapped in authentic western tension, as William Conrad's narration guides us through a Dodge City where the usual rules of law and order become dangerously inadequate.

*Gunsmoke* revolutionized radio drama by treating the western genre with literary sophistication, moving far beyond simple good-versus-evil shootouts. Running from 1952 through the early 1960s, the show became CBS's longest-running dramatic program, captivating millions with its morally complex storytelling and the weathered authenticity that Conrad brought to Matt Dillon. Episodes like "Passive Resistance" showcased the program's remarkable ability to explore contemporary social questions—in this case, the nature of resistance and authority—while remaining firmly rooted in the American West's dust and danger.

Step into the Long Branch Saloon and the dusty streets of Dodge City for an evening of masterful radio theater. *Gunsmoke* demands your full attention and rewards it generously with compelling characters, taut writing, and drama that unfolds in your imagination more vividly than any television could manage. Tune in and discover why millions of listeners made this their appointment with adventure.
