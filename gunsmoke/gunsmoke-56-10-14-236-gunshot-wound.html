When a stranger stumbles into Dodge City bleeding from a mysterious gunshot wound, Matt Dillon must unravel a tangled web of deception, desperation, and frontier justice. As the wounded man drifts in and out of consciousness in Doc Adams' clinic, each fevered word he utters raises more questions than answers. Who shot him—and why? Is he a desperate outlaw fleeing the law, or an innocent caught in someone else's crossfire? The tension crackles through every scene as Matt pieces together conflicting testimonies from the townspeople, each with their own secrets and suspicions. With time running out before the stranger either recovers or takes his secrets to the grave, Dillon must navigate the murky terrain between law and morality that defines life in the Old West. The episode showcases what made Gunsmoke a phenomenon: intimate character work layered beneath a compelling mystery, all delivered with the understated authenticity that made listeners feel like they were sitting right there in that dusty Kansas town.

Gunsmoke broke new ground for radio westerns by treating its frontier setting not as a backdrop for shootouts and derring-do, but as a genuine community with real moral complexities. Creator John Meston crafted stories that reflected the ambiguities of American justice and human nature itself. Rather than simple tales of good versus evil, Gunsmoke episodes explored the gray areas where conscience and duty collide. The show's success on CBS from 1952 onwards proved that radio audiences craved substance alongside entertainment.

Tune in for "Gunshot Wound" and experience the masterful storytelling that captivated millions. Settle in with your radio and prepare for an evening of authentic western drama where every clue matters and justice itself remains uncertain.
