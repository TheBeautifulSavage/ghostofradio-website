Gunsmoke 57 09 29 (286) Another Man's Poison

The dusty streets of Dodge City grow even more treacherous when poison enters the equation. In this tense episode of Gunsmoke, Marshal Matt Dillon finds himself investigating a murder that's far more sinister than a simple gunfight—someone has systematically eliminated a man through slow, calculated poisoning. As the marshal digs deeper into the victim's past, he uncovers a web of deception, jealousy, and revenge that implicates nearly everyone in town. William Conrad's gravelly narration guides listeners through each clue with mounting dread, while the expert sound design—the clink of bottles, the scrape of chairs, whispered accusations in the saloon—pulls you deeper into the mystery. This is Gunsmoke at its finest: a show that proves a western isn't just about quick draws, but about the darker human emotions that simmer beneath the frontier's veneer.

Gunsmoke's enduring success came from its willingness to venture beyond shoot-outs and cattle rustling. The CBS network's commitment to authentic storytelling, combined with William Conrad's magnetic performance as Matt Dillon, transformed radio westerns from simple adventure fare into genuine drama. Episodes like "Another Man's Poison" showcase the show's sophistication—the way it examines morality, justice, and the complexities of small-town life. Listeners in the 1950s craved this kind of nuanced storytelling, and Gunsmoke delivered night after night, becoming one of radio's most beloved programs before its legendary transition to television.

Don't miss this gripping tale of betrayal and justice. Tune in to "Another Man's Poison" and discover why Gunsmoke commanded the attention of millions of Americans huddled around their radios. Some mysteries demand to be solved—and some poisons cut deeper than any bullet.
