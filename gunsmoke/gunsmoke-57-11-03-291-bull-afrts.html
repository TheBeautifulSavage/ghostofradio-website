The dust hasn't settled on Front Street when Marshal Dillon faces a crisis that no quick draw can solve. In this tense November broadcast, a massive, unpredictable bull has escaped its pen and now roams Dodge City like a furious force of nature, threatening to trample innocent townsfolk and destroy everything in its path. Doc Adams, Miss Kitty, and Chester all find themselves caught in the crosshairs of bovine fury, and it falls to Matt Dillon to think faster than he's ever had to before. Will brute strength or quick thinking prevail? The atmospheric tension crackles through your speaker as the sound effects team brings the thundering hooves and snorting rage of the beast to vivid life—this is radio drama at its most visceral.

Gunsmoke revolutionized western storytelling by trading in the simplistic good-versus-evil formula for something far more nuanced and human. Unlike the shoot-'em-up adventures dominating the airwaves, these scripts explored the real moral complexities facing a frontier lawman and his community. The show's ensemble cast—William Conrad's gravelly Matt Dillon, Parley Baer's loyal Chester, and the vibrant supporting players—created an authentic Kansas that felt lived-in and genuine. This particular episode exemplifies the show's commitment to character-driven storytelling, finding drama not in gunfights but in how ordinary people cope with extraordinary circumstances.

Don't miss this gripping chapter in the ongoing saga of Dodge City. Tune in to "Bull" and discover why Gunsmoke captivated millions of listeners and earned its place as one of radio's most enduring and beloved programs. You'll hear why this show proved that the western genre could be intelligent, touching, and unforgettable.
