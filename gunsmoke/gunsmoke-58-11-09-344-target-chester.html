# Target Chester

When Chester Proudfoot becomes the unwitting target of a vicious revenge plot, Dodge City transforms from the familiar frontier town listeners know into a pressure cooker of danger and moral complexity. As Matt Dillon works against the clock to uncover who's hunting his devoted deputy, the episode crackles with suspense—every shadow could conceal a killer, every seemingly innocent stranger might be the instrument of Chester's doom. The stakes feel personal in a way that cuts deeper than the typical cattle rustler or claim jumper, for this isn't about property or principle. It's about blood, betrayal, and the dark underbelly of frontier justice where enemies can wait months or years to settle a score. Dennis Weaver's performance captures Chester's growing fear with remarkable subtlety, while William Conrad's Matt Dillon embodies the protective determination of a lawman who won't lose a friend on his watch.

By 1958, Gunsmoke had evolved into something remarkable—no longer merely a western, but a sophisticated examination of frontier morality where good men faced impossible choices and justice often proved as complicated as the crimes themselves. The show's commitment to character-driven storytelling set it apart from flashier competitors, and this episode exemplifies why CBS's Sunday evening radio drama had become appointment listening for millions. With Chester in the crosshairs, the writers explore loyalty, accountability, and what a man owes to those who've stood beside him through Dodge City's most dangerous moments.

Don't miss "Target Chester," where the rules of western justice bend under the weight of personal vendetta. Tune in as Matt Dillon races to protect his friend before vengeance claims another victim in the dusty streets of Dodge City.
