Gunsmoke 59 01 11 (353) The Wolfer

When the wind howls across the Kansas plains and a stranger rides into Dodge City with blood on his hands and vengeance in his heart, Marshal Dillon knows trouble has arrived. In "The Wolfer," listeners will find themselves drawn into a tense tale of frontier justice where the line between hunter and hunted blurs under the merciless prairie sun. William Conrad's gravelly narration sets the scene as a man driven by years of bitterness seeks the one responsible for his family's destruction. Parley Baer's Deputy Burt and Amanda Blake's Miss Kitty add their own concerns to the mounting tension, while the marshal must navigate between the stranger's raw hunger for revenge and his duty to uphold the law. This episode crackles with the authentic moral ambiguity of the frontier—where survival often demanded choices no civilized court could judge.

By 1950, Gunsmoke had already begun its legendary run on CBS radio, creating the definitive sound of the American West through superior writing, subtle performances, and a protagonist who understood that wearing a badge meant bearing the weight of impossible decisions. Unlike the shoot-'em-up stories that cluttered the airwaves, Gunsmoke presented the frontier as a complex landscape where violence had consequences and justice required wisdom, not just a fast draw. Norman Macdonnell's innovative sound design—the crack of a whip, the jingle of spurs, the distant whistle of prairie wind—transported listeners directly into Matt Dillon's world. These weren't cartoonish adventures; they were intimate dramas about human nature set against the backdrop of a lawless land.

Settle in by your radio, dim the lights, and let the opening theme carry you back to Dodge City. "The Wolfer" awaits, and Marshal Dillon has another long night ahead.
