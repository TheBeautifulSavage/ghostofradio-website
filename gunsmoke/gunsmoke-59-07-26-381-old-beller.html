When Marshal Matt Dillon rides out to investigate a dispute over a prize-winning mule named Old Beller, he discovers that the stakes are far higher than simple animal husbandry. The dusty streets of Dodge City crackle with tension as two hardened ranchers lock horns over the beast, each convinced they have a rightful claim. As Doc Adams and Chester gather around the Marshall's office, the case unfolds with all the moral complexity that defines the best of frontier justice—where property lines blur into questions of honor, desperation, and the law of the land. Listeners will find themselves drawn into a web of conflicting testimonies, hidden debts, and the kind of personal vengeance that could turn neighbor against neighbor in a heartbeat.

This 1952 broadcast represents Gunsmoke at the height of its creative powers, when the show had already become America's most trusted source for authentic western drama. Created by writer John Meston, Gunsmoke distinguished itself from shoot-'em-up fantasies by grounding its stories in the real moral dilemmas of frontier life—land disputes, poverty, and the sometimes heartbreaking compromises required to maintain civilization at the edge of the wilderness. William Conrad's narrator voice, weathered as saddle leather, lends gravitas to each tale, while the supporting cast of Parley Baer and Doc Adams brings a warm familiarity that made millions return week after week.

"Old Beller" encapsulates everything listeners cherished about the show: intelligent writing, atmospheric sound design that transports you straight to Kansas Territory, and a protagonist guided by conscience rather than gunplay. Tune in to discover how Marshal Dillon untangles this unexpected conflict with wisdom and compassion—a true masterpiece of radio drama.
