# Have Gun — Will Travel: "Englishman"

Picture a dusty frontier town where East meets West, and honor means everything to a man—even when he's thousands of miles from home. On this January evening in 1959, listeners are transported to the American wilderness to witness the arrival of the 9th Earl of Aylsbury, a British nobleman whose refined sensibilities clash violently with the raw justice of the frontier. When this aristocrat finds himself entangled in a deadly conflict, he discovers that a man's courage cannot be measured by his accent or breeding, and that sometimes the greatest strength lies in standing firm when every instinct urges retreat. Paladin, the mysterious gunslinger with a conscience, becomes an unlikely guide through a maze of cultural misunderstanding and mortal danger—where the clash between British propriety and American frontier morality creates explosive drama.

*Have Gun—Will Travel* captured America's imagination during television's golden age by presenting something revolutionary: a western hero defined not by his quick draw alone, but by his intelligence, philosophy, and uncompromising moral code. Richard Boone's portrayal of Paladin—the erudite mercenary who fights only for principle, not profit—set the show apart from its contemporaries, making it appointment listening for millions. By 1959, the program had become a cultural phenomenon, its influence extending from middle-class living rooms to the halls of Hollywood. This particular episode exemplifies the show's willingness to explore complex social themes beneath its adventure-serial surface, using the stranger-in-a-strange-land premise to examine what it truly means to be civilized.

Tune in to witness a masterclass in tension and character drama, where wit proves as deadly as bullets and an English earl must learn that courage wears many faces. This is vintage radio storytelling at its finest.
