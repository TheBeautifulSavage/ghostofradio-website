# A Matter Of Ethics

When Paladin accepts what seems like a straightforward case in this February 1959 episode, he quickly discovers that the line between justice and morality is far thinner than he'd like to believe. A client presents him with an ethical dilemma that no amount of gunslinging can resolve—a situation where doing what's legal might mean betraying everything his code of honor stands for. As the tension mounts, listeners will find themselves drawn into Paladin's internal struggle, hearing the uncertainty creep into Richard Boone's voice as the unflappable gunslinger confronts a problem that bullets simply cannot solve. The atmospheric San Francisco setting provides the perfect backdrop for this cerebral western, where the real drama unfolds not in a dusty gunfight, but in conversations laden with moral weight and the kind of ambiguity that keeps audiences awake long after the final commercial.

*Have Gun Will Travel* stood apart from typical western fare by treating its protagonist as a thinking man's hero—educated, cultured, and bound by a personal code rather than mere law and order. During the show's peak years in the late 1950s, as America grappled with questions of conformity and individual conscience, Paladin's explorations of ethical gray areas resonated deeply with audiences. This particular episode exemplifies the show's sophisticated approach to the genre, eschewing simple good-versus-evil narratives in favor of complex human dilemmas that challenge both character and viewer alike.

Don't miss this compelling episode that reminds us why *Have Gun Will Travel* earned its place as one of radio's greatest westerns. Tune in and experience the moment when Paladin discovers that the real battles aren't always won with a gun.
