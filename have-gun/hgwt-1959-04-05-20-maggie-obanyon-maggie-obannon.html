# Have Gun Will Travel - April 5, 1959

Step into the dusty streets of a frontier town where a woman's pride and a man's code of honor collide in the most unexpected of circumstances. When Paladin receives word of Maggie O'Banyon—a spirited Irish woman whose late husband's name has been dragged through the mud by corruption and false accusation—he finds himself drawn into a case far more complicated than a simple matter of right and wrong. As the smooth-voiced narrator guides us through the evening, listeners will discover that sometimes the gun isn't the answer; sometimes what a person needs most is someone willing to fight for their reputation when the whole town has turned its back. The tension builds as Paladin must navigate the treacherous waters of small-town prejudice, family pride, and the question of whether a man's legacy can ever truly be restored once it's been shattered.

*Have Gun Will Travel* distinguished itself from typical western fare by focusing on the psychological and moral complexities of frontier justice rather than mere gunplay. Richard Boone's portrayal of Paladin—the educated, philosophically-minded gunslinger for hire—elevated the CBS series to something approaching literature, attracting both rural audiences and urban sophisticates who appreciated sharp writing and nuanced performances. By 1959, the show had become a cultural phenomenon, proof that radio drama could still captivate America even as television threatened the medium's dominance.

This is the kind of story that made *Have Gun Will Travel* unmissable listening—where character and conscience matter as much as quick reflexes. Tune in this evening and discover why audiences made this show one of the most celebrated dramas in radio history.
