# The Statue of San Sabastian

When Paladin rides into the dusty borderlands of old California, he discovers a town gripped by superstition and fear—all centered on a crumbling statue of a Spanish saint that locals believe holds miraculous power. But is the statue truly blessed, or is someone exploiting the townspeople's desperation for personal gain? This May 1959 episode weaves mystery and morality with the kind of sophisticated storytelling that made *Have Gun Will Travel* stand apart from typical western fare. As tension mounts and Paladin peels back layers of deception, listeners will find themselves drawn into a tale where the real battle isn't between bullets and brawn, but between faith and fraud. Richard Boone's measured voice guides us through each revelation with the calm precision of a man who understands human nature as well as he understands his Colt .45.

*Have Gun Will Travel* arrived at CBS in 1958 at a moment when television was beginning to eclipse radio, yet the network's commitment to quality audio drama meant this show became a beacon for those who still cherished the intimacy of the spoken word. Set against a meticulously researched Old West, the series elevated the western genre by focusing on Paladin as a gunslinger with ethics, intelligence, and a business card—a man who solved problems with wisdom as often as with weaponry. The show's success lay in its refusal to offer simple answers, instead exploring the moral complexities that radio drama could illuminate in ways no visual medium could match.

Don't miss this gem from *Have Gun Will Travel's* final season. Tune in and let the scratch of the needle and the resonant tones of classic radio transport you to a time when stories came alive in the theater of the mind, and a single statue could shake the foundations of an entire community.
