Have Gun Will Travel CBS · September 6, 1959

(42) All That Glitters

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

Picture a dusty frontier town where greed glimmers like fool's gold under the desert sun. In this week's episode of Have Gun Will Travel, Richard Boone's Paladin once again finds himself in the complicated shadows between right and wrong, where a fortune in precious metals promises salvation for some and damnation for others. When a prospector's claims attract the attention of unscrupulous claim jumpers and desperate fortune hunters, Paladin must navigate a treacherous landscape of broken promises and bloodthirsty ambition. The tension crackles through your radio speaker as our hero discovers that some treasures, no matter how glittering, exact a price far beyond their worth. Don't expect simple heroics—this is a tale of moral complexity where the line between justice and revenge blurs as dangerously as a mirage on the horizon.

Have Gun Will Travel arrived at CBS in 1958 as something revolutionary: a western that treated its audience like thinking adults. Rather than glorifying frontier violence, the show positioned Paladin as a gunslinger-for-hire with a conscience, a man who solved problems with wit and wisdom as often as with his revolver. Richard Boone's measured, intelligent portrayal transformed the cowboy archetype into something more philosophical, reflecting the postwar American appetite for complexity beneath the Western's traditional surface. During these years when television was just beginning to challenge radio's dominance, Have Gun Will Travel proved that radio drama still commanded unmatched power to create intimate, intelligent storytelling.

Whether you're a devoted follower of the Paladin saga or discovering this classic for the first time, "All That Glitters" delivers the sophisticated drama and expert performances that made this series a benchmark of golden-age radio. Tune in and experience why America couldn't get enough of Paladin's adventures.
