Have Gun Will Travel CBS · October 25, 1959

(49) When In Rome

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

Picture this: it's a crisp October evening in 1959, and you settle into your favorite chair as the unmistakable guitar theme of Have Gun Will Travel fills your living room. But tonight's episode takes Paladin far from the dusty frontier towns that define his legend. "When In Rome" strips away the familiar saloon backdrops and whiskey-soaked showdowns, instead plunging our dapper gunslinger into a world of Old World intrigue where his quick draw means nothing against cunning, deception, and the weight of honor itself. As the drama unfolds, you'll find yourself drawn into a taut psychological battle where wit becomes sharper than any Colt .45, and Paladin must navigate treacherous waters using nothing but his intellect and moral compass. The stakes feel impossibly high, the tension crackles through the airwaves, and you're left wondering whether even the greatest gunfighter alive can solve every problem with his famous calling card.

What makes this particular installment remarkable is how it exemplifies the show's evolution during its golden years. By 1959, Have Gun Will Travel had transcended the simple shoot-'em-up formula that defined earlier Westerns, becoming instead a sophisticated anthology series that explored complex moral questions wrapped in Western trappings. Richard Boone's Paladin—a man of culture, intelligence, and principle—represented a new breed of television hero, one who carried Shakespeare as comfortably as ammunition. This episode perfectly captures that sophisticated sensibility, proving that a Western could be intelligent, worldly, and utterly gripping.

Don't miss "When In Rome," where Paladin discovers that sometimes the most dangerous showdowns happen far from the Wild West, and a gentleman's code proves mightier than any gun.
