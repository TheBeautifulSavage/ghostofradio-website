# Have Gun Will Travel - "Fair Fugitive" (1959)

Picture this: a dusty frontier town where nothing is quite as it seems, and even the most innocent arrival carries the weight of dangerous secrets. In this November 1959 broadcast, Paladin finds himself entangled in a mystery that demands every ounce of his legendary cunning. A young woman has ridden into town—beautiful, composed, yet visibly running from something far more sinister than the law. As Paladin investigates her past and uncovers the truth of her flight, listeners will experience the trademark tension that made *Have Gun Will Travel* an unmissable Thursday night ritual: moral ambiguity wrapped in quicksilver dialogue, a protagonist whose honor isn't always convenient, and the kind of atmospheric western storytelling that transformed radio drama into an art form.

By 1959, *Have Gun Will Travel* had already established itself as CBS's crown jewel of dramatic broadcasting, with Richard Boone's silky baritone and carefully measured delivery creating an unforgettable anti-hero in Paladin—that rarest of gunfighters who solved problems with his mind as often as his pistol. The show's appeal lay in its refusal to paint the Old West in primary colors. Episodes like "Fair Fugitive" showcased why the program attracted sophisticated audiences beyond the typical western demographic, offering complex character studies and morally textured plots that rivaled television's best drama.

This is radio drama at its finest—no visual shortcuts, no reliance on spectacle, just masterful performances and a script that trusts the listener's imagination. Whether you're a longtime devotee of Paladin's adventures or discovering this classic for the first time, "Fair Fugitive" exemplifies why *Have Gun Will Travel* remains the gold standard of western radio fiction. Tune in and let Paladin guide you through this intricate tale of deception and redemption.
