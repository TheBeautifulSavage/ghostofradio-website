Have Gun Will Travel CBS · May 1, 1960

(76) Dressed To Kill

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

Picture this: a dusty frontier town where appearances can be as deadly as a six-gun. In "Dressed to Kill," Paladin finds himself caught in a web of deception where a woman's elegant attire masks something far more sinister than anyone suspects. As the enigmatic gunslinger with a code of honor, Richard Boone guides you through a taut 30-minute mystery where fashion becomes a weapon and trust becomes a luxury no one can afford. The tension builds with each carefully placed word, the ambient sounds of saloon life and rattling spurs creating an immersive soundscape that puts you squarely in the dusty streets of the Old West. You'll find yourself guessing at motivations and second-guessing every character's true intentions until the final, satisfying reveal.

By 1960, Have Gun Will Travel had become CBS's crown jewel of western programming, attracting millions of listeners who tuned in religiously each week to follow Paladin's adventures. The show brilliantly transcended typical shoot-'em-up fare, offering intelligent scripts with moral complexity and psychological depth that appealed to both adults and younger audiences alike. Unlike many radio westerns that relied on action and spectacle, this series showcased sophisticated storytelling where the mind proved mightier than the revolver. Boone's sophisticated portrayal of a gunslinger-for-hire with principles made Paladin a hero for thinking listeners, proving that radio drama could be both entertaining and intellectually engaging.

Settle in with "Dressed to Kill" and experience why Have Gun Will Travel remained one of radio's most beloved programs. This is old-time radio at its finest—crafted writing, stellar voice acting, and a mystery that will keep you guessing until the very end.
