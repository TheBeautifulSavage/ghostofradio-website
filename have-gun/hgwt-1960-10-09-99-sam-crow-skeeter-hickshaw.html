Have Gun Will Travel CBS · October 9, 1960

(99) Sam Crow (skeeter Hickshaw &)

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

October brings a chill to the frontier as Paladin finds himself embroiled in a tangle of revenge and mistaken identity that cuts to the very heart of western justice. When the notorious outlaw Sam Crow crosses paths with Skeeter Hickshaw, a seemingly ordinary drifter, the stage is set for a confrontation that will test whether violence begets more violence—or if redemption might still be possible in the dust and blood of the Old West. Listen as Paladin navigates the murky moral waters between right and wrong, law and lawlessness, racing against time to prevent a tragedy born from old wounds and bitter grudges. Richard Boone's measured tones guide us through a world where a man's reputation can condemn him as surely as a bullet, where one mistake can echo across years, and where the only certainty is danger.

By 1960, Have Gun Will Travel had cemented itself as CBS's most intelligent western—a show that understood the genre could explore something deeper than quick-draw heroics and black-hat villainy. While other westerns trafficked in simplistic morality plays, Paladin's cases revealed the complex web of human motivation, justice, and consequence that colored the real frontier. Each week, millions of Americans tuned in to hear not just tales of gunplay, but meditations on honor, duty, and the price of civilization in a lawless land. "Sam Crow" exemplifies this sophisticated approach, offering listeners a narrative where clever writing and psychological tension matter as much as any gunfight.

Don't miss this October 9th installment of one of radio's finest dramatic achievements. Settle in as the sun sets on another perilous adventure for the man with the calling card, the quick wit, and the faster gun.
