# Have Gun Will Travel: Stardust

When Paladin receives a cryptic message from a woman named Lola Blackwood, he finds himself drawn into the glittering but treacherous world of a traveling carnival—a place where nothing is quite what it seems. As the golden dust of the midway settles in the evening air, mysterious circumstances swirl around a fortune teller's tent, and Paladin must navigate between genuine misery and elaborate deception. This October 1960 episode captures the show at its finest: a taut, character-driven western that trades gunfights for psychological intrigue, where the real danger lurks not in obvious villainy but in the shadowy motives of desperate people caught between survival and morality. Lola Blackwood herself emerges as one of radio's most intriguing femme fatales—a woman whose past and present collide in ways that will keep you guessing until the very final moments.

*Have Gun Will Travel* revolutionized the western genre during its run on CBS, proving that radio drama could deliver sophisticated storytelling alongside adventure. By 1960, the show had refined its formula into something approaching art: each episode presented not merely a conflict to be resolved but a moral complexity to be untangled. Richard Boone's portrayal of Paladin—the educated gunslinger with business card in hand—allowed writers to explore themes of justice, redemption, and ambiguity that anticipated the more nuanced westerns of television's golden age. The show's success lay in understanding that the West was ultimately a place of human nature, not just six-shooters and dust.

Settle into your favorite chair and prepare for an evening of mystery and atmosphere. "Stardust" showcases everything that made *Have Gun Will Travel* essential listening for thousands of devoted fans. Let Paladin guide you through the shadows of the carnival midway—you won't want to miss where this trail leads.
