Picture yourself settling into your favorite armchair on a November evening in 1960, the glow of your radio dial cutting through the darkness as Paladin's iconic theme swells—that unmistakable guitar riff signaling danger and intrigue ahead. In this week's episode, "The Map," our fastidious gunslinger finds himself entangled in a mystery far more complex than a simple six-gun showdown. When a dying prospector presses a tattered map into Paladin's hands, whispering of hidden fortune, the Man in Black discovers that gold isn't always the most valuable treasure—and that desperate men will stop at nothing to possess it. With each revelation, the stakes climb higher, and Paladin must navigate a web of deception, greed, and betrayal that tests not just his quick draw, but his moral compass. Can he uncover the truth before blood is spilled, or will the curse of hidden riches claim yet another victim?

Have Gun Will Travel stood apart from the crowded western landscape of late-1950s radio and television, offering something audiences craved: a protagonist as comfortable discussing philosophy over drinks as he was drawing iron. Richard Boone's Paladin was no simple frontier gunslinger—he was a man of letters, of principle, operating from his San Francisco base with a calling card bearing only his symbolic gun and address. As the show entered its final season on CBS radio, episodes like "The Map" showcased the sophisticated storytelling that kept loyal listeners tuning in, blending classic western adventure with psychological depth and moral ambiguity.

Don't miss this gripping tale of fortune and folly. Tune in and discover why Have Gun Will Travel remains the gold standard of western radio drama.
