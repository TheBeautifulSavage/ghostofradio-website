# Ten Strike Gold

Picture this: the desert sun beats down mercilessly as Hopalong Cassidy and his trusted companions ride toward a remote mining camp where fortune and treachery lie buried in equal measure. When a panicked prospector stumbles into their camp with tales of a spectacular gold strike—and an even more spectacular murder—our white-hatted hero finds himself caught between claim jumpers, hired guns, and a mystery that runs deeper than any mine shaft. The tension crackles across the airwaves as Hoppy must use both his quick draw and his sharper wit to uncover the truth before another body hits the dust. With each commercial break, listeners are left wondering: will greed triumph, or will justice prevail in this isolated corner of the Old West?

What made *Hopalong Cassidy* such a phenomenon on the radio waves was its perfect balance of action and integrity. William Boyd's portrayal of the iconic character—originally created by Clarence E. Mulford in 1904—brought a moral compass to the adventure, never glorifying violence but always standing firm for what was right. Unlike some of the more cynical westerns that would emerge later, this show represented radio's golden age at its finest: wholesome entertainment that didn't talk down to its audience. The program thrived during the 1940s, a time when Americans tuned in for escape and reassurance that good men still rode the range. Each episode was crafted with meticulous attention to authentic western detail, making listeners feel they were truly riding alongside Cassidy across the frontier.

Join Hopalong Cassidy as he rides into danger once more in "Ten Strike Gold"—a tale of ambition, deception, and the unshakeable code of the West. Saddle up for adventure!
