# The King of Spades

When Hoppy rides into a dusty Nevada mining town where fortune and treachery run as thick as desert heat, he discovers a deadly game far more dangerous than any outlaw he's faced. A ruthless gambler known only as "The King of Spades" has established himself as the de facto law of the land, using rigged poker games and hired guns to control everything from the silver claims to the very souls of desperate men. As our hero peels back the layers of deception surrounding this mysterious figure, he finds himself caught between loyalty to an old friend and justice for the innocent—with the final hand played not at the card table, but in a tense showdown where one wrong move means death. Listeners will feel the oppressive weight of the saloon's smoke-filled air, the sharp click of poker chips, and the barely contained violence lurking beneath every word of dialogue in this taut 15-minute adventure.

Hopalong Cassidy was more than just another Western; it was the crown jewel of CBS and Mutual's adventure programming during the golden age of radio. William Boyd's measured, authoritative voice brought dignity and moral clarity to a character that had already captured millions of hearts through film. The show's writers crafted stories that transcended simple good-versus-evil narratives, exploring themes of redemption, friendship, and the corrupting influence of greed. Episodes like "The King of Spades" showcase why Hoppy remained radio's most trusted lawman for over a decade, combining pulse-pounding action with genuine human drama that resonated across America's living rooms.

Don't miss this thrilling installment where quick wits and quick draws collide. Tune in to "The King of Spades" and discover why families gathered around their radios to hear Hopalong Cassidy ride toward justice—one dangerous mystery at a time.
