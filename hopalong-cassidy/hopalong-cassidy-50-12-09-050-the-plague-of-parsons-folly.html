# Hopalong Cassidy: The Plague of Parson's Folly

When a mysterious sickness sweeps through the isolated mining town of Parson's Folly, panic grips the citizens and suspicion runs wild. Our hero, the silver-haired Hopalong Cassidy, rides into quarantine to discover whether this is a natural calamity—or something far more sinister. As the townspeople grow desperate and desperate men turn to violence, Hoppy must navigate accusations, medical mysteries, and the thin line between mob justice and truth. The episode crackles with period authenticity, complete with the creak of saddle leather, the thunder of hooves, and the haunting wail of a harmonica beneath the desert stars. What begins as a health crisis becomes a test of character and courage.

*The Plague of Parson's Folly* exemplifies why Hopalong Cassidy became one of America's most beloved radio westerns during the 1940s. Unlike the shoot-first adventures of competitors, William Boyd's portrayal of the aging cowboy emphasized wisdom, integrity, and quiet heroism. The show ran successfully across CBS and Mutual networks for over a decade, attracting millions of listeners who tuned in for stories that felt grounded in authentic frontier struggles—cattle rustling, water rights, and yes, the unexpected catastrophes that could devastate a frontier community. Boyd himself was deeply invested in the character, having spent years establishing Hoppy as a moral exemplar for young and old alike.

Don't miss this gem from radio's golden age. Whether you're a longtime Cassidy fan or discovering the legend for the first time, this episode delivers everything that made the show a cultural phenomenon: adventure, mystery, and a hero you can truly believe in. Adjust your dial and settle in—Hopalong Cassidy awaits.
