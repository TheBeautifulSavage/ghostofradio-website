# Hopalong Cassidy: Hoppy Elects A Sheriff

The dusty streets of Bar 20 territory are in turmoil as corrupt officials have left the town without law and order. When Hopalong Cassidy rides into this lawless haven, he discovers that the townspeople are paralyzed by fear and indecision—they need a new sheriff, but no honest man dares step forward. In this gripping installment, Hoppy must navigate not bullets and outlaws, but the treacherous politics of frontier justice itself. As election day approaches, hidden agendas emerge from unexpected quarters, and our hero finds himself caught between a widow woman's surprising ambitions, a cowardly banker's schemes, and the town's genuine desperation for leadership. With his characteristic wisdom and quick draw, Hopalong must unmask the true villains before a wolf in sheep's clothing pins on the badge—but this time, his greatest weapon isn't his Colt .45, it's his unwavering moral compass.

*Hopalong Cassidy* stands as one of radio's most enduring adventures, grounded in William Boyd's actual film legacy as the iconic cowboy hero. Airing six nights a week throughout the 1940s, the show brought authentic Western atmosphere into living rooms across America during an era hungry for heroes and escapism. The writing balances classic gunslinger action with genuine human drama, exploring how civilization and justice take root in frontier communities. This particular episode exemplifies the show's strength: rather than relying solely on action, it examines the moral questions that built the American West.

Don't miss this stirring chapter in the saga of Hopalong Cassidy. Tune in as the Bar 20 confronts its greatest challenge yet—not rustlers or desperados, but the corruption that threatens to undermine justice itself. Stay tuned to your local station.
