# Death Crosses The River

When danger lurks at every bend of the Rio Grande, even a quick river crossing becomes a life-or-death gamble. In this tense episode of *Hopalong Cassidy*, our silver-haired hero finds himself cornered on the wrong side of the border with murderers closing in fast. With his trusty horse Topper and his keen frontier instincts as his only allies, Hoppy must outwit a ruthless gang determined to silence him permanently. The bridge spanning the river offers escape—but it's a trap waiting to spring. Will he make it across before bullets find their mark, or will his enemies finally claim the legendary lawman? Settle in for a thrilling ride as the familiar thunder of hoofbeats mingles with gunfire and treachery in this gripping tale of survival against the odds.

*Hopalong Cassidy* became one of radio's most beloved adventures during the golden age of broadcasting, drawing millions of listeners each week to their sets. Created by Clarence E. Mulford's literary character but reimagined for the airwaves, the show starred the suave and resourceful William Boyd, whose portrayal captured the sophistication of the modern cowboy hero—intelligent, honorable, and always one step ahead of villainy. Between 1942 and 1952, the CBS and Mutual networks brought listeners hundreds of episodes set in the dusty landscapes of the American West, where good always triumphed through wit and bravery rather than brutality. The program represented the golden standard of western adventure radio, combining authentic frontier atmosphere with compelling storytelling that captivated families across the nation.

Don't miss this masterclass in radio suspense. Tune in and experience why audiences made *Hopalong Cassidy* a staple of American entertainment for an entire decade.
