# Hopalong Cassidy: Hook, Line and Murder

When our hero Hopalong Cassidy rides into a quiet riverside town on this May evening in 1951, listeners know trouble won't be far behind. *Hook, Line and Murder* opens with the discovery of a body tangled in fishing nets—a local businessman whose enemies are as numerous as stars in the prairie sky. As William Boyd's distinctive voice carries us into the mystery, the carefully crafted sound design brings us right to the water's edge: the gentle lap of waves against the dock, the creak of weathered wood, the distant cry of gulls. But beneath this deceptive calm lurks danger. With each clue uncovered and each suspect interrogated, tension mounts like a gathering storm. Hopalong must unravel a web of greed, jealousy, and desperation before the real killer strikes again—and this time, it might be someone close to him.

For over a decade, *Hopalong Cassidy* captivated millions of Americans during the golden age of radio, transforming the character from pulp magazines and B-movies into a household name. What set the show apart was its commitment to clever storytelling and genuine suspense, refusing the simplistic good-versus-evil narratives of lesser westerns. Boyd's portrayal added gravitas to every episode, while the supporting cast of colorful characters and intricate plots kept loyal listeners tuning in faithfully each week. The show became a cultural phenomenon, spawning merchandise, television adaptations, and a devoted fan base that cherished Hopalong as their personal friend riding the range.

Don't miss *Hook, Line and Murder*—where the mystery runs as deep as the river itself, and justice rides on the back of a white horse. Tune in tonight and discover why audiences from coast to coast made Hopalong Cassidy their weekly appointment with adventure.
