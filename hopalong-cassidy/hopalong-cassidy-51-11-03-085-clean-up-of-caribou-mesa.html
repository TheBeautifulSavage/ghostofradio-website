# Hopalong Cassidy: Clean Up of Caribou Mesa

Picture this: the sun hangs low and merciless over the sprawling badlands of Caribou Mesa, where dust devils spiral like phantoms across the parched earth. Our hero, the silver-haired Hopalong Cassidy, rides into a town gripped by lawlessness and fear—crooked officials line their pockets while honest ranchers lose everything to rustlers and claim jumpers. In this tense November broadcast, Hoppy must navigate a web of corruption that runs deeper than any outlaw's bullet, confronting not just hardened criminals but the very institutions meant to protect the innocent. With California Carlson and the boys at his side, Cassidy faces his most dangerous adversary yet: a conspiracy that could consume the entire mesa if one man doesn't have the courage to stand alone.

What made Hopalong Cassidy a fixture in American homes throughout the 1940s was William Boyd's masterful portrayal of a hero defined not by quick reflexes alone, but by moral clarity and unwavering justice. Unlike the rougher protagonists of pulp fiction, Hoppy embodied a courteous, intelligent lawman—a cowboy who solved problems with wisdom as often as with his trusty six-shooter. The show's popularity translated directly into one of radio's most loyal audiences, with listeners tuning in religiously to Mutual and CBS networks, eager to follow adventures set in a West that felt both mythically timeless and comfortingly familiar during wartime America.

This episode captures everything that made the series essential listening: complex plotting, genuine danger, and a hero worth believing in. Settle in with the crackling static, let Hoppy's steady voice and the percussion of hoofbeats transport you back to 1940s America, and discover why millions of listeners made this appointment with the Old West a cherished part of their weekly ritual.
