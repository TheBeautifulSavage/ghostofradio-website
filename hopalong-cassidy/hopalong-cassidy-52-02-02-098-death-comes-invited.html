# Hopalong Cassidy: Death Comes Invited

Picture yourself by the radio on a winter's evening as Hopalong and his Bar 20 outfit uncover a sinister plot where murder wears a gentleman's smile. In "Death Comes Invited," our silver-haired hero finds himself tangled in a web of deception when a seemingly respectable stranger arrives at the ranch bearing gifts and honeyed words—but harboring intentions as dark as the desert night. What begins as polite hospitality transforms into a desperate race against time as Cassidy realizes his trusted home has become a trap. With nothing but his quick wits and quicker draw, Hoppy must expose the killer's identity before another body falls. The crackle of suspenseful dialogue, the tension punctuated by gunfire, and William Boyd's steady voice cutting through the darkness will keep you gripped until the final resolution.

This 1942 episode exemplifies why *Hopalong Cassidy* became one of radio's most enduring western adventures, captivating millions across CBS and Mutual networks throughout the 1940s. Boyd's portrayal of the principled, white-hatted hero offered post-war audiences a moral anchor—a man of action who relied on integrity as much as his six-shooter. Unlike the lawless gunfighter archetypes flooding the airwaves, Hoppy represented a civilized frontier, where justice prevailed through cleverness and character. The show's episodic format allowed writers to explore mysteries and moral dilemmas alongside classic western adventure, keeping audiences returning week after week.

For those who cherish the golden age of radio drama, when imagination completed what sound effects could only suggest, this episode remains essential listening. Let Hoppy's steady courage and the authentic Western atmosphere transport you back to an era when entertainment meant gathering around the set for genuine suspense.
