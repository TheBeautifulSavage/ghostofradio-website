Deep beneath the turquoise waters of the Caribbean, a mystery awaits—and Jack Armstrong is diving straight into danger. In this thrilling second installment of "Adventure of the Sunken Reef," our intrepid All-American hero and his loyal companions find themselves searching for a legendary Spanish galleon lost to the depths decades ago. But the murky waters hide more than sunken treasure; treacherous currents, rival salvagers, and the very real threat of the bends threaten to cut their expedition short—permanently. With each crackle of radio static and each gasp of surprise from the sound effects crew, listeners are transported to an underwater world of peril where quick thinking and unwavering courage are the only lifelines between triumph and tragedy.

Jack Armstrong captured the imagination of American youth throughout the Depression and war years precisely because it embodied a particular kind of American optimism: the belief that intelligence, determination, and good character could overcome any obstacle. First airing in 1933, the show became a cultural phenomenon, spawning comic strips, novels, and even a movie serial. By the 1940s, when this episode aired, Jack Armstrong had become synonymous with wholesome adventure itself—a boy scout's fever dream of exotic locales and impossible escapes. The show's longevity spoke to radio's golden age magic: the ability to transform a humble living room into an underwater kingdom with nothing but talented voice actors and imagination.

Step through the static this week and join Jack as he races against time and rival fortune-seekers to unlock the secrets of the sunken reef.
