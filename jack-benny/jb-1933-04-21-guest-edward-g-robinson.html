The Jack Benny Program NBC/CBS · 1933

Guest Edward G. Robinson

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

Tune in this Friday evening and witness the masterful collision of two titans of entertainment as Jack Benny welcomes the gravelly-voiced Edward G. Robinson into his studio. Robinson, fresh from his electrifying turn as the ruthless Rico in Little Caesar, brings his underworld menace and surprising comedic timing to the microphone. What unfolds is a symphony of interrupted gags, rapid-fire banter, and the kind of spontaneous magic that only live radio can deliver—complete with sound effects that punctuate every punch line and musical interludes from the orchestra. Benny's trademark stinginess and Robinson's tough-guy persona create an irresistible chemistry as the gangster star discovers just how far Jack will go to avoid picking up the check.

This 1933 broadcast captures The Jack Benny Program at a pivotal moment in radio history, when the medium had become America's primary source of entertainment and cultural communion. In an era of economic despair, these thirty minutes offered families gathered around their receivers an escape into laughter and sophistication. Benny's format—mixing comedy sketches, musical performance, and celebrity guests in an effortlessly natural conversational style—revolutionized radio entertainment and set the standard that would dominate the medium for the next two decades. Robinson's appearance exemplifies how Hollywood's brightest stars eagerly crossed over to radio, understanding its unparalleled reach and intimacy.

Don't miss this glittering artifact of radio's golden age. Settle into your favorite chair, extinguish the lights if you dare, and let your imagination fill the studio with the ghosts of April 1933. This is radio as it was meant to be heard—live, unpredictable, and absolutely captivating.
