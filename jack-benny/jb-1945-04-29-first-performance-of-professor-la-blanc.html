First Performance Of Professor La Blanc

Settle into your favorite chair on this April evening in 1945, and prepare yourself for an evening of pure comedic mayhem as Jack Benny unveils one of his most delightfully absurd creations: Professor La Blanc, the world's most incompetent violin instructor. As the studio audience roars with anticipation, you'll witness the collision between Jack's earnest pretension and the ridiculous reality of his musical ambitions—a tension that has made The Jack Benny Program radio's most reliably hilarious half-hour. When Professor La Blanc takes the stage, with his thick accent and impenetrable teaching methods, the very fabric of Jack's carefully constructed dignity begins to unravel. What unfolds is a masterclass in comic timing, as Jack's iconic band—including the ever-faithful Rochester—navigates the chaos with perfect precision.

For nearly a decade and a half, Jack Benny has transformed the American living room into a theater of the absurd, mining comedy gold from the most mundane domestic situations and his own carefully crafted persona as vain, cheapskate, and perpetually thirty-nine years old. This 1945 broadcast arrives during the show's golden era, when Jack had already perfected the art of the running gag and created an ensemble of characters—Mary Livingstone, Phil Harris, Dennis Day, Don Wilson—who had become as familiar to listeners as family members. Professor La Blanc represents the show at its creative peak, introducing a character whose very existence seems calculated to torment Jack's musical pretensions.

Don't miss this legendary moment in American radio comedy. Tune in as Professor La Blanc makes his unforgettable debut—you'll discover why millions tuned in each week to hear Jack Benny's world delightfully fall apart.
