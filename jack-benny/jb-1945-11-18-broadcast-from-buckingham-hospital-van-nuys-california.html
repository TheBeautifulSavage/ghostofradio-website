The Jack Benny Program NBC/CBS · 1945

Broadcast From Buckingham Hospital Van Nuys, California

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

Picture this: the elegant strains of the "La Marche des Zouaves" theme fade into the warm, familiar chuckle of America's favorite miser as Jack Benny broadcasts live from Buckingham Hospital in Van Nuys, California. This isn't your typical Sunday evening performance—it's a special wartime broadcast bringing laughter directly to servicemen and hospital patients, a moment when the boundary between entertainer and audience dissolves. You'll hear Jack in his element, trading barbs with his loyal announcer Don Wilson, orchestrating comedic chaos with perfect timing, and perhaps encountering the unexpected complications that only a live broadcast from a hospital could produce. With Mary Livingstone, Phil Harris, and Rochester Van Jones joining the fun, this episode captures something precious: the power of radio to lift spirits when the nation needed it most, just as soldiers were beginning to return home from the Pacific and Europe.

For over a decade, Jack Benny had perfected the art of the radio comedy-variety show, building an empire on his impeccable sense of timing and the chemistry between his regular cast members. By 1945, he was already a national institution, yet his willingness to take his show on the road to entertain those who'd sacrificed for the country demonstrated the deeper purpose radio served beyond mere entertainment. These remote broadcasts were the golden thread connecting civilians to the home front experience, reminding everyone—especially those in hospital beds—that they hadn't been forgotten.

If you want to experience American radio at its most vital and purposeful, when a comedian's mission extended beyond laughs to genuine morale-boosting, this special edition of The Jack Benny Program from Buckingham Hospital awaits you. Settle in and let Jack's masterful comedic timing transport you back to November 1945.
