# The Jack Benny Program: From New York with Al Jolson

Step into the Rainbow Room high above Manhattan on this May evening in 1947, where Jack Benny finds himself caught in one of his most delightfully uncomfortable predicaments. When the legendary Al Jolson—the man, the myth, the incomparable entertainer—arrives unannounced at Jack's exclusive broadcast location, mayhem erupts in the most civilized way possible. Listen as Jack's trademark stingy nature collides head-on with Jolson's boundless charisma, while Don Wilson's booming announcements and the orchestra's swelling arrangements punctuate each comedic beat. You'll hear Rochester's dry asides cut through the Manhattan glamour, and feel the genuine spark of two entertainment titans engaging in quick-witted banter that only radio's golden age could deliver. The tension simmers beneath every carefully timed pause—will Jack's famous vanity survive the encounter intact?

By 1947, The Jack Benny Program had become America's heartbeat, commanding Sunday evenings with a consistency that made Jack Benny's name synonymous with radio itself. The show's genius lay not in broad slapstick but in the meticulous construction of character and timing, a formula perfected over fifteen years of continuous broadcasting. This episode captures the program at its zenith, when guest stars of Jolson's magnitude were still willing to participate in humorous skits that gently mocked their own legends. The Benny-Jolson dynamic showcases the generational bridge in entertainment—the vaudeville master meeting radio's supreme craftsman.

Dial in to this classic confrontation and discover why audiences made The Jack Benny Program an unmissable ritual for over two decades. In an era before television fragmented America's attention, Jack Benny brought the nation together, one perfectly crafted laugh at a time.
