Step into the drawing room of the swank Racquet Club on this April evening in 1948, where Jack Benny and his cavalcade of comedic conspirators find themselves tangled in the web of a most perplexing murder mystery. As Part Two unfolds, the plot thickens considerably—secrets spill from behind velvet curtains, suspicious characters dart across darkened hallways, and our stammering hero Jack must navigate the treacherous waters of detective work while maintaining his trademark nervous charm. What clue will unravel the mystery? Will Don Wilson's booming announcer's voice inadvertently reveal the culprit? Will Mary Livingstone's sharp wit cut through to the truth? The tension crackles through the airwaves as our beloved ensemble cast turns sleuth, blending the sophisticated mystery thriller with the comedic timing that made Benny's program America's most anticipated appointment with their radio set.

By 1948, The Jack Benny Program had cemented itself as the gold standard of American radio comedy—a masterclass in timing, character, and ensemble chemistry that would influence entertainment for decades to come. Benny's migration from NBC to CBS in 1948 marked a pivotal moment in broadcasting history, and episodes like this murder mystery serial demonstrated why sponsors and listeners alike couldn't resist his particular magic. The show's ability to seamlessly weave mystery and mirth, using radio's invisible stage to paint elaborate scenes in listeners' minds, exemplified why radio comedy remained at the cultural zenith even as television loomed on the horizon.

Don't miss this delightfully twisted conclusion to one of radio's most entertaining whodunits. Tune in now and discover whether justice prevails at the Racquet Club—or whether our lovably bumbling hero will stumble upon the answer purely by accident.
