Last Show For Nbc

On this historic December evening, Jack Benny bids farewell to NBC after sixteen glorious years, and listeners across America can feel the electricity crackle through their speakers. The master of comic timing has orchestrated a farewell spectacular that's equal parts vaudeville spectacle and intimate goodbye, with his entire troupe—the lovable Don Wilson, the perpetually exasperated Rochester, the wisecracking Mary Livingstone, and even the screeching Dennis Day—gathered to celebrate their time together on the Peacock Network. There's genuine poignancy beneath the carefully crafted gags as Benny reflects on the show that made him America's most beloved entertainer, yet his comedic instincts never waver; even in sentimentality, he finds the laugh, the perfectly timed pause, the self-deprecating jab that keeps his audience chuckling through the nostalgia.

This broadcast marks a pivotal moment in radio history and Benny's own career trajectory. For over a decade and a half, The Jack Benny Program had been appointment listening, a weekly masterclass in comedic construction that influenced an entire generation of performers. Benny's decision to move to CBS represented a seismic shift in broadcasting, driven partly by better financial terms but also reflecting the industry's turbulent transformation as television loomed on the horizon. Even as radio's golden age begins its slow sunset, Benny's consummate professionalism and comedic genius ensure his legacy will endure far beyond this final NBC broadcast.

Don't miss this extraordinary moment—the chance to hear one of entertainment's greatest minds deliver his final bow on the network that made him a household name. It's a privileged glimpse into radio's most dazzling era, preserved forever in time.
