# Jimmy Durante Show 471008

Picture yourself settling into your favorite chair on an October evening in 1947, the warm glow of your radio dial illuminating in the darkened living room. Tonight, Jimmy Durante and his special guest Eddie Cantor have decided to take your audience on a nostalgic journey down memory lane, trading stories from the early days of vaudeville when two ambitious young men first dreamed of making people laugh. You'll hear the unmistakable rasp of Durante's voice—that gravelly, infectious tenor—as he and Cantor reminisce about the dressing rooms, the grueling schedules, the penny-ante performances in smoky theaters before either had achieved stardom. Expect the spontaneous laughter, the musical interludes, and those genuine moments of warmth that made Durante such a beloved figure in American entertainment, as two pioneers of show business reflect on their humble origins and the crazy journey that brought them to network radio.

By the late 1940s, The Jimmy Durante Show had become an institution in American homes, a reliable source of comedy and heart in an era when radio still reigned supreme. During's distinctive persona—the self-deprecating charm masked by that famous schnoz and boundless energy—represented a vanishing world of vaudeville entertainment. Episodes like this one, featuring conversations with fellow entertainers, offered listeners something radio did best: intimate access to the personalities they adored, unscripted moments that captured genuine affection between legends.

This is radio at its most enchanting—pure storytelling, warm camaraderie, and the sound of America's greatest entertainers simply being themselves. Tune in and discover why millions made this their weekly appointment with laughter and nostalgia.
