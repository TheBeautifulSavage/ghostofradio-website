# Jimmy Durante Show 471022: Arthur Treacher Moves In With Jimmy

Picture yourself settled into your favorite chair on a crisp October evening in 1947, the warm glow of your radio set casting dancing shadows across the room. As Jimmy Durante's distinctive gravelly voice crackles through the speaker with his trademark "Ha-CHA!", you know you're in for a rollicking evening of mayhem and mirth. In this installment, the great schnozzola finds himself in a predicament of his own making when the urbane and impeccably mannered Arthur Treacher—the very epitome of British propriety—moves into Jimmy's humble abode. What follows is a collision of comedic worlds: Treacher's prissy refinement against Durante's boisterous, anything-goes approach to life. Expect misunderstandings galore, physical comedy that translates beautifully through sound effects alone, and Durante's infectious laugh punctuating every catastrophe as his household descends into glorious chaos.

The Jimmy Durante Show was a beacon of post-war American entertainment, a place where variety entertainment still reigned supreme and radio was the undisputed king of home entertainment. Durante himself was already a legend from vaudeville and Broadway, and his gift for improvisation and genuine warmth made him perfect for the intimate medium of radio. This particular episode showcases exactly why audiences made the show appointment listening—the unpredictable chemistry between Durante's Italian-American wiseguy persona and Treacher's affected aristocratic pomposity creates comedic friction that sparkles with authenticity and rehearsed spontaneity.

If you've never experienced the magic of classic radio comedy, or if you're a longtime devotee of the Durante canon, this episode is an absolute must-listen. Tune in and let yourself be transported back to an era when laughter was shared across the nation through the airwaves, when timing was everything, and when a man's voice and a few well-placed sound effects could conjure an entire world of entertainment.
