# Jimmy Durante Show 471105: Jimmy Sings With Bing Crosby

Picture yourself huddled around the radio on a November evening in 1940, the warm glow of the dial casting amber light across the parlor as Jimmy Durante's unmistakable gravelly voice crackles through the speaker: "Eeeeeverybody wants to get into the act!" This is the magic moment when two of radio's greatest entertainers collide in comic brilliance and musical harmony. Listeners are in for a treat of masterful banter—Durante's rapid-fire wisecracks and physical comedy translated into pure sound, punctuated by Crosby's smooth, crooning interjections. The pair trades barbs with the timing of vaudeville veterans while the orchestra swells behind them, and you can almost hear the audience roaring with laughter in the studio. Whether they're attempting a duet that dissolves into comedic chaos or riffing on the latest cultural absurdities, Durante and Crosby prove why variety radio was America's living room entertainment.

Durante's show represented the pinnacle of NBC's comedy-variety programming during the Golden Age, a format that would soon give way to television. The Schnozzola's peculiar genius—part slapstick maestro, part surprisingly genuine vocalist—made him a singular radio presence, and pairing him with the relaxed charm of Bing Crosby created an irresistible chemistry that audiences adored. These weekly broadcasts were appointment listening, the communal heartbeat of American entertainment before television fragmented the national audience into millions of isolated viewers.

Tune in to this classic encounter and experience radio comedy at its most spontaneous and charming. In this episode, you'll witness the precise moment when two American entertainment legends prove that greatness recognizes greatness, even when separated by microphone and ad-lib.
