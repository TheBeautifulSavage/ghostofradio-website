# Jimmy Durante Show 471126: Preparing A Live Turkey For Thanksgiving

Picture this: it's late November, 1947, and America's households gather around their radios as Jimmy Durante bounds onto the NBC stage with that unmistakable, raspy voice and infectious energy. Tonight's chaos promises to be unforgettable—Durante has procured a live turkey for the occasion, and what begins as a simple cooking demonstration quickly spirals into comedic pandemonium. With his trademark fractured English and physical comedy translating brilliantly through sound effects and dialogue, Jimmy attempts to wrangle an increasingly panicked bird while his supporting cast provides running commentary and perfectly-timed interruptions. The studio audience erupts in laughter as feathers fly (or at least the illusion of them does), creating the kind of genuine, spontaneous entertainment that made live radio the heartbeat of American culture. You can almost hear the chaos through your speaker—the flapping, the squawking, Durante's exasperated pleas as his carefully laid plans collapse into hilarity.

The Jimmy Durante Show represented something extraordinary in radio's golden age: variety entertainment that celebrated the unpredictability of live performance. Durante's appeal transcended the medium itself—his distinctive personality, the famous schnoz of his own comedy, and his ability to command a room made him one of radio's most bankable stars. These episodes captured the spirit of vaudeville translated for the microphone era, where anything could happen and frequently did, keeping listeners perpetually on edge and thoroughly entertained.

If you crave authentic, unscripted laughter and want to experience why millions tuned in faithfully each week, this episode delivers the goods. Settle in with your own pre-Thanksgiving preparations and let Jimmy Durante remind you why live radio comedy remains utterly irreplaceable.
