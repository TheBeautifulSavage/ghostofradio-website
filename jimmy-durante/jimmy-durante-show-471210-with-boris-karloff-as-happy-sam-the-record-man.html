# The Jimmy Durante Show - October 12, 1947

Picture yourself huddled around a wooden radio console on a crisp autumn evening in 1947, the warm amber glow of the dial casting shadows across your living room. Jimmy "The Schnoz" Durante's unmistakable gravelly voice crackles through the speaker with characteristic warmth and mischief as he welcomes special guest Boris Karloff—fresh from his latest horror picture—into the studio. What unfolds is pure radio magic: the master of the deadpan delivery transforms the legendary monster-maker into "Happy Sam the Record Man," a character comedy sketch that builds with perfectly timed pratfalls and verbal mishaps. Karloff, known primarily for sending shivers down spines, proves himself a consummate comedian, playing against type with impeccable comic timing. The studio audience roars with laughter; you can almost hear the delight in their applause as Durante and Karloff riff on misheard song titles and confused record orders.

This episode encapsulates why The Jimmy Durante Show remained a beloved institution throughout the 1940s—a variety program that balanced slapstick comedy with genuine affection for its audience. During the post-war years, such programs offered America a cherished escape into wholesome entertainment, mixing beloved comedians with guest stars who broke their typical public personas. Durante's refusal to be cynical and his infectious good humor made his show a reliable respite from the tensions of the era.

Tune in now and experience the golden age of radio comedy in its finest form. This is entertainment as it was meant to be heard—live, spontaneous, and utterly delightful.
