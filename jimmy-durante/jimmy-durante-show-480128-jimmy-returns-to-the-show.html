# The Jimmy Durante Show - Episode 480128

Tune in as "The Schnoz" himself makes a triumphant return to the microphone in this delightful 1948 episode, "Jimmy Returns To The Show." After a brief hiatus that had left his devoted fans counting the days, Durante bursts back onto the NBC stage with all the infectious energy and comedic mayhem audiences had desperately missed. You can almost hear the roar of the studio audience as the unmistakable rasp of his voice fills the airwaves, launching into rapid-fire jokes and musical interludes that showcase why he remained one of America's most beloved entertainers. This episode captures the electric atmosphere of live radio at its peak—the ad-libbing, the orchestra swells, the perfectly timed gags—all building to a climactic musical number that will have you grinning ear to ear.

The Jimmy Durante Show stands as a monument to the golden age of NBC and CBS radio comedy, representing that precious moment when variety entertainment was king and a single performer could command a nation's attention. Durante's irreverent style, his theatrical zaniness, and his gift for making audiences feel like they were in the room with him helped define the era. By the late 1940s, as television loomed on the horizon, these radio shows represented the last flowering of a uniquely American entertainment tradition—live, spontaneous, and utterly dependent on vocal performance and comedic timing. This particular episode serves as a perfect snapshot of that world at its most confident and assured.

Don't miss your chance to experience Durante in his element, reminding listeners everywhere why he was "The Man of a Thousand Laughs." Adjust your dial and settle in for an evening of genuine, unscripted comedy gold.
