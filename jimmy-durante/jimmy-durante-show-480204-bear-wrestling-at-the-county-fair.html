# Jimmy Durante Show: Bear Wrestling At The County Fair

Step right up, folks, and prepare yourself for mayhem of the most magnificent kind! In this uproarious April 1942 broadcast, Jimmy Durante takes his whole cavalcade of zaniness down to the county fair, where chaos erupts when a traveling circus bear gets loose among the livestock and carnival games. You'll hear the unmistakable, hilarious nasal voice of "The Schnozzola" as he tangles with the bewildered beast, complete with sound effects of tumbling hay bales, startled livestock, and Durante's signature sputtering dialogue. The sketches that follow are pure vaudeville gold—complete with mistaken identities, slapstick mishaps, and the kind of rapid-fire comedy banter that made Durante a household name. There's also a musical number from the show's talented orchestra that'll have you humming for days.

The Jimmy Durante Show represents a crucial moment in American entertainment history, when radio was the nation's primary source of laughter and escape during wartime. Durante himself was a genuine star—a veteran of vaudeville stages and Broadway—and he brought that refined comic energy to NBC's airwaves during the Golden Age of radio. This particular episode, with its country fair setting, tapped into the nostalgia and Americana that audiences craved during the 1940s, offering a brief respite from wartime anxiety through wholesome, family-friendly humor that somehow never felt saccharine.

Don't miss your chance to experience one of radio's true comedic treasures. Tune in and let Jimmy Durante's irrepressible charm transport you back to an era when laughter traveled through the airwaves and into living rooms across America. This is comedy the way it was meant to be experienced—live, unpredictable, and absolutely unforgettable.
