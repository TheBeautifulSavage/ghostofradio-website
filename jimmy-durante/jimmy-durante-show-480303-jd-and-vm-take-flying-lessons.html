# Jimmy Durante Show 480303 - JD and VM Take Flying Lessons

Picture this: it's a brisk March evening in 1948, and across living rooms from coast to coast, families are settling in with their radios as that unmistakable gravelly voice crackles through the speaker—"Cha cha cha cha!" Jimmy Durante's signature laugh announces another evening of unbridled comedic mayhem. Tonight, the old schnozzola himself and his partner Vivian Vance are about to climb aboard an imaginary aircraft, and if you know Jimmy, this flying lesson is going to be anything but routine. Expect slapstick dialogue performed at breakneck speed, musical interludes that suddenly veer into chaos, and the kind of sound effects that made radio comedy an art form. The orchestra swells, the studio audience roars with anticipation, and somewhere over the airwaves, comedic disaster is about to take flight.

The Jimmy Durante Show was radio's answer to pure, unfiltered entertainment—a vaudeville-inflected variety program that thrived during the post-war boom when American audiences were hungry for escape and laughter. Durante, who'd spent decades honing his craft on the stage, brought that same physical comedy sensibility to the microphone, relying on vocal timing, sound design, and chemistry with his cast to conjure elaborate visual gags listeners could see with their minds. These episodes represent a golden moment in broadcasting when radio could still command the nation's collective attention, before television would eventually claim its crown.

Don't miss this chance to experience one of radio's greatest entertainers at the height of his powers. Tune in and let Jimmy's irrepressible energy transport you back to an era when comedy was live, immediate, and wonderfully unpredictable. Pure vintage entertainment awaits.
