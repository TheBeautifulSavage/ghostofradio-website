# Jimmy Durante Show 480310: Jimmy Runs For Vice President With Van Johnson

Picture this: it's March 10th, 1948, and America's most beloved schnozzola, Jimmy Durante, has decided the nation needs him in the highest offices of power. In this hilarious romp, Durante throws his fedora into the political ring as a surprise vice-presidential candidate, dragging the charming Van Johnson along as his bewildered campaign manager. What ensues is pure comedic mayhem—expect rapid-fire one-liners, Durante's infectious laugh bouncing between the microphones, and elaborate sight gags that listeners can practically *see* through the airwaves. The orchestra swells with patriotic flourishes that quickly devolve into chaos, while the live studio audience roars with approval at every absurdist campaign promise and political parody. It's escapist entertainment at its finest, a perfect remedy for post-war America seeking laughs and levity in 1948.

The Jimmy Durante Show represented everything audiences loved about radio's golden age: a star personality with genuine charisma hosting a variety program that blended sketch comedy, music, and guest stars in unpredictable combinations. Durante's raspy voice and vaudeville roots gave the show an authenticity that younger comedians couldn't match; he was old-school charm filtered through modern broadcast technology. The program's success on both NBC and CBS demonstrated Durante's enduring appeal across networks and demographics. Episodes like this one—topical, absurdist, yet deeply anchored in Durante's particular brand of everyman humor—captured the optimism and irreverence of late 1940s America.

Don't miss this rollicking political satire preserved from radio's greatest era. Tune in and discover why Jimmy Durante was called the "Schnoz" with affection by millions, and why Van Johnson's bewilderment makes the perfect foil for Durante's unstoppable comic energy.
