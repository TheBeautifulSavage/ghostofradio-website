# The Jimmy Durante Show: "Don Ameche Has Wife Problems"

Picture yourself huddled around the radio on a spring evening in 1944, the warm glow of the tube casting dancing shadows across your living room. Tonight, the unmistakable raspy voice of Jimmy Durante, "The Schnoz" himself, promises another evening of uproarious comedy and musical merriment. But there's a special treat in store: guest star Don Ameche, the matinee idol himself, arrives bearing tales of domestic discord that will send audiences roaring with laughter. As the script unfolds, Ameche finds himself caught in Durante's masterful web of comedic chaos—a barrage of wisecracks, slapstick misdirection, and that signature Durante charm that somehow makes even the most absurd situations feel warmly human. The supporting cast plays beautifully off these titans of entertainment, while the studio orchestra punctuates every joke with perfectly timed musical stabs and comedic underscoring.

Durante's variety show represented the golden pinnacle of radio comedy, a weekly institution where millions of Americans escaped the anxieties of wartime for an hour of pure, wholesome entertainment. This particular episode captures the show at its height, when Durante's blend of vaudeville sensibility and radio savvy had made him a genuine national treasure. His ability to work with fellow stars—drawing out their comedic timing while keeping himself at the center of the mayhem—set the template for talk and variety radio that would echo through the medium's remaining decades.

If you've never experienced the particular magic of a classic Jimmy Durante broadcast, this episode offers the perfect entry point: genuine star power, sophisticated comedy writing, and that ineffable quality of liveness that only radio could deliver. Tune in and discover why audiences made Durante's show essential listening, night after night, week after week.
