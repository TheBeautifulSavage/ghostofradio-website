# John Steele, Adventurer: "Cargo Unknown"

A ship cuts through midnight waters off the coast of Singapore, and John Steele finds himself trapped below deck with nothing but the sound of waves against the hull and the unmistakable ticking of a mysterious package. In this July 1949 episode, our globe-trotting adventurer discovers that sometimes the most dangerous cargo isn't what you can see—it's what you can only hear in the dark. As the crew above grows increasingly suspicious and the clock counts down to an unknown hour, Steele must use every ounce of cunning and courage to unravel the truth before the ship becomes a floating coffin. The tension crackles through the airwaves as accents shift between English officers, suspicious dock workers, and shadowy figures who seem to know far more than they should.

*John Steele, Adventurer* arrived on the Mutual network just as post-war audiences hungered for escapism and international intrigue. In the late 1940s, with the world still catching its breath after global conflict, listeners found themselves captivated by tales of a sophisticated American operative navigating exotic locales and improbable circumstances. The show's strength lay in its commitment to believable danger—Steele was neither superhero nor bumbling fool, but rather a resourceful man perpetually out of his depth and rising magnificently to meet it. Each episode was a tightly constructed puzzle wrapped in atmosphere, where sound design and sparse but evocative dialogue did the work of thousand-word descriptions.

If you've never experienced John Steele's world of perilous voyages and moral ambiguity, "Cargo Unknown" is the perfect place to begin. Tune in and let yourself drift into a world where every shadow conceals possibility and every choice carries weight.
