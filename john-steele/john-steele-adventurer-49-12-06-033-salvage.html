# John Steele, Adventurer: "Salvage" (December 6, 1949)

Deep in the South China Sea, fortune and peril wait beneath the waves. When John Steele receives word of a sunken merchant vessel laden with cargo worth a king's ransom, he assembles a crew of cutthroats and dreamers for one last, desperate salvage operation. But the wreck isn't just a prize—it's a death trap. Rival treasure hunters circle like sharks, local authorities demand their cut, and the very ocean seems determined to keep its secrets buried. As Steele descends into the murky depths, his wits and nerve will be tested against impossible odds. Can he claim the salvage before his competitors beat him to it, or will the sea claim him instead? The tension crackles through every scene in this vintage adventure tale, where betrayal lurks around every corner and a man's greed can be his undoing.

*John Steele, Adventurer* arrived on the Mutual Broadcasting System just as post-war audiences craved escapism and exotic thrills. The show captured that golden age of adventure radio when a character's resourcefulness and quick thinking could overcome any obstacle. Broadcasting from 1949 to 1952, the series drew listeners into a world of international intrigue and high-stakes danger, with Steele as the quintessential wanderer—charming, ruthless, and forever one step ahead of disaster. Each episode was a masterclass in sound design and dramatic pacing, transporting millions of listeners far from their ordinary lives.

Tune in now and experience the roar of the ocean, the tension of conflicting ambitions, and the cunning mind of John Steele as he navigates treacherous waters both literal and human. This is radio adventure at its finest—a thrilling reminder of when imagination and a quality script could transport an entire nation.
