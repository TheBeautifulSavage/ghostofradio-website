# John Steele, Adventurer: "Dead Ringer"

When our intrepid correspondent John Steele arrives in a fog-shrouded European port city with nothing but a cryptic telegram and a nagging sense of danger, he discovers that his closest friend has been murdered—and someone is using that grim fact to frame *him* for the crime. In this taut episode of breathless escapes and midnight interrogations, Steele must navigate a labyrinth of false identities, double-crosses, and deadly mistaken assumptions to clear his name before the authorities close in. The title says it all: somewhere in the shadowy streets of this ancient city, a dead ringer for our hero is being used as the perfect instrument of betrayal. Can Steele stay one step ahead of both the law and the real killer, or will he become another unsolved mystery lost to the fog?

*John Steele, Adventurer* thrived during the golden age of radio adventure, when audiences craved exotic locales and morally complex heroes operating in the murky spaces between right and wrong. This 1949 episode exemplifies the show's greatest strengths—crisp dialogue, genuine suspense, and a protagonist who relies on his wits as much as his fists. Broadcast over the Mutual network during the height of the Cold War's paranoia, the series captured America's fascination with international intrigue and the lonely American abroad, operating in worlds where trust is a luxury and survival depends on quick thinking.

Whether you're a devoted fan of vintage radio adventure or discovering this gem for the first time, "Dead Ringer" delivers everything that made *John Steele* essential listening for millions of Americans tuning in after dinner. Adjust your dial, dim the lights, and prepare for an evening of genuine suspense—because in John Steele's world, everyone's got secrets, and the truth is always more dangerous than the lie.
