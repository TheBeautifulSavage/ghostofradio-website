# John Steele, Adventurer: "The First Stone"

December 26, 1950 — A tense night in Cairo as John Steele finds himself ensnared in a deadly game of international intrigue where trust is a luxury no man can afford. When a mysterious artifact surfaces in the shadow of the pyramids, our intrepid adventurer must navigate a labyrinth of double-crossing operatives, black market dealers, and shadowy government interests all vying for possession. The stakes climb as each player reveals their hand, and Steele discovers that the "first stone" may be the key to something far larger—and more sinister—than anyone anticipated. You'll feel the suffocating heat of the desert night, hear the whispered negotiations in darkened bazaars, and sense the cold calculation behind every handshake as alliances form and shatter like glass.

*John Steele, Adventurer* thrived during those golden years of network radio when audiences hungered for tales of derring-do beyond their living room windows. Broadcast over the Mutual network from 1949 to 1952, the show captured the postwar era's fascination with espionage and international intrigue, offering listeners a sophisticated protagonist who could match wits with agents, diplomats, and criminals across the globe. Unlike the pulpier adventure serials that preceded it, *John Steele* brought a touch of realism and moral ambiguity to the format—here was a hero who sometimes bent the rules, who navigated gray zones, and whose victories came at genuine cost. "The First Stone" exemplifies this approach, delivering not just surface-level excitement but genuine geopolitical tension.

Don't miss this masterclass in old-time radio craftsmanship. Settle in with the static, let your imagination conjure Cairo's shadowed streets, and discover why *John Steele, Adventurer* remains a crown jewel of the adventure genre.
