# John Steele, Adventurer: "The Sixth Bullet"

Picture this: the year is 1951, and you're settling into your favorite chair as the familiar theme music swells through your radio speaker. John Steele, that globe-trotting man of mystery and action, finds himself in a deadly game of cat-and-mouse where every second counts and trust is the rarest commodity of all. In "The Sixth Bullet," our hero must navigate a shadowy web of espionage and betrayal across the exotic ports and hidden corners of the Mediterranean. Five bullets have already been fired. Five have found their marks. But who holds the gun that houses the sixth—and is John Steele truly the intended target, or merely a pawn in a far more sinister game? The tension crackles through every word of dialogue as Steele's quick wit and quicker reflexes are tested against adversaries who remain hidden in shadow and whisper.

John Steele, Adventurer captured the imagination of post-war American audiences hungry for excitement and international intrigue. Broadcasting on the Mutual network during the height of radio's golden age, the show delivered exotic locales and sophisticated danger to living rooms across the nation, offering listeners an escape into a world where danger lurked around every corner and fortune favored the bold. The character embodied a particular brand of 1950s masculine sophistication—urbane, resourceful, and unflappable in the face of impossible odds. These episodes represent a vanishing era when sound and storytelling alone could transport millions to far-flung adventures.

This is classic adventure radio at its finest—sharp dialogue, breathless pacing, and a protagonist you'll root for every step of the way. Don't miss "The Sixth Bullet." Tune in and discover why John Steele became a legend.
