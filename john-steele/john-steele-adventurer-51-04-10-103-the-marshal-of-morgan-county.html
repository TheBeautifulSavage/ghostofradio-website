# John Steele, Adventurer: The Marshal of Morgan County

As the opening theme swells with exotic brass and that unmistakable call of "John Steele, Adventurer!", listeners in October 1951 would have settled into their favorite chair for a tale of frontier justice gone terribly wrong. In this episode, our hero finds himself in the dusty badlands of Morgan County, where a corrupt marshal has turned his authority into a weapon against innocent settlers. With nothing but his wits, a loaded revolver, and an unwavering moral compass, Steele must navigate a web of blackmail, false accusations, and frontier vengeance. The episode crackles with tension—you can almost hear the creaking saddle leather and the ominous wind whistling through canyon passes as Steele uncovers the marshal's darkest secrets. Will justice prevail, or will our adventurer become another unmarked grave in the desert?

*John Steele, Adventurer* occupied a unique space in the golden age of radio drama. Unlike the more fantastical adventure serials of the era, the show grounded itself in plausible international and domestic scenarios, treating its audience as intelligent adults craving sophisticated storytelling. The Mutual Network's production values were exceptional, employing sound effects wizards who could convincingly recreate everything from monsoon rains to gunfights in saloons. This particular episode showcases what made the series special: its willingness to grapple with real American corruption and moral ambiguity, even while maintaining the thrilling pacing of a Saturday matinee adventure.

Don't miss this classic installment—tune in and experience why *John Steele, Adventurer* remains one of radio's most underrated gems. Your evening won't be complete without it.
