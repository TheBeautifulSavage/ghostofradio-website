# John Steele, Adventurer: "Counterpoint"

Picture yourself in a smoky living room on a December evening in 1949, the radio's warm glow casting shadows across eager faces as the familiar theme swells through the speaker. In this installment, "Counterpoint," our intrepid adventurer finds himself entangled in a dangerous game of espionage in post-war Vienna, where the lines between allies and adversaries blur like fog along the Danube. John Steele must navigate a web of double agents, stolen documents, and a mysterious woman whose loyalties remain tantalizingly unclear. The tension crackles through every tense dialogue exchange—will our hero's quick wits and steady nerve be enough when he discovers that his most trusted contact may be working for the very forces he's sworn to oppose? The clock is ticking, and in the shadows of occupied Europe, time is a luxury John Steele simply cannot afford.

*John Steele, Adventurer* thrived during radio's golden age by tapping into post-war anxieties and the public's insatiable appetite for international intrigue. The show premiered on the Mutual Broadcasting System at a moment when listeners were still adjusting to a new world order—when yesterday's enemies occupied neighboring zones and tomorrow's conflicts seemed inevitable. Unlike the comic book heroics of pulp adventure, Steele's world felt disturbingly plausible, grounded in real geopolitics and the moral ambiguity of the Cold War's early days. This episode exemplifies the show's sophisticated approach, refusing simple patriotic cheerleading in favor of complex scenarios where nobody's hands stayed entirely clean.

Don't miss this masterclass in radio drama. Tune in now to experience one of adventure fiction's finest hours—where danger lurks around every corner and trust becomes the most valuable commodity of all.
