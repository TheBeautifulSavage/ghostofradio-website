# John Steele, Adventurer: The False Door (Adventure in Cairo)

Picture yourself in a smoky Cairo nightclub, where moonlight filters through latticed windows and the air itself seems to pulse with danger. John Steele, globe-trotting operative extraordinaire, finds himself entangled in a web of deception that runs deeper than the Nile itself. An ancient tomb holds secrets that powerful forces will kill to keep hidden, and a mysterious false door stands between our hero and certain death. With only his wits, his revolver, and the crackling static of shortwave radio to guide him, Steele must navigate the labyrinthine streets of Cairo's old quarter while Cairo's shadowy underworld closes in. Every shadow conceals a potential enemy; every conversation could be his last. The intricate sound design transports you directly to the bazaars and temples of Egypt, where the past refuses to stay buried and the present demands blood.

*John Steele, Adventurer* captured the post-war imagination of American listeners with its globe-trotting escapades and larger-than-life protagonist, airing across the Mutual network during those golden years when radio adventure serials dominated the airwaves. This particular episode, broadcast in July 1949, exemplifies the show's signature formula: exotic locales, archaeological intrigue, and the kind of breathless pacing that kept families huddled around their receivers. The writing crackles with period authenticity while maintaining the fantastical adventure spirit that made the show indispensable entertainment during the late 1940s.

Don't miss this thrilling chapter in John Steele's remarkable career. Tune in and join thousands of listeners who discovered that the greatest adventures were always just a radio dial away. The False Door awaits—but be warned: in Cairo, nothing is quite what it seems.
