# John Steele, Adventurer: "Three Minus Two"

Picture this: the crackling of a shortwave radio cutting through static, a single trumpet note piercing the darkness, and then that distinctive baritone—"This is John Steele, Adventurer." In this December 1949 episode, our intrepid hero finds himself entangled in a deadly equation of espionage and betrayal somewhere in the shadowed corners of postwar Europe. With only cryptic clues and his wits as weapons, Steele must unravel the meaning of "three minus two" before time runs out. The tension mounts as he discovers that what initially appears to be a simple smuggling operation conceals a far more sinister plot—one that could reshape the balance of power in a world still reeling from the ashes of global conflict. Every footstep echoes with danger, every telephone call might be his last, and trust is a luxury Steele cannot afford.

*John Steele, Adventurer* thrived during the golden age of radio drama, when millions of Americans tuned in to follow their heroes across impossible landscapes and into impossible situations. The show captured perfectly that post-war hunger for excitement and reassurance—proof that American courage and quick thinking could triumph in any corner of the globe. With scripts that balanced genuine international intrigue against the theatrical flourishes audiences craved, *John Steele* became a fixture on the Mutual Broadcasting System, spawning episodes that continue to crackle with authenticity and urgency decades later.

This is radio drama at its finest: no visual distractions, just superb storytelling and sound design that paints the entire scene in your imagination. Tune in and let yourself be transported back to an era when adventure came through a simple speaker, and a hero's fate hung on solving one man's cryptic puzzle. Three minus two—but what does it mean?
