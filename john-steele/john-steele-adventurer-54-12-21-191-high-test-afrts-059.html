# John Steele, Adventurer — Episode 59: "High Test Afrit"

Picture this: the year is 1949, and you've settled into your favorite chair as the familiar theme music crackles through your radio speaker. Tonight, adventurer John Steele finds himself in the sweltering deserts of North Africa, where ancient folklore collides with modern danger. A sinister force—an afrit, the malevolent spirit of Islamic legend—seems to be terrorizing an isolated outpost, leaving rational men gasping in terror. But is it supernatural menace or something far more earthly and cunning? As Steele navigates shadowy bazaars and forbidden temples, you'll grip the armrest of your chair, every sound effect—the howl of desert winds, the clash of steel, the strangled cries of men—pulling you deeper into the mystery. With each commercial break, you'll be left wondering: can even the resourceful John Steele survive forces beyond the mortal realm?

*John Steele, Adventurer* represented the golden age of radio adventure programming, when the Mutual Broadcasting System brought globe-trotting thrills into American living rooms three nights a week. The show masterfully blended genuine historical and geographical detail with pulp fiction excitement, allowing listeners to vicariously explore exotic locales without leaving home. In 1949, with post-war America eager for escapism, these tales of international intrigue and derring-do struck a perfect chord. The program's writers drew inspiration from real expeditions and archaeological discoveries, grounding their fantastical scenarios in just enough authenticity to make the impossible feel tantalizingly plausible.

Step through the static and into a world of peril and wonder. Tune in to *John Steele, Adventurer* and discover why audiences made this show an appointment with destiny. Who knows what dangers await?
