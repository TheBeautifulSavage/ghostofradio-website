# John Steele, Adventurer: "Starboard Light"

Picture yourself huddled around the radio on a crackling evening in 1950, the amber dial glowing warmly in the darkness of your living room. As the familiar opening theme swells—that infectious brass fanfare that signals adventure on distant shores—you settle in for tonight's episode: "Starboard Light." John Steele finds himself aboard a merchant vessel cutting through treacherous waters, where a mysterious passenger holds secrets that could reshape international politics. A coded message, a suspicious crew member, and a rendezvous planned for midnight create tension that builds with each passing minute. You'll hear the authentic sounds of the ship groaning around you—the slap of waves against the hull, the rhythmic clanging of the engine room—as our intrepid hero uncovers a conspiracy that reaches from the docks of Shanghai to the highest corridors of power. This is vintage Mutual Network adventure radio at its finest.

*John Steele, Adventurer* emerged during the golden age of radio, when Americans hungered for escapism and thrills in the postwar years. The show's appeal lay in its sophisticated international intrigue—far removed from the superhero serials that dominated the airways—and its grounding in real-world geopolitics. Each episode transported listeners across continents and oceans with remarkable sound design, creating an almost cinematic experience in the listener's imagination. The show's writers understood that their audience craved intelligent storytelling alongside danger and excitement.

If you've never experienced the particular thrill of classic adventure radio, "Starboard Light" offers the perfect entry point. Tune in and let yourself be transported back to an era when radio commanded the nation's attention and imagination, when mystery and danger waited at the turn of every commercial break.
