# The Macormack Matter (Part 2)

Johnny Dollar's shoes echo through the rain-slicked streets of a nameless coastal town as our intrepid insurance investigator finds himself tangled in a web of deception that grows darker by the minute. In this second installment, broadcast on October 4th, 1955, Dollar must navigate between a mysterious widow's half-truths, a dockside operator with everything to hide, and a claim that refuses to add up. Bob Bailey's measured, world-weary voice guides listeners through shadowy hotel corridors and smoky back rooms where every conversation could be a trap. The stakes have multiplied overnight—what began as routine policy fraud now carries the unmistakable scent of something far more sinister. With only his wits and a determination to uncover the truth, Johnny Dollar inches closer to the heart of the Macormack matter, though danger lurks in the spaces between each clue.

This episode represents *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* at the height of its powers, during the golden age when radio noir captivated millions of American listeners. Bob Bailey's portrayal of the working-class detective set a standard for the genre—a man neither superhero nor saint, but driven by professional integrity and genuine curiosity. The show's focus on the procedural elements of investigation, combined with believable dialogue and atmospheric sound design, created something revolutionary: a mystery that rewarded careful listening, where every detail mattered and the audience pieced together the puzzle alongside their hero.

For those seeking an authentic window into 1950s entertainment, when a single broadcast could hold an entire household transfixed, *The Macormack Matter Part 2* delivers the goods. Tune in and discover why millions tuned in week after week.
