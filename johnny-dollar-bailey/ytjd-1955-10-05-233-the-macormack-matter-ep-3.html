# Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar: The Macormack Matter (Part 3)

The rain hammers against the windows of a shabby Manhattan office as private investigator Johnny Dollar finds himself trapped between a desperate widow and a web of forged documents that could bury an entire shipping empire. In this third installment of *The Macormack Matter*, our hero must navigate the shadowy underworld of insurance fraud where every clue leads to another dead end—and every contact might be his last. With his expense account running hot and time running cold, Johnny's sharp wit and sharper instincts are all that stand between the truth and a perfectly executed frame job. Listeners will find themselves swept into a tense game of cat-and-mouse through the neon-lit streets of 1955 New York, where corruption lurks in the highest corporate offices and danger waits in the most unexpected places.

This episode represents *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* at its finest—a show that brought American radio audiences something they craved during the postwar years: a tough-talking everyman hero operating in a morally ambiguous world. Bob Bailey's portrayal of the expense-account-conscious investigator became iconic precisely because it married hard-boiled detective fiction with relatable humanity; Johnny worried about his per diem as much as solving crimes. CBS's commitment to producing these detailed, multi-part cases demonstrated radio's enduring power as a storytelling medium, even as television's shadow began creeping across the medium in the mid-1950s.

Don't miss this gripping installment where Johnny Dollar's investigation reaches its critical turning point. Tune in and discover why this series remained one of radio's most compelling offerings—where every case costs more than expected, and the price of truth can be measured in more than just dollars.
