# The Molly K Matter – Part One

The fog rolls thick over the San Francisco docks as insurance investigator Johnny Dollar steps into a case that promises danger at every turn. When a valuable cargo mysteriously vanishes from the hold of the fishing vessel *Molly K*, Johnny finds himself entangled in a web of maritime intrigue, double-crossing dock workers, and suspects who have everything to lose. Bob Bailey's gravelly voice guides you through shadowy warehouses and smoky interrogation rooms, where every answer spawns three new questions. The salty sea air seems to drift through your radio speaker as Johnny navigates this noir landscape, his quick wit and sharper instincts the only tools keeping him alive. Part one of this gripping two-part adventure sets the hook immediately—by the final fade-out, you'll be counting the days until the conclusion.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* represents the golden age of radio drama at its finest, arriving at CBS in 1955 just as television threatened to make the medium obsolete. Yet the show thrived, running until 1960, because it offered something visual entertainment simply couldn't match: the ability to conjure entire worlds in the listener's imagination. Bob Bailey became the definitive Johnny Dollar, a character who'd appeared in earlier radio incarnations but found his truest expression in Bailey's capable hands. The "Molly K Matter" episodes exemplify why the series became a benchmark for the insurance investigator subgenre, blending hard-boiled detective fiction with genuine maritime atmosphere and authentic procedural detail.

Tune in now and rediscover why audiences abandoned their evening plans to follow Johnny Dollar's investigations. This is radio drama as it was meant to be experienced—thrilling, atmospheric, and utterly captivating.
