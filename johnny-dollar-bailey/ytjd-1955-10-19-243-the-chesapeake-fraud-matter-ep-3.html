# The Chesapeake Fraud Matter (Part 3)

As fog rolls thick across the Chesapeake Bay and ship horns keen mournfully in the darkness, insurance investigator Johnny Dollar finds himself trapped in a web of maritime deception that grows more sinister with each passing hour. In this third installment of "The Chesapeake Fraud Matter," our weary operative must navigate shadowy dockside warehouses and questionable witnesses whose stories shift like the tides themselves. A valuable shipment has vanished, insurance claims mount suspiciously, and Johnny—armed with nothing but his wits, his expense account, and his unflinching determination—must separate truth from fabrication before a killer decides he knows too much. Bob Bailey's cigarette-rough voice crackles with barely contained tension as Johnny squares off against forces far more dangerous than mere fraud.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* represented the golden age of radio storytelling in the 1950s, when episodic drama could weave complex narratives across multiple nights, keeping listeners hunched over their sets night after night. The show's genius lay in its documentary-style realism—Johnny's meticulous case files, his actual expenses read aloud, his matter-of-fact narration—creating an illusion of authenticity that made every clink of a glass or footstep on wet pavement feel utterly present. Bailey's portrayal of a rumpled, competent investigator became the template for hard-boiled radio heroes, influencing decades of detective fiction to follow.
