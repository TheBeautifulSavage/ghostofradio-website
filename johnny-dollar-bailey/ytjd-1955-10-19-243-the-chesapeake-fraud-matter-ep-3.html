# The Chesapeake Fraud Matter, Part 3

As autumn fog rolls thick across the Maryland waterfront, insurance investigator Johnny Dollar finds himself knee-deep in a case that smells worse than the brackish waters of Chesapeake Bay. A shipping company's cargo has vanished—or has it?—and with each clue Dollar uncovers, the line between legitimate loss and calculated deception grows murkier. In this third installment of the Chesapeake affair, Bob Bailey's world-weary voice guides you through dimly lit warehouses and smoke-filled interrogation rooms where everyone has something to hide and no one is telling the whole truth. The tension crackles like the interference on a ship's radio as Dollar closes in on the mastermind, but first he must navigate a maze of false leads, double-crosses, and dangerous men who'd prefer he stop asking questions altogether.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* stands as one of radio's most sophisticated detective programs, and Bailey's portrayal of the titular investigator—a man driven by integrity but weary from a lifetime of human deception—became the show's beating heart during its CBS run in the mid-1950s. Rather than relying on heroic bravado or supernatural gimmickry, the program grounded itself in procedural realism and moral ambiguity, treating insurance fraud with the gravity of murder. Each episode was designed to unfold in real-time over three or four installments, allowing listeners to genuinely wonder alongside Dollar whether the evidence would lead where it seemed to point. This multi-part format was radical for the era, demanding listener loyalty and engagement.

Don't miss the conclusion of Dollar's Chesapeake investigation. Tune in for this October 1955 broadcast and discover whether our hero will crack the case—or whether the truth is far more complicated than anyone imagined.
