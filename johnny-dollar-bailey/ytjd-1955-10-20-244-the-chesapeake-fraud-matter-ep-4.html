# The Chesapeake Fraud Matter (Episode 4)

As fog rolls thick across the Baltimore waterfront and ship horns moan like distant ghosts, Johnny Dollar finds himself neck-deep in a case that stinks of saltwater and deception. The fourth chapter of "The Chesapeake Fraud Matter" pulls you into a maze of shipping manifests, suspicious insurance claims, and characters with more secrets than a dockside speakeasy. Bob Bailey's weathered voice guides you through shadowy warehouses and dimly-lit offices where every handshake might be the last thing you feel before trouble finds you. What began as a routine investigation has spiraled into something darker—someone's lying, someone's stealing, and someone might just be willing to kill to keep the truth buried beneath the Chesapeake's murky waters.

In an era when television was still finding its footing, *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* reigned as CBS's answer to the hunger for intelligent, adult mystery programming. This 1955 episode comes from the show's golden period, when Bob Bailey had perfected his portrayal of the cynical-yet-honest insurance investigator—a blue-collar everyman with a sharp mind and sharper instincts. The episode's serialized structure was deliberately crafted to hook listeners night after night, a storytelling technique that would influence television's later golden age. The writing crackles with period authenticity, the sound design creates a fully realized world through nothing but voices and effects, and Bailey's performance remains a masterclass in radio acting—restrained, believable, thoroughly American.
