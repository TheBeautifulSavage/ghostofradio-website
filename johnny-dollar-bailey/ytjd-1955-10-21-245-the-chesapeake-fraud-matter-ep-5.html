# The Chesapeake Fraud Matter – Part Five

As Johnny Dollar steps off the midnight train into the fog-shrouded docks of Baltimore, he knows the case is closing in—but not before one final, dangerous turn. The Chesapeake Fraud matter has already cost him three sleepless nights and more than a few bruised ribs, but the real trouble is just beginning. In this climactic fifth episode, airing October 21st, 1955, listeners will find themselves caught between competing lies: a shipping magnate's suspicious alibi, a widow's desperate claim, and a $50,000 insurance payout that doesn't add up. The rain hammers against warehouse windows as Dollar pieces together the final clues, his gravelly narration cutting through the noir darkness with the precision of a man who knows insurance fraud the way a detective knows the city's back alleys.

This is the Johnny Dollar listeners tuned in for week after week—not a superhero, but a working man solving real crimes with nothing but his wits, a stubborn conscience, and a pocket notebook. Bob Bailey's portrayal became the definitive voice of the expense-account detective, making *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* the most successful insurance investigation series radio ever produced. Between 1949 and 1962, Bailey brought over 600 cases to life across multiple networks, but these CBS years (1955-1960) represent the show at its peak, when the writing was sharpest and the mysteries most intricate. Each episode was a complete case, serialized throughout the week—a format that kept America hooked.

Don't miss the resolution to the Chesapeake mystery. Tune in for *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* and discover how one man's integrity stands against the tide of greed and deception—one case, one night, one carefully documented expense at a time.
