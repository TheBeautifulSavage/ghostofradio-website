# The Alvin Summers Matter, Part Three

The rain hammers against the windows of a Chicago hotel room as Johnny Dollar closes in on the truth—or so he thinks. In this third installment of "The Alvin Summers Matter," our wisecracking insurance investigator finds himself ensnared in a web of double-crosses and hidden motives that grow more tangled with each passing moment. Bob Bailey's world-weary voice cuts through the static and orchestral swells as Johnny pieces together contradictory testimonies, forged documents, and the uncomfortable reality that everyone he's encountered has something to hide. The forty-five-minute investigation that CBS's listeners had come to crave builds toward a revelation that promises to rewrite everything Johnny thought he knew about the case—and raise troubling questions about who's really paying him to find the truth.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* was the thinking listener's answer to hard-boiled detective radio. Unlike the shoot-'em-up adventures that cluttered the airwaves, creator/writer Jack Moyles crafted a show built on procedural detail and psychological complexity, where the real mystery was human nature itself. Bob Bailey's five-year tenure in the role (1955-1960) represented the show's golden age, when the "Man with the Action-Packed Expense Account" became less archetype and more flesh-and-blood cynic navigating a morally ambiguous 1950s America. Each multi-part case unfolded like a serialized short story, rewarding devoted listeners with narrative sophistication rarely heard in commercial radio.

Don your fedora and step into the noir-soaked world of mid-century insurance investigation. "The Alvin Summers Matter" awaits—three parts of mystery, misdirection, and the kind of dialogue that crackles like a live wire. Tune in and discover why *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* remains the gold standard of detective radio drama.
