# Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar: The Alvin Summers Matter (Part 4)

The case has twisted again, and Johnny Dollar finds himself deeper in the murky waters of deception than ever before. In this fourth installment of "The Alvin Summers Matter," our intrepid insurance investigator navigates a web of lies, misdirection, and carefully guarded secrets—each thread pulling him closer to a truth that someone desperately wants to keep buried. Bob Bailey's distinctive voice drips with world-weary determination as Johnny inches toward the heart of this elaborate scheme, each clue raising more questions than it answers. The atmosphere crackles with tension; you can almost hear the rain-slicked streets of the city and feel the suffocating pressure of a case that refuses to break cleanly. Whether you've been following Johnny's investigation from the beginning or you're jumping in now, this episode delivers the precise blend of sharp dialogue, genuine menace, and methodical detective work that made this show legendary.

"Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar" stands as one of broadcasting's finest achievements—a show that elevated the insurance investigator premise into genuine noir artistry. Running from 1955 to 1960, the series broke new ground by utilizing the "transcribed narrative" format, with Johnny directly addressing the audience as he recounts cases with an authenticity that pulled listeners into his private office. Bob Bailey's performances became the gold standard for the genre, his delivery capable of shifting from comedic timing to hard-boiled gravitas within a single sentence. This particular episode, from October 27th, 1955, captures the show at its creative peak, when writer-producers had perfected the formula of personal stakes, complex plotting, and genuine character development.
