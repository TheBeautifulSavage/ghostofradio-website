# Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar: The Alvin Summers Matter (Episode 5)

As the cigarette smoke curls through a dimly lit hotel corridor and Johnny Dollar's footsteps echo against marble floors, listeners know they're in for another evening of taut, intelligent mystery. In this fifth installment of "The Alvin Summers Matter," our insurance investigator finds himself entangled in a case that grows murkier with each revelation—a web of deception where every clue contradicts the last, and trust is a luxury no one can afford. Bob Bailey's gravelly narration guides you through the shadows with the assured confidence of a man who's seen every angle of human nature, while the precise sound design—the jingle of ice in a glass, the snap of a lighter, the distant wail of a siren—transports you directly into Johnny's world. This is the fifth chapter of an intricately woven serial arc, where the stakes have never been higher and the truth remains tantalizingly just out of reach.

What makes Johnny Dollar distinctive among radio's detective pantheon is its devotion to authentic insurance investigation rather than spectacular crime-solving. Created in an era when CBS sought sophisticated programming for discerning adults, the show eschews melodrama for procedural realism—Johnny bills by the day, travels extensively, and spends as much time examining documents as exchanging gunfire. Bob Bailey's portrayal, spanning these golden years of 1955-1960, became definitive: neither hardboiled parody nor noble paladin, but a competent professional navigating moral ambiguity with dry wit and hard-won wisdom. The serial format, with cases unfolding across five or more episodes, allowed for genuine narrative complexity rarely heard on the airwaves.

Step into the world of post-war corporate intrigue and small-town secrets. Tune in to Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar and discover why listeners made this program appointment radio—where every detail matters, and one man's judgment stands between insurance companies and elaborate fraud schemes.
