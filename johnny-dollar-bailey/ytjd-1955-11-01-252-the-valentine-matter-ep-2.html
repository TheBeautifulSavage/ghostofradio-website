# The Valentine Matter (Episode 2)

When Johnny Dollar enters the gleaming offices of the Olympus Insurance Company on this November evening in 1955, he finds himself neck-deep in a case that smells of old money, older grudges, and the kind of betrayal that makes even a seasoned investigator wince. A missing diamond brooch, a society widow's suspicious claims, and a paper trail that leads to some very uncomfortable questions—this is the stuff that keeps a man awake at three in the morning, nursing cold coffee and wondering if the next phone call will be his last. Bob Bailey's world-weary narration guides you through shadowy hotel corridors and tense interviews, where every word carries weight and danger lurks behind polite conversation. The Valentine Matter refuses to offer easy answers, pulling you deeper into a web of lies where the most convincing suspect might be the most dangerous.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* stands as one of radio's greatest achievements—a genuine novelty that placed listeners inside the mind of an insurance investigator rather than a traditional gumshoe. Where other detective shows relied on gunfire and fisticuffs, Johnny Dollar solved cases through meticulous detective work, conversation, and the kind of sharp thinking that made audiences lean closer to their receivers. Bob Bailey's portrayal became iconic during this golden CBS run, embodying a character both tough and thoroughly human, capable of cracking a case while wrestling with its moral implications. The show's emphasis on procedure and authenticity resonated with post-war audiences hungry for intelligent drama.

Tune in now to part two of *The Valentine Matter* and discover why Johnny Dollar earned his reputation as one of radio's most compelling investigators. The mystery awaits.
