# Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar: The Valentine Matter, Episode 3

Picture this: a smoke-filled office on a rain-slicked November evening in 1955, the kind of night when every shadow harbors secrets and every alibi unravels a little more. In this third installment of "The Valentine Matter," Johnny Dollar finds himself deeper in the labyrinth of a case that started with a simple claim and has spiraled into something far more sinister. Bob Bailey's weathered voice cuts through the static as our insurance investigator navigates the dark underbelly of a case involving betrayal, hidden identities, and a mysterious figure named Valentine who seems to haunt every crucial turn. As the pieces begin to fall into place, listeners will find themselves teetering on the edge of revelation—wondering which lead will prove fatal, and whether Johnny's quick wit and sharper instincts will be enough to survive what's coming next.

What makes this moment in broadcast history so captivating is that we're witnessing the golden age of CBS radio when *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* had become America's most consistently performed radio show. From 1949 through 1962, this program dominated the airwaves with a formula that never grew stale: a clever protagonist, intricate plotting, and authentic New York cynicism that made listeners feel they were inside a detective novel. Bailey's five-year run starting in 1955 represents the show at its artistic peak, with scripts that balanced hard-boiled dialogue with genuine mystery and characterization that transcended the medium's typical limitations.

This is essential listening for anyone who believes radio drama died with television's rise—here's proof that it merely transformed into something more refined. Tune in and rediscover why millions of Americans cleared their schedules for Johnny Dollar's exploits.
