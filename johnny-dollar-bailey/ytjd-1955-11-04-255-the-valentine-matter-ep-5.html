# Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar: The Valentine Matter (Part 5)

The case has gone cold, but Johnny Dollar's blood runs hot. As he closes in on the fifth installment of "The Valentine Matter," our insurance investigator finds himself navigating the shadowy corridors of post-war corruption where nothing is quite what it seems. A missing emerald, a mysterious woman, and a trail of lies stretching from the high-rise offices of Manhattan to the dimly lit corners of small-town America—all converging in an episode that crackles with tension and the unmistakable snap of dialogue that keeps you glued to your radio speaker. Bob Bailey's world-weary yet dogged Johnny Dollar peels back another layer of deception in this November broadcast, where every clue is a double-edged sword and trust is the most valuable commodity of all.

What made "Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar" a phenomenon on CBS radio was its unflinching commitment to the hard-boiled detective formula refined for the domestic insurance world. Airing during radio's golden twilight in the mid-1950s, the show offered something television couldn't yet capture—the intimate, noir-soaked atmosphere of inner monologue and perfectly timed sound effects. Bailey's portrayal became the definitive voice of the calculating, morally flexible insurance dick, a character whose expense account narration became as iconic as any protagonist in American radio. This episode, preserved from November 1955, captures the show at its peak, before the medium itself would fade into memory.
