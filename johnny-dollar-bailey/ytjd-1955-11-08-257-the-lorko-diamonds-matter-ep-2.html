# The Lorko Diamonds Matter - Episode 2

The rain hammers against the window of a dingy São Paulo hotel as Johnny Dollar's cigarette smoke curls through the darkness—somewhere in this sprawling Brazilian port city, a fortune in diamonds has vanished, and every lead pulls our intrepid insurance investigator deeper into a web of corruption, seduction, and murder. Episode two of "The Lorko Diamonds Matter" plunges listeners into the humid, dangerous heart of South American intrigue, where a $50,000 claim becomes a death sentence for those who know too much. Bob Bailey's gravelly, weary voice guides us through shadowy streets and suspicious interviews, each conversation crackling with tension as our hero inches closer to the truth—or perhaps closer to his own grave. The writing crackles with authentic period noir: smart dialogue, exotic danger, and the kind of moral ambiguity that made audiences lean closer to their radio sets each Tuesday night.

By 1955, *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* had already revolutionized the insurance investigator genre, moving beyond simple procedural storytelling into genuine psychological drama. This was radio's answer to the hardboiled detective fiction of Chandler and Hammett, but with a unique twist—every case filed a genuine insurance claim, grounding the fantasy in real-world consequence. Bob Bailey's performance as Johnny became the gold standard for the character across multiple incarnations, bringing a world-weary humanity that separated him from his two-fisted competitors. CBS's sophisticated audience craved this intelligent, atmospheric crime programming.

Don't miss a moment of this exotic adventure. Tune in for Episode 2 of "The Lorko Diamonds Matter" and discover why *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* became one of radio's most celebrated and frequently rebroadcast series—where every case matters, and every dollar counts.
