# The Lorko Diamonds Matter – Part Three

The clock tower chimes midnight as Johnny Dollar finds himself trapped in a web of deception spun across three continents. In this third installment of "The Lorko Diamonds Matter," our insurance investigator must navigate the glittering underworld of diamond smuggling, where every handshake conceals a knife and every confession hides a darker truth. Bob Bailey's world-weary narration guides you through fog-shrouded docks and exclusive hotel suites, where the trail of the missing Lorko diamonds grows colder—or perhaps more dangerous—with each passing hour. Will Johnny's quick wit and street smarts uncover the mastermind before the case goes cold, or will he become just another casualty in the high-stakes world of precious gems and precious lies?

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* stands as the epitome of radio's golden age detective series, and this 1955 episode exemplifies why audiences remained riveted throughout the show's five-year run on CBS. Unlike the heroic, seemingly invincible detectives of earlier radio fare, Johnny Dollar operates in shades of gray—a working man doing a dangerous job for a day rate, always mindful of his expenses and always aware that the next case could be his last. Bailey's distinctive delivery, combining cynical narration with genuine vulnerability, transformed the series into something more literary and sophisticated than its contemporaries, earning devoted listeners who appreciated both the intricate plotting and the authentic noir atmosphere.

Step into the shadows of 1955 and experience radio drama at its finest. *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar*—where every case has a price, and the cost is always higher than expected.
