# Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar - The Broderick Matter (Part 1)

As the CBS airwaves crackle to life on that November evening in 1955, insurance investigator Johnny Dollar steps into a case that promises danger at every turn. A missing heiress, a tangled web of family secrets, and a matter that reaches far deeper than the simple insurance claim on its surface—this is no ordinary inquiry. Bob Bailey's weathered voice guides listeners through the shadows of the investigation as Dollar begins to unravel the Broderick matter, where nothing is quite what it seems and trust becomes the rarest commodity of all. The noir atmosphere is thick and palpable: the soft shuffle of office papers, the clink of glasses in dimly lit rooms, and always that sense that someone is watching, waiting to strike. This opening installment plants the seeds of intrigue that will blossom across multiple episodes, pulling listeners into a mystery where the stakes are as high as they come.

Johnny Dollar represented something quintessentially American in the post-war years—the independent operator, the man who could navigate the complex machinery of insurance claims while uncovering the human truths beneath. This particular series, running from 1955 to 1960 with Bailey in the role, became the definitive version of the character, establishing the format that would define the show's success: complex, multi-part cases that demanded listener loyalty and rewarded it with tight plotting and genuine suspense. The Broderick Matter exemplifies this golden age of radio drama, when scripts were tight, performances were live or semi-live, and audiences gathered around their sets expecting quality storytelling.

Tune in to hear how Dollar's dogged determination and keen eye begin to pierce the veil of deception surrounding the Broderick family. This is radio drama at its finest.
