# The Broderick Matter (Episode 3)

As the CBS airwaves crackle to life on this November evening in 1955, insurance investigator Johnny Dollar finds himself deeper in quicksand than ever before. The Broderick case has taken a sinister turn—what began as a routine claim has spiraled into a web of blackmail, hidden identities, and danger that threatens to consume everyone involved. With each commercial break, the tension mounts as Johnny navigates shadowy hotel corridors and interrogates suspects who may or may not be who they claim to be. Bob Bailey's world-weary voice cuts through the static like a searchlight through fog, capturing every nuance of suspicion and determination as our hero pieces together a puzzle where one wrong move could be his last.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* represents the twilight golden age of radio drama—a moment when the medium had perfected the art of intimate storytelling, and Bob Bailey had become synonymous with the hard-boiled insurance investigator. The show's serialized format was revolutionary for its time, allowing listeners to invest deeply in continuing storylines over multiple episodes. This particular episode, airing in 1955, captures the show at its creative peak, when writers crafted intricate narratives that rewarded loyal audiences and demonstrated that radio could deliver sophisticated, adult entertainment. The sound design—rain pattering against windows, cigarette lighters clicking open, footsteps echoing in empty hallways—creates a noir atmosphere that would rival any black-and-white film of the era.

Don't miss this installment of *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar*. As Johnny Dollar himself might say, this case is worth every cent—and every minute of your time.
