# The Amy Bradshaw Matter (Part 2)

The clock strikes midnight as Johnny Dollar finds himself deeper in the mire of the Amy Bradshaw case—and deeper in danger. When our insurance investigator last left off, the pieces of this puzzle were scattered across a landscape of lies and misdirection. Now, with the second installment, the tension ratchets tighter as Johnny navigates a web of suspects whose motives shift like shadows under a street lamp. Someone is hiding something valuable, and someone else might be willing to kill to keep it hidden. With only his wits, his experience, and his Smith & Wesson for company, Johnny must separate truth from deception in a case where nothing—and no one—is quite what they seem. The stakes have never felt more personal, the danger more palpable, as this second chapter unfolds with the crackle of authentic noir atmosphere that makes *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* the gold standard of radio detective drama.

It's 1955, and while television begins its tentative grasp on American living rooms, radio still commands an evening's attention with shows like this one. Bob Bailey's portrayal of Johnny Dollar became iconic precisely because of his ability to inhabit the character's world with such convincing realism—the weariness, the sharp observations, the moral complications of a man working in the gray areas between law and commerce. This era of the show represents the height of its popularity, when the "silver dollar" method of storytelling (Johnny earning one dollar per day plus expenses) had become beloved ritual for millions of listeners.

Don't miss this compelling second installment. Tune in to *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* and discover why Johnny's investigation into the Amy Bradshaw matter grows more treacherous by the minute.
