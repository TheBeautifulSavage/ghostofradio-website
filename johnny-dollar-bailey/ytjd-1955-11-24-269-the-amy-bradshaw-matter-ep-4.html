# Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar: The Amy Bradshaw Matter (Episode 4)

As fog rolls thick through the streets of a nameless city, insurance investigator Johnny Dollar finds himself deeper in the murky waters of the Amy Bradshaw case than ever before. This fourth installment crackles with the tension of a man who knows too much and trusts too little—danger lurks around every shadowy corner, and every witness harbors secrets that could unravel the entire investigation. Bob Bailey's weary, world-worn voice cuts through the atmospheric static as Johnny navigates a labyrinth of lies, blackmail, and desperation. With each clue uncovered, the case grows darker and more personal, pulling our hero inexorably toward a confrontation that promises betrayal and revelations. The pace never lets up; neither does the dread.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* stands as one of radio's most enduring detective serials, and by 1955, Bob Bailey had perfected the art of portraying a man caught between duty and conscience. The show's innovation lay in its expense-account narrative structure—each episode opens with Johnny dictating case details for his insurance company file, a device that grounds the fantastical mystery in quotidian procedure. This particular season found Bailey at the height of his powers, having assumed the role after Edmond O'Brien's celebrated tenure. The Amy Bradshaw Matter represented the kind of serialized storytelling that kept listeners tuned in night after night, desperate to discover how Johnny would untangle the web before it consumed him entirely.

Don't miss this gripping chapter in one of radio's greatest detective sagas. If you've yet to experience the distinctive blend of hard-boiled noir and intimate narrative voice that made *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* a golden age classic, Episode 4 of the Amy Bradshaw Matter is the perfect entry point into Johnny's dangerous world.
