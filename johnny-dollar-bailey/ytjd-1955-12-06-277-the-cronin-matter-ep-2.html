# The Cronin Matter, Episode 2

Picture this: it's December 6th, 1955, and you're settling into your favorite chair with a tumbler of rye as Bob Bailey's world-weary voice crackles through your radio speaker. Johnny Dollar, the man with the action-packed expense account, is neck-deep in the Cronin matter—and the waters are murky. In this second installment, the case thickens like fog rolling into a midnight harbor. Someone's lying, the stakes have just gotten considerably higher, and Dollar's sharp mind is working overtime to separate fact from fiction. Expect plot twists that'll make you sit up straighter, dialogue crackle with tension, and that unmistakable signature of hard-boiled insurance investigation noir that made this show appointment listening for millions across America.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* arrived at CBS in 1955 to immediate acclaim, with Bob Bailey's portrayal of the insurance investigator becoming the definitive version of the character. What set the show apart in an increasingly crowded radio landscape was its commitment to serialized storytelling—each week's adventure built methodically on the last, rejecting the formulaic wrap-it-up-in-twenty-eight-minutes approach favored by many competitors. These meticulously crafted cases unfolded over multiple episodes, demanding genuine listener loyalty and rewarding attentive ears with layers of intrigue. Bailey's naturalistic delivery and the show's sharp writing made Johnny Dollar feel less like a pulp caricature and more like a real man trudging through a world of deception and greed.

Don't let this chapter of the Cronin matter slip away into the static. Tune in now and discover why *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* became the golden standard of radio noir—where every clue matters, every voice could conceal a secret, and the only thing you can trust is the man behind the microphone.
