# The Cronin Matter - Part Four

The fog rolls thick over the docks this December evening, and Johnny Dollar finds himself in deeper trouble than a missing insurance claim. As our wisecracking investigator closes in on the truth behind the Cronin matter, the case threatens to unravel into something far more sinister than anyone anticipated. Bob Bailey's dry, world-weary narration carries listeners through shadowy warehouses and smoky hotel rooms where every stranger could be friend or foe. This fourth installment promises the kind of taut dialogue and unexpected twists that made listeners anxiously await each new episode—conversations that crackle with danger, and a protagonist whose quick wit is the only thing sharper than the case getting under his skin.

By 1955, *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* had become one of radio's last great bastions of sophisticated detective storytelling. While television was beginning its assault on radio's golden throne, CBS kept the show alive with scripts that crackled with intelligence and cynicism, drawing heavily from the hardboiled fiction that captivated postwar America. Bob Bailey's portrayal of Johnny Dollar—a man caught between honor and pragmatism, always following the money but never losing sight of right and wrong—resonated with audiences craving adult entertainment that didn't talk down to them. The show's willingness to stretch cases across multiple episodes deepened the mythology, transforming individual installments into chapters of something larger and more complex.

Whether you've been following Johnny Dollar since the beginning or you're discovering him for the first time, *The Cronin Matter* Episode Four offers everything that made this show essential listening: mystery, atmosphere, and a protagonist you absolutely believe when he says danger is coming.
