# The Lansing Fraud Matter – Part Three

The rain hammers against the office windows as Johnny Dollar lights another cigarette, his mind churning through the contradictions that threaten to unravel the Lansing case. In this third installment, our insurance investigator finds himself caught between a desperate widow's tears and a corporation's cool denial—with fifty thousand dollars hanging in the balance. Bob Bailey's world-weary narration guides you through the shadowed streets of a mid-century city where every witness has something to hide, every document might be forged, and the line between legitimate claim and elaborate con grows perilously thin. The tension builds methodically as Johnny pieces together the puzzle, his sharp dialogue cutting through lies like a blade through silk, while behind every door lurks the question: who's really defrauding whom?

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* represents the gold standard of radio's final flourishing years, when the medium's technical sophistication and star power reached their zenith. Bob Bailey's portrayal of the quick-thinking, morally anchored investigator became the definitive voice of the character during CBS's acclaimed 1955-1960 run—a period when radio drama proved it could still captivate millions even as television's shadow lengthened across the industry. These continuing cases, serialized across multiple episodes, demanded a different kind of storytelling than the tidy resolutions of earlier radio mystery shows. The *Johnny Dollar* format allowed complexity, allowed characters to breathe, allowed audiences to genuinely wonder how the puzzle would be solved.

Slip on your headphones and step into the December 1955 darkness. The Lansing matter awaits its resolution, and only Johnny Dollar has the cunning to navigate the treacherous waters between truth and deception. This is detective work as it was meant to be heard.
