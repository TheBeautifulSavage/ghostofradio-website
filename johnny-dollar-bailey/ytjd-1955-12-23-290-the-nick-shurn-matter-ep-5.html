# The Nick Shurn Matter - Episode 5

Picture this: Christmas Eve, 1955. Snow dusts the Manhattan streets while insurance investigator Johnny Dollar sits in a dingy office, the glow of a desk lamp cutting through the darkness like a knife through butter. In this fifth installment of "The Nick Shurn Matter," our weary protagonist finds himself deeper in a web of deception, double-crosses, and dangerous secrets that someone would kill to keep buried. Bob Bailey's gravelly voice carries the weight of a man who's seen too much—a man who knows that in this business, trust is a luxury nobody can afford. The case that seemed straightforward has spiraled into something far more sinister, and as Christmas carols drift through the city streets outside, Johnny realizes his next move could be his last.

What made *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* exceptional was its commitment to authenticity and its tireless star. Bob Bailey became synonymous with the role during these CBS years, delivering over 450 episodes with a naturalistic style that set him apart from more theatrical radio actors of the era. The show pioneered the "expense account" storytelling format, with Johnny's investigations spanning exotic locations and morally complex situations. At a time when American audiences craved both escapism and sophistication, *Johnny Dollar* delivered noir sensibilities right into living rooms across the nation, proving that radio drama could be as psychologically nuanced as any literary mystery.

This episode represents the show at its prime—taut, intelligent writing paired with Bailey's authentic performance creates an experience that still crackles with tension nearly seventy years later. Tune in and discover why *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* remained radio's most popular dramatic program, one expense report at a time.
