# The Forbes Matter (Episode 4)

As 1955 draws to a close, insurance investigator Johnny Dollar finds himself ensnared in a web of deception that grows more twisted with every revelation. In this fourth installment of "The Forbes Matter," Dollar pursues a lead that promises answers—but may instead deliver him straight into danger. The Manhattan skyline glitters coldly outside rain-streaked windows as our protagonist navigates shadowy hotel corridors and smoke-filled back rooms, following a paper trail that connects respectable businessmen to crimes they'll kill to keep buried. Bob Bailey's weary, world-wise narration guides listeners through a labyrinth where trust is currency and a single mistake means permanent silence. The tension crackles through your radio speaker as Dollar inches closer to the truth, knowing all the while that someone is counting the days until he stops looking.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* emerged during the golden age of radio drama as something genuinely new—a serialized detective show that treated its audience like adults, unfolding complex mysteries over multiple episodes rather than wrapping everything up in thirty minutes. Bob Bailey, taking over the role in 1955, brought a sophisticated, hardboiled authenticity to the character, transforming Dollar into less a caped crusader and more a weary professional doing dirty work for honest pay. The show's commitment to serialized storytelling was revolutionary for CBS, allowing writers to develop real character arcs and genuine suspense across weeks of broadcasts.

If you haven't yet fallen under the spell of Johnny Dollar's investigation into the Forbes affair, episode four is the perfect moment to jump into the murky waters. Whether you're a devoted fan who's been following every twist or a newcomer curious about what made radio drama essential listening, this episode captures everything that made *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* unforgettable: danger, mystery, and a protagonist determined to find the truth, no matter the cost.
