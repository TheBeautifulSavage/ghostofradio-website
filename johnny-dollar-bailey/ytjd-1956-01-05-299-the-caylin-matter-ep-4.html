# The Caylin Matter - Episode 4

Steel rain hammers the pavement outside a dimly-lit office as Johnny Dollar leans back in his chair, reviewing the Caylin case files spread across his desk like pieces of a puzzle that refuses to fit. By episode four of this five-part investigation, the insurance investigator's cool demeanor is showing cracks—someone is lying, the evidence contradicts itself, and a fifty-thousand-dollar claim hangs in the balance. Bob Bailey's masterful narration pulls you into the smoke-filled shadows of post-war America, where trust is currency and a single misspoken word can unravel everything. In this installment, Dollar confronts key witnesses, uncovers a crucial discrepancy, and edges closer to the truth that will determine whether justice—or clever deception—prevails. The tension builds inexorably toward a revelation that listeners of the era found absolutely gripping.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* was revolutionary for American radio in the 1950s, pioneering the "continuing case" format that kept audiences returning night after night, week after week. With Bob Bailey in the lead role from 1955 to 1960, the show became the last major radio drama to achieve genuine mass popularity before television claimed the medium's crown. Each case unfolded across multiple episodes, allowing for genuine character development and narrative complexity that transcended radio's typical thirty-minute constraints. The writing was crisp, the sound design immaculate, and Bailey's world-weary delivery became iconic—a template for every cynical investigator that would follow in television and film.

Don your fedora and step into the fog with Johnny Dollar. The Caylin Matter awaits, and the fourth installment holds secrets that will change everything. This is radio drama at its finest—compelling, intelligent, and absolutely unforgettable.
