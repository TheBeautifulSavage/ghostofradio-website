# The Caylin Matter - Episode 5

Deep in the fog-shrouded streets of San Francisco, insurance investigator Johnny Dollar finds himself ensnared in a web of deception more tangled than any he's encountered before. In this fifth installment of "The Caylin Matter," the case that has consumed Dollar's every waking moment reaches a fever pitch. A missing person, a questionable death claim, and witnesses who seem to know far more than they're telling—all converge in this episode as Johnny peels back another layer of lies. Bob Bailey's weary, determined voice cuts through the atmospheric sound design—the distant fog horns, the shuffle of footsteps on wet pavement—as Dollar inches closer to the truth. The tension builds relentlessly: who is behind the Caylin disappearance, and what dangerous secrets will be exposed before the case is finally closed?

"Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar" stands as a towering achievement in American radio drama, and Bailey's portrayal of the globe-trotting insurance investigator remains definitive. Unlike the moralizing protagonists of earlier detective serials, Johnny Dollar inhabits a morally complex world where greed and desperation drive ordinary people to extraordinary crimes. The show's five-act structure—where episodes unfold across multiple broadcasts—was revolutionary, allowing genuine narrative complexity and character development impossible in standalone tales. From 1955 to 1960, the program captivated millions, proving that radio drama could sophisticate audiences with genuine mysteries rather than simple melodrama.

If you've been following Johnny Dollar's investigation since the beginning of "The Caylin Matter," you won't want to miss this pivotal fifth episode. And if you're new to the program, there's no better time to step into the shadowy world of insurance fraud and human desperation. Tune in, dim the lights, and let Bob Bailey's voice transport you to a San Francisco where nothing is quite what it seems.
