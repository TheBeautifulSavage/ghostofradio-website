# The Ricardo Amerigo Matter, Pt. 2

Johnny Dollar's voice cuts through the static like a knife through velvet as he finds himself deeper in a South American maze of corruption and double-dealing. In this second installment of "The Ricardo Amerigo Matter," our expense-account hero discovers that nothing in this sultry port city is quite what it seems—not the elegant businessman who hired him, not the mysterious woman with knowing eyes, and certainly not the fifty-thousand-dollar claim that set this whole desperate affair in motion. With danger lurking in shadowed cantinas and government offices alike, Dollar must navigate treacherous alliances and veiled threats to uncover the truth. Bob Bailey's masterful delivery captures every nuance of suspicion and weariness as Johnny realizes he may have stepped into something far more sinister than a simple insurance fraud. The tension builds relentlessly through this episode, with crackling dialogue and atmospheric sound design transporting listeners directly into a world of intrigue where trust is currency and loyalty a luxury no one can afford.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* represented radio's last great flowering, a sophisticated adult drama that treated its audience as intelligent and demanding. During the mid-1950s, as television began stealing away younger listeners, CBS kept this gem alive with sharp writing and Bailey's incomparable talent for inhabiting a fully realized character. Bailey's Johnny Dollar became the gold standard for the insurance investigator—cynical yet principled, world-weary but never defeated—and episodes like this one showcase exactly why the show commanded such devoted listeners during its five-year run.
