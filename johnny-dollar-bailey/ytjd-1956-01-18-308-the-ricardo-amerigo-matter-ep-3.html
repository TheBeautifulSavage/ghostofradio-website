# The Ricardo Amerigo Matter - Episode 3

The rain hammers against the windows of a Manhattan office as insurance investigator Johnny Dollar finds himself caught between two worlds—one foot in the glittering nightclubs of the Upper East Side, the other in the shadowy underworld of forged documents and stolen identities. In this third installment of *The Ricardo Amerigo Matter*, our five-hundred-dollar-a-day operative must navigate a treacherous web of false claims and darker secrets that suggest someone is willing to kill to keep the truth buried. Bob Bailey's measured, world-weary narration cuts through the static like a cigarette lighter in the dark, as Johnny inches closer to the heart of a conspiracy that could topple more than one carefully constructed life. The tension builds methodically—a phone call here, a veiled threat there—until the very walls of the investigation seem to close in. You can almost smell the cigarette smoke and aged bourbon as Johnny pieces together evidence that refuses to fit the official story.

This episode represents *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* at its creative peak, when the show had settled into the formula that made it one of CBS Radio's most enduring hits. Bailey's portrayal of the everyman detective gave the series an authenticity that distinguished it from more theatrical competitors, grounding the impossible cases in a gritty, recognizable reality. The five-part case structure allowed for genuine narrative complexity—mysteries that couldn't be neatly resolved in twenty-two minutes, but demanded sustained attention and investment from loyal listeners.

Tune in tonight and discover why this 1956 adventure remains a masterclass in radio noir. *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* awaits.
