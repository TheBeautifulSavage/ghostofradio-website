# Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar: The Ricardo Amerigo Matter (Episode 4)

The fog rolls thick over the docks as insurance investigator Johnny Dollar closes in on the truth behind a sprawling international swindle that's already claimed one man's reputation and threatens another's life. In this fourth installment of "The Ricardo Amerigo Matter," our hard-boiled hero navigates a maze of false identities, forged documents, and desperate men willing to kill to keep their secrets buried. Bob Bailey's distinctive rapid-fire delivery crackles with urgency as Johnny traces the threads of deceit across continents and bank accounts, each clue bringing him closer to a revelation that could topple an empire—or cost him everything. The episode pulses with that distinctive mid-1950s noir tension: the ambient sounds of typewriters and telephone rings, the tinny jazz of a late-night radio station, and always, always, the underlying threat of danger lurking just beyond the next interrogation.

What made *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* such a phenomenon during its golden-age run on CBS was Bob Bailey's unparalleled commitment to the role—he logged over 900 episodes as Johnny, making him one of radio's most recognizable voices. This 1956 episode exemplifies the show's sophisticated approach to detective drama, trading in the supernatural gimmicks of lesser programs for meticulous plotting, snappy dialogue, and the kind of moral ambiguity that defined the best noir fiction. The serialized "Matter" format kept listeners tuning in night after night, invested in following Johnny's expense account as intimately as his investigation.

Settle in with the lights dimmed low, let the static crackle to life, and step into Johnny Dollar's world—where every case has angles, every witness has something to hide, and the next wrong move could be your last. This is essential listening for anyone who understands that radio drama, at its finest, could rival any visual medium for suspense, atmosphere, and sheer narrative craft.
