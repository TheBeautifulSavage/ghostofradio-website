# The Ricardo Amerigo Matter – Episode 5

Picture this: a moonless night in a shadowy corner of the Caribbean, where insurance investigator Johnny Dollar finds himself knee-deep in a case that reeks of deception and danger. In this fifth installment of "The Ricardo Amerigo Matter," Dollar must navigate a web of forged documents, desperate men, and secrets buried deeper than the ocean itself. Bob Bailey's world-weary voice cuts through the static as our hero inches closer to the truth, each clue pulling him further into a labyrinth where trust is currency and betrayal lurks around every corner. The stakes have never been higher—and neither have the lies. What started as a routine insurance claim has blossomed into something far more sinister, and Johnny Dollar's quick wits may be all that stands between justice and a killer's escape.

By 1956, *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* had become the gold standard of radio noir, proving that audiences hungered for intelligent, morally complex protagonists and stories that refused easy answers. Bob Bailey's portrayal revolutionized the insurance investigator archetype—Dollar wasn't a glamorous detective but a working man, methodical and pragmatic, navigating a post-war world where contracts meant everything and honor meant little. Each episode ran with remarkable continuity; five-parters like "The Ricardo Amerigo Matter" allowed listeners to sink into fully realized mysteries that unfolded with genuine suspense, episode by episode.

If you've been following Johnny Dollar's investigation since the beginning of this week's broadcast, don't miss how this chapter unfolds. And if you're new to the series, there's no better time to join in—the tension is already building to a crescendo. Tune in and discover why *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* became one of radio's last and finest achievements.
