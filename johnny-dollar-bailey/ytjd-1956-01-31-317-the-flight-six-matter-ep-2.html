# The Flight Six Matter – Episode 2

Picture this: it's a cold January night in 1956, and you're settling in with your radio as Johnny Dollar's weary voice crackles through the speaker, pulling you back into a mystery that refuses to stay closed. A charter flight has vanished without a trace, and the insurance company's golden boy investigator finds himself entangled in a web of competing interests, desperate witnesses, and the kind of danger that doesn't announce itself politely. With Bob Bailey at the helm—his delivery precise yet world-weary, his timing impeccable—you're drawn deeper into an investigation where every clue seems to raise two new questions, and nobody's story quite adds up. The stakes intensify when Johnny realizes that somewhere in this tangle of greed, fear, and desperation, someone is willing to kill to keep Flight Six's secrets buried.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* represented something special in the twilight years of radio drama: a thinking man's detective show that trusted its audience's intelligence and rewarded their attention. Unlike the superhero fantasies and broad comedies still commanding the airwaves, this CBS series offered intelligent, tightly-plotted mysteries grounded in the everyday world of insurance claims and investigative work. Bob Bailey's portrayal became definitive—every episode a masterclass in noir narration and character development, with supporting casts that brought authenticity to a world of adjusters, con artists, and ordinary people in extraordinary circumstances. The show's continued popularity through the mid-1950s proved that radio listeners hungered for sophistication.

So dim the lights, still your mind, and let Johnny Dollar take you down the dark corridors of The Flight Six Matter. In this second installment, mysteries deepen and danger closes in—this is detective fiction at its finest, when radio could transport you anywhere and *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* knew exactly how to get you there.
