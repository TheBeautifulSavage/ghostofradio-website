# The McClain Matter – Part Two

The rain hammers against the windows of a seedy San Francisco hotel as Johnny Dollar finds himself deeper in a case that refuses to stay buried. Last week's investigation into the disappearance of a young woman has led him down a darker path than anticipated—one lined with blackmail, false identities, and people who would kill to keep their secrets safe. With nothing but his wits, a pack of cigarettes, and the ticking sound of a hotel clock marking his dwindling time, Johnny must navigate a labyrinth of lies to uncover what really happened to Miss McClain. The stakes have never been higher, and for the first time in this investigation, Johnny himself may be in danger.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* revolutionized the radio detective genre during its golden age, and this 1956 episode exemplifies why. Bob Bailey's portrayal of the world-weary insurance investigator became the definitive voice of post-war cynicism—a man who could navigate both high society drawing rooms and back-alley poker games with equal aplomb. The show's focus on insurance fraud cases rather than murders was refreshingly original, grounding the drama in the mundane realities of crime that most people never see. By 1956, Bailey had perfected his craft, delivering rapid-fire dialogue with the kind of weathered charm that made listeners feel like they were right there in the room with him.

If you haven't yet experienced the electric tension of a Johnny Dollar investigation, *The McClain Matter* is the perfect entry point—a tightly plotted two-parter that showcases everything the show does best. Tune in and discover why audiences kept their radios tuned to CBS every week for five unforgettable seasons.
