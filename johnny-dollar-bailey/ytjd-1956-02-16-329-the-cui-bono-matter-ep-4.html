# Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar: "The Cui Bono Matter" (Episode 4)

Step into the rain-slicked streets of 1956 as insurance investigator Johnny Dollar pursues the tangled threads of "The Cui Bono Matter"—a case that demands the ancient question: who benefits? In this fourth installment of a serialized investigation, Dollar finds himself navigating a maze of corporate intrigue, hidden motives, and danger lurking behind respectable facades. Bob Bailey's world-weary voice cuts through the static as our protagonist uncovers layers of deception, each revelation pulling him deeper into a web where everyone has something to gain and everything to lose. With his expense account and quick wits as his only reliable companions, Dollar must separate truth from misdirection in a case where greed and ambition become the most dangerous currencies of all.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* stood as CBS Radio's crown jewel of detective drama, and for good reason. Running from 1954 to 1962, the series pioneered the "documentary noir" approach—Dollar's cases unfolded across multiple episodes, building tension methodically rather than wrapping up neatly in thirty minutes. Bob Bailey's portrayal became legendary among radio aficionados for its authenticity and vulnerability; this wasn't a superhero in a trench coat, but a working man solving puzzles for his insurance company. The 1956 episodes represent the show at peak popularity, when millions tuned in weekly to hear what Johnny had uncovered. "The Cui Bono Matter" exemplifies the show's strengths: intricate plotting, genuine suspense, and the crackling dialogue that made radio drama unforgettable.

Whether you're a longtime devotee of old-time radio or discovering this golden age of broadcasting for the first time, let Johnny Dollar guide you through this gripping four-part case. Your expense account awaits.
