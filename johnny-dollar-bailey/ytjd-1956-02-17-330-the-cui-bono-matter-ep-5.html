# The Cui Bono Matter - Part Five

The fifth installment of "The Cui Bono Matter" plunges Johnny Dollar into the darkest corner of his investigation yet, where the question of *who benefits* transforms into a labyrinth of greed, betrayal, and murder most calculated. As our man-with-the-action settles into yet another smoky hotel room—expense account growing thinner by the hour—the evidence begins to crystallize into something far more sinister than a simple insurance fraud. Who stands to gain? Everyone. Who's telling the truth? No one. With Bob Bailey's distinctive rasp cutting through the static and decay of 1956, this episode crackles with the kind of noir tension that defined the golden age of radio drama: the methodical unraveling of lies, the closing walls of suspicion, and the unsettling realization that money—cold, indifferent money—may be the only honest thing in this twisted affair.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* represented something unique in post-war broadcasting: the thinking listener's detective show. Unlike the flashy heroics of *The Shadow* or the procedural rhythms of earlier noir programs, Johnny Dollar's investigations unfolded with the deliberate precision of an actual insurance claim, complete with expense reports that audiences adored. Bob Bailey's portrayal of Dollar wasn't swaggering or invincible—he was clever, weary, and entirely human. The show ran during radio's final flowering, when television was already stealing the spotlight, yet it maintained an fiercely devoted audience who appreciated its sophistication and authenticity.

This episode stands as essential listening for anyone seeking to understand what made radio drama irreplaceable—before the medium faded into memory, these investigators walked the same streets we did, speaking in our language, solving our mysteries. Tune in and discover why millions huddled close to their speakers in the darkness, waiting for Johnny Dollar's next move.
