# Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar – "The Bennet Matter" (Episode 3)

As Johnny Dollar settles into the smoky shadows of a mid-fifties insurance investigation, listeners are drawn into a labyrinth of deception that grows more twisted with each passing moment. This third installment of "The Bennet Matter" finds our quick-witted hero navigating a minefield of suspicious characters, questionable alibis, and the kind of moral ambiguity that keeps you glued to your radio speaker. Bob Bailey's world-weary narration cuts through the atmospheric hum of office phones and footsteps on rain-slicked pavement, while every clue uncovered seems to spawn three new questions. The stakes are unmistakably higher now—someone's lying, someone's hiding something, and Johnny Dollar's reputation depends on uncovering the truth before the case goes cold.

What made *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* essential listening for millions of Americans in the post-war era was its grounding in the mundane realities of everyday crime. Unlike the fantastical adventures of radio's masked vigilantes, Johnny Dollar investigated insurance fraud with the methodical precision of a seasoned professional. Bob Bailey's portrayal became iconic precisely because it captured the weariness and wit of a man who'd seen human nature at its most vulnerable. During the mid-1950s, when Americans were eager for sophisticated adult entertainment that didn't insult their intelligence, Johnny Dollar delivered exactly that—smart writing, plausible mysteries, and a protagonist who could crack wise while cracking cases.

Don't miss this pivotal moment in "The Bennet Matter," where the investigation deepens and the noose tightens. Tune in and discover why Johnny Dollar remained one of radio's most beloved detectives, one expense account entry at a time.
