# The Bennet Matter - Episode 4

The fourth installment of "The Bennet Matter" finds insurance investigator Johnny Dollar deep in the shadowy complications of a case that refuses to resolve cleanly. As he narrates the peculiar details of his expenses and the mounting contradictions in witness statements, listeners will find themselves ensnared in the trademark atmosphere of *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar*—a world where nothing is quite what it seems and every lead spawns three new questions. The episode crackles with the kind of tense, methodical investigation that defined the show's reputation: no car chases or gunfights, just the meticulous work of a man determined to uncover the truth, one expense report entry at a time. The February 1956 broadcast captures Bob Bailey at the height of his performance, his distinctive voice carrying the weight of suspicion, exhaustion, and dogged determination as Johnny navigates the Byzantine world of the Bennet case.

What makes this episode particularly significant is its arrival during the golden age of network radio's decline—yet CBS continued to champion *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* as an island of sophisticated, adult entertainment in a medium increasingly surrendering to television. The show's innovative use of the "expense account" as a narrative framework was revolutionary, allowing viewers an intimate glimpse into the private thoughts and methods of its protagonist. Bob Bailey's tenure as Johnny Dollar (1955-1960) is regarded by radio historians as definitive, infusing the character with a world-weary believability that made each investigation feel urgently real.

Don't miss this intricate chapter in one of radio's finest detective dramas. Tune in and experience the craftsmanship that kept millions of listeners on the edge of their seats long after network radio's prime.
