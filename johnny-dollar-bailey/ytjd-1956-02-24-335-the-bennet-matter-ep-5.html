# The Bennet Matter – Episode 5

The case grows darker by the minute. Johnny Dollar finds himself tangled in a web of lies, blackmail, and desperate people willing to do anything to keep their secrets buried. In this fifth installment of "The Bennet Matter," our intrepid insurance investigator discovers that the simple claim he began investigating has spiraled into something far more sinister—perhaps even murderous. As tension crackles through every scene, you'll hear the unmistakable click of a lighter, the scratch of a match, and the low hum of suspicion in every conversation. Someone is lying. Everyone seems to have a motive. And Johnny, with only his wits and his expense account to guide him, must navigate this treacherous landscape before the truth—or the killer—catches up with him first.

By 1956, *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* had become the gold standard of radio noir, and Bob Bailey's weary, intelligent portrayal of the title character set him apart from the era's other hardboiled detectives. What made the show revolutionary was its commitment to serialized storytelling—cases didn't wrap up in twenty-two minutes, but unfolded across multiple episodes, allowing listeners to sink deeper into the mystery week after week. This particular case showcases exactly why the series remained a ratings powerhouse for CBS, blending the cynical atmosphere of film noir with the intimate immediacy of radio drama.

If you've been following "The Bennet Matter" from the beginning, you won't want to miss this crucial development. If you're new to Johnny Dollar, this is the perfect place to discover what made this program a beloved fixture in American living rooms throughout the 1950s.
