# The Fathom Five Matter (Part Four)

The Caribbean sun beats down mercilessly as Johnny Dollar finds himself trapped between warring salvage companies, missing dive equipment, and a murder that refuses to stay buried beneath the waves. In this fourth installment of "The Fathom Five Matter," the insurance investigator faces his most treacherous case yet—one where every suspect has something to hide and the ocean itself seems determined to keep its secrets. With Bob Bailey's gravelly voice guiding us through moonlit docks and sweltering interrogation rooms, listeners are pulled deeper into a mystery where greed, revenge, and desperation collide. The tension crackles through the airwaves as Johnny pieces together contradictions in testimony, each revelation dragging him closer to a truth that someone would kill to conceal.

"Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar" stands as CBS's crown jewel of dramatic radio, a show where sophisticated storytelling met the golden age of American broadcasting. Bob Bailey's portrayal of the quick-witted, hard-bitten insurance investigator set the standard for dramatic radio in the mid-1950s, combining the cynical charm of film noir with the intimate immediacy of the medium itself. Serialized cases like "The Fathom Five Matter" allowed writers to develop complex plots across multiple episodes, transforming radio drama into something approaching novelistic depth. Each fifteen-minute installment was crafted to leave listeners desperate for the next broadcast, a cliffhanger formula that kept America tuned in throughout the decade.

If you've never experienced Johnny Dollar's world of missing persons, suspicious claims, and dangerous clients, now is your chance to step into a booth at a smoky bar circa 1956 and hear broadcasting at its finest. Tune in and discover why "Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar" remains the gold standard of American radio drama.
