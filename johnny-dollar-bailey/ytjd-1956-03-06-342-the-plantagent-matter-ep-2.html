# The Plantagent Matter - Episode 2

Picture this: it's a cold March evening in 1956, and you're settling in with your radio set as Johnny Dollar's gravelly voice cuts through the static. The Plantagent case has already spiraled into dangerous territory, and now, on night two of this serialized investigation, our hard-boiled insurance investigator finds himself deeper in the mire of deception and double-cross. Someone doesn't want Johnny asking questions about the Plantagent claim, and they're willing to play rough to keep their secrets buried. As the drama unfolds across this tense half-hour, you'll hear the snap of a lighter, the clink of ice in a glass, and the unmistakable sound of a man walking down a dark street knowing someone might be following him. The pace quickens, the stakes rise, and by the time the closing theme swells, you'll be counting the days until episode three.

What made *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* special among the crowded field of radio noir was its commitment to real insurance investigations and procedural authenticity. Bob Bailey's portrayal—the weariness, the wit, the world-weary competence—became iconic precisely because it never pandered or sensationalized. The show ran strong from 1955 to 1960, winning multiple awards and setting the standard for detective radio drama. Multi-part cases like "The Plantagent Matter" showcased the show's ability to sustain tension and character development across several nights, keeping listeners glued to their sets.

Don't miss your chance to experience one of radio's finest. Tune in and let Johnny Dollar guide you through the shadowy world of insurance fraud, blackmail, and murder. This is radio drama at its most sophisticated and compelling.
