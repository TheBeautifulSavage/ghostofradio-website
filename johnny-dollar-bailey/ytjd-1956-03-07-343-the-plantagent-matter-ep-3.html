# The Plantagent Matter – Episode 3

Johnny Dollar's expense account is about to take another beating as he finds himself neck-deep in the murky underbelly of postwar high society. In this gripping third installment of "The Plantagent Matter," our everyman insurance investigator must navigate a labyrinth of blackmail, deception, and dangerous secrets that could unravel an entire family's carefully constructed empire. Bob Bailey's world-weary narration carries you through dimly-lit hotel corridors and smoky interrogation rooms, where every conversation bristles with hidden meaning and the constant threat of violence lurks just beneath the surface of polite discourse. You'll hear the clink of ice in a whiskey glass, the snap of a lighter, the pregnant pause before a confession—the sonic details that made radio drama an art form. As Johnny inches closer to the truth, the case grows increasingly personal, and he discovers that in matters of the heart and honor, the truth is rarely worth the price it demands.

What makes "Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar" a landmark achievement in American radio is its elegant marriage of hard-boiled detective fiction with the intimate medium of audio drama. Running for nearly a decade on CBS, the show pioneered the five-part episode format, allowing for genuine plot complexity that rivaled the serialized novels that inspired it. Bob Bailey's portrayal transcended the archetype—Johnny Dollar wasn't a superhero or a colorful eccentric, but rather a competent, flawed professional trying to make an honest living in a dishonest world, which resonated powerfully with post-war American audiences navigating their own moral ambiguities.

Don't miss the explosive conclusion of Johnny's investigation into the Plantagent family's web of intrigue. Tune in and discover why this program remains the gold standard of insurance-investigator noir.
