# The Clinton Matter - Episode 3

As Johnny Dollar steps into the rain-slicked streets of a coastal town, the case that's been dogging him grows darker by the hour. A missing heiress, a forged insurance claim, and a cast of suspects who seem to know far more than they're saying—this third installment of "The Clinton Matter" tightens the noose around our man-of-the-world investigator. Bob Bailey's weathered voice carries that perfect blend of weariness and determination as Johnny pieces together a puzzle where every answer spawns three new questions. You can practically hear the desperation in the dialogue, the cigarette smoke hanging heavy in dim hotel rooms, the clink of glasses in speakeasies where loose lips might spill dangerous secrets. This is insurance investigation at its most perilous, where the wrong word to the wrong person could mean Johnny becomes a permanent fixture in the local police files.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* was something special in the golden age of radio—a show built not on weekly reset buttons but on sustained narrative arcs that kept listeners coming back, desperate to know what happened next. During the 1955-1960 CBS run with Bailey in the title role, the show perfected the art of serialized mystery storytelling, presenting cases that unfolded across multiple episodes like chapters in a hardboiled novel. Bailey's interpretation became the definitive Johnny Dollar, a man equally comfortable in penthouse suites and back alley card games, solving cases for modest fees while navigating a world where trust was currency more precious than money itself.

Don't miss this gripping continuation of "The Clinton Matter." Tune in now and discover why Johnny Dollar became one of radio's most beloved investigators—a man willing to follow the truth wherever it leads, no matter how dark the path.
