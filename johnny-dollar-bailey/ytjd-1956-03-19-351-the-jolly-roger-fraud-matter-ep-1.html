# Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar: The Jolly Roger Fraud Matter (Part 1)

Picture yourself in the smoke-filled office of an insurance investigator on a cold March evening in 1956, as Johnny Dollar settles into his chair and begins dictating the details of his latest case into a wire recorder. The Jolly Roger Fraud Matter opens with all the hallmarks of what made this show essential listening: a dame with a suspicious story, a maritime insurance claim that doesn't quite add up, and Johnny's razor-sharp instincts already detecting the scent of deception. The Atlantic wind howls outside as our hero uncovers the first threads of a tangled web—a ship, stolen cargo, and questions that won't stay quiet. Bob Bailey's distinctive voice, world-weary yet relentlessly curious, guides listeners through the darkness with the confidence of a man who's seen every con in the book.

By 1956, Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar had become the gold standard of radio noir, a show that proved detective fiction could thrive in the post-war era when television was beginning to lure audiences away from their sets. Unlike flashy rivals, Johnny Dollar succeeded through meticulous storytelling and the intimate immediacy of Bailey's performance—listeners weren't watching a scene, they were *inside* Johnny's mind, hearing his thoughts and suspicions in real time. The show's innovation of the "matter" structure, where cases unfolded across multiple episodes, kept audiences hungry for resolution week after week.

Don the earphones and step back into 1956. The Jolly Roger awaits, and Johnny Dollar is ready to separate the truth from the lies, one carefully documented detail at a time. Tune in now for Part 1 of a case that will test his wits and his nerve.
