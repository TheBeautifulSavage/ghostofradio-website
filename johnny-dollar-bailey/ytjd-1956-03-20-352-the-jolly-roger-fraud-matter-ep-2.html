# The Jolly Roger Fraud Matter (Part Two)

The fog rolls thick over San Francisco's waterfront as Johnny Dollar descends deeper into a web of maritime deception that grows more tangled with each passing minute. A seemingly straightforward insurance claim has blossomed into something far more sinister—counterfeit documents, missing cargo, and a cast of suspicious characters with secrets worth killing for. In this second installment, our intrepid investigator must navigate treacherous docks and darker corners of the city, where every conversation could be his last and trust is a luxury he cannot afford. Bob Bailey's distinctive raspy voice guides listeners through the darkness with the weariness of a man who's seen too much and believes too little, while the atmospheric sound design of creaking ships, lapping water, and distant foghorns transports audiences directly into the murky underworld of post-war San Francisco.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* represented the pinnacle of American radio drama during the 1950s—a sophisticated, adult-oriented program that elevated the insurance investigator to unlikely protagonist status. The show's success rested on Bob Bailey's shoulders, and he carried it brilliantly; his performance as Johnny Dollar became the gold standard for radio noir, balancing cynicism with genuine curiosity about human nature. Produced during CBS's final golden years of original drama, these episodes showcase the network's commitment to intelligent, serialized storytelling that demanded listener engagement and rewarded careful attention. The "Jolly Roger" case exemplifies why fans still cherish these broadcasts nearly seventy years later—intricate plotting, moral ambiguity, and the kind of character development rarely heard on modern radio.
