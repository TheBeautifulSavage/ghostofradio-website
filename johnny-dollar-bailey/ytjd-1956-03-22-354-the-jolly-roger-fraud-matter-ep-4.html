# Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar - The Jolly Roger Fraud Matter (Episode 4)

Picture this: a smoke-filled insurance office on a rain-slicked Manhattan evening, March 1956. Johnny Dollar, the world's most honest investigator, finds himself tangled deeper in the twisted threads of maritime deception as he pursues the fourth installment of "The Jolly Roger Fraud Matter." With nothing but his wits, his expense account, and a stubborn determination to uncover the truth, Johnny navigates a murky world of shipping fraud, shadowy dock workers, and suspects who smile while they lie. Each carefully laid clue builds tension that crackles through your speaker like electricity—you'll find yourself leaning closer to the radio, desperate to learn what Johnny discovers behind closed cabin doors and in whispered conversations in waterfront dives.

Bob Bailey's portrayal of the insurance investigator became the definitive voice of post-war American noir skepticism. From 1955 to 1960, *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* dominated the CBS schedule with gripping, serialized cases that treated insurance investigation as high drama rather than bureaucratic tedium. What set this show apart was its commitment to realism; Johnny's investigations were methodical, intelligent, and genuinely unpredictable. The multi-part case structure—uncommon in radio by this era—allowed listeners to develop genuine investment in outcomes, making the show essential listening for the commute home or the quiet evening at home.

Whether you're a devoted fan revisiting a classic or discovering this golden age of radio for the first time, "The Jolly Roger Fraud Matter" offers everything that made *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* indispensable entertainment: stellar writing, masterful sound design, and Bob Bailey's weary, intelligent delivery cutting through deception like a knife through fog. Tune in now and let Johnny Dollar lead you into one of radio's most compelling mysteries.
