# The Lamarr Matter (Part Two)

The rain hammers the pavement outside Johnny Dollar's office as he sits alone with a cup of cold coffee and a case that's growing darker by the minute. In part two of "The Lamarr Matter," our quick-witted insurance investigator finds himself tangled in a web of deception where every lead twists into another dead end, and every confession raises a dozen new questions. The dame at the center of it all may not be who she claims—or perhaps she's exactly who Johnny feared. With danger lurking in the shadows and the clock ticking toward a deadline that could cost his agency everything, Johnny must navigate a maze of conflicting testimonies, hidden motives, and double-crosses that keep listeners guessing right up to the final, shocking revelation. Bob Bailey's weary, world-worn delivery makes every clue feel like a personal punch to the gut.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* emerged during the golden twilight of network radio, when sponsors like Lipton Tea paid premium rates for drama that could grip an audience of millions in their living rooms. The show's genius lay in its formula: each episode featured a self-contained insurance claim that required Johnny's cunning mind and street-smart instincts to unravel. Bailey's portrayal of the protagonist became definitive—a man who understood human nature's darker impulses while maintaining an incorruptible moral compass. The late 1950s episodes like this one represent the show at its creative peak, when writers crafted increasingly intricate plots that rewarded close listening.

Don't miss this masterclass in old-time radio suspense. Tune in to "The Lamarr Matter (Part Two)" and discover why *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* captivated audiences for nearly a decade—and why these meticulously preserved recordings remain unmissable listening today.
