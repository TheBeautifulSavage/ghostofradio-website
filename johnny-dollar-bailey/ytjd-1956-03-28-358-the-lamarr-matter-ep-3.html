# The Lamarr Matter - Episode 3

The rain hammers against the windows of a Miami hotel room as Johnny Dollar sits across from a woman with secrets written all over her face. A missing necklace worth twenty grand, a jealous husband, a nightclub singer with expensive tastes—and a trail of lies that leads straight into the heart of the Sunshine State's shadowy underworld. In this third installment of *The Lamarr Matter*, Bob Bailey's world-weary investigator peels back another layer of deception, his sharp dialogue cutting through the noir atmosphere like a blade through silk. You'll hear the clink of ice in a whiskey glass, the crackle of tension between suspects, and that unmistakable snap in Bailey's voice as he corners another lead. What began as a routine insurance claim has spiraled into something far murkier, and by the final commercial break, Johnny Dollar will have uncovered a truth that nobody wanted found.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* was CBS's crown jewel of the detective drama format during the 1950s, and this 1956 episode showcases exactly why. Bob Bailey's portrayal of the wisecracking but incorruptible insurance investigator became the gold standard for radio noir, giving listeners not just a mystery to solve but a guide through the psychological underbelly of postwar American life. The show's episodic format—serialized arcs that could span multiple episodes—allowed Bailey and his writers to build genuine tension and character development rarely found in stand-alone radio dramas.

If you've never experienced the peculiar magic of Golden Age radio, this is your invitation. Tune in and let the darkness of *The Lamarr Matter* wash over you—no screen required, just your imagination and a voice that sounds like hard experience talking.
