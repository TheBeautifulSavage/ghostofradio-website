# The Lamarr Matter - Episode 4

Picture it: a smoke-filled insurance office in the heart of 1950s Manhattan, where Bob Bailey's weary voice cuts through the static like a dame's perfume cutting through desperation. In this fourth installment of "The Lamarr Matter," our quick-witted investigator finds himself tangled deeper in a web of blackmail, misdirection, and motives murkier than a Chicago back alley. Someone's lying—probably everyone is—and Johnny Dollar needs to separate the desperate from the dangerous before the case closes in on him. Expect sharp dialogue delivered at breakneck speed, the kind of snappy repartee that made listeners lean closer to their radios, and atmospherics that transform your living room into a shadowy world where nobody's quite what they seem. The stakes are climbing, and Dollar's expense account is about to earn every penny.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* represented something revolutionary in the twilight years of radio drama: a show built almost entirely on atmosphere, dialogue, and the singular talent of its lead actor. From 1955 to 1960, Bob Bailey became the definitive voice of the modern detective—not the hardboiled tough guy of yesterday's pulps, but a clever, fast-talking professional who treated insurance investigation like chess played in the dark. This particular serial, with its methodical unraveling across multiple episodes, showcases the format at its finest: intricate storytelling that demanded listeners return night after night to hear how it all resolved.

If you're craving drama that respects your intelligence, plotting that twists and rewards close attention, and a protagonist whose quick wit is matched only by his moral clarity, tune in now. *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* awaits—and the Lamarr case won't solve itself.
