# Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar: The Lamarr Matter, Episode 5

The rain hammers against the windows of a ritzy hotel suite as Johnny Dollar finds himself caught between a missing starlet, a web of blackmail, and a list of suspects with everything to lose. In this fifth installment of "The Lamarr Matter," our world-weary insurance investigator must untangle a knot of lies and misdirection that seems to grow more complicated with each passing hour. Bob Bailey's gravelly voice cuts through the static of post-war Los Angeles as Johnny questions alibis, searches for hidden letters, and follows leads that lead only to more questions. The orchestra swells with menace as our hero realizes he's being played—but by whom? With the clock ticking and the insurance company breathing down his neck, Johnny must decide who to trust and how far he's willing to go to crack this case wide open.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* stands as one of the finest achievements in radio drama, and for good reason. Running from 1955 to 1960, the show pioneered the "continuing case" format that would influence storytelling for decades to come, with some cases spanning multiple episodes to build genuine suspense. Bob Bailey's portrayal of the quick-thinking, morally grounded investigator became iconic precisely because Bailey brought authentic weariness and wit to a character navigating the corruption and glamour of the mid-century underworld. These episodes were recorded with meticulous attention to sound design—every footstep, every door slam, every distant telephone bell contributing to an immersive world that made listeners feel like they were shadowing Johnny through the seedy underbelly of American crime.

Tune in now and discover why listeners made Johnny Dollar appointment radio. *The Lamarr Matter* awaits—and the clock is ticking.
