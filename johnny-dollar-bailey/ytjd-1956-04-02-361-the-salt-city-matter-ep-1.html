# Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar: The Salt City Matter (Part 1)

When insurance investigator Johnny Dollar arrives in Syracuse on the trail of a missing shipment, he finds himself tangled in a web of small-town secrets that runs far deeper than any misplaced cargo. The salt deposits that built this upstate city's fortune hide something far more valuable—and far more dangerous. As Johnny checks into a modest hotel and begins his interviews, the April night air grows thick with suspicion. Every person he meets offers a different story, each one carefully rehearsed, each one incomplete. By episode's end, Johnny has uncovered enough to know he's touched something volatile, something that powerful people want buried. Bob Bailey's measured, world-weary narration guides you through shadowy streets and dimly lit offices, his voice a cool constant in a world where nobody—not the factory owner, not the railroad man, not even the woman with the nervous smile—is telling him the whole truth.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* stands as one of radio's finest achievements in the hard-boiled detective genre, and Bailey's portrayal across the series' five-year run remains definitive. Airing live twice weekly on CBS, the show earned devoted listeners through its commitment to intricate plotting, authentic detail, and Bailey's naturalistic delivery—a refreshing contrast to the theatrical bombast of earlier radio detectives. The Salt City Matter, broadcast in April 1956 during the show's creative peak, showcases everything that made Johnny Dollar essential listening: mystery, atmosphere, and a protagonist who treated his audience like intelligent adults.

Settle in tonight with this classic two-part investigation, and discover why fans still regard these broadcasts as the gold standard of radio noir nearly seventy years later. The case is waiting. Johnny Dollar is ready. Are you?
