# The Salt City Matter - Episode 4

Johnny Dollar arrives in Syracuse on a case that seems routine—insurance fraud, a missing shipment, the usual trail of lies that Johnny knows too well. But nothing stays routine in the Salt City. As our hero digs deeper into the case, the fog rolls thick through those upstate New York streets, and every lead seems to pull him further into a web of double-crosses and hidden motives. In this fourth installment, Johnny finds himself caught between a desperate widow, a smooth-talking businessman with too many secrets, and evidence that refuses to add up. Bob Bailey's weathered voice carries us through the shadows of mid-50s America, where a ten-cent cup of coffee and a sharp mind are Johnny's only real assets. The tension builds with each cigarette lit and each phone call made, leading to a confrontation that will either crack the case wide open or leave our intrepid investigator with nothing but more questions.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* represents the golden twilight of radio drama, airing during a period when television was beginning its rise but when the intimate magic of the spoken word still held audiences spellbound. Bob Bailey's portrayal of the hardboiled insurance investigator became definitive during the show's CBS run from 1955 to 1960, offering listeners something television couldn't quite match: the power of imagination meeting genuine noir atmosphere. Each episode was crafted with remarkable attention to detail, from the realistic insurance procedurals to the authentic American locales that grounded Johnny's investigations in a recognizable world.

Tune in now and discover why millions once huddled around their radios for *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar*—where the mystery deepens with every dollar amount and every evasive answer. This is classic radio at its finest.
