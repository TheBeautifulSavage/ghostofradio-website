# Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar: "The Salt City Matter" (Episode 5)

On this spring evening in 1956, insurance investigator Johnny Dollar arrives in Syracuse—a city of salt mines and shadowy secrets—where a seemingly routine claim has begun to unravel like a spool of thread pulled in the dark. Bob Bailey's world-weary voice carries you through neon-lit streets and into the back rooms of industrial Syracuse, where nothing is quite what it seems and everyone, from the nervous factory owner to the mysteriously absent witness, has something to hide. This is the fifth installment of a five-part case, and the tension has been simmering since the first episode aired. As Johnny closes in on the truth, the stakes rise with each commercial break, and you'll find yourself leaning closer to the radio speaker, desperate to know whether he'll solve the puzzle before time—and fate—run out.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* stands as one of the last great detective programs of radio's golden age, a show that understood that post-war audiences craved sophisticated urban noir delivered at the speed of thought. Bailey's portrayal of the sharp, cynical yet fundamentally decent claims investigator set the standard for radio detectives of the mid-1950s. The show's genius lay in its serialized cases—sprawling five-part mysteries that forced listeners to tune in night after night, unable to resist the cliffhangers and reversals that Bailey's confident narration promised. This was radio at its most addictive, before television would eventually dim the golden age into memory.

Don't miss the conclusion of "The Salt City Matter." Settle in tonight, adjust the dial to find that perfect signal, and let Bob Bailey's unmistakable voice pull you into a mystery that demands to be solved.
