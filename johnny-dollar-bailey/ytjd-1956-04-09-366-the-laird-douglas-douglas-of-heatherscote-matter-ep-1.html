# The Laird Douglas Douglas Of Heatherscote Matter, Episode 1

Picture this: a fog-shrouded Scottish estate, ancient stone walls thick with secrets, and our man Johnny Dollar stepping off the boat with nothing but his wits and a blank expense account. In this two-part investigation, insurance investigator Johnny finds himself entangled in a peculiar case involving a missing Scottish nobleman and a fortune that seems to vanish like Highland mist. Bob Bailey's measured, world-weary narration guides you through candlelit halls and shadowy corridors as Johnny untangles a web of claims, counterclaims, and suspicious circumstances. The atmospheric sound design—creaking floorboards, the distant howl of wind across the moors, the clink of whisky glasses in a cold stone keep—transports you straight to the heart of mystery. By the episode's end, Johnny has more questions than answers, and the real intrigue is only beginning to unfold.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* was radio's thinking man's detective show, and Bob Bailey's five-year tenure as the character (1955-1960) represented the series at its most sophisticated. Unlike the gunplay and fisticuffs of typical noir fare, Johnny solved cases through cunning, conversation, and careful observation—the perfect vehicle for radio drama's intimate medium. The show's meticulous attention to procedural detail and Bailey's naturalistic delivery gave audiences something genuinely intelligent to sink their teeth into, week after week.

If you've never experienced Johnny Dollar's particular brand of mystery-solving, or if you're a devoted fan seeking to revisit this classic case, settle in with a cup of coffee and prepare yourself for one of radio's finest moments. The 1956 season finds the show firing on all cylinders—don't miss the first installment of this Scottish adventure.
