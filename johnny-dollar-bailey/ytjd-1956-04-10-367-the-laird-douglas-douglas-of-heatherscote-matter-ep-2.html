# The Laird Douglas Douglas Of Heatherscote Matter – Episode 2

Johnny Dollar's Scottish case grows more tangled in this April 1956 episode, where the insurance investigator finds himself deeper in a labyrinth of Highland deception. The fog thickens—both literally and figuratively—as our hero navigates the misty moors and shadowy manors of Heatherscote, where nothing is quite as it seems and everyone has something to hide. With Bob Bailey's distinctive rasp cutting through the static, Dollar pieces together the second chapter of a mystery involving inheritance, identity, and old-world secrets that refuse to stay buried. The tension mounts as new suspects emerge and familiar faces reveal unexpected depths, pulling listeners further into this international caper where a humble insurance claim becomes a gateway to something far more sinister.

This episode represents *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* at its peak popularity, when the show had found the perfect formula that would captivate audiences throughout the mid-1950s. Bob Bailey's portrayal of Dollar had become legendary—the thinking man's private eye, more cerebral than his hardboiled contemporaries, yet equally capable with his fists and wits. CBS's sponsorship and the show's consistent Tuesday evening slot made it a fixture in American living rooms, and episodes like this multi-part adventure showcased why the series earned its reputation as one of radio's finest detective dramas. The Scottish setting allowed writers to expand beyond the typical urban American noir, creating exotic atmosphere without sacrificing the show's trademark realism.

Step back in time to 1956 and experience the golden age of radio mystery at its finest. Settle in with *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* and discover what kept millions of listeners on the edge of their seats, week after week. This is detective storytelling pure and simple—no visual distractions, just masterful writing, compelling performances, and the timeless appeal of a mystery that demands to be solved.
