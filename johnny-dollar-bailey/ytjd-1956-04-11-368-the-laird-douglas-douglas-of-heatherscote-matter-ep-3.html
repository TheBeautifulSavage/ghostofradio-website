# Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar - The Laird Douglas Douglas of Heatherscote Matter (Episode 3)

The fog rolls thick over the Scottish highlands as insurance investigator Johnny Dollar finds himself ensnared in a web of aristocratic secrets and dangerous deception. In this third installment of a five-part case, Dollar's sharp wits and sharper instincts are tested by a tangle of Scottish inheritance claims, dubious identities, and the kind of family intrigue that makes a man wonder if the truth is worth the trouble—or the danger. Bob Bailey's iconic deadpan delivery carries you through shadowy corridors and urgent telephone conversations, where every accent and alibi could conceal murder. The mystery deepens as Johnny works the angles, collecting his famous daily fee while piecing together whether the charming Laird Douglas is exactly who he claims to be. It's mystery radio at its finest: smart, stylish, and absolutely impossible to predict.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* stands as one of the most respected detective shows of the 1950s, with Bailey's portrayal of the world-weary insurance investigator becoming legendary among radio enthusiasts. Unlike the superpowered sleuths and bumbling comedians of earlier radio, Johnny Dollar was grounded, realistic, and refreshingly cynical—a working man who followed the money and the logic, not hunches and heroics. The show's intricate five-part cases, like this Scottish adventure, allowed for genuine narrative complexity that kept listeners puzzled and engaged across an entire week's broadcasts.

Tune in and follow the thread as Johnny Dollar unravels the mystery of the Laird. With Bailey's iconic narration guiding you through each clue and confrontation, you'll experience what made this show a beacon of intelligent drama during radio's golden age—a time when imagination and voice could transport millions into the darkest corners of the human heart.
