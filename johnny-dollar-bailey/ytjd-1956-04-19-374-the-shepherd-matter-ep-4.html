Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar (Bob Bailey) CBS · 1956

The Shepherd Matter

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

When the fog rolls in thick over the docks and a missing person becomes a matter of cold, hard dollars and cents, Johnny Dollar finds himself in the murky gray world where insurance claims collide with human desperation. "The Shepherd Matter" pulls listeners into a labyrinth of deception where nothing is quite as it seems—not the victim's pristine reputation, not the grieving family's motives, and certainly not the trail of evidence that Johnny must painstakingly unravel. Bob Bailey's weary, methodical voice guides us through dingy hotel rooms and shadowy street corners, his cigarette-smoke delivery cutting through the noir atmosphere with the precision of a man who's learned that insurance fraud often wears an honest face. The stakes are high, the clues are contradictory, and somewhere in this tangled web lies the truth—if Johnny can find it before another body turns up.

This episode exemplifies why "Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar" became the longest-running dramatic series in radio history. Rather than relying on shootouts or fantastical plots, the show built its reputation on intelligent, character-driven mysteries grounded in the mundane world of insurance investigation. Bob Bailey's 1955-1960 tenure brought a particular gravitas to the role, transforming Johnny Dollar from a comic relief character into a genuine hard-boiled detective whose cases felt disturbingly plausible. In 1956, when this episode aired, Americans were captivated by stories that revealed the corruption lurking beneath their postwar prosperity.

For those seeking genuine noir atmosphere served through the golden age of radio, "The Shepherd Matter" delivers exactly what makes this era unforgettable. Settle in, dim the lights, and let Bob Bailey's voice transport you to a world where insurance claims hide darker secrets than anyone anticipated.
