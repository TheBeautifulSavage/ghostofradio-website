Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar (Bob Bailey) CBS · 1956

The Shepherd Matter

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

When insurance investigator Johnny Dollar arrives in the sleepy mountain town of Millbrook to investigate a suspicious livestock claim, he finds himself ensnared in a web of rural deception far more dangerous than any city con he's encountered. A shepherd's prized flock has vanished without a trace, and the evidence points everywhere and nowhere at once. As Dollar prowls through fog-shrouded pastures and cramped farmhouse parlors, each witness spins a different tale—and each one carries the ring of plausible truth. The investigation becomes a cat-and-mouse game played out in hushed conversations and meaningful silences, where the real crime may be hidden beneath decades of small-town secrets. Bob Bailey's weathered voice cuts through the rural darkness like a cigarette ember in the night, his characteristic blend of world-weary skepticism and dogged determination the only reliable compass in a landscape of deception.

"The Shepherd Matter" exemplifies why *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* became the benchmark for sophisticated radio drama between 1955 and 1960. While competitors relied on gunfire and melodrama, CBS's answer-man detective stories unfolded with the methodical precision of actual investigative work. Bob Bailey's portrayal—a sharp departure from the hard-boiled shouting matches of earlier detective shows—brought an intellectual rigor to the format, allowing listeners to puzzle alongside Dollar as he separated fact from fiction. These weren't tales of derring-do; they were puzzles of human nature, greed, and the lies ordinary people tell to survive.

Settle in with the static and the distant sound of mountain wind. *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* awaits—where the real mystery isn't what happened to the flock, but why people are willing to kill to keep it hidden.
