Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar (Bob Bailey) CBS · 1956

The Lonely Hearts Matter

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

Step into the rain-slicked streets of 1956 as Johnny Dollar, the world's most honest insurance investigator, takes on a case that cuts deeper than most. When a wealthy widow's bequest mysteriously disappears, Dollar finds himself tangled in a web of forged love letters, desperate con artists, and the bitter loneliness that preys on the hearts of the grieving. Bob Bailey's distinctive drawl—weary yet incisive—guides you through smoky hotel lobbies and cramped boarding rooms where everyone has something to hide. The case unfolds with the precision of a Swiss watch and the emotional weight of a Hemingway short story, as Dollar peels back layers of deception to uncover the truth. With each carefully gathered clue and tense interrogation, the investigator edges closer to a culprit whose only real crime may be understanding the hunger for human connection all too well.

Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* represented something revolutionary for radio audiences of the mid-1950s: serialized storytelling that treated listeners as intelligent adults capable of following complex, morally ambiguous narratives. Unlike the clear-cut heroes of earlier radio dramas, Bailey's Johnny Dollar inhabited a grayer world of insurance fraud, ethical compromises, and the collateral damage of American ambition. "The Lonely Hearts Matter" exemplifies this approach, refusing to reduce its antagonists to simple villains while maintaining the crackling energy and procedural detail that made the show essential listening for millions tuning in each week.

Don't miss this masterwork of noir radio drama, where the real investigation isn't just about recovering stolen money—it's about understanding what drives ordinary people to extraordinary desperation. Tune in now and discover why *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* remains the gold standard of detective radio drama.
