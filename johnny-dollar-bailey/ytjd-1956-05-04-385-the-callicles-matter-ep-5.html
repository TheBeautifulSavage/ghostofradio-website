Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar (Bob Bailey) CBS · 1956

The Callicles Matter

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

When insurance investigator Johnny Dollar steps off the train in a fog-shrouded coastal town, he knows something isn't right. A widow's claim for her husband's death seems straightforward enough—until Johnny uncovers a tangle of forged documents, missing witnesses, and a dame with secrets that could bury more than one man. As he digs deeper into the Callicles affair, the walls close in, and every answer leads to a more dangerous question. Bob Bailey's trademark rapid-fire delivery crackles with urgency as Johnny navigates back alleys and smoke-filled offices, where trust is a luxury and death comes cheaply. The tension builds with each commercial-break cliffhanger, pulling listeners deeper into a mystery where nothing—and no one—can be taken at face value.

Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* arrived at CBS in 1955 as something fresh in the golden age of radio drama: a hardboiled insurance investigator whose cases were always "interesting"—a catchphrase that became part of radio folklore. Bob Bailey's portrayal set the standard for the character across three decades of broadcasts, capturing the weary sophistication of a man who'd seen every con and heard every lie. The show's five-minute episodes—compact narratives crammed with plot, atmosphere, and snappy dialogue—proved that radio drama didn't need sprawling time slots to captivate millions. This 1956 episode exemplifies the series at its peak, blending genuine mystery with the sophisticated noir sensibility audiences craved.

Settle into your chair, dim the lights, and let Johnny Dollar take you on another case where the stakes are high and the truth is stranger than fiction. *The Callicles Matter* awaits.
