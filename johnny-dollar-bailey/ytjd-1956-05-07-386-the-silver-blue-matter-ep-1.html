Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar (Bob Bailey) CBS · 1956

The Silver Blue Matter

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

The rain hammers against the windows of a San Francisco insurance office as Johnny Dollar finds himself tangled in a web of art forgery, murder, and a dame who can't be trusted—though that's never stopped him before. In "The Silver Blue Matter," the world-weary insurance investigator must track down a stolen painting worth enough to make grown men kill, navigating through shadowy galleries, seedy nightclubs, and the kind of dangerous characters who deal in lies as easily as currency. Bob Bailey's distinctive gravelly voice cuts through the atmospheric sound design—the screech of tires, the muffled shots, the ominous violin that underscores each wrong turn—as Dollar inches closer to the truth, his expense account growing fatter with each perilous clue. This is quintessential mid-1950s radio noir, where every telephone conversation could be your last and the line between victim and villain blurs in the smoky darkness.

Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* became an institution precisely because it took the insurance adjuster—typically a background character in crime stories—and made him the hero of his own hard-boiled adventures. Airing during radio's final golden age, the show offered something detective fiction hadn't fully explored: an ordinary man doing an extraordinary job, solving crimes not for justice alone but for his paycheck. Bailey's seven-year run became the gold standard for insurance investigator drama, influencing everything from television's later spy and crime procedurals. These 1956 episodes represent the show at peak form, when production values were lush and the writing had found its perfect stride between action, mystery, and wry humor.

Settle in with your radio, dim the lights, and let Johnny Dollar guide you through a case where nothing is quite what it seems. "The Silver Blue Matter" awaits—a masterclass in old-time radio drama that proves sometimes the best mysteries come wrapped in expensive canvas and dangerous secrets.
