Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar (Bob Bailey) CBS · 1956

The Silver Blue Matter

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

Picture this: it's late evening in a smoky San Francisco hotel corridor, and insurance investigator Johnny Dollar has just discovered a dead body in Room 412—a society dame draped across a mahogany desk, a pearl-handled revolver glinting under the desk lamp. In "The Silver Blue Matter," Johnny finds himself entangled in a web of industrial espionage, blackmail, and old money gone sour. With each commercial break, the stakes rise higher as Johnny's sharp interrogations peel back layers of lies from bitter ex-lovers, ruthless business competitors, and a mysterious fence with connections to the waterfront. Bob Bailey's distinctive gravelly voice carries you through every shadowy twist, his trademark expressiveness making every accusation bite and every revelation land like a fist in the gut. The question haunts the episode's tense final act: who wanted the victim silenced, and what exactly is the "silver blue matter" worth killing for?

By 1956, *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* had perfected the formula that made it CBS radio's crown jewel of crime drama. Bailey's portrayal of the wisecracking, incorruptible dollar-a-day man became a cultural touchstone, defining the private eye archetype for millions of listeners even as television began its assault on radio's golden age. The show's writers crafted episodic mysteries with genuine ingenuity, avoiding the formulaic trap that plagued lesser detective programs. Each case felt lived-in, grounded in real procedural detail and human complexity, with Johnny Dollar functioning less as a pulp hero and more as a cynical but principled observer of the American underworld.

Don't miss this masterclass in radio mystery. Settle into your favorite chair, dim the lights, and let Bob Bailey transport you back to an era when a man's voice and a few carefully placed sound effects could construct entire worlds of suspense. *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* awaits.
