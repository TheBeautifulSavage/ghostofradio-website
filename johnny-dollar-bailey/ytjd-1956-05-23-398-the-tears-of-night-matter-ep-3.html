Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar (Bob Bailey) CBS · 1956

The Tears Of Night Matter

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

When insurance investigator Johnny Dollar arrives in a rain-slicked San Francisco to examine a suspicious claim, he finds himself tangled in a web of blackmail, betrayal, and a woman whose tears might be genuine—or the perfect performance. In this 1956 episode, Bob Bailey's world-weary detective voice cuts through the fog and neon of a city where trust is currency and everyone's got something to hide. Dollar must navigate smoky nightclubs and shadowy back alleys, piecing together a puzzle where the smallest detail—a mislaid photograph, a trembling voice on the telephone, a convenient "accident"—could be worth hundreds of dollars or a human life. The orchestral jazz scoring swells and recedes like the tides, underscoring moments of quiet dread and sudden violence as our hero inches closer to a truth that someone is desperate to keep buried.

Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* stands as the gold standard of radio's insurance investigator dramas, and Bob Bailey's portrayal remains definitive—a hard-boiled everyman unafraid to pursue cases into the darkest corners of post-war America. The show's episodic format, with stories contained in single broadcast hours, created tight, propulsive narratives that demanded complete attention. By 1956, when this episode aired, Bailey had perfected his craft, delivering dialogue with the precise cadence of a man who'd seen everything and trusted nothing. The show's realism—grounded in actual insurance procedures and contemporary crime—gave listeners a procedural authenticity that elevated it beyond mere melodrama.

Settle in with a glass of something strong and dial in to *The Tears of Night Matter*. In sixty minutes, you'll discover why Johnny Dollar became radio's most memorable investigator, and why CBS audiences kept him on the air for five golden years.
