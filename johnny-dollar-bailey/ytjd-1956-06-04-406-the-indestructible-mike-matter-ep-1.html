Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar (Bob Bailey) CBS · 1956

The Indestructible Mike Matter

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

When Johnny Dollar arrives in a rain-slicked alley behind a shuttered warehouse, he finds himself entangled in a case that defies logic itself. A man who should be dead—Mike Matter, swindler and two-bit hustler—surfaces in the shadows like an unwelcome ghost, and suddenly Johnny's simple insurance claim transforms into a maze of double-crosses, false identities, and desperate men willing to kill to keep the past buried. As Bob Bailey's world-weary voice cuts through the static and crackle of 1950s sound design, listeners are pulled into the smoky underbelly of postwar America, where nothing is quite what it seems and everyone has something to hide. The ticking sound effect that marks each passing moment of peril echoes like a countdown to disaster.

"Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar" stands as a masterwork of radio suspense, one of the medium's last golden-age jewels before television claimed the nation's attention. During its run from 1955 to 1960, the show won accolades for its realistic dialogue, intricate plotting, and Bob Bailey's incomparable performance—a private investigator who solved cases not through superhuman feats but through dogged persistence and hard-boiled wisdom. Each episode was a complete mystery, making "The Indestructible Mike Matter" representative of why audiences tuned in faithfully: genuine storytelling crafted for the ear, where imagination supplied what the screen could not.

Settle into your chair, dim the lights, and let the opening theme transport you back to 1956. This is radio at its finest—a fully realized world conjured by voice, sound, and narrative artistry. Johnny Dollar awaits, and Mike Matter's secrets won't stay buried for long.
