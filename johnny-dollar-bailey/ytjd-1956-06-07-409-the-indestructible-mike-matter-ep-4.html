Step into the rain-slicked streets of 1956 as Johnny Dollar takes on a case that defies belief—a man who seemingly cannot be killed. With each new attempt on Mike Matter's life, the danger escalates and the mystery deepens. Bob Bailey's trademark weary-yet-determined delivery carries listeners through a labyrinth of double-crosses, hidden identities, and desperate men willing to do anything for survival. As Johnny digs deeper, the line between accident and conspiracy blurs dangerously. Who is really trying to kill Mike Matter, and why? The answer lies buried somewhere in the city's underworld, waiting to trap our hero in a web of lies more tangled than anything he's encountered before. This episode crackles with the kind of genuine suspense that made millions tune in faithfully each week.

Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* stands as one of radio's most enduring achievements, and Bob Bailey's portrayal of the quick-witted insurance investigator remains unsurpassed. During these golden 1955-1960 CBS years, the show perfected a formula that balanced hard-boiled noir atmosphere with genuine mystery-solving. Bailey's naturalistic approach—rarely shouting, always thinking aloud—made listeners feel like they were sitting across from Johnny himself, cigarette smoke curling between them as he pieced together impossible cases. "The Indestructible Mike Matter" exemplifies the show's mastery, combining pulp thrills with surprisingly sophisticated storytelling.
