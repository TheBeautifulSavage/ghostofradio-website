Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar (Bob Bailey) CBS · 1956

The Pearling Matter

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

Picture this: a humid night in an exotic port town, the smell of salt water mingling with cigarette smoke, and Johnny Dollar nursing a highball as another case lands square in his lap. In "The Pearling Matter," our tireless insurance investigator finds himself tangled in a web of smuggling, deception, and murder that stretches from the Australian outback to the glittering salons of Manhattan's elite. A valuable pearl shipment has vanished, and the trail of clues leads through shadowy characters who'd kill to keep their secrets buried. With Bob Bailey's distinctive gravelly voice pulling you deeper into the mystery with each dead-end and unexpected twist, this episode delivers exactly what made the show a phenomenon: smart dialogue, genuine danger, and the sense that nothing is quite what it appears to be in the insurance game.

By 1956, "Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar" had become CBS's answer to hard-boiled detective fiction, proving that radio's golden age wasn't fading but evolving. While television was beginning to steal audiences away, the show thrived because it offered something visual media couldn't quite capture—the intimate immediacy of a man's voice confessing his troubles directly into your ear. Bob Bailey, who played Dollar from 1955 through the show's final year, brought a weariness and authenticity to the role that transformed the insurance investigator from a dry bureaucrat into a genuine antihero navigating a morally ambiguous world. Each episode featured Bailey as a one-man tour guide through America's underbelly, where greed, passion, and desperation collided in stories ripped from the day's headlines.

Settle in with "The Pearling Matter" and rediscover why millions of listeners made this show essential radio. You'll understand why, even as television conquered the nation, Johnny Dollar refused to fade away.
