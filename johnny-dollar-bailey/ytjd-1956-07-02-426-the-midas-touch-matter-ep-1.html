Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar (Bob Bailey) CBS · 1956

The Midas Touch Matter

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

Step into the rain-slicked streets of 1956 San Francisco, where everything that glitters is decidedly not gold. When a seemingly straightforward insurance claim lands on Johnny Dollar's desk, the silver-tongued operative finds himself entangled in a web of counterfeit currency, desperate smugglers, and a femme fatale whose motives are as murky as the fog rolling across the bay. Every dollar—both literal and metaphorical—is at stake in this masterclass of misdirection and double-crosses. Bob Bailey's smooth narration guides you through the maze with the precision of a seasoned gumshoe, while the expertly layered sound design—the *crack* of a gunshot echoing off warehouse walls, the sultry jazz notes bleeding through a nightclub door—transports you completely into Johnny's world. This is insurance investigation as high art, where spreadsheets become crime scenes and routine paperwork conceals extraordinary danger.

"Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar" represented the golden age of episodic radio drama at its finest, particularly during these CBS years when the show hit its creative stride. Unlike the detectives of pulp fiction, Johnny Dollar investigated real insurance claims—stolen jewels, suspicious fires, vanished heiresses—lending the series a uniquely grounded authenticity that made every case feel disturbingly plausible. Bob Bailey's five-year tenure became legendary among radio enthusiasts for his ability to convey world-weariness, quick thinking, and surprising warmth within the span of a thirty-minute episode.

If you've never experienced the particular thrill of Johnny Dollar unraveling a seemingly impossible case, "The Midas Touch Matter" is the perfect entry point into this beloved series. Pour yourself a drink, dim the lights, and let the voice of Bob Bailey remind you why millions huddled around their radio sets night after night—some mysteries never grow old.
