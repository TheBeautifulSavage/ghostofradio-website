The Midas Touch Matter

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

Step into the rain-slicked streets of 1956 San Francisco where nothing glitters quite as dangerously as gold—especially when a wealthy industrialist's fortune becomes the centerpiece of a murder investigation that threatens to consume everyone around it. In this week's episode of *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar*, our intrepid insurance investigator finds himself tangled in a web of greed, jealousy, and secrets that span continents and generations. When a claim lands on Johnny's desk involving a supposedly insured fortune that's mysteriously vanished, he must navigate corrupt business partners, desperate heirs, and a femme fatale whose motives are as opaque as the fog rolling through the Bay. Bob Bailey's world-weary narration pulls you deeper into the darkness with each commercial break, his trademark deadpan delivery cutting through the orchestral tension like a knife through silk.

This episode exemplifies what made *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* the crown jewel of CBS radio's golden age—a show that proved detective fiction could thrive in episodic radio format without sacrificing intelligence or sophistication. Bailey's portrayal of Dollar became iconic precisely because it grounded the show in psychological realism rather than mere plot mechanics; listeners didn't just follow cases, they inhabited Johnny's exhausted, cynical mindset. The 1950s production values—the crisp sound design, the live orchestration, the meticulous foley work—create an immersive experience television couldn't yet match, making radio the supreme medium for this brand of intimate noir storytelling.

Tune in now and discover why *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* maintained a devoted audience throughout the decade. It's insurance investigation as poetry, mystery as meditation, and radio at its absolute finest.
