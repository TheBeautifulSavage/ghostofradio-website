Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar (Bob Bailey) CBS · 1956

The Midas Touch Matter

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

When insurance investigator Johnny Dollar arrives in San Francisco to investigate a claim involving a murdered jeweler and a missing cache of rare gemstones, he finds himself tangled in a web of greed, blackmail, and family secrets that stretches from the waterfront dives to the penthouses of Nob Hill. As Johnny navigates smoky hotel lobbies and dangerous nighttime rendezvous, his sharp wit and sharper instincts become his only allies against shadowy figures who will stop at nothing to keep their secrets buried. With each clue leading deeper into a labyrinth of corruption, listeners are drawn into an atmospheric tale of desire and desperation where nothing—and nobody—is quite what they seem. Bob Bailey's distinctive voice captures every nuance of Johnny's internal conflict as he uncovers a truth far more complex and troubling than any simple case of theft.

Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* represents the apex of radio drama's detective genre, transforming the insurance investigator into an unlikely American hero. Unlike hard-boiled detectives driven by justice or personal vengeance, Johnny Dollar works for the actuarial bottom line, yet his investigations consistently reveal the profound human cost of greed and moral compromise. The 1955-1960 CBS run featuring Bailey became the definitive version of the character, lauded for its sophisticated writing and Bailey's naturalistic performance that made listeners feel they were eavesdropping on a real man solving real crimes. This episode exemplifies why the show became a listener favorite: compelling storytelling wrapped in the authentic atmosphere of mid-1950s America.

Step into the neon-lit shadows of *The Midas Touch Matter* and discover why this episode remains a treasured gem in radio's golden age. The case begins at sundown.
