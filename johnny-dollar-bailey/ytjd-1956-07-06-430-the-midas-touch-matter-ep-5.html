Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar (Bob Bailey) CBS · 1956

The Midas Touch Matter

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

When Johnny Dollar steps off the train in San Francisco, he thinks it's just another routine insurance investigation—a missing shipment of gold bullion, a closed case waiting to be cracked open. But within hours of checking into his hotel, he discovers that everyone connected to the case is either dead or desperately afraid for their lives. As fog rolls across the bay and the city's neon signs blur into the gathering darkness, our man from the insurance company finds himself trapped in a web of blackmail, murder, and a conspiracy that reaches far deeper than anyone suspected. With only his wits, his cigarette lighter, and a snub-nosed .38, Johnny must navigate the shadowy underworld of wartime profiteers and desperate men before he becomes the next body the police find floating in the harbor.

Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* was the very last dramatic anthology series to premiere on network radio, arriving just as television was already stealing audiences away from the medium. Yet Bob Bailey's portrayal of the everyman detective became legendary precisely because he brought an unhurried, naturalistic style to the noir formula—no grandstanding, no unnecessary dramatics, just a tired professional trying to solve puzzles and stay alive. The show's meticulous attention to insurance details, locations, and procedural realism set it apart from the pulp fantasies of radio's earlier years, creating a world that felt urgent and authentic even as the medium itself was fading into history.

This is golden-age radio at its twilight, capturing the last glimmer before the curtain fell. If you've never experienced Johnny Dollar's particular brand of hard-boiled reasoning and quick thinking, *The Midas Touch Matter* is the perfect entry point—tense, expertly paced, and utterly absorbing from the moment that train whistle blows. Tune in.
