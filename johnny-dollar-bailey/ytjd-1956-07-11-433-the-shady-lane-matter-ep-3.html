Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar (Bob Bailey) CBS · 1956

The Shady Lane Matter

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

When Johnny Dollar steps off the train in the fog-wrapped suburbs of Connecticut, he finds himself tangled in a web of deception that stretches from a modest colonial mansion to the highest echelons of Hartford's insurance underworld. A wealthy widow's claim seems straightforward enough—until Johnny discovers that nothing in the Shady Lane matter is what it appears to be. With only his wits, a worn expense book, and the kind of dogged persistence that has made him the most trusted investigator in the business, Johnny must untangle a conspiracy involving forged documents, a suspicious housemaid, and a murder that everyone swears never happened. The Connecticut darkness presses in around him as he navigates drawing rooms and dim speakeasies, each clue drawing him closer to a truth that someone is willing to kill to keep buried. Bob Bailey's world-weary narration guides you through every twist with the cadence of a man who has seen too much but forgotten nothing.

Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* represented something revolutionary in 1950s radio—a complete departure from the superhero serials and comedic fare that dominated the airwaves. Here was an everyman detective, grounded in the mundane reality of insurance claims, yet operating in a richly noir universe of corruption and moral ambiguity. Each episode of this CBS classic was built on a single claim investigation, with Bailey's distinctive delivery creating an intimate bond between listener and protagonist. The show proved that radio audiences craved authenticity and complexity, that a man with an expense book could be just as compelling as any caped crusader.

Step into the rain-slicked streets and mahogany-paneled offices of 1956. *The Shady Lane Matter* awaits your discovery—a masterclass in investigative drama that will remind you why radio's golden age still captivates the imagination today.
