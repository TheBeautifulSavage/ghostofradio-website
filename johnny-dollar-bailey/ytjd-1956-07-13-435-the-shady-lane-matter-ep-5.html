Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar (Bob Bailey) CBS · 1956

The Shady Lane Matter

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

When Johnny Dollar steps off the train into the fog-shrouded streets of coastal Connecticut, he's after more than just another insurance claim—he's chasing shadows that refuse to stay buried. A widow's inheritance hangs in the balance, a suspicious fire tells lies only a seasoned investigator can expose, and someone in this picturesque town would rather see Johnny silenced than see the truth revealed. With nothing but his wits, a pack of cigarettes, and an expense account the network kept ruthlessly in check, Dollar navigates a maze of deceptions where every suspect has motive and every alibi conceals a darker secret. Bob Bailey's world-weary narration pulls you into the investigation with documentary precision, his expense account becoming a character itself—a running gag that somehow deepens the realism of a man scraping by on per diem and determination. The sound design crackles with authenticity: the hiss of rain on awnings, the menacing silence of empty rooms, and the quick snap of dialogue that cuts through pretense like a detective's accusation.

Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* represented the last great golden age of radio drama, when sponsors still believed in substantive crime narratives and CBS invested in quality that television would eventually claim. Bailey's portrayal elevated the series beyond typical detective fare—his Johnny Dollar was no hard-boiled cliché, but a working man whose vulnerability and integrity made him instantly compelling. The show's meticulous attention to detail and psychological complexity offered listeners something rarer than mere entertainment: genuine mystery and genuine character.

Tune in tonight and discover why Johnny Dollar endures. The Shady Lane Matter awaits—and Johnny Dollar *always* gets to the bottom of things.
