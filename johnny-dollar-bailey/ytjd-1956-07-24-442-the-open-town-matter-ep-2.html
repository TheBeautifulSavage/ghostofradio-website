Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar (Bob Bailey) CBS · 1956

The Open Town Matter

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

When Johnny Dollar arrives in the dusty border town of San Lázaro, he finds a case that smells worse than the desert heat pressing down on its weathered storefronts. A missing person, a questionable insurance claim, and a town where everybody's got something to hide—including the local law. As Bob Bailey's world-weary voice guides you through smoky saloons and cramped hotel rooms, the tension mounts with each clue that leads nowhere and every witness who knows too much. There's violence lurking beneath the surface of this "open town," where corruption runs deeper than the Rio Grande, and Johnny must navigate a treacherous landscape of double-crosses and dangerous secrets to uncover the truth before it's buried forever.

By 1956, *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* had become the gold standard of radio mystery programming—a show that proved you didn't need a sponsor's jingle or comedy relief to keep America glued to their sets. Bob Bailey's portrayal of the hard-boiled insurance investigator set a new standard for the genre, trading in the broader theatrics of earlier detective shows for something grittier and more psychologically complex. The show's innovative "open-ended" format, where listeners heard only Johnny's side of conversations, created an intimacy and immediacy that film and television struggled to match. Episodes like "The Open Town Matter" showcased what made the series essential listening: meticulous writing, atmospheric sound design, and Bailey's ability to convey world-weariness and moral conviction in equal measure.

Tune in and discover why *Johnny Dollar* captivated millions during radio's final golden age. The case is waiting.
