Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar (Bob Bailey) CBS · 1956

The Sea Legs Matter

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

When Johnny Dollar steps aboard the *SS Meridian* with nothing but his wits and a pocket full of expense receipts, he finds himself adrift in a fog thicker than the Atlantic itself. A fortune in smuggled jewels has vanished from a locked cabin, a ship's captain lies dying from a mysterious poisoning, and the crew harbors secrets as deep as the ocean trenches below. Bob Bailey's distinctive drawl cuts through the maritime mystery like a knife, as our intrepid insurance investigator navigates the cramped quarters and paranoid passengers of a vessel where everyone has something to hide. Every creak of the ship's timbers, every mournful blast of the horn, every whispered conversation in dimly-lit corridors pulls listeners deeper into a case where the sea itself becomes a character—isolating, indifferent, and treacherous.

Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* represents the golden age of American radio drama at its finest, and this 1956 episode exemplifies why the show became a listener favorite throughout its five-year CBS run. Bailey's masterful performance transformed the insurance investigator into an everyman hero—pragmatic, world-weary, yet fundamentally decent. Unlike the superhuman detectives of earlier radio serials, Johnny Dollar felt real, grounded in the mundane world of claim adjustments and investigative legwork. The show's meticulous attention to procedural detail gave listeners a fascinating window into 1950s insurance practices, while the sophisticated writing and intricate plotting kept audiences guessing right until the final revelation. *The Sea Legs Matter* showcases this perfect balance between educational realism and compelling entertainment.

Tune in to experience radio mystery the way millions did in 1956—where the only special effects are the ones created in your imagination, and a voice in the darkness can transport you anywhere on earth.
