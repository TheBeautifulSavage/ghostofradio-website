Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar (Bob Bailey) CBS · 1956

The Sea Legs Matter

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

Picture this: the salt-spray mist of a fogbound harbor at midnight, the creak of ship's timbers groaning against their moorings, and somewhere in the darkness, a dead man with sand in his pockets and lies in his past. When insurance investigator Johnny Dollar steps off the dock onto the *S.S. Meridian*, he's hunting for answers in a case where everyone aboard has something to hide—and someone has everything to lose. Bob Bailey's distinctive rapid-fire narration guides you through narrow companionways and dimly-lit cabins as Johnny untangles a web of maritime fraud, blackmail, and murder. With each interview and clue, the tension tightens like a ship's hawser, building to a climax that will leave you breathless. This episode exemplifies what made *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* the thinking listener's detective show: smart dialogue, authentic detail, and a protagonist as comfortable in a stevedore's bar as in an insurance company boardroom.

During the mid-1950s, when television was still finding its footing, radio had rediscovered its power through shows like this one. *Johnny Dollar* ran for five exceptional seasons on CBS, becoming the last great commercial detective series in radio's golden age. Bob Bailey's portrayal—more sardonic and world-weary than his pulp predecessors—captured a post-war America learning to distrust easy answers. Each episode was a self-contained mystery that rewarded close listening, written with the precision of a balance sheet and the soul of a noir novel.

Tune in tonight and discover why millions of Americans made *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* part of their evening ritual. One case, one night, one man determined to get at the truth—no matter how deep the darkness runs.
