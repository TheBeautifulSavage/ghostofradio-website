Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar (Bob Bailey) CBS · 1956

The Alder Matter

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

When insurance investigator Johnny Dollar arrives in the rain-slicked streets of a nameless industrial city, he discovers that the Alder case is far more twisted than any policy claim ought to be. A missing shipment, a nervous widow, and a cast of characters who seem to know far more than they're willing to admit set the stage for one of the most labyrinthine mysteries of Dollar's career. Bob Bailey's iconic narration guides listeners through the fog with the weary confidence of a man who's seen every con in the book—and invented a few himself. The soundscape crackles with authentic detail: typewriter keys clacking in shadowy offices, the ambient hum of a late-night diner, the sinister click of a closing door. By the time Johnny uncovers the truth buried beneath layers of misdirection and greed, listeners will find themselves thoroughly entangled in a web that feels startlingly real.

Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* stood apart from its competitors during radio's golden age by prioritizing authentic investigative procedure over sensational theatrics. Running from 1955 to 1960, the show featured Bob Bailey as the thinking man's detective—cerebral, methodical, and utterly believable. "The Alder Matter" exemplifies this approach, eschewing cheap plot twists for genuine psychological complexity and the grinding reality of insurance work. The episode showcases the remarkable sound design that made CBS's crime dramas legendary, transforming everyday locations into noir landscapes that played entirely within the listener's imagination.

For anyone seeking a masterclass in old-time radio drama, *The Alder Matter* is an unmissable entry point. Tune in and discover why audiences huddled around their sets night after night, waiting to hear what Johnny Dollar would uncover next.
