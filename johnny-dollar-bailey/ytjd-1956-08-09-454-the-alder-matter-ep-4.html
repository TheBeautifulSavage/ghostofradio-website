Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar (Bob Bailey) CBS · 1956

The Alder Matter

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

When insurance investigator Johnny Dollar arrives in the fog-shrouded streets of San Francisco, he finds himself tangled in a web of betrayal that cuts deeper than any claim he's investigated before. The Alder Matter pulls listeners into a labyrinth of questionable witnesses, forged documents, and a client whose story keeps shifting like sand beneath their feet. Bob Bailey's trademark vocal precision captures every nuance of suspicion and doubt—the slight hesitation when a story doesn't add up, the hardened cynicism of a man who's seen too many lies wrapped in expensive suits. As Johnny digs deeper into the case, the tension mounts with each revelation, crackling through the static like live electricity. The sound design is immaculate: the lonely wail of foghorns, the distant clatter of cable cars, the sinister quiet of empty offices where secrets are kept. By the episode's climax, listeners will find themselves as bewildered and intrigued as Johnny himself.

Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* represented something revolutionary in radio drama during the mid-1950s—the procedural investigation brought to vivid life, where the audience followed not a superhero or a master detective with implausible powers, but an ordinary man doing extraordinary detective work for insurance companies. Bob Bailey's version of the character became iconic precisely because of his ability to convey world-weariness and moral complexity without ever resorting to melodrama. Episodes like The Alder Matter showcase the show's strength: taking seemingly mundane insurance claims and transforming them into genuine mysteries that could happen to anyone.

Tune in to *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* and step into the shadows where insurance claims become crime scenes. The Alder Matter awaits—and Johnny Dollar never leaves a case unsolved.
