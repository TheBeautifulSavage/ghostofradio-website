Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar (Bob Bailey) CBS · 1956

The Alder Matter

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

Johnny Dollar lights another cigarette in the smoky waiting room of the Consolidated Insurance building, his notepad already thick with the kind of details that most people overlook—the nervous twitch in a witness's eye, the inconsistency in a timeline, the way someone's story comes apart under gentle pressure. In "The Alder Matter," our man faces a case that seems straightforward enough on the surface: a fire, a claim, a question of arson. But as Johnny digs deeper into the lives and secrets of the people surrounding the Alder estate, he discovers that the truth is far more tangled than anyone wanted him to find. With each scene, the tension tightens like a noose—conversations crackle with subtext, danger lurks in unexpected places, and you'll never quite be sure who's lying until that final, satisfying revelation. Bob Bailey's weathered voice guides you through every twist, his Johnny Dollar a man who's seen enough human nature to know that greed, passion, and desperation are far more destructive than any fire.

By 1956, "Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar" had become something of a phenomenon among radio enthusiasts—a show that proved the medium could deliver sophisticated crime drama with the texture of real detective work. Bailey's portrayal elevated the insurance investigator from a mere plot device into a fully realized character, someone who understood that his job was less about recovering money and more about understanding the hearts of broken, desperate people. The show's serialized format meant listeners tuned in night after night, hooked by mysteries that unfolded with novelistic complexity.

If you appreciate crime stories built on clever dialogue, atmospheric tension, and genuine uncertainty about where the plot will turn next, "The Alder Matter" is essential listening. Settle in, pour yourself something strong, and let Johnny Dollar show you why radio drama still matters.
