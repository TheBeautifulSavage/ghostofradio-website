As the winter rain hammers against the windows of a remote Alpine hotel, insurance investigator Johnny Dollar finds himself cornered between lies, betrayals, and a secret that someone will kill to keep buried. In this fourth installment of *The Kranesburg Matter*, the case spirals into darker territory—a missing emerald, a woman with two identities, and a murder that may not have been murder at all. Bob Bailey's world-weary narration carries us through fog-shrouded Swiss streets and into dimly-lit hotel corridors where every suspect has motive and nobody can be trusted. The tension crackles as Johnny inches closer to the truth, but with each answer he uncovers, the danger multiplies. By the episode's end, you'll understand why he's been called to this godforsaken place—and why getting out alive may be the hardest case of all.

Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* was CBS radio's crown jewel of investigative drama, running from 1954 to 1962 with Bob Bailey delivering some of the finest hard-boiled narration ever committed to magnetic tape. The show's strength lay in its serialized storytelling—these five and six-part cases were novellas unfolding across an entire week, allowing for genuine character development and plot intricacy that the typical single-episode format couldn't match. The Kranesburg Matter showcases exactly why the show won critical acclaim: sophisticated writing, atmospheric sound design, and Bailey's incomparable ability to make listeners believe every word of his world-weary investigations.

Don't miss the penultimate chapter of this masterwork of radio noir. Tune in for *The Kranesburg Matter, Part Four*—where one wrong move could cost Johnny Dollar everything.
