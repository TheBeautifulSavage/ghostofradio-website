Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar (Bob Bailey) CBS · 1956

The Meg's Palace Matter

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

Picture this: it's a fog-shrouded San Francisco waterfront, and insurance investigator Johnny Dollar finds himself tangled in a web of blackmail, corrupted longshoremen, and a desperate nightclub owner who may have signed away more than just her business to the wrong people. In "The Meg's Palace Matter," our silver-tongued hero must navigate the seamier side of the Bay City, where every witness has something to hide and the line between victim and perpetrator blurs like neon through rain-streaked glass. Bob Bailey's weathered, world-weary delivery cuts through the crackling tension as Johnny peels back layers of deception—and the pace is relentless, keeping you on edge from the moment that first saxophone wail pierces the darkness.

Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* stands as one of radio's finest achievements in the hardboiled detective tradition, and Bailey's five-year run with CBS represents the show at its absolute peak. Here was a program that understood the medium's greatest strength: the human voice, stripped bare, could conjure entire cities and moral universes without a single visual flourish. Each episode was a self-contained short story, meticulously crafted, with Bailey bringing a depth of characterization and authentic weariness that elevated the genre beyond mere pulp. The show ran five nights a week, making it a vital appointment for listeners who craved intelligent, adult entertainment in an era when radio was being eclipsed by television.

Don't miss your chance to step back into 1956 and experience one of radio's finest practitioners at work. Settle in, dim the lights, and let Johnny Dollar guide you through *The Meg's Palace Matter*—where nothing is as it seems, and a single missing detail might just cost someone everything.
