Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar (Bob Bailey) CBS · 1956

The Meg's Palace Matter

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

Step into the fog-choked streets of San Francisco's Barbary Coast as insurance investigator Johnny Dollar confronts a mystery as murky as the bay itself. When a priceless jade collection vanishes from the notorious Meg's Palace—a gilded den of vice where fortunes are won and lost on the turn of a card—Johnny finds himself navigating a labyrinth of blackmail, jealousy, and long-buried secrets. Bob Bailey's world-weary voice guides you through smoky gambling halls and dimly-lit back rooms, where every suspect has motive and nobody's telling the truth. The tension builds with each revelation, each false lead, until Johnny must separate the con artists from the truly desperate, and uncover which of Meg's Palace's colorful denizens would kill to keep their past hidden. This is hard-boiled detective work at its finest—no magic, no gimmicks, just a sharp investigator with his wits and his case notes.

Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* stands as one of radio's last great achievements, arriving just as the medium faced its twilight in the television age. Bob Bailey's portrayal became the definitive Johnny Dollar across multiple networks and runs, bringing an authentic weariness and intelligence to a character who represented the everyman detective—more accountant than action hero, yet somehow more compelling for it. The show's tight scripts and stellar guest casts made each adventure feel like a complete film noir experience, and the 1955-1960 CBS run showcases the program at peak sophistication.

If you've never experienced the golden age of radio drama, or if you're a devoted fan seeking that perfect case file, *The Meg's Palace Matter* delivers everything that made Johnny Dollar essential listening: atmosphere, intrigue, and the voice of Bob Bailey turning mystery into pure audio magic. Tune in and let the investigation begin.
