The Meg's Palace Matter

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

Picture this: it's a fog-choked evening in San Francisco, 1956, and insurance investigator Johnny Dollar is about to step into the most tangled case of his career—one that begins with a simple claim and spirals into blackmail, passion, and murder at the notorious Meg's Palace nightclub. Bob Bailey's weary, world-wise voice cuts through the static as Johnny discovers that some debts can't be settled with money alone. With each clue uncovered, the web tightens around a cast of desperate characters, from the club's enigmatic proprietor to the society dame with everything to lose. You'll hear the clink of glasses, the wail of a saxophone, and the unmistakable click of a revolver being loaded. This is noir radio at its finest—a tale where nothing is quite what it seems and trust is the rarest commodity in the game.

Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* was the golden age of radio investigation, and this 1956 episode exemplifies why CBS's series became one of the most celebrated crime dramas of the medium's twilight years. Bob Bailey's portrayal of the hardboiled insurance dick set the standard for radio protagonists—smart, cynical, and incorruptible in a corrupt world. What distinguished the show was its obsessive attention to detail and its willingness to let cases unfold with the messy complexity of real detective work. The "Meg's Palace Matter" showcases this perfectly, layering multiple suspects and motives into a narrative that keeps listeners guessing until the final revelation.

So dim the lights, tune in your radio, and let Johnny Dollar guide you through the shadows of San Francisco's underworld. *The Meg's Palace Matter* awaits—a case that proves sometimes the biggest insurance claims come with the highest human cost.
