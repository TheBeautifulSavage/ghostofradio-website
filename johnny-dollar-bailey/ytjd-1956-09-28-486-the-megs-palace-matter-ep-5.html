Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar (Bob Bailey) CBS · 1956

The Meg's Palace Matter

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

Picture this: it's late evening in San Francisco's Chinatown, and insurance investigator Johnny Dollar has just stepped into the kind of trouble that makes seasoned operatives nervous. A fabulous jade necklace—the Meg's Palace—has vanished from the safe of a nightclub owner with connections that run deeper than the fog rolling in off the bay. As Dollar pursues leads through dimly-lit establishments and shadowy back alleys, he discovers that everyone involved has something to hide, and nobody's story quite adds up. Danger lurks in every corner conversation, and by the time the final act rolls around, you'll be gripping the edge of your seat, uncertain whether the missing treasure will ever surface—or whether Johnny himself will make it out unscathed. The atmospheric sound design brings you straight into the investigation: the clink of glasses, the shuffle of feet on wet pavement, the crackle of tension in every exchange.

Bob Bailey's portrayal of Johnny Dollar became legendary during the show's CBS run from 1955 to 1960, a period many consider the golden age of audio drama. Bailey's distinctive delivery—rapid-fire, intimate, full of world-weary knowing—became the voice of hard-boiled insurance investigation, each case a compact noir masterpiece where the real mystery often lay in human nature itself. "Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar" represented radio's final flourish as a dramatic medium, before television would claim the nation's attention.

If you've never experienced Johnny Dollar's particular brand of investigative intrigue, *The Meg's Palace Matter* is the perfect place to begin. Tune in and let yourself be transported back to an era when radio created entire worlds in your imagination—no image required, just the finest storytelling a golden-age ear could demand.
