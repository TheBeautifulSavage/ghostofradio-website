Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar (Bob Bailey) CBS · 1956

The Picture Postcard Matter

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

When a simple postcard arrives at the desk of insurance investigator Johnny Dollar, it sets in motion a chain of events that will pull him from the glittering casinos of Las Vegas into the shadowy underbelly of a blackmail scheme that reaches into the highest echelons of society. What begins as a routine investigation spirals into a web of deception, misdirection, and danger—where nothing is quite what it seems and every clue leads deeper into the mystery. As Bob Bailey's gravelly voice guides you through the neon-lit nights and desperate encounters, you'll find yourself riveted by the meticulous detective work, the snappy dialogue, and the constant threat lurking just beyond each commercial break. The picture postcard itself becomes an obsession, a puzzle piece that Johnny must fit into place before time runs out.

Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* was the gold standard of radio mystery dramas, and for good reason. Debuting nationally in 1955, the show represented the twilight flowering of radio drama, combining the sophisticated storytelling of American noir fiction with the intimate immediacy of the medium itself. Each episode, narrated entirely from Johnny's perspective, became a masterclass in how to sustain tension and character development within a tight narrative structure. Bob Bailey's portrayal of the everyman detective—professional, clever, yet vulnerable to the perils around him—made Johnny Dollar an archetype that would influence television and film detectives for decades to come.

Step into the world of mid-1950s America and experience why listeners tuned in religiously each week. *The Picture Postcard Matter* delivers everything that made this series unforgettable: mystery, atmosphere, and one of radio's finest dramatic performances. Don't miss it.
