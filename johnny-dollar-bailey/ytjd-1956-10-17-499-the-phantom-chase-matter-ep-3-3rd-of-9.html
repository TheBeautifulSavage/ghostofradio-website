When Johnny Dollar arrives in the fog-shrouded streets of San Francisco, he's already three steps behind a mystery that seems designed to keep him perpetually off-balance. An insurance claim that shouldn't exist, a client who may be lying through his teeth, and a chase that materializes only to vanish like smoke—this is the terrain of *The Phantom Chase Matter*, where nothing is quite as it seems and danger lurks in the spaces between what Johnny knows and what he merely suspects. In this third installment, our intrepid investigator finds himself tangled in a web of conflicting testimonies and suspicious circumstances, where every answer spawns two new questions. Bob Bailey's world-weary narration guides you through rain-slicked alleyways and dimly-lit offices, his voice carrying the unmistakable weariness of a man who's seen too much of human nature's seamy underside. The tension builds methodically—not through frantic action, but through the creeping realization that Johnny may be chasing a phantom, or worse, that someone wants him to.

This episode exemplifies why *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* became CBS's most celebrated dramatic offering of the mid-1950s. Bailey's performance set the gold standard for the insurance investigator archetype, blending hard-boiled cynicism with genuine human decency. These nine-part story arcs were unusual for radio drama, allowing for the narrative complexity and character development that kept listeners coming back night after night. The show's attention to procedural detail and psychological intrigue gave it a sophistication that elevated it above typical crime programming of the era.

Tune in and let the fog roll in. Johnny Dollar's got a case that won't let him sleep, and neither will you.
