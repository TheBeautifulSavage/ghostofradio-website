When insurance investigator Johnny Dollar arrives in the fog-shrouded streets of San Francisco, what begins as a routine claim quickly spirals into a bewildering game of cat and mouse. A man is being hunted—or is he the hunter? With each clue leading nowhere and shadows seeming to move of their own accord, Johnny finds himself caught between a phantom adversary and a mystery so convoluted that even his sharp investigative instincts struggle to pierce the darkness. The tension crackles through this sixth episode as danger closes in from unexpected angles, forcing our hero to question everyone he meets, including himself. Bob Bailey's weary but determined narration perfectly captures the paranoia and gritty determination of a man desperate to separate fact from fiction before the case consumes him entirely.

Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* stands as one of broadcasting's finest achievements—a show that elevated the insurance investigation into high art, proving that procedural drama didn't need gangsters or spies to captivate millions. Airing on CBS during the golden age of radio, the series was groundbreaking for its serialized "matter" format, where complex cases unfolded over multiple episodes with genuine narrative momentum. Bailey's portrayal transcended the typical hard-boiled archetype; Johnny Dollar was intelligent, vulnerable, and refreshingly honest about the moral ambiguities he encountered. "The Phantom Chase Matter," spanning nine episodes in 1956, exemplifies the show's sophisticated storytelling—a labyrinthine puzzle that rewards patient listeners willing to trust the narrator's journey into uncertainty.
