Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar (Bob Bailey) CBS · 1956

The Silent Queen Matter

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

When Johnny Dollar steps off the train in Atlantic City on a sweltering August evening, he finds himself entangled in a web of high-stakes blackmail that threatens to topple one of the East Coast's most prominent jewelry families. A priceless diamond necklace—the legendary "Silent Queen"—has vanished from a sealed vault, and the only clue is a cryptic photograph left behind by the thief. As Johnny navigates the glittering boardwalk casinos and the shadowy backrooms where deals are made in whispers, he discovers that the heist is merely the surface of something far more sinister. With each lead he follows, the case grows darker: forged documents, a mysterious woman with ties to the European underworld, and a murder that Johnny suspects he's being framed for. Bob Bailey's Johnny is at his finest here—world-weary but relentless, firing off rapid-fire dialogue while keeping his wits sharp enough to stay one step ahead of both the criminals and the cops.

This 1956 episode represents the show at peak form, during the golden age when *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* had become CBS radio's most audacious crime drama. Bailey's naturalistic delivery and the show's innovative sound design—featuring everything from shattering glass to the rhythmic ticking of a clock marking Johnny's deadline—made listeners feel as though they were riding along in the passenger seat. What set the series apart was its refusal to play things safe; stories dangled moral ambiguities, and Johnny himself was no perfect hero, just a man trying to collect his fee while navigating a world of greed and desperation.

Tune in and discover why audiences huddled around their radios every week to follow Johnny Dollar's cases—where the mysteries were labyrinthine, the danger was real, and redemption was always just out of reach.
