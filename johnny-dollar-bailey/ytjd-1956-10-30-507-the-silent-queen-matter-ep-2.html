Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar (Bob Bailey) CBS · 1956

The Silent Queen Matter

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

When Johnny Dollar steps off the train in Atlantic City on a crisp November evening, he's expecting a routine insurance claim—a stolen diamond necklace, a society theft, the usual. But the "Silent Queen," a legendary 47-carat stone with a history steeped in European intrigue, proves to be anything but routine. As Dollar navigates the shadowy corridors of high-society hotels and the shadier backrooms of the boardwalk, he discovers that every person connected to the jewel has something to hide—and someone wants to keep those secrets buried. With each clue uncovered, the danger tightens like a noose, and Dollar must use every ounce of his wits and street savvy to distinguish between red herrings and genuine threats. The static-laced phone calls, the mysterious woman in the red coat, the nervous fence with too many answers—all of it points toward a conspiracy far deeper and more dangerous than insurance fraud.

This 1956 episode exemplifies the golden age of *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* under Bob Bailey's incomparable stewardship. Bailey's rapid-fire delivery and nuanced portrayal of the everyman detective set a new standard for radio drama, making Johnny Dollar feel less like a pulp hero and more like a genuine investigator working the American underbelly. The show's "matter" format—where each case ran multiple episodes—allowed for complex plotting and character development impossible in earlier detective serials. It's a masterclass in noir atmosphere translated perfectly to audio.

Tune in and discover why *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* became one of radio's final golden successes, a show that proved the medium still had life left to live. Johnny Dollar's voice—weary, determined, unflinching—will guide you through every twist of this captivating case.
