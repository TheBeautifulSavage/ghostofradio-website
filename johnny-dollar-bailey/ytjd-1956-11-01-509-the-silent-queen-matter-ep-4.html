Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar (Bob Bailey) CBS · 1956

The Silent Queen Matter

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

Picture this: a dame walks into an insurance office with a story that doesn't add up, and Johnny Dollar's got forty-eight hours to crack it before her claim expires. In "The Silent Queen Matter," our intrepid insurance investigator finds himself tangled in the disappearance of a priceless diamond known only by whispered rumors—a stone so valuable that silence itself becomes currency. The night air crackles with tension as Johnny's fedora-clad silhouette moves through fog-shrouded hotel corridors and smoky back rooms, his wry narration cutting through each scene like a knife. You'll hear the desperate edge in Bob Bailey's voice as he uncovers lies layered upon lies, each revelation pulling him deeper into a web where the most dangerous secret is the one nobody's willing to speak aloud.

By 1956, *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* had established itself as CBS's crown jewel of hard-boiled detective storytelling, and Bailey's performance defined an era. Unlike the superhero posturing of some contemporaries, Johnny Dollar worked for the insurance companies—he was everyman's detective, tracking down fraud with the methodical patience of a true professional. The show's five-minute daily episodes, later expanded to full thirty-minute cases, demanded taut writing and razor-sharp acting. "The Silent Queen Matter" exemplifies this golden age when radio drama still commanded the nation's imagination, just as television was beginning to flicker in American living rooms.

Tune in to discover whether Johnny can talk the truth out of his tightest-lipped suspects before time runs out. In a world where keeping quiet might be worth a fortune—or a life—silence has never been so dangerous.
