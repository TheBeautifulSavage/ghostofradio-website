# Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar – The Trans Pacific Matter (Part B)

Settle in with a strong cup of coffee and prepare yourself for international intrigue on the high seas. This November evening in 1952, insurance investigator Johnny Dollar finds himself tangled in a web of deception spanning continents, where every clue leads deeper into shadowy waters and trusted associates reveal hidden motives. John Lund's audition performance crackles with the weary confidence of a man who's seen too much and believes too little—his voice cutting through the atmospheric fog of San Francisco's waterfront like a knife through silk. As Johnny Dollar pursues the Trans Pacific Matter, listeners will discover that the most valuable cargo isn't always what's listed on the manifest, and that in this world of maritime fraud and international espionage, a man's word is worth considerably less than the insurance premium on his lies.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* represented something revolutionary for the detective drama format. Rather than rely on a stable supporting cast or recurring locations, each episode featured Johnny Dollar as a freelance insurance investigator tackling standalone cases with only his wits, a magnifying glass, and an expense account. This particular episode marked John Lund's audition for the role—a test to see if the film and radio actor possessed the right blend of noir cynicism and quick-thinking heroism the part demanded. CBS was searching for a new voice to anchor their fall programming, and Lund's performance here would prove instrumental in shaping what would become one of radio's most beloved mystery series.

Don't miss this rare glimpse into broadcasting history, where talent met opportunity and a classic was born. Tune in now to experience the moment Johnny Dollar came to life.
