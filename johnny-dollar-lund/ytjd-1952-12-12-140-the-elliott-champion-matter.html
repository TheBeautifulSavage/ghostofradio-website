# The Elliott Champion Matter

When Johnny Dollar arrives in a fog-shrouded coastal town to investigate the death of shipping magnate Elliott Champion, he finds himself tangled in a web of maritime intrigue where every suspect has something to gain and no one's story quite adds up. Was it accident, suicide, or something far more sinister? As Johnny peels back the layers of Champion's seemingly respectable empire, he uncovers hidden debts, jealous rivals, and a fortune that's worth killing for. The sea air itself seems to harbor secrets in this taut episode, where the insurance angle becomes a lens into human nature at its most desperate and corrupt.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* represented something revolutionary in early 1950s radio—the antihero investigator who worked for money, not justice, and who examined each case with hardboiled pragmatism rather than moral certainty. Unlike the police procedurals and detective shows that dominated the airwaves, Johnny Dollar was an insurance investigator, and CBS's commitment to authenticity meant each episode tackled genuine claims scenarios with procedural rigor wrapped in noir atmosphere. John Lund's portrayal captured a world-weary professionalism that seemed distinctly modern even then—a working man in a dangerous trade, where the cases were real but the dangers were equally genuine. During its brief but influential run from 1952 to 1955, the show became a template for the insurance investigator genre and influenced everything that followed.

Don your fedora and settle into the static-crackled darkness. Johnny Dollar is on the case, and "The Elliott Champion Matter" awaits—a masterclass in radio suspense where every clue matters and the insurance ledger tells the true story of greed and betrayal.
