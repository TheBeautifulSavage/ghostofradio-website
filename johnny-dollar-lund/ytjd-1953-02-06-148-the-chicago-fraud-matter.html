# Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar: The Chicago Fraud Matter

Picture this: a snow-dusted Chicago street corner, the elevated train rumbling overhead, and insurance investigator Johnny Dollar stepping into a case thick with deception and danger. In "The Chicago Fraud Matter," broadcast on February 6th, 1953, our intrepid investigator finds himself ensnared in a web of false claims and shadowy figures who'll stop at nothing to keep their scheme hidden. As Johnny himself might say, the expense account is running hot—and so is the case. With each clue, the stakes climb higher, and you'll find yourself drawn deeper into the murky world of big-city corruption where a man's word means nothing and a dame's smile can be your undoing.

John Lund's portrayal of Johnny Dollar became the gold standard for the hard-boiled insurance investigator during the early 1950s. The character, obsessed with meticulous record-keeping and running a tally of his case expenses, offered audiences something unique: a detective protagonist driven not by justice alone but by professional duty and the simple ledger of an honest day's work. *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* thrived during CBS's golden age, when radio drama still commanded millions of listeners hungry for sophisticated storytelling. Episodes like this one showcased the medium's ability to craft intricate, fast-paced narratives in just thirty minutes—a tightly wound spring of suspense that left audiences breathless.

Don't miss this classic slice of 1950s noir atmosphere. Slip on your overcoat, tune in your receiver, and follow Johnny Dollar into the Chicago underworld. The mystery awaits, and the meter's running.
