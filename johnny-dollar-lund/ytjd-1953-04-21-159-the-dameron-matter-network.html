# The Dameron Matter

Picture this: a rain-slicked street corner somewhere between the seediest gin joints and the glitziest hotel lobbies of 1950s America. Johnny Dollar, expenses notebook in hand and a cigarette dangling from his lips, finds himself entangled in the Dameron case—a labyrinth of claims, counterclaims, and carefully constructed lies that would make the average insurance adjuster reach for the whiskey bottle. Over thirty minutes of taut, snappy dialogue and genuine mystery, listeners are pulled into a world where nothing is quite what it seems, where a routine claim investigation spirals into something far darker and more dangerous. John Lund's world-weary narration guides you through the fog of deception with the confidence of a man who's seen every con in the book, even if this one's got a fresh twist. The orchestra swells, footsteps echo in empty corridors, and you'll find yourself holding your breath as Johnny inches closer to the truth.

What made *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* truly exceptional among the golden age of radio was its commitment to the procedural real-ity of insurance investigation—a refreshingly unglamorous world compared to typical noir detectives. These weren't cases solved by lucky gunplay; they were unraveled through dogged questioning, careful observation, and good old-fashioned American business acumen. Broadcasting on CBS during the show's peak years, it captured listeners with authentic, character-driven storytelling that proved you didn't need murder and mayhem to keep an audience riveted.

If you've never experienced Johnny Dollar's particular brand of metropolitan mystery, "The Dameron Matter" is the perfect entry point. Tune in and discover why radio audiences lined up week after week to follow this insurance man into the shadows.
