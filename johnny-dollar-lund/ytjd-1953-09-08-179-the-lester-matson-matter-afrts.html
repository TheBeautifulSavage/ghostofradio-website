# The Lester Matson Matter

The rain hammers against the windows of a small-town insurance office as Johnny Dollar lights another cigarette, squinting at the photograph of a man who may—or may not—be dead. When a routine claim threatens to unravel into something far darker, our intrepid investigator finds himself navigating the murky intersection of greed, deception, and small-town secrets. The Lester Matson matter seems straightforward enough on the surface, but Johnny knows better than to trust surfaces. As the hours tick away and the lies pile up higher than the case files on his desk, the stakes grow personal. In this September 1953 episode, you'll hear the sharp crackle of noir atmosphere that made Johnny Dollar unmissable radio—the clipped dialogue, the cynical wisdom of a man who's seen too much, and that signature moment when the truth hits like a sucker punch nobody saw coming.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* was the golden standard of insurance investigator drama, a show that proved the mundane could be thrilling when filtered through the world-weary eyes of John Lund's unforgettable protagonist. CBS knew it had struck gold with this series, running it through the early 1950s as audiences couldn't get enough of Johnny's cases—each one a self-contained mystery that delivered genuine suspense without relying on gimmicks or melodrama. This particular episode, preserved in AFRTS transcription, captures the show at its peak, when listeners tuned in faithfully, knowing they'd get intelligent writing, tight direction, and performances that crackled with authentic tension.
