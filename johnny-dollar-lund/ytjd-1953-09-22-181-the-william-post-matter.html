# Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar – "The William Post Matter"

Step into the rain-slicked streets of post-war America as insurance investigator Johnny Dollar pursues a case that promises easy money but delivers only lies, blackmail, and betrayal. When a man named William Post disappears under suspicious circumstances, Johnny must navigate a maze of conflicting testimonies and hidden motives, his sharp instincts and sharper tongue the only tools that separate truth from fiction. The September 22nd, 1953 episode crackles with authentic noir tension—the kind only radio could deliver—as each twist in the investigation pulls our hero deeper into a world where appearances deceive and everyone has something to hide. Hear the unmistakable sound design of 1950s CBS radio bringing these shadowy scenes to life: the hiss of cigarette lighters, the distant wail of police sirens, and John Lund's distinctive voice narrating Johnny's thoughts with world-weary precision.

What made Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar a standout during radio's golden age was its relentless commitment to procedural realism combined with genuine entertainment. Unlike the superhero spectacles dominating the airwaves, this show treated its audience as intelligent adults, crafting insurance cases that felt plausible while maintaining the atmospheric dread of classic detective fiction. The series became famous for its innovative tape-recorded format and its ability to blend hardboiled cynicism with surprising moments of human vulnerability—Johnny Dollar was neither invincible nor infallible, qualities that made him unforgettable.

This episode exemplifies everything that made the show essential listening in 1953. Whether you're a devoted fan revisiting a classic case or a newcomer discovering Johnny Dollar for the first time, "The William Post Matter" delivers authentic mystery wrapped in the warm crackle of vintage radio.
