# The Philip Morey Matter

Picture this: a fog-choked Manhattan evening in October 1953, where insurance investigator Johnny Dollar finds himself tangled in a web of lies that stretches from the glittering penthouses of Park Avenue to the shadowy docks of the East River. When a routine claim investigation into the death of prominent businessman Philip Morey takes a sinister turn, Johnny discovers that nothing about this case is what it seems—not the grieving widow with her convenient alibi, not the business partner with everything to gain, and certainly not the mysterious woman who keeps appearing in the margins of his investigation. With nothing but his wits, a expense account limited by his penny-pinching insurance company, and the kind of hard-boiled determination that comes from years in the game, Johnny must unravel the truth before another body turns up. The city closes in around him with each revelation, the orchestra swelling ominously as danger lurks around every corner.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* stands as one of radio's most innovative entries into the detective genre, moving beyond the typical gumshoe formula to center every episode around a single, meticulously detailed case. During its peak years on CBS, the show became a benchmark for sophisticated serialized storytelling, with John Lund's world-weary delivery and the show's noir sensibilities perfectly capturing the anxious mood of post-war America. These episodes represent a golden age when radio audiences craved smart, adult entertainment delivered with impeccable production values and intelligent writing.

If you've never experienced a genuine vintage radio mystery in its full atmospheric glory—complete with live orchestral scoring, sound effects creating an entire world through audio alone, and the kind of tight plotting that rewards careful listening—this is the episode to start with. Tune in and let Johnny Dollar solve the Morey case the old-fashioned way.
