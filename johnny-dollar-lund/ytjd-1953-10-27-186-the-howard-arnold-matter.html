# The Howard Arnold Matter

The fog rolls thick over a rain-slicked harbor as insurance investigator Johnny Dollar finds himself entangled in a case that promises easy money but delivers only danger. When a routine claim investigation into a missing person threatens to uncover something far more sinister, Johnny must navigate the treacherous waters between grieving family, suspicious authorities, and his own growing conviction that nothing in this case is what it appears to be. With each interview, each shadowy corner he explores, the walls seem to close tighter around him. This October evening in 1953, Dollar's trademark cool competence is tested against a mystery that grows more complex—and more deadly—with every passing hour.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* stands as one of the most enduring jewels of radio's golden age, and this episode exemplifies why the show captivated millions during its run on CBS. Unlike the fantastical adventures of contemporaries like *The Shadow* or *The Green Hornet*, Johnny Dollar inhabited a grittier, more realistic world of insurance fraud, missing persons, and the small-town secrets that lurk beneath respectable surfaces. John Lund's portrayal brought a weary professionalism to the role—a man of modest means doing difficult work for modest pay, armed with nothing but his wits and an expense account he meticulously documented. The show's writers crafted tightly plotted narratives that rewarded careful listeners, drawing inspiration from actual investigative techniques and the mundane yet compelling realities of the insurance trade.

If you've never experienced the particular thrill of radio noir—that intoxicating blend of genuine mystery, expert voice acting, and sound design that lets your imagination do the heavy lifting—*The Howard Arnold Matter* is your invitation into one of broadcasting's most sophisticated entertainments. Settle in, dim the lights, and prepare yourself for an evening with a man who solves mysteries one carefully documented dollar at a time.
