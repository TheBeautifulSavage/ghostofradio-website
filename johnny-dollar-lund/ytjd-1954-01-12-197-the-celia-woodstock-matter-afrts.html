# The Celia Woodstock Matter

When Johnny Dollar walks into the offices of his insurance company on this cold January evening in 1954, he has no idea that the Celia Woodstock case will pull him into a web of secrets, false identities, and the kind of danger that no claim settlement could ever compensate for. As the preliminary investigation unfolds through crackle-laden phone lines and shadowy encounters in dimly-lit rooms, listeners will hear Dollar's distinctive voice—that perfect blend of world-weary detective and methodical professional—piecing together a puzzle where nothing is quite what it seems. The stakes mount with every clue uncovered, the tension building as Johnny realizes that someone has a vested interest in keeping the truth buried.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* became the gold standard of insurance investigator noir during CBS's golden age, and John Lund's portrayal of the titular character defined the era. What made the show revolutionary was its commitment to realism: rather than pure fantasy, each episode grounded its mystery in the actual procedures and protocols of claim investigation, making the danger feel genuinely urgent rather than contrived. Broadcast for educational purposes to American servicemen through AFRTS (Armed Forces Radio and Television Service), episodes like this one carried a special weight, reaching soldiers stationed across the globe who tuned in for escapism and found instead a reflection of the cunning and deception they themselves might encounter in post-war life.

Here is your chance to experience radio drama at its finest—a masterclass in atmospheric storytelling where every sound effect, every pause, every inflection of Lund's voice works to create a complete world. Tune in to *The Celia Woodstock Matter* and discover why listeners made this show an institution.
