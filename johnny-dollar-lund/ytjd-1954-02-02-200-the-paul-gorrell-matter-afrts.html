# The Paul Gorrell Matter

On a rain-slicked February evening in 1954, insurance investigator Johnny Dollar steps into a case that will test both his wits and his conscience. When a routine claim investigation leads him into the shadowy world of embezzlement and betrayal, Johnny finds himself caught between corporate interests and a man whose desperation might excuse—or condemn—his crimes. With each clue uncovered, the moral landscape grows murkier, and Johnny must decide whether justice and the law are always one and the same. The Paul Gorrell Matter is quintessential Dollar: sharp dialogue crackles like electricity, the ambient sounds of nocturnal cities create an almost tactile sense of danger, and the case pivots on Johnny's ability to see what others miss—not just facts, but the human truth beneath them.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* arrived on CBS Radio during the golden age of detective noir, when millions of listeners tuned in to follow their heroes through America's criminal underworld. John Lund's portrayal of Johnny Dollar was definitive—a freelance investigator billing out his per diem with dry humor and unflinching moral clarity. The show ran from 1952 to 1955, establishing itself as one of radio's most sophisticated crime dramas, where cases were solved through deduction rather than gunplay, and where a single misread motive could unravel everything. This particular episode survives in the AFRTS (Armed Forces Radio and Television Service) archives, a reminder of how radio shows were shared with servicemen stationed around the globe.

If you've never experienced Johnny Dollar's particular brand of investigative intensity, *The Paul Gorrell Matter* is the perfect entry point—a tightly constructed mystery that showcases why radio drama, when done right, rivals anything television would later produce. Tune in and discover why millions hung on Johnny's every word.
