# The Harpooned Angler Matter

Picture a fog-shrouded New England wharf where fishing boats creak against their moorings and the salt spray mingles with something far more sinister—the stench of murder. When insurance investigator Johnny Dollar arrives in this desolate coastal town, he finds himself tangled in a web of maritime mystery that goes far deeper than a simple drowning. A prominent angler lies dead, harpooned through the chest under circumstances that suggest either a tragic accident or calculated vengeance. As Johnny peels back the layers of small-town secrets—jealous rivals, hidden debts, and lovers intertwined with deceit—listeners will find themselves swept into the kind of noir nightmare that made radio's golden age unforgettable. The fog horn's mournful wail punctuates every revelation, every false lead, every twist that brings our intrepid investigator closer to a truth someone would kill to keep buried.

From 1952 to 1955, *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* stood as CBS's crown jewel of detective programming, with John Lund's dry, world-weary narration and quick wit defining an entire era of radio noir. The show's brilliance lay in its absolute commitment to the procedural—Johnny was an insurance investigator, not a police detective, pursuing mysteries with a pencil and expense report rather than a badge and revolver. This AFRTS recording (distributed to Armed Forces Radio Service stations serving American troops overseas) represents the show at its peak, when writers had perfected the formula of combining hardboiled atmosphere with genuinely puzzling mysteries that rewarded careful listening.

For anyone seeking an authentic taste of 1950s radio drama—where sound effects painted pictures words alone could never achieve—this episode is essential listening. Tune in and discover why *Johnny Dollar* captivated millions of Americans huddled around their radios, waiting for the next installment of danger and deception.
