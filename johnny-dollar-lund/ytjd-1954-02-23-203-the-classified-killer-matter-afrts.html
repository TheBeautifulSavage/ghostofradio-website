# The Classified Killer Matter

When insurance investigator Johnny Dollar arrives at a Manhattan newspaper office on a cold February evening in 1954, he steps into a labyrinth of secrets and shadows where every classified ad might conceal a motive for murder. A dead reporter, a stack of mysterious advertisements, and a killer who communicates through the want columns set the stage for one of the season's most ingeniously plotted cases. As Johnny methodically pieces together the coded messages hidden in plain sight, listeners will find themselves entangled in a web of deception where the everyday newspaper becomes a weapon, and each page turn could mean life or death. The episode crackles with authentic Manhattan noir atmosphere—the clack of typewriters, the murmur of the city room, and Johnny's weary, incisive narration guiding us through a case that demands both street smarts and intellectual cunning.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* represented something revolutionary in 1950s radio drama: a procedural that treated its audience like adults, favoring genuine detective work over melodrama. Starring John Lund as the titular dollar-a-day operative, the show became CBS's answer to the demand for sophisticated crime entertainment during radio's final golden age. Each episode cost Johnny exactly one dollar, a gimmick that anchored the show in practical reality even as the cases spiraled into the fantastical. By 1954, the show had refined its formula into near-perfect form, blending hardboiled dialogue with intricate plotting that rewarded careful listening.

Step into the newsroom with Johnny Dollar and experience one of old-time radio's finest hours. Whether you're a devoted fan or discovering this classic for the first time, *The Classified Killer Matter* showcases everything that made radio drama essential listening for millions of Americans. Tune in and solve the case yourself.
