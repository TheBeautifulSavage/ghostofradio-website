# Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar: The Punctilious Firebug Matter

It's a sweltering May evening in 1954, and insurance investigator Johnny Dollar finds himself ensnared in a labyrinth of arson and obsession that defies easy answers. When a meticulous firebug begins torching properties with surgical precision—each blaze a calculated statement rather than a desperate crime—Dollar must navigate the treacherous intersection of criminal psychology and high-stakes insurance fraud. The episode crackles with tension as our hero peels back layer after layer of motive, uncovering a suspect whose twisted logic proves far more dangerous than mere greed. With each sinister revelation, the case grows darker, the stakes higher, and Johnny's trademark quick wit becomes both his shield and his sword against an adversary who prizes perfection as much as destruction.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* stands as one of radio's most enduring achievements, a show that transformed the insurance investigator into an unlikely hero for the atomic age. Running on CBS from 1952 to 1955, the series pioneered a documentary-style realism that set it apart from other detective fare. John Lund's dry, world-weary delivery brought authenticity to each case, while the show's focus on genuine insurance matters—from maritime disputes to arson—grounded the drama in a world audiences recognized. The writing was sharp, economical, and deeply influenced by the noir tradition, yet it maintained an intellectual edge that appealed to both casual listeners and serious radio enthusiasts.

This is radio at its finest: a perfectly crafted forty-five-minute journey into moral ambiguity and human nature, where the lines between victim and perpetrator blur dangerously. Settle in, turn down the lights, and let Johnny Dollar guide you through *The Punctilious Firebug Matter*—a case that will linger long after the final fade-out.
