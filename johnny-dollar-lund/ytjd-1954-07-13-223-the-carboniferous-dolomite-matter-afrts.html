# The Carboniferous Dolomite Matter

Picture this: it's a sweltering July evening in 1954, and insurance investigator Johnny Dollar steps off a train into a web of industrial intrigue that will test his quick wits and quicker fists. The Carboniferous Dolomite Matter unfolds like a blueprint for danger—a case involving valuable mineral claims, suspicious characters with everything to lose, and the kind of double-cross that leaves an honest man wondering whom to trust. With the crack of fedora-brim noir dialogue and the atmospheric sound of creaking floorboards and ringing telephones, this episode pulls you directly into Johnny's office and out into the field where greed meets ruthlessness. Every expense—every incidental fee—matters when you're working the Johnny Dollar way, and this case promises plenty of both danger and mystery to justify every penny the insurance company will grudgingly reimburse.

What made *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* essential listening in post-war America was its star John Lund's authentic delivery of the hard-boiled investigator archetype combined with the show's meticulous attention to radio craft. Unlike the increasingly formulaic detective shows flooding the airways, Johnny Dollar episodes presented real human stakes wrapped in procedural detail, where the smallest clue could unravel the entire case. This particular episode, preserved in the Armed Forces Radio and Television Service archives, captures the show at its peak during its CBS years—when radio drama still commanded the rapt attention of millions and a single voice in the darkness could hold an entire nation spellbound.

Tune in now and join Johnny Dollar as he navigates the murky world of industrial theft and personal vengeance. The Carboniferous Dolomite Matter awaits—bring your attention, your imagination, and your willingness to believe that radio's golden age deserved every bit of its glory.
