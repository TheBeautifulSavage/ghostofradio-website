# The Port Au Prince Matter

When insurance investigator Johnny Dollar steps off the plane into the sultry darkness of Port-au-Prince, he knows trouble is waiting—he can taste it in the humid Caribbean air. A missing shipment worth thousands, whispered accusations of theft, and a cast of characters with everything to hide: corrupt dock workers, desperate businessmen, and a mysterious woman whose motives remain deliciously unclear. As Johnny navigates the narrow streets and shadowed back rooms of Haiti's capital, the tension mounts with each interview, each discovery, each carefully laid trap. Edmond O'Brien's measured, cynical narration guides you through this web of deception like a cigarette smoke curling through a darkened office, his voice the steady heartbeat of an investigation that grows more dangerous by the minute.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* represented the golden age of radio noir—a time when CBS's commitment to sophisticated detective storytelling elevated the medium into genuine art. Broadcast in 1950-51, when film noir was at its cultural peak, the show brought that same cynical world-weariness and moral ambiguity to the airwaves, with O'Brien's distinctive delivery making Johnny Dollar an unforgettable protagonist. Unlike the high-flying heroics of traditional adventure serials, *Johnny Dollar* grounded its narratives in the mundane yet treacherous world of insurance investigation, where the real crime was human nature itself.

Settle into your chair, dim the lights, and let yourself be transported to a Caribbean night thick with mystery and menace. Johnny Dollar is on the case, and you won't want to miss a single moment of *The Port Au Prince Matter*.
