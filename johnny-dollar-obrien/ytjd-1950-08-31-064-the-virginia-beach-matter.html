# The Virginia Beach Matter

Picture this: the salt-stung boardwalks of Virginia Beach in the dead of night, where a missing heiress and a fortune in stolen bonds collide in a web of lies that would make even the most seasoned insurance investigator question everything he thought he knew. In "The Virginia Beach Matter," Johnny Dollar finds himself chasing shadows through a landscape of duplicitous socialites and desperate men, where every clue leads deeper into a mystery that spans from the glittering hotels of the coast to the seediest back alleys of the docks. Edmond O'Brien's distinctive voice—that perfect blend of world-weary confidence and genuine curiosity—guides you through a labyrinth of interviews and revelations, as our protagonist slowly peels back the layers of a case that seems designed specifically to confound him. The stakes climb relentlessly as Johnny realizes that someone is playing for keeps, and the Virginia Beach matter is far more sinister than a simple insurance claim.

What made *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* essential listening in 1950 was its unflinching commitment to the insurance investigation as a vehicle for genuine mystery. Unlike the detectives of radio drama who chased murderers and gangsters, Johnny Dollar investigated fraud, embezzlement, and the small but perfectly crafted cons that reflected the anxieties of postwar America—a nation suddenly awash in prosperity and paranoid about its loss. O'Brien brought a rare authenticity to the role, transforming procedural details into genuine suspense while maintaining the nimble humor that kept listeners coming back week after week.

Don't miss this glimpse into the golden age of radio drama—a time when great acting, tight writing, and vivid sound design could transport you completely to another place and time. Tune in and let Johnny Dollar unravel the mystery that still echoes from the Virginia Beach boardwalks of 1950.
