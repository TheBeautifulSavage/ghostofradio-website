# The Adam Kegg Matter

Step into the fog-shrouded streets of a city where trust is currency and every claim conceals a secret. In this November 1950 broadcast, insurance investigator Johnny Dollar finds himself entangled in *The Adam Kegg Matter*—a case that promises simple answers but delivers only deeper questions. A man's death. An insurance policy. And somewhere in between, a lie waiting to be exposed. As Edmond O'Brien's world-weary voice guides you through shadowy interviews and dangerous discoveries, you'll feel the weight of suspicion settling over each witness, each alibi, each carefully constructed story. The cigarette smoke seems to curl right through your radio speaker, and the steady ticking of a clock reminds you that time, like money, runs out for everyone.

What makes *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* a cornerstone of radio's golden age is its unflinching commitment to the procedural—Johnny doesn't chase criminals so much as chase truth, one expense report and hard question at a time. Born from the postwar boom in detective programming but grounded in insurance investigation rather than police work, the show offered something genuinely novel: a protagonist driven by evidence, logic, and the daily grind of investigative work rather than gunplay and heroics. O'Brien's Johnny is a man of the atomic age, navigating mid-century moral ambiguity with sharp dialogue and sharper instincts. This particular episode, recorded in the show's early CBS run, captures that distinctive blend at its finest.

Tune in now to *The Adam Kegg Matter* and experience radio drama at its most absorbing—where every clue matters, every conversation cuts deeper than the last, and the truth, as always, demands a price.
