# The Month End Raid Matter

Picture this: it's late evening in a smoke-filled insurance office, and insurance investigator Johnny Dollar is about to stumble into a case that'll test every ounce of his street smarts. In "The Month End Raid Matter," our hero uncovers a web of deception surrounding what appears to be a routine claim—but nothing is routine in the world of insurance fraud. As Johnny digs deeper into the month-end accounting anomalies, he discovers that someone's cooking the books, and they're willing to go to dangerous lengths to keep their secrets hidden. With each clue drawing him closer to the truth, the tension builds relentlessly; the stakes grow higher, and the threats more sinister. Edmond O'Brien's distinctive, world-weary voice guides us through shadowy hotel lobbies and tense interrogation rooms, painting a vivid noir landscape where trust is currency and deception lurks behind every corner.

What made *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* special was its commitment to authentic insurance investigation—a decidedly unglamorous subject that became absolutely compelling in the hands of CBS's talented writers and star Edmond O'Brien. Running from 1949 to 1962, the show pioneered a documentary-realism style of radio drama, grounding its mysteries in actual claim procedures and investigative techniques. O'Brien's rapid-fire delivery and razor-sharp timing became iconic, creating a character that was equal parts cynical gumshoe and meticulous professional. Each episode, presented as Johnny's case file, felt urgent and immediate—the listener wasn't just hearing a story, they were reviewing the evidence.

Turn up your radio dial and settle in for an evening of first-rate mystery storytelling. This is classic American radio drama at its finest—where the mundane world of insurance adjusters becomes a portal to noir intrigue and human nature's darker impulses. *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* awaits.
