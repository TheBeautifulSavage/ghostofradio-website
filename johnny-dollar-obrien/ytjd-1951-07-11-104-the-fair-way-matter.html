# Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar: The Fair Way Matter

When Johnny Dollar steps onto the lush grounds of an exclusive country club, he finds himself tangled in a web of jealousy, deception, and stolen diamonds that runs far deeper than any fairway. A trusted caddy has vanished, a prominent club member lies dead, and the insurance claim sitting on Johnny's desk smells worse than the morning fog rolling off the seventh hole. As our intrepid investigator digs into the genteel world of golf and old money, each interview peels back another layer of secrets—illicit affairs, gambling debts, and a missing gem that everyone swears they never saw. With danger lurking in the shadows of the clubhouse and time running out before the case goes cold, Johnny must navigate a landscape where the real hazard isn't sand traps, but the human capacity for greed and violence.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* represents the golden age of radio mystery at its finest. Starring the distinctive voice of film noir legend Edmond O'Brien, this CBS series transformed insurance investigation into high drama, proving that the mundane world of claims could yield stories as thrilling as any hard-boiled detective yarn. O'Brien's weary, intelligent delivery—his pauses perfectly timed, his sardonic observations expertly delivered—became the voice of mid-century skepticism. The show ran only briefly in 1951-52, but in that short span carved out a loyal following that recognized something special: a thinking man's detective show, where the mystery mattered as much as the atmosphere.

Slip on your headphones and step into the smoky corridors of ambition and deceit. *The Fair Way Matter* awaits, and Johnny Dollar is ready to collect—both his fee and the truth.
