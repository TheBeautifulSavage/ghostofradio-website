# Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar: The Neal Breer Matter

Step into the humid Manhattan night with insurance investigator Johnny Dollar as he unravels a case that begins with a simple claim and spirals into deception, double-crosses, and danger. When the seemingly straightforward matter of Neal Breer lands on his desk, Dollar finds himself threading through a labyrinth of conflicting stories and shadowy motives—each interview peeling back another layer of lies. With his quick wit, sharper instincts, and the ever-present tick of the clock working against him, Dollar must separate fact from fiction before someone else pays the ultimate price. Edmond O'Brien's distinctive voice—world-weary yet tenacious—guides you through smoke-filled rooms and tense confrontations as the case grows darker and more complex with every passing hour.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* arrived on CBS at precisely the moment American audiences craved sophisticated, adult mystery programming that treated them as intelligent listeners. Unlike the supernatural thrills or masked avengers populating the airwaves, Johnny Dollar operated in a grittier, more believable world—the realm of insurance fraud, corporate malfeasance, and human weakness. Edmond O'Brien brought authentic gravitas to the role, lending credibility to Dollar's investigations through crisp dialogue and naturalistic delivery. This 1951 episode exemplifies the show's golden period, when the formula was sharp and every case felt dangerously unpredictable.

If you've never experienced the particular thrill of classic radio noir, *The Neal Breer Matter* is an ideal entry point. Settle in, dim the lights, and let the crackling broadcast transport you back seven decades to a time when mystery and suspense lived entirely in the theater of the mind. Johnny Dollar is waiting for you.
