# The Amelia Harwell Matter

Picture this: a sweltering summer evening in 1952, the kind where the heat shimmers off the pavement and makes you question everyone's motives. Johnny Dollar, CBS's most dogged insurance investigator, takes on a case that promises nothing but trouble from the moment it lands on his desk. The Amelia Harwell Matter pulls him into a tangled web of deception, blackmail, and the kind of secrets that respectable people kill to keep buried. With Edmond O'Brien's distinctive growl cutting through the static and jazz, listeners are transported into smoky back rooms and dimly lit hotel corridors where every suspect has something to hide and the truth costs more than the insurance payout itself.

This episode arrived during the show's CBS run—a golden moment when Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar had perfected its formula of hard-boiled mystery and crackling dialogue. O'Brien, himself a seasoned actor in Hollywood's shadow world, brought an authenticity to the role that transcended the medium; you believed every cynical observation, every calculated move. The early 1950s were radio's final hurrah before television would claim America's living rooms, and Johnny Dollar stood among the format's finest achievements—proof that imagination and a skilled performer could create drama more vivid than anything flickering on a screen.

The Amelia Harwell Matter awaits you now, preserved through the decades by devoted collectors and historians who understood that these broadcasts deserved immortality.
