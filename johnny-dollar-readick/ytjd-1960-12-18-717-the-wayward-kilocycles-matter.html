# The Wayward Kilocycles Matter

Picture this: a shadowy broadcasting studio where the airwaves themselves become a conduit for crime. When insurance investigator Johnny Dollar arrives to investigate a claim involving a pirate radio station, he steps into a world of secret frequencies, mysterious transmissions, and characters operating in the electromagnetic underground of 1960 America. Robert Readick's distinctive gravelly voice cuts through the static as Johnny navigates a maze of false identities and coded messages, following a trail that leads from legitimate radio networks into the murky realm of unlicensed broadcasts. With each commercial break dissolved into the next scene, listeners will find themselves caught in a taut game of cat-and-mouse where information—and misinformation—travels at the speed of sound. The "Wayward Kilocycles" promises the kind of intricate plotting that made *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* a benchmark of radio drama: a case where nothing is what it seems, and danger lurks behind every dial.

By 1960, *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* had become a institution of sophisticated storytelling, and this particular episode showcases why. Rather than relying on tired conventions, the show explored the margins of American commerce and society with genuine intelligence. Robert Readick brought a seasoned weariness to Johnny Dollar that set him apart from other radio detectives—here was a man who understood that insurance fraud was rarely simple, that people rarely fell into clean categories of guilty or innocent. The episode's focus on radio itself reflects a medium aware of its own power and mystique during an era when broadcasting was still shrouded in technical mystery for average listeners.

This is essential listening for anyone who understands that great radio drama isn't about spectacle—it's about voice, timing, and the terrible clarity of human motive. Tune in and discover why *Johnny Dollar* remained one of radio's final masterpieces before television claimed the evening hours.
