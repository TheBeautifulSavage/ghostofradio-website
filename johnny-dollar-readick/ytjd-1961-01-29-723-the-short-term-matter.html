# The Short Term Matter

Picture this: it's a gray January evening in 1961, and you're settling into your favorite chair with a cup of coffee as the CBS airwaves crackle to life. Johnny Dollar, that laconic insurance investigator with a nose for trouble and a expense account to match, finds himself entangled in a case that's far shorter on answers than it is on questions. What begins as a routine claim investigation spirals into a maze of deception where nothing—and no one—is quite what they seem. Robert Readick's world-weary voice guides you through shadowy hotel corridors and tense interrogations, where a mere five-day assignment could cost Johnny everything. The tension mounts as evidence crumbles, alibis crumble faster, and the truth becomes increasingly elusive. This is vintage noir craftsmanship: sharp dialogue, authentic procedural detail, and the palpable sense that danger lurks around every corner of Manhattan's underbelly.

By 1961, *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* had already become an institution—a show that proved radio drama could compete with television through sheer storytelling excellence and Readick's incomparable performance. Serialized across multiple episodes and presented as Johnny's personal case file, the show innovated by letting listeners piece together complex investigations week after week. "The Short Term Matter" exemplifies why this series remained vital during radio's twilight years: it combined hard-boiled detective fiction with genuine procedural authenticity, creating a world where every detail mattered and every choice had consequences.

Don't miss this gripping chapter in Johnny Dollar's casebook. Tune in and discover why *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* remains the gold standard of detective radio drama—where the mystery runs deep and the stakes run even deeper.
