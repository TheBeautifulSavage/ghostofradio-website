# Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar: The Wayward Fireman Matter

When a missing fireman vanishes into the smoky underbelly of a coastal city, insurance investigator Johnny Dollar finds himself chasing shadows through dockside bars and cramped tenement stairwells where danger lurks in every darkened corner. This February 1961 episode crackles with the authentic tension of mid-century noir—the scratch of a match, the distant wail of a siren, the measured, world-weary drawl of Robert Readick's Dollar as he pieces together a puzzle where nothing is quite as it seems. Listeners will find themselves drawn into a case where a man's disappearance unravels a web of desperation, loyalty, and the thin line between heroism and ruin. The episode exemplifies the show's gift for transforming an ordinary insurance claim into a human drama of genuine consequence.

By 1961, *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* had become the longest-running dramatic series in radio history, a distinction earned through relentless craftsmanship and Readick's magnetic performance in the lead role. Each episode was a self-contained mystery built on meticulous writing and naturalistic dialogue—no melodrama, just the hard work of investigation and the moral ambiguities that follow. In an era when television threatened radio's dominance, this show proved that the medium's greatest strength was its ability to forge an intimate connection between storyteller and listener, painting vivid worlds in the theater of the mind.

Don your fedora and settle in for a case that will keep you guessing until the final fade-out. *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* awaits.
