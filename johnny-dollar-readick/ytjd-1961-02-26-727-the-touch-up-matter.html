# Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar - "The Touch Up Matter"

When a beautiful woman walks into Johnny Dollar's office with a story about a stolen masterpiece and a desperate forger's scheme, you know the insurance investigator's world is about to get considerably more dangerous. In this February 1961 episode, Robert Readick's trademark gravelly voice guides us through the shadowy underworld of art theft and blackmail, where a "touch up"—a simple act of artistic restoration—becomes the linchpin in a conspiracy that could cost someone their life. The atmosphere crackles with suspense as Dollar peels back layers of deception, each revelation pulling him deeper into a web of forged signatures, insurance fraud, and the kind of desperation that drives ordinary people to extraordinary crimes. You can almost hear the rain on the streets of whatever city Dollar finds himself in, smell the cigarette smoke curling through dimly-lit galleries and back-room offices.

By 1960-61, *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* had become CBS radio's crown jewel of dramatic programming, a show that proved the detective noir format could thrive even as television was supposedly killing radio drama. Readick's performance set the gold standard for the character—world-weary yet sharp, cynical but ultimately principled—making Johnny Dollar feel less like a pulp fiction cliché and more like a real man navigating a morally complicated world. The show's meticulous attention to insurance details and procedural realism grounded even its most outlandish plots, creating a unique hybrid that appealed to sophisticated audiences hungry for intelligent adult drama.

This is essential listening for anyone who wants to experience radio drama at its finest, when storytelling was carried entirely by voice, sound effects, and music. Tune in and discover why audiences made *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* must-listen radio throughout the golden age.
