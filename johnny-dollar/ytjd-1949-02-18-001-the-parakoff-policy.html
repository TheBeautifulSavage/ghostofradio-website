# The Parakoff Policy

Step into the rain-slicked streets of postwar America as insurance investigator Johnny Dollar opens the case file on "The Parakoff Policy," an episode that crackles with the electric tension of a perfectly executed noir mystery. Our world-weary hero finds himself tangled in a web of suspicious claims and shadowy figures when a valuable policy comes under question—and with it, a corpse that raises more questions than answers. Through dimly lit offices and dangerous interrogations, you'll hear the snap of evidence building, the tension in Johnny's voice as he navigates betrayal and deception, all underscored by that distinctive sound design that made early 1949 radio unforgettable: the ring of a telephone, the slam of a desk drawer, the ominous creak of a closing door.

This episode represents the very foundation of the Johnny Dollar phenomenon that would captivate audiences for over a decade. Premiering in February 1949 on CBS, *Yours Truly Johnny Dollar* emerged during radio's golden age when millions of listeners depended on their sets for evening entertainment and genuine suspense. The show's genius lay in its accessible formula—intelligent storytelling, a resourceful protagonist who was neither superhero nor fool, and mystery plots rooted in the mundane world of insurance investigation, making danger feel genuine and urgently real. Actor Bob Bailey's deadpan delivery and the show's commitment to sonic authenticity set it apart, establishing the template that would keep Johnny Dollar thriving as one of radio's most enduring characters.

Don't miss your chance to experience where it all began. Tune in to "The Parakoff Policy" and discover why Johnny Dollar became America's favorite investigator—one carefully documented case file at a time.
