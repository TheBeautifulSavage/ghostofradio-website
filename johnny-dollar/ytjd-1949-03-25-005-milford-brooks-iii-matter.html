# The Milford Brooks III Matter

When insurance investigator Johnny Dollar checks into a case involving the seemingly respectable Milford Brooks III, he finds himself drawn into a web of deception that stretches far beyond a simple claims dispute. On this March evening in 1949, listeners tuned to CBS will experience the unmistakable crackle of a world where nothing is quite as it appears—where a gentleman's handshake means nothing and a polite smile might precede a bullet. Dollar, with his sharp wit and sharper instincts, must navigate the shadowy corridors of high society and low-down treachery to uncover what really happened. The episode pulses with that classic noir atmosphere: smoky rooms, suspicious glances, and the constant threat that danger lurks just around the corner. As the mystery deepens, you'll hear why this young investigator has become radio's most trusted man when the stakes are highest.

*Yours Truly Johnny Dollar* arrived at CBS radio in 1949 precisely when America's appetite for sophisticated detective fiction was reaching its peak. The show distinguished itself through Johnny's first-person narration and his matter-of-fact approach to cases that would confound ordinary investigators. Rather than relying on violence or heroic theatrics, Johnny uses his head—his ability to read people, to spot inconsistencies, and to follow the money. This particular episode stands as an early example of the formula that would make the show a beloved staple for over a decade, a time when radio dramas commanded the nation's undivided attention each evening.

Don't miss "The Milford Brooks III Matter"—a tale where one man's careful investigation might save an insurance company thousands, and where the truth demands a price. Tune in and discover why Johnny Dollar became a legend.
