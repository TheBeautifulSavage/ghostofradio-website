# The Case of the Hundred Thousand Dollar Legs

Picture yourself in a smoke-filled insurance office on a rain-soaked April evening in 1949. Johnny Dollar, America's most famous insurance investigator, takes a call that will plunge him into a dizzying world of theatrical deception, where a dancer's million-dollar legs become the unlikely centerpiece of fraud, passion, and betrayal. In this early episode of the series, you'll follow Dollar's razor-sharp mind as he navigates between nightclubs and insurance claims offices, unraveling a mystery where the line between theatrical artistry and cold-blooded criminal intent blurs dangerously. The plot unfolds with breakneck pacing—false leads, double-crosses, and moments of genuine danger that keep you riveted to your radio set.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* was revolutionary broadcasting, debuting just months before this episode aired. It was one of the first radio dramas to cast insurance investigation as genuinely thrilling material, transforming what could have been mundane bureaucratic work into hard-boiled noir adventure. The show's structure—told largely through Johnny's dictated expense reports to his insurance company—became its signature, lending an intimate, documentary-like quality to each case. At a time when American audiences were hungry for post-war entertainment, Johnny Dollar arrived as the thinking person's detective: sophisticated, resourceful, and always operating in the gray spaces between legality and justice.

This 1949 episode represents the show in its prime, when the writers were still experimenting with the format and Johnny's character was being finely honed. Whether you're a devoted fan of vintage radio or discovering this classic for the first time, "The Case of the Hundred Thousand Dollar Legs" delivers everything that made *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* essential listening for three decades. Tune in and discover why Johnny Dollar became one of radio's most enduring heroes.
