When a beautiful nightclub singer named Bonnie Goodwin turns up dead in a seedy Los Angeles hotel room, insurance investigator Johnny Dollar finds himself navigating a labyrinth of lies, desperate alibis, and dangerous secrets that reach far beyond what any insurance claim should cover. Dollar's sharp mind and sharper tongue cut through the smoky haze of 1949 Los Angeles as he interviews suspects who all seem to have something to hide—a jealous lover, a calculating club owner, a mysterious out-of-towner with money to burn. With Mandel Kramer's distinctive deadpan delivery bringing our protagonist to life, listeners are pulled into a taut, atmospheric investigation where every clue could be a red herring and every witness a potential killer. The tension builds as Dollar closes in on the truth, his internal monologue revealing the weary cynicism of a man who's seen too much and trusts too little.

Yours Truly Johnny Dollar premiered in 1949 as CBS's answer to the golden age of hard-boiled detective fiction, translating the noir sensibility of pulp magazines directly into radio's intimate medium. What set it apart was its documentary-like realism—each episode was built around actual insurance cases, lending an air of procedural authenticity that captivated audiences. Johnny Dollar wasn't a private eye chasing fame and fortune; he was a working man solving everyday mysteries for everyday paydays, making him somehow more relatable and more human than his flashier radio contemporaries. The show became a cultural phenomenon, running for thirteen years and spawning hundreds of cases that defined the sound of American noir.

Tune in to Murder Ain't Minor and discover why listeners kept Johnny Dollar's frequency dialed in week after week—where every case crackles with danger and nobody's innocence is guaranteed.
