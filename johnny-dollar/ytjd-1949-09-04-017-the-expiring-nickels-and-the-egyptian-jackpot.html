# The Expiring Nickels and the Egyptian Jackpot

Picture yourself in a smoky Manhattan office on a September evening in 1949, as insurance investigator Johnny Dollar lights a Lucky Strike and settles into a case that will take him from glittering casino floors to shadowy hotel corridors where every nickel tells a story. In "The Expiring Nickels and the Egyptian Jackpot," our man-for-hire unravels a tangled web of forged currency, high-stakes gambling, and a fortune hidden in plain sight—all beneath the hot desert sun of Cairo. With only his wits, his expense account, and his knack for catching liars in their own contradictions, Johnny tracks a trail of vintage coins and desperate people, each with their own reasons to see justice—or ruin—prevail. The tension builds with every commercial break, as you'll find yourself wondering alongside our hero: who's running the con, and how deep does this case really go?

What makes Johnny Dollar's world so intoxicating is creator Phillips Lord's brilliant formula: authentic insurance cases drawn from real claims, a protagonist who speaks directly to you about his expenses and observations, and the sophisticated, hardboiled atmosphere that defined the golden age of radio drama. By 1949, when this episode aired, listeners were hungry for smart, adult entertainment—shows that treated them as intelligent beings capable of following complex mysteries without spoon-feeding solutions. Johnny Dollar delivered that in spades, becoming one of CBS's most reliable hits throughout the 1950s.

Slip on your earphones and step into a world where danger lurks behind every deal, where a handful of coins can spark murder, and where one private investigator's integrity is the only thing standing between truth and deception. "The Expiring Nickels and the Egyptian Jackpot" awaits—case closed, mystery solved, but only if you tune in.
