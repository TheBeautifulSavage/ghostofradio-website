# The Fishing Boat Affair

Picture yourself in a dimly lit insurance office on a fog-shrouded evening in 1949, the crackle of a transatlantic telephone line cutting through the static as our hero, Johnny Dollar, listens intently to a tale of maritime mystery and murder. In "The Fishing Boat Affair," the world-weary insurance investigator finds himself entangled in a case that begins with a simple claim—a fishing vessel lost at sea—but quickly spirals into something far more sinister. With nothing but his wits, his expense account, and the subtle menace lurking beneath every confession, Johnny must navigate treacherous waters both literal and figurative to uncover the truth. Who sabotaged the boat? Why does everyone have something to hide? As rain hammers against the windows and suspects spin their carefully constructed lies, listeners will find themselves gripping their radio sets, desperate to know what Johnny will uncover next.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* arrived on CBS radio at precisely the moment post-war America craved sophisticated adult entertainment—smart, cynical, and utterly devoid of sentimentality. Unlike the bombastic superhero serials of the 1930s, this show offered something revolutionary: a protagonist who paid his own way, filed meticulous expense reports, and solved cases through cunning rather than superhuman powers. The show's early episodes, like this October 1949 broadcast, established the formula that would captivate audiences for thirteen years: tight scripting, atmospheric sound design, and the unforgettable baritone of Bob Bailey's Johnny Dollar, a man whose tired voice somehow conveyed both world-weariness and unshakeable integrity.

Tune in now and discover why *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* became the most popular dramatic program in radio history. Some mysteries are waiting to be solved.
