# The Department Store Swindle Matter

Picture this: Christmas Eve, 1949. The department stores are packed with last-minute shoppers, their arms laden with gifts wrapped in colorful paper and tied with ribbon. But beneath the festive cheer and jingling bells, insurance investigator Johnny Dollar smells something rotten. When a major retailer's inventory mysteriously vanishes just before the holiday rush, Johnny finds himself donning a red suit and white beard to go undercover as Santa Claus—the perfect vantage point to observe the back rooms and employee corridors where the real crime is unfolding. What begins as a routine fraud investigation becomes a desperate race against the clock as Johnny navigates a web of blackmail, employee theft, and corporate greed, all while maintaining his cover in a department store packed with unsuspecting shoppers and children waiting to sit on Santa's lap.

Since its debut in 1949, *Yours Truly Johnny Dollar* carved out a unique niche in the crowded landscape of radio crime drama. Unlike the hard-boiled detectives and police procedurals that dominated the airwaves, Johnny Dollar was an insurance investigator—a man solving cases not for justice alone, but for the bottom line. Created by Blake Edwards and performed by the incomparable Bob Bailey, the show distinguished itself through its meticulous attention to expense accounts (Johnny famously narrated his case fees in real time) and its clever, tightly plotted mysteries. This Christmas episode showcases the show's particular genius: taking ordinary commercial settings and transforming them into stages of deception and danger.

Don your headphones and step into the holiday season of 1949 with Johnny Dollar. You'll find no sentimental Christmas wishes here—just sharp dialogue, intricate plotting, and one resourceful investigator determined to uncover the truth behind the season's biggest swindle.
