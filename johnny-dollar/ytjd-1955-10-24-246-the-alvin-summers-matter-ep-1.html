# The Alvin Summers Matter (Part 1)

Picture this: a smoke-filled office where insurance claims become puzzles of human nature, and a man with a quick mind and quicker instincts navigates the shadowy underbelly of post-war America. On October 24th, 1955, listeners tuning into CBS found themselves in precisely that world as Johnny Dollar, the man with the action-packed expense account, takes on the curious case of Alvin Summers. What begins as a routine claim—or so it appears—unravels into something far more sinister. The first installment crackles with that distinctive Johnny Dollar tension: the clinking of ice in a glass, the scratch of a lighter, the underlying threat in every telephone conversation. This is noir distilled into radio drama, where your imagination supplies the shadows and the danger feels entirely real.

By 1955, *Yours Truly Johnny Dollar* had perfected the art of the investigation procedural, making it the most consistently popular mystery series in broadcasting. Created by writer-producer Jack Johnstone and performed with tireless precision by star Mandel Kramer, the show distinguished itself through meticulous attention to realistic details—Johnny doesn't just solve crimes, he *investigates* them, filing reports and calculating expenses with the same intensity he brings to unmasking deception. This particular episode represents the show at its creative peak, when the formula of cynicism, humor, and genuine mystery-solving had gelled into something irresistible for America's radio audiences.

So settle in, dear listener, and prepare yourself. Johnny Dollar is about to earn his fee. The Alvin Summers matter awaits, and in the first of this compelling multi-part investigation, you'll discover that sometimes the most dangerous cases are the ones that don't look dangerous at all.
