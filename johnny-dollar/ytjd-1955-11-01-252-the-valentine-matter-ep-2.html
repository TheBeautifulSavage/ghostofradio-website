# The Valentine Matter - Episode 2

Step into the rain-slicked streets of 1955 as insurance investigator Johnny Dollar pursues a case that grows more sinister by the minute. Our hero finds himself entangled in a web of blackmail, misdirected affections, and dangerous secrets—all stemming from a seemingly innocent valentine that has somehow triggered a chain of events nobody saw coming. With his characteristic cool-headed determination and sharp eye for detail, Johnny must navigate the shadowy underworld where love becomes a weapon and trust is the rarest commodity. The second installment of "The Valentine Matter" crackles with tension as our protagonist inches closer to the truth, but every answer only spawns more questions. Who sent the fateful card? What are they really after? And will Johnny uncover the answers before someone ends up dead?

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* stands as one of radio's finest achievements—a show that elevated the detective genre beyond simple whodunits into genuine art. Unlike the formulaic mysteries that dominated the airwaves, these episodes, written with meticulous care by writer-producer Edson, present morally complex cases where the lines between right and wrong blur considerably. The show's success lay in Johnny himself: not a superhero or a lucky bumbler, but an ordinary man of principle navigating an extraordinary world. By 1955, the program had become a listener favorite, celebrated for its intelligent writing and the seamless chemistry between Johnny and the diverse cast of characters he encounters in each investigation.

This episode is pure radio noir—the kind of storytelling that made people gather around their sets at dinner time, the kind that proved radio could deliver sophistication and suspense in equal measure. Don't miss part two of "The Valentine Matter."
