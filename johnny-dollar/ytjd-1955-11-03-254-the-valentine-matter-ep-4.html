# The Valentine Matter, Episode 4

Picture yourself in a smoky Manhattan office as November rain pelts the windows, and insurance investigator Johnny Dollar finds himself knee-deep in a case that refuses to stay buried. In this gripping fourth installment of "The Valentine Matter," Dollar circles back to the scene of a seemingly settled claim, only to discover that the threads he thought were tied off are unraveling in dangerous directions. The writing crackles with classic noir atmosphere—double-crosses lurk around every corner, and a mysterious woman from Dollar's past may hold the key to unlocking the truth. As always, Mandel Kramer's steady voice cuts through the tension, treating listeners to an evening of sophisticated mystery where nothing is quite what it seems and a man's integrity is his only currency.

By 1955, "Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar" had become the gold standard of radio's investigative drama, refined over six years into a masterclass of storytelling economy. Unlike the typical whodunit, the show focused less on who committed the crime and more on the intricate, human details of the investigation itself—the expenses, the legwork, the moral ambiguities that arise when profit meets principle. The episode format, with its serialized cases spanning multiple installments, gave CBS's writers the freedom to develop complex narratives that rewarded loyal listeners while welcoming newcomers to the fractured, morally gray world of insurance fraud investigation.

Settle in with a cup of coffee or something stronger and surrender to an evening of first-rate radio drama. Johnny Dollar awaits, case file in hand, ready to remind you why this show captivated millions during radio's golden age. The Valentine Matter won't solve itself—and neither will your need for genuine, artfully-crafted entertainment.
