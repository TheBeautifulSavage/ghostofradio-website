# The Lorko Diamonds Matter (Part 3)

Picture this: a smoke-filled hotel room in the heart of a sprawling metropolis, where insurance investigator Johnny Dollar stands at a crossroads between two dangerous men and a fortune in stolen diamonds. As this third installment of "The Lorko Diamonds Matter" unfolds, the tension ratchets tighter with each passing minute. Johnny's sharp wit and sharper instincts are his only weapons as he navigates a labyrinth of double-crosses, femme fatales, and underworld figures who'd just as soon see him six feet under as let him uncover the truth. The case that seemed straightforward on day one has spiraled into something far more sinister—and now, with the diamonds still missing and suspects multiplying faster than lies at a poker table, our hero must separate fact from fiction before the real culprit strikes again.

This November 1955 episode represents the golden age of radio drama, when millions of Americans tuned in each week to follow Johnny Dollar's globe-trotting investigations. Created by writer/producer Jack Johnstone and brought to life by the dulcet tones of Bob Bailey, "Yours Truly Johnny Dollar" became the most popular dramatic show in radio history precisely because it combined the gritty allure of hard-boiled noir with the relatable everyman quality of an insurance adjuster. Each case was presented as authentic expense reports, lending the show a documentary-like credibility that captivated audiences during radio's twilight years.

Don't miss this pivotal chapter in "The Lorko Diamonds Matter." Tune in and experience the crackle of genuine suspense, the atmospheric orchestral score, and the incomparable delivery of an era when storytelling meant everything. Johnny Dollar's on the case—and the truth is closer than anyone realizes.
