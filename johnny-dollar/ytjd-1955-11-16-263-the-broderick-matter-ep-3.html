The Broderick Matter Ep 3

As November rain hammers the streets of San Francisco, insurance investigator Johnny Dollar finds himself trapped in a web of deception that grows darker by the hour. In this third installment of "The Broderick Matter," our world-weary hero discovers that nothing—and no one—is quite what they seemed. A seemingly straightforward claim has spiraled into something far more sinister, with blackmail, hidden identities, and a shadowy figure pulling strings from the shadows. Dollar's sharp wit and sharper instincts are pushed to their limits as he races to uncover the truth before time runs out. The sound effects crackle with authenticity: the hiss of cigarette smoke, the heavy footfalls of pursuit, the click of a gun's safety. You'll hear the desperation in Dollar's voice as his investigation tightens around a mystery that threatens to consume everything he's worked for.

"Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar" stands as one of radio's most sophisticated detective dramas, pioneering a format that gave listeners one continuous case spread across multiple episodes—a narrative technique unheard of in 1955. Created by Blake Edwards, the show transformed the insurance claim into high art, exploring the murky space where legitimate business meets criminal enterprise. Johnny Dollar himself became an icon of post-war noir sensibility: cynical yet honorable, surviving in a gray world where moral absolutes crumble. This episode, recorded during the show's peak years on CBS, showcases the program at its finest, with sharp writing and performances that rival any television drama of the era.

Don't miss this gripping chapter in Johnny Dollar's ongoing battle against corruption and deception. Tune in and discover why listeners made this show an appointment with destiny—and danger.
