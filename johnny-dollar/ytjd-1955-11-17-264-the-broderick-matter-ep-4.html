# The Broderick Matter - Episode 4

Picture this: a dimly lit hotel room where secrets fester like wounds, and Johnny Dollar's quick wit is the only thing standing between a client and financial ruin. In this fourth and final installment of *The Broderick Matter*, our man Johnny finds himself cornered by lies that multiply like shadows in a darkened corridor. The stakes have never been higher—a fortune hangs in the balance, and someone in this tangled web of deceit is willing to kill to keep it buried. Expect the kind of sharp dialogue and breakneck pacing that made Johnny Dollar must-listen radio, as our insurance investigator peels back layer after layer of misdirection to expose the truth. The tension crackles through every word, every accusation, every revelation.

What made *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* such a phenomenon in post-war America was its perfect alchemy of hard-boiled sophistication and everyday accessibility. Unlike the supernatural mysteries and costumed vigilantes that dominated the airwaves, Johnny was a regular working man—an insurance investigator solving crimes that could happen to anyone. The show's focus on actual investigative procedure, combined with its witty repartee and genuine moral complexity, elevated it beyond typical mystery fare. By 1955, after six years on CBS, the program had become the gold standard of detective radio drama, with Johnny Dollar himself—voiced by the incomparable Bob Bailey—becoming as familiar to listeners as a trusted friend stopping by for an evening drink.

If you've been following the Broderick case since episode one, this conclusion promises answers that will justify every twist and turn. And if this is your first encounter with Johnny Dollar's world, *The Broderick Matter* finale is the perfect entry point into a legend. Tune in and discover why listeners couldn't wait for the next case.
