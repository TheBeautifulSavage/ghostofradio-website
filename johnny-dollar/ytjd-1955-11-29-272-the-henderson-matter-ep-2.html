# The Henderson Matter - Part Two

Our man Johnny Dollar returns to the murky underbelly of postwar insurance fraud, where nothing—and no one—is quite what they seem. In this second installment of "The Henderson Matter," the expenses mount as quickly as the suspects, and our dapper investigator finds himself tangled in a web of blackmail, mistaken identity, and cold cash that someone would kill to keep hidden. The November night air crackles with danger as Johnny navigates dimly lit hotel corridors and late-night diners, his sharp mind the only weapon against men with sharper intentions. With each lead he follows, each witness he corners, the real story behind the Henderson claim becomes more sinister—and Johnny Dollar becomes increasingly expendable. Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar isn't just about solving the case; it's about surviving long enough to collect your fee.

What made *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* a radio phenomenon throughout the 1950s was its unflinching commitment to the gritty world of claims investigation—a decidedly unglamorous profession that radio writers transformed into pure noir drama. Unlike the glamorous detectives and federal agents dominating the airwaves, Johnny Dollar was an ordinary man in an extraordinary profession, where every case was a puzzle and every puzzle had a price tag. The show's innovative "expense account" format, where Johnny would recite his itemized costs at episode's end, grounded the narrative in authentic detail that audiences found irresistible. This 1955 episode exemplifies the show's maturity, blending psychological tension with procedural authenticity.

If you crave the authentic voice of American noir radio—stripped of sentimentality, powered by sharp dialogue and darker human impulses—settle in and tune in to Johnny Dollar's continuing investigation. The Henderson matter awaits, and the clock is ticking.
