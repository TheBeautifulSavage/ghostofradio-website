# The Cronin Matter - Part Four

Picture yourself in a smoke-filled insurance office on a winter's night in 1955, as Johnny Dollar closes in on the fourth and final chapter of the Cronin case. The trail has grown colder, the stakes have risen higher, and someone out there is willing to do whatever it takes to keep the truth buried. As our intrepid investigator peels back the last layers of deception, you'll find yourself gripped by mounting tension—not through screaming violins or manufactured scares, but through the crackle of authentic danger that seeps through your radio speaker. This episode crackles with the authenticity of hard-boiled detective work, where every clue matters, every conversation carries weight, and the solution waits in the shadows, waiting to be discovered.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* stood apart in radio's golden age as perhaps the most consistently excellent adventure program ever broadcast. For thirteen years, audiences tuned in faithfully to follow Johnny Dollar—a freelance insurance investigator operating on a per-diem expense account—as he unraveled corporate conspiracies, elaborate frauds, and crimes of passion. What made the show endure was its commitment to intelligent storytelling and character-driven narratives. Unlike the pulp excesses of some contemporaries, Johnny Dollar presented cases that felt plausible, complex, and genuinely perplexing. By 1955, the show had refined its formula to perfection, with four-part cases becoming the standard format, allowing writers like Phillips Lord and Robert Sloan to develop rich mysteries worthy of the time investment.

Don't miss this final piece of the puzzle. The solution to the Cronin Matter awaits—if you have the nerve to discover what really happened. Tune in and let one of radio's finest mysteries transport you back to an era when drama meant something, when every word counted, and when a good story could captivate a nation.
