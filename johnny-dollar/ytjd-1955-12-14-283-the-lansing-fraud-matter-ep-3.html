The rain hammers against the windows of a dingy Portland hotel as Johnny Dollar closes in on the truth—but the deeper he digs into the Lansing insurance claim, the murkier the waters become. In this third installment of our three-part investigation, our intrepid insurance detective finds himself cornered between a desperate widow, a shady warehouse manager, and a fortune that simply doesn't add up. With danger lurking in every shadow and time running out before the policy pays out, Johnny must navigate a labyrinth of lies, false identities, and double-crosses to prevent a massive fraud—or worse, to stay alive long enough to expose it. Bob Bailey's legendary performance captures every moment of tension as Johnny races against the clock, his quick wit and sharper instincts the only things keeping him one step ahead of those who'd see him silenced.

By 1955, Yours Truly Johnny Dollar had become the gold standard of radio mystery programming, captivating millions of listeners with its fast-paced storytelling and hard-boiled authenticity. The show's serialized cases—like this three-part Lansing matter—represented radio drama at its finest, building suspense across multiple episodes with cliffhangers that left audiences breathless. Bailey's naturalistic delivery revolutionized the genre, moving away from theatrical melodrama toward genuine grit and procedural detail that audiences craved in the atomic age.

Whether you're a devoted follower of the Dollar investigations or discovering this classic for the first time, this climactic episode delivers everything that made radio's greatest detective show unmissable: mystery, danger, and the voice of Johnny Dollar cutting through the darkness. Tune in now and discover why, for thirteen thrilling years, America couldn't wait to hear what case Johnny Dollar would crack next.
