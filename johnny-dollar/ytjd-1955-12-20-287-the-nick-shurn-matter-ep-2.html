# The Nick Shurn Matter - Part Two

The rain hammers against the window of a dingy Chicago hotel room as Johnny Dollar lights another cigarette, his expense account notebook already thick with notes from a case that refuses to stay closed. In this second installment of "The Nick Shurn Matter," our hard-boiled insurance investigator finds himself deeper in the murky underworld of maritime fraud and missing persons, where every lead twists into another shadowy corridor. A dame with secrets, a corpse that shouldn't exist, and a criminal underworld that's willing to kill to keep the truth buried—Johnny's quick wit and sharper instincts are his only armor as the night closes in. This is noir storytelling at its finest: crackling dialogue, genuine danger lurking in every scene, and the unmistakable sense that one wrong move could be Johnny's last.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* remains one of radio's most enduring achievements, and the 1955 season represents the show at peak form. Actor Bob Bailey's portrayal of the world-weary investigator became iconic during this era, his ability to convey both vulnerability and steely determination making Johnny feel utterly real to listeners huddled around their sets. The show's intricate plots, drawn from actual insurance cases and criminal histories, lent credibility that fantasy couldn't match. Each episode was a complete adventure—complex enough to reward devoted listeners, yet accessible enough to draw in casual audiences seeking an evening's escape.

Step into the shadowy world of insurance investigation with Johnny Dollar. Pour yourself a drink, dim the lights, and prepare for an evening of genuine suspense. Part two of "The Nick Shurn Matter" awaits, promising twists that will keep you guessing right through to the final, satisfying resolution.
