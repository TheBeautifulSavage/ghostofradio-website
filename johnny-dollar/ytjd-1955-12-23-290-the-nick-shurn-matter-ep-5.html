# The Nick Shurn Matter – Episode 5

Picture yourself huddled near the radio on a winter's evening in 1955, the crackle of the speaker cutting through the cold darkness as Johnny Dollar's weary voice drawls into your living room. In this fifth installment of "The Nick Shurn Matter," our insurance investigator finds himself deeper in a labyrinth of deception and danger, where every shadowy character he encounters might be the key to solving the case—or the next threat to his life. The episode crackles with authentic noir atmosphere: the ping of a taxi horn, the distant wail of sirens, the heavy silence of a warehouse at midnight. Dollar's trademark dry wit and sharp instincts are tested as he unravels another layer of conspiracy, his expense account growing as perilously as his list of suspects. What began as a routine claim has become something far more sinister, and by evening's end, Johnny will have navigated betrayals, narrow escapes, and the kind of moral ambiguity that made this show essential listening.

*Yours Truly Johnny Dollar* represented the pinnacle of radio drama sophistication—a show that treated its audience like intelligent adults capable of following complex plots and appreciating genuine mystery writing. Unlike the cartoonish detectives that populated the airwaves, Johnny Dollar was grounded, cynical, and refreshingly real; his cases unfolded across multiple episodes, mirroring actual investigative work rather than neat forty-five-minute resolutions. This five-part "Shurn Matter" exemplifies that commitment to depth, allowing character and circumstance to develop naturally across a full week of broadcasting.

Don't miss the fifth piece of this intricate puzzle. Settle in, dim the lights, and let the golden age of radio transport you to a time when mysteries were genuinely mysterious, and a man's word—and his expense account—meant everything.
