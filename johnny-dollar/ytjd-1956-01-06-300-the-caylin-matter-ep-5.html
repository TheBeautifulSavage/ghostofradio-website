The fifth installment of this gripping five-part serial finds our intrepid insurance investigator Johnny Dollar deep in the shadowy heart of a case that grows more twisted with every revelation. As the episode opens, the line between victim and perpetrator blurs dangerously, and Dollar's sharp instincts—honed across countless cases—are pushed to their limit. The atmosphere crackles with tension: we're in murky territory now, where every phone call could be a trap and trust is a luxury no one can afford. Listeners will be drawn into the claustrophobic world of forged documents, desperate motives, and the kind of moral ambiguity that keeps you awake long after the final dramatic flourish of the announcer's sign-off. By this fifth episode, the plot has accumulated enough momentum to feel genuinely dangerous.

Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar stands as one of radio's finest achievements, a show that perfected the marriage of hard-boiled detective fiction with the intimate immediacy of audio drama. This 1956 episode represents the show at its zenith, during the period when CBS had refined the format into something nearly flawless: a tight narrative structure, snappy dialogue, and the incomparable Bob Bailey as Johnny Dollar—his voice equal parts weariness and determination. The show's five-part serials became legendary, unfolding like miniature novels across the week, each episode ending on a hook that made listeners desperate to tune in the next night.

If you've never experienced the peculiar magic of old-time radio drama, or if you're already devoted to Johnny Dollar's cases, The Caylin Matter offers the perfect entry point—or the perfect deepening of your addiction. Settle in, tune out the world, and let your imagination do what only radio can do.
