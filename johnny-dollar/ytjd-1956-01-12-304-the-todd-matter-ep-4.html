# The Todd Matter (Episode 4)

When the curtain rises on this January evening in 1956, insurance investigator Johnny Dollar finds himself ensnared in the fourth installment of a Byzantine case that refuses to yield its secrets. The Todd Matter has proven to be as labyrinthine as a back-alley poker game where nobody's showing their real hand, and as Dollar prowls through shadowy corridors of deception, the tension crackles with the electricity of a man who's beginning to suspect that everyone—everyone—has something to hide. This is the hour when a seemingly straightforward insurance claim transforms into a web of lies, misdirection, and genuine danger. Listeners should prepare themselves for the kind of taut, atmospheric storytelling that made this series essential listening: a world of fedoras and cigarette smoke, where a careless word might cost you everything and trust is the rarest commodity of all.

What made *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* revolutionary in the golden age of radio was its episodic serialization—something virtually unheard of in the medium at the time. Rather than wrapping up cases in twenty-six minutes, creator and writer Jack Johnstone allowed complex investigations to unfold across multiple episodes, deepening character development and narrative intrigue in ways that presaged the television dramas of decades to come. The show's success hinged entirely on star Mandel Kramer's ability to embody Dollar: part weary cynic, part righteous crusader, always compelling. By 1956, the show had become a cultural institution, and episodes like this one showcased why audiences remained genuinely uncertain about where each case might lead.

Don't miss this pivotal chapter of *The Todd Matter*. Tune in and discover why, for over a decade, Johnny Dollar's cases became water-cooler conversation for millions of devoted listeners across America.
