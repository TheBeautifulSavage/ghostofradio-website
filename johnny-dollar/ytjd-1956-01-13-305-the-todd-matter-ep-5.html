# The Todd Matter - Episode 5

When Johnny Dollar picks up the telephone on that cold January evening in 1956, he has no idea that the seemingly routine Todd insurance claim is about to spiral into a labyrinth of deception, blackmail, and murder. This fifth installment finds our hard-boiled investigator deep in the mire, where every witness contradicts the last and the five-thousand-dollar expense account burns away like cigarette smoke in a back-alley interrogation room. The pacing crackles with urgency as Johnny navigates dimly lit hotel corridors and seedy nightclubs, his sharp dialogue cutting through the fog of half-truths like a blade. You can almost hear the ice clinking in his glass and the distant wail of a saxophone as the case takes yet another dangerous turn—and the real culprit remains tantalizingly out of reach.

What makes *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* endure across these 1956 broadcasts is its meticulous realism wrapped in pulp excitement. Created by writer Phillips Lord and starring Edmond O'Brien's rapid-fire delivery, the show pioneered a unique format where Johnny literally itemizes his expenses week by week, building a documentary-like authenticity that grounded the noir fantasies in everyday financial precision. CBS listeners had grown accustomed to this insurance investigator as their trusted guide through the criminal underworld—a working man's detective who solved mysteries not through superhuman abilities but through dogged legwork and moral conviction.

*The Todd Matter* stretches across five episodes precisely because the mystery demands it, refusing the easy resolution of lesser shows. Tune in tonight and discover why Johnny Dollar became the longest-running adventure series in radio history—because in his world, like in ours, the truth is always worth the extra expense.
