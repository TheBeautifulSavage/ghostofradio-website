# The Ricardo Amerigo Matter, Episode 2

Picture this: a rain-slicked Manhattan street corner at midnight, the neon signs of a jazz club bleeding crimson into puddles on the pavement. Johnny Dollar's fedora is pulled low as he inches deeper into a case that stinks of deception and stolen diamonds. In this second installment, our wisecracking insurance investigator finds himself trapped between a beautiful woman with secrets and a syndicate enforcer who'd just as soon see him at the bottom of the East River. The dialogue crackles with tension—snappy, cynical, dangerous—as Johnny unravels the mystery surrounding the disappearance of priceless artwork connected to the shadowy Ricardo Amerigo. There's a double cross lurking around every corner, and by the episode's climax, you won't be certain who's working for whom.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* epitomized radio's golden age of sophisticated noir entertainment, running thirteen years on CBS with Bob Bailey's unmistakable drawl anchoring hundreds of tales of fraud, theft, and revenge. What set the show apart was its obsessive attention to financial detail—Dollar narrated his expense accounts with the same relish he brought to solving crimes, grounding each fantastic mystery in the mundane world of insurance investigation. By 1956, the show had perfected its formula: tight scripts, expert sound design that transformed your living room into a dangerous underworld, and a protagonist who was equal parts cynic and idealist. This particular episode showcases why radio audiences couldn't get enough of the mystery-of-the-week format.

Whether you're a devoted fan rediscovering a forgotten gem or new to the golden age of radio, this episode serves up everything that made *Johnny Dollar* essential listening for millions. So dim the lights, pour yourself a drink, and step into the shadows with America's most famous expense account detective.
