# The Ricardo Amerigo Matter, Part 4

On this January evening in 1956, Johnny Dollar finds himself navigating the shadowy corridors of international intrigue where a simple insurance claim has unraveled into something far more sinister. As our weary investigator closes in on the truth about the Ricardo Amerigo matter, the stakes have never been higher—or deadlier. Listen as Dollar's razor-sharp mind cuts through layers of deception, false identities, and double-crossed betrayals. The gritty streets and smoky rooms come alive through the brilliant sound design of CBS Radio, where every footstep echoes with menace and every telephone ring might bring either salvation or doom. This concluding chapter demands your full attention; answers are finally within reach, but not before danger closes in from all sides.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* stands as a towering achievement in radio drama, the only series to win a Peabody Award for excellence in dramatic writing. From 1949 through 1962, the show's rotating cast of writers—including some of the finest talents in broadcasting—crafted intricate mysteries that treated listeners as intelligent partners in investigation rather than passive audiences. The "Johnny Dollar Matter" format, where each case unfolded across multiple episodes, became the template for serialized radio storytelling. By 1956, the show had hit its creative stride, with writers like Robert Tallman delivering plots of genuine sophistication and moral complexity. Each five-minute segment was crafted with surgical precision, maximizing drama while respecting the listener's time.

Don't miss this pivotal installment. Whether you're a longtime devotee of the series or discovering Johnny Dollar for the first time, *The Ricardo Amerigo Matter, Part 4* represents radio drama at its finest—proof that the golden age of broadcasting earned every golden note of its reputation.
