# The Flight Six Matter (Episode 3)

The rain hammers against the windows of a Los Angeles airport hotel as Johnny Dollar finds himself caught between corporate lies and a mystery that refuses to stay buried. Someone aboard Flight Six knows something—something worth killing for. In this third installment of *The Flight Six Matter*, our hard-bitten insurance investigator must navigate a treacherous web of suspects, each with motive and opportunity, while the clock ticks toward a deadly resolution. The stakes have escalated from a routine policy claim to a full-scale murder investigation, and Johnny's quick wit and sharper instincts are his only protection in a case that grows darker and more dangerous with each passing hour. Expect tension that crackles through your radio speaker, dialogue sharp as a switchblade, and the unmistakable sound of jazz-inflected noir—the perfect soundtrack to deception and double-crosses.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* stood apart from its radio contemporaries as the industry's most acclaimed dramatic series, celebrated for its sophisticated storytelling and commitment to intelligent scripting. By 1956, the show had perfected the formula that made it legendary: genuine mystery plotting that rewarded careful listeners, characters drawn with psychological depth rather than cardboard simplicity, and the dulcet baritone of Mandel Kramer as Johnny Dollar—America's most memorable insurance investigator. Multi-part episodes like *The Flight Six Matter* became the show's signature, serializing complex cases across three or four installments that kept audiences returning night after night, unable to resist the pull of resolution.

Don't miss this chapter in one of radio's finest achievements. Tune in as Johnny Dollar pursues the truth through darkness and deception—where every answer spawns new questions, and the only certainty is that danger waits around every corner.
