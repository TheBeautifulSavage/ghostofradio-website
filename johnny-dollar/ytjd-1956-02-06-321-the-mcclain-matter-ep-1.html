Step into the fog-shrouded streets of 1956 as insurance investigator Johnny Dollar picks up a case that will test his wits, his conscience, and his survival instincts. When a routine claim lands on his desk, the details don't quite add up—and Johnny knows better than to ignore his hunches. In this first installment of The McClain Matter, you'll hear the distinctive click of a lighter, the clink of ice in a glass, and the unmistakable snap of a case about to unravel into something far darker than anyone anticipated. Dollar's signature bravado meets genuine danger as he begins asking the wrong questions to the right people—and some of those people don't appreciate inquiries into their affairs.

By 1956, Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar had become CBS Radio's crown jewel of the mystery-adventure genre, and for good reason. The show's creator, Blake Edwards, crafted a protagonist who stood apart from the hard-boiled detectives flooding the airwaves—Johnny Dollar was everyman's investigator, sharp-tongued but fundamentally decent, operating in a world of gray morality where insurance claims masked deeper human dramas. Each episode was a self-contained case, presented with meticulous attention to detail and a naturalistic style of dialogue that made listeners feel they were eavesdropping on a real investigation. The year 1956 found the show at its creative peak, just as American anxieties about trust, corruption, and hidden truths were reaching a fever pitch in the atomic age.

Don't miss this gripping two-part investigation. Tune in as Johnny Dollar lights another cigarette and dials the first number that will pull him into The McClain Matter—a case where the premium on human deception proves far costlier than any policy ever written.
