The case that's been dogging insurance investigator Johnny Dollar for days reaches its boiling point in this February 1956 broadcast—and the walls are closing in fast. Johnny's been chasing shadows and half-truths through the McClain affair, and now, in part five of this gripping five-part serial, the real culprit finally emerges from the fog. The tension crackles through the airwaves as our hard-boiled hero confronts corruption that runs deeper than anyone suspected. Listen as Johnny's quick wit and sharper instincts become his only allies in a dangerous room where every word could be his last. With the backing orchestra swelling ominously and that distinctive voice-over narration cutting through the darkness, this is radio drama at its most hypnotic—mystery unraveling in real time, with no second chances and no rewind button.

What made Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar a cornerstone of CBS's golden age was its documentary-like realism wedged into pure entertainment. Unlike the costumed heroes dominating the airwaves, Johnny was an everyman in a suit, solving cases through legwork and deduction rather than superpowers. The show's emphasis on actual insurance fraud and procedural details gave listeners a window into a professional world most would never see—all while keeping them perched on the edge of their seats. By 1956, when this episode aired, the show had already cultivated a devoted following who trusted Johnny's narration the way they trusted the morning newspaper.

This five-part McClain Matter represents the show at its finest: a complete investigation unfolding across an entire week of broadcasts. Tune in now to hear how it all resolves, and discover why, decades later, critics still rank Johnny Dollar among radio's greatest achievements.
