# Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar: The Cui Bono Matter (Part 2)

The fog rolls thick over San Francisco's waterfront as Johnny Dollar picks up where he left off, deeper now into a maze of murder, inheritance, and the oldest question in the criminal book: who benefits? When a wealthy industrialist turns up dead under suspicious circumstances, everyone stands to gain—the grieving widow, the ambitious nephew, the business partner with debts to cover. In this second installment, our quick-witted insurance investigator must navigate a web of lies and misdirection, armed with nothing but his sharp mind, his expense account, and an unshakeable instinct for trouble. The pressure mounts as Johnny closes in on the truth, each carefully placed question bringing him closer to a killer who has everything to lose. What begins as routine insurance fraud investigation spirals into something far more dangerous, where one misstep could mean Johnny's final case.

Since its debut in 1949, *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* captured America's imagination with its perfect blend of hard-boiled detective work and the intimate vulnerability of first-person narration. Johnny's running commentary—part cynical wiseccrack, part genuine concern for justice—made listeners feel like confidants in each investigation. The show's meticulous attention to detail, from Johnny's expense accounts (which became a running joke among fans) to authentically researched insurance schemes, elevated it above typical crime drama. By 1956, the show was in its golden age, crafting episodes that balanced noir atmosphere with genuine mystery-writing craft, anchored by star Bob Bailey's magnetic performance.

Join Johnny Dollar as he uncovers secrets in the city by the bay. *The Cui Bono Matter (Part 2)* airs this week—a case where everyone has motive, everyone has means, and only one person holds the truth. The question is: can Johnny find it before the killer strikes again?
