# The Cui Bono Matter (Episode 3)

As Johnny Dollar steps into the fog-choked streets of the financial district, he's up to his neck in a case that stinks of greed and double-dealing. The third installment of "The Cui Bono Matter" finds our intrepid insurance investigator unraveling a web of lies so intricate that even his sharp mind struggles to separate the guilty from the merely desperate. With nothing but his wits, a telephone, and a expense account that makes his bean-counting boss wince, Johnny pursues the question that haunts every noir mystery: *cui bono*—who benefits? This episode crackles with the kind of tension that only radio could deliver, where every footstep echoes with menace and every phone call might be the one that cracks the case wide open.

"Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar" stood apart from the crowded field of detective shows because it grounded its stories in the insurance world, a setting that lent an air of gritty realism to every caper. By 1956, the show had perfected its formula: a mystery that could only be solved through meticulous investigation, moral ambiguity that felt distinctly American, and Bob Bailey's weathered delivery that suggested a man who'd seen every angle of human nature. The show's obsessive accounting of Johnny's expenses became its signature quirk, a running gag that somehow underscored the mundane reality lurking beneath every glamorous crime.

If you've never experienced the Golden Age of radio drama, this is the episode to start with. Settle into your chair, dim the lights, and let the sound effects and sterling voice work transport you back to an America where mysteries unfolded in real time, one carefully chosen word at a time. The answer to "cui bono" awaits.
