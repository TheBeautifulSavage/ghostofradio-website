The Fathom Five Matter Ep 2

When Johnny Dollar arrives at the fog-shrouded docks of a New England fishing town, he discovers that the simple maritime insurance claim he's investigating conceals something far more sinister. A crew of five fishermen vanished without a trace from their vessel, The Fathom Five, leaving behind only cryptic logbook entries and the captain's widow clutching a half-paid claim. As Johnny navigates the weathered fishing shacks and smoky harbor taverns, he uncovers a tangle of debts, rivalries, and secrets that someone in this tight-knit community would rather keep submerged. With each interview, the walls close tighter, and Johnny realizes that the disappearance at sea may be no accident at all—but a carefully orchestrated vanishing act designed to defraud the insurance company. The hard-boiled detective must separate truth from the deliberate misdirection before the killer strikes again.

This 1956 episode exemplifies why "Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar" became one of radio's most enduring series, earning the Peabody Award and captivating millions of listeners with its sterling combination of procedural realism and pulp mystery. Unlike the supernatural melodrama or broad comedy that dominated the airwaves, Johnny Dollar offered something distinctly American: the meticulous, world-weary investigator working the margins of commerce and crime. Mandel Kramer's iconic portrayal brought a cynical charm to every case, treating each insurance investigation with the gravity of high-stakes detective work. The show pioneered the "expense account" format that would later influence television's greatest detective series.

Tune in now to experience Johnny Dollar at the height of his powers, solving the Fathom Five Matter with the keen eye and sharper wit that made him radio's most memorable investigator. You'll hear why audiences rushed to their sets each week for another perfectly crafted mystery from the golden age of audio drama.
