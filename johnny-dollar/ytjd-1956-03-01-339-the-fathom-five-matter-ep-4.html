# The Fathom Five Matter – Episode 4

When Johnny Dollar steps into the murky waters of salvage operations and sunken treasure, you know the case is about to get a whole lot deeper—and a whole lot deadlier. In this fourth installment of "The Fathom Five Matter," our dollar-a-day man finds himself entangled in a web of greed, deception, and maritime mystery that reaches from the glittering surface of respectability down to the crushing depths of the ocean floor. With each commercial break, another layer peels away: coded messages, rival salvagers with dangerous intentions, and insurance claims that don't quite add up. Bob Bailey's voice cuts through the static with measured skepticism, as Johnny navigates between honest crews and snake-oil artists, all while the clock ticks toward a revelation that could sink more than just ships.

By 1956, "Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar" had already established itself as radio's thinking man's detective drama. Unlike the pulp heroics of Shadow or the hardboiled one-liners of lesser noirs, this CBS gem grounded its stories in authentic insurance investigation—the unglamorous, methodical work of following paper trails and questioning witnesses. Johnny Dollar wasn't fighting supervillains; he was fighting human nature, the kind of everyday corruption that could hide behind a three-piece suit and a firm handshake. The show's writers crafted cases with genuine mystery architecture, rewarding listeners who paid close attention to seemingly trivial details.

"The Fathom Five Matter" stands as a perfect example of the series at its peak. If you've never experienced Johnny Dollar's particular brand of cool, methodical detection, or if you're a longtime fan seeking to revisit the cases that made radio's greatest private investigator legendary, set your dial to CBS this evening. Just remember: in Johnny's world, nothing is quite what it seems—and the deeper you dive, the darker it gets.
