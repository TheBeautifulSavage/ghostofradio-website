# The Plantagent Matter (Episode 2)

When our hero Johnny Dollar checks into the swanky Plantagent Hotel in this March 1956 episode, he's walking into a web of blackmail, betrayal, and big money that would make even a seasoned insurance investigator break a sweat. Someone's threatening to expose a dangerous secret, and Johnny's got exactly forty-eight hours to uncover the truth before an innocent person takes the fall. The rain hammers against the windows of dimly lit hotel suites, cigarette smoke curls through cramped office spaces, and every suspect—from the nervous desk clerk to the mysterious woman in 412—might just be the key to solving a case that could topple the hotel's entire operation. With Mandel Kramer's distinctive raspy voice leading us through the shadows, you'll find yourself hanging on every carefully placed clue and double-cross.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* became CBS radio's most prolific dramatic series precisely because it understood the appeal of the everyman gumshoe in post-war America. Unlike the superhero detectives of earlier radio drama, Johnny Dollar was an expense-account detective—wry, resourceful, and utterly believable. By 1956, the show had found its perfect rhythm, with Kramer having fully inhabited the character's weary competence and sardonic wit. Each fifteen-minute episode was crafted like clockwork, packing genuine mystery into an impossibly tight format while maintaining the atmospheric authenticity that made listeners feel like they were right there in the hotel lobby, notebook in hand.

Don't miss this masterclass in compressed storytelling. Tune in for *The Plantagent Matter* and discover why millions of Americans made Johnny Dollar their nightly appointment with danger and intrigue.
