# The Plantagent Matter (Episode 4)

When Johnny Dollar steps off the rain-slicked platform in Portland, Oregon, he carries with him the weight of three prior investigations that have led him deeper into the murky world of forged documents and disappeared heiresses. In this fourth installment of "The Plantagent Matter," our tireless insurance investigator finds himself trapped between a ruthless claims syndicate and a family desperate to hide their darkest secrets. The case that began as a routine $50,000 policy check has spiraled into something far more sinister—and as Johnny navigates fog-shrouded docks and shadowy hotel corridors, the line between investigator and victim grows dangerously thin. Each voice he encounters carries a hidden motive; each clue might be a carefully laid trap. This is noir radio at its finest: the steady, methodical voice of Mandel Kramer's Johnny Dollar cutting through the static of lies and misdirection, guided only by his wits, his .38, and the unwavering code of an honest man in a dishonest world.

"Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar" revolutionized the insurance investigation genre by grounding its hero in procedural realism rather than flashy heroics. Broadcasting from 1949 to 1962, the show earned its reputation through intelligent scripts, complex multi-part cases like "The Plantagent Matter," and Kramer's naturalistic performance—a voice that sounded like your slightly weary but incorruptibly decent neighbor, not a caped crusader. The detailed expense accounting that Johnny meticulously reported became the show's signature, making audiences feel like they were partners in genuine investigative work.

If you've followed Johnny Dollar's journey through the first three chapters of this compelling case, you cannot afford to miss Episode 4—where secrets surface and danger closes in. Tune in now and discover why, for thirteen years, millions of listeners made "Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar" their appointment with suspense.
