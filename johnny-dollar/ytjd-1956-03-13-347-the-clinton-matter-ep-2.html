# The Clinton Matter (Part 2)

The rain hammers against the windows of a dimly lit office as Johnny Dollar, expense account in hand, closes in on a web of deception that grows more sinister with each revelation. In this second installment of "The Clinton Matter," our intrepid insurance investigator finds himself trapped between competing lies, each more dangerous than the last. The missing heirloom, the grieving widow, the too-convenient alibi—nothing adds up, and Johnny knows that somewhere in this tangle of noir shadows and whispered confessions lies the truth that will either close the case or close his casket. Listen as the tension mounts with each tick of the clock and every cigarette-stained clue, as Johnny's sharp mind and sharper tongue cut through the fog of misdirection. This is insurance investigation at its finest: unglamorous, ungracious, and utterly gripping.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* stands as one of the golden age's most enduring triumphs, the longest-running dramatic series in radio history. Premiering in 1949 and dominating CBS schedules through 1962, the show captured postwar America's fascination with corruption, complexity, and the gritty underworld lurking beneath respectable surfaces. Each episode followed Johnny's expense account—that famous "Johnny Dollar, your faithful friend" narrator would catalog every cost from cab fare to coffee—grounding the fantastic tales of crime and deception in meticulous, mundane detail. Bob Bailey's world-weary delivery became iconic, his voice the very embodiment of the cynical private eye navigating a morally ambiguous landscape where even the victims might be perpetrators.

Don't miss this masterclass in radio suspense. Tune in now and discover why audiences made Johnny Dollar an institution, one carefully itemized case at a time.
