# The Clinton Matter (Episode 4)

The spring rain hammers against the windows of a shadowy Manhattan office as Johnny Dollar closes in on the truth—but someone in the Clinton organization is determined to keep him from finding it. In this fourth installment of a gripping four-part investigation, our insurance detective finds himself caught between a grieving widow's desperate plea and the calculated silence of powerful men who have everything to lose. With each carefully placed question, the danger mounts. Is it accident, suicide, or something far more sinister? The answers lie buried in a ledger, a hastily arranged funeral, and the nervous glances exchanged between men in expensive suits. Dollar's steady voice cuts through the tension like a knife as he inches closer to the truth—unaware that someone has already decided he knows too much.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* defined the golden age of radio detective fiction during a golden age itself. Running from 1949 through 1962, the show became the longest-running dramatic series in radio history, with Bob Bailey's weary, world-wise performance as America's only $50-a-day plus expenses insurance investigator earning him a devoted national audience. Each episode was crafted with meticulous attention to atmosphere and character, transforming mundane insurance claims into labyrinthine mysteries populated by desperate people and moral ambiguity. By 1956, the show had perfected its formula: tight scripts, noir sensibilities, and stories that reflected the anxieties of post-war America. The Clinton Matter represents the show at its finest—a complex, multi-episode arc that builds tension methodically across installments.

Step into the rain-slicked streets of 1950s America and discover why millions of listeners made this appointment radio a staple of their evenings. *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* awaits.
