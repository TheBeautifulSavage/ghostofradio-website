# The Jolly Roger Fraud Matter - Episode 2

Step into the fog-shrouded docks of a coastal mystery where nothing is quite as it seems. In this riveting continuation, insurance investigator Johnny Dollar finds himself neck-deep in a scheme involving pirate-themed novelties, suspicious claims, and characters whose stories don't quite add up. The second installment crackles with the kind of breathless tension that made radio audiences lean closer to their speakers—a carefully orchestrated web of deception begins to unravel as our cynical hero applies his street-smart investigative prowess and razor-sharp instincts. Every knock on a warehouse door, every hushed phone conversation, and every veiled threat carries the weight of danger. With Sterling Holloway's crisp narration guiding us through Johnny's methodical unraveling of the fraud, listeners will find themselves suspended between trust and suspicion, never quite sure who's being straight with the insurance man.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* represented the golden age of adult-oriented radio drama—intelligent, hard-boiled storytelling that appealed to post-war American sensibilities hungry for sophisticated entertainment. Between 1949 and 1962, the show established itself as a masterpiece of serialized drama, with storylines that could stretch across multiple episodes while maintaining nail-biting momentum. This March 1956 episode exemplifies the show's finest qualities: intricate plotting, snappy dialogue, and that unmistakable noir atmosphere where morality exists in shades of gray.

Don't miss this expertly crafted second installment of *The Jolly Roger Fraud Matter*. Tune in and discover why Johnny Dollar became legendary—where every case reveals something darker lurking beneath the surface, and the truth is always worth the risk.
