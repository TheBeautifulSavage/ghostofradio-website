# The Jolly Roger Fraud Matter, Episode 4

Picture yourself in a smoke-filled insurance office on a rain-slicked Manhattan evening in 1956. Johnny Dollar, America's most famous insurance investigator, finds himself entangled in a maritime swindle that grows more treacherous by the minute. As he closes in on the truth behind the fraudulent claim, the stakes escalate from mere dollars to something far more sinister—his very life. This fourth installment of "The Jolly Roger Fraud Matter" crackles with the authentic tension of a man navigating between desperate criminals and shifting alibis, all while the ticking of a clock reminds us that every decision could be his last. Expect explosive confrontations, clever misdirection, and those signature Johnny Dollar observations that cut through the fog of deception like a knife through darkness.

What makes this 1956 episode quintessential *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* is its meticulous construction of suspense—writer-producer Don Fedderson and star Bob Bailey crafted a show that set the gold standard for audio drama. Unlike the pulp theatricality of earlier detective shows, *Johnny Dollar* grounded its mysteries in real investigative procedure and psychological realism. By the mid-1950s, the program had become a cultural phenomenon, with listeners tuning in religiously to follow the "man with the action-packed expense account." This particular episode showcases the show's ability to sustain complex, multi-part narratives that demanded listener attention and rewarded genuine investment in the cases.
