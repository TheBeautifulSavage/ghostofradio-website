# The Lamarr Matter - Episode 5

Picture yourself in a dim Manhattan office on a rain-slicked evening in 1956, as private investigator Johnny Dollar leans back in his chair, fedora tilted low, reviewing a case that's grown murkier by the hour. In this fifth installment of "The Lamarr Matter," Dollar finds himself entangled in a web of deception that stretches from glittering nightclubs to shadowy warehouse docks, where nothing—and no one—is quite what they seem. A missing actress, forged insurance documents, and a cast of suspects with secrets worth killing for converge in an episode that crackles with the authentic snap of noir dialogue and the mounting tension of a man who's beginning to realize he's being played. Listen as the atmosphere thickens: the ambient sound of a city after dark, the sharp ring of a telephone, the barely concealed desperation in a woman's voice—all building toward a revelation that could unravel everything Johnny thought he knew about the case.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* was CBS's answer to the golden age of detective radio, and by 1956, the show had perfected its formula of sophisticated storytelling and genuine mystery. Unlike the comedic detectives or action-heavy adventures that dominated the airwaves, Johnny Dollar offered something rarer: a thinking man's mystery show, where every detail mattered and the investigation unfolded with methodical precision. Bob Bailey's portrayal of Dollar—weary, sharp, and perpetually skeptical—made each case feel startlingly real, grounded in the actual procedures of insurance investigation rather than pulp fiction theatrics.

Don't miss this pivotal chapter in "The Lamarr Matter." Tune in and discover why *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* remains one of radio's most beloved mysteries—where the truth is always more complicated than it first appears, and danger lurks in the spaces between the words.
