# The Laird Douglas Douglas Of Heatherscote Matter - Part Four

The Scottish Highlands have never sounded so sinister as they do through your radio speaker on this April evening in 1956. Johnny Dollar finds himself entangled in a web of ancient family secrets and modern insurance fraud, where every shadow seems to conceal a motive and every character harbors a dangerous truth. As our insurance investigator digs deeper into the fourth installment of this five-part adventure, the stakes have never been higher—a substantial claim hangs in the balance, but so does Dollar's very safety. With the sparse, haunting accents of the Highlands providing the perfect noir backdrop, you'll find yourself on the edge of your seat, wondering whether the mysterious Laird's death was truly accidental or something far more sinister. The writing crackles with tension, and the sound design transports you across the Atlantic to a place where old-world aristocracy collides with modern greed.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* was radio's most ambitious insurance investigation program, and for good reason. Running successfully from 1949 through 1962, the show pioneered the "floating" format—each case was its own self-contained novel, some spanning multiple episodes like this one. CBS gave the writers and producers remarkable freedom to craft complex, literary narratives that rivals the hard-boiled detective fiction dominating newsstands. This particular serial demonstrates the show's mastery of international intrigue and character development, proving that radio drama could be just as sophisticated and gripping as anything in print.
