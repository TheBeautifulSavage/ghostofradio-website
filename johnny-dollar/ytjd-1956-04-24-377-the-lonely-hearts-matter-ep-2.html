# The Lonely Hearts Matter – Episode 2

When Johnny Dollar picks up the phone on that fateful April evening in 1956, he finds himself entangled in a case that cuts deeper than the usual maritime claims and stolen jewels. A lonely widow's desperate search for companionship has led her into the web of a calculated con artist, and somewhere between the forged love letters and empty promises lies the bitter truth that will cost her everything. This second installment of "The Lonely Hearts Matter" plunges listeners into the shadowy world of romance scams—a uniquely modern tragedy unfolding in mid-century America. The investigation crackles with tension as Johnny's sharp mind and sharper tongue navigate between sympathetic witnesses and slippery suspects, while Mandel Kramer's world-weary narration captures the detective's growing conviction that some crimes prey on the most vulnerable parts of the human heart.

By 1956, *Yours Truly Johnny Dollar* had firmly established itself as CBS's premier adventure program, and this episode exemplifies why. Created by Blake Edwards and others, the show revolutionized the insurance investigator formula by making Johnny less a superhero and more a genuine problem-solver—a man whose quick wits and dogged persistence mattered more than guns or gadgets. The series' willingness to explore contemporary social issues, like the isolation that made elderly widows targets for exploitation, gave the show an unexpected depth that resonated with post-war audiences grappling with rapid social change.

Tune in to discover how Johnny Dollar unravels this intricate deception. His expense account may be modest, but his commitment to justice never wavers—and in a case where emotion clouds judgment, only a cool investigator's eye can separate fact from fiction.
