On a rain-slicked Manhattan evening, insurance investigator Johnny Dollar steps into a web of deception that stretches from the glittering showrooms of Fifth Avenue to the shadowy underworld of stolen gems. When a quarter-million-dollar sapphire vanishes under impossible circumstances, Johnny finds himself navigating a treacherous landscape of conflicting testimonies, suspicious jewelry merchants, and a cast of characters who each have everything to lose. This two-part adventure crackles with the tension of a man who knows the game—and knows that in the insurance business, everyone lies until proven honest. The clack of Johnny's typewriter, the ambient buzz of the city at night, and the distinctive trill of a mysterious woman's laugh create an atmosphere thick with intrigue and danger.

By 1956, Yours Truly Johnny Dollar had become the gold standard of radio noir, a show that perfectly captured the postwar American appetite for hard-boiled mystery and sophisticated urban storytelling. Unlike the flashy crime dramas that relied on gunfire and car chases, Johnny Dollar worked with his wits and his expense account—famously narrating each case as a meticulous accounting of every dollar spent in pursuit of the truth. The show's dedication to character development and plot intricacy earned it a devoted following and critical respect that many peers couldn't match.

Don your fedora and tune in for "The Silver Blue Matter," where every shadow conceals a suspect and every clue leads deeper into deception. This is insurance investigation at its finest—smart, tense, and absolutely essential listening.
