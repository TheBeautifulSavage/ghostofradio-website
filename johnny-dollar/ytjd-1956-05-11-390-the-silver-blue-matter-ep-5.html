# The Silver Blue Matter (Part 5)

As the clock strikes midnight in a Manhattan penthouse, insurance investigator Johnny Dollar finds himself trapped in a web of deception where every witness claims ignorance and every lead dissolves like smoke. In this fifth installment of "The Silver Blue Matter," our hard-boiled hero must navigate the treacherous underworld of stolen gems and international smuggling rings, where a seemingly simple claim has blossomed into a labyrinth of corruption spanning three continents. The tension crackles through your radio speaker as Johnny's raspy voice cuts through the darkness—each accusation, each revelation, drawing closer to a truth that someone would kill to keep buried. Will he uncover the mastermind behind the theft, or will he become just another loose end in a criminal enterprise that reaches far higher than any insurance company dares acknowledge?

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* stands as radio's finest achievement in the detective genre, and this 1956 episode exemplifies why the show commanded such devoted audiences during its thirteen-year run on CBS. Unlike the fantastic adventures flooding the airwaves, Johnny's cases ground themselves in procedural authenticity—insurance investigations, not comic book heroics—lending credibility that audiences craved in the increasingly skeptical post-war era. Star Bob Bailey's masterful delivery, combined with writer Jack Johnstone's intricate plotting, created mysteries that demanded listeners' full attention; there were no distractions, only voices, sound effects, and imagination.

Settle into your favorite chair, dim the lights, and prepare yourself for the penultimate chapter of one of radio's most cunningly constructed five-part mysteries. Johnny Dollar is waiting on the case that pays—and this Silver Blue Matter promises to deliver the noir atmosphere and clever twists that made *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* the thinking person's detective show. The answer lies waiting in the darkness. Are you ready to find it?
