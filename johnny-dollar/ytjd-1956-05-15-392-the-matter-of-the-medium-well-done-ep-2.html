# The Matter Of The Medium, Well Done — Part 2

Picture yourself in a dimly lit spiritualist parlor on a fog-shrouded evening, where desperate hearts seek communion with the departed—and where Johnny Dollar finds something far more sinister than ghostly whispers. In this second installment of "The Matter Of The Medium, Well Done," our insurance investigator finds himself deeper in a web of séances, fraudulent mediums, and a murder that refuses to stay buried. With only a five-day expense account and his razor-sharp instincts, Johnny must determine whether a woman's death was genuine tragedy or carefully orchestrated deception. The tension crackles through every scene: Is the sultry Madame Zelda a charlatan preying on the vulnerable, or is she herself being manipulated? The episode builds with mounting dread as Johnny peels back layer after layer of occult trickery to expose the cold, calculated crime beneath the theatrical trappings of the spirit world.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* represented the golden age of American radio drama, where the half-hour format allowed for serialized storytelling that kept audiences coming back night after night. Debuting in 1949 and running through 1962, the show became the longest-running dramatic program in radio history, with Johnny Dollar's expense-account cases becoming legendary among radio enthusiasts. The mid-1950s episodes like this one showcase the program at its creative peak, blending hard-boiled detective noir with the experimental storytelling possibilities unique to radio. Creator/producer Jack Johnstone crafted scripts that exploited sound design and voice acting to create psychological depth that visual media couldn't match.

Tune in now and discover why millions once crowded around their radios for Johnny Dollar's investigations. This meticulously preserved episode captures a moment when American radio drama was at its most inventive, most thrilling, and most deeply woven into the fabric of popular culture.
