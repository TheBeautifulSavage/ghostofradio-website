# The Tears of Night Matter, Part Two

The rain falls hard on the neon-slicked streets as insurance investigator Johnny Dollar finds himself deeper in a web of deception and danger than he bargained for. In this second chapter of "The Tears of Night Matter," our quick-witted hero must navigate a shadowy world of false identities, blackmail, and murder—where every witness has something to hide and every clue could lead to either salvation or a one-way ticket to the morgue. With only his sharp mind, sharper tongue, and a .38 Special at his side, Johnny Dollar closes in on the truth behind a claim that's worth far more than the insurance payout. Expect the crackling tension of a master detective under pressure, the atmospheric sound design that places you squarely in post-war America, and dialogue so quick and clever you'll want to hear it twice.

"Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar" became a cornerstone of CBS radio's golden age precisely because it married classic hard-boiled detective fiction with the authenticity of insurance investigation—a world most listeners had never glimpsed. The show's 1956 episodes represent the series at its creative peak, with writer John Dial and star Bob Bailey delivering scripts that rival any film noir of the era. These "Wp" episodes (recorded in the show's later seasons) showcase the technical sophistication radio had achieved, with layered sound effects and musical cues that create entire worlds in the listener's imagination.

Tune in now and discover why millions of Americans in the 1950s made Johnny Dollar their appointment listening. This is radio noir at its finest—sophisticated, thrilling, and utterly unforgettable.
