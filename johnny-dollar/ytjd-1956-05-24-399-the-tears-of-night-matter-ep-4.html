# Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar – "The Tears of Night Matter" (Episode 4)

Step into the rain-slicked streets of a city where nothing is quite what it seems. In this fourth installment of "The Tears of Night Matter," insurance investigator Johnny Dollar finds himself entangled in a web of deception that cuts deeper than his usual cases. A woman's desperate plea, a missing fortune, and a mystery that seems to shift with each new interview—Dollar must navigate the treacherous space between truth and convenient lies, where every witness has something to hide and the clock is ticking. With Bob Bailey's world-weary narration guiding you through the shadows, this episode captures the essence of post-war noir: cynical, atmospheric, and utterly gripping.

By 1956, *Yours Truly Johnny Dollar* had become the gold standard of detective radio drama, and for good reason. The show's commitment to serialized storytelling—with cases that unfolded across multiple episodes—was revolutionary, giving listeners genuine narrative stakes that extended beyond a tidy thirty-minute resolution. This episode, broadcast during the show's CBS glory years, represents the program at its creative peak, when talented writers understood that the real mystery wasn't just *what* happened, but *why* people lie about it. The show's influence on American entertainment cannot be overstated; it proved radio could deliver sophisticated, morally complex storytelling that would later inspire television and film noir.

If you've never experienced the authentic thrill of classic radio mystery, or if you're a devoted fan seeking to revisit this particular chapter in Johnny Dollar's casebook, this episode is an essential listen. Adjust the dial, dim the lights, and prepare yourself for an investigation where nothing—and no one—can be trusted.
