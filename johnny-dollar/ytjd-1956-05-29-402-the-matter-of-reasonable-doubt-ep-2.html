# The Matter of Reasonable Doubt (Part 2)

When the second installment of this two-part case opens, insurance investigator Johnny Dollar finds himself knee-deep in murky waters where nothing is quite what it seems. A seemingly straightforward claim has unraveled into a web of lies, alibis, and dangerous deceptions—and Johnny's meticulous eye for detail is his only shield against a killer who's counting on confusion. As the evidence stacks up contradictorily, listeners will experience that delicious tension as Johnny methodically peels back each layer of the case, his narration sharp and world-weary over a haunting orchestral score. The actors' performances crackle with suspicion and barely concealed desperation; you can practically hear the sweat on the suspects' brows as Johnny's questions grow sharper and his patience thinner.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* stands as one of the finest achievements in American radio drama, and episodes like this showcase why. Premiering in 1949 on CBS, the show pioneered the single-investigator format that would influence television's detective genre for decades. By 1956, Johnny Dollar had become a fixture in living rooms across America—a protagonist for the post-war everyman, solving cases with hard-boiled wisdom and relentless logic rather than luck or heroics. Each case was presented as a genuine insurance claim, grounding the drama in everyday reality while delivering genuine mystery and suspense.

This episode exemplifies the show at its peak, when writer-producer Jack Johnstone had refined the formula into something approaching perfection. The pacing, the performances, the moral ambiguity—it's radio storytelling at its finest. Tune in and discover why audiences still consider *Johnny Dollar* essential listening; this case will grip you from the opening moment to its final, satisfying resolution.
