# The Laughing Matter, Part Three

Picture this: it's a sweltering June evening in 1956, and insurance investigator Johnny Dollar finds himself cornered in a Los Angeles hotel room where nothing—and nobody—is what it seems. In this third installment of "The Laughing Matter," the case that began as a straightforward policy claim has spiraled into a maze of blackmail, theatrical intrigue, and murder most foul. As Dollar closes in on the truth, the walls literally close in around him. The script crackles with that signature blend of hard-boiled dialogue and genuine peril that made the show unmissable—you can almost feel the oppressive heat, hear the distant sirens, sense the moment when Johnny realizes he's been played for a fool. This is noir radio at its most assured, where the mystery deepens with every commercial break and nothing is resolved until the final, shocking revelation.

By 1956, *Yours Truly Johnny Dollar* had become CBS's crown jewel of dramatic programming, a show that proved radio was far from dead even as television began casting its long shadow across American entertainment. What set it apart was its meticulous plotting and the brilliant performance of actor Bob Bailey, whose naturalistic delivery made you believe Dollar was a real man working real cases—complete with actual expense accounts read aloud. The show's commitment to serialized storytelling, like this three-part adventure, was revolutionary for its time, demanding listener loyalty and rewarding devoted fans with intricate mysteries that unfolded across multiple episodes.

Don your fedora and settle into that armchair—Johnny Dollar needs you to solve this one alongside him. Tune in now and discover why audiences from coast to coast considered this the finest detective series radio had to offer.
