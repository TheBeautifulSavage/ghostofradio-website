# The Laughing Matter, Episode 5

On this June evening in 1956, insurance investigator Johnny Dollar finds himself tangled in the darkest corners of a case that began with seemingly innocent laughter—but has spiraled into something far more sinister. As our intrepid dollar-a-day operative digs deeper into the fifth installment of this five-part serial, he discovers that the jovial mask concealing a major fraud scheme is beginning to crack. With Mandel Kramer's distinctive voice guiding you through rain-slicked streets and shadowy office corridors, you'll experience the mounting tension as Johnny closes in on the truth. The stakes have never been higher, the deceptions never more elaborate, and the danger lurking in the shadows never more real. This is where the investigation reaches its crescendo—where every clue, every suspicious conversation, every misdirection finally begins to reveal its purpose.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* stood apart in the golden age of radio as the first and only series to be sponsored by an insurance company, yet it maintained the grit and authenticity of hard-boiled noir. Airing on CBS from 1949 to 1962, the show presented Johnny as a freelance insurance investigator tackling cases that blended mystery, crime, and the everyday moral ambiguities of post-war America. The serialized format—particularly these multi-part investigations like "The Laughing Matter"—allowed listeners to develop a genuine investment in Johnny's pursuit of truth, making each episode an event to be anticipated.

For those who cherish the golden age of radio drama, or anyone seeking a masterclass in suspenseful storytelling, this fifth episode represents the thrilling payoff to an expertly woven tale. Tune in now and discover why millions of Americans made Johnny Dollar their nightly appointment with mystery and intrigue.
