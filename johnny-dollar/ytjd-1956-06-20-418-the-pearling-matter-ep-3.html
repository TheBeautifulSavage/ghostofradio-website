# The Pearling Matter (Part Three)

The humid Queensland night closes in around Johnny Dollar as he stalks through shadowed pearl warehouses and dimly-lit hotel corridors, chasing down a fortune in stolen lustre and blood-soaked secrets. In this third installment of "The Pearling Matter," our quick-witted insurance investigator finds himself caught between ruthless smugglers, desperate pearlers, and a mysterious woman whose motives remain as opaque as the dark waters from which these treasures were pulled. With danger lurking in every transaction and the case spiraling deeper into corruption and double-crosses, Johnny must rely on his sharp instincts and quicker draw to survive the Australian underworld and recover what's been lost—or die trying. The percussion and moody brass of the original soundtrack propel each scene with mounting tension, while the crackling authenticity of mid-century sound design transports listeners directly into the thick, sultry danger of a noir thriller unfolding thousands of miles from home.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* represented the golden age of radio drama at its finest—a sophisticated blend of hard-boiled detective fiction and serialized storytelling that captivated millions of CBS listeners throughout the 1950s. The show's genius lay in its ability to weave intricate multi-part cases that kept audiences returning night after night, coupled with the incomparable voice work of star Bob Bailey, whose Johnny Dollar became the quintessential American gumshoe. These episodic adventures, recorded with meticulous attention to sound and pacing, became a template for what sophisticated radio drama could achieve.

Don't miss this electrifying third chapter. Tune in as Johnny Dollar navigates betrayal and danger in the exotic pearling ports of Australia, where every clue brings him closer to a solution that could cost him everything.
