# The Long Shot Matter - Part One

Picture this: a dimly lit insurance office where the rain hammers against the windows like an impatient creditor, and you're about to hear one of the most intricately plotted cases of Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar's career. In this June 1956 episode, our quick-witted investigator finds himself tangled in a web of high-stakes gambling, hidden identities, and a fortune that hinges on the outcome of a horse race. As Dollar's distinctive voice guides you through shadowy hotel corridors and smoky backroom deals, you'll find yourself questioning every character's motives—including Johnny's own. The tension builds masterfully across this two-part mystery, where the "long shot" proves to be far more dangerous than anyone anticipated, and nothing—not even a seemingly straightforward claim—is quite what it appears to be.

This episode arrives during the golden age of Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar, when the show had already established itself as CBS's crown jewel of detective radio drama. Unlike the flat, formulaic mysteries that plagued lesser programs, Johnny Dollar stories were built on meticulous plotting and authentic insurance industry details that lent credibility to every case. This particular serial format, spanning two episodes, showcases the show's confidence in its writing and audience intelligence. By 1956, millions of listeners tuned in faithfully, making Johnny Dollar the highest-rated detective show on radio—a testament to its superior storytelling and the magnetic performance of star Edmond O'Brien.

If you've never experienced the unique thrill of a classic radio mystery, this is the perfect place to begin. Close your eyes, let the sound effects transport you, and allow Johnny Dollar's measured narration to pull you into a case where the stakes are personal, the clues are carefully planted, and the truth lurks just beyond the next commercial break.
