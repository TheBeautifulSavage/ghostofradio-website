# The Long Shot Matter (Part 2)

Picture this: it's a sweltering June night in 1956, and Johnny Dollar finds himself deeper in the shadows than ever before. In part two of "The Long Shot Matter," our quick-witted insurance investigator has crossed into genuinely dangerous territory—where the stakes aren't just dollars and cents anymore, but cold, hard survival. The orchestra swells with that signature noir tension as Johnny pieces together a puzzle that grows more sinister by the minute. Expect taut dialogue, unexpected betrayals, and the kind of atmospheric storytelling that made listeners grip their radio dials. There's a gun that shouldn't exist, a victim nobody remembers, and Johnny Dollar standing alone with nothing but his wits and his expense account between him and oblivion.

What made *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* a phenomenon on CBS radio was its realism wrapped in glamorous mystery. Unlike the pulp fantasies of superhero shows, Johnny operated in a world of insurance claims, corrupt small-town officials, and the unglamorous machinery of crime that nobody else wanted to investigate. Created by writer Blake Edwards, the show became the longest-running dramatic series on network radio for good reason—it treated its audience like intelligent adults. This 1956 episode exemplifies the show's golden period, when the writing had perfected its balance of hard-boiled vernacular and genuinely clever plotting, before tape recording would eventually replace the live broadcast format that made every performance electric.

Switch on your receiver and join Johnny Dollar as he navigates the murky underbelly of small-town corruption. "The Long Shot Matter" waits for no one—and neither should you.
