# The Long Shot Matter (Ep. 4) - June 28, 1956

Johnny Dollar finds himself tangled in a web of high-stakes gambling, broken promises, and desperate men as the fourth installment of "The Long Shot Matter" unfolds through the crackling speakers. This episode plunges listeners into the smoky underbelly of race tracks and back-room poker games, where the line between luck and larceny blurs dangerously. As Johnny digs deeper into a case that began innocuously enough, the stakes escalate with each revelation—a missing insurance claim worth serious money, witnesses who won't talk, and a dangerous figure lurking just beyond the shadows. The atmosphere is thick with suspicion and mounting tension; you can practically hear the rain-slicked streets and the nervous shuffle of cards in eager hands. This is classic noir territory, where every lead could be a dead end, and every ally might be an enemy in disguise.

By 1956, "Yours Truly Johnny Dollar" had become CBS's crown jewel of the detective genre, combining the gritty realism of insurance investigation with the dramatic flair that made radio's golden age unforgettable. Johnny Dollar was no hard-boiled cliché—he was methodical, intelligent, and deeply human, navigating America's postwar landscape of greed and desperation. The show's multi-part episodes like "The Long Shot Matter" allowed for sophisticated storytelling that rivaled any dramatic presentation of the era, with complex plots that demanded listeners' full attention across multiple evenings.
