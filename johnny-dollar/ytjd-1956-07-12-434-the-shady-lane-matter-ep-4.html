# The Shady Lane Matter, Episode 4

Picture yourself in a dimly lit office on a rain-soaked evening in 1956, the blue glow of a cigarette ember the only light as Johnny Dollar leans back in his chair, turning over the pieces of a puzzle that refuses to fit. In this fourth installment of "The Shady Lane Matter," our intrepid insurance investigator finds himself caught between a widow's desperate plea and a web of secrets that runs deeper than anyone suspected. The stakes have never been higher—or the motives more murky—as Johnny's sharp questioning and sharper instincts cut through layers of deception. What begins as a routine claim investigation spirals into dangerous territory, where every answer spawns two new questions, and trust becomes the most valuable currency of all. The tension crackles through every scene, every telephone conversation, every shadowy encounter on dimly-lit streets.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* revolutionized the detective drama when it premiered in 1949, creating something wholly unique in radio: a series told entirely in the first person, with Johnny's sardonic narration and internal monologues drawing listeners directly into his world of moral ambiguity and hard-boiled logic. Unlike the glamorous detectives of the era, Johnny was an everyman—an insurance investigator obsessed with getting to the truth, not for glory, but because the job demanded it. By 1956, the show had perfected its formula, balancing character-driven storytelling with genuine mystery, proving that radio drama could be sophisticated, nuanced, and utterly compelling.

Don your fedora and step into the shadows with Johnny Dollar. Episode 4 of "The Shady Lane Matter" awaits—where nothing is quite what it seems, and the truth may cost more than anyone is willing to pay.
