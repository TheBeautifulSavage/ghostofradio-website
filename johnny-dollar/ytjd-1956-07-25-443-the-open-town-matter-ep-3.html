# The Open Town Matter – Part Three

Picture this: the scorching Nevada desert at midnight, where the neon glows of a crooked frontier town cast lurid shadows across dust-covered streets. Johnny Dollar, America's most celebrated insurance investigator, finds himself caught between corrupt sheriff's deputies and a murder that threatens to unravel a web of corruption reaching into the highest offices of the state. In this thrilling third installment of "The Open Town Matter," Dollar's expense account grows as perilous as his situation—bribes, blackmail, and bullets punctuate his investigation into a policy claim that may cost him his life. The tension crackles through your speaker as Johnny navigates back rooms and back alleys, where every witness has something to hide and trust is a luxury no man can afford.

By 1956, *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* had already become the gold standard of radio drama, proving that the insurance claim could be just as gripping as any detective mystery. CBS kept listeners on the edge of their seats week after week with intricate plots, clever dialogue penned by writer Blake Edwards and others, and the magnetic presence of star Bob Bailey, whose rapid-fire delivery and everyman charm made Johnny Dollar the working man's hero. Multi-part stories like "The Open Town Matter" showcased the show's sophistication—these weren't simple whodunits but complex moral puzzles wrapped in authentic noir atmosphere.

Don't let this investigation pass you by. Tune in now to hear how Johnny Dollar navigates the open town and emerges from the Nevada heat. With every episode, you'll discover why this program captivated millions and remains essential listening for any golden age radio enthusiast. Dollar's adventure awaits.
