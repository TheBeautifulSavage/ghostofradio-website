The Sea Legs Matter Ep 4

The salt spray mingles with cigarette smoke as Johnny Dollar finds himself neck-deep in maritime mayhem, pursuing a case that bleeds money, murder, and maritime mystery across the briny deep. In this fourth installment of The Sea Legs Matter, our quick-witted insurance investigator trades his usual city streets for the creaking decks and shadowy cargo holds of a vessel where nothing—and no one—can be trusted. Listen as Johnny navigates treacherous waters, both literal and figurative, piecing together a puzzle where every witness has something to hide and the ocean itself seems complicit in the crime. The fog-laden atmosphere crackles with danger, the steady hum of ship engines underscoring tense interrogations that could end in revelation... or Johnny taking a one-way plunge into the dark Atlantic.

Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar represented something revolutionary in 1956: a sophisticated, adult-oriented mystery series that demanded its listeners' full attention without relying on visual spectacle. Starring Mandel Kramer as the titular investigator, the show pioneered the "operating expense" narrative device—Dollar would constantly quote his mounting case costs, grounding the fantastical adventures in mercenary reality. By the mid-1950s, when this episode aired, the show had perfected its formula: tight writing, atmospheric sound design, and the wry, world-weary delivery of a man who'd seen every angle of human corruption. This wasn't comic-book heroics; this was noir for the discerning radio audience.

If you crave the golden age of radio drama—where every creak, every pause, every perfectly-timed gunshot spoke volumes—then The Sea Legs Matter awaits you. Tune in and discover why Johnny Dollar remains one of broadcasting's most compelling detectives.
