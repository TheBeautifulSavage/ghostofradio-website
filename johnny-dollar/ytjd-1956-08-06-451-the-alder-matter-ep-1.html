# The Alder Matter, Episode 1 (1956-08-06)

Picture this: the rain hammers against a downtown office window as insurance investigator Johnny Dollar lights another cigarette, his expense account growing fatter by the hour. Tonight, listeners are invited into *The Alder Matter*—a case that begins with a simple claim but unravels into something far more sinister. Dollar's dry narration guides us through dimly-lit streets and smoky interrogation rooms, where nothing is quite what it seems and trust is the most dangerous commodity of all. This is classic mid-1950s noir, where every witness has something to hide, and the clicking of a secretary's typewriter sets the rhythm for deception. As the first episode builds, you'll find yourself suspended in that uniquely American world where crime and commerce collide, and a man's word—and his investigation—is all that stands between truth and a very profitable lie.

By 1956, *Yours Truly Johnny Dollar* had become the gold standard of radio detective fiction, having evolved from a sponsored quarter-hour into a full-bodied series that prided itself on authentic insurance-investigation procedure married to compelling human drama. What set the show apart was its commitment to realism: the writers consulted actual claim adjusters, and Johnny's cases were grounded in the mundane yet morally complex world of insurance fraud, theft, and negligence. While other detective shows reveled in outlandish plots, Johnny Dollar worked smaller, closer, digging into the messy motivations of ordinary people caught in extraordinary circumstances.

Don't miss this first installment of *The Alder Matter*—a case sure to remind you why this show captivated millions of Americans huddled around their radios. Johnny Dollar has fifty cents a mile, plus expenses, and a nose for trouble. Tonight, the meter starts running.
