# The Kranesburg Matter - Episode 3

The case grows darker as Johnny Dollar's investigation into the Kranesburg matter spirals deeper into a web of deception that stretches across state lines and into the shadowy corners of post-war corporate America. In this third installment, our hard-boiled insurance detective finds himself cornered between competing interests, each with their own reasons for keeping the truth buried. The familiar crackle of mid-century telephone lines and the ambient hum of a late-night office set the stage for Johnny's methodical interrogations, punctuated by dramatic musical stings that announce each new revelation. Listeners will find themselves suspended in that delicious moment before the truth breaks open—where every alibi is suspect, every motive questionable, and the seven-hundred-fifty-dollar-a-day man must decide whom, if anyone, to trust.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* stands as one of radio's most underrated achievements, a show that proved the medium could sustain sophisticated serialized storytelling well into the television age. By 1956, when this episode aired, the program had already established itself as a master class in narrative construction—what made the series revolutionary was its commitment to the multi-part case arc, allowing complex mysteries to unfold across several episodes rather than resolving neatly in twenty-two minutes. Johnny Dollar, portrayed with world-weary authenticity by Edmond O'Brien, embodied the postwar American obsession with efficiency and rationality even as the cases themselves revealed a world of moral ambiguity that no amount of detective work could fully resolve.

Settle in with a cup of coffee and experience radio drama at its peak. *The Kranesburg Matter* awaits—three episodes in, and the real danger is only beginning to emerge from behind the lies.
