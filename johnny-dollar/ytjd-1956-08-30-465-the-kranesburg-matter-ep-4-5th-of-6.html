# The Kranesburg Matter - Episode 4

The humid Manhattan night closes in like a fist as Johnny Dollar navigates the treacherous backrooms of high finance and higher stakes. Our insurance investigator finds himself trapped in a web of forged documents, missing witnesses, and a client whose story shifts with each passing hour. With the case spinning toward its climax, every lead Johnny pursues seems to tighten the noose—whether around the guilty party's neck or his own remains agonizingly unclear. The fourth installment of "The Kranesburg Matter" delivers the mounting tension and moral ambiguity that made this series a fixture in American living rooms: the snap of a lighter, the crackle of a telephone line, the sharp intake of breath before a dangerous confession.

"Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar" represented something uniquely American in the golden age of radio—the everyman detective, more accountant than action hero, solving crimes with sharp wit and persistence rather than derring-do. Starring Bob Bailey in the role he inhabited for fourteen years and over 300 episodes, the show pioneered the five-part storyline format that would influence serialized storytelling for decades. Set against the backdrop of 1950s corporate America, these episodes captured a nation navigating Cold War anxieties, insurance fraud schemes, and the moral complexity of modern commerce. By 1956, when this episode aired, Johnny Dollar had become as essential to the radio audience as the evening newspaper.
