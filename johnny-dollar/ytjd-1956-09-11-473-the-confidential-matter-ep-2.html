# The Confidential Matter - Part Two

The rain hammers against the windows of a Manhattan office building as Johnny Dollar finds himself caught between a beautiful widow's tears and a corpse that shouldn't exist. In this gripping second installment, our intrepid insurance investigator discovers that the fifty-thousand-dollar claim on his desk is merely the tip of a far darker iceberg. With only his wits, his expense account, and a growing list of suspects who'd kill to keep their secrets buried, Johnny must navigate a labyrinth of lies, blackmail, and passion gone wrong. The stakes have never been higher, and every shadow in the night could conceal a killer. Mandel Kramer's crisp narration pulls you deeper into the mystery with each perfectly timed revelation, building tension that crackles through your radio speaker.

This September 1956 episode represents the golden age of radio drama, when sponsors trusted writers to craft serialized narratives of genuine sophistication and moral complexity. *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* stands apart from its contemporaries—no fantastic gadgetry, no superhuman powers, just a smart man with a calculator and a conscience trying to separate fact from fiction. The show's innovation lay in its five-day serialized format, where a single case unfolded across an entire week of broadcasts, allowing listeners to genuinely invest in outcomes and characters. CBS's commitment to this program during radio's twilight years speaks to its loyal audience and critical esteem.
