# The Imperfect Alibi Matter (Episode 3)

As the CBS airwaves crackle to life on this September evening in 1956, Johnny Dollar finds himself entangled in a case that hinges on the most fragile of defenses: an alibi that doesn't quite add up. With his world-weary narration guiding listeners through dimly-lit hotel corridors and smoky interrogation rooms, Dollar pursues the threads of deception with the dogged determination that has made him America's most trusted insurance investigator. The stakes are personal this time—a woman's freedom hangs in the balance, and every detail Johnny uncovers seems to complicate rather than clarify the truth. The taut script crackles with tension as witnesses contradict one another, timelines shift like shadows under a flickering neon sign, and Dollar must separate fact from carefully constructed fiction.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* represented something singular in radio's golden age: a show that treated its audience like adults, serving up complex mysteries wrapped in authentic hard-boiled cynicism. Unlike the cartoonish detectives that populated the airwaves, Johnny Dollar was a working professional, handling insurance cases that ranged from simple fraud to elaborate schemes involving murder. The series' success lay not just in its plots but in Dollar's voice—wry, observant, and perpetually skeptical—delivering some of radio's finest narrative voice-over work. This September 1956 episode exemplifies the show at its peak, when the formula had been perfected and the writing sharp as a private eye's instincts.
