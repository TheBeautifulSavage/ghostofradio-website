# The Imperfect Alibi Matter (Part 4)

Johnny Dollar's shoes click against rain-slicked pavement as he closes in on the truth—and a killer who's running out of time. In this fourth installment of "The Imperfect Alibi Matter," our quick-witted insurance investigator finds himself trapped in a web of contradictions, where every witness claims an airtight alibi, yet someone is lying. As the noose tightens, Dollar must navigate dimly-lit offices, suspicious hotel clerks, and the kind of desperate characters who would commit murder to keep their secrets buried. The tension crackles through your speakers as Johnny pieces together the final puzzle, each revelation bringing him closer to danger—and to the one alibi that will crack the whole case wide open.

This episode represents the golden era of *Yours Truly Johnny Dollar*, when the show had found its perfect voice: sharp dialogue, genuinely suspenseful plotting, and Bob Bailey's brilliant performance as a detective who was equal parts street-smart and cerebral. Unlike the pulpier crime shows of the era, Johnny Dollar cases unfolded with meticulous logic and real consequences. The multi-part "matter" format kept audiences glued to their radios night after night throughout 1956, creating appointment listening during an era when radio dramas still commanded millions of devoted fans despite television's rising popularity.

If you haven't yet experienced the thrill of following Johnny Dollar through his cases, "The Imperfect Alibi Matter" is the perfect entry point—complex enough to satisfy devoted listeners, accessible enough for newcomers, and utterly gripping from first word to last. Tune in and discover why *Yours Truly Johnny Dollar* remains one of the finest examples of American radio drama ever broadcast.
