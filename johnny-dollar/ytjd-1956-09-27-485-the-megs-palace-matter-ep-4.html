# The Meg's Palace Matter (Episode 4)

Picture this: the foggy docks of San Francisco, 1956. Insurance investigator Johnny Dollar steps into the murky world of nightclub dealings, blackmail, and a murder that refuses to stay buried. As our hero works the fourth installment of the Meg's Palace case, the tension crackles through your radio speaker—a mysterious woman with secrets, a dead body with complications, and Johnny caught between powerful forces who'd rather see this investigation disappear. Mandel Kramer's crisp narration guides you through labyrinthine clues and red herrings, while the sound effects team paints a noir landscape of clinking glasses, footsteps on wet pavement, and the ever-present threat lurking just beyond the next commercial break.

Yours Truly Johnny Dollar represented the golden age of radio drama at its finest, and this September 1956 broadcast showcases why the show captivated millions. Created by Blake Edwards and featuring Bob Bailey's iconic performance, Johnny Dollar was radio's only privately financed insurance investigator—a brilliant conceit that allowed writers to spin stories far beyond typical detective fare. The Meg's Palace Matter was one of the show's celebrated multi-part cases, the kind that kept listeners rushing home to catch the next episode. By 1956, the program had already proven itself a masterclass in serialized storytelling, blending hard-boiled dialogue with genuine mystery and character depth that elevated it above simple pulp entertainment.

This episode stands as a perfect entry point into the world of classic radio drama, where imagination supplants special effects and superb writing carries the day. Don't let this one slip past you—tune in and experience what millions heard firsthand, a moment when radio was America's greatest storyteller.
