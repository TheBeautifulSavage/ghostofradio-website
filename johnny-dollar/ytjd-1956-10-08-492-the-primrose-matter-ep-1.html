# The Primrose Matter, Part 1

Step into the smoky offices of an insurance investigator for whom the case always comes first—and the danger is never far behind. On this October evening in 1956, Johnny Dollar finds himself entangled in *The Primrose Matter*, a mystery that begins with a simple claim and spirals into shadowy complications that will test both his wits and his conscience. The case opens in a fog of uncertainty: missing documents, suspicious timelines, and clients who aren't quite telling the whole truth. As Dollar presses deeper into the investigation, listeners will discover why this insurance investigator has earned his reputation as radio's most dependable sleuth. With each commercial break comes the delicious anticipation of what revelation awaits in the next act.

By 1956, *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* had already become a fixture in American living rooms, part of that golden age when radio drama could transport millions to a world of intrigue and moral ambiguity in real time. The show's brilliance lay in its formula—the everyday business of insurance investigation transformed into something genuinely thrilling through superior writing and the magnetic personality of star Bob Bailey. Unlike the superhero detectives dominating the airwaves, Johnny Dollar was everyman's gumshoe: intelligent, skeptical, and driven by professional integrity rather than ego. This episode represents the show at its peak, when the serialized multi-part cases had become more sophisticated and psychologically complex than ever before.

If you've never experienced the particular magic of old-time radio, or if you're a devoted follower of Johnny Dollar's investigations, *The Primrose Matter* is essential listening. Settle in, adjust the dial, and let the sound effects and voices transport you back to an era when radio was America's theater of the mind.
