# The Primrose Matter - Part Three

The autumn rain hammers against the window of a seedy Manhattan hotel room as insurance investigator Johnny Dollar finds himself cornered—literally and figuratively. A seemingly simple claim has spiraled into a web of blackmail, false identities, and a missing heiress whose disappearance may not have been voluntary at all. In this third installment, our quick-witted dollar-a-day operative must navigate the treacherous streets of the city's underworld, where every shadow conceals a potential suspect and every phone call could be his last. Expect the sharp staccato dialogue, the layered plot twists, and the razor-sharp timing that made Johnny Dollar the thinking man's detective—a show where brains, not bullets, solve the case.

By 1956, *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* had become a masterclass in serialized storytelling, proving that radio drama had lost none of its power in the age of television's rise. CBS's commitment to the show meant top-tier writing from master craftsmen who understood the medium's intimate possibilities—that unique ability to create tension through voice alone, to let listeners' imaginations paint the noir landscape more vividly than any image ever could. The "Primrose Matter" multi-part episodes showcased this brilliance, unfolding complex mysteries across several broadcasts that kept audiences on edge all week long, gathered around their receivers.

If you've never experienced the golden age of radio drama, or if you're a devoted fan seeking to revisit these treasured broadcasts, this episode awaits you. Step back into 1956, pour yourself a drink, dim the lights, and let Johnny Dollar guide you through a mystery that still captivates nearly seventy years later. These voices, this story, this world—they're waiting for you.
