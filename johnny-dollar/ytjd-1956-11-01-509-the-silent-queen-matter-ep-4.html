# The Silent Queen Matter - Part 4

The fog rolls thick through San Francisco's Chinatown as Johnny Dollar closes in on a mystery that grows more dangerous with every passing hour. When a priceless jade statue—the legendary Silent Queen—vanishes from a locked museum vault, all evidence points to an elaborate con that reaches far beyond the City by the Bay. In this fourth installment, our world-weary investigator finds himself caught between warring tong leaders, a beautiful art dealer with secrets of her own, and a shadow figure who seems always one step ahead. The tension mounts as Johnny realizes someone will kill to keep the statue's true location buried. With only his wits, his expense account, and his hard-won street instincts, Johnny must navigate the treacherous underworld of black market antiquities to uncover the truth before his investigation becomes his obituary.

By 1956, *Yours Truly Johnny Dollar* had become CBS's crown jewel of adventure radio, with Bob Bailey's iconic performance as the cynical yet principled insurance investigator captivating millions of listeners nationwide. The show's brilliance lay in its formula: complex, multi-part cases that rewarded loyal audiences with intricate plotting worthy of the best detective fiction. Unlike the straightforward melodramas of earlier radio fare, *Johnny Dollar* offered sophisticated storytelling that appealed equally to hard-boiled fiction devotees and casual radio fans. The "Silver" era of the show, which includes this episode, represented the series at its creative peak—writers crafting intricate puzzles set against vivid locales, from exotic ports to corporate boardrooms, all brought to life through evocative sound design and Bailey's distinctive narration.

Don't miss the electrifying conclusion of "The Silent Queen Matter." Tune in and discover whether Johnny Dollar lives to collect his fee—or whether this case proves his last.
