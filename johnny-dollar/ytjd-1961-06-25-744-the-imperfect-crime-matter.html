# The Imperfect Crime Matter

Step into the rain-slicked streets of a city where nothing is quite what it seems. When insurance investigator Johnny Dollar receives a case that appears almost too straightforward—a robbery so perfectly executed it might as well be textbook—he knows better than to accept the surface story. As he peels back the layers of this supposedly impeccable crime, Johnny discovers that perfection itself becomes the clue. With each witness interview and dusty file examined, the contradictions multiply, and what seemed like a clean break-in unravels into something far more sinister. The tension builds through crackling phone calls, tense interrogations, and those signature Johnny Dollar moments where a single overlooked detail cracks the entire case wide open. The stakes aren't merely monetary—they're deeply personal for someone, and Johnny's legendary ability to separate fact from carefully constructed fiction may be the only thing standing between truth and a perfect cover-up.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* entered its golden age in 1961, with creator-writer Jack Johnstone crafting stories that elevated the insurance investigation genre beyond simple procedurals. Johnny Dollar became radio's quintessential everyman hero—neither a hardboiled gumshoe nor a bumbling amateur, but a thinking man's investigator, armed with logic, intuition, and an unflinching dedication to the truth. The show's sophisticated writing and Robert Readick's nuanced performance made each case feel immediately credible, grounding listeners in a world of plausible crime and human motivation rather than melodrama.

If you've never experienced Johnny Dollar, this episode is a perfect entry point into one of radio's finest achievements. And if you're a devoted fan, this 1961 installment represents the show at its intelligent, suspenseful peak. Tune in and discover why, decades later, *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* remains the gold standard of detective radio drama.
