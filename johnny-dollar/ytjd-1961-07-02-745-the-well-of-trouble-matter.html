# The Well of Trouble Matter

When the phone rings in Johnny Dollar's Manhattan office on this sweltering July evening in 1961, it brings news of a desperate situation in the American heartland. A mining accident at a remote prospecting operation has left the insurance company with more questions than answers—and Johnny Dollar smelling trouble. The recording crackles with authentic period detail as our hero finds himself navigating dusty mining towns, interviewing suspicious foremen, and uncovering a web of sabotage that goes far deeper than anyone suspected. Bob Bailey's weathered voice carries you through dimly lit mine shafts and tense confrontations, while the sound design—echoing footsteps, grinding machinery, the ominous rumble of distant explosions—pulls you directly into the mystery. By the time the final twist reveals itself, you'll understand why Johnny Dollar earned his reputation as insurance's most resourceful investigator.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* emerged during the golden age of radio as something refreshingly different: a hardboiled mystery series built entirely around the meticulous work of insurance investigation rather than police work or private detective clichés. The show's documentary-like attention to procedural detail, combined with genuine noir atmosphere, made it wildly popular through the 1950s and early '60s. This 1961 episode represents the series at its peak, when writers had perfected the balance between authentic investigative procedure and genuine suspense. Bailey's portrayal of Dollar—a man who solves crimes through dogged determination and keen observation rather than gunplay—defined a uniquely American archetype that resonated with post-war audiences.

Don't miss this chance to experience radio drama at its finest. Settle in, dim the lights, and let *The Well of Trouble Matter* transport you back to an era when mysteries unfolded through words alone, and the human voice could conjure entire worlds of intrigue and danger.
