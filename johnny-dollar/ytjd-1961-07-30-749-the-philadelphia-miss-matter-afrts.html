# Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar: The Philadelphia Miss Matter

On a sweltering July evening in 1961, insurance investigator Johnny Dollar finds himself entangled in a case that reeks of deception and danger in the City of Brotherly Love. When a mysterious woman surfaces with a claim that threatens to unravel a carefully constructed web of lies, Johnny must navigate the shadowy streets of Philadelphia with nothing but his wits, his expense account, and an unwavering commitment to the truth. The stakes climb higher with each revelation, and the temperature—both literal and figurative—keeps rising as Johnny uncovers secrets someone desperately wanted to keep buried. Listeners will find themselves drawn into the smoky, tension-laden atmosphere that defines the world of mid-century insurance investigation, where a single misstep could mean the difference between a solved case and a very final goodbye.

By 1961, *Yours Truly Johnny Dollar* had already carved out its legendary place in radio history as one of the last surviving dramatic series of the golden age. The show's brilliance lay in its meticulous attention to detail—those meticulously logged expense accounts that framed each episode became as much a character as Johnny himself. This particular installment, preserved from Armed Forces Radio Service archives, captures the show at its peak, when creator-writer Jack Moyles had perfected the formula of hard-boiled mystery meeting the bureaucratic mundanities of insurance work. It's a uniquely American blend of noir cynicism and procedural realism that influenced decades of detective fiction to come.

Don your fedora and dim the lights—*The Philadelphia Miss Matter* awaits. This is the sound of America's golden age of radio at its finest, where every word crackles with authenticity and every case reveals something darker lurking beneath polished surfaces. Tune in and discover why Johnny Dollar remains unforgettable.
