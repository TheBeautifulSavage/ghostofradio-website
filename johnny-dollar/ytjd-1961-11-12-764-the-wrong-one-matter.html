When a dame walks into an insurance office claiming she's the victim of a switched identity, seasoned investigator Johnny Dollar knows the case smells fishier than the docks at low tide. In The Wrong One Matter, our two-fisted hero finds himself tangled in a web of mistaken identities, forged documents, and desperate people willing to kill to stay hidden. As Dollar methodically unravels the threads—conducting interviews in shadowy hotel rooms, shadier nightclubs, and the sterile offices of suspicious bureaucrats—the tension mounts with each revelation. By the episode's climax, listeners will find themselves questioning which clues matter and which are red herrings, all while the distinctive snap of the Johnny Dollar theme cuts through the darkness like a neon sign on a rainy night.

Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar commanded CBS airwaves for thirteen years as one of radio's most innovative detective programs, and by 1961—its final season—the show had perfected the art of the self-contained mystery. Unlike episodic serials where narratives dragged across weeks, each Johnny Dollar case began and ended in a single thirty-minute broadcast, giving writers freedom to experiment with structure and plot complexity. The show's emphasis on procedure and insurance investigation offered something refreshingly grounded compared to the superheroics of other crime dramas, while Bob Bailey's world-weary narration and deadpan delivery became the gold standard for hard-boiled radio prose.

If you appreciate mysteries that reward careful listening, dialogue sharp as broken glass, and production values that transport you directly to a 1961 that never quite was, The Wrong One Matter demands your attention. Tune in and discover why Johnny Dollar remained America's favorite insurance investigator right up until the final curtain call.
