# The Burma Red Matter

When insurance investigator Johnny Dollar arrives in a shadowy corner of the Far East, he finds himself ensnared in a web of smuggling, political intrigue, and murder most foul. A shipment of rare Burmese rubies—the notorious "red matter" that gives this case its sinister name—has vanished without a trace, and with it, a fortune in claims and a dangerous secret that someone is willing to kill to protect. As Johnny pieces together the fractured testimonies of dock workers, corrupt officials, and a mysterious woman with ties to the black market, the humid Rangoon nights grow colder with each new threat. His trademark dry wit provides little comfort when a bullet whistles past his ear or when a trusted source turns out to be something far more complicated. This is vintage Johnny Dollar: clever, cynical, and caught between the law and those who profit from breaking it.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* was a phenomenon that defined the golden age of radio drama, and by 1962, the show had already become a legend. With over 700 episodes under its belt, the series pioneered the episodic mystery format that would later influence television detective shows for decades. Bob Bailey's world-weary narration and the show's intricate plotting proved that radio audiences hungered for intelligent, adult entertainment—nothing too simple, nothing too pure. Each case examined the moral gray zones of post-war America, where insurance fraud, corporate greed, and human desperation collided with Johnny's lonely pursuit of truth.

For those seeking the authentic sound of classic radio investigation—the crackle of distant voices, the tension of a slowly tightening mystery, and a protagonist who's seen too much to be shocked by anything—*The Burma Red Matter* offers everything that made this series essential listening. Tune in, settle into the dark, and let Johnny Dollar show you why radio's greatest detective never needed a badge.
