# The Two Steps To Murder Matter

When insurance investigator Johnny Dollar steps off the train in a quiet railroad town, he expects a routine case—a simple claim to verify, a few questions to ask, and a check to write. But the moment he sets foot on the platform, something feels wrong. The air is thick with secrets, and within hours, what should have been a straightforward investigation spirals into a maze of deception where every witness lies and every clue points in a different direction. As Johnny digs deeper into the dusty corners of this sleepy community, he discovers that someone is willing to kill to keep the truth buried—and that the path to murder is paved with just two seemingly innocent steps. This May 20th, 1962 episode crackles with the tension that made the series legendary: danger lurking beneath small-town normalcy, a protagonist relying on his wits and experience rather than his fists, and a mystery that unfolds with the methodical precision of a master craftsman.

*Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* occupied a unique space in the golden age of radio drama. As CBS's answer to the hard-boiled detective story, it transformed the tired insurance claim into legitimate noir territory, proving that mystery and menace could exist anywhere—not just in the dark alleys of the big city. By 1962, in its final season, the show had perfected its formula: sharp dialogue, atmospheric sound design, and plots that rewarded careful listening.

Don't miss this final-season gem that demonstrates why *Johnny Dollar* remained a must-listen for over a decade. Tune in and discover why solving the "Two Steps To Murder" requires all of Johnny's cunning and experience.
