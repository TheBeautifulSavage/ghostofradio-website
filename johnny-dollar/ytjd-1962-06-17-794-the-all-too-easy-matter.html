# The All Too Easy Matter

When insurance investigator Johnny Dollar arrives in town, he knows better than to trust anything that falls into his lap without a fight. But in this June 1962 episode, a seemingly straightforward claim begins to unravel into something far more sinister than anyone anticipated. The case appears simple enough on the surface—too simple, in fact—and Johnny's seasoned ear catches the discordant notes in every alibi, every convenient coincidence. As the investigation deepens, listeners will find themselves drawn into a web of deception where the most obvious answer is precisely the wrong one. This is classic noir territory: dim-lit offices, the clink of ice in a glass, and the creeping realization that someone is playing a very dangerous game. With each interview and twist, the episode builds tension with the precision that made Johnny Dollar radio's most compelling private investigator.

*Yours Truly Johnny Dollar* stood apart from its contemporaries through meticulous attention to authenticity and character development. Rather than relying on superhuman feats or wild melodrama, the show grounded itself in the mundane reality of claims adjustment and investigation, allowing genuine human psychology to drive the narrative. By 1962, after thirteen years on CBS, the show had perfected its formula while continually refreshing its storytelling. This particular episode exemplifies why the program earned legions of devoted listeners: it respects the audience's intelligence and rewards careful attention with intricately plotted mysteries that satisfy both emotionally and intellectually.

Don't let this case slip through your fingers. Tune in to experience Johnny Dollar at the height of his powers, unraveling an "easy" matter that proves anything but.
