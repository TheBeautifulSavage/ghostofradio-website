# The Hood of Death Matter

On a sweltering June evening in 1962, settle into your parlor as insurance investigator Johnny Dollar descends into the shadowy underworld of organized crime in "The Hood of Death Matter." When a routine claim becomes entangled with the city's most dangerous power players, Dollar finds himself navigating a labyrinth of double-crosses, veiled threats, and midnight rendezvous where a single misstep could be his last. The fog-thick atmosphere crackles with tension as our protagonist—armed with nothing but his quick wit, sharper instincts, and a .38 Special—peels back layers of deception to uncover a truth that powerful men would kill to keep buried. You'll hear the squeal of tires on rain-slicked streets, the clink of glasses in smoky back rooms, and the unmistakable menace in every carefully chosen word of dialogue. This is noir at its finest, broadcast live from the golden age of radio.

By 1962, *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* had become an institution—a show that transcended the typical detective drama through its meticulous writing, taut pacing, and the incomparable performance of Bob Bailey as Dollar, a man for whom integrity was both currency and code. The show's "matter" format, where each episode was a self-contained investigation, allowed writers to craft intricate mysteries that kept audiences guessing until the final revelations. In its final year on CBS, the show remained uncompromisingly sophisticated, refusing to pander to the television age that was claiming radio's audience.

Don't miss this gripping tale of corruption, violence, and redemption. Tune in tonight as Johnny Dollar pursues justice through the hood of death—where the stakes have never been higher and the night has never been darker.
