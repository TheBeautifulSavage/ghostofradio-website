# Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar: The Skimpy Matter

Step into the shadowy world of insurance investigation as Johnny Dollar arrives in a dusty backwater town where nothing—and nobody—is quite what they seem. In this taut 1962 episode, our hard-boiled protagonist finds himself chasing a case involving a suspicious diner waitress, a mysterious businessman with too much cash, and an insurance claim that smells fishier than three-day-old mackerel. With only his quick wit, sharper instincts, and expense account to guide him, Johnny must untangle a web of small-town secrets before someone decides he knows too much. The crackle of tension builds throughout this lean, mean mystery as Johnny's narration cuts through the darkness—his cigarette-rough voice and rapid-fire deductions the only things standing between him and a very permanent retirement.

What makes *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* a cornerstone of American radio drama is its brilliant formula: the insurance company's finest investigator brings listeners into a world of real detective work, one reimbursable expense at a time. Running from 1949 to 1962, the show pioneered the "freelance insurance detective" archetype and influenced everything from television's *Mannix* to modern streaming mysteries. By 1962, the series had perfected its craft—economical scripts, breakneck pacing, and Dollar's wisecracking narration created an intimacy that made listeners feel like they were right beside him in the shadows. "The Skimpy Matter" represents the show at its mature peak, when every word counted and every dollar—both narrative and literal—drove the story forward.

Don your fedora and light up your radio set. *Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar* waits, and somewhere in the static, another case is beginning to unfold. Tune in and discover why millions huddled around their sets to follow the exploits of radio's greatest insurance detective.
