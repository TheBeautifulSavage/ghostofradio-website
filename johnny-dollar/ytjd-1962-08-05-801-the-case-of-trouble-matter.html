# The Case Of Trouble Matter

When Johnny Dollar's telephone rang on that sweltering August evening in 1962, it brought news of a case that would pull the world-weary insurance investigator into the murky depths of corporate sabotage and double-dealing. *Trouble Matter* crackles with all the noir atmosphere that made Johnny Dollar a fixture in American living rooms—the precise tick of his expense account being tallied, the shadowy characters with their alibis and secrets, and the distinctive snap of cigarette lighters punctuating dangerous conversations. As Johnny checks into another seedy hotel and begins his methodical rounds of questioning, listeners are drawn into a labyrinth where nothing is quite what it seems and every clue cuts closer to the truth. The sophisticated sound design of CBS Radio brings the era vividly to life: the hum of fluorescent lights, the distant wail of sirens, and those pregnant silences where danger lurks just beyond what the ear can perceive.

By 1962, *Yours Truly Johnny Dollar* had become something of a relic in an industry rapidly succumbing to television's golden age—yet it remained among radio's most intelligent and tightly crafted programs. Created by writer Blake Edwards, the show eschewed formula violence in favor of sharp dialogue, intricate plotting, and a protagonist whose greatest tool was his sharp mind. Johnny Dollar became an everyman detective for the atomic age, managing insurance claims that spiraled into genuine mysteries. This particular episode represents the show's final season, a moment when radio drama was becoming a lost art, making it a precious artifact of entertainment from a vanished era.

Step back into 1962 and experience the final chapters of a broadcasting legend. *The Case Of Trouble Matter* awaits—where danger speaks softly and the truth is always worth investigating.
