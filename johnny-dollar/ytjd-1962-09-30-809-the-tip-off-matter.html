# The Tip Off Matter

As September winds carried autumn's first chill across America in 1962, listeners tuning into CBS settled in for another case with insurance investigator Johnny Dollar—a man whose expense account was as legendary as his ability to sniff out deception. In "The Tip Off Matter," Dollar finds himself ensnared in a web of whispered warnings and shadowy informants, where a simple tip transforms into a labyrinth of misdirection and danger. The episode crackles with the authentic tension of a noir thriller: the scratch of a telephone receiver, the hollow echo of footsteps in a darkened warehouse, the measured drawl of Mandel Kramer's voice guiding listeners through Dollar's methodical unraveling of the case. You'll feel the weight of each clue, the uncertainty of each new lead, as Johnny navigates a world where trust is currency and information is weaponized.

By 1962, "Yours Truly, Johnny Dollar" had become a cornerstone of American radio drama, spawning 876 episodes that showcased the golden age's finest storytelling. The show's unique format—presenting Dollar's expense account as the episode's backbone—was revolutionary, grounding fantastic detective work in the mundane reality of business and money. In an era when television was beginning to steal radio's audience, this program proved that audio drama could be intellectually sophisticated and utterly absorbing, requiring listeners to imagine danger rather than watch it unfold.

"The Tip Off Matter" stands as a testament to the show's enduring formula: smart writing, expert sound design, and a protagonist whose curiosity and cunning made him essential listening for millions. Tune in to experience why Johnny Dollar remained America's most trusted investigator, one expense report—and one thrilling case—at a time.
