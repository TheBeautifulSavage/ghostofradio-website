Guest Lee Wiley, The Mystery Singer

0:00 --:--

Picture yourself huddled around a warm mahogany console radio on a Wednesday evening in 1934, the dial glowing softly as the Kraft Music Hall orchestra swells into their signature opening theme. Tonight promises something extraordinary—the mysterious Lee Wiley, whose sultry contralto voice and elegant phrasing have captivated audiences from speakeasies to Broadway stages, joins host Bing Crosby for an evening of sophisticated jazz and popular song. Listeners will experience the thrill of live performance, the crackle and spontaneity of a broadcast where anything might happen, as Wiley trades vocals with Crosby and the orchestra, her smoky mezzo blending perfectly with his crooning style. The chemistry between these two jazz-influenced singers creates an intimate atmosphere despite the thousands tuning in across the nation, their voices carrying the elegance and worldliness of an era just beginning to emerge from Prohibition's shadow.

The Kraft Music Hall represents the golden age of radio entertainment, when corporate sponsorship allowed networks to invest in top-tier talent and production values. This 1934 appearance captures Lee Wiley at a pivotal moment—a jazz-trained vocalist respected by fellow musicians yet often underrated by the mainstream, performing before millions who knew her primarily as a mysterious voice rather than a familiar name. The episode stands as a snapshot of early 1930s entertainment: live, unrehearsed, and absolutely vital.

Tune in to experience the artistry that defined an era, when radio brought America's greatest singers directly into the parlor, unfiltered and unforgettable. This is broadcast history at its finest.
