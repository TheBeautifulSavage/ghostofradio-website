First Song Praise The Lord And Pass The Ammunition, Guest Ginny Simms, Edgar Buchanan

As the big band era swings into full patriotic fervor, the Kraft Music Hall opens its studio doors for an evening that crackles with wartime urgency and infectious optimism. This 1942 broadcast brings listeners the stirring premiere of "Praise The Lord And Pass The Ammunition," the rousing Frank Loesser composition that has already captured America's fighting spirit, performed by the luminous Ginny Simms—whose crystalline voice had made her a star on radio's most prestigious stage. The episode pulses with that peculiar blend of entertainment and morale-building that defined the home front during World War II, as the orchestra swell builds beneath patriotic fervor and genuine emotion. Character actor Edgar Buchanan brings his folksy charm to sketch comedy that laughs at the absurdities of wartime rationing and civilian defense, offering working Americans a moment of levity in an anxious age. You'll hear the genuine warmth of a nation tuning in together, finding courage and community through the radio speaker.

The Kraft Music Hall stood as NBC's crown jewel of variety programming, a showcase for America's finest musical talent that had been entertaining listeners since 1933. By 1942, with the nation at war, the program had become something more—a cultural touchstone where popular music merged seamlessly with patriotic purpose. Ginny Simms's performance of this song would help make it one of the war's most enduring anthems, while the show's sophisticated production values represented radio's golden age at its absolute peak.

Step back into 1942 and experience the electricity of live radio entertainment at war. Tune in to hear a nation's heartbeat, captured in real time, as the Kraft Music Hall reminds us why radio once held America spellbound.
