First Song San Fernando Valley, Guest George Murphy, Jane Frazee

Step into the warm glow of Studio 8-H at NBC's Rockefeller Center on this memorable evening in 1944, when Kraft Music Hall opens its doors to a constellation of entertainment's brightest stars. Host Bing Crosby, America's crooning sweetheart, welcomes the effervescent Jane Frazee and the multi-talented George Murphy to celebrate a sparkling new number, "First Song San Fernando Valley." What unfolds is pure magic—the kind only possible when a live orchestra, three gifted performers, and a nationwide audience of millions converged through the miracle of broadcast radio. You'll hear the urbane wit of Crosby at his finest, the crystalline vocals of Frazee that made her a movie favorite, and Murphy's easy charm that would soon carry him from the silver screen into the halls of Congress. The musical arrangements shimmer with the golden-age polish that made this program an institution in American homes, while the audience's genuine laughter and applause crackle with authentic delight.

By 1944, Kraft Music Hall had become more than a variety show—it was a weekly touchstone for families seeking respite from wartime anxieties. The program perfectly captured the optimism and escapism of the era, blending sophisticated comedy with world-class entertainment. Kraft Cheese's sponsorship lent the proceedings a sense of stability and prosperity, even as the nation remained engaged overseas. This episode, with its celebratory spirit and regional pride in the San Fernando Valley's growing cultural significance, reflects a moment of American confidence and cultural vitality.

Join us as these legendary performers deliver an evening of unscripted charm and musical excellence. This is radio at its finest—intimate yet grand, spontaneous yet expertly crafted. Tune in and discover why Kraft Music Hall remained America's favorite appointment with entertainment.
