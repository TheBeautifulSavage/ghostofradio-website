First Song Bless 'em All, Guest Richard Haydn

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

Step into the warm glow of the Kraft Music Hall stage this evening, where the orchestra strikes up with the infectious strains of "Bless 'em All"—a song that has become an anthem of hope for servicemen and their families across America. As the horns swell and the audience settles in, host Bing Crosby's golden voice carries the familiar melody, a moment of unity in a nation at war. But tonight brings something special: the arrival of the brilliant British character actor Richard Haydn, whose impeccable timing and versatile vocal talents promise a program of unexpected delights. With Haydn's quick wit and theatrical flair complementing Crosby's easy charm, listeners can anticipate sparkling dialogue, surprising musical turns, and perhaps a few comedic sketches that capture the sophisticated humor of the moment.

For four seasons now, Kraft Music Hall has dominated Thursday evenings on NBC, becoming the beating heart of American popular entertainment. Sponsored by the makers of Kraft cheese, the show has maintained an enviable tradition of pairing the biggest names in music with comedy and drama, all wrapped in the genuine warmth that defined the best radio programs of the era. This particular 1944 episode sits at a crucial moment—as the Allies surge toward victory in Europe, Americans turn to radio for both escape and reassurance. The choice of "Bless 'em All," with its bittersweet tribute to those in uniform, reflects radio's sophisticated understanding of its audience's emotional landscape.

So settle into your favorite chair, tune your dial to NBC, and prepare for an evening of first-rate entertainment. The Kraft Music Hall awaits—where melody, laughter, and genuine star power converge under the warm, crackling embrace of the golden age of radio.
