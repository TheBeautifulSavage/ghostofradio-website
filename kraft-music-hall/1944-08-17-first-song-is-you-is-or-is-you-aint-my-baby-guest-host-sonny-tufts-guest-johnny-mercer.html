# Kraft Music Hall: "You Is Or Is You Aint My Baby"

Step into the warm glow of Studio 8-H on this November evening in 1944, where the unmistakable aroma of Kraft cheese and the electric crackle of live radio fill the air. Tonight, the incomparable Sonny Tufts takes the helm as guest host, bringing his easy charm and Hollywood sophistication to America's living rooms, while the legendary Johnny Mercer—one of the finest lyricists ever to grace the American songbook—joins the orchestra to premiere "Is You Or Is You Aint My Baby." The show opens with the iconic Kraft theme that has become synonymous with sophistication and entertainment, before swinging into a lively opening number that sets the perfect tone for an evening of surprises, comedy sketches, and musical delights. Listen as the audience erupts in applause, transported by Mercer's wit and the orchestra's polished arrangements—this is the golden age of American entertainment at its finest.

The Kraft Music Hall represented the pinnacle of network radio's variety format, a weekly ritual for millions of Americans seeking escape and elegance during the turbulent war years. In 1944, with servicemen overseas and the home front focused on the war effort, these broadcasts offered something precious: moments of glamour, humor, and the reassurance that American culture was thriving. Sonny Tufts, then at the height of his film career, brings the allure of Hollywood directly into homes across the nation, while Johnny Mercer's participation underscores the show's commitment to featuring only the finest talent in entertainment.

Don't miss this remarkable glimpse into the golden era of radio—a time when sponsors, stars, and songwriters collaborated to create an unforgettable evening of music and mirth. Tune in and discover why millions tuned in each week to Kraft Music Hall.
