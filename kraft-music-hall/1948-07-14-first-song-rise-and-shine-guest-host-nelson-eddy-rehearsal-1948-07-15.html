First Song Rise And Shine, Guest Host Nelson Eddy (rehearsal, 1948 07 15)

Step into Studio 8-H at NBC's Rockefeller Center on a sweltering July afternoon as Nelson Eddy takes the helm of America's most beloved musical variety program. In this rarely-heard rehearsal broadcast, the legendary baritone and Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer star guides the Kraft Music Hall orchestra and guest performers through "Rise and Shine," a spirited number that crackles with the easy sophistication and spontaneous charm that made this program a Thursday night institution for fifteen years. You'll hear the genuine camaraderie between performers, the crisp orchestral arrangements that defined an era, and the technical precision required to broadcast live across the entire NBC network—all captured in an intimate moment before the evening's official broadcast. This is radio as it was truly made: unscripted conversations, musical numbers rehearsed just hours before airtime, and the palpable electricity of live entertainment.

The Kraft Music Hall had become more than a show by 1948—it was a national ritual, a gilded thread connecting millions of American families to the glamour of Broadway and Hollywood each week. Under the sponsorship of Kraft cheese, the program had hosted everyone from Bing Crosby to Ethel Merman, showcasing the cream of American popular music. Nelson Eddy's guest hosting appearance represents the show's enduring prestige: only the most distinguished entertainers could command the podium. This rehearsal recording survives as a precious artifact of an art form now vanished—the live musical variety broadcast.

Tune in to experience the golden age of radio entertainment in its purest form, where talented musicians and singers gathered under the lights for an evening of music that would reach into living rooms across the nation. This is the Kraft Music Hall at the height of its powers.
