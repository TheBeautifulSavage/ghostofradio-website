First Song Drums In My Heart, Guest Host Nelson Eddy

Step into the warm glow of studio lights on this enchanting evening as the mellifluous baritone of Nelson Eddy takes the helm of America's most beloved musical showcase. Fresh from his triumphs on the silver screen, Eddy brings his matinee-idol charm and commanding presence to guide listeners through an evening of pure vocal enchantment. The orchestra swells with anticipation as he introduces "Drums In My Heart," a number that allows his voice to soar with the kind of romantic intensity that made him a household name. You'll hear the intimate banter between host and guests, the rustle of sheet music, the clink of coffee cups in the control room—all the living, breathing magic that made radio the heartbeat of American entertainment. This is variety at its finest, where a single evening could transport you from symphony halls to comedy sketches, all from the comfort of your parlor.

By 1948, Kraft Music Hall had already established itself as the gold standard of musical radio, a program that launched careers and defined the sound of an era. The show's rotating guest hosts—luminaries from opera, Broadway, and Hollywood—ensured that each episode felt like an exclusive invitation to an elegant soirée. Nelson Eddy's guest appearance represents the convergence of old-world operatic tradition with the modern entertainment machinery of network radio, a moment when classical training and popular appeal could coexist beautifully on the airwaves.

Tune in now and experience the sophistication, the artistry, and the sheer joy that made Kraft Music Hall an institution. This is radio as it was meant to be heard—live, unscripted, and utterly captivating.
