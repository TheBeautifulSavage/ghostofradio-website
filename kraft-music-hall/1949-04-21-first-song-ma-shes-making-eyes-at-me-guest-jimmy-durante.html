# Kraft Music Hall: "First Song Ma! She's Making Eyes At Me" (1949)

Step into the gleaming studios of NBC as the velvet curtain rises on an evening of pure entertainment magic. It's 1949, and the legendary Jimmy Durante—"The Schnozzola" himself—takes center stage at Kraft Music Hall, armed with his trademark rasping voice, infectious charm, and comedic timing that had made him a vaudeville sensation. As the orchestra swells with the unmistakable arrangement of "Ma! She's Making Eyes at Me," you can practically hear the audience leaning forward in their seats, ready for Durante's impish take on this romantic chestnut. What unfolds is a masterclass in live variety entertainment: Durante's playful banter with the host, his mugging delivery of the standard, and the kind of spontaneous comedic moments that made radio's golden age truly golden. Whether he's cracking jokes about his famous schnoz or pivoting to a surprisingly tender rendition, Durante reminds listeners why he became one of America's most beloved entertainers.

By 1949, Kraft Music Hall had already established itself as the gold standard of sponsored radio variety shows, a program where listeners could count on discovering both established stars and fresh talent every Thursday night. The show's 16-year run represented radio's most glamorous era, when a cup of Kraft cheese and an evening of live music and comedy felt like an evening at the finest nightclub. Each episode was a carefully orchestrated production featuring the show's signature orchestra and rotating guest stars, but it was the authentic energy of live performance—the genuine laughter, the occasional flubbed line, the palpable connection between performer and audience—that made these broadcasts unmissable.

Don't miss this rare glimpse into an era when radio was America's living room entertainment. Tune in and experience the warmth, humor, and musical excellence that made Kraft Music Hall legendary.
