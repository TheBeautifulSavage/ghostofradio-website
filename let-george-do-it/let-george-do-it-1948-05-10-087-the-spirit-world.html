Let George Do It 1948 05 10 (087) The Spirit World

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

When the fog rolls thick through the streets of a spiritualist's parlor, George Valentine finds himself caught between the realm of the living and something far more sinister. A wealthy widow's séance takes a deadly turn when the medium's exposed fraud becomes the least of their problems—someone in that darkened room has committed murder, and the spirits themselves seem to be bearing witness. As George peels back layers of deception and greed, listeners will be drawn into an atmosphere thick with menace: the creak of floorboards, the whispered incantations of a charlatan medium, and the unmistakable sound of a revolver's shot cutting through the supernatural haze. This episode masterfully blends the skeptical detective's pragmatism with genuinely unsettling moments that blur the line between rational investigation and the inexplicable.

Let George Do It remains one of radio's most underrated detective series, and this 1948 episode exemplifies why. Rather than relying on the hard-boiled clichés that dominated noir radio, the show's writers crafted smart, character-driven mysteries where George's everyman charm and quick thinking could shine. The post-war audience was hungry for stories that didn't take themselves too seriously yet still delivered genuine suspense—and this supernatural-tinged mystery captures that perfect balance. The Mutual network's superior audio engineering also means listeners get crisp, immersive sound design that makes every shadow and whisper feel disturbingly real.

Don your headphones and prepare for a case where the dead may know more than the living. The Spirit World promises everything that made classic radio detective drama so captivating: a clever mystery, an engaging lead character, and the kind of atmospheric storytelling that only radio could deliver. Tune in and discover why audiences made George Valentine their trusted companion through the darkest hours of the evening.
