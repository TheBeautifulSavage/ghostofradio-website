# Let George Do It – Mayhem By Experts (January 31, 1949)

Step into the shadowy world of George Valentine as he stumbles into a case where the criminals have become *too* professional. When a series of expertly executed crimes plague the city—each one executed with surgical precision—George finds himself outmatched by masterminds who've turned their trade into an exact science. Someone is orchestrating mayhem like a conductor leading a symphony, and every clue leads deeper into a web of calculated violence. With danger closing in from every direction and time running out, our reluctant detective must use his wits and street smarts to stay alive. This is noir at its finest: the crackle of gunfire, the snap of dialogue, and the constant threat of double-cross hanging heavy in the cigarette smoke.

*Let George Do It* was Mutual Broadcasting's answer to the golden age of detective radio, and by 1949, the show had honed its craft to perfection. Starring Bob Bailey as the quick-thinking George Valentine—a man who takes on the jobs nobody else will—the series captured the gritty, cynical spirit of post-war America. Unlike the glamorous sleuths of the East Coast airwaves, George was street-level and scrappy, solving cases through intuition and improvisation rather than deduction. The show's rapid-fire dialogue and atmospheric sound design made listeners feel they were walking those rain-slicked alleys alongside George, and "Mayhem By Experts" showcases why this series became one of radio's most enduring detective programs.

Tune in to experience the golden age of detective radio when mystery didn't announce itself—it crept up on you in the dark. George Valentine is waiting.
