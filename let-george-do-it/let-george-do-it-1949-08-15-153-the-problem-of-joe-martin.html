# Let George Do It: "The Problem of Joe Martin" (August 15, 1949)

George Valentine steps into the humid August night with a cigarette and a problem that won't stay solved. When a desperate woman arrives at his office with a simple request—find Joe Martin—George knows nothing is ever simple in this business. What unfolds is a taut, twisting affair of mistaken identities and dangerous secrets, where every clue pulls our detective deeper into a web of lies. The creeping violin score and the sharp crack of revolver shots echo through fifteen minutes of pure noir tension, as George discovers that some problems don't have clean solutions, only survivors and the rest.

*Let George Do It* arrived on the Mutual network in 1946 as the golden age of radio was burning brightest, proving that detective shows still had new tricks to teach eager audiences huddled around their sets. Bob Bailey, the show's star, became the definitive voice of the hard-boiled private eye for millions of listeners—his measured drawl and quick thinking made George Valentine as real as the next-door neighbor, yet thrillingly dangerous. By 1949, the show had found its perfect rhythm: snappy dialogue, genuine mystery, and the kind of moral ambiguity that kept listeners guessing. Episode 153, "The Problem of Joe Martin," exemplifies why *Let George Do It* remained among radio's most respected detective programs, rivaling *The Shadow* and *Sam Spade* in the hearts of noir enthusiasts.

Don't miss this masterpiece of suspense. Tune in as George tackles a case where finding the missing man might be the easiest part—keeping him found is something else entirely.
