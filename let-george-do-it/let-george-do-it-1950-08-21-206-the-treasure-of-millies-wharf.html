# Let George Do It: "The Treasure of Millie's Wharf"

When George Valentine arrives at the fog-shrouded docks on a sweltering August night, he's expecting a simple errand—but the waterfront has a way of complicating even the straightforward jobs. A desperate woman named Millie claims her late husband hid a fortune somewhere along the weathered planks of his namesake wharf, and she's willing to pay handsomely for Valentine's discretion and detective skills. What begins as a treasure hunt quickly becomes something far more sinister when George discovers that others—some desperate, some dangerous—are searching for the same prize. As the night deepens and danger closes in from the shadows, our resourceful hero must navigate rival treasure seekers, cryptic clues, and the very real possibility that someone would rather see him sleeping with the fishes than let him walk away with answers. The tension crackles like the static of a ship's radio as George navigates the treacherous world where greed and desperation collide under the cover of darkness.

*Let George Do It* exemplified the golden age of detective radio drama during the post-war boom of 1946-1954, when listeners craved fast-paced mystery and the comforting confidence of a hero who could handle any situation. Bob Bailey's portrayal of George Valentine struck the perfect balance between tough-talking cynicism and genuine compassion—he was a man of action who could also think his way out of trouble. "The Treasure of Millie's Wharf" showcases exactly why the series thrived during this period: snappy dialogue, atmospheric sound design from the Mutual network's talented engineers, and a mystery that keeps you guessing until the very final moment.

Don't miss this classic slice of noir mystery. Tune in now and discover why *Let George Do It* remains one of radio's most thrilling detective adventures.
