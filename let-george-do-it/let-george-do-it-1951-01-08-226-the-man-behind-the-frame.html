# Let George Do It - "The Man Behind The Frame"

When George Valentine answers the phone on a cold January evening in 1951, he has no idea that a simple frame job will unravel into a web of blackmail, desperation, and murder most foul. A desperate client arrives at his office claiming innocence—caught red-handed with stolen diamonds he swears he never took. As snow falls on the Chicago streets outside, George must navigate a treacherous landscape of crooked cops, femmes fatales, and shadowy figures who profit from ruined reputations. The atmosphere crackles with tension as our hero peels back layer after layer of deception, his sharp wit and sharper instincts the only tools between justice and a frame that could send an innocent man to the electric chair. This is classic noir radio at its finest—where every cigarette-lit shadow hides a secret, and trust is the most dangerous commodity of all.

*Let George Do It* defined the detective noir genre during radio's golden age, with Bob Bailey's rapid-fire delivery and impeccable timing making George Valentine an unforgettable protagonist. Airing on the Mutual network from 1946 to 1954, the show captured the post-war American spirit of cynicism and moral ambiguity, blending hard-boiled mystery with genuine human stakes. By 1951, the show had refined its formula to perfection, balancing sharp writing with Bailey's chemistry with his supporting cast. This episode exemplifies why critics and listeners alike considered *Let George Do It* essential listening—smart, tightly plotted, and endlessly entertaining.

Don't miss "The Man Behind The Frame"—a masterclass in radio mystery that'll keep you guessing until the final revelation. Tune in and discover why audiences huddled around their receivers every week to hear what case George Valentine would tackle next.
