# Let George Do It: The Bad Little God

Step into the neon-soaked streets of a corrupt American city where a desperate woman arrives at private investigator George Valentine's office clutching a cryptic photograph and a story that doesn't quite add up. When a small-time crook known only as "the Bad Little God" begins eliminating his rivals with methodical precision, George finds himself entangled in a deadly game where every shadow conceals a suspect and every alibi crumbles under scrutiny. Bob Bailey's weary, world-weary narration pulls you deeper into this January 1952 installment, where the line between justice and vengeance blurs as dangerously as the cigarette smoke hanging in George's cramped office. The orchestra swells with menacing strings as the body count rises, and you'll find yourself racing to solve the mystery before George's next appointment with fate.

*Let George Do It* arrived at radio's golden hour when the medium had perfected the art of intimate storytelling, and this episode exemplifies why the show became a Mutual staple for eight remarkable years. Bailey's naturalistic delivery revolutionized the detective genre, stripping away the theatrical bombast of earlier shows for something rawer and more authentically American. By 1952, radio audiences craved this grittier realism—the Cold War anxieties, urban corruption, and moral ambiguity that defined the postwar era. Each episode of *Let George Do It* captured that transitional moment when noir aesthetics migrated from cinema into America's living rooms, creating an intimate yet cinematic experience.

Don't miss "The Bad Little God." Tune in to experience one of radio's finest detective dramas at the peak of its powers, when George Valentine became the conscience of a morally compromised world.
