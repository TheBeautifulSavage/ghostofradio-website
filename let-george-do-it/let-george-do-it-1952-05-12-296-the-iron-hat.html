# Let George Do It: The Iron Hat (May 12, 1952)

George Valentine returns to the shadowy streets of the city in this week's mystery, where a stolen iron hat becomes the unlikely catalyst for murder, blackmail, and a web of secrets that reaches into the highest corners of society. Our private investigator stumbles into a case far more dangerous than it first appears—what begins as a simple retrieval job quickly transforms into something darker, where everyone from corrupt cops to society ladies with hidden pasts seems willing to kill to keep the iron hat's true significance under wraps. Listen as George navigates the fog-shrouded alleys and smoke-filled offices of 1952 America, where danger lurks around every corner and trust is the rarest commodity. Bob Bailey's world-weary narration guides you through each twist and turn, his voice dripping with the knowing cynicism of a man who's seen every con in the book—and invented a few himself.

*Let George Do It* stood as the Mutual network's answer to the golden age of radio detective fiction, a show that proved the hard-boiled private eye format could still captivate audiences well into the 1950s. Bob Bailey's portrayal of George Valentine became legendary for its authenticity—a detective who got beaten up, got paid poorly, and solved crimes through a combination of luck, street smarts, and genuine compassion for the forgotten people caught in dangerous situations. By 1952, five years into its run, the show had perfected its formula, delivering stories that balanced clever plotting with genuine atmosphere and human drama.

Don't miss your appointment with George Valentine as he unravels the mystery of The Iron Hat. Tune in now and discover why *Let George Do It* remains one of radio's most enduring detective classics—where every case reminds us that in this city, nothing is ever quite what it seems.
