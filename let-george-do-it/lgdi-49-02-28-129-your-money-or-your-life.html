# Your Money Or Your Life

The fog rolls thick through the streets of the city tonight, and George Valentine is walking straight into a trap laid by the smoothest con artist to ever work the racket. When a desperate widow arrives at his office with a tale of blackmail and a leather envelope stuffed with hush money, George knows he's stumbled onto something far deadlier than petty extortion. The clock is ticking. Somewhere in the shadows, a killer is collecting debts—and tonight, someone will pay with more than just cash. As the tension mounts and the walls close in, you'll hear every step George takes toward the truth, every nervous breath, every gunshot that might be his last. This is the kind of case that separates the clever operators from the corpses, and George Valentine has never been one to back down from a fight.

*Let George Do It* was the quintessential American detective series for the post-war radio audience—a show that captured the cynical, hard-boiled spirit of 1940s noir while remaining accessible to millions of listeners huddled around their sets each week. Bob Bailey's portrayal of the freelance troubleshooter became iconic, a voice as recognizable as the scratch of a record needle or the wail of a police siren. The Mutual network gave the show five seasons to build its loyal following, crafting self-contained mysteries that could be solved in exactly twenty-eight minutes, leaving listeners breathless and eager for next week's case.

If you've never experienced the electric thrill of classic radio drama—where every sound effect, every pause, every inflection of the human voice carries the weight of an entire scene—*Your Money Or Your Life* is the perfect entry point. Tune in, turn off the lights, and let George do what he does best.
