# Laura's House

Step into the shadowy corridors of Laura's mansion as George Valentine takes on a case that promises danger at every turn. When a desperate woman arrives at his office with trembling hands and a story that doesn't quite add up, George knows he's being pulled into something far more sinister than a simple missing persons case. *Laura's House* pulls you into the kind of mystery where the walls themselves seem to whisper secrets, where every shadow could hide a suspect, and where trust is the rarest commodity of all. Bob Bailey's world-weary narration guides you through dimly lit rooms and tense confrontations, building toward a revelation that will leave you questioning everyone's motives. The sound design crackles with period authenticity—the distant rumble of a 1940s city, the shuffle of papers, the unmistakable click of a revolver's safety. This is detective noir at its finest, where morality is as murky as the fog-bound streets outside.

By the summer of 1949, *Let George Do It* had become the gold standard of detective radio drama, outlasting competitors and captivating audiences with its sophisticated plotting and Bailey's incomparable performance. The show's clever writing never talked down to listeners, treating them as intelligent partners in unraveling complex mysteries set against the gritty underbelly of American urban life. Each episode rewarded close attention, layering clues and misdirection with the precision of a master craftsman.

Don't miss this chance to experience radio drama the way millions did—with nothing but your imagination and the actors' voices to transport you to another era. Tune in to *Let George Do It* and discover why this show remained a listener favorite for nearly a decade.
