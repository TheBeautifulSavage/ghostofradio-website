# Tag! You're It!

When George Valentine answers a late-night telephone call from a desperate woman, he finds himself drawn into a deadly game of cat-and-mouse through the shadowed streets of the city. A simple tag game from childhood has been twisted into something sinister—each player marked for death, each elimination bringing George closer to becoming the hunter's next prey. As he races against time to uncover the identity of this macabre game master, the line between predator and prey blurs in classic noir fashion. Expect the sharp crack of a revolver echoing through fog-shrouded alleys, cryptic clues whispered in smoky nightclubs, and the relentless tick of a clock counting down to George's final reckoning. This is detective work stripped to its most primal—survival itself becoming the ultimate mystery.

*Let George Do It* stands as one of radio's most intoxicating adventures, showcasing the golden age when noir wasn't just a visual style but a symphony of sound. Herbert Marshall's world-weary narration—that perfect blend of cynicism and determination—became the voice of the postwar private detective, influencing countless adaptations across media. The show's genius lay in its ability to transform the intimate medium of radio into something claustrophobic and dangerous; listeners found themselves alone in the dark with only their imaginations to conjure the menace lurking in every shadow. "Tag! You're It!" exemplifies the program's mastery of sustained tension and plot complexity, delivering exactly what fans craved: mystery, action, and the reassuring presence of an unflappable hero.

Tune in to hear how George turns the hunter's own game against him. *Let George Do It* continues to prove why it captivated millions of listeners during radio's greatest era.
