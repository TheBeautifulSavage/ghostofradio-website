# The Gray Stone Ghost

Private investigator George Valentine ventures into the shadowy underworld of 1940s corruption when a desperate widow appears at his office with a mystery that defies explanation. Someone—or something—is haunting the abandoned Gray Stone mansion on the city's north side, and three men connected to its dark past have already vanished without a trace. As George descends into the fog-shrouded grounds, he'll discover that the most terrifying ghosts are often those with flesh and blood, and that old debts paid in blood have a way of collecting interest. With only his wits, his .38, and the help of his faithful operator Brooksie back at the office, George must uncover whether he's hunting a phantom or a very human killer intent on settling scores—before he becomes the ghost's next victim.

*Let George Do It* became a cornerstone of detective radio drama during its eight-year run on the Mutual Broadcasting System, offering listeners a grittier, more psychologically complex hero than many of his contemporaries. Unlike the smarter-than-you detectives of other shows, George Valentine felt like a real man navigating real dangers—clever but vulnerable, professional but human. This episode, from 1952, represents the show at its peak, balancing the supernatural dread that captivated post-war audiences with the hard-boiled investigative work that made the series essential listening for anyone craving authentic noir atmosphere delivered through the intimate medium of radio.

Tune in now and step into the fog-laden streets of George Valentine's world, where danger lurks in shadows both literal and psychological. *The Gray Stone Ghost* awaits—and trust us, you won't want to face this mystery alone.
