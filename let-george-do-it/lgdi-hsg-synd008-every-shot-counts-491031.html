# Every Shot Counts

The neon-soaked streets of the city pulse with danger as George Valentine takes on a case that cuts closer to home than he'd like. When a sharpshooter's bullets start finding their marks with lethal precision, George finds himself caught between a desperate client, a ruthless killer, and a web of lies that threatens to unravel everything he thought he knew. With each passing moment, the body count rises and the clock ticks down—every shot counts, and George knows that one mistake could be his last. Bob Bailey's world-weary voice guides you through cigarette smoke and shadow, where trust is a luxury and survival demands quick thinking and quicker action. The tension mounts as clues pile up, motives become muddied, and George realizes he may be chasing a phantom more dangerous than any criminal he's faced before.

*Let George Do It* stands as a masterpiece of American radio detective fiction, capturing the golden age of the medium when talented actors could paint entire worlds through voice alone. Airing on the Mutual Broadcasting System, this series gave audiences the urbane, intelligent detective George Valentine—a protagonist far removed from the hard-boiled clichés of his contemporaries. Bob Bailey's nuanced performance brought sophistication and genuine warmth to the role, making George a character listeners genuinely cared about. Each episode was a finely crafted mystery, blending tight scripting with atmospheric sound design that transported millions of listeners directly into George's dangerous world.

If you've never experienced the thrill of classic radio detection, *Every Shot Counts* offers the perfect entry point—a taut, exciting mystery that showcases everything that made this show beloved for over a decade. Tune in and discover why audiences gathered around their radio sets each week, desperate to learn what trouble George Valentine would stumble into next.
