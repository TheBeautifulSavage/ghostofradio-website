# The Lone Ranger: Missouri Mike's Horse Race

Thunder hooves and the cry of a desperate man echo across the dusty plains as the masked avenger rides headlong into a world of corruption and greed. In "Missouri Mike's Horse Race," listeners will find themselves at a remote frontier track where honest stakes have turned sinister, and a humble rancher's prized stallion has become the prize in a scheme that could mean his ruin. With only his trusted companion Tonto at his side, the Lone Ranger must navigate a web of crooked gamblers, doctored races, and dangerous secrets to uncover the truth—all while keeping his true identity hidden and justice within reach. The tension builds with every measured word from announcer George Stenius, as the familiar William Tell Overture gives way to the unmistakable crack of a starter's pistol and the roar of spectators who have no idea that their entertainment masks a crime waiting to be exposed.

By the 1940s, *The Lone Ranger* had become America's most cherished radio drama, captivating over forty million listeners with tales that proved virtue could triumph even in lawless territories. This episode exemplifies the show's genius—blending the romance of the Old West with contemporary anxieties about trust, deception, and the power of individual conscience. The program's creators understood that radio's intimate medium could make each listener feel personally chosen by the masked stranger, recruited into his quest for righteousness. Every galloping horse and gunshot reverberated in living rooms across the nation, transforming ordinary evenings into adventures.

Don't miss this thrilling installment from radio's golden age. Adjust your set, dim the lights, and prepare for an evening when a mysterious figure in a black mask proves that one honest man with unwavering principles can still change everything. The Lone Ranger rides again!
