The masked rider returns to the saddle in this thrilling chapter of Western justice, where a simple promise to a dying man sets the Lone Ranger on a collision course with greed and corruption. When a trusted ranch hand presses his final wish upon our hero—to care for Toby, the faithful horse that has served him faithfully through decades of hardship—the Lone Ranger accepts without hesitation. But Toby is no ordinary mount, and word of the animal's remarkable bloodline spreads like wildfire across the frontier. Ruthless cattlemen and unscrupulous speculators descend upon the territory, determined to claim the horse by any means necessary. As tension mounts and danger closes in, listeners will find themselves gripped by the Lone Ranger's desperate race against time, testing whether loyalty to a dying man's wish can survive the onslaught of frontier avarice.

Throughout its remarkable twenty-one-year run on ABC, *The Lone Ranger* captured the American imagination by embodying the ideals of justice, honor, and selflessness in an age when radio was the nation's primary window into narrative adventure. This 1940s episode exemplifies what made the program essential listening: the seamless blend of pulse-pounding action with deeply human moments of compassion and moral conviction. Paul Rhyson's distinctive narration and the iconic "William Tell Overture" theme became the soundtrack to millions of dinnertime gatherings, while the Lone Ranger's unwavering code of ethics offered a steady moral compass during turbulent times.

Don your virtual ten-gallon hat and saddle up for an unforgettable journey into the Old West. *The Lone Ranger* awaits—where justice never wears out and one man's word is worth more than gold.
