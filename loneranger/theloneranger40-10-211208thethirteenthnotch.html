# The Thirteenth Notch

As the familiar strains of Rossini's William Tell Overture crackle through the speaker, you're transported to the dusty frontier where justice rides on horseback. In "The Thirteenth Notch," our masked avenger faces a cold-blooded killer whose gun handle bears twelve deadly marks—and the Lone Ranger himself stands to become the thirteenth. The tension crackles like summer lightning as the Ranger and faithful Tonto race against time to stop a calculating gunslinger whose obsession with adding one final notch to his tally threatens to turn legend into tragedy. This is radio at its finest: the thunder of hoofbeats, the crisp snap of dialogue, and the mounting dread of a showdown that could silence the West's greatest crime-fighter forever.

By the 1940s, The Lone Ranger had become a cultural phenomenon spanning newspapers, magazines, and the silver screen—yet the radio program remained the beating heart of the Ranger's legend. ABC's serialized adventures reached millions of listeners every weekday, capturing the imaginations of both children and adults who thrilled to hear how the mysterious masked man and his Native American companion Tonto outwitted outlaws and corrupt officials. This episode exemplifies what made the show endure: not merely gunfights and chases, but morally complex tales where intelligence and principle triumph over brutality. The Ranger wasn't just a vigilante; he was a symbol of frontier justice tempered with wisdom.

This is your chance to experience radio drama as audiences experienced it—live, vital, and utterly compelling. Tune in to "The Thirteenth Notch" and discover why, nearly a century later, the Lone Ranger still gallops through our collective memory.
