When the noose tightens around an innocent man's neck and the crowd roars for justice, there seems no escape—yet the Lone Ranger knows that truth often wears a disguise as cunning as his own mask. In this gripping episode from the golden age of radio drama, our masked avenger must race against time itself to expose a conspiracy of lies that threatens to send a wrongly accused rancher to the gallows. As you settle into your chair this evening, prepare yourself for the sound of hoofbeats pounding through the desert night, the crack of a whip, and the unmistakable cry of "Hi-yo, Silver!"—all set against a backdrop of courtroom intrigue, hidden evidence, and a town on the brink of a terrible mistake. The tension mounts with every passing minute, every false accusation, every moment that brings the condemned man closer to his appointment with the executioner.

For over two decades, The Lone Ranger captivated millions of American listeners with its perfect blend of action, morality, and the romantic notion of frontier justice. Airing during an era when radio was the nation's primary source of entertainment, the show became a cultural phenomenon, spawning comic strips, movies, and an indelible place in American mythology. This particular episode exemplifies why the program endured: it's not merely a tale of good versus evil, but a sophisticated exploration of how the innocent can be betrayed by circumstance and how one man's unwavering commitment to truth can overcome the machinery of corruption.

Don't miss "Hanged But Not Dead"—tune in tonight and discover why audiences across the country have made the Lone Ranger's fight for justice their own. Some mysteries are meant to be heard, not told.
