# The Lone Ranger: "The Fifteenth Ring"

As the familiar strains of Rossini's "William Tell Overture" fade into the Texas night, listeners are drawn once more into the dusty realm of the masked avenger and his faithful Indian companion, Tonto. In this gripping episode from the early 1940s, a mysterious killer is leaving behind a calling card unlike any other—a series of ornate rings, each one marking another victim in a sinister vendetta. The Lone Ranger and Tonto must race against time to decipher the meaning of these deadly tokens before the fifteenth ring finds its final owner. With crackling sound effects of galloping hooves, gunshots echoing across the frontier, and the Lone Ranger's distinctive "Hi-yo, Silver!" cry piercing through the static of time, this episode captures everything that made the show a cornerstone of American radio entertainment.

*The Lone Ranger* premiered in 1933 and became one of the most beloved programs in radio history, captivating millions of listeners across three decades. The show's genius lay in its perfect alchemy of adventure, justice, and moral clarity—themes that resonated deeply during the Great Depression and World War II era, when Americans craved heroes of unwavering principle. The masked avenger represented an idealized frontier justice that transcended the mundane struggles of daily life, while the relationship between the Lone Ranger and Tonto subtly offered a vision of partnership across racial lines, revolutionary for its time.

Don't miss this electrifying tale of mystery and vengeance. Tune in now and experience *The Lone Ranger* as audiences did generations ago—when radio was king and a masked man on a white horse could transport you straight to the heart of adventure.
