EPISODE SUMMARY:

The Grand Duchess and The Waiter is a Lux Radio Theatre production that weaves a tangled web of deceit, love, and social class, set against the backdrop of pre-war Europe in the late 1930s. The story revolves around the Grand Duchess, a high-society figure with a dark secret: she's been embezzling funds from her family's charity to maintain a lavish lifestyle. When her personal assistant falls ill, she sees an opportunity to increase her own power and influence by exploiting the vulnerable young woman's position of trust. Enter our protagonist, The Waiter, who unwittingly becomes entangled in the Grand Duchess's web of manipulation, forcing him to confront his own moral compass.

As the episode unfolds, the Lux Radio Theatre masterfully crafts a sense of foreboding, evoking the anxieties of a world on the brink of war. The tension is palpable as our characters navigate the treacherous landscape of social hierarchy and personal desire. The actors deliver nuanced performances, bringing depth and complexity to their respective roles.
