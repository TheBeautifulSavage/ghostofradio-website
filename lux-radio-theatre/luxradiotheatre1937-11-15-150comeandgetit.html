# Come and Get It - November 15, 1937

Picture yourself settling into your favorite chair on a crisp November evening, the warm glow of your radio set casting flickering shadows across the parlor. As the Lux Radio Theatre theme swells through your speaker—that unmistakable orchestral fanfare that has become as familiar as a trusted friend—you're transported into the untamed wilderness of the North Woods during the logging boom. "Come and Get It" unfolds with the raw vitality of frontier life: a sprawling tale of ambition, desire, and the clash between civilization and the wild, featuring some of Hollywood's finest talent brought directly into your living room. You'll hear every axe swing, every whispered confession, every moment of passion crackle with the intimate immediacy only radio drama can achieve. The actors breathe life into characters caught between the demands of a growing nation and the personal costs of progress.

Lux Radio Theatre had perfected the art of bringing Hollywood to the American home, and by 1937, the show had become a national institution, broadcast live each Monday evening. These weren't mere condensed films—they were theatrical experiences, complete with a full orchestra, expert sound effects artisans, and the finest dramatic talent of the era performing with the precision of live theater and the magic of cinema. "Come and Get It" exemplifies the show's ambition to deliver sophisticated drama to millions simultaneously, a feat that feels almost unimaginable in our fragmented media age.

Don't miss this remarkable evening of entertainment, when radio was truly the golden medium. Tune in and experience the artistry and immediacy that captivated a nation during radio's finest hour.
