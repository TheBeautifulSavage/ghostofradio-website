The Episode

EPISODE SUMMARY:

"Enter Madame" is a gripping Lux Radio Theatre episode from January 10, 1938, that masterfully weaves together elements of psychological suspense and mysticism. The story follows Emily Wilson, a timid young woman struggling to cope with the loss of her fiancé in World War I. Her life takes an unexpected turn when she meets Madame Louise, a charismatic and enigmatic fortune teller who claims to possess supernatural powers. As Emily becomes increasingly enthralled by Madame Louise's promises of love and success, she begins to question the true nature of reality and her own identity.

The atmosphere in this episode is one of creeping unease, as Emily's grip on sanity falters in the face of Madame Louise's seductive manipulations. The Lux Radio Theatre's masterful use of sound design and music heightens the sense of tension, from the ominous opening strains of the violin to the unnerving whispers that seem to emanate from nowhere. The performance by actress Bessie Love as Emily Wilson is particularly noteworthy, conveying a fragile vulnerability that makes the audience empathize with her plight.

As Madame Louise's grip on Emily tightens, the lines between reality and fantasy blur, leaving the listener wondering what is real and what is just a product of Madame Louise's imagination. The episode's themes of psychological manipulation and the blurring of boundaries between truth and deception remain eerily relevant today, making "Enter Madame" a gripping listen that still holds audiences enthralled.

HISTORICAL CONTEXT:

The year 1938 was a tumultuous one in America, marked by the ongoing tensions leading up to World War II. The Great Depression had left many Americans struggling to make ends meet, and the country was gripped by fear and uncertainty about its place in the world. Radio drama like Lux Radio Theatre played a significant role in providing escapism and entertainment for audiences during this time.

Meanwhile, around the globe, fascist regimes were on the rise, and the threat of war loomed large. The Japanese invasion of China had just occurred, and the Nazi party was consolidating power in Germany. As radio listeners tuned in to Lux Radio Theatre, they would have been unaware of these global events, but they would have been attuned to the same anxieties and fears that drove their favorite actors onto the airwaves.

The popularity of radio drama like Lux Radio Theatre during this period can be attributed, at least in part, to its ability to provide a welcome respite from the hardships of everyday life. Listeners could immerse themselves in a fictional world, forgetting for a while about the troubles that plagued their own lives. "Enter Madame" taps into these anxieties and fears, offering a thrilling narrative that is both captivating and thought-provoking.

WHY IT MATTERS:

The masterful storytelling and atmospheric sound design in "Enter Madame" demonstrate the exceptional craft of Lux Radio Theatre's writers and performers. This episode remains worth hearing today for its timeless themes and captivating storytelling.
