# The Letter – April 21, 1941

On this spring evening in 1941, Lux Radio Theatre presents *The Letter*, a searing drama of passion, betrayal, and the deadly consequences of a moment's rage. Picture yourself in your living room as the orchestra swells and the announcer's velvet voice draws you into the steamy world of colonial Malaya, where a woman of seemingly unimpeachable virtue harbors a dangerous secret. When a jealous shot rings out in the darkness, it sets in motion a taut psychological thriller that builds inexorably toward its shocking climax. The radio's warm glow flickers as you listen to every word, every pause, every breath—knowing that somewhere in this woman's past lies a damning piece of evidence that could unravel everything. This is radio drama at its finest: sophisticated, adult, and absolutely gripping.

*Lux Radio Theatre* had already established itself as America's premier dramatic program by 1941, bringing Hollywood's biggest stars to the microphone each Monday night to recreate classic films and celebrated stage plays. The show's prestige was unmatched—sponsors knew that appearing on Lux meant association with quality and glamour. *The Letter*, originally a Broadway sensation and later a celebrated film, proved irresistible material for the program's ambitious producers. In an era before television, this was how Americans experienced premium entertainment: live performances by major talent, adapted for the intimate medium of radio. The crackle and hum of the broadcast, the sound effects artists creating worlds with everyday objects, the perfectly timed dramatic pauses—all combined to create something uniquely powerful.

Don't miss this masterclass in suspense and sophisticated storytelling. Tune in as *Lux Radio Theatre* transforms *The Letter* into an unforgettable hour of radio drama. This is the Golden Age of Radio—experience it as audiences did, live and in real time.
