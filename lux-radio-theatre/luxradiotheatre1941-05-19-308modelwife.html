# The Model Wife

Step into the glittering world of 1941 as Lux Radio Theatre presents *The Model Wife*, a sparkling comedy of errors that crackles with the quick wit and sophisticated charm that made radio drama America's favorite pastime. Picture yourself huddled around the dial on a May evening, the orchestra swelling with romantic strings as the story unfolds: a humble factory worker dreams of marrying a high-society model, but when circumstance throws them together, neither expects the hilarious complications that follow. You'll hear every shuffle of feet, every door slam, every breathless pause—the sound effects team painting vivid pictures in the theater of your mind while the cast delivers dialogue with the practiced timing of seasoned Broadway actors. The chemistry crackles between the leads as misunderstandings mount and hearts flutter, building toward a resolution that rewards the audience's investment with warmth and laughter.

By 1941, Lux Radio Theatre had become the undisputed king of dramatic anthology programming, commanding millions of listeners nationwide with its commitment to stellar production values and A-list talent. Sponsored by Lux soap—whose product placement was woven seamlessly into the host's opening remarks—the show commanded the resources to attract top Hollywood stars who brought their movie-star polish to the medium. Each weekly broadcast was a theatrical event, a full hour of carefully crafted entertainment that spoke to American audiences' desire for glamour, sophistication, and escapism during increasingly uncertain times. Radio drama was the streaming service of its era, and Lux Radio Theatre was appointment listening.

Don't miss this gem from radio's golden age. Tune in for *The Model Wife* and discover why millions gathered weekly around their sets to experience stories told with an artistry that survives the decades. This is radio drama at its finest.
