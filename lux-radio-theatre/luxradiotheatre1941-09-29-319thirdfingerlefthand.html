# The Third Finger, Left Hand

Step into the parlors and penthouses of Manhattan on this September evening in 1941, where *Lux Radio Theatre* presents a glittering adaptation of *The Third Finger, Left Hand*—a comedy of romantic deception that crackles with wit and worldly sophistication. A career woman invents a convenient husband to ward off unwanted suitors, only to have her fabricated marriage unravel in the most delicious ways imaginable. Listen as the orchestra swells, as doors slam and telephones ring with urgency, as voices collide in rapid-fire dialogue brimming with innuendo and charm. The sound effects team brings New York to life—the clatter of typewriters in a magazine office, the intimacy of a restaurant rendezvous, the tension of confrontation. This is escapist drama at its finest, when Americans tuning in could forget, for ninety minutes, the darkening world beyond their radios.

*Lux Radio Theatre* had become America's most prestigious dramatic program by 1941, commanding sponsorship from Lux Toilet Soap and attracting Hollywood's biggest stars who eagerly performed on live broadcast. Cecil B. DeMille's steady hand as host and producer ensured that each adaptation captured the glamour and emotional punch of its source material, whether the evening called for tragedy or, as in this case, romantic comedy. The show represented the golden age of radio drama—that magical moment when an intimate voice in the darkness could transport millions into worlds of intrigue and desire.

Tune in to experience live broadcast drama as it was meant to be heard: performed by accomplished actors, scored by a full orchestra, and delivered with the energy and spontaneity of genuine theatre. No second takes, no retakes—just the thrill of performance unfolding in real time. This is *Lux Radio Theatre*.
