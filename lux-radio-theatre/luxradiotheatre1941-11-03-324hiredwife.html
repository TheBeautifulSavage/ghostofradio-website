Picture yourself settling into your favorite chair on a crisp November evening in 1941, the warm glow of your radio's dial beckoning you into the sophisticated world of Lux Radio Theatre. Tonight's presentation brings you "The Hired Wife," a delicious comedy-drama of mistaken identity and romantic complications that will have you leaning forward in suspense. When a secretary accepts a job that requires her to pose as the wife of a wealthy businessman, the stage is set for witty repartee, genuine emotion, and the kind of sparkling dialogue that only Hollywood's finest could deliver. As the night deepens and complications multiply, you'll find yourself wondering whether true love can blossom from such calculated deception—or whether the masquerade will unravel entirely. The tension crackles through your speakers as the superb cast navigates each twist with the polished precision that made Lux the crown jewel of American radio drama.

For seventeen glorious years, Lux Radio Theatre brought the glamour and artistry of motion pictures directly into American homes, adapting the greatest films and literary works for the intimate medium of radio. By 1941, with the nation on the precipice of war, these Thursday evening broadcasts offered audiences a cherished escape into elegantly crafted drama and heartfelt storytelling. Each episode featured a stellar cast of radio regulars and film stars who understood that radio acting demanded a special skill—the ability to convey emotion, character, and entire worlds through voice alone, with only sound effects and a live orchestra to complete the picture.

Don't miss this exemplary episode of radio's most distinguished dramatic series. Tune in and discover why millions of listeners made Lux Radio Theatre an unmissable appointment with entertainment during radio's golden age.
