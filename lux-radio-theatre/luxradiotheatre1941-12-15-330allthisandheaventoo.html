# All This and Heaven Too – December 15, 1941

As the Lux Radio Theatre orchestra swells with dramatic strings, listeners are transported to 1840s France, where passion and scandal collide behind the gilded doors of aristocratic drawing rooms. In *All This and Heaven Too*, a forbidden love affair threatens to unravel the carefully constructed world of a noble family, with hearts set against the inexorable march of duty and social propriety. The cast delivers breathless performances as secrets threaten to spill forth, each revelation tightening the emotional noose around characters caught between desire and decorum. You'll hear every crack in a marriage, every whispered confession, every anguished decision that echoes across the Atlantic and through decades—all rendered in that crystalline audio clarity that made radio drama the most intimate form of entertainment America had ever known.

This December broadcast arrives at a watershed moment in American history, just one week after Pearl Harbor, when families gathered around their receivers seeking both escape and reassurance from the world outside. *Lux Radio Theatre* had become the gold standard of dramatic radio, commanding audiences of millions with its lavish productions, Hollywood talent, and impeccable craftsmanship. Each episode was a complete theatrical experience compressed into a tight hour, with live orchestras, sound effects artists, and stars of stage and screen delivering performances that rivaled anything Broadway could offer—yet free to everyone with a radio set.

This particular adaptation brings the romantic turmoil of *All This and Heaven Too* into vivid life, a tale of impossible choices and enduring love that would have resonated deeply with wartime listeners. Tune in to experience the finest that classic radio drama had to offer: superior storytelling, world-class talent, and that unmistakable magic that only the golden age of radio could create.
