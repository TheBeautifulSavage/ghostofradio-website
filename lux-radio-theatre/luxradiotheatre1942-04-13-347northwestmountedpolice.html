# North West Mounted Police - April 13, 1942

Picture yourself settling into your favorite chair on a spring evening in 1942, the radio glowing warmly in the darkness of your living room. Tonight, Lux Radio Theatre brings you *North West Mounted Police*, a thrilling tale of the Canadian frontier where duty, honor, and passion collide beneath vast prairie skies. The roar of the orchestra swells as you're transported to a world of mounted constables, treacherous landscapes, and a love triangle that threatens to unravel a man's sacred oath. You'll hear the crack of rifle fire, the thunder of hoofbeats across frozen ground, and the unmistakable tension in every voice as these characters face impossible choices. It's the kind of drama that makes you forget your wartime worries for an entire evening—a perfect escape into adventure and high stakes that only radio can deliver.

This particular broadcast arrives during America's second year of World War II, when audiences hungered for stories of courage, loyalty, and men bound by duty—themes that resonated deeply with a nation sending millions to war. Lux Radio Theatre, already in its eighth wildly successful year, had become the gold standard of dramatic broadcasting, commanding an enormous audience with its lavish productions, star-studded casts, and adaptations of celebrated films. Bringing literary and cinematic classics to the airwaves with prestigious Hollywood talent made the show irresistible; listeners could experience the grandeur of a major motion picture from the comfort of home, free of charge.

Don't miss this magnificent evening of entertainment. Tune in to *North West Mounted Police*—where the Canadian wilderness comes alive, and the code of honor is tested against the tempest of human emotion. It's radio drama at its finest.
