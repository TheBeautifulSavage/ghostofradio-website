# TO MARY WITH LOVE

Step into the warm glow of Monday evening, November 16th, 1942, as Cecil B. DeMille welcomes you to the Lux Radio Theatre for a stirring presentation of *To Mary with Love*. Picture the orchestra swelling with romantic strings as the announcer's velvet voice introduces a tale of marriage, devotion, and the small miracles that sustain love through the years. This evening's drama follows a couple on their wedding anniversary, as memories of their courtship and the tender moments of their shared life unfold in vivid, poignant detail. The cast brings crackling intensity to every whispered confession and earnest embrace, while the sound effects department creates an intimate world of drawing rooms, rain-swept streets, and the quiet sanctuary of home. You'll hear the rustle of silk, the clink of champagne glasses, and the hushed conversations of two people rediscovering why they promised forever.

Broadcasting live in 1942—a year when American homes craved comfort and reassurance—the Lux Radio Theatre stood as a beacon of sophisticated entertainment. With DeMille's legendary direction and the era's finest film and stage talent gracing the microphone, each episode became an event, a golden hour when families gathered around their radios for prestige drama they couldn't find anywhere else. Hollywood's biggest stars donated their talents for the war effort, and the broadcast's sponsor, Lux soap, became synonymous with glamorous escapism during uncertain times.

Tune in now to hear a masterful performance that captures the bittersweet beauty of enduring love, performed before a live studio audience whose laughter and applause remain forever preserved. This is radio drama as it was meant to be heard—immediate, emotional, and utterly unforgettable.
