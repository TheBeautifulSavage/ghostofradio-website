Lux Radio Theatre CBS/NBC · February 1, 1943

Step into the glittering world of small-town American ambition this evening as Lux Radio Theatre brings you George Kelly's enduring comedy The Show Off. Picture yourself in a cozy living room, the amber glow of your radio dial casting familiar light across the parlor, as the orchestra swells and you're transported to a household turned upside down by a brash, fast-talking charlatan. A young man desperate to impress uses every trick in the book—exaggerated tales of conquest, borrowed finery, and unbridled confidence—to win over a skeptical family. As the evening progresses, you'll find yourself caught between laughter and tension, wondering whether charm and persistence can overcome the considerable doubt of those who know him best. In wartime February 1943, this comedy lands with particular resonance: a story about American hustle, reinvention, and the thin line between confidence and deception.

Lux Radio Theatre had become America's premier dramatic showcase by this point in the war years, commanding some fifteen million listeners each Monday evening. The program's signature opening—the famous Lux Toilet Soap commercial, crackling with period enthusiasm—preceded full-length radio adaptations of Broadway's greatest hits, featuring Hollywood's most glamorous stars. What made Lux distinctive was its commitment to faithful, star-studded renditions of stage classics, bringing theatrical prestige directly into American homes when gas rationing and wartime restrictions kept many from attending live performances.

Don't miss this energetic production of Kelly's American comedy classic. Tune in Monday evening at 9 PM as Lux Radio Theatre reminds us why The Show Off has delighted audiences for nearly two decades—a perfect escape from the anxieties of wartime, and proof that some characters never go out of style.
