Picture this: a sweltering New York City summer, the kind that drives young women to seek adventure and romance in the cramped quarters of a Greenwich Village basement apartment. As your radio crackles to life on this July evening, you'll find yourself transported into the delightfully chaotic world of My Sister Eileen—a sophisticated comedy brimming with romantic entanglement, sisterly mishaps, and the intoxicating clash between small-town naiveté and big-city sophistication. The Lux Radio Theatre brings this beloved Broadway hit to vivid life with impeccable comic timing and a cast that captures every nuance of jealousy, exasperation, and affection between two sisters navigating love and opportunity in Depression-era Manhattan.

In 1943, as American soldiers fought overseas and the home front mobilized for war, the Lux Radio Theatre remained a beacon of escapist entertainment, broadcast live from Hollywood and sponsored by Lux soap—a pairing of glamour and domesticity that defined the era. This particular production showcases the magic of the program's golden age: professional stage and film actors delivering Broadway material with the immediacy and intimacy that only live radio could provide. The episode captures a moment when audiences desperately needed laughter and romance, when the anxieties of wartime made these tales of ordinary girls seeking happiness feel profoundly meaningful.

Tune in for an evening of genuine theatrical artistry delivered straight into your living room. Settle back, let the orchestra's opening notes transport you, and discover why millions of Americans made the Lux Radio Theatre their weekly appointment with entertainment. This is radio drama at its finest—sophisticated, beautifully performed, and utterly enchanting.
