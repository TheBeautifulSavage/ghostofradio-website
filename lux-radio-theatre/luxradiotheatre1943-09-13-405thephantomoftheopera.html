# The Phantom of the Opera – September 13, 1943

Step into the darkened corridors of the Paris Opera House as Lux Radio Theatre brings Gaston Leroux's timeless masterpiece to life through your loudspeaker. On this September evening in 1943, listeners are transported to a world of Gothic mystery and forbidden romance, where a masked genius lurks beneath the stage, consumed by obsession for a beautiful soprano. The production crackles with theatrical tension—the screeching of chandelier ropes, the phantom's haunting organ music echoing through cavernous halls, and the raw emotion of Christine caught between her devoted Raoul and the tortured music teacher who commands her heart. This is radio drama at its most immersive, where sound design becomes architecture, and the listener's imagination completes what the eye cannot see.

Lux Radio Theatre stood as the crown jewel of American dramatic broadcasting, commanding millions of listeners each week throughout its two-decade reign. By 1943, the show had already established itself as the premium venue for adapting Hollywood's greatest stories and literary classics, recruiting major film stars to recreate their most beloved roles before the microphone. This particular production of *The Phantom* capitalized on the American appetite for escapism during wartime—audiences hungry for passion, mystery, and the supernatural found all of it in Leroux's unforgettable tale. The program's meticulous attention to dramatic nuance and sound effects made it the gold standard of prestige radio, backed by Lux soap's deep pockets and commitment to quality entertainment.

Join us in revisiting this haunting transmission from 1943, a magnificent showcase of radio's golden age when stories were painted entirely in sound and imagination. Tune in to hear why millions gathered around their sets for Lux Radio Theatre.
