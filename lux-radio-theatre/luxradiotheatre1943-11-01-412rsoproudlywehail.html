As the velvet CBS orchestra swells into the Lux Radio Theatre theme on this November evening in 1943, listeners across America settle into an intimate evening with one of radio's most glamorous showcases. Tonight's presentation brings the silver screen home in living, breathing drama: As Proudly We Hail, a stirring wartime tale that captures the courage of those who serve while the nation watches anxiously from their living rooms. The cast delivers performances that crackle with authenticity—tension builds through carefully crafted dialogue, while the sound effects team conjures the very atmosphere of danger and duty. You'll hear the hum of military machinery, the rush of emotion in a soldier's voice, and the quiet strength of those left behind. This is drama as radio masters it: intimate, immediate, and deeply human.

In 1943, with American sons and daughters serving across two oceans, Lux Radio Theatre served a vital cultural purpose, adapting contemporary Hollywood stories while honoring the very real sacrifice defining the era. Host Cecil B. DeMille's introduction carries the weight of the moment—this isn't mere entertainment, but a reflection of the nation's values during its darkest hours. The show's commitment to bringing Broadway and film's finest productions to the radio audience had already made it a Tuesday night institution, but wartime episodes like this one resonated with particular poignancy, validating the struggles Americans faced daily.

Don't miss this remarkable window into 1943 America—a time when radio brought people together around shared stories of valor and perseverance. These carefully preserved broadcasts remain treasured artifacts of how entertainment sustained the American spirit through uncertainty. Tune in and experience the golden age of radio drama at its most meaningful.
