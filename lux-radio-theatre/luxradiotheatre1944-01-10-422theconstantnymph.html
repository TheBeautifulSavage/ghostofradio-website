# The Constant Nymph

As your radio crackles to life on this January evening in 1944, prepare yourself for an intimate journey into the bohemian corners of post-war Europe. *The Constant Nymph*, adapted for radio's golden stage, brings to vivid life the passionate, doomed romance between a struggling composer and a devoted young woman whose unwavering love becomes both his salvation and his undoing. In this production, listeners will find themselves transported to a world of artistic struggle and emotional complexity, where the clash between duty and desire plays out against the backdrop of a talented but troubled household. The chemistry between the lead performers crackles with genuine tension—you'll hear it in every pause, every breathless confession, every moment of anguished realization.

*Lux Radio Theatre* had by 1944 established itself as America's premier dramatic broadcast, bringing Hollywood's finest to the intimate medium of radio each Monday night. This production represents the show at its peak, when high production values, stellar casting, and meticulous direction could transform a Broadway favorite into something luminous and immediate. The sponsors at Lux understood that radio drama demanded not spectacle but authenticity—the power of voice, music, and sound design to unlock the deepest chambers of the listener's heart. This particular adaptation showcases that philosophy perfectly, stripping away theatrical artifice to expose raw human longing.

Settle into your favorite chair, dim the lights, and allow yourself to surrender to this tale of constancy and consequence. Whether you're a devoted fan of *Lux Radio Theatre* or discovering this remarkable program for the first time, *The Constant Nymph* promises an evening of superb dramatic entertainment that will remind you why, for over two decades, millions of Americans made this broadcast an essential ritual. Tune in—the curtain rises at nine o'clock.
