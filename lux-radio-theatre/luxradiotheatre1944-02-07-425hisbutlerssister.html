# His Butler's Sister

Step into the elegant drawing rooms and shadowy corridors of prewar sophistication as Lux Radio Theatre presents *His Butler's Sister*, a sparkling comedy of manners that crackles with the wit and romantic entanglements only Hollywood's golden age could conjure. This February evening in 1944 brings you a tale of mistaken identities and class collision, where the lines between servant and society blur in the most amusing ways imaginable. Picture the intimate orchestral score swelling as our heroine—caught between duty and desire—navigates a world where a single deception could unravel everything. The stellar cast delivers each line with the precision of master craftsmen, their voices painting vivid scenes in your mind's eye while the live studio audience hangs on every carefully timed pause and perfectly executed comedic beat.

The Lux Radio Theatre stood as the crown jewel of dramatic broadcasting during radio's golden age, where Hollywood's biggest stars would abandon their film sets to perform live before microphones and a studio audience. Each week from 1934 through 1955, listeners across America would tune in to witness cinema's most celebrated talents—Cary Grant, Bette Davis, James Stewart—breathing new life into beloved films and original theatrical productions. The show's lavish production values and meticulous attention to performance transformed radio drama into an event, a appointment with entertainment that millions refused to miss. By 1944, as wartime gripped the nation, programs like this provided essential escape and glamour to households far from Hollywood's bright lights.

Don't miss this enchanting evening of comedy and sophistication. Settle into your favorite chair, adjust the dial to the Lux frequency, and prepare for an hour of first-rate entertainment that only radio's golden era could deliver. *His Butler's Sister* awaits—a perfect reminder of when radio drama reigned supreme.
