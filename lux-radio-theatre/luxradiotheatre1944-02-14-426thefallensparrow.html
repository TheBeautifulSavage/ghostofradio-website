# The Fallen Sparrow - February 14, 1944

On this Valentine's Day broadcast, Lux Radio Theatre brings you *The Fallen Sparrow*, a tale of intrigue and moral reckoning that cuts deep into the American conscience during wartime. John Garfield inhabits the role of a war-damaged pilot haunted by his captors and searching for redemption among those he suspects of treachery. As the drama unfolds across your radio, you'll feel the claustrophobic tension of paranoia, the sharp edges of betrayal, and the desperate yearning of a man caught between his past and his country's future. The chemistry between Garfield and his co-star crackles with dangerous chemistry—part romance, part interrogation—while the orchestra swells beneath revelations that will keep you riveted until the final fadeout.

This broadcast represents Lux Radio Theatre at its commercial and artistic zenith, when the program commanded millions of listeners each Monday night and commanded Hollywood's biggest talents to appear for union scale. In wartime 1944, *The Fallen Sparrow* resonated with particular urgency; adapted from John D. Marquand's novel, it explores themes of loyalty, physical trauma, and the psychological cost of service that spoke directly to a nation invested in distant battlefields. Lux's sponsorship—always subtle, always tasteful—became synonymous with prestige drama, making radio's golden age accessible even as competition from film studios threatened the medium's dominance.

Whether you're a devoted listener who's been tuning in since the early '30s or discovering this lost artifact for the first time, this February 14th episode deserves a place in your collection. It captures a singular moment when American radio drama reached its artistic peak, when a sponsor's commitment to quality storytelling and a brilliant cast's dedication created something that still resonates across eight decades.
