# Lux Radio Theatre: "Destroyer" (April 3, 1944)

As your radio crackles to life on this spring evening in 1944, you're transported to the steel decks of a naval destroyer cutting through dark waters. The sound engineers at CBS have outdone themselves—the throb of mighty engines, the shriek of air raid sirens, the deafening clang of combat stations all surround you in your living room. This is no mere sailor's tale, but a gripping drama of courage under fire, of men bound by duty and fear, facing an enemy that lurks beneath the waves. With America fully committed to the war in the Pacific, the stakes feel immediate and real. The tension builds as the crew scrambles to locate a U-boat threat, each moment crackling with authenticity that only those who've lived through wartime can truly appreciate.

For over a decade, *Lux Radio Theatre* has been America's premier dramatic showcase, gathering Hollywood's brightest stars to recreate their films and perform original scripts before a live studio audience. The show's $4,000 per-episode budget—an astronomical sum in the 1940s—ensures only the finest production values and the biggest names willing to grace the microphone. During these war years, episodes like "Destroyer" serve a dual purpose: they entertain a nation hungry for escape, while simultaneously reflecting the very real heroism and sacrifice unfolding across two oceans. The program has become appointment listening, a cultural touchstone where quality drama meets patriotic duty.

Don't miss this powerful episode of *Lux Radio Theatre*. Settle in, dim the lights, and let your imagination fill in what no camera could capture. "Destroyer" airs Monday night at 9 o'clock—be there when the drama unfolds.
