As your radio crackles to life on this spring evening, you're transported to the snow-capped peaks and hidden valleys of Colorado, where a seemingly simple vacation becomes entangled in deception and heartfelt revelation. In this charming adaptation of the Twentieth Century-Fox musical, a young woman arrives at a rustic lodge seeking solitude and escape, only to find herself caught between genuine affection and the comfortable lies of a man determined to win her back. The Lux Radio Theatre orchestra swells with the romance of mountain air and uncertain promises, while the cast delivers performances that capture both the lightness of a musical's grace and the weighty complexity of second chances. You'll hear the authentic crackle of a fireplace, the distant call of wilderness, and dialogue that seems to whisper directly into your living room—this is radio drama at its most intimate and affecting.

The Lux Radio Theatre had perfected the art of bringing Hollywood's finest to the intimate medium of radio during these wartime years, when Americans gathered around their sets seeking both escapism and connection. By 1944, the show had become a Sunday night institution, featuring A-list motion picture talent performing abridged versions of recent films in real-time, before live audiences. This particular May broadcast represented the show's unique ability to distill cinematic storytelling into forty-five minutes of pure dramatic electricity, proving that visual spectacle mattered far less than the emotional truth between characters.

Tune in to experience why millions of listeners made Lux Radio Theatre an indispensable part of their weekly ritual. Settle into your chair, let the opening fanfare wash over you, and rediscover what radio drama offered that no other medium could—the power of imagination sparked by voice, music, and the subtle art of human performance.
