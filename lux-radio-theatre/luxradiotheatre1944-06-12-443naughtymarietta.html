# Lux Radio Theatre: Naughty Marietta (June 12, 1944)

Step into the gilded world of old New Orleans as Lux Radio Theatre transports you to a sumptuous evening of romance, intrigue, and musical splendor. On this June night in 1944, listeners gathered around their sets to experience Victor Herbert's beloved operetta *Naughty Marietta* brought vividly to life through the magic of radio drama. The sophisticated orchestration swells as our heroine—a French countess fleeing scandal—arrives in colonial Louisiana to remake her fortune and her heart. What unfolds is a captivating tale of mistaken identities, daring escapades, and a love that defies the rigid social conventions of the era. The Lux cast, assembled from Hollywood's finest, delivers every witty aside and tender moment with the polish and precision that made this program the crown jewel of American radio entertainment.

By 1944, Lux Radio Theatre had already established itself as the most prestigious dramatic anthology series on the airwaves, consistently drawing millions of listeners every Monday night. What set Lux apart was its unwavering commitment to bringing Broadway and Hollywood's greatest successes to radio audiences with star power that rivaled the silver screen itself. This particular adaptation showcases the enduring appeal of Herbert's 1910 operetta, a work that had already proven its staying power through decades of theatrical revivals. The wartime audience found particular solace in such lavish escapism—a brief respite from the anxieties of global conflict, wrapped in the elegance of period romance and the sophisticated wit that defined the golden age of radio drama.

Tune in now and let the lush orchestral arrangements and stellar performances transport you across time and space. Experience the charm and sophistication that captivated forty million Americans weekly during radio's finest hour.
