# Lux Radio Theatre: Maytime (September 4, 1944)

As the orchestra swells and the familiar Lux Radio Theatre signature music crackles through your speaker, you're transported to turn-of-the-century Paris and a world of operatic passion and impossible love. Tonight's presentation of *Maytime* captures the aching romance of Sigmund Romberg's beloved operetta, a tale of star-crossed lovers whose devotion transcends the boundaries of duty and ambition. The dulcet tones of the cast will carry you through moonlit gardens and grand concert halls, where a prima donna must choose between her career and the heart of a composer who loves her with desperate intensity. With every dramatic pause, every soaring musical interlude, the radio becomes a stage, and your living room transforms into an intimate theater where the full richness of this classic story unfolds before your very ears.

*Maytime* arrives at a poignant moment in American history—September 1944, with victory in Europe finally within reach and the nation's attention fixed on returning soldiers and the promise of peace. The Lux Radio Theatre, now in its eleventh celebrated season, has become America's most treasured appointment with radio drama, presenting Hollywood's finest actors in elaborate productions that rival stage performances. This episode exemplifies why sponsors and audiences alike championed the show: sophisticated storytelling, lavish production values, and the freedom to explore the full emotional complexity of timeless tales without the constraints of wartime newsprint or rationed film stock.

Whether you're a devoted regular or discovering Lux Radio Theatre for the first time, *Maytime* offers an irreplaceable glimpse into golden-age broadcasting—when radio was theater, drama was destiny, and an evening's entertainment could transport millions simultaneously into worlds of beauty and heartbreak. Tune in and let yourself surrender to the magic.
