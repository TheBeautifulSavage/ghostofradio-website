# Break of Hearts

On this September evening in 1944, Cecil B. DeMille welcomes you back to the *Lux Radio Theatre*, where the intimate tragedies of the human heart play out across your living room with all the grandeur of a Hollywood soundstage. Tonight's presentation, *Break of Hearts*, brings you a haunting tale of love, ambition, and the terrible price of devotion. As the orchestra swells with plaintive strings, you'll find yourself drawn into a world where passion becomes torment, where a woman's sacrifice for her beloved composer-husband transforms into heartbreak when his genius demands everything—including her own artistic dreams. The performances crackle with authenticity; every pause, every tremor in the voice, every dramatic intake of breath carries the weight of a marriage unraveling behind closed doors. This is radio at its most emotionally devastating, where the absence of a visual image forces the listener's imagination to conjure their own visions of despair.

*Lux Radio Theatre* had become America's premier dramatic anthology by 1944, commanding millions of listeners each Monday evening with its incomparable blend of Hollywood glamour and theatrical excellence. DeMille's exacting direction and the program's generous budget ensured only the finest scripts and performers graced the microphone, making it the benchmark for quality radio drama. During wartime, when audiences hungered for escapism and emotional resonance, *Lux* provided both—proving that radio could deliver the sophistication and star power of cinema while offering something cinema could not: the immediacy of live performance and the boundless canvas of imagination.

Tune in now for *Break of Hearts*, a masterclass in romantic tragedy. Let yourself be transported to that golden age when drama was an art form, and a single voice could break your heart completely.
