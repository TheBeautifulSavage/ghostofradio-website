# Lux Radio Theatre: Berkeley Square (December 18, 1944)

Step into the fog-shrouded streets of eighteenth-century London as Lux Radio Theatre transports you across centuries with *Berkeley Square*, a haunting drama of love, loss, and the inexplicable power of time itself. On this December evening in 1944, listeners huddled around their radios will experience the enchanting tale of a modern American who mysteriously finds himself inhabiting the body of his ancestor, desperately attempting to alter the course of history to save the woman he loves from a fate sealed two hundred years in the past. The intimate voice acting, the carefully orchestrated sound effects of period London—the clip-clop of horse-drawn carriages, the murmur of Georgian society—and the lush orchestral score create an atmosphere thick with longing and temporal dislocation. You'll feel the protagonist's mounting desperation as he battles against the very fabric of time itself.

*Lux Radio Theatre* had become America's premier dramatic showcase by 1944, transforming Broadway's finest plays and Hollywood's greatest films into intimate sonic experiences that brought sophisticated entertainment into millions of American homes every Monday night. The program's reputation for mounting lavish productions with top-tier talent—all underwritten by Lux soap—made it the gold standard of radio drama. *Berkeley Square* exemplifies the show's ambition: a complex, literary work of fantasy requiring subtle acting and imaginative sound design rather than mere melodrama.

Don't miss this mesmerizing journey through time and devotion. Tune in Monday night and surrender yourself to the magic of *Lux Radio Theatre*, where the best stories ever told come alive through the invisible artistry of radio's greatest medium.
