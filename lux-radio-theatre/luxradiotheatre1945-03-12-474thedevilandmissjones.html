# The Devil and Miss Jones – March 12, 1945

Picture yourself settling into your favorite chair on a Friday night in wartime America, the amber glow of your radio dial beckoning you into a world of sophisticated comedy and romantic entanglement. Tonight, Lux Radio Theatre presents "The Devil and Miss Jones," a sparkling tale of celestial mischief and earthly desire that promises to transport you far from the anxieties of 1945. When a smooth-talking devil makes a wager with fate itself, a spirited young woman becomes the unwitting pawn in a cosmic game of chance. As the plot twists and comedic misunderstandings accumulate, you'll find yourself thoroughly enchanted by snappy dialogue, expert timing, and the kind of witty repartee that only Hollywood's finest actors could deliver. The orchestra swells, the stakes rise, and by evening's end, you won't be quite sure whether to root for heaven or hell.

For over a decade, Lux Radio Theatre had been America's premier dramatic showcase, bringing the glamour of Hollywood directly into living rooms across the nation. With A-list film stars eager to extend their fame into the intimate medium of radio, the show became an institution—a Tuesday or Friday night ritual where listeners could experience the magic of cinema reimagined for the ear. This particular adaptation represents the show at its creative peak, when writers understood how to maximize radio's unique power to spark imagination and emotion through voice alone.

Don't miss this delightful evening of entertainment. Tune in to experience a masterclass in timing, charm, and the kind of escapism that sustained an entire nation through uncertain times.
