Picture yourself in 1945, settling into your favorite chair as the opening fanfare swells through the radio speaker—that unmistakable Lux Radio Theatre signature that millions of Americans have come to cherish. Tonight brings Cecil B. DeMille's presentation of Kentucky, a sweeping drama of passion and conflict set against the rolling bluegrass country of the American South. As the orchestra builds beneath DeMille's cultured introduction, you'll find yourself transported to a world of thoroughbred horses, family honor, and the kind of romantic entanglement that can tear a household apart. The stellar cast breathes life into characters caught between duty and desire, their voices capturing every nuance of heartbreak and determination in performances that only radio's greatest dramatic talents could deliver. You can almost hear the thundering hooves and taste the dust of the racing track as this classic Hollywood story unfolds in vivid, intimate detail—the way only your imagination and a master storyteller's voice can create.

By 1945, Lux Radio Theatre had become America's most prestigious dramatic anthology, having already brought Hollywood directly into living rooms for over a decade. This episode represents the show at its peak influence, when a Lux broadcast could rival—or exceed—a theatrical release in capturing the nation's attention. Cecil B. DeMille's personal involvement as host and creative director lent an air of cinematic grandeur to every production, while the weekly presentation of adapted Hollywood films created a cultural bridge between radio's golden age and cinema's glamorous world.

Don't let this evening pass without experiencing one of radio's finest hours. Press play, dim the lights, and let yourself drift into the world of Kentucky—a masterclass in dramatic storytelling from an era when radio was America's heartbeat.
