# Mrs. Skeffington - October 1, 1945

Picture yourself settled into the warm glow of your living room radio on a crisp autumn evening in 1945. The opening fanfare swells—that unmistakable orchestral flourish that signals the beginning of *Lux Radio Theatre*—and you're transported into the glamorous, treacherous world of pre-war Baltimore society. Tonight's presentation brings Bette Davis to the microphone in *Mrs. Skeffington*, a penetrating drama of vanity, sacrifice, and redemption that cuts to the very heart of what it means to love and be loved. As Davis's voice fills your home, you'll follow the arc of a beautiful woman's journey from self-absorbed socialite to a woman whose inner grace becomes her truest beauty. The supporting cast surrounds her with understated excellence, while the sound effects team—those unsung architects of radio drama—paints every parlor scene, every moment of heartbreak with meticulous care.

*Lux Radio Theatre* stood as America's premier dramatic anthology during radio's golden age, bringing Hollywood's finest directly into American homes for over two decades. By 1945, the show had become a national institution, a weekly appointment with sophistication and star power. This particular broadcast captures the program at its height, when the marriage of stellar performances and intimate broadcasting created something television could never quite replicate—the listener's imagination as full collaborator in the drama unfolding before them.

Don't miss this remarkable documentation of radio's greatest era. Tune in and experience why millions of Americans considered *Lux Radio Theatre* essential weekly listening—a moment of escape, elegance, and genuine emotional truth in an uncertain world.
