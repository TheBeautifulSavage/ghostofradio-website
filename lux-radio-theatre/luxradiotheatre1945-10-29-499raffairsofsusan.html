# The Affairs of Susan - Lux Radio Theatre (October 29, 1945)

Picture yourself in the autumn of 1945, tuning your radio dial to catch the opening fanfare of Lux Radio Theatre—that unmistakable musical signature that signaled an evening of Hollywood glamour beamed directly into your living room. Tonight, settle in for "The Affairs of Susan," a delicious romantic comedy that promises to entangle you in the romantic misadventures of a woman caught between multiple suitors, each convinced he knows the *real* Susan. As the drama unfolds across thirty electrifying minutes, you'll experience the brilliance of skilled voice actors creating an entire world of mistaken identities and romantic chaos, with perfectly timed comedic beats that will have you laughing aloud. The witty dialogue crackles with the sophistication of 1940s Hollywood, a far cry from the somber war reports dominating the headlines just weeks after the surrender of Japan.

By 1945, Lux Radio Theatre had cemented itself as America's premier dramatic showcase, commanding audiences of millions who turned out weekly to hear major Hollywood stars performing live adaptations of recent films. This particular episode represents the show at its height, when radio drama still rivaled cinema itself as the nation's primary entertainment medium. The live broadcast format meant tension both on and off the microphone—any flubbed line or missed cue was permanent, making each performance a high-wire act of artistry and nerve. These were radio's golden years, when millions gathered around their sets as a shared national ritual.

Don't miss this chance to experience the sophistication, spontaneity, and sheer entertainment value that made Lux Radio Theatre an institution. Tune in and discover why listeners made this appointment with their radios as essential as dinner itself.
