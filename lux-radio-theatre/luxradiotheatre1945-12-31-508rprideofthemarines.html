# Pride of the Marines – December 31, 1945

As the clock counts down toward midnight on New Year's Eve, Lux Radio Theatre brings you one of the year's most stirring dramas: *Pride of the Marines*. Tonight's broadcast captures the raw emotion and quiet heroism of a true American story—a Marine who loses his sight in service to his country and must navigate not just darkness, but the devastating uncertainty of rebuilding a life forever changed. Expect taut dialogue, poignant silences, and performances that cut right through the static to reach your heart. The ensemble cast delivers every line with the weight it deserves, transforming your living room into a hospital ward, a dusty barracks, and the intimate spaces where a man learns to see again through love and perseverance.

*Pride of the Marines* arrives at a singular moment in American broadcasting. The war has only just ended this August, and the nation is still processing the staggering human cost of victory. Lux Radio Theatre, now in its twelfth triumphant season, has become America's premier venue for quality drama—where Hollywood's biggest stars performed live before a studio audience each Monday night, competing for sponsorship by Lux soap while audiences competed for tickets. This particular adaptation showcases some of radio's finest talent in a story that mirrors the real struggles of thousands of returning servicemen, making it less a theatrical escape than a cultural reckoning played out in real time.

Don't let this New Year's Eve pass without experiencing this powerful drama. Settle in with family and friends, let the orchestra set the mood, and prepare yourself for ninety minutes of unforgettable storytelling. *Pride of the Marines* airs tonight on Lux Radio Theatre—a fitting way to bid farewell to 1945 and contemplate the long road ahead.
