Picture yourself in a wood-paneled study on a crisp evening in 1940, your radio glowing warmly as the familiar strains of the Mr. District Attorney theme fade into that unmistakable voice: "Hello, good evening, and thank you for being with us!" What unfolds is a labyrinth of intrigue centered around a museum's most treasured possession—a priceless miniature portrait that has vanished without a trace. Was it a clever theft by a master criminal, or does the answer lie closer to home, hidden among the very walls meant to protect it? As our fearless District Attorney and his able assistants navigate shadowy museum corridors and interrogate suspects with motives as varied as their alibis, the tension builds with each dramatic revelation. The crisp dialogue snaps with period authenticity, while the subtle sound design—the click of a museum lock, the whisper of turning pages in a ledger—transports listeners directly into the mystery.

For thirteen years, Mr. District Attorney captivated millions by transforming the public prosecutor's office into a compelling microcosm of justice itself, with scripts ripped from actual case files that made listeners feel they were witnessing real legal battles. The show's meticulous attention to procedural accuracy, combined with its sophisticated urban drama, elevated it above typical pulp radio mystery fare, attracting devoted audiences and critical acclaim throughout its NBC and ABC broadcasts.

Step into the shadowy world of 1940s crime and justice. Tune in as the District Attorney pursues truth through a maze of deception, where one small portrait may unlock secrets worth far more than gold. Mr. District Attorney awaits—where the law always prevails, and justice is always served.
