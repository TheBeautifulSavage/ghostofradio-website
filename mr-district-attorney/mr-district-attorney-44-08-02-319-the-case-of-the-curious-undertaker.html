Mr. District Attorney became a cornerstone of American radio justice during an era when the legal system itself held an almost mythical place in the national consciousness. Premiering in 1939, the show reflected post-Depression America's faith in institutional order and the power of law enforcement to protect the innocent. Jay Jostyn's authoritative voice as the unnamed District Attorney made him the public face of righteousness, while the program's meticulous plotting and courtroom precision appealed to listeners seeking both entertainment and reassurance. This particular episode from the early 1940s exemplifies the show's golden age, when Hollywood-quality production values met compelling crime narratives adapted from real case files, giving audiences the thrill of genuine legal drama.

If you've never experienced the golden age of radio justice, or if you're a devoted fan seeking to revisit these classics, this chilling tale of an undertaker's secrets demands your attention.
