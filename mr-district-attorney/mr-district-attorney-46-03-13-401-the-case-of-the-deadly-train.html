# The Case of the Deadly Train

A passenger train hurtles through the darkness, its rhythmic clacking a hypnotic prelude to murder most foul. When a prominent businessman is found dead in his compartment—locked from the inside—the District Attorney must navigate the claustrophobic corridors of this steel serpent to separate the guilty from the innocent. With the train barreling toward its destination and suspects confined in close quarters, every alibi becomes suspect, every shadow potentially conceals a killer. This 1940s episode crackles with the authentic tension of a locked-room mystery, where the detective's keen mind and relentless pursuit of justice are the only forces that can pierce through deception and fear.

*Mr. District Attorney* emerged from the crime-saturated airwaves of the late 1930s as something distinct: a show that gave legal procedure and courtroom drama equal weight to the thrills of investigation. Starring Jay Jostyn as the crusading District Attorney, the series became NBC's prestige crime program, celebrated for its tight scripts, authentic police procedural details, and moral certitude. By the 1940s, when this episode aired, the show had perfected its formula—blending newspaper headlines with penetrating character work, reflecting the era's faith in law and order as America prepared for global conflict. These weren't tales of rogue cops cutting corners; they were celebrations of institutional justice pursued through integrity and intellect.

Settle into your chair, adjust the dial, and let the golden age of radio transport you to a speeding train where mystery and danger converge. *The Case of the Deadly Train* stands as a masterwork of vintage suspense—a reminder of when radio drama could confine listeners to a single, terrifying location and hold them rapt until justice prevailed. Don't miss this gripping tale of crime, deception, and the relentless pursuit of truth.
