Picture this: a winter's night in the city, snow falling thick and silent over the streets, when a woman's body is discovered in an alley—apparently murdered with nothing but a delicate snowflake as a clue. Impossible? Perhaps. Yet our intrepid District Attorney believes otherwise. In this gripping installment, listeners will find themselves drawn into a labyrinth of deception where the most insignificant detail becomes evidence, and a killer hides in plain sight behind an alibi as fragile as ice. With each interrogation, each revelation, the net tightens around a suspect pool that includes a society jeweler, a spurned lover, and a mysterious stranger seen fleeing the scene. The tension builds relentlessly—will our DA uncover the truth before another victim falls to this ingenious murderer?

Mr. District Attorney stood as one of radio's most enduring crime dramas, captivating audiences with its unflinching portrayal of the American legal system in action. Unlike the sensationalized tales of private detectives, this show celebrated the earnest work of public prosecutors, those unsung heroes dedicated to justice. During the 1940s, as America grappled with actual crime waves and wartime anxieties, listeners found comfort in these narratives of order prevailing over chaos. The show's commitment to legal procedure—authentic courtroom language, real investigative techniques—lent it a documentary-like credibility that set it apart from competitors.

Tune in now to experience why millions of Americans made Mr. District Attorney appointment listening every week. Whether you're a devoted fan of vintage crime drama or discovering this classic for the first time, "The Case of the Deadly Snowflake" reminds us why radio's Golden Age earned its name. Sometimes the smallest clues lead to the biggest justice.
