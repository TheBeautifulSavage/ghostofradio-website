Picture yourself settling into your favorite chair on a crisp autumn evening, tuning the dial to NBC as the familiar dramatic orchestral sting cuts through the static. "Mr. District Attorney" is on the air, and tonight's case will chill you to the bone—a seemingly philanthropic charity director whose generous donations mask something far more sinister. As the D.A. and his assistant Harrington begin to unravel the threads of what appeared to be benevolent giving, they discover that charity never began for this smooth-talking operator. Every dollar donated, every ribbon cut at a charity gala, was merely camouflage for a scheme that preys on the city's most vulnerable. With each clue methodically presented and each witness interrogation growing more tense, you'll find yourself leaning closer to the speaker, desperate to know whether justice will triumph before another victim falls prey to this wolf in philanthropist's clothing.

Throughout its remarkable thirteen-year run, "Mr. District Attorney" earned its place among radio's elite crime dramas by balancing meticulous legal procedure with genuine human drama. Unlike shows that relied on shoot-outs and fisticuffs, this series showcased the true power of detective work, courtroom strategy, and moral reasoning. The show's accuracy and respect for the legal process earned it the endorsement of actual district attorneys and law enforcement officials, making listeners feel they were getting an insider's perspective on real American justice. Hosted by Jay Jostyn, whose commanding presence defined the character, each episode unfolded like an authentic case file brought to thrilling life.

Don't miss "The Case of Charity Never Began"—a masterclass in suspense that proves the greatest crimes often hide behind the most respectable facades. Tune in and discover why America trusted Mr. District Attorney to deliver truth, justice, and unforgettable entertainment night after night.
