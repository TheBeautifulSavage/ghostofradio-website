Step into the smoke-filled offices of the District Attorney on a crisp November evening as an insidious gambling racket threatens to corrupt the very heart of the city. When a young man's life spirals into ruin over a rigged card game, our stalwart District Attorney must unravel a web of deceit that reaches into the highest echelons of power. With gritty dialogue snapping across the airwaves and the tense orchestral underscore building to fever pitch, this episode delivers everything listeners crave: moral ambiguity, clever detective work, and justice hanging by a thread. The clock is ticking, the stakes are personal, and only the DA's keen intellect and unshakeable integrity stand between the guilty and freedom.

"Mr. District Attorney" became an American institution during the golden age of radio, captivating millions with its unflinching portrayal of urban crime and legal maneuvering. Unlike the superhero fantasies that dominated the dial, this show grounded itself in procedural realism—the grinding, methodical work of building an airtight case. Broadcast live from NBC and later ABC studios, each episode was performed before an audience, lending an electric energy to the performances. The program reflected post-Depression America's complex relationship with authority and justice, exploring how the system worked (or didn't) for ordinary citizens. By the 1940s, when "The Case of Free Play" aired, the show had earned a devoted following of listeners who trusted the District Attorney as a voice of reason in an increasingly complex world.

Don't miss this masterwork of dramatic radio—a reminder of when justice wasn't just entertainment, but a shared national conversation broadcast into living rooms across America.
