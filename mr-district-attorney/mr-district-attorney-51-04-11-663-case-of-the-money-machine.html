# Case of the Money Machine

Step into the smoke-filled offices of the District Attorney as a daring counterfeiting ring threatens to flood the city's streets with worthless paper. In this crackling installment, our intrepid DA and his trusted investigator uncover a sinister operation hidden in the bowels of the industrial district—where a ingenious mechanical press churns out perfect replicas of American currency with alarming precision. The tension mounts as our heroes race against time to penetrate the criminal conspiracy before the counterfeit bills circulate through the banking system, threatening the very economic foundation of the nation. With nothing but sharp wits, dogged determination, and the occasional well-placed pistol, they must navigate a labyrinth of dangerous leads, double-crossing informants, and ruthless mobsters who will stop at nothing to protect their lucrative scheme.

What makes *Mr. District Attorney* such a cornerstone of radio drama is its unique position as the voice of law and order itself—broadcast directly from the halls of justice with the endorsement of actual district attorneys across America. Premiering in 1939, the show capitalized on the era's intense fascination with organized crime and governmental authority, offering listeners a reassuring glimpse into how the legal system protected everyday citizens. This particular episode exemplifies the show's strength: the marriage of procedural authenticity with pulse-pounding suspense, all delivered through masterful sound design that puts you directly in the room with hardened criminals and determined lawmen alike.

Don't miss this thrilling descent into the criminal underworld. Tune in as justice prevails and the counterfeiting operation is brought to light—a reminder that no scheme is too clever, no criminal too cunning, when the District Attorney is on the case.
