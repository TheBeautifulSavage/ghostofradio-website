When the curtain rises on this tense episode, listeners are thrust into the gleaming aisles of a modern supermarket—that shining symbol of American progress—now transformed into a house of shadows. A killer moves among the checkout counters and fluorescent displays, striking with cold calculation. The District Attorney's office crackles with urgency as evidence arrives, witnesses contradict one another, and the pressure mounts to catch a murderer before panic empties the stores and destroys a thriving community business. Len Dressler's assured narration guides us through the procedural maze with the authority of seasoned law enforcement, while the sound design—the ping of a cash register, footsteps echoing down empty aisles at night, the hushed conference room voices—creates an atmosphere of mounting dread. This is detective work at its finest, methodical yet gripping, where justice demands both cunning and integrity.

Mr. District Attorney captured the American imagination during its remarkable thirteen-year run by bringing the machinery of law enforcement directly into living rooms across the nation. Created by Ed and Pauline Dressler, the show pioneered the procedural drama format, following cases from crime scene to courtroom with documentary-like precision. Broadcast originally live from NBC, then later on ABC, it became appointment listening for millions who thrilled to see the District Attorney's office triumph through proper legal channels rather than vigilante justice. This 1940s episode exemplifies the show's golden period, when postwar Americans were reassured by the competence of their civic institutions—and when the supermarket itself represented a bold new frontier of American life worth protecting.

Don't miss this classic episode where law, order, and everyday American progress collide in the supermarket's harsh fluorescent light.
