Picture this: a smoky courthouse corridor, the clack of hard-soled shoes on marble floors, and a district attorney racing against the clock to prevent a dangerous criminal from slipping through the fingers of justice—all because of a trail gone cold fifteen years prior. In this gripping episode, our intrepid DA must resurrect evidence long thought buried in the dusty files of history, following breadcrumbs of testimony and physical proof that threaten to crumble with each passing day. The tension builds as witnesses' memories fade, documents yellow and curl, and a suspect's alibis grow more polished with time. Will justice prevail when memory itself becomes the unreliable witness? Tune in as the DA's sharp legal mind cuts through decades of fog to expose the truth.

Mr. District Attorney stands as one of radio's most authentic crime dramas, bringing the austere majesty of the American legal system into living rooms across the nation. Debuting in 1939, the show distinguished itself through meticulous attention to procedural detail and the moral authority of its protagonist—a crusader who wielded the law itself as his weapon. Unlike sensationalized detective shows, this program celebrated the deliberate, methodical work of prosecution: interviews, cross-examinations, and the patient assembly of evidence. The show's influence extended beyond entertainment; listeners gained genuine insight into how justice actually worked, making Mr. District Attorney both educational and dramatically compelling.

This particular episode, recorded in the early 1940s, exemplifies why audiences remained captivated by seven seasons and hundreds of cases. Don't miss this masterclass in legal drama—adjust your dial and prepare yourself for a case where the most dangerous evidence is time itself.
