Picture yourself tuning in to your radio set on a crisp evening, the amber glow of the dial illuminating your living room as the familiar strains of the "Mr. District Attorney" theme burst forth—that commanding, authoritative overture that signals justice is about to be served. In this gripping 1940s episode, our intrepid D.A. and his trusted investigator Harrington wade into the shadowy underworld of stolen automobiles and reckless youth, where a simple joy ride through the city streets spirals into something far more sinister: murder. The case unfolds with breakneck pacing as our heroes pursue leads through speakeasies and back alleys, interrogating suspects whose alibis crumble under scrutiny. You'll hear the screech of tires, the snap of evidence being uncovered, and the rapid-fire dialogue that makes your pulse quicken—all building to a stunning courtroom revelation that challenges everything listeners thought they understood about the crime.

Throughout its unprecedented thirteen-year run, Mr. District Attorney established itself as the gold standard of legal drama on radio, pioneering the procedural format that would later dominate television. Unlike the sensationalized crime stories of its era, the show's creator and star Don Hanmer crafted narratives rooted in authentic courtroom procedure and forensic methodology, working closely with real district attorneys to ensure accuracy. The program became required listening for millions of Americans during the Depression and War years, offering both entertainment and reassurance that the legal system worked with integrity and intelligence.

Don't miss this masterwork of old-time radio drama. Settle into your favorite chair, dim the lights, and prepare yourself for an evening of suspense, intrigue, and masterful storytelling. Because when the District Attorney is on the case, justice—and compelling radio—is never far behind.
