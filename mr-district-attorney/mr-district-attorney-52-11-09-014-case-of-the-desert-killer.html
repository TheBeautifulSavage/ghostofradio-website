When the sun sets over the badlands and a killer stalks the shadow-soaked arroyos, there's nowhere to hide—and nowhere the law can't follow. In this gripping installment, listeners are transported to the scorching deserts of the Southwest where a mysterious murderer has claimed multiple victims, leaving nothing but cryptic clues and desperate townsfolk in their wake. As Mr. District Attorney and his trusted investigative team close in on the truth, the tension mounts with every interrogation, every false lead, and every tense confrontation. The crackling sound effects of gunfire echoing across empty plains, the weary determination in the District Attorney's voice, and the mounting dread of an unsolved case all converge in this masterfully crafted episode that exemplifies the show's signature blend of procedural precision and nail-biting suspense.

Mr. District Attorney was the thinking person's crime drama, a show that arrived during radio's golden age when listeners craved both entertainment and intellectual engagement. Unlike the pulpy detective serials of the era, this NBC/ABC juggernaut emphasized legal rigor and investigative methodology, making heroes not of trigger-happy gumshoes but of dedicated public servants bound by oath and conscience. The show's popularity reflected America's growing fascination with the machinery of justice itself—how evidence is gathered, how witnesses are questioned, how the system protects the innocent and punishes the guilty. With its rapid-fire dialogue and carefully plotted mysteries, Mr. District Attorney set the standard for legal dramas that would echo through television decades to come.

Tune in now to experience the desert heat, the moral certainty, and the meticulous detective work that made this program an essential part of America's evening radio schedule.
