# Case of the Lady Gamblers

Step into the smoky, neon-lit underworld of 1940s Manhattan as Mr. District Attorney takes on a case that promises intrigue, moral complexity, and the kind of danger that lurks behind closed doors and drawn shades. When a sophisticated ring of female gamblers begins bleeding the city's elite dry through high-stakes poker games and rigged wheels, the DA's office finds itself investigating a criminal operation run by women—a twist that scandalized audiences of the era and forced listeners to confront uncomfortable questions about ambition, desperation, and the lengths people will go for easy money. The familiar gravelly voice of Jay Jostyn guides us through a taut investigation filled with wiretapped conversations, double-crosses, and a climactic confrontation that cuts to the heart of organized vice in Depression-era America.

*Mr. District Attorney* was appointment radio—the show that premiered in 1939 and became NBC's crown jewel of legal drama, eventually transitioning to ABC where it thrived throughout the 1940s and early 1950s. What distinguished it from other crime shows was its meticulous attention to procedural detail and its unflinching examination of urban corruption. Each episode was ripped from the headlines of real cases, and the writing crackled with authenticity. By featuring criminal enterprises operated by women, this particular episode reflected a subtle shift in postwar attitudes, acknowledging that criminality knew no gender—a surprisingly progressive stance for mainstream radio entertainment.

Tune in to this gem of vintage radio drama and experience the craftsmanship that made *Mr. District Attorney* essential listening for millions of Americans. Whether you're a devoted fan of old-time radio or discovering this classic for the first time, the Case of the Lady Gamblers delivers everything that made the show legendary: sharp dialogue, compelling characters, and justice served with style.
