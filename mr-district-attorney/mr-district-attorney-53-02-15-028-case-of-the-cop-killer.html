When the gavel falls on this February evening in 1940, listeners will be thrust into one of the most explosive cases ever to cross the District Attorney's desk—a murder that strikes at the very heart of law enforcement itself. A police officer lies dead, gunned down in the darkness of the city streets, and the evidence points in a dozen different directions at once. The DA and his trusted investigator must navigate a labyrinth of false leads, criminal informants, and corrupt officials who would prefer the case remain unsolved. You'll hear the crackle of police radios, the urgent shuffle of papers in the DA's office, and the tense interrogations where truth and deception hang in the balance. This isn't a case for the faint of heart—it's a war of wits against an enemy embedded in the criminal underworld itself.

Mr. District Attorney became a cultural phenomenon because it spoke directly to Americans' faith in justice during turbulent times. Premiering in 1939, the show arrived just as the nation grappled with organized crime, corruption, and social upheaval. Unlike detective stories that glamorized private eyes, this series showcased the unglamorous but vital work of prosecutors and investigators grinding away in government offices. The realistic courtroom procedures and authentic legal language gave listeners a rare window into how justice actually worked. The show's narrator would famously intone that the D.A. represented "the people," reminding every listener that they had a stake in every verdict.

Step into the shadows of a crime scene where justice hangs by a thread and corruption lurks in unexpected places. Tune in to hear how the District Attorney prosecutes Case of the Cop Killer—a case that will test his integrity, his courage, and his absolute dedication to the people he serves.
