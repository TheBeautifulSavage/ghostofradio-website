# Mr. District Attorney: Case of the Wounded Killer

Picture yourself huddled around the radio on a crisp evening, the crackle of the speaker cutting through the living room silence as the D.A.'s office springs to life with purposeful urgency. In "Case of the Wounded Killer," listeners are thrust into a thrilling manhunt where the hunter becomes the hunted. A dangerous criminal, bleeding and desperate, leaves a trail of clues across the city—but is he fleeing justice, or walking into a trap? With each commercial break an eternity away, you'll find yourself hanging on every word as investigators close in, unaware that their quarry might be closer than they think. The tension mounts like steam in a pressure cooker as the D.A. himself takes the case, his measured voice cutting through the darkness with the precision of a prosecutorial blade.

*Mr. District Attorney* embodied everything Americans craved during the turbulent 1940s: justice swift and certain, heroic officials who stood against the criminal element, and proof that order could prevail in an uncertain world. Airing from 1939 to 1952, the show was groundbreaking in its procedural approach, educating listeners about actual legal processes while entertaining them with taut, believable narratives. Jay Jostyn's iconic portrayal of the D.A. made him a household name, and episodes like this showcased why—his delivery conveyed both compassion and unwavering moral authority that resonated with audiences desperate for reassurance during wartime and its aftermath.

Step back in time and experience the golden age of radio drama exactly as 1940s audiences did. "Case of the Wounded Killer" awaits—a perfectly preserved slice of vintage entertainment where danger lurks in every shadow and the law stands ready to restore order. Press play and let the mystery unfold.
