As the DA's investigation deepens, red herrings multiply and the pressure mounts. Is the killer a disgraced doctor? A jealous rival? Someone hiding in plain sight? Tune in for a masterclass in legal detective work that keeps you guessing until the final gavel falls.

Mr. District Attorney emerged as one of radio's most enduring crime dramas, running for thirteen years and captivating millions of Americans during an era when the airwaves delivered evening entertainment to families across the nation. The show's procedural approach—focusing on investigation, evidence, and courtroom drama rather than sensationalism—reflected a post-Depression hunger for authority figures who could restore order and justice. Sponsored by Colgate-Palmolive and starring Jay Jostyn as the determined DA, the program became appointment listening for those who craved intelligent, adult-oriented storytelling. Episodes like "The Silent Killer" showcased the writers' ability to craft intricate mysteries that rewarded attentive listeners while exploring the genuine complexities of law enforcement.
