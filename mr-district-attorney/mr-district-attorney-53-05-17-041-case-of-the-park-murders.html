As the opening fanfare crackles through your radio speaker on this May evening, you're about to step into one of the most chilling cases ever to cross the District Attorney's desk. A series of brutal murders has gripped the city, their victims discovered among the quiet pathways and shadowed groves of the municipal park—a place meant for leisurely strolls now transformed into a hunting ground. The D.A. and his trusted investigator, Harrington, must navigate a labyrinth of conflicting witness accounts, circumstantial evidence, and a killer whose motives remain shrouded in darkness. With each revelation, the noose of suspicion tightens around unexpected suspects, and listeners will find themselves wrestling with the same questions that plague our tenacious prosecutors: Who walks freely among us with murder in their heart? And have they already claimed their next victim?

Mr. District Attorney captured the imagination of millions during the golden age of radio, bringing real legal procedure and authentic crime-fighting drama into American living rooms. Unlike the sensationalized pulp detectives of the era, this show grounded itself in courtroom realism and investigative methodology, often drawing inspiration from actual case files. Throughout its thirteen-year run, it became must-listen entertainment for those fascinated by justice, procedure, and the thin line between guilt and innocence. The D.A. himself became an American archetype—the incorruptible public servant fighting for truth against the forces of crime and corruption.

Don't miss this gripping installment as justice hangs in the balance. Tune in and discover whether our District Attorney can unravel the mystery of the park murders before another innocent life is lost.
