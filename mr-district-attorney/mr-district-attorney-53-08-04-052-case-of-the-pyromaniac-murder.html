When the curtain rises on this taut episode from the golden age of radio drama, listeners are plunged into the smoky underworld of arson and murder most deliberate. The District Attorney's office crackles with urgency as investigators piece together a deadly puzzle: a raging inferno has claimed an innocent life, and somewhere in the city's shadowy corners, a dangerous pyromaniac walks free. With the unmistakable gravitas of Everett Sloane's commanding voice guiding us through the investigation, you'll hear the frantic shuffle of case files, the ring of telephones demanding answers, and the steady march of justice closing in on a killer who thought fire would conceal their crime. This is law and order at its most compelling—the cat-and-mouse game between society's protectors and those who prey upon the innocent.

Mr. District Attorney stood as one of radio's most authentic explorations of criminal justice, celebrated for its meticulous attention to legal procedure and its refusal to sensationalize. Premiering in 1939, the show became a cultural institution by bringing listeners inside the real machinery of prosecution—the careful detective work, the courtroom showdowns, and the moral weight of seeking justice. Episode 52 arrives during the show's peak years, when the writing was sharpest and the performances most electrifying, capturing that perfect moment when radio drama had mastered the art of building suspense through words alone.
