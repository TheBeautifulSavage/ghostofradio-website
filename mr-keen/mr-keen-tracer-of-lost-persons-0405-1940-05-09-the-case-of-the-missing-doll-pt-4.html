As Mr. Keen's footsteps echo through the shadowed corridors of Manhattan's Upper West Side, a young girl's fate hangs in the balance. In this fourth installment of "The Case of the Missing Doll," our intrepid tracer finds himself ensnared in a web of deception far more tangled than he anticipated. A cherished porcelain doll—seemingly insignificant to the casual observer—has become the key to unraveling a mystery involving blackmail, family secrets, and the desperation of a mother willing to do anything to recover her daughter's most prized possession. With each revelation, the stakes climb higher, and Mr. Keen must separate truth from fabrication among the conflicting testimonies of suspects whose motives remain frustratingly unclear. Will he crack the case before the trail goes cold?

Since its premiere in 1937, "Mr. Keen, Tracer of Lost Persons" has captivated millions of Americans with its gritty, realistic approach to detective work. Unlike the more theatrical mystery programs saturating the airwaves, this show grounds itself in the pedestrian yet compelling world of missing persons investigations—the bread and butter of actual private detective work. By 1940, the program had already established itself as a beloved institution, with listeners tuning in religiously to follow Keen's methodical investigations across the country. The show's success lay in its ability to transform ordinary people and everyday objects into instruments of compelling drama.

If you've been following this gripping four-part mystery, don't let impatience rob you of its resolution. Tune in Thursday evening for the thrilling conclusion, where Mr. Keen finally reveals the truth behind the missing doll and brings justice to those who deserve it.
