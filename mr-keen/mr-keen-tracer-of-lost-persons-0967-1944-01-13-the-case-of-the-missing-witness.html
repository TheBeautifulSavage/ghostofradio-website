When a crucial witness vanishes mere hours before testifying in a high-profile trial, Mr. Keen finds himself racing against the clock in this January 1944 episode that crackles with wartime tension and moral complexity. As the detective pieces together a web of false leads and desperate motives, listeners will discover that the missing witness may hold secrets far more dangerous than anyone suspected. With the trial's outcome hanging in the balance and shadowy figures working in the darkness, each clue draws Keen deeper into a labyrinth where justice itself seems to hang by a thread. The steady, methodical voice of Mr. Keen cuts through the murk of deception, his keen intellect our only light in an increasingly sinister case.

Mr. Keen, Tracer of Lost Persons achieved remarkable success during its seventeen-year run by tapping into a distinctly American fascination with the private detective—part mystery solver, part social worker. Broadcast during the war years, this 1944 episode reflects the anxieties of the period, where trust was currency and disappearances carried an added layer of dread. The show's enduring appeal lay in its focus on the human element: missing persons weren't mere plot devices, but real lives fractured by circumstance, desperation, and hidden shame. Unlike the flashy detective stories of pulp magazines, Mr. Keen offered something more thoughtful and grounded, earning devoted listeners who tuned in faithfully to NBC and CBS throughout the 1940s.

Don't miss this compelling cold case from the golden age of radio. Settle in with the crackle of static and the unmistakable sound of a mystery demanding to be solved—because in Mr. Keen's world, the truth always surfaces.
