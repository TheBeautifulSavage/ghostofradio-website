When Mr. Keen steps into his office on this January evening in 1944, he discovers a mystery wrapped in melody and menace. A young songstress has vanished without a trace, leaving behind only questions and the haunting memory of her voice. Was her talent her greatest gift—or her deadliest curse? As Keen methodically unravels the threads of her disappearance, listeners will find themselves drawn into a shadowy world of nightclub intrigue, jealous rivals, and dangerous secrets. The atmosphere crackles with tension as our protagonist pursues each lead with his characteristic thoroughness, piecing together a puzzle where every clue might bring him closer to the truth—or deeper into peril. In the tradition of the show's finest episodes, the mystery builds with each scene, each telephone call, each cryptic conversation bringing fresh complications to the case.

Mr. Keen, Tracer of Lost Persons stands as one of radio's most enduring detective programs, and this 1944 episode exemplifies why. Broadcast during America's wartime years, the show offered audiences a reassuring presence: a calm, methodical investigator who could navigate the moral complexities of urban life and restore the lost to their loved ones. Unlike the flashy action-adventure programs that dominated the dial, Mr. Keen relied on intelligence, persistence, and old-fashioned detective work. For nearly two decades, listeners trusted him with their anxieties about missing persons and unsolved mysteries—and he never failed to deliver satisfying answers rooted in careful observation and deduction.
