When a mysterious canine becomes the only witness to a crime that has baffled the police, Mr. Keen and his trusty assistant Mike Clancy must follow an impossible trail of paw prints and desperate clues across the fog-shrouded streets of the city. A fashionable family's prized setter, trained to jump through hoops at the local theater, bolts from its handler and vanishes into the night—but not before glimpsing something terrible. As Keen applies his legendary deductive methods to an animal's behavior, listeners will find themselves captivated by the detective's patient reasoning and the mounting tension of a case that hinges on understanding the silent language of a frightened dog. The 1944 broadcast crackles with authenticity, featuring the distinctive sound design that made Mr. Keen a must-listen across America: the clop of horse hooves, the screech of streetcar brakes, and the measured, confident voice of actor Bennett Kilpack as the world's first and greatest tracer of missing persons.

For nearly two decades, Mr. Keen, Tracer of Lost Persons captivated audiences with its singular premise—each week, ordinary people vanished, and only Keen's extraordinary ability to piece together human nature with forensic thinking could bring them home. Airing during the war years, when real families tuned in desperate for hope and resolution, the show became a cultural touchstone. Unlike other detective programs heavy with gunplay and melodrama, Keen's cases turned on psychology, observation, and relentless perseverance—qualities that resonated deeply with Americans enduring the uncertainties of 1944.

Step into the world of Mr. Keen for this genuinely inventive mystery where a dog's devotion becomes the key to justice. You won't forget the sound of those paws hitting pavement or the moment when Keen finally understands what his four-legged witness has been trying to tell him.
