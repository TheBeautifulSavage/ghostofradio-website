# The Case of the Ruthless Murderers

October 27th, 1949 brings one of the darkest cases yet to Mr. Keen's office, as the master tracer finds himself ensnared in a web of calculated violence and cold-blooded murder. When a desperate client arrives with blood on their hands and fear in their voice, Keen must navigate the treacherous line between justice and survival. The case spirals deeper with each clue uncovered, revealing a conspiracy far more sinister than anyone anticipated. Listeners will grip their radios as the ingenious detective pursues leads through shadowed alleyways and into the homes of the city's most dangerous criminals—where one wrong step means becoming another victim of ruthless killers who'll stop at nothing to protect their secrets.

*Mr. Keen, Tracer of Lost Persons* had captivated audiences since 1937, becoming one of radio's most enduring detective dramas. Unlike the wisecracks of *The Shadow* or the gadgetry of futuristic crime-fighters, Keen represented something uniquely appealing to Depression and post-war America: a methodical, intelligent man who solved mysteries through patient investigation and keen observation. By 1949, after more than a thousand episodes, the show had perfected its formula—combining genuine procedural police work with the intimate drama of human desperation. The production values were stellar, with sound effects that transported listeners directly into the world of modern detective work, from ringing telephones to slamming car doors.

Don't miss this gripping installment of radio's most reliable detective. Tune in and discover why *Mr. Keen, Tracer of Lost Persons* remained a listener favorite for eighteen years, delivering suspense that no visual medium could match.
