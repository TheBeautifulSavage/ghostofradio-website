When a young woman vanishes without a trace near the limestone caves of southern Indiana, Mr. Keen receives a desperate plea from her frantic mother—but the trail grows colder by the hour. As our intrepid tracer follows a labyrinth of clues through forgotten caverns and small-town secrets, he uncovers a decades-old murder that someone would kill to keep buried. With only the whisper of a dying man's confession and a mysterious locket bearing initials worn smooth by time, Keen must navigate treacherous terrain both literal and psychological in "The Forgotten Cave Murder Case." The suspense builds as the boundary between the missing girl's disappearance and the old crime becomes dangerously unclear—and Keen realizes that uncovering the past may be the only way to save her future.

For over a decade, Mr. Keen, Tracer of Lost Persons captivated millions of Americans with its unflinching exploration of human loss and redemption. Unlike the wisecracking private eyes that dominated radio, Keen embodied quiet determination and genuine compassion, treating each lost person as a human being with a family waiting desperately for answers. This November 1949 episode exemplifies the show's golden period, when writers crafted intricate mysteries that rewarded careful listeners with satisfying emotional payoffs. The program's authentic procedural elements—the painstaking detective work, the false leads, the crucial overlooked details—gave audiences a ringside seat to real investigative craft.

Settle into your favorite chair, adjust the dial to capture every whispered confession and ominous cave echo, and prepare yourself for an evening of genuine mystery. Mr. Keen awaits, ready to trace the threads connecting past sins to present danger. This is detective radio at its finest—suspenseful, humane, and utterly unforgettable.
