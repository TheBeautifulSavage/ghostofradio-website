# The Airport Murder Case

When the control tower lights flicker to life on a fog-shrouded evening in 1951, Mr. Keen receives an urgent call that will plunge him into one of the most baffling cases of his career. A prominent businessman lies dead in the private hangar of a major metropolitan airport, and the clues point in every direction—yet nowhere. As Keen and his trusty assistant Mike Clancy navigate the shadowy corridors of aircraft maintenance bays and interrogation rooms thick with cigarette smoke, listeners will find themselves drawn into a web of international intrigue, mistaken identities, and secrets that someone was willing to kill to protect. The ticking clock of a departing flight, the suspicious movements of well-dressed strangers, and the motive that lurks beneath a facade of respectability all converge in this gripping mystery that demands your complete attention from the opening gong to its startling resolution.

For nearly fifteen years, Mr. Keen, Tracer of Lost Persons had captivated millions of Americans huddled around their radios, establishing itself as one of the most reliable entries in the golden age of detective drama. By 1951, when this AFRS broadcast was recorded for American servicemen worldwide, the show had perfected its formula: sterling performances from leads Karl Swenson and Bennett Kilpack, sharp dialogue that crackled with authenticity, and mysteries that rewarded careful listening. The show's strength lay not in flash or sensationalism, but in its methodical, intelligent approach to detective work—Mr. Keen was a thinking man's hero for a thinking audience.

Don't miss "The Airport Murder Case," a masterclass in suspenseful radio drama that proves why Mr. Keen remained essential listening throughout the 1950s. Tune in and discover why generations of listeners made this broadcast appointment radio.
