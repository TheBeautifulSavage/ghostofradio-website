# The Case Of Murder At A Mile A Minute

Step into the velvet darkness of a December evening in 1951, where Mr. Keen's world moves at breakneck speed—literally. A body has surfaced along a railroad line, and the evidence suggests a killer who operates at sixty miles per hour. As the steady, commanding voice of Mr. Keen cuts through the static, listeners are thrust into a labyrinth of suspects and motives that hurtles forward as relentlessly as the locomotives that thunder through this mystery. The clacking of train wheels becomes both alibi and weapon in this masterfully crafted tale, where distance, timing, and alibi converge in a case that demands Mr. Keen's legendary powers of deduction.

By 1951, *Mr. Keen, Tracer of Lost Persons* had become an institution of American radio, having captivated audiences since 1937 with its promise of real detective work unfolding in real time. Unlike the costumed adventurers and hard-boiled private eyes populating the airwaves, Mr. Keen represented something more grounded and procedural—the methodical work of a man who finds the lost and solves the mysteries that plague ordinary people. This episode showcases the show's gift for transforming the mundane details of modern life—in this case, the railways that connected America—into the sinister backdrop for crime and suspense.

If you've never experienced the controlled urgency of Mr. Keen's investigations, this is the perfect entry point: a tight, ingeniously plotted mystery that unfolds with the precision of a timetable and the heart-pounding momentum of a runaway train. Tune in to hear how a single thread of evidence unravels an entire tapestry of deception, and discover why radio audiences made Mr. Keen a trusted companion in their homes for nearly two decades.
