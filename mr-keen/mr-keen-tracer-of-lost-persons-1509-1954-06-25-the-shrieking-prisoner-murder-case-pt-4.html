The walls of the county jail seem to close in tighter with each passing moment as Mr. Keen races against time to uncover the truth behind the mysterious death that has shaken the prison to its core. In this fourth installment of "The Shrieking Prisoner Murder Case," our resourceful tracer finds himself navigating a labyrinth of false confessions, hidden motives, and desperate inmates who may know far more than they're willing to admit. The unmistakable cry that echoes through the cellblock in the dead of night—that blood-curdling shriek that gives this case its chilling name—remains unexplained, and tensions boil over as guards and prisoners alike become suspects. With each clue examined and every lead pursued, Keen draws closer to the truth, but danger lurks in the shadows. Will he solve the mystery before another life is claimed?

Since its premiere in 1937, "Mr. Keen, Tracer of Lost Persons" has captivated audiences with its straightforward yet captivating formula: a man of modest means but extraordinary detective skills, armed with nothing but his wits and determination, solves the impossible. By the 1950s, the show had become a fixture of American radio, with listeners tuning in faithfully to follow Keen's meticulous investigations. This particular episode from June 25, 1954, exemplifies why the program endured through nearly two decades of broadcasting—combining genuine suspense with clever plotting and the authentic sound design that only radio could deliver.
