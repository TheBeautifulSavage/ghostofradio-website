On this chilling February evening in 1955, Mr. Keen ventures into the shadowy corners of Manhattan where the living and the dead seem perilously close to settling old scores. A young woman has vanished without a trace, leaving behind only whispered accusations of a phantom—a vengeful spirit said to walk the fog-laden streets seeking retribution for a murder buried deep in the past. As our intrepid tracer unravels the tangled threads of family secrets and decades-old betrayals, listeners will find themselves suspended in that delicious space between the rational and the supernatural, wondering whether Mr. Keen is pursuing a flesh-and-blood killer or chasing shadows. The eerie sound design—distant footsteps echoing through empty corridors, the mournful wail of wind through abandoned buildings—transforms your living room into a portal to a world where justice may come from beyond the grave.

For nearly two decades, Mr. Keen, Tracer of Lost Persons has captivated audiences with stories of mystery and human drama, establishing itself as one of radio's most dependable fixtures. This 1955 episode exemplifies the show's mature period, when the writers had perfected the art of weaving contemporary crime with gothic atmosphere, elevating the procedural detective story into something approaching genuine literature. At a time when television was beginning to lure audiences away from their radios, this episode showcases the irreplaceable power of audio drama—the ability to conjure entire worlds of suspense through voice and sound alone.
