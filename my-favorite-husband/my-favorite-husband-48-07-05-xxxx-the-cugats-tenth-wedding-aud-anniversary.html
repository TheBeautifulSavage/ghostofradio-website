# My Favorite Husband: "The Cugats' Tenth Wedding Anniversary"

Picture this: it's a sweltering summer evening in 1948, and you've settled into your favorite chair with the radio crackling to life just as Lucille Ball's voice cuts through the static with characteristic warmth and mischief. Tonight, Liz and Alvin Cugat face a domestic crisis of the highest order—their best friends' tenth anniversary party is tomorrow, and neither Liz nor her bewildered husband George can remember the gift they promised to bring. What unfolds is a rollicking half-hour of misunderstandings, scrambling solutions, and that particular brand of marital banter that made *My Favorite Husband* appointment listening for millions of Americans. As the Cugats' anniversary looms, so too does the threat of social embarrassment, forcing our protagonists into increasingly absurd schemes that spiral into genuine comic chaos. You can almost hear the studio audience erupting at each new twist, their laughter punctuating every punch line.

This episode captures the show at its cultural sweet spot, before Lucy would revolutionize television comedy with *I Love Lucy*. Here on radio, Ball was already perfecting the rapid-fire delivery and clever wordplay that would make her a legend, working opposite Richard Denning's everyman George, who serves as the perfect foil to her schemes. The show's domestic setting—with its focus on the everyday troubles of a married couple navigating money troubles, social obligations, and their own comedic incompetence—resonated deeply with post-war audiences building their own lives and households.

Tune in to this gem of radio comedy and discover why *My Favorite Husband* remains a testament to Lucy's gifts as a comedian, before the cameras started rolling.
