# My Favorite Husband: "Literary Clubs Speaker Comes To Dinner"

When the doorbell rings on this July evening in 1948, it signals the beginning of domestic chaos of the highest order. Lois Appleby has invited a prestigious literary speaker to dinner—a coup for her women's club that promises to elevate her social standing considerably. But her well-meaning husband George has other ideas, and as the hour approaches, disaster multiplies like rabbits. Will the roast survive the kitchen catastrophe? Can George master the art of civility before the guest arrives? Tune in as this delightful comedy unfolds with the snap and crackle of authentic 1940s wit, where every misunderstanding leads to another, and the threat of social embarrassment hangs over the Appleby household like a dark cloud about to burst.

*My Favorite Husband* arrived on CBS at precisely the right cultural moment—when post-war America was rediscovering domestic humor and the battle-of-wits between spouses became prime entertainment. Starring the irrepressible Lucille Ball before her television triumph, the show captured the spirit of American marriage with genuine warmth and intelligence. This particular episode exemplifies the show's genius: taking the smallest domestic scenario (a dinner party) and mining it for comedy gold while revealing the genuine affection beneath the bickering. The writing crackles with wordplay and physical comedy, translated through sound effects and expert comic timing.

Don't miss this gem from radio's golden age—a reminder of when sophistication, slapstick, and sentiment could coexist in thirty minutes of pure entertainment. Step into the Appleby home and experience the particular magic of classic radio comedy.
