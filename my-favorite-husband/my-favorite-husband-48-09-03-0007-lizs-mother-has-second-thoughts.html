Picture this: a modest Connecticut home on a crisp autumn evening, 1949. Liz Cugat receives a telephone call that sends her into a tailspin—her mother, whom she's convinced has finally accepted her marriage to the charming but perpetually hapless George, is suddenly having doubts. What follows is a delightful comedy of misunderstandings, desperate schemes, and the timeless struggle between a wife and her meddlesome mother-in-law, all unfolding in real time through the intimate medium of the radio microphone. As Liz frantically attempts to shore up her mother's confidence in her marriage, George stumbles innocently from one comic catastrophe to another, creating the perfect storm of domestic chaos that had millions of listeners tuning in weekly to hear what scrape the Cugats would find themselves in next.

This episode exemplifies what made My Favorite Husband such a phenomenon in post-war American radio. Starring the incomparable Lucille Ball before her television triumph, the show captured the anxieties and humor of young married life with remarkable authenticity. Broadcast during the golden age of domestic comedy, when the American family was being reshaped by returning servicemen and changing social dynamics, the program offered both escapism and recognition—audiences saw their own marriages reflected in the Cugats' gentle battles and tender reconciliations. The chemistry between Ball and co-star Richard Denning became legendary, their timing honed to perfection over 120 episodes.

Don't miss this charming slice of 1940s Americana. Tune in for thirty minutes of laughter, heart, and the peculiar wisdom that comes from two people learning to navigate married life—one comic misadventure at a time.
