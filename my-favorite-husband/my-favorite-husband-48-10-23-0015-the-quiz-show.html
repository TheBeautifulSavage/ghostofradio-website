# My Favorite Husband — "The Quiz Show" (October 23, 1948)

When Liz Cooper's dreams of winning big money on a radio quiz show collide with her husband George's competitive ego, domestic comedy reaches a fever pitch. In this delightful October episode, listeners will find themselves in the cozy, chaotic living room where a seemingly innocent application form becomes the spark for hilarious marital mayhem. As Liz prepares to test her knowledge on the air—with real prize money dangling before them—George's wounded pride threatens to derail everything. What begins as gentle ribbing between husband and wife escalates into a battle of wits and wills, complete with frantically whispered arguments, perfectly timed door slams, and the kind of bickering that only two people deeply in love can manage. The live studio audience roars with recognition at every moment, their laughter a testament to how acutely the show captures the tensions simmering beneath the surface of post-war American marriages.

This gem from 1948 represents the golden age of domestic sitcoms, when radio comedy thrived on the chemistry between husband and wife, and the real stakes of everyday life provided endless material for laughs. Lucille Ball and Richard Denning's performances crackle with genuine warmth and impeccable timing, setting a template that television comedy would eagerly embrace in the years to come. The show's rapid-fire dialogue and absurd predicaments became the blueprint for the domestic comedies that would dominate American entertainment throughout the 1950s and beyond.

Tune in for "The Quiz Show" and discover why audiences lined up at CBS studios week after week, eager to spend another evening in the Cooper household. It's vintage comedy that proves some arguments are simply too entertaining to miss.
