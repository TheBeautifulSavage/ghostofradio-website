# My Favorite Husband: Over Budget Beans

When Liz Cooper decides to stretch the household budget by serving beans three nights running, her hapless husband George discovers that domestic economy comes at a price—and not just the pennies saved at the grocer. This delightful episode captures the particular anxiety of post-war American marriage: the wife determined to manage a lean household budget, the husband yearning for his meat and potatoes, and the inevitable collision between good intentions and marital harmony. As the week progresses and beans appear in increasingly creative—and increasingly suspect—culinary guises, George's mounting desperation provides the perfect counterpoint to Liz's cheerful frugality. What begins as a simple cost-cutting measure spirals into misunderstanding and comedic chaos, with each character convinced of their own righteousness. The timing, the delivery, the shared exasperation between the leads—it's the stuff that made this show a fixture in living rooms across America.

*My Favorite Husband* arrived at precisely the moment American radio audiences craved it: as soldiers returned home and millions of couples learned to navigate married life under one roof again. Starring Lucille Ball and Richard Denning, the show transformed the simple premise of domestic conflict into something genuinely touching, grounded in the real pressures facing 1940s families. Ball's timing was already legendary, her gift for physical comedy translated through the microphone into vocal precision and impeccable comic instinct. Episodes like "Over Budget Beans" reflect authentic anxieties about money, rationing's lingering memory, and the delicate dance of compromise that marriage demanded.

Tune in to hear how George survives his week of legumes, and discover why this humble domestic sitcom paved the way for everything that followed—including Ball's later triumph on television.
