# My Favorite Husband: "Hair Dyed" (June 10, 1949)

Picture this: It's a lazy Friday evening in 1949, and you've just tuned in to CBS for your weekly dose of domestic bliss—or rather, domestic chaos. In this delightfully titled episode, hapless Richard Ames discovers that his wife Liz has done something drastic to her appearance, and what should be a simple compliment spirals into a hilarious misunderstanding that will keep you guessing until the final commercial break. The chemistry between our leads crackles with that perfect blend of exasperation and genuine affection, as Richard navigates the treacherous waters of commenting on his wife's new look. What starts as innocent small talk transforms into a comedy of errors, complete with misheard remarks, wounded pride, and desperate attempts at damage control. You can almost hear the studio audience roaring with laughter as the tension builds with each scene.

*My Favorite Husband* arrived at precisely the right cultural moment, capturing the post-war American household with witty precision. Starring Lucille Ball and Richard Denning, the show became a prototype for domestic sitcoms—establishing comedic patterns that would echo through television's golden age. The program's success on radio directly paved the way for Ball's later television triumph, making this seemingly humble CBS broadcast historically significant in entertainment history. These episodes showcase the kind of sharp, naturalistic dialogue and relatable marital mishaps that made radio comedy an essential part of American life.

Don't miss this vintage gem of domestic comedy. "Hair Dyed" is a perfect introduction to why millions of listeners made *My Favorite Husband* their weekly ritual, and why the show remains a treasured artifact of radio's golden age.
