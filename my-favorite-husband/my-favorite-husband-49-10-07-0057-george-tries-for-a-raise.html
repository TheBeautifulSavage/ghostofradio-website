# My Favorite Husband - "George Tries For A Raise"

Picture this: it's a crisp October evening in 1949, and George Cooper is pacing nervously before his mirror, rehearsing the speech of his life. He's decided—finally, *finally*—that it's time to march into his boss's office and demand the raise he so richly deserves. But as anyone who knows George will tell you, his grand plans rarely survive first contact with reality. What unfolds is a hilarious cascade of mishaps, misunderstandings, and marital mayhem as his wife Liz—sharp-tongued, quick-witted, and infinitely more capable than her bumbling husband—attempts to coach him toward success. Will George find the confidence to seal the deal, or will he bungle it spectacularly? Tune in to find out, as the sparkling dialogue crackles with that signature blend of romantic tension and domestic comedy that made this show an absolute sensation in living rooms across America.

*My Favorite Husband* was a groundbreaking program that proved domestic comedy could be sophisticated, witty, and genuinely funny without relying on slapstick or cheap gags. Starring the real-life husband-and-wife team of Richard Denning and Lucille Ball, the show was set in the affluent Connecticut suburbs and explored the genuine tensions and joys of modern married life with remarkable nuance for the era. It became a template for countless shows to follow and launched Ball's meteoric rise to stardom, eventually leading directly to the creation of *I Love Lucy* just a few years later.

Don't miss "George Tries For A Raise"—a perfectly polished gem from radio's golden age that reminds us why millions of Americans gathered around their receivers each week to escape into the warm, witty world of the Coopers. Available now for your listening pleasure.
