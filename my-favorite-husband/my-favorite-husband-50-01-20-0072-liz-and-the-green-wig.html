# My Favorite Husband: "Liz and the Green Wig"

Picture this: it's a quiet evening in the Cugat household when Liz stumbles upon a mysterious green wig—and suddenly, her ordinary Tuesday becomes anything but ordinary. What begins as innocent curiosity spirals into a delightful web of misunderstandings as George catches his wife modeling the outlandish hairpiece, leading him to wild (and hilariously wrong) conclusions about her intentions. As the episode unfolds, listeners will find themselves caught between Liz's exasperated attempts to explain herself and George's escalating jealousy, punctuated by perfectly timed commercial breaks and the warm laughter of the studio audience. The chemistry between the leads crackles with the kind of electric domestic banter that had millions of Americans tuning in during their dinner hour, eager to see what ridiculous predicament the Cugats would find themselves in next.

*My Favorite Husband* was groundbreaking television for the radio age—a show that transformed Lucille Ball from a supporting player into a comedic force of nature during its four-year CBS run. The program's genius lay in its refusal to sentimentalize marriage; instead, it presented wedded life as a series of lovingly absurd misadventures, where intelligence, quick wit, and physical comedy (expertly conveyed through vocal performance and sound effects) were the real weapons. This January 1950 episode exemplifies the show's formula at its finest: a simple premise, escalating chaos, and the underlying warmth that suggested these two characters would somehow stumble through to tomorrow still laughing.

For anyone seeking the roots of American comedy—the DNA of the sitcoms that followed—this episode is essential listening. Settle in, relax, and let the golden age of radio remind you why families once gathered around their sets, united in delighted anticipation of what Liz would do next.
