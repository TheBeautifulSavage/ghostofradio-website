# My Favorite Husband: "Liz Redecorates The House"

Picture this: It's a Tuesday evening in 1950, and you've settled into your favorite chair with the radio humming to life. Richard and Liz are at it again—this time, our favorite housewife has decided their modest home is desperately in need of a modern makeover, and poor George doesn't know what's about to hit him. What begins as an innocent discussion about fresh wallpaper and new curtains spirals into a domestic whirlwind of comedic chaos, with Liz's decorating ambitions clashing hilariously against George's very reasonable wallet. Listen as the couple navigates sample swatches, furniture disasters, and the eternal struggle between domestic bliss and marital survival—all wrapped in the warm, crackling intimacy of live studio performance.

*My Favorite Husband* was groundbreaking television in audio form, essentially inventing the domestic sitcom template that would later define American television. The show starred the real-life husband-and-wife team of Richard Denning and Lucille Ball, whose genuine chemistry and impeccable comic timing made the Carters' squabbles feel both universal and utterly fresh. Broadcast live from CBS studios with a studio audience roaring with laughter, these episodes captured the post-war American obsession with homeownership and modern living—a theme that clearly resonated with audiences hungry for relatable humor about everyday married life.

This episode is quintessential *My Favorite Husband*: a simple domestic premise that spirals into delightful absurdity, showcasing the verbal sparring and tender affection that made millions of listeners tune in week after week. If you've never experienced the golden age of radio comedy at its finest, or if you're a devoted fan seeking a favorite rediscovered, this gem deserves a place in your listening queue.
