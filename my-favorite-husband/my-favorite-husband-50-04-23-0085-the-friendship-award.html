Picture this: it's a lazy afternoon in the Cugat household, and Liz is absolutely convinced she deserves recognition for her unwavering devotion to her best friend—never mind that her "friendship" mostly consists of borrowing clothes, dispensing unsolicited advice, and generally creating absolute chaos. When word arrives that a local civic group is handing out a prestigious Friendship Award, Liz becomes obsessed with winning it, dragging poor George into an increasingly absurd campaign that includes staged acts of generosity, carefully timed chance encounters, and enough scheming to make her friend deeply suspicious. As the episode unfolds, listeners will be treated to the rapid-fire banter, escalating misunderstandings, and perfectly timed domestic squabbles that made this show an absolute sensation in American living rooms.

Starring the incomparable Lucille Ball as Liz, with Richard Denning as her perpetually bewildered husband George, My Favorite Husband was groundbreaking radio comedy that proved domestic situations—especially those filtered through a woman's decidedly unorthodox perspective—could be endlessly entertaining. Airing on CBS from 1948 to 1951, the show became a testing ground for the comedic talents that would later dominate television, particularly Ball's genius for physical comedy translated into expert verbal delivery. This particular episode, from April 23rd, 1950, exemplifies what made the series so beloved: it takes the most mundane marital scenario imaginable and spins it into pure comedic gold through sheer force of personality and brilliant scriptwriting.

So settle in, adjust your radio dial, and prepare yourself for twenty-eight minutes of laughter, exasperation, and the kind of domestic mayhem that only true love—and true comedy—can survive.
