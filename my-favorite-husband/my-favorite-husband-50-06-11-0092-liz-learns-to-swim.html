# My Favorite Husband – "Liz Learns To Swim"

Dive into a splash of comedic chaos as Liz Cugat decides it's high time she conquered the swimming pool—much to her husband George's bewilderment and considerable exasperation. What begins as an innocent summer afternoon quickly spirals into hilarious misadventure when Liz's determined efforts to master the backstroke collide with George's well-meaning but hopelessly muddled attempts at instruction. Expect slapstick encounters with pool attendants, a parade of interruptions from nosy neighbors, and the kind of domestic friction that only a husband and wife can generate when pride meets panic in the shallow end. The stakes are delightfully low, yet somehow everything feels urgently important—a signature touch of the show's warmth and humor.

*My Favorite Husband* was a groundbreaking entry in the domestic comedy canon during the golden age of radio, pioneering the affectionate marital bickering that would later define television sitcoms for generations. Airing on CBS from 1948 to 1951, the show starred Lucille Ball in one of her most celebrated radio roles, opposite Richard Denning as George. The chemistry between the leads was electric and naturalistic, capturing the genuine give-and-take of real marriage—the eye-rolling moments, the tender compromises, the absolute conviction that your spouse is thoroughly ridiculous yet utterly irreplaceable. This June 1950 episode exemplifies exactly why audiences tuned in weekly, eager to eavesdrop on the Cugats' endearingly relatable troubles.

Don your imaginary swimming cap and settle in for an evening of timeless humor. This episode reminds us why radio comedy remains so potent: with nothing but voices, sound effects, and expertly timed comic writing, the intimate world of marriage becomes a stage for both universal truth and pure, unfiltered fun.
