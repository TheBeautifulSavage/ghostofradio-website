# My Favorite Husband: The Football Game

Picture this: it's a crisp autumn evening in 1950, and Liz Cooper is determined to attend the big game with her husband George—only George has made other plans, and he's not particularly eager to break them. What follows is a delightful spiral of domestic scheming, misunderstandings, and the kind of rapid-fire banter that made America fall in love with this couple week after week. Lucille Ball, in her pre-*I Love Lucy* prime, delivers every comedic line with impeccable timing, while Richard Denning's George serves as the perfect foil to her schemes. The supporting cast buzzes with energy as Liz enlists friends, concocts elaborate excuses, and pursues her quarry with the determination of a championship team itself. You can practically hear the studio audience roaring with laughter as the situation spirals into absurdity—because in the Cooper household, even a simple football game becomes an adventure.

*My Favorite Husband* was the laboratory where Lucille Ball perfected the comedic techniques that would revolutionize television just a few years later. Running from 1948 to 1951 on CBS, this domestic sitcom proved that audiences craved smart, fast-paced humor centered around the battle of wits between husband and wife. Ball's performance here showcases the physicality and verbal dexterity that would make her legendary, while the show's writing set the template for countless domestic comedies to follow. This episode, recorded in that golden age of radio when comedians could push boundaries and experiment freely, captures something irreplaceable—live performance without a net.

Don't miss this chance to hear comedy royalty in her element. *My Favorite Husband* reminds us why radio was theater for the mind, and why Lucille Ball became one of entertainment's greatest treasures.
