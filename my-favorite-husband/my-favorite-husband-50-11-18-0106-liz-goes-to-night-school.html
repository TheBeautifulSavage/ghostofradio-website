# My Favorite Husband: "Liz Goes to Night School"

Picture the cozy Henderson living room on a crisp autumn evening in 1950, where domestic tranquility is about to collide with ambition. When Elizabeth decides to enroll in night school to better herself, her well-meaning but perpetually befuddled husband George finds his comfortable routine completely upended. What begins as enthusiastic support quickly devolves into comedic chaos as Liz juggles housework, marriage, and her newfound studies, while George stumbles through increasingly hilarious attempts to manage the household alone. Will he burn down the kitchen? Will Liz pass her exams? Tune in for sharp dialogue, perfectly timed physical comedy, and the kind of heartfelt domestic squabbles that made America laugh during the golden age of radio.

This episode captures exactly why *My Favorite Husband* became one of CBS Radio's most beloved programs—a show that balanced genuine affection between its characters with razor-sharp comedy that spoke to post-war American anxieties. Starring real-life couple Lucille Ball and Richard Denning, the program explored the modern marriage with surprising sophistication for the era, touching on women's independence and self-improvement even as it wrapped these themes in hilarious misunderstandings. Though overshadowed today by Ball's later television fame, this radio series was where she honed the comedic timing that would make her a legend, proving that radio audiences craved smart, character-driven humor alongside their domestic situations.

Whether you're a devoted radio enthusiast or discovering the golden age of broadcasting for the first time, this charming episode reminds us why millions tuned in faithfully each week. The writing sparkles, the ensemble cast shines, and the stakes—though played for laughs—feel genuinely important to the characters we've come to love. Don't miss "Liz Goes to Night School."
