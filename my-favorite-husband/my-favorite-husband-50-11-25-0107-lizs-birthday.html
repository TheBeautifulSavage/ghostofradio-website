# My Favorite Husband: "Liz's Birthday" (November 25, 1950)

Picture yourself settling into your favorite armchair on a Saturday evening, the warm glow of your radio cabinet illuminating the living room as Lucille Ball's voice crackles through the speaker with that unmistakable blend of exasperation and charm. In this delightful episode, poor George Cugat finds himself in hot water once again—he's forgotten his wife's birthday, and Liz is determined to teach him a lesson he won't soon forget. What unfolds is a masterclass in domestic comedy as misunderstandings pile upon misunderstandings, complete with the rapid-fire banter and physical comedy that made audiences roar with laughter week after week. You'll find yourself caught between sympathy for George's increasingly desperate attempts at redemption and admiration for Liz's cleverly orchestrated revenge, all wrapped up in the cozy, chaotic warmth of 1950s married life.

This episode captures the golden age of radio comedy at its absolute peak, featuring the creative partnership between Lucille Ball and her real-life husband Desi Arnaz, whose chemistry was simply electric. "My Favorite Husband" served as the direct precursor to the couple's revolutionary television phenomenon "I Love Lucy," and you can hear why—the writing is sharp, the timing impeccable, and the performances bursting with energy that would soon captivate millions of television viewers. These radio episodes represent a crucial chapter in entertainment history, where Ball honed the comedic instincts that would make her a legend.

Tune in now and experience the wit, warmth, and wonderful chaos that made America fall in love with Lucille Ball. This is radio comedy as it was meant to be heard—intimate, immediate, and utterly infectious.
