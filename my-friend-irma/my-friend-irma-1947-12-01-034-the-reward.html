Picture yourself settling into your favorite chair on a crisp December evening, the warm glow of your radio set casting familiar shadows across the living room. This week's installment of My Friend Irma promises the kind of chaos that has made CBS's breakout comedy the nation's guilty pleasure—when Irma stumbles upon a substantial reward, the stage is set for mayhem. Will her dimwitted optimism lead her to fortune, or will her schemes unravel in spectacular fashion? Marie Wilson's rubber-faced delivery of madcap dialogue has become appointment listening for millions, and alongside her befuddled friend Jane and the hapless Al, she's about to embark on yet another wild goose chase that will keep you laughing through the commercial breaks.

Just months into its network run, My Friend Irma has already become a phenomenon, transforming Marie Wilson from supporting player to radio star. This 1947 episode captures the show at the height of its fresh appeal—before it becomes a film franchise, before it dominates the airwaves for seven consecutive seasons. The chemistry between Wilson and co-star Cathy Lewis crackles with the ease of performers still discovering their characters' rhythms, and the show's writers are confidently mining the rich territory between romantic misadventure and slapstick nonsense that will define the era's domestic comedies.

Tune in this evening to experience a slice of post-war America's entertainment landscape, when radio comedy meant ensemble casts, snappy banter, and the kind of innocent mayhem that had the whole country tuning in to hear what Irma would do next. Press play and step back into 1947.
