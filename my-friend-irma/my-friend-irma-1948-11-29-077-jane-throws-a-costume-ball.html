# My Friend Irma: Jane Throws A Costume Ball

Picture this: it's late November, 1948, and America is settling into its living rooms as the golden glow of radio fills the darkened parlors of millions. Tonight, listeners will follow the irrepressible Jane, Irma's sophisticated friend, as she attempts to throw the season's most glamorous costume ball—a plan that, naturally, goes delightfully awry. What begins as an elegant evening of masked revelry quickly devolves into comedic chaos when Irma's well-intentioned meddling and the romantic entanglements of the gang conspire to turn high society into hilarious mayhem. Expect mistaken identities, slapstick misunderstandings, and Marie Wilson's priceless delivery as Irma stumbles through every carefully laid plan Jane has orchestrated.

*My Friend Irma* thrived during these post-war years when Americans craved wholesome comedy and the comfort of familiar characters week after week. The show's success lay in its ensemble cast—Wilson's dizzy charm balanced perfectly against the straighter-man antics of her foils—and its ability to find humor in the everyday social anxieties of ordinary people trying to navigate romance, friendship, and ambition. By 1948, the show was hitting its stride, having found the perfect rhythm between physical comedy and witty repartee that would keep audiences tuning in faithfully for six seasons.

The writing and performances capture a specific moment in American popular culture: sophisticated yet accessible, innocent yet knowing, speaking to audiences who understood both the aspirations of upward mobility and the universal comedy of human error. Whether you're a devoted fan of classic radio or discovering this gem for the first time, this November broadcast promises the perfect blend of charm, chaos, and the kind of laughter that defined an era.
