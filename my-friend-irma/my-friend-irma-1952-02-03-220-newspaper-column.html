# My Friend Irma: Newspaper Column (February 3, 1952)

Step into the apartment of America's favorite scatterbrained heroine as Irma Peterson sets her sights on becoming a newspaper columnist—with predictably hilarious results. This February 1952 episode crackles with the rapid-fire wit and physical comedy that made *My Friend Irma* appointment listening for millions. As Irma's best friend Jane exasperatedly watches from the sidelines, our lovable disaster-magnet attempts to pen a gossip column that might just expose every secret in town—including a few that should have stayed buried. The dialogue snaps like a whip, filled with double-takes, misunderstandings, and the kind of comedic timing that only works when you're imagining it all in your head, letting your mind paint the scene in glorious detail.

By 1952, *My Friend Irma* had become a cultural phenomenon, spawning two Hollywood films and cementing Marie Wilson's Irma as the prototypical "dumb blonde" of the era—though listeners knew better. The show's success lay in Wilson's impeccable delivery and her ability to make Irma's illogical logic somehow charming and endearing. In an age before television dominated American living rooms, radio comedy demanded sharp writing and sharper performances. This episode captures the show at its peak, when CBS had perfected the formula: take one innocent girl, add one exasperated best friend, season with romantic entanglements and workplace chaos, and let the comedy simmer until something absolutely ridiculous boils over.

Don't miss this gem of early 1950s radio comedy. Tune in and discover why America fell in love with Irma—and why her brand of lovable chaos still enchants listeners today.
