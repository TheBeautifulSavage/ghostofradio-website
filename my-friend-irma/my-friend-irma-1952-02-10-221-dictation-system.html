# My Friend Irma: "Dictation System" — February 10, 1952

Step into the bustling world of 1950s Manhattan as Jane and her dizzy roommate Irma stumble headlong into yet another hilarious predicament involving the latest office technology. When a newfangled dictation system arrives at their workplace, what could possibly go wrong? Everything, naturally—and in the most delightfully unexpected ways. This episode crackles with the rapid-fire banter and physical comedy that made *My Friend Irma* a must-listen for millions of Americans huddled around their radio sets. Listen as misunderstandings multiply, secrets are inadvertently recorded, and the mechanical marvel of modern office equipment becomes the unwitting conspirator in comedy gold. The supporting cast—including Irma's dim-witted but lovable boyfriend Al—weaves seamlessly through a plot that careens from one comedic catastrophe to the next, perfectly capturing the post-war American obsession with efficiency and automation, even as Irma's spectacular incompetence proves that some things can never be streamlined.

For five seasons, *My Friend Irma* dominated CBS's comedy lineup, launching the careers of Marie Wilson (as the wonderfully dim Irma) and Diana Lynn as the sensible foil Jane. The show's success was so tremendous it spawned two Hollywood films and proved that radio audiences had an insatiable appetite for the clash between urban sophistication and lovable stupidity. This 1952 episode sits at the show's creative peak, when the writers had perfected the formula of placing Irma in situations that gleefully mocked modern progress while celebrating the timeless appeal of human folly.

Tune in to experience why *My Friend Irma* remains an enduring classic of radio comedy—where every episode promised genuine laughs and the irresistible charm of simpler times.
