# My Friend Irma: Lost Friendship Ring (February 24, 1952)

Picture yourself settling into your favorite chair on a winter evening in 1952, the amber glow of your radio dial glowing warmly in the darkened living room. As the familiar opening theme swells, you're transported back to the modest apartment where chaos and laughter collide in equal measure. In this week's episode, a seemingly simple lost friendship ring spirals into a comedy of errors that will leave you breathless with laughter. Jane and Irma's apartment becomes ground zero for misunderstandings and wild accusations as the girls search desperately for the missing token—but of course, nothing goes according to plan. Al and the boys get involved in ways that only make matters worse, and before you know it, the entire neighborhood is caught up in the mayhem. You'll find yourself gripping the armrest, wondering how on earth this situation could possibly be resolved, all while laughing at the perfectly timed comedic beats that made this show a national phenomenon.

*My Friend Irma* occupied a unique space in American radio during the early 1950s—a show that proved women could carry a comedy without relying on slapstick or demeaning tropes. Marie Wilson's portrayal of the lovably dim Irma and Virginia Gregg's sensible Jane created a dynamic that audiences adored for seven consecutive years. By 1952, the show had already spawned two successful films and was at the height of its popularity, drawing millions of listeners who tuned in weekly to escape post-war anxieties through the gentle chaos of these ordinary characters.

Don't miss this delightful chapter in radio's golden age. Tune in now and rediscover why *My Friend Irma* remained one of CBS's most beloved comedies—where friendship always wins, even when a friendship ring goes missing.
