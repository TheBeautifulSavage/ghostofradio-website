# My Friend Irma - "Irma Gets A Raise" (January 27, 1953)

Picture yourself in a modest Manhattan apartment on a crisp winter evening in 1953, tuning your radio dial to CBS just as the familiar theme song swells. This week, our lovably scatter-brained Irma is on a mission—she's determined to wrangle a raise from her boss, and what follows is a masterclass in comedic chaos. With her best friend Jane looking on in bemused exasperation, Irma devises a scheme so elaborate and wonderfully misguided that it spirals into situations only she could manufacture. The interplay between Marie Wilson's breathy, ingenuous delivery and the supporting cast creates an atmosphere of warm domestic comedy that had millions of listeners chuckling in their living rooms, kitchens, and bedrooms across America.

By 1953, *My Friend Irma* had become a cultural institution—a show that launched careers, spawned two feature films, and proved that radio comedy didn't require elaborate production values or highbrow humor. What made the series special was its brilliant simplicity: the chemistry between its leads, the snappy writing that balanced slapstick with genuine heart, and Marie Wilson's unforgettable portrayal of Irma, the well-meaning but perpetually misguided girl next door. This episode arrives during the show's peak popularity, when radio was still America's evening entertainment and comedy shows like this were can't-miss listening events for families gathered around their sets.

Whether you're a devoted fan revisiting a favorite program or discovering *My Friend Irma* for the first time, this episode exemplifies everything that made the show beloved for seven glorious years. Don't miss it—tune in and let Irma's latest escapade remind you why classic radio comedy remains so utterly charming and thoroughly entertaining.
