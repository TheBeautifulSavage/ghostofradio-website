# My Friend Irma - June 30, 1953

Step into the sweltering heat of a New York summer, where Jane and her irrepressible roommate Irma have reached their breaking point. The apartment is stifling, the fans are useless, and Irma's solution to every problem is about to turn their lives upside down. When she decides to purchase an air conditioner, what could possibly go wrong? Everything, naturally. This episode crackles with the kind of domestic chaos that made *My Friend Irma* a must-listen across America—Irma's schemes collide with reality in the most hilarious ways, and poor Jane is caught in the middle, desperately trying to maintain some semblance of sanity while her roommate transforms their humble apartment into absolute pandemonium. The writers masterfully weave together physical comedy, snappy dialogue, and perfectly-timed punchlines that somehow come alive even more vividly through sound alone, with the warm chemistry between the cast elevating every absurd moment.

By 1953, *My Friend Irma* had already cemented its place as one of radio's most beloved comedies, a show that bridged vaudeville traditions with modern broadcasting sensibilities. Marie Wilson's portrayal of the ditzy but endearing Irma had spawned two feature films and spawned countless imitators, yet the radio program remained the heart of the franchise. This episode captures the show at its zenith—during the golden age of network radio's final years, when comedies like this one offered a weekly escape from the anxieties of the atomic age. The AFRS (Armed Forces Radio Service) transcription preserved here allowed servicemen stationed worldwide to share in the same laughter enjoyed by millions of stateside listeners.

Tune in now and discover why America couldn't get enough of Irma and Jane's misadventures. This classic comedy gem awaits—no air conditioning required.
