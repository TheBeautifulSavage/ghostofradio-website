# My Friend Irma: "Mr. Clyde Divorcing"

April 27th, 1954 brings another hilarious crisis to the apartment shared by our favorite scatterbrained heroine and her more level-headed best friend Jane. When Mr. Clyde announces he's divorcing, pandemonium erupts—and listeners familiar with Irma's particular brand of well-intentioned chaos know exactly what that means. What begins as simple neighborly concern quickly spirals into a series of increasingly absurd misunderstandings, romantic entanglements, and comedic misadventures that only Irma could orchestrate. Will she inadvertently save the marriage? Sabotage it further? Or create an entirely new romantic catastrophe in the process? The snappy dialogue crackles with the kind of rapid-fire wit that made this show a weekday essential for millions of Americans, while the supporting cast brings depth and genuine heart beneath the laughs.

*My Friend Irma* thrived during the golden age of situation comedy, when radio comedy had evolved beyond simple gags into character-driven narratives that audiences genuinely cared about. Marie Wilson's iconic portrayal of the lovably dim Irma became one of the era's most beloved characters, proving that comedy could be intelligent, fast-paced, and completely character-centered. By 1954, the show had already spawned two motion pictures and demonstrated remarkable staying power against increasingly stiff television competition. This episode captures the show at its creative peak, when the writing had been perfectly honed and the ensemble cast moved together with practiced precision.
