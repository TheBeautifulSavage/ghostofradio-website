# My Friend Irma: Al Manages Murderin' Mushy

Picture this: it's late evening, the living room bathed in the soft glow of your radio dial, and you're about to witness one of radio's most chaotic love triangles spiral into absolute hilarity. When Al decides to take matters into his own hands and "manage" the romantic entanglements of the neighborhood's most melodramatic suitor, Murderin' Mushy, you know disaster is just a commercial break away. The rapid-fire dialogue crackles with the kind of screwball energy that made audiences across America howl with laughter—misunderstandings pile upon misunderstandings, schemes backfire spectacularly, and poor Al finds himself in increasingly ridiculous situations, all while trying to salvage what little dignity he has left. Marie Wilson's performance as the scatterbrained Irma remains the beating heart of the show, but it's the supporting cast's chemistry that elevates this episode into comedic gold.

*My Friend Irma* arrived on CBS in 1947 at the perfect moment in American radio history, capturing the post-war appetite for lighthearted domestic comedy. The show became an instant sensation, proving that audiences craved the kind of warm, character-driven humor that didn't require belly laughs so much as knowing smiles. What made *Irma* special was its willingness to explore the mundane absurdities of everyday life with genuine affection for its characters, no matter how ridiculous their predicaments became. This AFRS transcription represents the show at its creative peak, preserving not just comedy but a snapshot of an era when radio was America's primary source of entertainment.
