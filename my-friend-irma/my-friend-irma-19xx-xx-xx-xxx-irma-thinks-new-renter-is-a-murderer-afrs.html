# My Friend Irma: "Irma Thinks New Renter Is A Murderer"

Picture this: it's a quiet evening in the boarding house, and Irma's overactive imagination is about to turn an innocent Tuesday into a comedy of errors. When a mysterious new tenant moves into the building, Irma becomes absolutely convinced that he's a fugitive from justice—or worse. What follows is a hilarious cascade of misunderstandings, wild accusations, and slapstick mishaps as Irma drags her long-suffering roommate Jane into an amateur detective caper. With each new "clue"—a suspicious newspaper clipping, a cryptic phone call, an ordinary suitcase—Irma's certainty grows more unshakeable, even as the evidence crumbles around her. The tension builds beautifully between genuine peril and pure comedy as the situation spirals deliciously out of control.

*My Friend Irma* captured the hearts of millions during radio's golden age, becoming one of CBS's most beloved programs throughout the late 1940s. The show's genius lay in its perfect balance: endearing characters facing genuinely funny situations without ever feeling mean-spirited. Irma's breathless naïveté and misguided logic made her simultaneously exasperating and utterly lovable, while the supporting cast—especially the patient, sardonic Jane—provided the perfect foil. This episode exemplifies why audiences tuned in faithfully each week, eager to discover what peculiar jam Irma would get into next. The show's success even spawned a feature film and became a cultural touchstone of the era.
