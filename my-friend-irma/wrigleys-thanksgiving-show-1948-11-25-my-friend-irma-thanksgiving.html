Gather 'round the wireless this Thanksgiving evening and prepare yourself for a hilarious domestic disaster in the making. When Jane and her scatterbrained roommate Irma attempt to orchestrate the perfect holiday dinner, chaos erupts faster than you can say "pass the gravy." From the moment Irma volunteers to handle the turkey—much to Jane's immediate regret—the evening spirals into a comedy of errors involving burned stuffing, mysterious telephone callers, and Irma's latest romantic entanglement with Al. This special Wrigley's-sponsored broadcast captures the golden warmth of the season while delivering the rapid-fire wisecracks and physical comedy that made this show America's favorite comedy program. The entire cast crackles with energy as they navigate burnt pies, unexpected guests, and Irma's well-intentioned but catastrophically misguided attempts to save the day.

Since debuting on CBS in 1947, My Friend Irma has become a cultural phenomenon, spawning not only this beloved radio program but also two feature films and countless imitators. The show's appeal lies in its perfect blend of gentle, accessible humor rooted in the everyday struggles of working women in post-war America. Marie Wilson's iconic portrayal of the delightfully dim but lovable Irma, alongside Cathy Lewis's exasperated Jane, captured the anxieties and dreams of a generation finding its footing in peacetime. This Thanksgiving episode represents the show at its peak popularity, when millions of listeners tuned in weekly to escape into Irma's world of romantic mix-ups and comedic misunderstandings.

Don't miss this festive installment that combines genuine heart with timeless laughter. Whether you're nostalgic for classic radio or discovering this gem for the first time, this Thanksgiving special promises the perfect blend of holiday spirit and vintage entertainment to warm your evening.
