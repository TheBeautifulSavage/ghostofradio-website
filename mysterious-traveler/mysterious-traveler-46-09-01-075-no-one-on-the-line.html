# The Mysterious Traveler: "No One On The Line"

As your radio crackles to life on this September evening, the familiar silhouette of the Mysterious Traveler materializes from the static, his voice cutting through the darkness like a blade. Tonight's tale draws you into a suffocating world of crossed wires and phantom voices—a mystery that unfolds entirely through the telephone receiver pressed to an unsuspecting victim's ear. What begins as routine business calls transforms into something sinister when the voice on the other end knows far too much, speaks of things impossible to know, and seems determined to unravel the listener's carefully constructed secrets. With each ring, the tension mounts. With each conversation, reality becomes less certain. The Traveler's introduction promises you won't believe what happens, and for once, the promise feels uncomfortably credible.

*The Mysterious Traveler* arrived on Mutual Broadcasting in 1943 at precisely the moment American audiences craved intelligent suspense without the comedic relief that plagued other anthology series. By September 1946, when this episode aired, the show had already secured its reputation as one of radio's most sophisticated thrillers, featuring genuinely unsettling premises that lingered long after the final fade-out. Unlike its competitors, the series eschewed jump-scares for psychological dread, trusting listeners' imaginations to conjure horrors far more terrifying than any sound effect could produce. The Traveler himself—that enigmatic figure who introduces each tale—became radio's answer to the campfire storyteller, an ageless presence who'd witnessed the darkness lurking beneath ordinary life.

Don't miss your appointment with the Mysterious Traveler. "No One On The Line" awaits, and somewhere, a telephone is ringing—can you answer it?
