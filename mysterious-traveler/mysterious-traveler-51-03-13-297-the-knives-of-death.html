# The Knives Of Death

Steel gleams in candlelight as a man's fate tightens like a noose. When a mysterious traveler arrives at a remote estate on a fog-shrouded evening, he brings with him knowledge of a terrible secret—one written in blood across the pages of an old family history. A collection of ornate knives, each with its own sinister story, becomes the centerpiece of a tale where revenge, madness, and superstition intertwine with deadly precision. As shadows lengthen and the household becomes increasingly gripped by fear, listeners will find themselves wondering: are these instruments of death merely steel and bone, or do they carry something far more sinister? The tension crackles like static through the airwaves, building toward a revelation that cuts straight to the heart of human desperation.

*The Mysterious Traveler* stands as one of radio's most enduring masterpieces, a show that perfected the art of the psychological thriller during its remarkable nine-year run on the Mutual network. Each episode opens with the same hypnotic announcement—"Submitted for your approval: a case of the unusual"—before plunging listeners into a world of carefully constructed dread. The program's genius lay in its willingness to explore not just external menace, but the corruption that festers within the human soul. "The Knives of Death" exemplifies this approach, crafting a narrative where the true horror stems not from supernatural forces, but from the very human capacity for obsession and violence.

For anyone seeking an authentic glimpse into the golden age of radio drama, this episode remains essential listening. The superb writing, atmospheric sound design, and performances of a bygone era come together to create a listening experience that transcends nostalgia—this is thrilling entertainment, pure and simple. Tune in and discover why audiences huddled around their radios nearly eighty years ago, captivated by the voice of a stranger bearing dark tidings.
