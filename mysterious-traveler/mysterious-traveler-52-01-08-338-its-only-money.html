# It's Only Money

Picture this: a cold January evening in 1952, and you're settling into your favorite chair as the organ swells and that familiar voice cuts through the darkness—*"Good evening, friends. I am the Mysterious Traveler..."* Tonight's tale, "It's Only Money," will chill your bones with a deceptively simple premise: a man's obsession with wealth and a stranger's uncanny knowledge of his darkest secrets. What begins as a chance encounter spirals into a nightmare of moral reckoning, where every promise of fortune comes with an invisible price tag. You'll find yourself questioning whether greed is truly the root of all evil, or merely the soil in which evil takes root. The masterful pacing builds relentlessly, leading to a twist that reframes everything you thought you understood in those final, devastating moments.

*The Mysterious Traveler* carved out a unique niche during radio's golden age by distilling the anthology format to its purest essence—stories of ordinary people confronting extraordinary consequences. Premiering on the Mutual Network in 1943, the show became legendary for eschewing the supernatural in favor of psychological terror and moral philosophy. Each episode presented a stranger who appeared at crucial junctures in people's lives, seemingly orchestrating cosmic justice for those who'd strayed from virtue. By 1952, when this episode aired, the show had perfected its formula: atmospheric production design, understated performances, and scripts that lingered in the listener's mind long after the final fadeout.

Don't miss this masterpiece of suspense. Tune in for "It's Only Money" and discover why *The Mysterious Traveler* remains one of radio's most thought-provoking thrillers—where every episode asks: what price would you pay for what you want most?
