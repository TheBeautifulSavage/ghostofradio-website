When the clock strikes midnight and a desperate man appears at Nick Carter's office with a bullet in his chest and murder in his past, the Master Detective has precisely nine hours to unravel a twisted web of blackmail, betrayal, and vengeance—before his mysterious client expires from his wound. In this taut installment of detective drama, listeners will experience the claustrophobic tension of a race against time, punctuated by sharp dialogue crackles, the ambient hum of 1940s New York, and the measured, methodical voice of Nick Carter as he pieces together clues with surgical precision. Every minute counts as motives multiply, suspects emerge from the shadows, and the listener must follow Carter's brilliant deductions through dimly lit apartments, late-night telephone calls, and dangerous encounters that could prove fatal.

Nick Carter had already been a legendary character for nearly a century when this Mutual broadcast series premiered—a pulp magazine hero who had captivated readers since the 1880s, now adapted for the golden age of radio. The show's enduring popularity through the 1940s and 1950s reflected America's appetite for cerebral mystery drama, where the detective's intellect triumphed over mere gunplay. This particular episode, "Nine Hours To Live," exemplifies the series' mastery of compressed storytelling: complex plots unfolding within 30 minutes of broadcast time, requiring listeners to stay alert and engaged as Carter navigates a labyrinth of secrets before the final commercial break.

If you appreciate detective fiction where intelligence and intuition intertwine, where every clue matters and the clock is always ticking, step into Nick Carter's world. Tune in to "Nine Hours To Live" and discover why audiences thrilled to the Master Detective's exploits night after night—mysteries that proved that the greatest weapon any detective could possess was his mind.
