When the curtain rises on this gripping installment, Nick Carter finds himself ensnared in a macabre puzzle where the only witnesses to a brutal crime lie cold in the city morgue. As our intrepid detective navigates the fog-shrouded streets and shadowy interrogation rooms of the underworld, he must piece together fragments of evidence from those who can no longer speak. The atmosphere crackles with that distinctive tension that made Nick Carter a household name—the relentless tick of the clock, the menacing undertone of a jazz trumpet, and the inevitable moment when our hero must outwit a killer grown desperate and unpredictable. Dead Witnesses delivers the swift pacing and razor-sharp dialogue that kept millions of listeners glued to their radios each week, a masterclass in dramatic economy where every line counts and danger lurks behind every revelation.

Throughout its remarkable twelve-year run on the Mutual Broadcasting System, Nick Carter became America's longest-running detective series, a direct descendant of the dime novels and pulp magazines that had captivated readers since the 1880s. By the 1940s, when Dead Witnesses aired, the show had perfected the formula that made radio drama essential evening entertainment—snappy repartee between Carter and his operatives, clever criminals who provided genuine intellectual challenges, and sound design so evocative you could almost smell the cigarette smoke and rain-slicked pavement. This episode showcases why the series remained relevant even as television loomed on the horizon.

Tune in for Dead Witnesses and experience why Nick Carter, Master Detective endured as a cornerstone of American broadcasting. This is detective fiction at its finest, where mystery and atmosphere become inseparable, and a master of deduction faces his most puzzling case yet.
