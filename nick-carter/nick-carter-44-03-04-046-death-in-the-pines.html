When the fog rolls thick through the pine forests of upstate New York, Nick Carter knows that murder isn't far behind. In this gripping installment, our master detective finds himself drawn into a web of secrets buried as deep as the forest floor itself, where a wealthy industrialist's mysterious death threatens to drag an entire family into scandal and ruin. With only cryptic clues and the nervous testimony of frightened witnesses to guide him, Carter must navigate the shadowy woods and darker corridors of small-town society to uncover whether this death was truly accidental—or calculated cold-blooded murder. Listeners will find themselves on edge with every creaking floorboard and every carefully placed word, as the detective's sharp mind peels back layer after layer of deception to expose the truth that someone desperately wanted to keep hidden.

Nick Carter, Master Detective enjoyed a remarkable twelve-year run throughout the 1940s and early '50s on the Mutual Broadcasting System, capturing audiences with lean, hardboiled storytelling that set the standard for detective radio drama. Based on the famous dime novel character, this interpretation brought a sophisticated, street-smart protagonist to the airwaves—one who relied on logic and keen observation rather than gunplay and theatrics. The show's writers crafted meticulously plotted mysteries that rewarded careful listeners, while the supporting cast and atmospheric sound design created a vivid world of danger lurking behind respectable facades.

Whether you're a devoted collector of classic detective programming or discovering this master of mystery for the first time, "Death In The Pines" exemplifies everything that made radio's golden age so captivating. Settle in with the lights low, tune in, and let Nick Carter guide you through the darkness—you won't believe where the evidence leads.
