When a seemingly innocent feline becomes the catalyst for murder, Nick Carter finds himself entangled in a case where the most unlikely clues unravel the darkest secrets. This gripping episode plunges listeners into a world of shadow and suspicion, where a simple house cat may hold the key to a killer's identity. As Nick applies his legendary detective prowess to the seemingly trivial details others overlook, the tension builds with each revelation. The atmospheric sound design of Mutual's production team transforms an ordinary Manhattan townhouse into a claustrophobic maze of danger, complete with the sharp crack of a gunshot, the eerie silence of a crime scene, and the mounting dread that danger lurks closer than anyone imagined. Nick's sharp wit and incisive questioning cut through the deception of suspects who thought themselves clever—until the Master Detective turns their own lies against them.

Nick Carter, Master Detective represented the golden age of radio mystery programming during the 1940s and early 1950s. Adapted from the legendary pulp magazine character who had captivated readers since the 1880s, the Mutual broadcast brought Nick's methodical approach to detective work to millions of American listeners huddled around their radio sets. Each fifteen-minute episode delivered taut drama with authentic police procedure and logical puzzle-solving, appealing to audiences who appreciated intelligence and cunning over mere sensation. The show became a staple of afternoon programming, offering respite and intrigue during post-war America's appetite for sophisticated entertainment.

Tune in to "A Cat Brings Death" and discover how the smallest detail can topple the most carefully constructed alibi. Join Nick Carter as he proves once again why criminals should never underestimate the Master Detective's ability to see what others cannot.
