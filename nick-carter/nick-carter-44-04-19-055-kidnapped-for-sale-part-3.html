The clock is ticking in the Manhattan underworld as Nick Carter closes in on a desperate kidnapping ring operating in the shadows of wartime New York. In this third installment of our gripping serial, the master detective finds himself trapped between ruthless criminals and a victim whose life hangs by a thread. With each clue pulling him deeper into a web of blackmail and violence, Nick must use every ounce of his legendary cunning to outwit syndicate thugs who stop at nothing—and time is running out before an innocent life is sold to the highest bidder. The tension crackles through your radio speaker as our hero navigates smoky back alleys and corrupt police precincts, racing against the clock in a case that proves even a master detective can find himself outmaneuvered.

Nick Carter, Master Detective stands as one of radio's finest detective programs, a show that perfected the art of serial storytelling during the golden age of network broadcasting. Airing on the Mutual network from 1943 through 1955, the program drew listeners into a meticulously crafted world of urban crime and complex mysteries, starring Părley Baer as the brilliant titular investigator. The show's writers understood what kept audiences riveted: not just the mystery itself, but the moral ambiguity and danger lurking in every shadow, reflecting the anxieties of Americans navigating a world at war. Each episode crackled with authenticity, grounded in real New York locations and criminal procedures that made the drama feel disturbingly plausible.

Don your overcoat and step into the fog-shrouded streets of 1940s New York. This is detective radio at its most compelling—where every commercial break leaves you desperate to know what happens next.
