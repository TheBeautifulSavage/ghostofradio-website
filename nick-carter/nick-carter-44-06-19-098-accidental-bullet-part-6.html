As the familiar theme music fades and Nick Carter's world-weary voice cuts through the static, listeners find themselves deep in a labyrinth of deception where nothing is quite as it seems. In this sixth and penultimate installment of "Accidental Bullet," our master detective races against time to untangle a murder mystery that hinges on a single, seemingly insignificant detail—a bullet fired in the darkness. With danger lurking in every shadow and a killer determined to cover their tracks, Nick must navigate a treacherous web of lies, false alibis, and red herrings to uncover the truth. The tension crackles through your radio speaker as the noose tightens around the guilty party, and listeners will find themselves breathlessly waiting for the revelation that could come at any moment.

Nick Carter, Master Detective became a touchstone of American radio entertainment during the golden age of broadcasting, captivating millions with tales of intrigue and cunning investigation. What set the show apart was its commitment to authentic detective work—no supernatural nonsense or impossible feats, just solid police procedure, clever deduction, and the kind of gritty realism that kept listeners invested. The character of Nick Carter himself drew from a rich literary tradition dating back to the dime novels of the 1800s, but this radio adaptation modernized the detective for post-war America, speaking to audiences navigating their own complex moral landscape through the safety of their living rooms.

If you've been following this gripping serial, the conclusion awaits. Tune in as Nick Carter peels back the final layers of deception to reveal the truth behind the accidental bullet—and discover whether justice will prevail.
