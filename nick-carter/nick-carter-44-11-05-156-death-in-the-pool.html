Picture this: a sweltering summer night at an exclusive country club, where the glittering surface of the Olympic-sized swimming pool reflects the stars—and hides a terrible secret. When a prominent businessman is discovered floating face-down in the chlorinated waters, the club's distinguished members find themselves trapped in a nightmare of suspicion and deceit. Nick Carter, Master Detective, arrives to uncover the truth, his sharp mind cutting through layers of alibis and misdirection as the clock ticks and memories grow hazier. Will the killer strike again before dawn? Listeners tuning in to this thrilling installment will experience the mounting tension that made Nick Carter a household name—every shadow could conceal a murderer, every guest list a potential suspect, and every minute brings detective and culprit closer to their inevitable collision.

For over a decade, Nick Carter, Master Detective captivated millions of Americans who gathered around their radio sets to follow the world's greatest fictional detective through impossible cases. Premiering during radio's golden age when mystery was the lifeblood of evening entertainment, the show built its reputation on atmospheric storytelling and clever plotting that rewarded attentive listeners. Broadcast over the Mutual network, it competed fiercely with shows like The Shadow and Inner Sanctum, yet carved its own niche through the steadfast brilliance of its protagonist and the meticulous construction of each week's puzzle. This 1944 episode exemplifies the show's mastery—combining the glamorous world of high society with the gritty realities of crime and investigation.

Don't miss this masterwork of classic radio mystery. Slip back in time to an era when entertainment meant using your imagination, when a detective's voice and sound effects could transport you into danger and intrigue. "Death in the Pool" awaits—if you dare to listen.
