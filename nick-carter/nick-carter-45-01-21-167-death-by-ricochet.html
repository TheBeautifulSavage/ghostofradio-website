When a seemingly innocent hunting accident leaves a prominent businessman dead in the Catskill Mountains, Nick Carter knows better than to accept the official story. As our master detective arrives at the crime scene on a bitterly cold January evening, the snowdrifts conceal far more than footprints—they hide a web of jealousy, greed, and carefully calculated murder. With only the peculiar trajectory of a single bullet and the testimony of nervous witnesses, Carter must unravel whether this was truly a ricochet or a masterfully staged crime. Listen as he moves through shadowed lodge rooms and silent forests, interviewing suspects who all have secrets to protect, while the killer grows increasingly desperate to silence him.

Nick Carter, Master Detective flourished during radio's golden age, capturing audiences nationwide with stories that proved the power of keen observation and logical deduction. By 1945, when "Death By Ricochet" aired, the show had already established itself as a favorite among listeners hungry for intelligent crime drama—the kind where the solution came not from gunplay, but from careful thinking and persistence. The program's success lay in its faithful adaptation of the original pulp magazine character, modernized just enough for wartime audiences who appreciated stories of order restored and justice served. The writing crackled with period authenticity, the sound effects immersive and convincing, and the supporting cast brought each suspect to vivid life.

If you've never experienced the theatrical magic of old-time radio, or if you're a devoted fan seeking to revisit this classic mystery, Death By Ricochet offers the perfect entry point. Settle into your chair, dim the lights, and let your imagination paint the scenes as Nick Carter's voice guides you through one of radio's most compelling detective yarns. A mystery awaits.
