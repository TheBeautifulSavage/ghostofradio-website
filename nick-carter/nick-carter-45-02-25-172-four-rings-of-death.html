When Nick Carter receives a cryptic warning in the form of four ornate rings, each engraved with a name, he knows he's facing an adversary of exceptional cunning and malevolence. As the master detective races against time through the fog-shrouded streets of New York, each ring represents a life hanging in the balance—and a puzzle that could only be solved by someone with Carter's legendary intellect and street savvy. The steady pulse of the orchestra underscores mounting tension as our hero navigates from shadowy nightclubs to the elegant penthouses of Manhattan's elite, gathering clues while mysterious forces work to silence him forever. Will Nick decipher the killer's obsession with these death-bringing rings before the final name is crossed off his list?

During the golden age of radio in the 1940s, Nick Carter became a household name as one of the most enduring detective characters in broadcasting history. Originally created as a dime novel hero in the 1880s, Nick Carter's transition to radio brought the character back to life for a new generation of mystery lovers, with the Mutual network's production showcasing stellar sound design and dramatic pacing that made listeners feel they were walking alongside the great detective himself. These episodes represent broadcasting at its finest—when plot, character, and atmosphere combined to create an immersive experience that required nothing but imagination and a working radio set.

This episode exemplifies everything that made Nick Carter a must-listen program for devotees of detective fiction. If you appreciate intricate plots, vintage radio atmosphere, and the thrill of a master sleuth matching wits with a formidable criminal mind, Four Rings of Death awaits your discovery. Tune in and experience the mystery as audiences heard it unfold over eighty years ago.
