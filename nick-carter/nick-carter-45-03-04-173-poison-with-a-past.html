# Nick Carter, Master Detective: "Poison With A Past"

When Nick Carter picks up the telephone on a rain-slicked Manhattan evening, the voice on the other end is barely a whisper—a woman's voice, trembling with something between fear and resignation. She speaks of a poison that shouldn't exist, a murder that shouldn't have happened, and secrets buried so deep they've taken root in the city's darkest corners. What follows is a masterclass in old-fashioned detective work, where Carter's razor-sharp deductions cut through layers of misdirection and carefully constructed alibis. The episode crackles with authentic 1940s atmosphere: the distant wail of sirens, the rapid-fire dialogue of nervous witnesses, and the inevitable moment when our master detective corners his quarry with an elegantly simple question that unravels everything. Listeners will find themselves racing alongside Nick through shadowy laboratories, posh brownstones, and the city morgue, where the solution to a seemingly impossible crime has been waiting all along.

Nick Carter, Master Detective enjoyed an impressive twelve-year run on the Mutual network, becoming one of radio's most enduring mystery franchises. Based on the pulp magazine character created in 1886, the show adapted the detective for the golden age of radio, presenting standalone mysteries that showcased both the sophistication of intelligent detective work and the fast-paced thrills audiences craved. By 1943, when this program was broadcast, Carter had already captivated millions of listeners with his methodical approach to solving crime—a refreshing alternative to the comedy-relief sidekicks and supernatural gimmicks of competing shows.

Tune in now to experience "Poison With A Past" and discover why Nick Carter remained a listener favorite throughout the 1940s. It's a reminder of when mystery radio meant genuine suspense, clever plotting, and a detective who always stayed one step ahead.
