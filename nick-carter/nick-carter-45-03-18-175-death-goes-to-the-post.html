# Death Goes To The Post

When a prominent racehorse trainer is found dead in the shadow of the grandstand, Nick Carter finds himself navigating the glittering corruption of the racing world—where fortunes change hands with every race, and murder is just another calculated bet. In this March 1945 adventure, our master detective must untangle a web of jockeys, gamblers, and society dilettantes, each with secrets worth killing for. The episode crackles with the tension of high stakes and higher tensions, as Nick's razor-sharp intellect cuts through alibis and misdirection. You'll hear the distant thunder of hoofbeats, the roar of the crowd, and the unmistakable click of a killer's trap closing around them—all rendered in that rich, atmospheric sound design that made tuning into Nick Carter an essential evening ritual for millions of Americans.

Nick Carter, Master Detective was among radio's most enduring detective programs, thriving throughout the golden age when a single broadcast could captivate the entire nation. Based on the pulp magazine character dating back to the 1880s, the Mutual network's adaptation brought sophistication and legitimacy to the detective genre that lesser shows could never match. The program's longevity—spanning over a decade—testified to listeners' insatiable appetite for clever plotting and the reassuring presence of a hero who could restore order from chaos when real-world anxieties felt overwhelming. By the 1940s, as America navigated wartime tensions, Nick Carter offered an escape into a world where justice still prevailed and truth could be uncovered through wit rather than brute force.
