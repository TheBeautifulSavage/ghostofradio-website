Deep in the shadowed hollows of Hangman's Wood, where gnarled oaks twist skyward like skeletal fingers and fog clings to the forest floor like a burial shroud, a terrible crime has been committed—and only Nick Carter, Master Detective, possesses the wit and courage to untangle its secrets. When a prominent citizen vanishes without trace, leaving behind only cryptic clues scattered among the twisted roots and rotting underbrush, Carter must navigate both the treacherous forest and an even more dangerous web of suspects. Local legends whisper of curses and spectral voices, but our hero knows that behind every mystery lurks cold logic and human motive. With his trusty sidekick Chick Williams at his side, Carter probes deeper into Hangman's Wood's dark past, where he discovers that some secrets have been buried for decades—and someone will kill to keep them hidden. Every creaking branch, every whispered accusation, every revelation draws listeners closer to a startling solution that defies everything the townsfolk believe about their own community.

For over a decade, Nick Carter's adventures captivated millions of American radio listeners, from Manhattan apartments to rural farmhouses where families gathered around their receivers. The character himself was no stranger to popular culture—drawn from dime novels that had thrilled audiences since the 1880s—but the Mutual network's adaptation brought him vividly to life through superb writing, atmospheric sound design, and performances that set the standard for detective drama on the airwaves. This episode represents the show at its peak creative power, blending classic whodunit elements with the visceral immediacy that only radio could provide.

Tune in to experience detective radio at its finest—where every sound effect heightens the tension, every pause carries weight, and the mystery unfolds with the inexorable logic that made Nick Carter a legend.
