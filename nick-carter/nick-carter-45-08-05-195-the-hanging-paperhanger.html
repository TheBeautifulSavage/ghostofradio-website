# Nick Carter, Master Detective: "The Hanging Paperhanger"

When a decorator's body is discovered dangling from the scaffolding of a society mansion, New York's most celebrated detective finds himself tangled in a web of deception that reaches far beyond Park Avenue. This episode plunges listeners into the smoky underbelly of 1940s Manhattan, where Nick Carter must navigate the treacherous intersection of high society secrets and street-level crime. With his trusted partner Patsy Bowen by his side, Carter peels back layers of motive and alibi in a case where nothing—not even death by hanging—is quite what it seems. The crisp dialogue, expertly timed sound effects of creaking ropes and stamping feet on scaffolding, and the subtle musical stings create an atmosphere of mounting dread as our hero closes in on a killer hiding in plain sight.

Nick Carter, Master Detective was a cornerstone of American radio mystery programming during its thirteen-year run on the Mutual network. Based on the legendary pulp detective character created in 1886, the show brought timeless noir sensibilities into the living rooms of post-war America, perfectly capturing the anxieties and fascinations of a nation grappling with crime, corruption, and moral ambiguity. Each episode showcased the keen logic and streetwise intuition that made Carter America's favorite private eye—a character whose popularity had already spanned decades across dime novels, films, and comic strips. The show's snappy writing and genuinely puzzling cases earned it a devoted following among radio enthusiasts who appreciated intelligent storytelling delivered with style and sophistication.

Step into the well-worn fedora of Nick Carter himself and discover why this particular case—with its peculiar title and darker implications—remains a standout mystery. Tune in and let the master detective guide you through one of radio's greatest unsolved puzzles, where every clue counts and the truth hangs by a thread.
