When a seemingly worthless alarm clock disappears from a modest brownstone apartment in Manhattan, Nick Carter knows better than to dismiss it as an ordinary theft. What begins as a simple missing object case quickly spirals into a web of deception, mistaken identities, and desperate motives that will test the master detective's legendary powers of deduction. As Carter moves through the shadowy streets and dimly lit rooms of the city, piecing together clues with meticulous precision, listeners will find themselves caught between red herrings and genuine danger. The ticking of time becomes both literal and metaphorical in this taut mystery where nothing is quite as it seems, and the smallest detail may unravel the entire case.

Nick Carter, Master Detective stands as a towering monument to radio's golden age of mystery programming, and this 1943 broadcast exemplifies why the show captivated millions of listeners throughout the 1940s and 1950s. Adapted from the legendary pulp magazine series that had thrilled readers since 1886, the radio program transformed Carter into a contemporary urban sleuth, operating in the bustling noir landscape of wartime America. The show's enduring appeal lay in its commitment to clever plotting, crisp dialogue, and the brilliant characterization of Carter himself—a detective who relied on brains rather than brawn, making him accessible to the average listener while maintaining an air of sophisticated intrigue. Each episode delivered exactly what audiences craved: a complete mystery solved within thirty minutes, with genuine surprises and satisfying logical conclusions.

If you appreciate mysteries where the puzzle pieces actually fit together, where a detective's reasoning matters more than mere luck, then this episode deserves your attention. Settle in with the lights dimmed and let Nick Carter guide you through a case where an alarm clock's disappearance proves far more sinister than anyone could have imagined.
