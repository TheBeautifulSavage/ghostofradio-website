When Nick Carter steps into the glittering world of high-fashion New York, he finds himself entangled in a web of jealousy, theft, and murder that reaches from the showrooms of Fifth Avenue to the shadowy underworld of stolen furs. A priceless blue mink coat—worth a fortune in wartime dollars—has vanished, and with it, a woman lies dead. Was it a simple robbery turned violent, or does the crime run deeper into the circles of wealth and desperation that prowled Manhattan's gilded streets? As Nick's razor-sharp mind unravels clue after clue, listeners will find themselves drawn into a atmosphere thick with suspicion: the rustle of expensive fabrics, the cool professionalism of Nick's voice cutting through the darkness, and that unmistakable musical sting that signals danger around every corner.

Nick Carter, Master Detective became one of radio's most enduring institutions during the Golden Age, running twelve consecutive years with an astonishing output of episodes that captivated millions of listeners. Broadcast over the Mutual network during the 1940s and early 1950s, the show represented everything audiences craved: intelligent mysteries, a protagonist as dependable as a trusted friend, and plots ripped from the headlines of a crime-obsessed era. Mandel Kramer's portrayal of Nick Carter created an icon—a detective who was tough enough for the streets yet cultured enough for high society, making him the perfect guide through America's most elaborate criminal conspiracies.

Don't miss this masterclass in mystery. Tune in now and experience why Nick Carter kept millions of Americans on the edge of their seats, week after week, year after year.
