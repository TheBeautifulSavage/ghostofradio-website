When Nick Carter settles into his office on this February evening in 1947, he finds himself entangled in a web of negligence and greed that threatens to topple a major industrial concern from within. A seemingly simple case of missing blueprints spirals into something far more sinister as our intrepid detective discovers that carelessness may be nothing more than a convenient disguise for sabotage. With Chutney, his ever-faithful assistant, at his side, Nick must navigate a treacherous factory floor where every worker harbors secrets and motives lurk beneath innocent explanations. The ticking clock of an imminent corporate crisis drives the narrative forward as Nick's sharp deductive reasoning peels back layer after layer of misdirection—each revelation more startling than the last.

Nick Carter, Master Detective thrived on radio during the golden age of mystery programming, capturing audiences nationwide with tales of metropolitan intrigue and intellectual detective work. Unlike the hard-boiled cynicism that would later dominate the genre, Carter represented a more cerebral tradition of detective fiction—think of him as America's answer to Sherlock Holmes, updated for the atomic age. The Mutual Network broadcast adventures from 1943 through 1955, maintaining steady popularity with listeners who craved intelligent mysteries that rewarded close attention and valued brains over bullets. This particular episode exemplifies the show's trademark blend of atmospheric storytelling and puzzle-box plotting that kept millions tuning in each week.

Don your headphones and dim the lights—The Case of The Careless Employees awaits, ready to transport you back to an era when radio drama reigned supreme and a master detective's voice could paint an entire world of suspicion and danger in your imagination.
