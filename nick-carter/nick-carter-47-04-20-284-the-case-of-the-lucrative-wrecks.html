When the fog rolls thick off the harbor and salvage operations turn deadly, Nick Carter finds himself entangled in a web of greed, deception, and murder most calculated. A shipwreck that should have spelled disaster for its owners instead becomes their jackpot—but at what cost? As bodies begin surfacing alongside the wreckage, our master detective must navigate treacherous waters both literal and figurative, untangling a conspiracy that reaches from the docks into the marble halls of insurance offices. With his trusted aide Chick Williams at his side, Carter probes deeper into a case where insurance claims conceal cold-blooded ambition, and where the lure of profit proves far more dangerous than any storm at sea. Expect twists at every turn, crisp dialogue crackle with tension, and the methodical brilliance that made Nick Carter a household name during radio's golden age.

Nick Carter, Master Detective commanded an impressive fifteen-year run on the Mutual network, becoming one of the era's most beloved detective serials. Originally based on the 19th-century dime novel character, the radio iteration transformed Carter into a contemporary 1940s sleuth whose encyclopedic knowledge of criminal methodology and unwavering moral compass made him the thinking listener's hero. Each episode delivered exactly what audiences craved: intelligent mystery plotting, atmospheric production values, and the reassuring sense that justice would prevail through brains rather than brawn. "The Case of the Lucrative Wrecks" exemplifies the show's mastery of urban noir atmosphere transplanted to exotic locales, proving why fans tuned in faithfully every broadcast.

Don't miss this gripping tale of maritime mystery and insurance fraud. Settle in with the lights dimmed low, let the sound effects transport you dockside, and discover why Nick Carter remained one of radio's most compelling detective programs. Your evening awaits.
