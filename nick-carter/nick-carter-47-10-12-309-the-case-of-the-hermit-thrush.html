Step into the shadow-laden streets of 1940s Manhattan as Nick Carter, America's greatest detective, unravels a murder most peculiar—one where a rare songbird becomes the only witness to a crime that threatens to shake the city to its core. In this masterfully crafted episode, listeners will find themselves drawn into a web of deception where ornithologists harbor dark secrets, and a simple hermit thrush's nocturnal song holds the key to exposing a killer walking free. As the iconic opening theme swells and Nick's distinctive voice cuts through the static, you'll hear the crack of a gunshot echo through the autumn night, followed by the detective's methodical investigation—each clue clicking into place like a perfectly calibrated lock. The supporting cast brings authentic New York grit to life, from the world-weary beat cops to the nervous suspects who crumble under Carter's penetrating interrogation. This is radio drama at its finest: suspense building with each door slam, each gasp of astonishment, each carefully measured silence that speaks volumes.

For over a decade, Nick Carter, Master Detective commanded Tuesday nights on the Mutual network, captivating millions of Americans with tales drawn from the detective's legendary case files. The show struck a perfect balance between hard-boiled realism and theatrical flair, presenting mysteries sophisticated enough for adult listeners while remaining accessible to the entire family gathered around the radio. These weren't mere whodunits—they were complete worlds rendered in sound and imagination, where Nat Polen's masterful sound design and the cast's ensemble work created an immersive experience television would later struggle to replicate.

Don't miss this haunting investigation where nature itself becomes an unwitting accomplice to justice. Tune in and discover why Nick Carter commanded such devoted legions of fans.
