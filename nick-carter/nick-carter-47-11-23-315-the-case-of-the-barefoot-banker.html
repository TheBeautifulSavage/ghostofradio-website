Picture this: it's a rain-soaked Manhattan evening in November 1947, and Nick Carter arrives at the offices of Consolidated Trust to find pandemonium. The vault stands open—empty—and the bank's president lies unconscious on the marble floor, his shoes missing. As Baxley, Carter's quick-witted operative, searches for clues, a curious detail emerges: muddy footprints lead not toward the street, but deeper into the building's labyrinthine basement. Who strips a banker of his shoes before fleeing with millions in securities? Is it a calling card, a threat, or something far more sinister? With only these cryptic clues and his razor-sharp intellect, Nick Carter must navigate a web of corporate espionage, embezzlement, and betrayal to unmask the barefoot bandit before they strike again.

Nick Carter, Master Detective had already captivated millions of Americans by the late 1940s, having made the leap from pulp fiction to radio with remarkable success. The character's origins stretched back to the 1880s dime novels, but this Mutual Broadcasting adaptation brought him into the modern age with contemporary settings, snappy dialogue, and mysteries that reflected post-war anxieties about crime and corruption in institutions once thought beyond reproach. Each episode crackled with authenticity, featuring detailed sound design—the squeak of leather shoes, the metallic click of vault doors, the mournful wail of distant sirens—that transported listeners directly into Carter's investigations.

Tune your dial to this classic mystery and experience why millions huddled around their radios each week to follow Master Detective Nick Carter into the darkest corners of crime. Can you solve this puzzle before Carter does?
