# Nick Carter, Master Detective: The Case Of The Graveyard Gunman

When the fog rolls thick across the cemetery gates and a killer strikes among the dead, even the Master Detective himself finds himself treading on unhallowed ground. In this chilling installment, Nick Carter must navigate the shadowy maze of headstones and crypts to unmask a murderer who uses a burial ground as both hunting ground and hideaway. With Chutney, his loyal sidekick, close at his heels and the eerie wail of wind through iron gates punctuating every revelation, listeners will experience the creeping dread of a case where the dead may hold secrets as dangerous as the living. The killer leaves no witnesses—only questions—and Nick must piece together a puzzle written in darkness and desperation before another innocent soul joins the permanent residents of that cursed plot of earth.

*Nick Carter, Master Detective* became one of radio's most enduring detective programs precisely because it understood the power of atmosphere married to clever plotting. Beginning its run in 1943 on the Mutual network, the series transformed a pulp fiction hero into an audio institution, where the sound of footsteps on wet pavement and the crackle of a revolver shot became as memorable as any visual spectacle. This particular episode, broadcast in January 1948, exemplifies the show's golden era—when writers crafted mysteries that rewarded careful listening and production values that made listeners forget they were sitting in their living rooms rather than standing in the fog-shrouded cemetery alongside Carter himself.

Tune in to *Nick Carter, Master Detective* and step into a world where logic battles the supernatural, where danger lurks in forgotten graves, and where one man's intellect stands as the only barrier between chaos and justice. The graveyard awaits.
