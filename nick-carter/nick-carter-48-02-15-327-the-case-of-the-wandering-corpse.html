When a dead man refuses to stay put, Detective Nick Carter knows he's stumbled into something far more sinister than a simple murder. In this spine-tingling February 1948 episode, our intrepid hero finds himself chasing a corpse across the fog-shrouded streets of the city—only to discover that every time he thinks he's cornered the mystery, a fresh complication emerges from the shadows. The body moves from morgue to abandoned warehouse to the back of a speeding truck, each discovery peeling back another layer of deception. With his loyal sidekick Chutney at his side and only his razor-sharp intellect to guide him through the darkness, Nick must determine who wanted this man dead badly enough to move heaven and earth—literally—to keep his secrets buried.

Nick Carter, Master Detective remained one of radio's most enduring mysteries programs precisely because it understood that the best detective stories aren't really about the puzzle—they're about the determination of one man who simply refuses to accept easy answers. Born from the pulp magazine tradition and perfected in the golden age of radio drama, the series brought the same hardboiled sensibility that listeners craved from their evening broadcasts, combining clever plotting with genuine atmospheric tension. By the 1948 broadcasts, Nick Carter had become an institution, a voice as familiar as a trusted friend who always knew the right questions to ask.

Don't miss this masterclass in old-time radio mystery-craft. "The Case of the Wandering Corpse" proves why Nick Carter remained America's most trusted detective well into the television age. Tune in and let yourself be transported to an era when a single voice and a masterful plot could hold millions in thrall.
