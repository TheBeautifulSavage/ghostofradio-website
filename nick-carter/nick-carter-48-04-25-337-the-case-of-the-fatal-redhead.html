When Nick Carter's telephone rings on a fog-shrouded evening in April 1948, the master detective finds himself drawn into a labyrinth of deception, desire, and murder most calculated. A beautiful woman with flame-red hair has been found dead in a locked study, her body posed with deliberate artistry—a calling card of someone far too clever for ordinary police work. As Nick and his trusted aide Chick Williams navigate the shadowy underbelly of New York's high society, listeners will experience the full arsenal of the detective's legendary methods: keen observation, brilliant deduction, and an unflinching willingness to venture into the darkest corners of human nature. The perpetrator is clever, ruthless, and always one step ahead—until Nick Carter enters the case.

For over a decade, Nick Carter, Master Detective dominated the airwaves as one of radio's most authentic and intellectually satisfying mystery programs. Unlike the flamboyant heroes of pulp fiction, Carter embodied the rational detective—a man who solved crimes through logic rather than luck, whose cases unfolded with the precision of a master craftsman. This particular episode, broadcast during the show's golden era on the Mutual Network, exemplifies why the series commanded such devoted listeners: smart writing, credible danger, and mysteries that genuinely challenge the audience to play along.

If you've never experienced the particular magic of Nick Carter, Master Detective, this episode offers the perfect entry point—a perfectly constructed puzzle box of crime and consequence. Settle into your chair, adjust the dial to the past, and let the master detective guide you through one of radio's greatest unsolved mysteries. Justice, after all, waits for no one.
