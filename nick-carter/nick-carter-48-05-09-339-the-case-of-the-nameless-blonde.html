A woman arrives at Nick Carter's office with no memory of who she is—only a cryptic photograph and a date circled in red. As the master detective begins his interrogation, the atmosphere thickens with suspicion: Is she a victim or a dangerous impostor? Within minutes of her arrival, the office telephone rings with a threat that changes everything. Nick finds himself racing against the clock through the fog-shrouded streets of Manhattan, chasing shadows and half-truths, pursued by figures whose motives remain maddeningly obscure. In true Nick Carter fashion, danger erupts without warning—a gunshot shatters a window, a confession comes too easily, and nothing is quite as it seems. This episode captures the show's signature blend of breakneck pacing and genuine mystery, with the master detective using intellect and instinct in equal measure to unravel a web of deception that reaches far beyond one forgotten woman's identity.

Airing during radio's golden age, Nick Carter, Master Detective brought one of literature's most enduring detective characters into living rooms across America. First popularized in dime novels of the late 1800s, Nick Carter proved perfectly suited to radio drama—his cases demanded the rapid-fire dialogue and sound effects that made the medium so thrilling. The Mutual network's production, running from 1943 to 1955, showcased impeccable acting and intricate plotting that rivaled any detective serial of the era, attracting listeners who craved both intellectual challenge and edge-of-your-seat excitement.
