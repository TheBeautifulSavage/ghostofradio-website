When Nick Carter arrives at the Excelsior Hotel on a rain-soaked Manhattan evening, he expects a routine insurance investigation. Instead, he discovers a corpse in the locked penthouse suite—a body that shouldn't exist, belonging to a man who was supposed to be three thousand miles away. As our Master Detective examines the crime scene with his characteristic cool precision, the web of deception only thickens. Was the victim a clever imposter? A desperate fugitive? Or something far more sinister? With his trusted sidekick Chatterbox Morton close at hand and the clock ticking against the police commissioner's patience, Nick must navigate a labyrinth of conflicting testimonies, hidden identities, and motives darker than the Manhattan night itself. This episode captures everything that made Nick Carter a fixture in American homes throughout the 1940s—the snap of crisp dialogue, the methodical unraveling of seemingly impossible crimes, and that electric tension that kept listeners glued to their sets.

Nick Carter, Master Detective represented the golden age of radio mystery programs, when a skilled detective's voice and the power of suggestion could transport millions directly into the mind of investigation. Broadcast on the Mutual network throughout the 1940s and 1950s, the show drew from decades of pulp magazine fame, transforming the character into a radio icon for an entire generation. The writers crafted intricate plots that rewarded careful listening, while the production's sound design—creaking floorboards, distant sirens, the rustle of evidence—created an atmosphere of genuine danger.
