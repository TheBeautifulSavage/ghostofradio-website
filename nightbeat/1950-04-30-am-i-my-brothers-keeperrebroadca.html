# Nightbeat: Am I My Brother's Keeper?

When the clock strikes midnight in Chicago, private investigator Frank Nightingale finds himself standing over a corpse in a warehouse along the darkened docks—and the dead man's face belongs to someone he's tried desperately to forget: his own brother. What begins as a routine case spirals into a labyrinth of deception, blackmail, and blood ties that blur the line between justice and vengeance. As rain hammers against rain-slicked pavement and neon signs flicker in the distance, Nightingale must navigate a moral minefield where every lead pulls him deeper into his family's shameful secrets. Frankie Sade's narration cuts through the noir atmosphere like a blade, his weary voice carrying the weight of a man who understands that some cases hit closer to home than others—and that loyalty to the badge might cost him his very soul.

*Nightbeat* stands as one of the finest expressions of radio noir, emerging in 1950 when the medium was reaching toward its sunset, refusing to go quietly. The show captured the authentic grit of postwar Chicago with a sophistication rarely matched on the airwaves, eschewing simple morality tales for complex examinations of urban corruption and personal compromise. Created for NBC, it became the network's answer to the crime procedural, grounding its stories in actual police work while never sacrificing the atmospheric dread that made listeners pull their chairs closer to the radio speaker.

Whether you're a devoted listener or discovering *Nightbeat* for the first time, "Am I My Brother's Keeper?" represents the show at its most potent—a masterclass in suspense that proves radio crime drama could plumb psychological depths that even television wouldn't reach for decades. Tune in and let the Chicago night wrap around you.
