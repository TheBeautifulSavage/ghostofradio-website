Nightbeat NBC · May 29, 1950

Harlan Matthews, Stamp Dealer

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

In the shadow of Chicago's post-war underbelly, Nightbeat conductor Frank McNally finds himself drawn into the seemingly innocent world of philately—where rare stamps hide darker secrets than any criminal could imagine. When a wealthy collector turns up dead in his Michigan Avenue shop, Frank's investigation spirals through a labyrinth of forged documents, blackmail schemes, and a stamp dealer whose meticulous cataloging habits mask a far more sinister collection. As the conductor prowls the rain-slicked streets from midnight to dawn, the episode masterfully weaves together the mundane details of the stamp trade with escalating danger, building toward a climax that proves even the most ordinary hobbies can conceal extraordinary crimes. Listeners will find themselves suspended in that distinctly noir atmosphere where every dealer's magnifying glass might be examining more than just perforations and watermarks.

Nightbeat, which aired this episode in 1950, represents a unique moment in radio history—the medium's final golden age before television would reshape American entertainment. Frank McNally, conducted by Frankie Laine (also famous as a singer), brought to life a private detective who worked nights, a character perfectly suited to radio's intimate, shadow-rich aesthetic. The show distinguished itself through meticulous sound design and scripts that treated Chicago's criminal landscape with genuine urban authenticity, neither sensationalizing nor sanitizing the city's postwar tensions and moral ambiguities.

If you're a devotee of classic noir or simply wish to experience radio at its sophisticated best, "Harlan Matthews, Stamp Dealer" offers everything that made Nightbeat essential listening for millions: atmospheric storytelling, sharp dialogue, and the sound of a city awake when respectable people sleep. Tune in tonight and discover why listeners kept their dials tuned to NBC long after sunset.
