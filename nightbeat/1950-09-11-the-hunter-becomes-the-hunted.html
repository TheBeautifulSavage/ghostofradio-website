# Nightbeat: The Hunter Becomes The Hunted

When private eye Frank Nightingale takes on what seems like a routine divorce case in a dingy Loop hotel, he stumbles into something far deadlier—a web of blackmail, corruption, and murder that reaches into Chicago's highest echelons of power. As Frank digs deeper into the shadowy world of midnight rendezvous and forged photographs, he discovers that he's become the target of forces far more ruthless than he anticipated. The hunter, it seems, has become the hunted. This gripping episode crackles with the authentic grit of 1950s Chicago: the squeal of tires in rain-slicked alleys, the menacing drone of police sirens, and the sharp crack of gunfire echoing through darkened streets. You'll feel the tension mounting as Frank realizes his very life hangs in the balance, and trust becomes a luxury he can no longer afford.

*Nightbeat* was a groundbreaking series that captured the post-war American obsession with hard-boiled detective fiction translated perfectly for the intimate medium of radio. Premiering on NBC in 1950, the show became an instant favorite among listeners hungry for authentic urban crime drama, featuring the brilliant narration and characterization of star Frank Lovejoy. Unlike the glamorized mystery programs of earlier decades, *Nightbeat* presented a Chicago that was gritty, morally ambiguous, and dangerously real—a city where even private eyes couldn't escape the machinery of corruption. "The Hunter Becomes The Hunted" exemplifies everything that made the series special: razor-sharp writing, explosive action sequences, and that unmistakable noir sensibility that defined the era's finest radio drama.

Don't miss this pulse-pounding classic—tune in and discover why *Nightbeat* remains one of radio's most celebrated crime dramas. Frank Nightingale is waiting in the shadows.
