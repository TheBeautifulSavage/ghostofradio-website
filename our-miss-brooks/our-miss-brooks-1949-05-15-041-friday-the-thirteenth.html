# Our Miss Brooks: Friday The Thirteenth

When Miss Brooks arrives at Madison High School on a fateful Friday the Thirteenth, she discovers that superstition has gripped the entire institution—and for once, her quick wit and unflappable charm may not be enough to navigate the chaos. With a black cat crossing her path, a ladder blocking the hallway, and Mr. Conklin convinced that doom awaits the school, our beloved English teacher finds herself caught between the rational world of education and the irrational fears of her colleagues. As the day unfolds with increasingly ridiculous mishaps, each one blamed on bad luck, Miss Brooks must use her considerable talents for comedy and common sense to restore order before superstition brings Madison High to a complete standstill. Eve Arden's impeccable timing and deadpan delivery transform what could have been a simple comedy sketch into a delightful exploration of how fear and folklore can overtake even the most sensible people.

This 1949 episode exemplifies why *Our Miss Brooks* became one of radio's most celebrated comedies during its nine-year run on CBS. The show, centered on the daily escapades of an intelligent and resourceful high school teacher, struck a perfect balance between witty sophistication and everyday relatability that appealed to audiences nationwide. Eve Arden's portrayal of Miss Brooks offered something revolutionary for the era: a female character who was neither a housewife nor a damsel, but rather a capable professional whose humor came from her sharp observations and resilience. The program's success would eventually lead to a television adaptation that extended the character's legacy far beyond radio.

Tune in to this classic broadcast and experience the golden age of radio comedy at its finest. You'll understand why millions of listeners tuned in each week to hear what fresh trouble Miss Brooks would encounter next.
