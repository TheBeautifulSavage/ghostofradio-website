Our Miss Brooks 1949 08 07 (053) Heat Wave

Beat the summer doldrums with Miss Connie Brooks as she navigates the sweltering corridors of Madison High School during the most oppressive heat wave to sweep the city in years. When the school's temperamental air cooling system fails entirely, our enterprising English teacher must contend with wilting students, irritable colleagues, and the ever-bumbling Principal Osgood as everyone struggles to maintain composure in the stifling heat. Eve Arden's dulcet tones carry us through a comedy of escalating desperation—where a simple lesson plan becomes a battle of wills against nature itself, and even the most composed educator finds her wit tested by the relentless thermometer. You'll hear the creak of ceiling fans, the hum of struggling machinery, and the theatrical groans of a cast melting faster than ice cream in July.

Our Miss Brooks, which premiered on CBS in 1948, had already become a beloved fixture in American living rooms by this August broadcast, with Arden's portrayal of the quick-thinking, good-hearted teacher striking a chord across the nation. The show's appeal lay in its witty, character-driven humor and the chemistry between Arden and her supporting cast—including Gale Gordon's perfectly pompous Principal Osgood. Set against the familiar backdrop of school life, each episode captured authentic moments of frustration and triumph that resonated with teachers and students alike, making the program as much a comfort to its audience as Miss Brooks herself was to her students.

Don't miss this sweltering slice of 1949 Americana—a masterclass in physical comedy translated through the medium of sound, where the thermometer rises but the laughs keep coming. Tune in and discover why this program remains a cherished memory of radio's golden age.
