# Our Miss Brooks: "Head Of The Board"

Picture this: It's a crisp September evening in 1949, and Connie Brooks, Madison High School's most resourceful English teacher, finds herself entangled in the delightful chaos of faculty politics. When a vacancy opens up on the school board, our determined Miss Brooks sees an opportunity—but standing between her and administrative glory is a labyrinth of competing egos, bureaucratic red tape, and the bumbling charm of Principal Osgood Conklin. What begins as a straightforward campaign for influence spirals into hilarious misunderstandings, featuring Miss Brooks' quick wit, Mr. Boynton's oblivious good nature, and Walter Denton's well-meaning but calamitous assistance. The stakes may seem small—it's just a school board position, after all—but in Connie's hands, even modest ambitions become the stuff of comedy gold, with laughter waiting around every corner.

Our Miss Brooks stands as a towering achievement in radio comedy, the rare show that balances intelligent humor with genuine warmth and character development. Created by Al Lewis and Joseph Staple, the program revolutionized the sitcom format by centering on a capable, unmarried professional woman navigating the world on her own terms—a radical concept in 1949. Eve Arden's performance as Miss Brooks became iconic, earning her the show's sustained success across multiple platforms: radio, television, and film. This particular episode, from the show's golden early years, captures the ensemble at peak form, with supporting players like Gale Gordon's perpetually flustered Principal Conklin providing perfect counterpoint to Arden's sophisticated comedy.

If you cherish smart dialogue, snappy one-liners, and a heroine who thinks faster than everyone around her, "Head Of The Board" is essential listening. Tune in and discover why Our Miss Brooks enchanted millions of Americans and remains a masterpiece of the golden age of radio.
