# Our Miss Brooks: School Mascot

Picture this: Madison High School is in an uproar. The beloved school mascot has gone missing, and Miss Brooks finds herself at the center of a comedy of errors that spirals from the classroom into the streets of the city. As our intrepid English teacher rushes to solve the mystery—partly out of genuine concern for school spirit, and partly because Principal Conklin has made it her personal responsibility—she's joined by the bumbling Mr. Boynton, the well-meaning but hapless Mr. Conklin, and a supporting cast of students and townspeople each convinced they know exactly where the mascot has gone. The result is a masterclass in radio timing and physical comedy, where listeners can practically hear the exasperation in Miss Brooks' voice as one ridiculous theory after another derails her increasingly frantic investigation. This November 1949 episode crackles with the energy that made the show a phenomenon.

*Our Miss Brooks* arrived on CBS radio at precisely the right cultural moment, capturing the post-war American fascination with the everyday lives of ordinary people. Eve Arden's Miss Brooks was revolutionary—a single, career-focused woman who wasn't desperate to find a husband, who stood up to authority without being shrill, and who could deliver a comedic line with the precision of a surgeon. The show's success on radio (1948-1957) proved so enduring that it spawned a motion picture and later a television series, making it one of the medium's rare crossover triumphs. Each episode, like this one, balanced genuine affection for its characters with razor-sharp satirical observations about small-town life and American institutions.

Whether you're a longtime devotee of classic radio or discovering this treasure for the first time, "School Mascot" offers everything that made *Our Miss Brooks* essential listening—wit, warmth, and the incomparable voice of Eve Arden leading us through another unforgettable day at Madison High.
