# Our Miss Brooks: "Cure That Habit!"

Step into Madison High School on a crisp January afternoon in 1950, where Miss Connie Brooks discovers that Principal Osgood Conklin has developed an irritating habit that's driving everyone to distraction—and she's determined to cure him of it, no matter the cost. What begins as a simple intervention spirals into a hilarious cascade of misadventures involving the school's hapless biology teacher Mr. Boynton, the mischievous student Walter Denton, and a series of increasingly absurd schemes. As Miss Brooks sets her considerable wit and charm into motion, listeners will find themselves caught between her earnest intentions and the comedy that inevitably unfolds when one tries to reform an administrator who's perfectly content with his quirks. The snappy dialogue crackles with the rapid-fire humor that made this show an American institution, while the warm affection between the characters keeps the laughter grounded in genuine human connection.

By 1950, *Our Miss Brooks* had already become a cultural phenomenon, with Eve Arden's portrayal of the quick-thinking, endlessly resourceful teacher striking a chord with millions of listeners. The show was groundbreaking for its time, presenting a strong, independent woman as the hero of her own story—a unmarried career woman whose intelligence and humor carried the narrative, rather than romantic pursuits or domestic concerns. In an era when radio was America's primary form of entertainment, this series exemplified the medium at its finest: intelligent writing, impeccable timing, and performances that seemed to reach directly into your living room.

Tune in for "Cure That Habit!" and discover why listeners tuned in faithfully every week to spend time with Miss Brooks and her unforgettable world. This is radio at its most entertaining and most human.
