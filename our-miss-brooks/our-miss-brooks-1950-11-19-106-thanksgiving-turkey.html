# Our Miss Brooks: Thanksgiving Turkey

Picture this: it's the day before Thanksgiving, 1950, and Miss Enid Brooks finds herself in the midst of small-town chaos at Madison High School. When a prize turkey intended for the school's holiday celebration goes missing, our quick-witted English teacher becomes an unlikely detective, scrambling through comedic misadventures to save the day before the festivities begin. The warm crackle of the studio audience's laughter punctuates every witty quip and pratfall as Brooks navigates the bumbling incompetence of Principal Osgood Conklin and the romantic fumbling of timid biology teacher Philip Boynton. There's warmth beneath the humor—a genuine affection for these small-town characters as they face the simple yet urgent crisis that mirrors the season's spirit of community and togetherness.

*Our Miss Brooks* arrived on CBS in 1948 as something revolutionary: a radio comedy built entirely around a female lead character who wasn't defined by romance or domesticity alone, but by her intelligence, independence, and resourcefulness. Eve Arden's portrayal of Miss Brooks became an American institution, beloved for her rapid-fire delivery and impeccable timing. The show thrived during radio's golden age, capturing perfectly the rhythms of American school life and small-town sensibilities. This November 1950 episode exemplifies what made the series essential listening—mixing genuine heart with sophisticated comedy that appealed equally to children and their parents gathered around the radio set.

Settle in for a half-hour of pure Americana this holiday season. Hear Miss Brooks at her improvising best, surrounded by the supporting cast that made Madison High feel like your own hometown. It's comedy that reminds us why radio's golden age earned its name.
