# Our Miss Brooks: "New School Bus" (January 27, 1952)

Tune in as Miss Connie Brooks discovers that Madison High School has finally acquired a shiny new bus for student transportation—but her joy quickly curdles into chaos when she becomes entangled in the comedic complications surrounding its debut. Eve Arden's quick wit and impeccable timing shine as our beloved English teacher navigates the inevitable mishaps and misunderstandings that arise, while Principal Osgood Conklin's bumbling authority and Stretch Snodgrass's pupil scheming add delightful layers of pandemonium. What begins as a simple matter of school improvement spirals into the sort of masterfully crafted farce that made this show a national treasure, complete with snappy one-liners, physical comedy, and the warm, relatable humor that audiences adored.

By 1952, *Our Miss Brooks* had already become CBS radio's crown jewel of situation comedy, and this episode exemplifies why. Eve Arden's portrayal of the sharp, unmarried teacher who could outwit any principal or scheming student became a template for sitcom heroines for decades to come. The show's brilliance lay in its ability to capture authentic school life—from faculty room gossip to the genuine affection between teachers and students—while maintaining laugh-out-loud comedy. Broadcasting during the post-war boom when radio still dominated American entertainment, *Our Miss Brooks* delivered intelligent humor that appealed to both parents and children, making it appointment listening in millions of homes.

Don't miss this charming slice of mid-century Americana where a new school bus becomes the catalyst for an unforgettable half-hour of comedy gold. Whether you're a devoted fan or discovering Miss Brooks for the first time, this episode reminds us why old-time radio remains such an enchanting escape.
