# Our Miss Brooks: "Mr. Travis' Three Acre Lot"

Step into Madison High School on this crisp January evening in 1955, where Miss Brooks faces her most delightfully absurd predicament yet. When Mr. Travis, the ever-beleaguered principal, becomes entangled in a scheme involving a mysterious three-acre property, our resourceful English teacher finds herself drawn into the chaos—complete with misunderstandings, comedic miscalculations, and the sort of rapid-fire banter that made this show a national treasure. What begins as an innocent conversation spirals into increasingly hilarious complications as Miss Brooks attempts to navigate between her principal's aspirations, the peculiar details of the land deal, and her own well-meaning interference. Listeners will recognize the familiar warmth of Desilu's stellar cast: Eve Arden's impeccable comedic timing as the quick-witted Miss Brooks, Gail Gordon's flustered Mr. Travis, and the supporting players who bring Madison High to vivid life.

By 1955, *Our Miss Brooks* had already proven itself a phenomenon, having originated on radio in 1948 before jumping to television in 1952. Yet the radio program remained a cultural institution, beloved by millions of Americans who appreciated its sophisticated humor and genuine affection for its characters. The show transcended simple sitcom formula—Miss Brooks was independent, intelligent, and remarkably modern for her era, a working woman navigating romantic possibilities while remaining firmly devoted to her career and her students. Episodes like this one showcase why the program earned multiple Emmy Awards and spawned a feature film.

Tune in now and discover why audiences tuned in faithfully for nearly a decade. This is radio comedy at its finest—clever, warm, and utterly timeless.
