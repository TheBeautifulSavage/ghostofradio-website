# Our Miss Brooks: "The School Board's Psychologist"

Picture this: It's a crisp March morning at Madison High School, and Miss Brooks has just learned that the school board has hired a psychologist to evaluate the faculty. What could possibly go wrong? Plenty, as it turns out. This delightful episode finds our intrepid English teacher navigating the treacherous waters of psychological evaluation while trying to maintain her composure—and her job. As the psychologist prowls the hallways with his clipboard and penetrating questions, Miss Brooks must contend with her own nervous energy, Principal Conklin's bureaucratic anxieties, and the wonderfully dim-witted antics of Walter Denton, her well-meaning student. The comedy crackles with that perfect blend of workplace absurdity and genuine human worry that made the show such essential listening for millions of Americans tuning in on their living room radios.

*Our Miss Brooks* stands as one of radio's finest comedies, a show that captured the particular chaos of American public school life with warmth and intelligence. Starring the incomparable Eve Arden as the quick-witted Miss Brooks, the series ran for nine glorious years on CBS, becoming a cultural touchstone for post-war America. What set the show apart was its refusal to condescend—the teachers were real people with real problems, and the students were endearing rather than mawkish. This 1955 episode exemplifies the show at its peak, when the writers had perfected the rhythms of their world and the cast could execute comedy with surgical precision.

Don't miss this charming slice of Golden Age radio. Settle in with *Our Miss Brooks* and rediscover why listeners couldn't wait to hear what fresh disaster would befall Miss Brooks next. You'll understand immediately why this show became a beloved institution in American homes.
