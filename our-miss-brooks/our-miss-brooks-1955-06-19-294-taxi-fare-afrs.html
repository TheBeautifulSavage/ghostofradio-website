# Our Miss Brooks: Taxi Fare

Picture this: Miss Connie Brooks, the witty and resourceful English teacher at Madison High School, finds herself in quite the predicament when an innocent taxi ride threatens to become the most expensive jaunt of her life. The meter keeps ticking, complications keep mounting, and what should be a simple trip across town spirals into a comedy of errors that only Connie could navigate with her trademark charm and quick thinking. This episode captures the show at its finest—taking an ordinary, relatable situation and mining it for genuine laughs while our beloved schoolmarm tries to talk her way out of financial disaster. Eve Arden's impeccable timing and sharp delivery make every moment sing, whether she's sparring with an exasperated cabbie or desperately trying to explain the situation to her colleagues. The supporting cast, including the bumbling Principal Osgood Conklin and the lovesick Mr. Boynton, circle in and out of the chaos, each adding their own comedic flavor to the mayhem.

For nearly a decade, *Our Miss Brooks* stood as one of radio's most beloved comedy programs, translating Connie's exploits into must-hear entertainment for millions of American families. Airing on CBS from 1948 to 1957, the show proved that a strong female protagonist with intelligence, independence, and humor could carry a series to the top of the ratings. Eve Arden became a household name, and the show's success would eventually lead to a film adaptation and television series, cementing its place in entertainment history. These AFRS transcriptions—created for Armed Forces Radio Service—preserved episodes for troops overseas, ensuring servicemen and women could enjoy a taste of home.

So tune in and experience why America couldn't get enough of Connie Brooks. You'll laugh, you'll cringe at her predicaments, and you'll discover why this 1955 gem remains a testament to classic radio comedy at its finest.
