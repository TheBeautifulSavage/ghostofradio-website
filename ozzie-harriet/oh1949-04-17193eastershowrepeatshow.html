# The Adventures of Ozzie & Harriet: Easter Show (April 17, 1949)

Tune in this spring morning for a delightful journey into the Nelson household as Ozzie and Harriet prepare for Easter Sunday—but naturally, nothing goes quite according to plan. As the family bustles about with preparations for the holiday, schemes begin to unravel, misunderstandings multiply, and the good-natured chaos that America has come to adore settles over the cozy Nelson home. Listeners will find themselves transported to a world of pastel-colored decorations, hidden Easter baskets, and the gentle humor that made this program a beloved fixture in living rooms across the nation. The warm, genuine chemistry between the cast crackles through the airwaves, creating an intimate portrait of American family life that feels both timeless and unmistakably of its moment.

By 1949, The Adventures of Ozzie & Harriet had already become a cultural phenomenon, with the real-life married couple bringing their authentic domestic comedy to CBS audiences. This particular repeat broadcast captures the show at its height, when families would gather around their radio sets with the same anticipation viewers would later reserve for television. The Nelson family—featuring the couple's actual sons, David and Ricky—represented an idealized yet relatable vision of American domesticity, free from the cynicism that would later characterize comedy, instead celebrating the gentle comedy of everyday life. The holiday episode format was always a special occasion, allowing writers to weave seasonal sentiment through the familiar domestic mishaps listeners cherished.

Don't miss this charming slice of radio history, a reminder of simpler times when families tuned in together for wholesome laughter and the gentle wisdom that Ozzie and Harriet dispensed with every broadcast. Press play and step back into 1949.
