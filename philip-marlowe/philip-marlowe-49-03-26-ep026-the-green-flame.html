Slip on your fedora and pour yourself a stiff drink—Philip Marlowe is about to walk into the kind of case that sticks to you like cigarette smoke in a dimly lit office. When a mysterious emerald known as the Green Flame vanishes from a locked vault, the hard-boiled detective finds himself entangled with blackmailers, society dames with dangerous secrets, and a killer who leaves no witnesses. What begins as a simple missing object case spirals into a web of extortion and murder that will test Marlowe's wits, his code of honor, and whether he'll live long enough to collect his fee. Through shadowy narration and the masterful direction of this CBS production, you'll hear every creak of a floorboard, every whispered threat, and every calculated move as Marlowe navigates the underworld's deadliest corners. The Green Flame isn't just a jewel—it's a death sentence waiting to find its next victim.

Drawing from Raymond Chandler's iconic literary detective, this radio adaptation captures the essence of 1940s noir with remarkable fidelity, preserving the moral ambiguity and cynical wit that made Marlowe a American icon. The CBS series was groundbreaking in bringing Chandler's complex, world-weary detective to millions of listeners in their living rooms, with stellar sound design that became the gold standard for mystery radio drama. This particular episode exemplifies the show's peak years, when writing was sharp, performances were electric, and every broadcast felt like genuine danger lurking just beyond the static.

Don't miss this classic episode of detective fiction at its finest. Experience the craftsmanship of golden-age radio—where imagination becomes reality, and one man's search for truth in a corrupt city reminds us why Philip Marlowe became a legend.
