# Philip Marlowe: "The Name to Remember"

Step into the fog-choked streets of Los Angeles as private investigator Philip Marlowe takes on a case that will test every ounce of his cynicism and his carefully guarded moral compass. In this April 1949 episode, a seemingly routine missing persons inquiry spirals into something far more sinister when Marlowe discovers that the name he's been hired to investigate carries dangerous weight in the city's criminal underworld. What begins in a modest office on Hollywood Boulevard leads through smoky nightclubs and shadowed alleyways, where every conversation crackles with tension and no one—not even Marlowe—can be certain who to trust. Van Heflin's measured, world-weary voice guides listeners through a meticulously plotted mystery where the smallest detail could prove fatal and the solution lies hidden in plain sight.

The Adventures of Philip Marlowe stands as one of the golden age's finest adaptations of Raymond Chandler's iconic detective, capturing the author's signature blend of hard-boiled action and genuine philosophical inquiry. Unlike the flashy heroics of other detective programs, this CBS series privileges atmosphere and character complexity, letting Marlowe inhabit a morally ambiguous world where justice and legality rarely align. The show's success in the post-war years reflected audiences' hunger for intelligent crime drama that treated listeners as adults capable of appreciating subtle storytelling and nuanced performances. "The Name to Remember" exemplifies why this series earned its devoted following among radio enthusiasts who craved something grittier than mainstream entertainment typically offered.

Tune in now to experience a masterclass in noir drama—where every shadow might conceal a secret, every name might change everything, and Philip Marlowe walks the line between survival and integrity. This is detective fiction at its finest.
