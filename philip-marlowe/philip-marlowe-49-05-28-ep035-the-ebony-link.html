Step into the fog-shrouded streets of 1940s Los Angeles as private detective Philip Marlowe pursues a mystery darker than the night itself. When a priceless ebony figurine vanishes from a locked study, Marlowe finds himself tangled in a web of deception involving black market art dealers, a desperate widow, and a shadowy figure from the past who'll stop at nothing to reclaim what was lost. As the tension builds with each expertly crafted scene, listeners will thrill to the unmistakable click of a loaded revolver, the sharp crack of a detective's wit cutting through danger, and the subtle desperation of characters caught between greed and survival. With every commercial break, you'll be left wondering: who holds the key to this mystery, and will Marlowe live to see daylight again?

This May 28th, 1949 broadcast represents the golden age of radio drama—when CBS brought Raymond Chandler's iconic gumshoe to life through crisp dialogue, atmospheric sound design, and stellar performances that made millions of Americans glue themselves to their receivers. "The Ebony Link" exemplifies the show's brilliant formula: blending hard-boiled detective fiction with the intimate immediacy of radio, where every footstep and whispered conversation draws you directly into Marlowe's dangerous world. The episode showcases why The Adventures of Philip Marlowe became one of the most beloved detective programs of the era, faithfully capturing Chandler's cynical worldview while leveraging radio's unique power to suggest danger and mystery through sound alone.
