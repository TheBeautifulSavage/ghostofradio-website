# The Gold Cobra

In the fog-shrouded streets of post-war Los Angeles, private detective Philip Marlowe finds himself ensnared in a deadly game of international intrigue when a mysterious golden artifact surfaces—one that powerful men are willing to kill for. This episode plunges listeners into a labyrinth of blackmail, betrayal, and cold-blooded murder, where nothing is as it seems and trust is the rarest commodity in the city. As Marlowe peels back layer after layer of deception, each revelation pulls him deeper into danger. You can almost hear the rain hammering against his office window, smell the cigarette smoke curling through dimly-lit hotel lobbies, and feel the weight of a .38 Special in his jacket pocket as he navigates a case where a priceless artifact becomes the centerpiece of a human tragedy. The Gold Cobra is classic noir tension—the kind that keeps you riveted to your radio set, anticipating the moment when Marlowe's sharp wit and sharper instincts will be his only salvation.

The Adventures of Philip Marlowe represents the golden age of detective radio drama, bringing Raymond Chandler's hardboiled protagonist to vivid life through exceptional writing and the masterful vocal performance of Van Heflin. Broadcast during the immediate post-war period, these episodes captured America's appetite for sophisticated crime stories populated by cynical detectives and morally ambiguous characters. The show's success established the template for countless detective programs that followed, proving that radio audiences craved intelligent mysteries delivered with style and attitude.

Step into the shadowy world of Philip Marlowe this evening. The Gold Cobra awaits—where desire, danger, and destiny collide in the most unforgettable forty-five minutes you'll spend with your radio.
