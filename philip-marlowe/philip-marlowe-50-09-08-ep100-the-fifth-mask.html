# The Fifth Mask

Step into the fog-shrouded streets of 1940s Los Angeles with private investigator Philip Marlowe as he unravels one of his most perplexing cases: *The Fifth Mask*. In this hundredth episode milestone, Marlowe finds himself entangled in a web of masquerade and murder, where nothing is quite what it seems and every suspect wears a different face—literally and figuratively. A wealthy socialite's stolen jade mask triggers a chain of events that leads our hard-boiled detective from glittering cocktail parties to the city's darkest corners, where blackmail, obsession, and revenge collide. The tension crackles through every line of dialogue as Marlowe peels back layers of deception, his trademark wit and world-weary wisdom the only light in an increasingly sinister darkness. You can almost hear the rain on the pavement and smell the cheap cigarette smoke as danger closes in.

By 1949, CBS's *The Adventures of Philip Marlowe* had become must-listen radio, bringing Raymond Chandler's iconic detective to life with a fidelity that captivated millions. Van Heflin's portrayal of Marlowe perfectly captured the character's moral compass and cynical charm, while the show's sophisticated writing elevated the detective genre beyond simple mystery-solving into genuine explorations of human nature. This hundredth episode represents the show at its creative peak, when writers had fully mastered the balance between hard-boiled action and psychological intrigue that made the program a critical darling and audience favorite.

Don't miss this landmark broadcast. *The Fifth Mask* showcases everything that made Philip Marlowe essential listening: the perfect marriage of noir atmosphere, intelligent storytelling, and unforgettable characters. Tune in and discover why audiences tuned in faithfully every week to follow the world's greatest private detective through the murky underbelly of Los Angeles.
