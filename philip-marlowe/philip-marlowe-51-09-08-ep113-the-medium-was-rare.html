# Philip Marlowe: The Medium Was Rare

Picture this: a fog-shrouded Los Angeles night where charlatans peddle false hope to the desperate and grieving, and Philip Marlowe finds himself caught between séance room shadows and murder most deliberate. In "The Medium Was Rare," our hard-boiled detective is hired to investigate a spiritualist whose phony communion with the dead may have crossed into something far more sinister—and profitable. As Marlowe navigates the dimly lit parlors of con artists and swindlers, he uncovers a web of blackmail, deception, and at least one very real corpse. Van Heflin delivers his trademark world-weary drawl with particular bite as Marlowe toys with a medium who's clearly playing for keeps, building tension that crackles through your radio speaker like static between stations.

CBS's *The Adventures of Philip Marlowe* stands as one of the finest adaptations of Raymond Chandler's iconic detective, faithfully translating the author's cynical wit and moral complexity to the intimate medium of radio. During the late 1940s, when this episode aired, radio drama was at its artistic peak—before television would claim America's attention—and shows like this represented the gold standard of entertainment. Heflin's portrayal of Marlowe captured what made Chandler's creation endure: a man of principle navigating a corrupt world, never quite fitting in, always questioning, forever skeptical of easy answers. The supporting cast and sound design create a Los Angeles that feels palpable and perilous in ways that still resonate today.

Step into the shadows with us as Philip Marlowe descends into the murky underworld of spiritualism and murder. Tune in now to discover whether our detective can separate the real from the counterfeit—and whether he'll survive the truth he uncovers.
