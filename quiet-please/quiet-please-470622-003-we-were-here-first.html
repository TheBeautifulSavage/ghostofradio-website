# Quiet Please: "We Were Here First"

Step into the shadows with us as an ordinary man discovers that his new home harbors extraordinary—and decidedly unwelcome—inhabitants. In this chilling installment of *Quiet Please*, the comfort of domestic life unravels when our protagonist begins to notice the unmistakable signs of previous occupants who seem reluctant to vacate. What starts as strange sounds in the walls and peculiar disturbances escalates into a sinister realization: he is not, and perhaps never was, truly alone. The masterful sound design that defines *Quiet Please* works in perfect concert with the script, creating an atmosphere of creeping dread that tightens around the listener's throat. Every creak, every whisper, every pregnant silence becomes a weapon of psychological terror, forcing you to confront the primal fear of the invaded sanctuary.

*Quiet Please* stands among radio's finest achievements in atmospheric horror, and this June 1948 episode exemplifies why the show earned its devoted following during radio's golden age. Eschewing the theatrical bombast of contemporaries, the series relied on subtlety, suggestion, and the listener's own imagination—arguably the most potent special effect ever created. The show's economy of production only enhanced its power; without elaborate sound effects or star-studded casts, *Quiet Please* proved that great drama emerges from intelligent writing and precise execution. Episodes like "We Were Here First" demonstrate the medium's unique ability to burrow into the listener's mind, creating terrors far more unsettling than anything visual could achieve.

Don't miss this masterwork of psychological suspense. Dim the lights, settle in, and prepare yourself for a tale that will make you question the safety of your own four walls. *Quiet Please* awaits.
