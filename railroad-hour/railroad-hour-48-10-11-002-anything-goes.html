Picture yourself settling into your favorite chair on this crisp October evening in 1948, the warm glow of your radio dial beckoning you aboard for a night of sophisticated entertainment. Tonight's broadcast of The Railroad Hour carries you straight into the glittering, reckless world of Cole Porter's Anything Goes—a musical comedy that captures the very essence of the Jazz Age aboard a luxurious ocean liner. As the orchestra swells with that unforgettable title number, you'll follow the comedic misadventures of a stowaway, a nightclub entertainer, and a young heiress as their fates intertwine at sea. The sharp wit, the romantic entanglements, and Porter's brilliantly crafted melodies create an atmosphere of pure theatrical magic, all unfolding before you in the intimate medium of radio drama where your imagination transforms voices and music into an entire world of glamour and intrigue.

The Railroad Hour represented something quite special in the landscape of late-1940s broadcasting—a weekly series that elevated musical theater to the radio audience with polished performances, stellar orchestration by the CBS Symphony, and an unwavering commitment to bringing Broadway's greatest hits into American living rooms. By bringing celebrated stage works to radio, the program democratized high entertainment, allowing listeners from coast to coast who might never see a Broadway production to experience these cultural treasures. This particular adaptation of Anything Goes exemplifies the show's talent for capturing the exuberance and romance of musical theater in audio form.

Don't miss this chance to experience a golden-age radio gem and discover why The Railroad Hour became essential listening for millions of Americans. Step aboard and let Porter's enchanting melodies and this engaging cast transport you to an era when life truly was anything but ordinary.
