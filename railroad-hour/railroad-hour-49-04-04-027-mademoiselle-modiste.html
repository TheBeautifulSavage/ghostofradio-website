# The Railroad Hour: Mademoiselle Modiste

Settle into your parlor chair and prepare yourself for an evening of continental romance and soaring melody. When you tune in to this April 4th broadcast of *The Railroad Hour*, you'll find yourself whisked away to the gaslit streets of Paris, where intrigue, passion, and mistaken identity collide in Victor Herbert's timeless operetta *Mademoiselle Modiste*. As the orchestra swells with that most enchanting of melodies—"Kiss Me Again"—our heroine, a clever young hat-maker named Fifi, finds herself caught between love and ambition, between the admiring chorus of Parisian suitors and the one man who truly sees her heart. The stellar cast brings wit and warmth to every scene, their voices blending in harmonies that will linger in your memory long after the final curtain falls. You can practically smell the fresh-cut flowers in the boutique and hear the distant music of street musicians as this beloved operetta unfolds before you.

*The Railroad Hour* was no ordinary variety program—it was a triumph of radio production that brought the golden age of operetta into American living rooms each week. Sponsored by the Association of American Railroads, the show married commercial messaging with genuine artistic merit, featuring elaborate orchestrations and Broadway-caliber talent that rivaled live theater. By the late 1940s, when this episode aired, radio drama was at its artistic apex, and *The Railroad Hour* stood among the finest programs the medium had produced.

Don't miss this luminous journey to Paris with one of operetta's most beloved masterworks. Tune in and let the magic of live radio transport you across the ocean and back through time itself.
