# The Railroad Hour: The New Moon

Climb aboard for an enchanting evening as The Railroad Hour presents Sigmund Romberg's timeless operetta *The New Moon*. This October broadcast transports listeners to the romantic shores of colonial Louisiana, where forbidden love blooms against a backdrop of aristocratic intrigue and sweeping melodies. As the orchestra swells with lush strings and the cast's magnificent voices fill your living room, you'll journey alongside the mysterious hero—a French nobleman exiled to the New World—and the headstrong woman who captures his heart. The drama crackles with genuine passion: star-crossed lovers, class conflict, and the soaring crescendos of some of operetta's most beloved numbers, including the unforgettable "Wanting You." Each scene seamlessly weaves narrative and music, creating an intimate theatrical experience that proves radio drama at its finest.

*The Railroad Hour* represented something revolutionary in American broadcasting: a weekly celebration of the musical theater that brought Broadway into American homes with production values rivaling the stage itself. Launched in 1948 by the Association of American Railroads as cultural programming, the series became a treasure trove of golden age operettas and musical comedies. This 1954 presentation of *The New Moon*, with its sumptuous orchestrations and powerhouse cast, exemplifies why the program earned devoted listeners across the nation who gathered around their sets each week, eager for both artistic sophistication and escapist romance.

Don't miss this glittering gem from radio's golden age. *The New Moon* awaits—tune in and let Romberg's enchanting melodies and a cast of virtuoso performers transport you to a world where love conquers all. The magic of the stage lives in your radio tonight.
