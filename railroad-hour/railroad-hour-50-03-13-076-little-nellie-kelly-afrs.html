Step aboard the express as The Railroad Hour presents a tender journey through the life of Little Nellie Kelly, a young Irish girl whose heart beats with the rhythm of the rails. On this March evening in 1950, listeners will find themselves swept up in a tale of immigrant dreams and small-town railroading, where a chance encounter on a platform becomes the thread that weaves together family, love, and redemption. The orchestra swells with period melodies as our heroine navigates the challenges of belonging in a new world, where the steady whistle of the locomotive becomes as much a character in her story as any of the colorful figures who cross her path. You'll hear the gentle clatter of wheels on steel, the comforting murmur of station crowds, and songs that capture the bittersweet longing of those who built this nation one mile of track at a time.

The Railroad Hour was America's most ambitious musical drama series, transforming the locomotive from mere transportation into a stage for human stories. Sponsored by the Association of American Railroads, the program celebrated the industry's golden age while crafting deeply human narratives that resonated with millions who depended on the rails for their livelihoods and dreams. With its stellar casts, lush orchestral arrangements, and scripts grounded in authentic railroad experience, the show became appointment listening for families across the country during the post-war era.

Don't miss this touching installment featuring the beloved "Little Nellie Kelly"—a reminder of when radio drama could transport you to a far-away station platform and leave you forever changed by what you found there.
