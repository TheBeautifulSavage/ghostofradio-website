# Song of Norway - The Railroad Hour

Picture yourself settling into your favorite armchair on a crisp April evening in 1950, the glow of your radio dial warming the parlor as The Railroad Hour's familiar overture swells through your speaker. Tonight, you're bound for the Norwegian fjords as the orchestra transports you to the Scandinavian highlands, where the passionate melodies of Edvard Grieg come alive through the voices of Broadway's finest. This musical drama weaves the composer's greatest works into a journey through his life—from youthful romance and artistic struggle to triumphant recognition—all while celebrating the beauty of Norway's rugged landscape. The locomotive of melody rolls forward with each song, carrying you through moments of genuine tenderness and dramatic intensity that only live radio performance could deliver.

The Railroad Hour represented radio's golden age at its most ambitious: a program that proved Americans' appetite for classical music was as voracious as their love of popular entertainment. By 1950, this ABC series had already earned its place in listeners' hearts by treating serious music not as highbrow intimidation but as thrilling drama, accessible and deeply human. Each episode featured full orchestration and Broadway talent, making concert hall experiences available to millions who would never set foot in a concert hall. "Song of Norway" exemplifies this mission perfectly, transforming Grieg's biography into an intimate story of artistic dedication that resonated across America's living rooms and farmhouses alike.

Don't miss your ticket aboard this musical express. Tune in to experience the romance, struggle, and triumph of a composer whose music still stirs the soul—heard here as it was meant to be, brought vividly to life by singers, orchestra, and the boundless imagination that only radio could offer. All aboard for Norway!
