# The Railroad Hour: Madame Sherry

Step into the opulent world of European high society as The Railroad Hour whisks you away aboard an express bound for Romance. In this April 17th broadcast of "Madame Sherry," listeners will encounter a delightful musical comedy of mistaken identities and theatrical intrigue. Picture the ornate parlor cars of a transatlantic liner, where a mysterious widowed countess upends the carefully ordered lives of a wealthy American family. As the plot twists and turns with the rhythm of the rails, sophisticated orchestral arrangements underscore moments of genuine pathos and sudden comedic revelations. The Railroad Hour's superb cast brings genuine warmth to this beloved operetta, their voices soaring through glittering production numbers that transport you directly to the gilded ballrooms of Paris and the drawing rooms of Manhattan society.

The Railroad Hour represents a golden age of American radio drama, when the networks could justify the expense of full orchestras, elaborate scripts, and Broadway's finest talent for the simple gift of an hour's entertainment in your living room. This particular episode, drawn from the 1910 operetta by Karl Hoschna and Otto Harbach, showcases why this weekly series became essential listening for millions of Americans. During the post-war years, such programs offered more than mere escapism—they provided cultural sophistication and theatrical artistry to families in small towns and big cities alike, all for the price of a radio set.

Don't miss this sparkling gem of theatrical radio. Tune in to hear how one woman's secrets could reshape an entire family's fortune, all wrapped in the infectious melodies and charming dialogue that made The Railroad Hour America's most beloved musical drama series. All aboard for an evening of sophisticated entertainment!
