# The Railroad Hour: Madame Sherry

Step aboard for an enchanting evening as The Railroad Hour presents the delightful operetta *Madame Sherry*, a sparkling confection of romance, mistaken identity, and toe-tapping melodies that will transport you from your living room to the sun-drenched streets of Paris. Picture the scene: a wealthy American widow arrives in the City of Light, her reputation preceding her, and suddenly the carefully laid plans of scheming relatives and lovesick suitors begin to unravel in the most hilarious ways imaginable. With the orchestra in fine form and the cast delivering performances that crackle with warmth and theatrical panache, this episode captures everything audiences adored about the golden age of light opera—the kind of sophisticated entertainment that made Sunday evenings something the entire family gathered around the radio to experience together.

*The Railroad Hour*, which graced ABC airwaves from 1948 to 1954, represented a uniquely American fusion of commerce and culture: a lavishly produced musical drama sponsored by the Association of American Railroads, designed to remind listeners of the romance and adventure that rail travel represented to a post-war nation. Each week, the show brought Broadway and operetta classics to life with first-rate singers, a full orchestra, and production values that rivaled theater itself. By broadcasting this particular April 1949 performance of *Madame Sherry*—originally a 1910 Broadway hit by Karl Hoschna—the producers ensured that this timeless gem reached millions who might never set foot in a concert hall, democratizing highbrow entertainment for America's heartland.

Settle in for an hour of pure escapism and melodic joy. *Madame Sherry* awaits, with all the elegance and exuberance of a bygone era preserved in pristine audio. Tune in and rediscover why radio drama was truly golden.
