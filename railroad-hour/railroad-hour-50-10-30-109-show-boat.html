# The Railroad Hour: Show Boat

Step aboard the Cotton Blossom as The Railroad Hour brings you Jerome Kern's immortal masterpiece, *Show Boat*, in a lavish production that captures the romance, heartbreak, and raw humanity of life along the Mississippi River. This evening's broadcast whisks you away to the golden age of riverboat theater, where a humble engineer falls hopelessly in love with a showboat magnate's daughter, their passion colliding against the social currents and racial tensions of a bygone era. With full orchestra accompaniment and a stellar cast bringing every note to life, you'll experience the soaring melodies—including the unforgettable "Ol' Man River"—that made Kern's 1927 composition an American classic. The drama unfolds with intimate character moments between musical numbers, allowing listeners to feel the weight of impossible choices and forbidden love as the story navigates from the grandeur of the Cotton Blossom's stages to the intimate moments that define these lives forever changed by their encounter.

*Show Boat* represents the gold standard of musical theater, and The Railroad Hour's adaptation honors that legacy while bringing new vitality to a work that had already captivated America for nearly two decades by the time this 1950 broadcast aired. The show's willingness to grapple with serious themes—miscegenation, abandonment, and the hollow promise of show business—set it apart as more than mere entertainment, making it a cultural touchstone that resonated across radio audiences nationwide.

Tune in as the calliope music beckons and the gangplank lowers. Whether you're a devoted admirer of Kern's genius or discovering *Show Boat* for the first time, this broadcast promises an evening of theatrical splendor that justifies its place in the American songbook. Don't miss it.
