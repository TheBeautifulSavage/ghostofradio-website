Railroad Hour 50 12 18 (116) Brigadoon

Step aboard for an enchanting journey into the misty Scottish Highlands as The Railroad Hour presents Brigadoon, the beloved Lerner and Loewe musical that captivated Broadway audiences. This December evening broadcast whisks listeners away to a magical village that appears only once every hundred years, where an American tourist stumbles upon love, wonder, and an impossible choice. With the orchestra in full bloom and the company delivering soaring renditions of "Fiona," "The Heather on the Hill," and the haunting "Almost Like Being in Love," you'll feel the fog rolling across the moors and hear the yearning in every note. The drama unfolds with touching sincerity—this is no mere musical revue, but a genuine love story caught between two worlds, two cultures, and the eternal question of whether true love can exist outside of time itself.

The Railroad Hour represented radio's golden age sophistication, bringing Broadway's finest directly into American living rooms during the post-war years. Brigadoon, having premiered on stage just months before this broadcast, was still fresh in the public imagination—a modern fairy tale that resonated deeply with audiences seeking escape and romance during the prosperous late 1940s. These radio adaptations served as a gateway for millions who would never see a Broadway production, democratizing high culture and proving that elaborate theatrical experiences could be recreated through skilled performances and imaginative orchestration.

Join host Gordon MacRae and a stellar supporting cast as they bring Fiona's enchanted world to life through your radio speaker. Whether you're a devoted musical theater enthusiast or simply seeking an evening of expertly crafted entertainment and timeless melodies, this Railroad Hour presentation promises an unforgettable trip to a village where magic and heartbreak intertwine. Tune in tonight—Brigadoon awaits.
