# The Railroad Hour: "The New Moon"

Step into the velvet darkness of a 1949 evening as The Railroad Hour presents Sigmund Romberg's "The New Moon," a sweeping operetta of passion and destiny set against the romance of colonial New Orleans. As the orchestra swells with lush strings and the cast's soaring voices fill your living room, you'll be transported to a world of masked balls, forbidden love, and the revolutionary fervor of the Louisiana bayou. The dramatic tension crackles between a mysterious French sea captain and a aristocratic beauty caught between duty and desire, their voices intertwining in some of the most achingly beautiful melodies ever composed for the American musical stage. This is radio drama at its most elegant—no elaborate sets needed, only the power of music and performance to paint an entire world in your imagination.

The Railroad Hour represented the golden age of radio's commitment to sophisticated entertainment, bringing Broadway's grandest spectacles directly into American homes each week. Airing on ABC during the post-war years when radio remained the nation's primary source of evening entertainment, the show championed operetta and musical theater with a reverence often lost in later decades. By adapting classic scores for the intimate medium of radio, The Railroad Hour kept these treasured melodies alive for millions of listeners who might never see a stage production, democratizing high culture in an era before television would forever change entertainment consumption.

"The New Moon" episode captures the show at the height of its artistry—a perfect marriage of superior musical material and broadcast excellence. Whether you're a devoted operetta enthusiast or simply seeking an evening of genuine romance and musical enchantment, this transmission promises to transport you across time itself. Tune in and discover why families gathered around their radios for this unforgettable hour of song and storytelling.
