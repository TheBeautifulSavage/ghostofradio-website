# Railroad Hour: Sari

Step aboard for an enchanting evening of musical romance as The Railroad Hour presents *Sari*, a sweeping operetta that transports listeners from the modern rails to the glittering ballrooms of Imperial Europe. This December evening in 1951 brings to life the passionate story of a Hungarian countess and her forbidden love, woven through some of the most magnificent melodies ever composed for the stage. As the orchestra swells with the distinctive sound that made this program America's most beloved musical drama, you'll find yourself utterly transported—caught between the whistle of steam trains and the intoxicating waltz of Old World elegance. The Radio City production values shine brilliantly here, with a stellar cast bringing authenticity and yearning to every note, every breath, every moment of heart-wrenching decision that defines this timeless tale.

The Railroad Hour occupied a singular place in American entertainment during the late 1940s and early 1950s, when radio drama still commanded the nation's evening attention with the power that television would soon claim. The show's genius lay in its fusion of two seemingly incompatible elements: the romance and adventure of America's railroad heritage, and the sophisticated world of grand opera and operetta. Each week, host Gordon MacRae would frame a beloved classical work with railroad-themed bookends, creating a unique listening experience that elevated popular taste while remaining thoroughly accessible. *Sari* exemplifies this formula at its finest—a European masterpiece now democratized for millions of Americans gathered around their sets.

Don't miss this magnificent presentation. Tune in as The Railroad Hour pulls into the station with one of operetta's most treasured gems. Settle back, close your eyes, and let the music carry you away—this is radio drama at its golden-age finest.
