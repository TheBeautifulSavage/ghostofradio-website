# The Railroad Hour: One Touch of Venus

Step aboard for an evening of celestial romance and sophisticated musical comedy as *The Railroad Hour* presents "One Touch of Venus," a delightful adaptation of S.J. Perelman and Ogden Nash's Broadway sensation. When a statue of the Roman goddess comes to life in modern-day Manhattan, chaos and charm collide in equal measure. Listen as the orchestra swells with lush arrangements while our hapless protagonist navigates the bewildering predicament of being pursued by immortal beauty—all wrapped in the witty, urbane dialogue that made the original stage production a sensation. This is radio at its most enchanting: comedy, romance, and melody woven together for an evening of pure escape from the mundane world.

*The Railroad Hour* represented something uniquely American in the post-war broadcasting landscape: theater brought directly into living rooms across the nation. Between 1948 and 1954, this ABC staple became a cultural institution, transforming Broadway's greatest musicals into intimate radio performances. The show's producers understood that radio audiences craved sophistication and quality—that the same listeners who packed theaters in New York and Chicago deserved first-rate entertainment at home. By adapting recent Broadway hits with stellar casts and full orchestral accompaniment, *The Railroad Hour* democratized the musical theater experience during an era when distance and economics made live theater inaccessible to millions.

Whether you're a devoted fan of classic radio, a devotee of Golden Age musicals, or simply seeking an evening of intelligent, beautifully-produced entertainment, "One Touch of Venus" offers the perfect portal to an era when radio was theater, and families gathered together to experience stories told with wit, warmth, and genuine artistry. Tune in and let yourself be transported.
