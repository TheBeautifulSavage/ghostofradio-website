# The Railroad Hour: "Rip Van Winkle"

Step aboard the midnight express and prepare yourself for a journey through time itself. In this enchanting September episode, *The Railroad Hour* brings Washington Irving's timeless tale to life with all the splendor of a Broadway musical unfolding in your living room. As our hero Rip Van Winkle stumbles aboard a mysterious train, the orchestra swells with haunting melodies—you'll hear the clash of steel wheels against track, the lonesome whistle cutting through a Catskill night, and the voices of a full chorus transporting you from colonial America to a bewildering future. The drama crackles with that peculiar magic only radio could conjure: the tension of Rip's disorientation, the romantic yearning of a forgotten love, all woven through unforgettable songs that would linger in listeners' minds long after the final fade-out.

*The Railroad Hour* itself was a marvel of American broadcasting—a weekly showcase where the railroads' golden age found its perfect medium. Between 1948 and 1954, ABC's ambitious program married the romance of train travel with theatrical storytelling, featuring Broadway talent and lavish orchestrations that rivaled anything on stage. Each week, a different classic tale received the full musical treatment, transforming familiar stories into something grander, more emotional, more *alive*. The sponsorship by the Association of American Railroads meant that the show could spare no expense, and that production value resonates through every crackling inch of preserved tape.

Don't miss this remarkable relic of radio's golden age. Tune in to hear how a simple folktale becomes an unforgettable musical experience, and discover why audiences in 1949 couldn't wait to hear what *The Railroad Hour* would deliver next week. Some magic simply cannot be recaptured—only experienced.
