# The Railroad Hour: "State Fair"

Step aboard for a journey through the heartland as *The Railroad Hour* brings you Rogers and Hammerstein's beloved musical classic, *State Fair*. Picture yourself in a small Iowa town on the eve of the great agricultural exposition—families buzzing with anticipation, prize livestock being groomed, and young hearts stirring with romance and possibility. In this enchanting episode, you'll follow the Frake family's adventures at the fair itself, where a young farm girl finds herself torn between a country boy's steady devotion and a charismatic newspaper reporter's promises of excitement beyond the cornfields. The orchestra swells as the famous melodies unfold: "It Might as Well Be Spring" and "That's for Me" carry the weight of youthful longing and the bittersweet knowledge that some summers change everything. The talented cast brings warmth and authenticity to every scene, and by the time the final note rings out, you'll feel the dust of the fairgrounds under your feet and the ache of summer love in your chest.

This 1949 broadcast represents *The Railroad Hour* at its peak—a program that distinguished itself by bringing Broadway's greatest musicals to America's living rooms each week. Unlike variety shows or dramatic serials, *The Railroad Hour* honored the integrity of its source material while adapting it for radio's intimate medium, where voices and music became the scenery. Sponsored by the Association of American Railroads, the show cleverly positioned trains as the romantic connective tissue linking America's heartland to its dreams and desires.

Don't miss this timeless celebration of American optimism and pastoral charm. Settle in with the static's gentle crackle, and let *The Railroad Hour* transport you to a state fair where anything still seems possible—where a train ticket might arrive in tomorrow's mail, and love waits at the next turn.
