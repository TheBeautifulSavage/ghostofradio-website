Step aboard the luxurious observation car as The Railroad Hour presents "Madame Sherry," a sparkling operetta that captures the wit and romance of turn-of-the-century Paris. In this enchanting episode, listeners will find themselves swept into a world of mistaken identities, sophisticated charm, and delightful musical numbers that exemplify everything audiences adored about Broadway's golden age. Picture the elegant dining car as our heroine navigates the complications of love and reputation, all while the orchestra swells with the melodies that made Karl Hoschna's operetta a sensation decades earlier. The warm voices of the cast carry you through drawing-room intrigues and tender romantic moments, punctuated by the distinctive sound effects that place you firmly in the world of turn-of-the-century romance—the clink of champagne glasses, the rustle of fine fabrics, and the gentle motion of wheels on track.

The Railroad Hour, which aired on ABC throughout the late 1940s and early 1950s, represented radio's commitment to preserving theatrical traditions during television's rise. Each week, the program transported listeners from their living rooms to grand opera houses and intimate theaters, featuring full orchestras, Broadway talent, and meticulously adapted classic operettas and musical plays. This 1948 broadcast of "Madame Sherry" exemplifies the show's sophisticated approach—bringing the glamour and artistry of the stage to America's families gathered around their radios.

For devotees of golden-age radio and those seeking a taste of entertainment's elegant past, "Madame Sherry" offers a perfect escape. Tune in and discover why The Railroad Hour earned its devoted following, reminding us of a time when radio was the gateway to Broadway magic, delivered directly into your home.
