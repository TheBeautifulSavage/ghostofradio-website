# Railroad Hour Episode: Rose Marie

Step into the plush elegance of a 1949 evening as *The Railroad Hour* transports you across the transcontinental rails and into the heart of French Canada, where the Canadian Rockies loom majestically against a starlit sky. This enchanting production brings Rudolph Friml's beloved operetta *Rose Marie* to vivid life, featuring the luminous voice of mezzo-soprano Rise Stevens in the title role. As the mysterious Rose Marie navigates the rugged wilderness and the conflicting claims of love and duty, listeners will find themselves swept into a world of soaring melodies, romantic longing, and the kind of theatrical grandeur that only live radio performance could deliver. The orchestra swells with each emotional crescendo, the sound effects team conjures the crackling of campfires and the whistle of mountain winds, and the ensemble casts their voices in perfect harmony—it's grand opera for the American living room, intimate yet undeniably magnificent.

*The Railroad Hour* represented something unique in radio's golden age: a weekly celebration of American and European musical theater, sponsored by the Association of American Railroads to remind listeners of the romance and adventure that train travel represented. From 1948 to 1954, this program became a cultural touchstone, bringing Broadway and operatic standards to millions of households. *Rose Marie*, with its exotic setting and passionate score, exemplified the show's mission to make high culture accessible to everyone, regardless of their proximity to a concert hall or theater district.

Dust off your imagination and tune in to this magnificent performance—no ticket required, just the simple pleasure of tuning your dial and letting the music transport you. This is radio at its most enchanting.
