# The Railroad Hour: Mademoiselle Modiste

Settle into your easy chair on this November evening in 1951 and prepare for a journey into the heart of Paris, where love blooms as delicately as the fashions that grace the grand boulevards. In this enchanting episode of *The Railroad Hour*, you'll be transported to Victor Herbert's beloved operetta *Mademoiselle Modiste*, where a humble shopgirl dreams of romance and a brighter future. As the orchestra swells with the composer's most lyrical melodies, our heroine's story unfolds against a backdrop of elegant gowns and earnest passion. The incomparable cast brings each character to life with vocal artistry that captures both the sophisticated charm of Parisian society and the tender vulnerability of a young woman's heart. Between the sweeping romantic duets and comedic flourishes of Gallic wit, you'll find yourself utterly enchanted—transported from your living room to the very heart of Belle Époque Europe.

*The Railroad Hour* was America's premier musical drama anthology, a weekly celebration of operetta and light musical theater that brought Broadway's greatest treasures into American homes with lavish orchestral arrangements and first-rate talent. Premiering in 1948 on ABC, the program became a cultural touchstone for listeners who craved sophisticated entertainment delivered with Hollywood-caliber production values. By featuring classic Victor Herbert, Rudolf Friml, and Sigmund Romberg scores, the show preserved these theatrical jewels for radio audiences who might never experience them on stage. Each week's episode was a complete dramatic experience, complete with full orchestration and talented vocalists who treated every performance as a Broadway opening night.

This is an opportunity to experience radio drama at its finest—to hear the golden age of American entertainment exactly as listeners heard it more than seventy years ago. Tune in and let *The Railroad Hour* whisk you away.
