# The Red Mill - Railroad Hour Episode from December 10, 1951

Step into the ornate parlor car as The Railroad Hour pulls into the station for an evening of pure theatrical magic with Victor Herbert's beloved operetta, *The Red Mill*. This December broadcast transports listeners to a quaint Dutch village where romance blooms as sweetly as the windmill's turning blades. As the orchestra swells and the cast brings Herbert's timeless melodies to life, you'll find yourself swept up in the tale of two American tourists caught in a whirlwind of mistaken identities and unexpected love. The careful staging that ABC's musical drama specialists have orchestrated crackles with energy—hear the authentic creaking of the mill, the infectious charm of the ensemble, and the soaring romantic duets that made Herbert a household name. It's the kind of evening that made millions of Americans fall in love with operetta during the golden age of radio.

The Railroad Hour represented something genuinely special in postwar American entertainment: a weekly commitment to preserving the great theatrical traditions of an earlier era while keeping them vibrantly alive for a new generation. Premiering in 1948, the series built its reputation by showcasing classic American operettas and musical comedies with lavish production values and stellar casts, all while framing each episode as a casual evening's entertainment aboard a sophisticated train journey. *The Red Mill*, first performed in 1906, was already a half-century old by this broadcast, yet the show's timeless humor and Herbert's unforgettable score proved that great music transcends decades.

Don't miss this beautifully preserved window into an America that cherished melody, theatrical grandeur, and the simple pleasure of quality entertainment delivered right into the home. Tune in to experience why millions gathered around their radios on December 10th for The Railroad Hour.
