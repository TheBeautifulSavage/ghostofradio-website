# The Railroad Hour: Kiss Me Kate

Step aboard for an evening of sophisticated Manhattan glamour and theatrical passion as The Railroad Hour presents Cole Porter's *Kiss Me Kate*, the smash hit that captured Broadway's golden heart. On this March 24th broadcast, you'll be transported from the comfort of your living room straight into the backstage drama of a theatrical production, where a temperamental divorced pair must confront their clashing egos—and their undeniable chemistry—while performing Shakespeare's *The Taming of the Shrew*. Expect the kind of glittering, urbane wit that made Porter a household name, coupled with elaborate musical numbers that showcase the finest voices radio's golden age could offer. The familiar crackle of the orchestra swells as our story unfolds, punctuated by the witty repartee and romantic tension that propelled this musical to become one of the decade's most beloved stage productions.

*The Railroad Hour* itself represents a remarkable achievement in radio entertainment—a weekly showcase that brought Broadway's greatest musicals directly into American homes, adapted and performed by some of the era's most talented performers. By 1952, when this episode aired, the show had already earned its reputation as radio's most prestigious musical drama program, carefully crafted for audiences who craved both sophistication and spectacle in their home entertainment. These adaptations preserved the essential magic of the original productions while making them accessible to millions who might never see a stage, democratizing theater in a way that anticipated later television culture.

This is radio at its most enchanting—orchestral grandeur, stellar talent, and a story that reminds us why we fell in love with Broadway's golden age. Tune in as The Railroad Hour carries you away on a journey of music, romance, and theatrical brilliance. A night of pure entertainment awaits.
