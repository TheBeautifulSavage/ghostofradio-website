# The Little Minister

Step into the misty highlands of Scotland as *The Railroad Hour* brings you an unforgettable journey through J.M. Barrie's beloved *The Little Minister*. This June evening broadcast transports listeners to a world of heather-covered moors and passionate hearts, where a young minister's faith is tested by love and duty. With soaring orchestral accompaniment and a stellar cast bringing warmth and nuance to every word, you'll find yourself spellbound by the tender romance at the story's core—a tale of sacrifice, conviction, and the transformative power of human connection. The richly layered dialogue crackles with emotion, punctuated by lush musical interludes that underscore the emotional stakes as secrets unravel and resolve beneath a Scottish sky.

*The Railroad Hour* represented something truly special in American broadcasting: a thirty-minute gateway to theatrical splendor that required no admission price, no journey to a distant cinema palace, just the turn of a dial. Between 1948 and 1954, the show brought classic literature and beloved plays to vivid life through stellar performances and ambitious musical arrangements, proving that radio drama could rival any stage production. Each week, listeners experienced the full sweep of human emotion and adventure, making culture accessible to millions of Americans in their own living rooms—a democratic spirit that defined the Golden Age of Radio.

Whether you're a devoted admirer of Barrie's work or simply seeking an evening's excellent entertainment, this stellar adaptation of *The Little Minister* awaits you. Tune in as our talented ensemble cast and orchestra weave together romance, faith, and destiny in a performance that captures everything that made classic radio theater immortal. Clear your schedule for an hour of timeless storytelling—you won't want to miss it.
