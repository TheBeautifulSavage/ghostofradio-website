# The Railroad Hour: Annie Laurie

As the opening whistle of The Railroad Hour pierces through your radio speaker on this September evening in 1949, you're transported to the misty Scottish Highlands where love and longing intertwine like the notes of a haunting melody. Tonight's presentation, "Annie Laurie," weaves the timeless Scottish ballad into a dramatic tapestry of passion and devotion, as accomplished singers and actors bring the tragic romance to life. You'll find yourself caught between the soaring orchestral arrangements and the intimate moments of dialogue that pulse at the heart of this musical drama—moments where a glance or a whispered confession carries the weight of an entire lifetime of yearning. The production builds toward its emotional crescendo with the kind of theatrical grandeur that only live radio could achieve, where every note and every spoken word reaches across the airwaves directly into your home.

The Railroad Hour represented something remarkable during American radio's golden age: proof that popular entertainment could be both accessible and artistically ambitious. Running for six seasons on ABC, the show became known for transforming classic songs and literary works into sophisticated musical dramas, performed by some of the finest voices in radio and supported by a full orchestra. "Annie Laurie," with its roots deep in Scottish folk tradition, exemplified the show's mission to celebrate cultural heritage through the medium that had become the nation's hearth. Each episode was a self-contained theatrical experience, broadcast live with all the spontaneity and precision that live performance demanded.

Tune in tonight and experience why millions of Americans made The Railroad Hour an appointment with their radios. Whether you're drawn to the soaring melodies, the compelling drama, or simply the magic of words and music transported through the air, this episode promises the kind of entertainment that defined an era—intimate, grand, and utterly unforgettable.
