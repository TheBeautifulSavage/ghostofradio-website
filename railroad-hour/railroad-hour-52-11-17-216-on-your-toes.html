# The Railroad Hour: On Your Toes

Step into the glittering world of Broadway as The Railroad Hour brings you Rodgers and Hart's sparkling masterpiece, *On Your Toes*. This November evening, as you settle into your favorite chair with the radio's warm glow illuminating the parlor, you'll be transported backstage to a tale of ambition, romance, and the irresistible pull of the stage. The orchestra swells with that unforgettable "Slap That Bass" number, and suddenly you're dancing alongside Junior Dolan, a vaudeville hoofer caught between his love of the footlights and his unexpected role in a Russian ballet company. Through crystal-clear performances and the Railroad Hour's signature lush orchestrations, this beloved 1936 Rodgers and Hart classic comes alive with all the sophistication and charm that made it a Broadway sensation. Expect wit, romance, and melodies that will linger in your mind long after the final notes fade.

The Railroad Hour represents a golden age of American radio, where Broadway's greatest hits were adapted and performed live for millions of listeners across the nation. By bringing theatrical masterpieces into American homes at no cost, the show democratized culture itself—families who might never set foot in a New York theater could experience world-class musical drama simply by turning a dial. Each episode showcased stellar casts and orchestras, making The Railroad Hour a cultural institution that rivaled the legitimate stage itself. In 1951, this particular adaptation of *On Your Toes* captured the sophistication and energy that made both Rodgers and Hart and the show itself timeless treasures.

Don't miss this chance to experience Broadway's golden age as it was meant to be heard—intimate, immediate, and utterly captivating. Tune in tonight for *On Your Toes* on The Railroad Hour.
