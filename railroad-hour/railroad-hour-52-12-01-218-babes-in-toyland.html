# The Railroad Hour: Babes in Toyland

Step aboard for a magical winter's journey as *The Railroad Hour* brings Victor Herbert's beloved operetta *Babes in Toyland* to vivid life on this December evening in 1952. Picture yourself in a cozy living room, the glow of the radio dial casting amber light across your face as the orchestra swells into that unmistakable overture—suddenly you're transported to a fantastic realm where toy soldiers march, villains scheme, and love blooms amid enchanted forests. This production features the luminous voices of the show's stellar cast performing Herbert's most enchanting melodies with the warmth and theatrical grandeur that made operetta a national obsession. The sound effects team brings the magical kingdom alive: the creak of gingerbread houses, the whimsical tinkle of toy shop bells, even the ominous footsteps of the Toymaker's enemies pursuing our innocent heroes through a winter landscape.

For over a decade, *The Railroad Hour* captured America's imagination by transforming grand operettas into intimate radio dramas, proving that musical theater didn't require footlights to cast its spell. Broadcast live each week from Studio A in Los Angeles, the program featured Broadway's finest talent and lush orchestral arrangements conducted by Wilfred Pelletier. The show became a beacon of sophistication during the early 1950s, when families gathered around their radios hungry for genuine artistry and melodic beauty. *Babes in Toyland*, with its blend of whimsy and classical music, exemplified the show's mission to make opera and operetta accessible to every American household.

Don't miss this enchanting adaptation—settle in with the family, extinguish the lights save for that amber dial glow, and let yourself be transported to a land where anything is possible.
