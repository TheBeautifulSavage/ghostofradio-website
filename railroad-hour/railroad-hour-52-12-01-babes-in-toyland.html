# Railroad Hour 52 12 01 – Babes in Toyland

Step aboard for a holiday journey into wonder as The Railroad Hour transforms Victor Herbert's beloved operetta *Babes in Toyland* into a magical broadcast spectacular. This December 1st presentation whisks listeners away to a toy-maker's enchanted realm where two orphaned children discover a world of music, mischief, and unexpected heartwarming moments. The orchestra swells with Herbert's soaring melodies—"Toyland," "March of the Toys," and "Go to Sleep, Slumber Deep"—while skilled voice actors bring the storybook characters to life with charm and theatrical verve. You'll hear the distinctive sound effects that make radio drama come alive: the tinkling of toy music boxes, the jingle of sleigh bells, and the ambient warmth of a perfectly realized fantasy world. As December snow falls outside your home, this broadcast becomes your fireside ticket to childlike enchantment and sophisticated musical theater combined.

The Railroad Hour represented something uniquely American in the post-war golden age of radio: ambitious musical drama that brought Broadway-quality productions directly into living rooms across the nation. Between 1948 and 1954, the series showcased classic operettas and musicals with full orchestras and accomplished vocalists, establishing itself as a cultural institution when television was still in its infancy. By choosing *Babes in Toyland*, the production tapped into decades of theatrical tradition—Herbert's 1903 operetta was already a holiday staple—while proving that radio could deliver lavish musical storytelling with intimacy that even theater couldn't match.

Tune in to this beautifully preserved artifact of radio's greatest era. The Railroad Hour awaits—all aboard for Toyland.
