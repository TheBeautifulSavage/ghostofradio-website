# Railroad Hour 53 01 12 (224): Two Hearts In Three Quarter Time

Step aboard for an evening of Continental romance as The Railroad Hour pulls into the station with "Two Hearts In Three Quarter Time," a lavish musical drama that sweeps listeners from the grand ballrooms of Vienna to the thundering rails of America. The unmistakable opening whistle fades into lush orchestral arrangements as our protagonists find themselves caught between old-world elegance and new-world ambition. A chance meeting on a luxury express train becomes the catalyst for a collision of hearts that threatens to derail both their lives—will duty or desire triumph when the final station approaches? The superb cast brings passionate intensity to this waltz-laced narrative, supported by the Radio Hour's signature orchestral magnificence, as musical interludes frame each emotional turning point with authentic Viennese charm.

The Railroad Hour occupied a unique space in late 1940s American entertainment, blending the theatrical spectacle of Broadway musicals with the intimate immediacy of radio drama. Between 1948 and 1954, the show transported millions of listeners into worlds of sophistication and sophistication—a welcome escape during the post-war years when America was eager for both glamour and escapism. With budgets that rivaled major theatrical productions, The Railroad Hour could afford marquee talent and orchestras of genuine concert caliber, making each episode feel like an elaborate stage production unfolding exclusively in the listener's imagination. "Two Hearts In Three Quarter Time" exemplifies this golden age ambition: a European romance rendered in real-time, with every swelling crescendo and intimate dialogue moment crafted for maximum emotional resonance.

Whether you're a devoted fan of classic radio drama or discovering this masterpiece for the first time, this January 1953 broadcast remains essential listening. Tune in and let the rhythmic pulse of the rails carry you toward an unforgettable evening of music, passion, and storytelling at its finest.
