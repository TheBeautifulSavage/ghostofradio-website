The Railroad Hour ABC · February 2, 1953

Railroad Hour 53 02 02 Carousel

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

Step aboard as The Railroad Hour brings you to a windswept New England carousel, where love blooms against impossible odds in this lush adaptation of Rodgers and Hammerstein's beloved musical masterpiece. On this winter evening in 1953, listeners across America will settle into their favorite chairs as the orchestra swells and the calliope music begins to turn—transporting them to a seaside town where a charming carnival barker named Billy Bigelow captures the heart of a young mill worker named Julie Jordan. What unfolds is a tale of passion, sacrifice, and redemption, punctuated by some of Broadway's most enchanting melodies. The stellar cast breathes genuine emotion into every word and song, their voices perfectly captured by the golden-age radio microphones, while the sound effects team creates the creaking boards, ocean breezes, and mechanical music of the carousel itself. It's a story about ordinary people experiencing extraordinary love—exactly the kind of intimate, human drama that made radio the heartbeat of American entertainment.

The Railroad Hour had become ABC's prestige vehicle for bringing Broadway's greatest musicals to the airwaves, and this production of Carousel represents the show at its artistic peak. Rather than simply performing the score, this radio adaptation deepens the psychological complexity of Billy and Julie's relationship, allowing the intimate medium of radio to explore the unspoken hopes and fears that the stage musical could only suggest. For dedicated listeners and newcomers alike, this is Broadway theater arriving in your living room with all its grandeur and emotional power intact.

Tune in tonight and rediscover why The Railroad Hour became one of radio's most cherished institutions—where great music, unforgettable stories, and masterful performances converged in those golden years when America listened as one.
