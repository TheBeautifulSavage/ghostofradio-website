# The Railroad Hour: Carousel

*February 2, 1953*

Step aboard for an unforgettable evening as The Railroad Hour brings Rodgers and Hammerstein's beloved *Carousel* to life through your radio speakers. Picture yourself in a turn-of-the-century New England seaside town, where the gentle glow of a carousel's painted horses beckons and a mysterious carousel barker sets fate in motion. This broadcast adaptation captures all the soaring romance, tender heartbreak, and magical wonder of the original stage musical—transformed into an intimate acoustic experience that only radio can deliver. Hear the lush orchestration swell as our cast brings Billy Bigelow and Julie Jordan's impossible love story into vivid focus, from that first enchanting encounter to the transcendent waltz that defies the boundaries between life and death itself.

The Railroad Hour had become America's premier musical drama program by the early 1950s, presenting complete Broadway shows in a format that made theatrical magic accessible to millions of listeners in their living rooms. With its sophisticated sponsorship model and commitment to quality, the series attracted top talent and maintained an impressive standard of production. *Carousel*, with its groundbreaking second act set in the afterlife and its unflinching portrayal of domestic struggle, represented precisely the kind of ambitious material that The Railroad Hour celebrated—not watered-down versions, but the full emotional weight of Hammerstein's searching libretto and Rodgers' soaring melodies.

This is radio at its finest: orchestral grandeur, dramatic intensity, and the kind of live performance energy that made Tuesday nights essential listening. Settle in with the golden-age quality that made The Railroad Hour a cultural institution, and experience why audiences considered these broadcasts treasured events.
