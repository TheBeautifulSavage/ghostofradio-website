# The Railroad Hour: Miss Liberty

Step into Grand Central Terminal as the orchestra swells and voices ring out across the airwaves in this stirring musical adaptation of Irving Berlin's beloved operetta, *Miss Liberty*. On this February evening in 1949, The Railroad Hour presents a sweeping tale of love, liberty, and the immigrant dream set against the backdrop of New York's golden age. A young reporter finds himself caught between duty and devotion as the Statue of Liberty dominates the skyline and the city pulses with romantic possibility. Berlin's iconic melodies—crafted specifically for this stage musical just two years prior—transport listeners to a Manhattan alive with possibility, where the promise of America shimmers in every note. The Radio City Symphony Orchestra, under the baton of distinguished conductor Joseph Stopak, provides lush orchestration that brings the story to vivid life through your speaker.

The Railroad Hour had become must-listen entertainment by 1949, offering dramatic adaptations of Broadway's greatest musicals with stellar casts and full orchestral accompaniment. This was appointment radio at its finest—a cultural event delivered free into American homes every Friday night. The show's commitment to theatrical excellence meant that listeners experienced Broadway spectacle without leaving their living rooms, a democratization of high culture that defined radio's golden age. Berlin's *Miss Liberty*, exploring themes of citizenship and belonging, resonated powerfully in post-war America, and The Railroad Hour's production captures that timely spirit with genuine warmth and sophistication.

Tune in to experience the magic of a bygone era when radio brought Broadway's glory into the American home. The melodies of Irving Berlin, the grandeur of a full symphony orchestra, and the romance of mid-century musical theater await. This is The Railroad Hour—where America's greatest songs find their perfect home.
