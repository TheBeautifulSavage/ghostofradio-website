# The Railroad Hour: "Miss Liberty"

As the familiar whistle of the 20th Century Limited pierces the evening air, listeners settle in for an unforgettable journey through American pride and romance. In this February 1949 broadcast, *The Railroad Hour* presents Irving Berlin's "Miss Liberty," a glorious musical celebration of immigration, ambition, and the transformative power of the rails. Picture the scene: a young immigrant's eyes widening as he catches his first glimpse of Lady Liberty from a train window, the orchestra swelling with strings and brass as the weight of possibility settles upon his shoulders. This isn't merely a show tune transplanted to radio—it's a living, breathing drama where Berlin's soaring melodies become the very heartbeat of the American Dream itself. The stellar cast brings authentic emotion to every note, their voices capturing both the trembling hope of newcomers and the proud thunder of the locomotive that carries them toward their futures.

*The Railroad Hour* stood as one of radio's most innovative achievements during its remarkable six-year run, merging the glamour of Broadway with the romance of rail travel at precisely the moment when trains still embodied the nation's forward momentum. By 1949, as post-war America accelerated toward prosperity, sponsorship by the Association of American Railroads ensured that every episode glorified not just the American spirit but the magnificent iron horses that had stitched the nation together. "Miss Liberty" exemplified this vision perfectly—a Berlin masterpiece about newcomers finding opportunity in a land of endless horizons, all set against the rhythmic pulse of America's greatest transportation triumph.

Don your finest listening attire and prepare for an evening of unmatched theatrical splendor. The engineers are ready, the rails are gleaming, and *The Railroad Hour* awaits your arrival at the station. All aboard!
