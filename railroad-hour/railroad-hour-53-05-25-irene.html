# The Railroad Hour: "Irene" (May 25, 1953)

Step into the glittering world of 1920s Manhattan as The Railroad Hour transports you to the glamorous stages of Broadway with "Irene," the beloved musical comedy that captured America's heart. This sparkling adaptation brings to life the story of a poor Irish washerwoman who stumbles into high society through a chance encounter, only to discover that true love and authenticity matter far more than wealth and status. As the orchestra swells with that unforgettable melody—"Irene, good night, Irene"—listeners will be swept up in a whirlwind of comedy, romance, and the kind of sophisticated charm that made Broadway the envy of the world. Watch as our heroine navigates ballrooms and backstage drama, all while a mysterious gentleman railway executive becomes her unlikely champion, and discover whether she'll choose her heart or her newfound fortune.

What makes The Railroad Hour such a treasure of American entertainment is its ingenious premise: a show sponsored by the Association of American Railroads, yet never preachy, where trains serve as the poetic connective tissue linking stories of human connection and transformation. These weren't mere adaptations of stage musicals—they were lovingly reimagined for radio, featuring stellar casts and orchestras that brought Broadway-caliber production values directly into living rooms across America. "Irene" remains one of the most enduring musical comedies ever created, and this 1953 broadcast captures the show at its finest, when radio drama could still rival any theatrical experience.

So settle in with a cup of coffee, let the static crackle clear, and surrender yourself to an evening of pure theatrical delight. The Railroad Hour awaits—all aboard for romance, laughter, and melodies that will linger long after the final fadeout.
