Step aboard for a delightful journey through the heartland as The Railroad Hour presents "State Fair," a musical drama that captures the enchantment of America's most beloved autumn tradition. As the orchestra swells with the strains of Rodgers and Hammerstein's immortal score, listeners are transported to the bustling fairgrounds where a farm family finds love, ambition, and self-discovery amid the carousel lights and cotton candy clouds. The magic unfolds through the voices of Broadway's finest talents, who bring warmth and authenticity to the timeless tale of a young woman's chance encounter with a smooth-talking newspaper reporter, and her brother's infatuation with a free-spirited performer. Between the musical numbers that have become standards in America's songbook—including the unforgettable title tune—the drama builds with the kind of innocent yet genuine emotion that made radio drama an essential part of American family life.

The Railroad Hour represented something singular in 1948: a prestige musical drama program that treated its audience to Broadway-quality entertainment in their own living rooms, free of charge. This November 1953 broadcast of "State Fair" showcases why the series earned such devoted listeners, adapting Rodgers and Hammerstein's 1945 film masterpiece with theatrical fidelity and emotional authenticity. The show's commitment to first-rate orchestration, casting, and production values set it apart from typical radio fare, making it a cultural touchstone for those seeking sophisticated entertainment.

Whether you're a devoted aficionado of old-time radio or discovering this golden-age gem for the first time, "State Fair" offers an evening of pure enchantment. Settle into your favorite chair, adjust the dial, and let the sounds transport you back to a simpler time when radio brought Broadway magic directly into American homes.
