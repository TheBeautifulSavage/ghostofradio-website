# The Railroad Hour: Snow White

As the opening whistle of the streamliner pierces through your living room, settle in for a magical journey into the enchanted forest where a young princess flees for her life. This December 1948 broadcast of *The Railroad Hour* transforms the beloved Grimm fairy tale into a glittering musical adventure, complete with a full orchestra, a stellar cast, and the kind of soaring production values that only network radio could deliver in its golden age. Listeners will be transported alongside Snow White as she escapes her cruel stepmother's jealous rage, discovering refuge with seven unlikely companions in a cottage deep in the woods. Every orchestral swell, every dramatic pause, every crystalline soprano note has been crafted to capture both the wonder and genuine peril of this timeless story—bringing to vivid life the danger, the romance, and the hope that made Snow White endure for centuries.

*The Railroad Hour*, ABC's prestige musical drama series, represented the pinnacle of American radio entertainment during the late 1940s. Each week, the show adapted grand stories—from classical literature to contemporary tales—wrapping them in elaborate theatrical productions that rivaled Broadway itself. The program served as a showcase for Broadway talent and emerging radio stars, all bound together by the program's signature railroading theme and the metaphor of a journey. This particular adaptation speaks to radio's unique ability to make fairy tales feel intimate and immediate, bringing Technicolor fantasy directly into homes across America through the power of sound alone.

Don't miss this sparkling gem from radio's most enchanting era. Tune in and let *The Railroad Hour* carry you away to a time when stories were told through music and imagination, and a single broadcast could transport millions of listeners to a world of wonder.
