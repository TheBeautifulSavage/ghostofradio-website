# Railroad Hour 53 12 21 Snow White

Step aboard this December evening broadcast and journey into an enchanted forest where danger lurks behind every snow-laden pine. As the locomotive whistle fades and the orchestra swells, listeners are transported to a fairy tale kingdom transformed into high musical drama. This adaptation of Snow White features soaring melodies and a full cast bringing warmth and menace to Grimm's classic tale—the Wicked Queen's jealousy burns as fiercely as a locomotive's firebox, while Snow White's pure soprano voice carries the hope of redemption through the frozen woodland. You'll hear the famous dwarfs rendered in character, their mining work paralleled cleverly to the show's railroad theme, and an ending that celebrates virtue and love with the kind of swelling orchestral grandeur that only radio could summon in the listener's imagination.

The Railroad Hour represented a unique American treasure: musical storytelling that brought Broadway-quality productions into living rooms across the nation. Running from 1948 to 1954 on ABC, the show was sponsored by the Association of American Railroads, ingeniously weaving classic fairy tales, literature, and original stories into narratives where trains symbolized progress, hope, and human connection. This December 1953 episode exemplifies the show's ambitious scope, transforming a centuries-old tale into a sophisticated musical experience featuring professional singers and a full orchestra. In an era before television dominated entertainment, The Railroad Hour proved that radio drama could be both commercially sponsored and artistically substantive.

Settle in with a warm beverage and let this golden-age broadcast remind you why millions tuned in faithfully each week. The magic of Snow White awaits—not in pixels or screens, but in the theater of the mind that only radio can create.
