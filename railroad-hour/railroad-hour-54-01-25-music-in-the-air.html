# Railroad Hour 54 01 25: Music In The Air

Step aboard the observation car as The Railroad Hour pulls into the station with a magnificent production of *Music In The Air*, that most enchanting operetta by Jerome Kern and Oscar Hammerstein II. As the locomotive's whistle fades into the night, you'll find yourself transported to the Austrian countryside where a young schoolmaster and his sweetheart discover that love and melody are inseparable companions. The rails seem to hum with the famous melodies—"I've Told Every Little Star" and "The Song Is You"—as our cast weaves a tale of artistic ambition, romance, and the transformative power of music. This January evening in 1954 promises sophisticated orchestration, charming banter, and those soaring Kern harmonies that made operetta the toast of Broadway.

The Railroad Hour had established itself as America's premier vehicle for bringing Broadway's greatest musicals to the intimate medium of radio, and this production exemplifies why the show earned such devoted listeners. Rather than simply performing musical numbers, the program's creative team adapted these celebrated works into dramatic narratives that could unfold across the airwaves, allowing middle America—farmers in Iowa, factory workers in Pittsburgh, housewives in small towns everywhere—to experience Broadway spectacle without leaving their living rooms. Kern's score, having premiered on stage in 1932, carried audiences through the Depression and beyond with its timeless elegance, and here in the early 1950s, it remains as captivating as ever.

Tune in tonight and surrender to the romance of *Music In The Air*. From opening overture to final curtain call, this is radio drama at its most elegant and emotionally satisfying—a perfect remedy for whatever troubles the world beyond your radio dial might bring. All aboard!
