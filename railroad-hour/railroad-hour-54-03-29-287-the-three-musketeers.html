# The Three Musketeers – March 29, 1954

As the opening theme swells through your radio speaker, you're transported to seventeenth-century France—but not the dusty textbooks' version. The Railroad Hour's stellar cast brings Alexandre Dumas's immortal adventure roaring to life with sweeping orchestration, sparkling dialogue, and the kind of theatrical flair that only live radio could deliver. Tonight, you'll follow young D'Artagnan's daring journey from the countryside to the glittering court of King Louis XIV, where sword-clashing adventure, loyal friendship, and romantic intrigue intertwine. The orchestra swells as our hero meets Athos, Porthos, and Aramis—and suddenly, three strangers become bound by the most sacred oath: "All for one, and one for all!" This isn't merely a serialized tale; it's a musical pageant where classical composition underscores every thrust of the blade and every declaration of honor.

*The Railroad Hour* carved its niche as a unique hybrid—a musical drama anthology series that treated classical literature and swashbuckling adventure with genuine sophistication. Running from 1948 to 1954, the show proved that radio audiences craved more than mere slapstick or melodrama; they hungered for stories of substance delivered with Broadway-caliber production values. Each episode became a complete theatrical experience, packed into thirty minutes of pure entertainment. By adapting timeless tales like *The Three Musketeers*, the show honored literature while democratizing culture—bringing the grandeur of stage productions into living rooms across America for the price of a radio.

Don't miss this magnificent evening of swordplay and camaraderie. Tune in tonight for *The Three Musketeers*, where honor, friendship, and adventure await at every turn of the dial. This is radio at its most glorious.
