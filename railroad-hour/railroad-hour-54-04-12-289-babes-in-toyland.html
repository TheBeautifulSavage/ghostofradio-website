# Railroad Hour: "Babes in Toyland"

Step aboard for a magical journey as The Railroad Hour presents Victor Herbert's beloved operetta *Babes in Toyland*, a delightful tale of wonder and adventure perfect for the entire family. On this April evening in 1949, listeners will find themselves transported to a fantastical realm where toys come to life and romance blooms in the most unexpected places. You'll hear the crystalline voices of the finest Broadway singers bringing to life the enchanting score that has captivated audiences for decades—from the rousing "March of the Toys" to the tender "Toyland" lullaby. The dramatic tension builds as our heroes navigate through mechanical marvels and whimsical perils, while the live orchestral accompaniment swells beneath every emotion, every laugh, and every soaring musical moment. This is radio as it was meant to be experienced: a complete theatrical production unfolding in your living room, where imagination becomes reality through the power of music and skilled storytelling.

*The Railroad Hour* occupied a unique and treasured place in American popular culture during the post-war years, serving as the great equalizer where high culture met the common listener. Each week, host Gordon MacRae and his stellar casts brought Broadway's greatest operettas and musical theater treasures directly into American homes, democratizing art that might otherwise remain confined to expensive theater seats. The series represented the golden age of radio drama, when sponsors like Studebaker recognized that quality programming could both entertain and elevate the national consciousness. These recordings stand as precious artifacts of American performance, capturing Broadway's golden voice and spirit preserved in amber.

Don't miss this chance to experience the wonder that captivated millions of listeners in 1949. Tune in to *The Railroad Hour* and rediscover why families gathered around their radio sets to hear these timeless melodies and cherished stories. All aboard!
