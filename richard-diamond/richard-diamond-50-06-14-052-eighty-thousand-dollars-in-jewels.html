Step into the shadowy world of Manhattan after dark, where a fortune in stolen gems sets off a desperate chain of events that will test Private Investigator Richard Diamond's wits and nerve. When eighty thousand dollars' worth of jewels vanish without a trace, Diamond finds himself entangled with anxious socialites, desperate thieves, and the kind of double-crosses that can get a man killed. The crackling dialogue and tight orchestral stings will keep you on the edge of your seat as our hero navigates a web of lies and misdirection, racing against time to recover the treasure before the case goes cold—or before he becomes expendable to someone with more to lose than money.

Richard Diamond, Private Detective was the gold standard of postwar noir radio, thriving during the golden age when millions of Americans gathered around their sets for intelligent, fast-paced crime drama. Starring Dick Powell in the title role, the show captured the essence of hard-boiled detective fiction with sophisticated writing and snappy repartee that elevated it above typical whodunits. Airing from 1949 to 1953 across NBC and CBS, the program showcased the medium at its finest—rich sound design, tight scripting, and a leading man whose world-weary charm made every case feel personal. This episode, from June 14, 1950, represents Richard Diamond at its peak, combining the show's trademark blend of danger, wit, and authentic detective work.

Tune in now and discover why Richard Diamond, Private Detective remains a masterpiece of radio drama. Whether you're a devoted fan of the noir era or discovering this classic for the first time, "Eighty Thousand Dollars In Jewels" promises an evening of gripping entertainment that only the golden age of radio could deliver.
