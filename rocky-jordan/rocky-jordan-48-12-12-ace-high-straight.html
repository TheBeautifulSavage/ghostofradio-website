When Rocky Jordan settles into the smoke-filled back room of Cairo's seediest gambling den, he's walking into a trap that reeks of double-crosses and blood money. A mysterious poker game becomes the backdrop for murder most foul in this explosive episode—one where the stakes are measured not in Egyptian pounds but in human lives. Listeners will find themselves swept into a world of marked cards, loaded dice, and men willing to kill for secrets buried beneath layers of deception. The crackle of tension builds as Rocky, ever the resourceful American agent, must navigate treacherous waters where every player at the table has something to hide and a gun within arm's reach.

Rocky Jordan, which commanded CBS airwaves from 1945 to 1951, stands as a masterclass in the adventure serial tradition, thriving during radio's golden age when audiences gathered around their sets hungry for danger and intrigue. The show distinguished itself through its exotic Middle Eastern setting—a refreshing departure from the typical American noir—and its protagonist's glib charm married to genuine investigative prowess. Jack Moyles' portrayal of Rocky made the character an instant favorite, balancing quick wit with authentic peril. Each episode transported listeners to a cosmopolitan world of intrigue where East met West, and a American troubleshooter could always be counted on to expose corruption and restore justice.

This particular episode showcases everything the series did best: tight plotting, genuine suspense, and the kind of atmospheric storytelling that proves why radio drama remains unmatched in its power to captivate the imagination. If you've never experienced the thrill of Rocky Jordan, or if you're revisiting a beloved classic, "Ace High Straight" is the perfect entry point into a world where danger lurks behind every smile and fortune favors the quick-witted. Tune in—adventure awaits.
