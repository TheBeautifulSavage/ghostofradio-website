When smoke curls through the Cairo night and a priceless artifact vanishes in the inferno, Rocky Jordan finds himself caught between desperate criminals and a mystery that burns hotter than the flames themselves. In this December 1948 episode, our globe-trotting adventurer must navigate the shadowy streets of the Egyptian capital where last-minute changes to the script only amplified the tension—what listeners will hear is a production that crackles with genuine urgency and improvisation. As fire consumes a warehouse and suspects scatter into the darkness, Rocky's quick wits and quicker fists become his only defense against men who will stop at nothing to reclaim their prize. The sound design of roaring flames, shattering glass, and the desperate shouts of men fighting in smoke-filled corridors creates an almost suffocating atmosphere that pulls you directly into the danger.

Rocky Jordan represented the golden age of radio adventure, when CBS brought exotic locales and morally ambiguous characters directly into American living rooms. Unlike the straightforward heroes of earlier serials, Rocky operated in shades of gray—a hotel owner and occasional smuggler whose connections to both the criminal underworld and legitimate society made him the perfect fixer for impossible problems. The show's strength lay in its gritty realism; these weren't comic-book adventures but stories rooted in actual Middle Eastern intrigue and wartime complexity. Jack Moyles' portrayal of Rocky became iconic for listeners who craved sophistication alongside their thrills.
