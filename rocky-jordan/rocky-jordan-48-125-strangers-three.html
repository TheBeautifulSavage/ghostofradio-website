In post-war Cairo, where shadows stretch long across the dusty bazaars and the night air hangs thick with intrigue, Rocky Jordan finds himself caught between three mysterious strangers whose paths converge at his night club with deadly consequences. When a valuable artifact surfaces in the underworld, and suddenly each of the three claims ownership, Rocky must navigate a treacherous web of deception, blackmail, and violence to uncover the truth before blood is spilled on his dance floor. With his faithful sidekick Atoll at his side and the dangerous streets of Egypt closing in, Rocky uses his quick wit and quicker fists to outmaneuver men who would kill without hesitation. This episode crackles with the palpable tension of a man in over his head, where every conversation could be the last.

Rocky Jordan arrived in 1945 when American audiences hungered for exotic adventure and morally complex heroes in morally ambiguous places. Unlike the Boy Scout straightforwardness of many radio heroes, Rocky operated in the gray zones of international intrigue—a casino operator, a gambler, a pragmatist who would bend rules but not break his code. The show's creator, Jack Johnstone, crafted a protagonist for the atomic age: sophisticated, world-weary, and entirely credible. Broadcast live twice weekly from CBS studios, these episodes captured the postwar fascination with far-flung glamour and danger that cinema would later obsess over in film noir.

Step into the smoke-filled night clubs and shadowy alleys of 1940s Cairo.
