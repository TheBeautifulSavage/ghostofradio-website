The fog rolls thick through the streets of Cairo tonight, and Rocky Jordan finds himself ensnared in a web of deception that could cost him everything. When the mysterious Demarco affair lands on his desk, our globe-trotting adventurer must navigate the shadowy corridors of international intrigue, where every contact could be an informant and every handshake a potential double-cross. With danger lurking around every corner and the stakes mounting with each passing moment, Rocky and his trusted sidekick Trigger must unravel a conspiracy that threatens to upend the delicate balance of power in North Africa. Expect tense dialogue crackled with barely-contained violence, the atmospheric sound of Cairo's night streets, and plot twists that will keep you guessing until the final dramatic revelation.

Rocky Jordan emerged during radio's golden age as a distinctly urbane alternative to the superhero serials that dominated the airwaves. Airing on CBS from 1945 to 1951, the show featured Jack Moyles as the smooth-talking, quick-thinking American operating from his nightclub perch in exotic locales—primarily Cairo—giving listeners an escape into sophisticated, globe-trotting adventure. The series distinguished itself through its emphasis on cunning and charm over superhuman powers, offering post-war audiences tales of espionage and intrigue that reflected the anxieties and fascinations of the emerging Cold War era. Each episode delivered the snappy repartee and moral ambiguity of film noir, transplanted to the sands of Egypt.

If you crave the romance of distant lands combined with the crackling tension of a masterfully plotted crime thriller, The Demarco Affair is essential listening. Tune in and discover why Rocky Jordan captivated millions of listeners who huddled around their radios, eager for the next adventure.
