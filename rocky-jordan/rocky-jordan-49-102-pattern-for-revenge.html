When the curtain rises on this taut episode of Rocky Jordan, our American adventurer finds himself ensnared in a deadly web of vengeance that stretches across the sun-scorched streets of Cairo. A mysterious figure from the past has returned with blood in their eyes, and Rocky's quick wits—along with the steady presence of his faithful sidekick Sapenza—may be his only salvation. As the tension mounts and clues pile atop one another like the shifting sands outside, listeners will find themselves gripped by a masterfully constructed plot where every shadow could conceal a killer and every bargain struck might be Rocky's last. The exotic locales and cosmopolitan intrigue that define the series shine brilliantly here, transporting audiences halfway around the world to a place where danger lurks behind every corner and trust is the rarest commodity of all.

Rocky Jordan occupied a special place in radio's golden age, offering audiences the kind of globe-trotting adventure and sophisticated crime-solving that appealed equally to the seasoned detective story enthusiast and the casual listener seeking escape. CBS recognized the show's appeal, keeping it on the air from 1945 to 1951, a testament to its writing, sound design, and the chemistry between star Jack Moyles and his supporting cast. These weren't stories confined to American city streets; they reached from Casablanca to Monte Carlo, from Port Said to the very heart of the Middle East, giving postwar audiences a sense of adventure that matched their expanding horizons.
