# Rocky Jordan 49-116: Black Ball

Picture yourself in a smoky Cairo nightclub as the sun dips below the desert horizon. Our hero Rocky Jordan, that quick-witted American adventurer with a talent for finding trouble, has stumbled onto something far darker than the usual con games and stolen jewels that cross his path. A mysterious black ball—a symbol of death in the criminal underworld—has appeared, and with it comes a web of blackmail, betrayal, and danger that threatens to unravel the delicate balance of power in the city's shadiest corners. As cigarette smoke curls through dimly lit rooms and the muezzin's call echoes from distant minarets, Rocky must navigate treacherous alliances and double-crosses to survive the night. Every shadow could hide an assassin; every conversation could be his last. The tension crackles through the airwaves as Rocky's quick thinking and faster wits become his only weapons against an enemy he can't quite see.

Rocky Jordan, played with charming confidence by the original voice of the character, became a fixture of adventure radio during the post-war years when American audiences hungered for exotic escapism and tight storytelling. This particular episode exemplifies what made the show endure for six seasons on CBS—the perfect blend of genuine danger, witty banter, and a protagonist who seemed equally at home in a fistfight or a high-stakes negotiation. The North African setting allowed writers to tap into real post-WWII tensions and spy craft anxieties, grounding the adventure in contemporary concerns while maintaining the thrilling escapism listeners craved.

Don't miss this masterclass in radio suspense. Tune in and discover why Rocky Jordan earned its place among the golden age's most underrated adventures. The black ball awaits—but will Rocky live to tell the tale?
