When the curtain rises on this November evening in 1949, listeners are plunged into the shadowy streets of Cairo, where mystery clings to the desert air like sand on a traveler's coat. Rocky Jordan, that quick-witted American adventurer and operator of the Mirador Hotel, stumbles onto a dangerous secret involving a society of hooded figures whose true identities—and true intentions—remain shrouded in darkness. As coded messages arrive and suspicious strangers gather in the hotel's corridors, Rocky must navigate a maze of deception where trust is a luxury he cannot afford. Jack Moyles' crisp delivery captures the mounting tension perfectly, while the sound effects team conjures the exotic chaos of a Cairo night: footsteps echoing in alleyways, the distant call to prayer, and the ever-present threat of violence lurking just beyond the next corner.

What sets Rocky Jordan apart from the parade of American crime dramas of its era is its genuine exoticism and cosmopolitan flair. Unlike shows confined to New York precincts or small-town mysteries, Rocky operates in the glamorous yet treacherous landscape of the Middle East, where international intrigue and timeless cultural complexities provide the backdrop for his investigations. The show's writers crafted stories that balanced action-adventure with authentic atmospheric detail, treating their audience as sophisticated enough to appreciate both pulp thrills and genuine cultural texture.

"The Veiled People" exemplifies the show at its finest—a masterclass in suspense that builds with each revelation, where nothing is quite what it seems and danger arrives as swiftly as a desert storm. For those seeking an evening of pure, intelligent entertainment, this episode offers exactly what the golden age of radio promised: escape, excitement, and the voice of a hero you'll root for against impossible odds. Tune in now and discover why audiences made Rocky Jordan an unmissable date with adventure.
