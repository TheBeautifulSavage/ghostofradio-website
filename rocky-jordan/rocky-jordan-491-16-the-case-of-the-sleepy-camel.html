Picture yourself settling into your favorite chair on a Cairo evening as organ music swells and Rocky Jordan's world-weary voice cuts through the darkness—another case, another mystery lurking in the shadowy streets of the Middle East. In "The Case Of The Sleepy Camel," our globe-trotting private investigator finds himself entangled in a bizarre affair involving a somnolent animal and the criminals who've learned to exploit its peculiar condition. What begins as a seemingly absurd premise unfolds into a crackling tale of smuggling, deception, and danger, where nothing is quite as it seems and every suspect harbors a secret. You'll hear the distant calls of vendors, the clip-clop of hooves on ancient cobblestones, and the unmistakable tension of a man who's learned that in the criminal underworld, even a sleeping camel can be the linchpin to a deadly scheme.

Rocky Jordan emerged in 1945 as CBS's answer to the demand for exotic adventure serials, offering listeners an escape to international intrigue when post-war America was hungry for tales beyond its borders. Set primarily in Cairo with Rocky as a trouble-solving American operative, the show became known for its atmospheric sound design and clever plotting that balanced noir sensibility with genuine adventure. The series ran for six years and 258 episodes, creating a devoted following who appreciated its blend of danger, humor, and the kind of sophisticated storytelling that defined radio's golden age.

If you're a longtime devotee of old-time radio or discovering classic adventure programming for the first time, this episode encapsulates everything that made Rocky Jordan essential listening. Dial in now and let the mystery unfold—you won't believe where the truth lies.
