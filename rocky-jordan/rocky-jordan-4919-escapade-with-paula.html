When Rocky Jordan receives an urgent message from the mysterious and alluring Paula, he knows trouble isn't far behind. This electrifying episode plunges our Cairo-based detective into a labyrinth of deception and danger, where every shadow conceals a potential enemy and trust is a luxury he can scarcely afford. As the city's neon-lit streets pulse with menace, Rocky must navigate a web of international intrigue, double-crosses, and femme fatales while his faithful companion Graystone watches his back. The crackling tension builds with each passing moment—will Rocky's quick wits and quicker reflexes be enough to escape Paula's dangerous game, or will he find himself ensnared in something far more sinister than he bargained for?

Rocky Jordan represents the golden age of adventure radio, when audiences huddled around their sets for tales of exotic locales and morally ambiguous protagonists. Set against the glamorous and perilous backdrop of post-war Cairo, the show captivated listeners with its blend of noir sensibility and international espionage. Starring the suave Jack Moyles in the title role, the series offered something refreshingly sophisticated for radio audiences—a hero who operated in shades of gray rather than stark black and white. During this pivotal late-1940s period, such programs proved Americans were hungry for worldly sophistication and complex storytelling that transcended the typical hero-versus-villain formula.

Whether you're a longtime devotee of classic radio or discovering this golden-age gem for the first time, "Escapade With Paula" exemplifies everything that made Rocky Jordan essential listening.
