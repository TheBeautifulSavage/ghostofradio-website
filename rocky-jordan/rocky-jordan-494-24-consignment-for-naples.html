Picture yourself huddled around the radio on a winter evening as Rocky Jordan, that wise-cracking American operative in Cairo, finds himself tangled in a deadly web of international intrigue. In "Consignment For Naples," our hero discovers that a seemingly innocent shipment bound for the Italian coast carries secrets worth killing for—and he's smack in the middle of a dangerous game where trust is a luxury he can't afford. With danger closing in from every direction and his trusted sidekick Salamone caught in the crossfire, Rocky must navigate the shadowy corridors of wartime espionage where one wrong move means death. The tension crackles through every scene as mysterious strangers with loaded guns materialize from the Cairo night, and Rocky's quick thinking becomes his only weapon against forces far larger than himself.

Rocky Jordan arrived on CBS in 1945 during the golden age of radio adventure, capturing audiences with its exotic Cairo setting and the perfect blend of hard-boiled detective work with international espionage. Tackling stories of smuggling rings, wartime subterfuge, and criminal conspiracies that spanned the Mediterranean, the show reflected listeners' fascination with a world beyond their own—a thrilling escape into danger and intrigue that felt disturbingly real in the post-war years. Jack Moyles' portrayal of Rocky brought authentic sophistication to the role, transforming the character into an everyman hero caught in extraordinary circumstances, while the supporting cast and atmospheric sound design transported millions of listeners directly to the bustling streets and shadowy back alleys of Cairo.

Don't miss this pulse-pounding adventure in the archives of radio's greatest espionage thriller. Tune in to "Consignment For Naples" and discover why Rocky Jordan kept audiences on the edge of their seats for seven thrilling seasons.
