When the curtain rises on this shadowy Cairo night, Rocky Jordan finds himself entangled in a web of theatrical deception that threatens far more than opening night applause. A master of disguise—a makeup man with talents worthy of the stage—becomes the linchpin in a crime that reaches from the smoky dressing rooms of Cairo's most exclusive theater to the underbelly of international intrigue. As Rocky and his trusty sidekick Atkins navigate backstage corridors and dimly lit alleyways, listeners will feel the mounting tension of a mystery where appearances deceive and identities shift like shadows under gaslight. The makeup man holds secrets that could unravel everything—but which side of the law does he truly serve?

Rocky Jordan's popularity during its six-year run on CBS captured the imagination of millions who tuned in for the exotic escapism of post-war America. Set in the cosmopolitan underworld of Cairo, the show offered listeners a sophisticated alternative to domestic comedies, delivering hard-boiled adventure with genuine star power—actor John Payne brought a world-weary charm to the title role that made every case feel personal and urgent. By the late 1940s, when this episode aired, radio drama was reaching its artistic peak, and the Rocky Jordan program exemplified the medium's capacity for cinematic storytelling delivered through sound alone: the crackle of static, the subtle scrape of a chair, the carefully modulated voice revealing character and motive.

Don't miss this compelling mystery where a single disguise can conceal a multitude of sins. Tune in to "The Makeup Man" and discover why Rocky Jordan remains one of the golden age's most thrilling adventures—where danger lurks behind every mask, and the truth is never what it seems.
