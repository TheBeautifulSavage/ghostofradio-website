# Rocky Jordan: Fall Guy

When the neon signs of Cairo's underworld flicker to life, private investigator Rocky Jordan finds himself entangled in a web of double-crosses and desperate men. In "Fall Guy," Rocky's search for a missing jewel thief leads him through the shadowed streets and smoke-filled cafés of the exotic Egyptian metropolis, where every contact could be his last. Listeners will thrill to the snap of dialogue, the close calls and clever deceptions as Rocky pieces together a puzzle where the innocent may be guilty and the guilty far more dangerous than they appear. The chemistry between Rocky and his faithful sidekick Reggie York crackles with tension and dark humor, even as danger closes in from all sides.

*Rocky Jordan* represented CBS radio at its adventurous peak, a show that understood the appeal of exotic locales and morally ambiguous situations that distinguished it from more straightforward hero tales. Set in 1940s Cairo, the series embraced a film noir sensibility translated perfectly to the audio medium—all sultry atmosphere and gritty realism. The show's producers crafted intelligent scripts that trusted audiences to follow complex plots, rewarding listeners with the kind of sophisticated crime drama that would later influence television detective shows. Episode 4951 exemplifies the series' mastery of tension and character, with Jack Moyles' Rocky delivering rapid-fire wisecracks while navigating impossible odds with cigarette-smoke cool.

For anyone who thrills to the golden age of radio drama, "Fall Guy" is an essential listen. Settle in, dim the lights, and let the sound effects and stellar voice work transport you to a world where nothing is quite as it seems and trust is the most precious—and rarest—commodity of all.
