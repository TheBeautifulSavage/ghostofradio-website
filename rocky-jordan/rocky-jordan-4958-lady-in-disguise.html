Picture the smoky streets of Cairo after dark, where nothing is quite what it seems and trust is a luxury no one can afford. In this electric installment of Rocky Jordan, our globe-trotting detective finds himself entangled with a mysterious woman whose true identity could mean the difference between a lucrative score and a fatal double-cross. As she moves through the shadowy corridors of the city's underworld, each revelation peels back another layer of deception—is she a refugee seeking protection, a cunning operative running her own game, or something far more dangerous? The tension crackles through every scene as Rocky must use all his wit and street smarts to uncover the truth before the stakes become deadly.

Rocky Jordan was CBS Radio's answer to listeners hungry for sophisticated adventure during the golden age of broadcasting. Starring John Conte as the suave American detective operating out of a Middle Eastern hotel, the show offered something different from typical crime dramas—an exotic setting, morally complex characters, and plots where international intrigue collided with personal betrayal. Running from 1945 to 1951, the series captured the post-war fascination with distant locales and espionage, presenting a world where American resourcefulness could navigate the labyrinthine politics of the Near East. Each episode balanced noir-style mystery with globe-trotting adventure, making Rocky Jordan a thinking listener's escape.
