# Rocky Jordan - "The Man From Damascus"

Picture yourself in a smoke-filled Cairo nightclub, the muezzin's call echoing across the Nile as danger closes in from the shadows. In this thrilling installment, our globe-trotting adventurer Rocky Jordan finds himself ensnared in a deadly game of international intrigue when a mysterious stranger arrives from Damascus with secrets worth killing for. With his faithful sidekick Grant Kirby at his side and the sultry Habib—proprietor of the Simoon Club—warning him of treachery afoot, Rocky must navigate a labyrinth of double-crosses and deception. The tension crackles through every scene as old enemies resurface and new threats emerge from the desert itself. Will Rocky's quick wits and quicker fists be enough to survive the night, or has he finally met his match?

*Rocky Jordan* captivated audiences during radio's golden age with its exotic locales and sophisticated storytelling that transported listeners far beyond their living rooms. The show's enduring appeal lay in its perfect balance of pulp adventure and genuine character development—Rocky was no two-dimensional action hero, but a complex man navigating moral gray areas in a dangerous world. Broadcast originally from 1945 to 1951, the series became a benchmark for adventure programming, influencing everything from later spy thrillers to international crime dramas. The rich sound design and masterful performances created vivid mental imagery that no television screen could quite replicate.

So tune in now to "The Man From Damascus" and experience the golden age of radio adventure. Let the atmospheric music pull you into Rocky's world, where danger lurks behind every corner and trust is the most precious commodity of all. These are the broadcasts that defined an era—don't miss this authentic slice of classic entertainment.
