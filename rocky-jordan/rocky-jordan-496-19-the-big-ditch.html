Picture the hazy streets of Cairo at dusk, where the Nile's ancient waters meet the whispers of international intrigue. In this gripping installment, Rocky Jordan finds himself entangled in a smuggling operation that runs deeper than the great river itself—literally. When a mysterious informant warns of contraband moving through Cairo's hidden canal system, Rocky must navigate the literal and figurative depths of corruption that threaten to drown him. With his trusted sidekick Salamander at his side and the ever-resourceful Captain Ah Choy standing ready, our globe-trotting detective descends into the shadowy underworld beneath the city's streets, where danger lurks at every turn and trust is a luxury he cannot afford. The tension mounts as rival gangs, double-crossing informants, and the ticking clock of an impending shipment push Rocky toward a climactic confrontation that will either expose the ringleaders or leave him permanently silenced in the depths.

Rocky Jordan captivated millions of listeners as one of radio's most compelling adventure series, offering the exotic escapism Americans craved during and immediately after World War II. Set in the atmospheric backdrop of Cairo, the show allowed audiences to experience international mystery while safely ensconced in their living rooms. This particular episode exemplifies the show's mastery of atmosphere and plot, combining genuine danger with the sophisticated wit that made Rocky a character listeners couldn't resist.
