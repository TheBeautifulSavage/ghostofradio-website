# Rocky Jordan Episode: Journey To Nashier

Picture this: Cairo, 1948. The desert night air hangs thick and suffocating as Rocky Jordan finds himself ensnared in a web of intrigue that stretches from the shadowy souks to the windswept dunes beyond the city limits. In "Journey To Nashier," our intrepid American adventurer must navigate treacherous alliances and double-crosses to uncover the truth behind a mysterious caravan headed toward the remote desert settlement of Nashier. With nothing but his wits, his trusty .38, and the street-smart savvy of his local companions, Rocky ventures into territory where the only law is survival. The tension crackles through your speaker as sinister voices whisper secrets in dimly-lit cafés, and every shadow might conceal danger. What awaits him in Nashier? Who can he truly trust? The answers lie somewhere in the unforgiving darkness of the Arabian night.

Rocky Jordan thrived during the golden age of radio adventure, when audiences craved escapism and exotic locales delivered from their living rooms. This program, which aired from 1945 to 1951 on CBS, distinguished itself through atmospheric storytelling and location authenticity—writers crafted tales that felt genuine, grounded in real Middle Eastern geography and politics even as they spun yarns of high adventure. Jack Moyles' portrayal of the title character became the gold standard for the resourceful American abroad, a man equally comfortable navigating bureaucratic corruption as he was surviving physical peril.

Don't miss this classic tale of desert mystery and danger. Tune in to hear how Rocky Jordan outwits his enemies and uncovers the secrets of Nashier—a journey you won't forget.
