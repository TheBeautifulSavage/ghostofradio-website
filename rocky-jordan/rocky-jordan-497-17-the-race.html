The fog rolls thick through the streets of Cairo as Rocky Jordan finds himself caught between a desperate smuggler and a fortune in stolen diamonds. When a beautiful woman walks into his office with a proposition that sounds too good to be true, our hard-boiled detective knows trouble is never far behind. This episode crackles with the kind of tension that only old-time radio could deliver—the roar of engines, the sharp crack of gunfire echoing down narrow alleyways, and the sultry voice of danger itself. As Rocky navigates a high-stakes race through the Egyptian desert, every turn of the wheel brings him closer to either his greatest prize or his final reckoning. The stakes have never been higher, and Rocky's quick wits are all that stand between justice and oblivion.

Rocky Jordan, broadcast throughout its six-year run on CBS, became the gold standard for adventure serials in the post-war era. The show's masterful blend of noir sensibility with exotic international settings captured audiences hungry for escapism after years of war-time uncertainty. Rocky's Cairo office served as the perfect crucible for morally ambiguous characters and high-stakes plots that kept listeners tuning in night after night. The writing crackled with authenticity, the sound effects immersed you completely in distant lands, and the cast brought their A-game to every broadcast. This particular episode exemplifies everything the show did best—combining breakneck pacing, genuine mystery, and the kind of character-driven drama that made radio the dominant entertainment medium of its time.

So settle back in your favorite chair, adjust that dial to CBS, and prepare yourself for an evening of pure adventure. Rocky Jordan awaits, and in "The Race," fortune, danger, and destiny converge in the sands of Egypt.
