# Rocky Jordan – Episode 497: "The Coward of Mutakahn"

Picture this: the dusty streets of Cairo after dark, where shadows stretch across ancient stone and danger lurks in every alley. Our hero Rocky Jordan steps into the smoky confines of a local tavern where whispers of a cowardly merchant named Mutakahn have spread like wildfire through the underworld. But nothing is as it seems in this den of intrigue. When a priceless artifact goes missing and fingers begin pointing in every direction, Rocky must untangle a web of lies, blackmail, and wounded pride to uncover the truth—and the real culprit behind the theft. Will Rocky's sharp wit and quick fists be enough to expose the coward before the guilty party strikes again? The tension builds with every clever exchange, every suspicious encounter, and every narrow escape in this thrilling tale of deception in the shadow of the pyramids.

Rocky Jordan was the thinking man's adventure serial, a show that proved radio audiences craved intelligent storytelling alongside exotic locales and pulse-pounding action. Running from 1945 to 1951 on CBS, the program starred Jack Moyles as Rocky, an American troubleshooter operating out of Cairo—the perfect setting for intrigue, international crime, and sophisticated crime-solving. Unlike some of its contemporaries, Rocky Jordan emphasized character development and clever plotting over mindless violence, making it a favorite among listeners who appreciated witty banter and genuine mystery alongside their thrills.

"The Coward of Mutakahn" exemplifies everything that made Rocky Jordan a standout in radio's golden age. Settle in with this classic episode and experience the crackle of authenticity, the craft of master storytellers, and the unforgettable chemistry between characters that made tuning in an event. This is radio adventure at its finest—intelligent, compelling, and absolutely riveting from first word to last.
