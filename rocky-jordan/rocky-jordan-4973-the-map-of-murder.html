When a mysterious cartographer turns up dead in Cairo's shadowy backstreets, Rocky Jordan finds himself holding the only clue to a fortune that ruthless criminals would kill to possess—a map that marks the location of untold riches, or perhaps something far more sinister. As midnight fog rolls through the exotic streets of the Middle East, our intrepid American hero must navigate a treacherous web of double-crosses, false leads, and cutthroat competitors who'll stop at nothing to claim the prize. Every shadow could hide an assassin, every ally might be a traitor, and the clock is ticking as Rocky pieces together the deadly puzzle. What begins as a simple investigation spirals into a race against time, where one wrong move means joining the cartographer in a permanent, silent darkness.

Rocky Jordan arrived on CBS airwaves in 1945 as American audiences craved adventure and exotic escapism in the post-war years. The series distinguished itself by setting its crime-solving action in Cairo rather than the typical American cityscape, giving listeners a thrilling sense of international intrigue and Far Eastern danger that few radio programs dared explore. Russell Thorson's dry, quick-witted performance as the calculating protagonist became iconic, embodying the cool, resourceful American abroad who could talk his way out of trouble as smoothly as he could fight his way through it. Episodes like "The Map of Murder" showcase the show's masterful blend of hardboiled detective work with adventure serial excitement, all wrapped in evocative sound design that transports listeners across continents.

Tune in now to experience the crackling tension, the rapid-fire dialogue, and the extraordinary world that made Rocky Jordan a beloved fixture of radio's golden age. You won't forget the moment when Rocky finally discovers what that map truly conceals.
