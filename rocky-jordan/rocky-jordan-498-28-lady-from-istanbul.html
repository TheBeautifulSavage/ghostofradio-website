When a mysterious woman arrives at Rocky Jordan's office in Cairo with a fortune in jewels and a target on her back, our hero finds himself entangled in a web of international intrigue that stretches from the mosques of Istanbul to the shadowy back alleys of the Middle East. Is she a refugee fleeing persecution, a con artist running an elaborate scheme, or something far more dangerous? As Rocky digs deeper, he discovers that the lady's past is as murky as the Nile at midnight, and everyone from black market dealers to foreign intelligence agencies seems desperate to find her first. With his trusted sidekick Salty at his side and the ever-helpful Cairo police commissioner in his corner, Rocky must navigate treacherous double-crosses and unexpected betrayals to stay one step ahead of those who would kill to possess what she knows.

Rocky Jordan embodied the golden age of radio adventure—a time when listeners huddled around their sets for tales of international intrigue and exotic locales conjured entirely through brilliant sound design and stellar voice work. The show's appeal lay in its perfect balance: the gritty realism of crime drama combined with the glamorous escapism of far-flung adventure, all delivered with the snappy dialogue and moral clarity audiences craved during the post-war years. This particular episode showcases why the series remained a CBS staple for six seasons, offering listeners an escape to a world where courage and quick thinking could overcome any obstacle.

Settle into your favorite chair, dim the lights, and let the sounds of Cairo transport you back to an era when radio reigned supreme. This is the kind of episode that reminds us why millions of Americans tuned in faithfully each week—pure entertainment, pure imagination, pure radio magic.
