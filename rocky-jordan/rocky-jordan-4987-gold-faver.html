Picture this: the sweltering back alleys of Cairo, where the desert heat shimmers off ancient stone and danger lurks in every shadowed doorway. In this week's installment of Rocky Jordan, our intrepid American adventurer finds himself ensnared in a web of deception surrounding a fortune in missing gold—but nothing is quite as it seems. As Rocky navigates the bustling souks and dimly-lit nightclubs of Egypt's most notorious quarter, he must outwit both cunning smugglers and his own duplicitous allies to uncover the truth. The stakes have never been higher, the tension never thicker, as our hero discovers that the price of greed may be far steeper than mere currency. Tune in for an episode brimming with double-crosses, breathless chases, and the kind of exotic intrigue that made listeners huddle around their radios in rapt anticipation.

Rocky Jordan occupied a singular place in radio's golden age—a show that thrived on atmosphere and character rather than relying solely on sensation. Broadcast over CBS from 1945 to 1951, the series featured Russell Thorson in the title role, bringing wry sophistication and genuine resourcefulness to a protagonist who operated in that thrilling gray zone between the legitimate and the lawless. The show's genius lay in its evocative setting: Cairo became not merely a backdrop but a living, breathing character itself, a city of mystery and moral ambiguity where American confidence met Old World cunning.

Whether you're a devoted follower of Rocky's exploits or new to the world of classic radio adventure, "Gold Fever" delivers exactly what the medium did best—transporting listeners across oceans and decades into a world of intrigue, where voices and sound effects conjured entire landscapes. Don't miss it.
