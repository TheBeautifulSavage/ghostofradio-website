As the opening theme swells with exotic intrigue, listeners are transported to the shadowy streets of Cairo, where trouble wears a beautiful face. In "Adventure With Andrea," our quick-witted American adventurer Rocky Jordan finds himself entangled with a mysterious woman whose motives are as murky as the Nile at midnight. Is she a fellow traveler seeking protection, or does she represent the danger that lurks around every corner of the Middle East? With his loyal sidekick Pinky Martindale at his side and danger closing in from all directions, Rocky must navigate a perilous web of deception where trust could prove fatal. The tension crackles through every scene as blackmail, betrayal, and breathless chases build toward an explosive climax that will keep you riveted until the final fade-out.

"Rocky Jordan" holds a unique place in radio history as one of CBS's most beloved adventure serials, capturing the postwar appetite for exotic locales and morally complex protagonists. Jack Moyles brought the character to vivid life with a performance that balanced charm with cynicism—Rocky was no boy scout, but rather a man shaped by the hard realities of international intrigue. During the mid-to-late 1940s, when this episode aired, America was hungry for stories that whisked them away to distant lands and suggested that adventure and fortune still awaited bold men willing to take risks. The show's attention to atmospheric detail, snappy dialogue, and genuine stakes made it a ritual appointment for millions of listeners.
