When the lights dim and the orchestra swells with that unmistakable Cairo jazz motif, listeners are transported once again to the sultry streets of Egypt where American adventurer Rocky Jordan prowls the shadowy bazaars and glittering casinos of post-war intrigue. In "Smoke Screen," Rocky finds himself ensnared in a dangerous game of deception where nothing—and no one—can be trusted. A shipment of black-market contraband, a mysterious femme fatale, and a conspiracy that reaches into the highest echelons of power converge in a thrilling narrative that crackles with danger and moral ambiguity. Eavesdrop on tense conversations in dim hotel rooms, the crack of a revolver in the night, and Rocky's dry, quick-witted commentary as he navigates between the criminals and authorities who would just as soon see him dead as alive.

Rocky Jordan captured the public imagination during the golden age of radio adventure serials, offering something the other shows couldn't quite match: exotic sophistication married to hard-boiled detective sensibilities. Jack Moyles' portrayal of Jordan became iconic—a cool, resourceful American operating in a liminal space between law and lawlessness, more complex than the typical square-jawed hero. Set against the backdrop of Middle Eastern intrigue during the 1940s, the series reflected genuine anxieties about international espionage and the murky realities of post-war geopolitics, wrapped in the glamorous packaging of high adventure.

Don't miss your appointment with danger. Tune in for "Smoke Screen" and discover why Rocky Jordan became one of CBS's most enduring adventure programs, celebrated by aficionados of old-time radio for its atmospheric storytelling and unforgettable protagonist.
