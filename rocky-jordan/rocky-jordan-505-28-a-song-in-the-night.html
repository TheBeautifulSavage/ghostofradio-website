# Rocky Jordan: A Song In The Night

Step into the shadowed streets of Cairo as Rocky Jordan finds himself entangled in a mystery that weaves together passion, danger, and the haunting power of music. When a beautiful woman's desperate plea reaches our hero at his favorite nightclub, he's drawn into a web of intrigue involving smuggling rings, stolen secrets, and a voice that may be worth killing for. As the night deepens, Rocky must navigate the treacherous bazaars and smoke-filled clubs of Egypt's underworld, where a simple song becomes the key to uncovering a conspiracy that threatens far more than one woman's life. The tension crackles through every scene—will Rocky's quick thinking and street smarts be enough, or will he become another victim of those willing to silence the truth?

Rocky Jordan arrived during radio's golden age when the medium dominated American entertainment, and this series distinguished itself by placing its action-adventure hero not in some generic big city, but in the exotic and mysterious Cairo. Broadcast over CBS from 1945 to 1951, the show captured post-war audiences' appetite for international intrigue and sophisticated crime-fighting, with Jack Moyles' portrayal of Rocky establishing the character as equal parts charming rogue and shrewd detective. These episodes represent a masterclass in old-time radio storytelling—economical dialogue, richly evocative sound design, and plots that moved with genuine momentum, all designed for listeners who gathered around their sets expecting genuine thrills.

This is radio adventure at its finest—prepare yourself for twenty-three minutes of masterfully crafted suspense that transports you across decades and oceans. Tune in to *Rocky Jordan: A Song In The Night* and discover why millions tuned in faithfully each week to follow their favorite Cairo-based detective into danger.
