When Rocky Jordan steps into a shadowy world of international currency smuggling, listeners are transported deep into the labyrinthine cafés and back alleys of Cairo, where trust is currency and every exchange could be a deadly trap. In this tense thirty-minute installment, our enterprising American adventurer finds himself entangled with black market money changers who deal in more than just foreign notes—they traffic in secrets, betrayal, and blood. As the plot tightens around him like a noose, Rocky must use his quick wits and quicker fists to uncover who's really pulling the strings. The episode crackles with danger: coded messages, midnight rendezvous, and the constant threat that any moment could be Rocky's last. Jack Moyles' smooth, assured narration guides us through each perilous turn as the music swells, drums punctuate close calls, and sound effects transport us to a Cairo seething with corruption and intrigue.

Rocky Jordan was a landmark program that captured post-war American imaginations with its exotic settings and modern sensibilities—far removed from the Victorian drawing rooms of earlier mystery serials. Airing on CBS from 1945 to 1951, the show represented a new breed of adventure radio that reflected America's growing global consciousness during the emerging Cold War. The program combined hard-boiled detective fiction with international intrigue, proving that American audiences hungered for stories that took them beyond their borders. Episodes like "The Money Changers" showcase the writers' sophisticated understanding of geopolitics and criminal enterprise, elevating the show beyond mere pulp entertainment into genuinely intelligent drama.

Don't miss this gripping chapter in Rocky's Cairo saga. Tune in to experience radio drama at its finest—where danger lurks in every shadow and one man's courage against impossible odds becomes the stuff of legend.
