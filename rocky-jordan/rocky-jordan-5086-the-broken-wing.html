High above the glittering streets of Cairo, danger descends with the screech of tortured metal and failing engines. When a mysterious aircraft limps toward the airfield with a shattered wing and a cargo of secrets, Rocky Jordan finds himself entangled in a web of espionage and desperation that reaches far beyond the desert city. As night falls over the Egyptian capital, our quick-witted American adventurer must navigate treacherous alliances, uncover hidden motives, and outmaneuver those who would kill to keep their identities concealed. The clock is ticking, the stakes are rising, and in the shadows of Cairo's most dangerous quarters, Rocky discovers that a broken wing may be the least damaged thing before this night is through.

Rocky Jordan arrived on CBS airwaves in 1945 as listeners' hunger for exotic adventure reached a fever pitch in post-war America. Set in Cairo, Egypt—a city that captured the public imagination as a crossroads of intrigue, romance, and danger—the show offered weekly escapes to a world of international crime and mystery. Jack Moyles brought Rocky to vivid life with a character equal parts charm and cunning, while the show's intricate plots and atmospheric sound design transported listeners thousands of miles from their living rooms. The series thrived during radio's golden age precisely because it understood what audiences craved: the promise of the unexpected lurking around every corner, in every shadowed alley, with every mysterious arrival.

If you've never experienced the particular thrill of classic adventure radio, or if you're a devoted fan seeking to rediscover this gem, "The Broken Wing" is essential listening. Settle in, dim the lights, and let the magic of 1940s radio craft take you away.
