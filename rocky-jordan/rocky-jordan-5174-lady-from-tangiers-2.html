When Rocky Jordan returns to his office in Cairo that fateful evening, the case he thought solved has only grown more complicated. A mysterious woman from the port city of Tangiers has disappeared, but not before passing him a cryptic message that changes everything he thought he knew about the smuggling ring he'd been tracking. As the clock strikes midnight and the desert winds howl outside his window, Rocky must navigate a treacherous web of false identities, double-crosses, and dangerous secrets. His trusted friend Westbrook stands by his side, but even their partnership may not be enough when a femme fatale's reappearance threatens to unravel the very fabric of their investigation. The tension crackles through your radio speaker as shadowy figures move through the narrow streets of Cairo, each one concealing motives as complex as the ancient city itself.

Rocky Jordan stands as one of the great undervalued gems of 1940s adventure radio, thriving during radio's golden age when CBS brought exotic intrigue directly into American living rooms. Unlike some of its contemporaries, the show grounded its far-flung melodrama in genuine atmosphere and character development, with lead actor Jack Moyles creating a protagonist who was equal parts tough detective and reluctant romantic. The North African setting allowed writers to tap into post-war fascination with international intrigue while maintaining the rapid-fire pacing and moral clarity that audiences craved during the late 1940s.

This is radio drama at its most alluring—where every creaking door, every whispered conversation, and every sudden revelation draws you deeper into a world of mystery and danger. Settle into your favorite chair, dim the lights, and let the sounds of Cairo at night transport you to an era when imagination was the only special effect you needed.
