# Rogue's Gallery: "Phyllis Adrian Is Missing"

The evening of June 29th promises intrigue most delicious as Detective Dick Rogue discovers that the elegant—and decidedly dangerous—Phyllis Adrian has vanished without a trace. What begins as a routine missing persons inquiry spirals into a labyrinth of double-crosses, forged alibis, and suspects more crooked than the gallery of rogues that fill Dick's casebook. Listeners will revel in the crackling banter between our hapless detective and his long-suffering secretary, punctuated by the show's signature comedic timing that somehow never undercuts the genuine mystery at hand. The orchestra swells with jazzy menace as each clue leads deeper into the shadowy underbelly of the city—will Dick uncover Phyllis before it's too late, or has she already become the perfect victim of the perfect crime?

*Rogue's Gallery* occupied a peculiar and beloved corner of 1940s radio comedy, blending the hard-boiled detective tradition with vaudeville timing in ways that made the genre accessible to families gathered around their sets. Debuting in 1945, the show capitalized on post-war audiences' hunger for clever entertainment that didn't take itself too seriously; the writers understood that mystery and humor weren't opposing forces but natural companions. This episode exemplifies the show's greatest strength: crafting plots intricate enough to genuinely confound listeners while maintaining the charm and wit that kept devoted audiences tuning in week after week.

Don't miss this tangled tale of vanishing heiresses and mysterious motives. Tune in as Dick Rogue stumbles brilliantly toward the truth, one wisecrack and wrong turn at a time.
