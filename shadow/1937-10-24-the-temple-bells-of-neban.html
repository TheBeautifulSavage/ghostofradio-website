The Temple Bells Of Neban

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

Deep in the heart of Asia, where ancient mysteries guard secrets that no civilized man should know, The Shadow pursues a sinister cult into the mist-shrouded temples of Neban. When a wealthy industrialist's daughter vanishes without a trace, our dark avenger discovers that a network of criminals—cloaked in the trappings of religious ceremony—has orchestrated her disappearance as part of a diabolical scheme spanning continents. As the temple bells toll their haunting melody through the night, The Shadow must navigate treacherous Oriental intrigue, unmask the cult's enigmatic leader, and rescue the girl from a fate worse than death itself. Expect gasping henchmen, the unmistakable whoosh of The Shadow's trademark laugh piercing through danger, and a climax of breathless intensity as our mysterious protagonist confronts evil in its most exotic and terrifying form.

The Temple Bells of Neban exemplifies radio's golden age at its finest—a time when CBS and the Mutual Broadcasting System commanded the imagination of millions with nothing but voice, sound effects, and the power of suggestion. The Shadow, voiced with chilling authority by the legendary Orson Welles in these formative years, became the prototype for the modern crime-fighting hero: invisible, all-knowing, capable of penetrating the criminal underworld's darkest corners. This 1937 episode perfectly captures the show's intoxicating blend of hard-boiled detective fiction and supernatural elements, set against exotic locales that transported Depression-era listeners to worlds beyond their daily experience.

Tune in now and rediscover why The Shadow captivated over twenty million listeners weekly. In an age before television, this is where thrills lived, where evil trembled, and where one man's unseen hand maintained the balance between civilization and chaos. The shadow knows... will you listen?
