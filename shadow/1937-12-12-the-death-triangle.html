# The Death Triangle

In the fog-laden streets of Manhattan, three prominent citizens lie dead—each at the precise apex of an invisible triangle drawn across the city's most exclusive neighborhoods. As police commissioner Weston scratches his head in bewilderment, a low, mirthless laugh echoes through the darkness. The Shadow knows the truth that hides in plain sight, and tonight he must unravel a killer's geometric obsession before the fourth corner of this deadly design is completed. With Margo Lane at his side and his uncanny ability to cloud men's minds, Lamont Cranston descends into a world of millionaires, mistresses, and mathematical murder where every clue points to someone impossibly innocent—and everyone harbors secrets worth killing for.

This 1937 episode represents the golden age of *The Shadow*, when the program had achieved the perfect balance between pulp adventure and psychological sophistication that would captivate millions of listeners for nearly two decades. Orson Welles's legendary 1938 tenure would later overshadow the show's origins, yet these early CBS and Mutual broadcasts showcase the character at his most mysterious and menacing. The writers understood something crucial: the real terror comes not from what you see, but from what you cannot—the invisible hand moving the pieces, the darkness that surrounds you even in daylight. Radio allowed listeners to experience this paranoia intimately, transforming their living rooms into crime scenes where danger lurked just beyond the static.

Dial in for an evening of sophisticated crime drama where the impossible becomes inevitable and justice takes a form both terrible and righteous. *The Shadow* awaits in the darkness.
