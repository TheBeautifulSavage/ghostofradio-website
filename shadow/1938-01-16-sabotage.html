The Shadow CBS/Mutual · 1938

Sabotage

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

As the clock strikes midnight and that unmistakable laugh echoes through the darkness—that chilling, otherworldly laugh that only The Shadow knows—listeners are drawn into a web of industrial espionage and treason that threatens the very heart of American manufacturing. In "Sabotage," a mysterious operative is systematically destroying vital defense contracts, and only The Shadow, who knows what evil lurks in the hearts of men, can penetrate the veil of deception to expose the saboteur. What unfolds is a taut, breathless journey through shadowy factories and hushed boardrooms, where danger lurks around every corner and trust becomes a luxury no one can afford. Orson Welles' velvet baritone guides us through this labyrinth of suspicion, while the meticulously crafted sound design—the hiss of steam, the screech of machinery, the sharp crack of gunfire—places you directly in harm's way alongside The Shadow himself.

This 1938 broadcast captures a uniquely anxious moment in American radio history, when the specter of industrial sabotage and foreign interference felt genuinely urgent to listening families. The Shadow had already become the gold standard of mystery programming, a show that transformed the potential of radio drama into high art, and episodes like "Sabotage" demonstrate why millions tuned in faithfully each week. The program's sophisticated plotting, combined with genuine performances and the revolutionary use of sound effects, elevated radio entertainment to something approaching literature.

If you've never experienced The Shadow at its peak, or if you're returning to revisit this golden age of broadcasting, "Sabotage" offers the perfect entry point—a masterclass in suspense that proves why, after more than eight decades, listeners remain captivated by the world's greatest detective.
