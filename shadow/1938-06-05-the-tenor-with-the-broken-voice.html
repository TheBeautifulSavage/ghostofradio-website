# The Tenor With The Broken Voice

When the curtain rises on this February evening in 1938, listeners are plunged into the glittering world of New York's Metropolitan Opera House—where applause mingles with intrigue and a celebrated tenor's voice harbors a sinister secret. A man's throat, they say, cannot lie; but what if it conceals a murderer? As The Shadow's familiar laugh echoes through the static, Lamont Cranston finds himself entangled in a web of jealousy, blackmail, and operatic ambition where the distinction between performance and reality blurs dangerously. The killer moves among velvet curtains and marble columns, leaving only a broken voice and a body as evidence. Every note sung is a clue; every silence, a confession waiting to be exposed.

The Shadow was at the height of its golden age in 1938, commanding millions of devoted listeners who tuned in weekly to witness the mysterious detective whose hypnotic powers of telepathy allowed him to penetrate the criminal mind itself. Orson Welles had made the character legendary before departing for Hollywood, but the show remained a flagship of CBS and Mutual's programming, showcasing the finest in sound design and storytelling that only radio could achieve. "The Tenor With The Broken Voice" exemplifies the show's brilliant formula: sophisticated urban settings, morally complex characters, and mysteries that demanded listeners' full attention and active imagination.

If you've never experienced The Shadow during its finest period, or if you're a longtime fan seeking to revisit these treasured broadcasts, this episode invites you into a world where danger whispers in the dark. Adjust your dial, dim the lights, and prepare yourself—The Shadow knows what evil lurks in the hearts of men, and tonight, that evil wears a tuxedo and sings tenor.
