# The Tomb of Terror

When The Shadow's hypnotic laugh echoes through the darkness this evening, listeners will find themselves transported to a cursed Egyptian tomb where greed and ancient vengeance converge in the most diabolical of murders. A wealthy collector's obsession with a pharaoh's burial chamber becomes his undoing as mysterious deaths plague those who dare disturb the sacred ground. The Shadow—that mysterious vigilante who "knows what evil lurks in the hearts of men"—must penetrate layers of deception, supernatural terror, and human depravity to unmask a killer operating in shadows deeper than his own. With the invaluable assistance of his trusted companion Margot Lane, The Shadow races against time as the body count rises and the line between curse and calculated crime blurs into thrilling uncertainty.

This 1938 episode represents The Shadow at the height of his popularity, when the character had already transcended his pulp magazine origins to become a cultural phenomenon commanding audiences in the millions. Lamont Cranston—the Shadow's sophisticated alter ego—was brought to vivid life by the incomparable Orson Welles, whose distinctive baritone and masterful command of dramatic tension made the program essential listening for the nation's radio audience. The Shadow's success lay not merely in its mystery-thriller framework, but in its exploration of moral ambiguity and psychological warfare, themes that would echo through decades of crime drama to come. Each episode balanced supernatural atmosphere with rigorous detective work, making listeners genuinely uncertain whether they were witnessing genuine occult phenomena or clever human manipulation.

Tune in now and surrender yourself to The Shadow's masterful unraveling of The Tomb of Terror—a case that proves some secrets are worth killing for, and some mysteries can only be solved by a man who walks in darkness itself.
