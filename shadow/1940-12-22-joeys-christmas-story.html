Joey's Christmas Story

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

The holiday season brings danger—not joy—to the mean streets this December evening, as The Shadow returns to CBS with a tale that strips away all sentimentality to reveal the dark heart lurking beneath Christmas cheer. When a street orphan named Joey stumbles upon a fortune in stolen jewels, he becomes the unwitting pawn in a ruthless criminal game where even the gift of hope can become a weapon. Lamont Cranston finds himself racing against time through snow-dusted alleyways and dimly-lit tenements, his hypnotic voice cutting through the static like a knife, as he pursues those who would exploit innocence for profit. Listeners will feel the bite of winter wind and hear the distant tolling of church bells mixed with the sinister orchestral flourishes that signal The Shadow's presence—that all-knowing entity who walks unseen among the desperate and the wicked.

By 1940, The Shadow had become radio's most iconic character, and this episode demonstrates exactly why. Actor Bill Johnstone's commanding presence transforms each episode into psychological theater, while the show's innovative sound design—created by the same team pushing the boundaries of what radio drama could achieve—creates an immersive world of shadow and menace. "Joey's Christmas Story" exemplifies the show's refusal to condescend to its audience with easy morality, instead presenting a complicated world where tragedy and redemption dance together in the darkness.

Tune in now to experience a masterpiece of 1940s radio drama, where the mystery isn't just whodunit, but whether virtue itself can survive in a world corrupted by greed. The Shadow knows. Do you?
