In the fog-shrouded streets of New York City, a murdered man returns from the grave—or does he? When the apparition of Caleb Mackenzie, a notorious smuggler thought dead these past ten years, materializes in the locked study of millionaire philanthropist Jonathan Ashford, murder follows in his spectral wake. The Shadow must pierce the veil between the living and the dead to uncover whether supernatural vengeance or cunning deception lurks behind the ghostly visitations. As Orson Welles' iconic voice drops to a whisper—"Who knows what evil lurks in the hearts of men?"—listeners are plunged into an atmosphere thick with dread, where every creaking floorboard and distant wail might herald the arrival of something far more sinister than any mortal criminal. The mystery deepens with each passing moment, and only The Shadow's uncanny ability to cloud men's minds stands between Ashford and oblivion.

By 1941, The Shadow had become America's most beloved crime fighter, a program that defined the golden age of radio drama. Originally appearing in Street & Smith's pulp magazine, the character had transcended his printed origins to become a cultural phenomenon, with Welles' masterful narration and the show's meticulous sound design setting the standard for all mystery programming that followed. "The Ghost of Caleb Mackenzie" exemplifies the show's genius: blending genuine detective work with supernatural intrigue, creating puzzles that challenge the mind while thrilling the spine.

Don't miss this masterwork of suspense and shadow. Tune in and discover why millions of Americans huddled around their radio sets each week, surrendering themselves to The Shadow's dark domain. The truth awaits in the darkness.
