# The Ruby Of Karavahl

*The Shadow* knows the evil that lurks in men's hearts—and in this gripping 1947 adventure, he must uncover the sinister truth behind a legendary jewel that has left a trail of death across continents. When a mysterious ruby surfaces in New York, traced to the remote Hindu kingdoms of Karavahl, our caped avenger finds himself entangled in a web of smugglers, obsessed collectors, and a dark curse that seems all too real. Listen as Orson Welles's velvety voice cuts through the darkness, as gunshots crack through opulent drawing rooms, and as Margot Lane desperately searches for her vanished companion. The Shadow's hypnotic laugh—that spine-tingling "Mwahahaha"—echoes through the night as he races against time to stop a murderer who believes himself invincible, protected by an ancient stone that promises unlimited power to those willing to kill for it.

By 1947, *The Shadow* had already captivated millions of Americans for over a decade, establishing itself as the gold standard of mystery radio drama. This particular episode represents the show at its creative peak—a time when the writers had perfected the art of combining hardboiled detective work with the supernatural undertones that made *The Shadow* unmistakably thrilling. The production values were lavish, the sound effects meticulous, and Welles, despite his growing Hollywood commitments, brought unmatched gravitas to his iconic role. "The Ruby Of Karavahl" showcases everything listeners adored: exotic locales, impossible crimes, and the reassuring presence of a hero who truly seemed to possess supernatural powers.

Don your imaginary fedora and step into the shadows with us. This is radio drama at its most hypnotic and suspenseful—a masterclass in tension that only the golden age of broadcasting could deliver. Tune in now and discover why *The Shadow* remains unforgettable.
