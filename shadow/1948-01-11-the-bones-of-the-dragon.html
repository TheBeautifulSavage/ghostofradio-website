The Bones Of The Dragon

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

When The Shadow's gravelly laugh pierces the fog-shrouded streets of New York City, you know evil is afoot—and on this chilling night in 1948, the evil runs deeper than the darkest tomb. "The Bones of the Dragon" pulls listeners into a mystery that begins in a dusty museum and spirals into murder most elaborate. A priceless Chinese skeleton has vanished from its display case, but when the theft's true purpose is revealed, the case becomes far more sinister than simple larceny. As Lamont Cranston navigates between high society collectors and desperate criminals, The Shadow must use both his powers of invisibility and his razor-sharp deductive mind to untangle a web of greed, obsession, and vengeance. Frank Readick's iconic performance crackles with barely contained menace; you can almost hear him moving through darkness you cannot see.

By 1948, The Shadow had become radio's most enduring vigilante, captivating audiences for over a decade with stories that blended gothic atmosphere with hard-boiled detective work. This particular episode exemplifies why the show remained a ratings juggernaut even as newer programs competed for listeners' attention—it showcases the perfect marriage of supernatural intrigue and procedural mystery that made The Shadow legendary. The production values are immaculate: the sound effects of footsteps, gunshots, and creaking doors create an almost tactile sense of danger, while the supporting cast delivers performances that feel urgently real.

Tune in now to experience "The Bones of the Dragon"—a masterclass in radio suspense where the line between the material and the mysterious blurs into shadow.
