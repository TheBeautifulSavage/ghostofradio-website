The Giant Of Madras

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

Picture yourself huddled near your radio on a crisp evening in 1948, the familiar intro swelling—"Who knows what evil lurks in the hearts of men? The Shadow knows!"—as Lamont Cranston's mysterious voice draws you into the exotic streets of Madras, India. In "The Giant Of Madras," listeners are transported to a shadowy underworld where a towering figure moves through the darkness with supernatural strength, leaving a trail of terror in his wake. What begins as an investigation into a series of impossible crimes—victims crushed by an unseen force—spirals into a tale of ancient curses, hidden identities, and The Shadow's relentless pursuit of truth. The episode crackles with tension as our cloaked hero navigates a labyrinth of deception, his hypnotic powers tested against a mystery that seems to defy explanation. Bill Johnstone's commanding performance as The Shadow captures every nuance of suspense, while the experthand-crafted sound effects transport you to a world of danger and intrigue.

By 1948, The Shadow had become more than a radio phenomenon—it was the gold standard of mystery programming, a show that had captivated audiences for over a decade with its psychological depth and sophisticated storytelling. The program's willingness to venture beyond New York's fog-shrouded streets to exotic locales like Madras reflected radio's unique power to take listeners anywhere imagination could reach. Each episode showcased the intricate dance between hero and villain, between seen and unseen forces, that made The Shadow essential listening for mystery enthusiasts nationwide.

Don't miss "The Giant Of Madras"—a masterclass in radio suspense that will keep you guessing until The Shadow's final revelation. Tune in and discover why audiences made this show an institution of American entertainment.
