A Mask For Murder

· GHOST OF RADIO ·
0:00 --:--

Deep in the Manhattan night, a society masquerade ball becomes the stage for an exquisite murder—one that only The Shadow can solve. When a priceless diamond vanishes alongside its owner, found cold and lifeless behind velvet curtains, the investigation spirals through a labyrinth of false identities and hidden motives. As Lamont Cranston moves through the glittering crowd, his hypnotic voice cuts through the orchestral swells and gasps of shock, peeling back layers of deception to reveal a killer hiding in plain sight. Listeners will be gripped by the crackling tension as our mysterious hero uses his uncanny ability to cloud men's minds, turning invisible on the ballroom floor to catch a murderer red-handed. The episode masterfully weaves together the glamour and danger of post-war New York society with the supernatural menace that made The Shadow radio's most thrilling adventure.

By 1948, The Shadow had perfected a formula that captivated millions—the brooding detective with extraordinary mental powers, brought to life by the legendary Orson Welles's successor, William Johnstone. In this golden age of radio drama, when sound effects were pure artistry and voice acting was the very heart of storytelling, The Shadow represented the peak of the medium's capacity to inspire fear and wonder. The show's influence on American popular culture cannot be overstated; it pioneered the template for the superhero detective and influenced everything from comic books to film noir. "A Mask For Murder" exemplifies why audiences across the nation gathered around their sets, suspended between worlds of crime and justice, reality and the supernatural.

Don't miss this masterwork of radio suspense. Tune in and experience the electricity that crackling through America's living rooms three-quarters of a century ago—where a single voice and the power of imagination proved mightier than any visual spectacle.
