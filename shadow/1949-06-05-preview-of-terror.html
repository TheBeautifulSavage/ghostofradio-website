When a mysterious fortune teller's prophecy of death comes terrifyingly true, The Shadow finds himself chasing a killer who seems to operate beyond the laws of chance and reason. In this gripping 1949 episode, listeners are drawn into the fog-laden streets of New York City where a séance becomes a death trap, and the line between supernatural terror and calculated murder blurs dangerously. As Lamont Cranston's uncanny powers of invisibility and mind-reading are tested against an adversary who kills with seemingly impossible precision, the sinister organ music that haunts each broadcast reaches fever pitch—will The Shadow's ability to know what evil lurks in men's hearts be enough when evil itself appears to command fate itself?

By 1949, The Shadow had become more than entertainment; it was a cultural institution that had captivated America for over a decade. The show's success lay in its perfect marriage of supernatural mystique and hard-boiled detective work—Cranston was both a phantom avenger and a rational investigator, appealing to listeners' desires for both wonder and logic. "Preview of Terror" exemplifies the show's golden-age formula at its peak, when writers had perfected the art of building unbearable suspense within a tight twenty-eight-minute format, and veteran actor Bret Morrison's silky, commanding voice had become as iconic as the character's black cloak itself.
