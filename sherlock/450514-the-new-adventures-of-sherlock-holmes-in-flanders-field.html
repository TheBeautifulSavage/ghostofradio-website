Step into the fog-shrouded streets of London during wartime as the great detective takes on a case that cuts to the very heart of the conflict consuming Europe. When a mysterious military intelligence officer arrives at 221B Baker Street bearing news of sabotage at a British airfield, Holmes and Watson find themselves drawn into a web of espionage, coded messages, and treacherous double agents. The clock ticks relentlessly as our heroes race against time to uncover a Nazi plot that could compromise the entire Allied war effort. Basil Rathbone's masterly portrayal of Holmes crackles with intellectual fire as he pieces together clues scattered across wartime Britain—a coded letter, a suspicious visitor, a chance remark overheard in a blackout. The tension builds inexorably toward a climactic confrontation where deductive brilliance must triumph over enemy cunning.

This episode exemplifies why The New Adventures of Sherlock Holmes captivated millions of listeners during America's most perilous hours. Rather than retreat into comfortable Victorian nostalgia, the program boldly transplanted the detective into contemporary wartime settings, proving that Holmes's timeless methods remained devastatingly relevant to modern threats. Rathbone and Nigel Bruce's chemistry was unmatched in radio history, their rapid-fire banter providing welcome levity between moments of genuine suspense. The show became essential listening for Americans hungry for stories of British pluck and American partnership against tyranny.

Tune in now to experience radio drama at its finest—when this medium could transport you across an ocean and into the very machinery of espionage. "In Flanders Field" reminds us why Sherlock Holmes endured: not as a period curiosity, but as a symbol of reason conquering darkness. Switch on your set and let Rathbone's voice guide you through one of detective fiction's finest hours.
