# Stars Over Hollywood: "Yesterday's Town"

Picture this: It's a warm August evening in 1940, and you've settled into your favorite chair with the radio glowing softly before you. As the opening theme swells—that unmistakable, dreamy orchestration that transports you straight to the Hollywood hills—you're about to step into "Yesterday's Town," an episode that captures something rarely heard on the airwaves: the bittersweet collision between a man's past and his present. A successful businessman returns to his childhood home, expecting to find it unchanged, frozen in amber. Instead, he discovers that time has rewritten every street corner, every friendship, every cherished memory. What unfolds is a haunting meditation on nostalgia and the impossibility of ever truly going home, delivered with the subtle emotional depth that made *Stars Over Hollywood* must-listen radio.

This anthology series, which ran throughout the 1940s on CBS, distinguished itself by refusing the melodrama that plagued so many competing programs. Instead, *Stars Over Hollywood* dealt in quiet revelations and interior crises—the small tragedies and unexpected grace notes of ordinary lives. "Yesterday's Town" exemplifies this approach, stripping away plot mechanics to focus on character and atmosphere. The episode's power lies not in what happens, but in what the protagonist realizes about himself, about the futility of recapturing what's been lost to time.

Don't miss this tender, intelligently crafted drama that reminds us why radio's golden age earned its name. Tune in as the mystery of memory unfolds, and discover why listeners kept *Stars Over Hollywood* on their dials week after week.
