# His First Mate

Picture this: It's a moonlit evening in the harbor, and the salt-spray mist rolls across the deck of a merchant vessel as our protagonist—a grizzled captain of thirty years—prepares to meet his new first mate. But there's a twist that neither man anticipated: the mate is a woman, sharp-tongued and determined, challenging every convention of the nautical world. In this tense forty-five-minute drama, *Stars Over Hollywood* brings you a story of professional respect clashing with old-fashioned pride, of two stubborn souls forced to navigate not just treacherous waters, but the murky depths of their own prejudices. Listen as barked orders give way to grudging admiration, as the creak of the ship's timbers becomes the backdrop for a battle of wills that will leave you breathless.

*Stars Over Hollywood* represented something rare in the golden age of radio: a series willing to explore unconventional relationships and social tensions with surprising nuance. Airing on CBS during the 1940s, when women were just beginning to claim their place in professions previously reserved for men, this anthology drama tackled real anxieties about changing gender roles with intelligence and humanity. "His First Mate" exemplifies the show's commitment to sophisticated storytelling—eschewing easy sentiment in favor of complex characters who learn and grow through their conflicts. The stellar cast brings these maritime professionals to vivid life, supported by the evocative sound design that made radio theater so transportive.

Don't miss this remarkable glimpse into post-war American anxieties and aspirations. Tune in now and experience why audiences kept their radios close and their hearts open during those golden broadcast nights. *Stars Over Hollywood*—where every drama cuts to the heart of what it means to be human.
