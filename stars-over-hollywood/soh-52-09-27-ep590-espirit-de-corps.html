Picture yourself tuning into CBS on a September evening in 1940, the amber glow of your radio dial settling into focus as the orchestra swells with that unmistakable Hollywood theme. Tonight's broadcast of Stars Over Hollywood presents "Esprit De Corps"—a taut drama of loyalty and ambition set against the glittering but cutthroat world of Tinseltown. As the cast takes their positions before the microphone, you'll find yourself drawn into a tale of camaraderie tested by professional jealousy, where the bonds between colleagues threaten to shatter under the weight of a career-making opportunity. The sound effects team stands ready—the muffled conversations of a studio lot, the creak of a dressing room door, the fateful telephone call that changes everything. This is radio drama at its finest: intimate, immediate, and utterly compelling.

Stars Over Hollywood was CBS's answer to audiences' insatiable fascination with show business itself. During the early 1940s, as America sought escape from gathering storm clouds abroad, these anthology dramas offered a glamorous peek behind the studio gates, starring established Hollywood names alongside gifted radio performers. The series thrived on exploring the human dramas beneath the star-studded surface—the compromises, the friendships forged in shared struggle, the moral complexities that no amount of studio lighting could illuminate. Each episode was a self-contained universe, yet collectively they painted an unflinching portrait of an industry built on dreams and desperation in equal measure.

Join us as actors breathe life into these timeless characters, their voices carrying across the decades to remind us that some stories—about ambition, loyalty, and what we're willing to sacrifice—never go out of style. Tune in to Stars Over Hollywood and discover why millions gathered around their radios to hear these tales night after night.
