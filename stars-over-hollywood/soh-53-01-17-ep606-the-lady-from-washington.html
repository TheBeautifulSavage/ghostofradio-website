# The Lady From Washington

Picture yourself huddled around the radio on a winter evening in 1941, the static crackling with anticipation as a woman's heels click sharply against marble corridors—the unmistakable sound of power and purpose. "The Lady From Washington" unfolds like a classified file being opened under dim lamplight. When a mysterious woman arrives in Hollywood bearing secrets from the nation's capital, the glittering façade of the film industry begins to crack. Is she a spy? A government investigator? A woman with a dangerous past she's trying to escape? Tension coils through every scene as our protagonist navigates a web of deception, studio corruption, and the kind of danger that doesn't make it into the gossip columns. The superb ensemble cast—voices you've come to know and trust through weeks of drama—delivers performances that crackle with urgency and barely concealed fear.

*Stars Over Hollywood* was CBS's crown jewel of dramatic anthology programming, a show that understood the American appetite for glamour mixed with genuine peril. Broadcast during the early 1940s, at a time when Washington held an almost mythic power in the national consciousness, this episode taps into contemporary anxieties about hidden government intrigue and the vulnerability of even our most celebrated institutions. The show's writers had an uncanny ability to ground Hollywood melodrama in real-world tension, reminding listeners that beneath the studio lights lurked moral complexity and genuine moral stakes.

Don't miss "The Lady From Washington"—a masterclass in suspense that proves *Stars Over Hollywood* was never just about the glamour. Tune in and discover why millions of Americans made this their must-listen appointment radio. Some mysteries, once started, demand to be solved.
