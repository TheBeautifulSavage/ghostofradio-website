# Soh 54 01 23 Ep660 Skin Deep

When the organ swells and the announcer's velvet baritone introduces tonight's tale, you'll find yourself transported to the glittering, merciless world of Hollywood's golden age. "Skin Deep" peels back the glamorous facade to reveal the desperation lurking beneath the studio lights—a story of ambition, deception, and the terrible price of reinvention. As the drama unfolds, you'll meet characters caught between their past selves and the manufactured versions the studios demand, each struggling with the question: who are we really when everything depends on the performance? The sound design is masterful, from the click of high heels on marble floors to the low, ominous strings that underscore each moment of moral compromise.

*Stars Over Hollywood* occupied a unique place in radio's landscape during its twelve-year run on CBS. Unlike lighter anthology programs, it specialized in the darker, more complex stories of Tinseltown—the kind of narratives the glossy fan magazines would never dare print. Broadcast in an era when Hollywood's power seemed absolute and its secrets were jealously guarded, these episodes offered listeners a rare, honest glimpse behind the curtain. The show attracted top-tier talent from the studios themselves, with professional actors lending authenticity and weight to every performance. By the 1940s, as the studio system reached its zenith, *Stars Over Hollywood* served as a cultural mirror, reflecting both the dreams and disillusionment of a nation captivated by cinema.

Tune in and discover why millions of listeners made this their appointment radio drama. "Skin Deep" awaits—a masterclass in tension and character, preserved now for nearly seventy years.
