# Stars Over Hollywood: "Listen To Your Heart"

On a sultry evening in September 1949, settle into your favorite chair and prepare yourself for a tale of passion, regret, and the choices that define us. In "Listen To Your Heart," a renowned concert pianist discovers that technical mastery means nothing when the soul has been neglected for ambition's sake. As string arrangements swell in the background and the signature sound effects of rustling evening gowns and distant applause transport you to the glittering world of Los Angeles high society, you'll find yourself drawn into a deeply human story of reconciliation and second chances. The narrator's warm, measured voice guides you through a evening where one man must confront the distance he's created between himself and the woman who believed in him first.

*Stars Over Hollywood* was CBS's answer to the insatiable American appetite for sophisticated drama in the forties and early fifties. During an era when radio was the primary medium for storytelling and escapism, these self-contained dramas offered something precious: affordable theater for the entire nation. Each episode was a pocket-sized morality play, crafted with genuine literary merit and performed by accomplished stage and screen actors who considered radio work a legitimate art form. The show's ability to capture the glamour and underlying pathos of Hollywood itself resonated with listeners across the country, making it a consistent favorite in the primetime lineup and spawning numerous imitators.

Don't miss your chance to experience the magic of golden-age radio drama. "Listen To Your Heart" remains a perfect example of why millions of Americans gathered around their sets night after night—a poignant reminder that sometimes the greatest performances happen not on stage, but in the moments when we finally listen to ourselves.
