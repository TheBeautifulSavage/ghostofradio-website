Studio One 47 05 20 Ep04 Alibi Ike

Step into the smoke-filled offices of a big-city detective bureau where nothing is quite what it seems. In this week's Studio One presentation, listeners will find themselves caught in a labyrinth of excuses, half-truths, and desperate alibis as a seemingly simple case spirals into something far more sinister. When a murder occurs and a suspect emerges with an explanation for every moment of his whereabouts, our detective must unravel whether he's dealing with an innocent man burdened by terrible luck or a criminal master so clever he's prepared an alibi for every possible accusation. The tension crackles through your speakers as each alibi unravels, each witness testimony contradicts the last, and the walls close in on someone—but who? The sound design places you directly in that interrogation room, where the scrape of a chair, the strike of a match, and the measured cadence of an experienced officer's questions become your window into deception and truth.

Studio One stands as CBS's flagship dramatic anthology, presenting a different story each week with the kind of production values and artistic ambition that defined the golden age of radio drama. By 1947, the program had earned a reputation for intelligent, character-driven narratives adapted from contemporary literature and original scripts written expressly for the medium. "Alibi Ike" showcases the show's particular strength: taking a simple premise—a man with too many explanations—and transforming it into a meditation on guilt, circumstance, and the unreliability of perception itself.

If you appreciate mysteries that keep you guessing until the final reveal, or simply want to experience radio drama at its finest, tune in to this classic episode. Studio One delivers the kind of storytelling that made radio the dominant medium of the era, where your imagination becomes the camera, and the human voice the most powerful special effect of all.
