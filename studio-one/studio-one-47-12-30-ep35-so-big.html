Studio One 47 12 30 Ep35 So Big

Step into the humble kitchen of a struggling immigrant family as Studio One brings Edna Ferber's beloved novel to vivid life on this December evening in 1947. "So Big" tells the story of Selina Peake DeJong, a woman of remarkable determination who arrives in the American heartland with nothing but her pride and her dreams, only to pour her entire life into the soil of a small Illinois farm. Listen as the intimate crackle of the broadcast transports you across decades, capturing the quiet intensity of a mother's sacrifice—the exhaustion in her voice, the defiant hope beneath her struggle, the slow realization that her own ambitions have been consumed by her fierce love for her son. As her boy grows into manhood, Selina must confront the painful distance between the future she imagined for him and the choices he makes for himself. This is radio drama at its finest: a deeply human story unfolding through superb performances and the subtle sound design that makes every moment feel immediate and real.

Studio One distinguished itself as CBS's prestige dramatic anthology, showcasing America's greatest literature adapted for the microphone during the medium's golden age. Producer Worthington Miner assembled exceptional talent—skilled actors, accomplished writers, and sound engineers who understood that radio's power lay not in spectacle but in emotional truth. Drawing from classic works and contemporary fiction, the series set the standard for sophisticated drama on the air, proving that radio could honor literature while creating something entirely its own.

Tonight, settle in and experience the profound emotional landscape of "So Big"—a portrait of the American immigrant experience and the universal struggle between personal dreams and familial duty. Tune in as Studio One demonstrates why listeners considered these Thursday evening broadcasts essential listening.
