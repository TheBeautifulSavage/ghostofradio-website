Picture yourself settling into your living room on a crisp January evening in 1948, the glow of your radio dial casting amber light across familiar furnishings, when you turn the dial to CBS and find yourself in the laboratory of Dr. Clitterhouse—a man of science whose curiosity has led him down a darkly fascinating path. What begins as clinical research becomes an obsession with crime itself, drawing our brilliant physician into the underworld with consequences both thrilling and terrifying. This episode crackles with psychological tension as the doctor's double life unravels, his impeccable mind warring against his increasingly dangerous impulses. You'll hear the sharp crack of gunfire, the tense whispers of criminal conspirators, and the internal monologue of a man watching himself lose control—all rendered with the precise, atmospheric direction that made Studio One's early broadcasts legendary.

Studio One arrived in 1947 as CBS's answer to radio drama's golden age, and this adaptation of John Wexley's stage play showcases exactly why the program became must-listen radio for discerning audiences. The show's commitment to literary merit and psychological realism set it apart from the melodrama factories of the day; these weren't simple morality plays but complex explorations of human nature adapted from Broadway's finest. By 1948, Studio One had already established itself as the thinking person's drama anthology, attracting top talent both before and behind the microphone. This particular episode exemplifies the show's mastery of suspense and characterization, transforming a medical man's fall from grace into something genuinely unsettling and profound.

Tune in and discover why Studio One captivated millions—where the best stories came not from pulp and fantasy, but from the darker corners of the human soul.
