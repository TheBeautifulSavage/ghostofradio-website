When the lights dim at CBS Studios on this April evening in 1948, you're transported into a shadowy world of political corruption and deadly secrets. The Glass Key crackles with tension from its opening moments—a tale of betrayal set against the glittering, treacherous backdrop of city politics where alliances are forged in smoke-filled rooms and broken just as quickly. As the drama unfolds, a murder investigation becomes a labyrinth of blackmail, false accusations, and a mysterious glass key that holds the power to destroy powerful men. The superb cast delivers every line with the crackling intensity that makes Studio One a must-listen program, their voices creating an atmosphere thick with moral ambiguity and danger. You'll find yourself leaning closer to your radio, hanging on every word, unable to predict where this dark tale of revenge and redemption will lead.

Studio One has established itself as CBS's premier dramatic anthology, a program unafraid to tackle complex stories with literary sophistication and psychological depth. Drawing from proven dramatic sources—in this case, Dashiell Hammett's hard-boiled noir novel—the show's producers craft narratives that treat their radio audience as intelligent listeners hungry for genuine drama. In 1948, as Americans emerged from wartime austerity into a new era of uncertainty, these gripping tales of corruption and moral compromise resonated deeply, reflecting the anxieties of a changing nation.

Tune in to Studio One and experience why this program has become the standard-bearer for intelligent radio drama. Whether you're a devoted fan or a newcomer to the medium, The Glass Key offers everything radio storytelling does best: masterful acting, expert sound design, and a story that will haunt you long after the final fade-out.
