EPISODE SUMMARY:

"Sorry Wrong Number - East Coast" is a masterclass in psychological suspense, as the eerie tales of "Suspense" came to life once again for listeners tuning in from coast to coast. The episode's central plot revolves around Mrs. Stevenson, a middle-aged woman whose life becomes increasingly entangled with that of a mysterious stranger, Mr. Greenleaf, who calls her using a wrong number at an unsettling hour of the night. As the conversation progresses, it becomes clear that this phone call is not just a mistake, but rather a calculated attempt to manipulate Mrs. Stevenson's life. The listener is taken on a thrilling ride as they navigate the blurred lines between reality and deception.

The atmosphere in this episode is thick with tension, with each carefully crafted scene building upon the last to create an air of escalating unease. Director William J. Bryan masterfully employs sound effects and music to heighten the sense of foreboding, drawing the listener into Mrs. Stevenson's desperate struggle to comprehend her situation. Meanwhile, actress Agnes Moorehead delivers a captivating performance as the increasingly distraught Mrs. Stevenson, imbuing her character with a vulnerability that makes the listener root for her even as she becomes embroiled in Mr. Greenleaf's sinister scheme.

As the episode hurtles towards its shocking climax, "Sorry Wrong Number - East Coast" showcases the genius of CBS's "Suspense" series, which expertly exploited the medium of radio drama to deliver suspenseful stories that rivaled those of films and stage plays of the time. With its intricate plot twists, memorable characters, and masterful storytelling, this episode remains a testament to the enduring power of radio as an art form.
