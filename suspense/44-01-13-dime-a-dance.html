HISTORICAL CONTEXT:

The year is 1942, a time when America was at war with Nazi Germany and Japan. The world was gripped by fear and uncertainty, as the threat of invasion loomed large on the horizon. Meanwhile, in the United States, tensions between labor unions and management were running high, leading to widespread strikes and protests that would eventually give rise to the post-war social movements of the 1960s.

Despite these dire circumstances, radio drama continued to captivate audiences across the country. Suspense was one of the most popular shows on CBS's lineup, with a loyal following of listeners who tuned in each week to escape into its thrilling world of crime and suspense. The show's success can be attributed in part to its focus on everyday people caught up in extraordinary circumstances, making it relatable and accessible to audiences from all walks of life.

Radio drama was also a powerful tool for social commentary, allowing writers to tackle issues that might have been too sensitive or complex for other forms of media. Suspense used this format to explore themes such as racial tension, police corruption, and the exploitation of women, tackling topics with sensitivity and nuance that were characteristic of the show's approach.
