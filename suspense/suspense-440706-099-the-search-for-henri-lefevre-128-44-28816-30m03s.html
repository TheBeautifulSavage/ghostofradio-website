# The Search For Henri Lefevre

As darkness falls over occupied France, a desperate manhunt begins for Henri Lefevre—a man whose very existence has become a question of life and death. Is he a hero or a traitor? A ghost or a fugitive? In this electrifying thirty-minute thriller, listeners will find themselves swept into a tense game of cat-and-mouse, where every shadow conceals a threat and every knock at the door could mean capture—or worse. The crackle of footsteps on cobblestone streets, the menacing whisper of pursuing agents, and the anguished pleas of those caught in Lefevre's wake create an atmosphere thick with danger and moral ambiguity. You'll experience the suffocating paranoia of a world where trust is currency and survival demands terrible choices.

*Suspense*, which ran on CBS from 1942 to 1962, became the gold standard of dramatic radio—a show where each week brought sophisticated storytelling that treated audiences as intelligent partners in fear. At the height of World War II, episodes like "The Search For Henri Lefevre" resonated with listeners living through their own anxieties about occupation, resistance, and the blurred lines between heroism and self-preservation. The program's commitment to psychological realism and character-driven narrative set it apart from mere pulp entertainment; these were plays that lingered in the mind long after the final chord of the closing organ music faded.

Tune in tonight and let the voice actors and haunting sound design transport you to a desperate moment in history. In an age before television's visual distractions, *Suspense* offers what cinema cannot: the raw power of imagination ignited by words, music, and the infinite possibilities of darkness.
