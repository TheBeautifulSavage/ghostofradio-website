# The Man Who Couldn't Lose

A desperate gambler sits across from fate itself, and tonight on Suspense, fortune's wheel spins toward a darkness he never imagined. In *The Man Who Couldn't Lose*, listeners will find themselves trapped in the suffocating heat of a casino back room where luck becomes a curse and every winning hand draws our protagonist deeper into a web of supernatural dread. As tension crackles through the radio speaker, you'll hear the clink of chips, the shuffle of cards, and the creeping realization that some bargains exact prices no earthly sum can settle. With only twenty-nine minutes to spare, the drama unfolds at the breakneck pace that made Suspense America's most trusted companion through the dark hours of night.

*Suspense* arrived on CBS in 1942 and immediately established itself as the gold standard of thriller radio, running for an unprecedented twenty years in a medium already crowded with dramatic offerings. What set it apart was an unwavering commitment to pure psychological terror—no monsters lurking under beds, but rather the monsters lurking in human nature itself. The show's writers understood that the radio's greatest strength lay in the listener's own imagination, and *The Man Who Couldn't Lose* exemplifies this perfectly, crafting a morality play wrapped in gambling intrigue that resonates with the postwar anxieties of its 1940s audience. Episodes like this one became essential listening, quoted in office break rooms and discussed in living rooms across the nation.

So dim the lights, settle into your chair, and prepare yourself for an evening of exquisite dread. *The Man Who Couldn't Lose* awaits—a masterclass in suspense from radio's greatest thriller program. This is one broadcast you won't want to miss.
