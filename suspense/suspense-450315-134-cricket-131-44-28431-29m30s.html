# Suspense: "Cricket"

In the gathering darkness of a remote countryside manor, a young woman finds herself trapped between reason and superstition when a peculiar sound—the persistent chirping of a cricket—becomes an omen of something far more sinister than mere insect noise. As the cricket's call grows louder and more insistent, reality begins to fracture around her. Is the creature real, or merely a harbinger of psychological unraveling? Through expertly crafted sound design and a mounting sense of dread, this episode transforms the humble cricket into something deeply unsettling, a relentless presence that seeps into the listener's consciousness with each deliberate pause and sudden crescendo. The intimate horror of isolation and paranoia grips the narrative, leaving you uncertain whether to dismiss the danger as fantasy or confront the possibility that some supernatural forces operate beyond our rational understanding.

*Suspense*, which aired for two decades beginning in 1942, became CBS's preeminent showcase for atmospheric terror and psychological thriller storytelling. The program's strength lay not in gore or explicit violence, but in what the audience's imagination could conjure from carefully orchestrated sound and dialogue. Episodes like "Cricket" exemplify why radio audiences huddled around their sets at night, surrendering themselves to unseen horrors. The show's writers and producers understood that true suspense lives in uncertainty—in the space between what is heard and what is feared, in the vulnerability of a voice crying out through the darkness.

Tune in to *Suspense* and discover why this legendary program still captivates listeners more than seventy years later. "Cricket" awaits—and once you hear that sound, you may never listen to the evening chorus quite the same way again.
