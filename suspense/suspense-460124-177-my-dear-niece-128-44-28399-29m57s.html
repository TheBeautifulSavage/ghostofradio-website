# My Dear Niece

Picture this: a woman's scream pierces the darkness of your living room as you dial in to CBS, the orchestra swelling with sinister strings. In *My Dear Niece*, you'll find yourself entangled in a web of family secrets and murderous intentions, where the bonds of blood become instruments of betrayal. When a wealthy elderly woman welcomes what she believes to be a cherished relation into her home, listeners are drawn into an atmosphere thick with suspicion—every creak of the floorboards, every carefully measured word of dialogue laden with hidden menace. The plot twists like a serpent, building toward a climax where trust itself becomes the most dangerous currency of all. This is Suspense at its finest: the intimate horror of deception within the family circle, where the greatest dangers lurk behind a smile and a kind word.

Throughout its two-decade reign, *Suspense* earned its reputation as "radio's outstanding theater of thrills" by masterfully exploiting what the medium does best—letting listeners' imaginations construct their own vivid nightmares. Without visual cues, the show's writers and sound designers created dread through dialogue, foley artistry, and the unsettling power of suggestion. *My Dear Niece* exemplifies this golden age of radio drama, when millions would gather around their sets for thirty minutes of pure psychological terror, completely transported by the power of voice and sound alone. The episode showcases the stellar acting that made CBS's dramatic programming legendary, with performers bringing nuance and conviction to characters whose true natures remain tantalizingly obscured.

Don't miss this classic tale of family treachery and darker impulses. Tune in and discover why, for over two decades, *Suspense* kept audiences on the edge of their seats—and why these episodes remain utterly gripping today. Some stories lose nothing in the telling; they only gain new power with time.
