# Your Devoted Wife

Picture a quiet suburban home as evening falls, the kind of place where neighbors wave from porches and secrets simmer behind lace curtains. When you tune into "Your Devoted Wife," you'll find yourself trapped in the claustrophobic world of a marriage that appears picture-perfect from the outside—but harbors something far darker within. As the tension builds across twenty-nine breathless minutes, you'll discover that devotion can mask obsession, and love can curdle into something unspeakably sinister. The cast delivers performances that crackle with barely contained menace, while the sound design—creaking floorboards, the soft clink of a glass, a door closing with terrible finality—draws you deeper into this domestic nightmare. This is suspense in its truest form: not the jump-scare of a monster in the shadows, but the creeping dread of betrayal in the one place you should feel safest.

*Suspense* ran for two decades on CBS as one of radio's most acclaimed thriller anthologies, pioneering a formula that would influence television and film for generations. Each week, listeners never knew whether they'd encounter psychological terror, murder most cunning, or supernatural horror—only that they'd be gripped by expert storytelling and stellar character work. The show attracted Hollywood's finest talent and some of radio's most inventive writers, all working to prove that the most terrifying stories play out not on a screen, but in the theater of the listener's own imagination. Episodes like "Your Devoted Wife" exemplify why millions tuned in faithfully, eager to be unsettled and thrilled in equal measure.

Don't miss this masterclass in marital menace. Settle in with the lights low and let *Suspense* work its sinister magic—because in matters of the heart, you never really know what someone is capable of.
