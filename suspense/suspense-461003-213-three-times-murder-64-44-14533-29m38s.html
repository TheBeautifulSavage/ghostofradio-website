# Suspense: Three Times Murder

Picture this: it's a moonless October evening, and you've settled into your favorite chair with the radio glowing warm in the darkness. When the organ strikes its iconic, unsettling chord, you know what's coming—*Suspense*. Tonight's offering, "Three Times Murder," pulls you into a labyrinth of deception where nothing is as it seems. A murder has been committed, but as the investigation unfolds through crackling phone lines and shadowy interrogations, you'll discover that the truth is buried beneath layers of misdirection and lies. Is the accused killer truly guilty, or is someone orchestrating an elaborate frame? With each twist, the noose tightens—for someone. The superb cast draws you deeper into paranoia and doubt, their voices conveying every flicker of suspicion and desperation with remarkable subtlety.

*Suspense* became CBS's crown jewel of terror because it understood something fundamental about radio drama: the most terrifying monsters live in the listener's own imagination. Debuting in 1942, the show became legendary for its ability to burrow under your skin without relying on special effects or visible threats—just masterful writing, impeccable sound design, and performances that made the impossible feel disturbingly plausible. "Three Times Murder" exemplifies this approach, crafting a psychological puzzle that rewards close listening while keeping you perpetually off-balance.

Don't let this gem slip away into the static. Tune in to "Three Times Murder" and experience why millions of Americans huddled around their radios each week, transfixed by tales that proved the greatest thrills need only a voice, a sound effect, and your willingness to believe. Once you hear those first haunting notes, you'll understand why *Suspense* remains unforgettable.
