Suspense, which ran from 1942 to 1962 on CBS, became the gold standard for radio drama by refusing easy answers and comfortable resolutions. With a rotating cast of Hollywood's finest actors and an orchestra that could make your spine tingle with a single violin note, the show earned its place as one of the most celebrated programs of radio's golden age. These weren't simple morality plays—they were exercises in psychological complexity, featuring protagonists who weren't always heroic and circumstances that challenged listeners' assumptions about right and wrong. "Three Faces At Midnight" exemplifies this signature approach, trapping its characters in a web of contingency where survival and conscience collide.
