# Suspense: "The Green-Eyed Monster"

As the CBS orchestra swells into that iconic theremin wail, you're transported into a world where jealousy becomes a living, breathing force—one capable of destroying everything in its path. "The Green-Eyed Monster" pulls no punches: a marriage already fractured by suspicion splinters further when a mysterious third party enters the scene. What begins as whispered accusations and lingering glances transforms into something far more sinister, as our protagonist's paranoia spirals into obsession. Every shadow suggests betrayal; every innocent conversation hums with hidden meaning. The crackling tension builds relentlessly as we're drawn into the fevered mind of someone consumed by doubt, where the line between what's real and what's imagined blurs dangerously. By the episode's climax, you'll find yourself breathless—uncertain whether you've witnessed a tragedy of passion or something far more calculated.

*Suspense* commanded America's airwaves during an era when radio was the nation's primary entertainment, and this 1947 broadcast exemplifies why the show became legendary. For two decades, CBS refused to compromise on quality, importing seasoned stage actors and top-tier writers who understood that true horror lives in psychology, not spectacle. Each episode delivered exactly what the title promised: unrelenting suspense built through dialogue, sound design, and the listener's own imagination—the most powerful special effect ever created. The show's writers understood that jealousy, doubt, and fear are universal human experiences, making these stories resonate across every demographic.

Don't miss "The Green-Eyed Monster." Settle into your chair, dim the lights, and let yourself fall under *Suspense*'s spell. Some broadcasts fade with time; this one will haunt you long after the final fadeout.
