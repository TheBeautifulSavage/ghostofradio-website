# Suspense: "Wet Saturday" and "August Heat"

On a sultry evening in the 1940s, settle into your favorite chair as CBS's *Suspense* presents two masterworks of psychological terror that will keep you riveted to your radio. In "Wet Saturday," the intimate horror of a family bound by dark secrets unfolds in real time, each line of dialogue a noose tightening around the listeners' nerves. The follow-up, "August Heat," offers a chilling meditation on fate and the uncanny—a tale where an artist's simple commission becomes a harbinger of something far more sinister. Across nearly an hour of pure dramatic tension, you'll experience the creeping dread that made radio horror America's most compelling entertainment, where the darkness exists only in your mind, making it all the more terrifying.

*Suspense* reigned as CBS's premier anthology thriller from 1942 through 1962, earning its reputation through meticulous writing, impeccable sound design, and performances from Hollywood's finest talent. This particular double-feature exemplifies why the show captivated millions of listeners during radio's golden age—these weren't mere shock tactics, but carefully constructed narratives exploring the psychological fractures of ordinary people confronting extraordinary evil. The program's influence on later horror and thriller television cannot be overstated; it established the template for suspenseful storytelling that would define the medium for decades.

If you've never experienced the visceral thrill of classic radio horror, or if you're a devoted fan seeking to revisit these gems, this pair of episodes represents *Suspense* at its finest. Dim the lights, tune in, and prepare yourself for an unforgettable journey into the darker corners of human nature—where every sound effect carries weight and every silence holds menace.
