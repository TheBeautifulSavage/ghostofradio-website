As the clock tower chimes approach the witching hour, a condemned man sits in his final cell, awaiting dawn and the gallows. But when a mysterious stranger arrives with an extraordinary proposition—a chance to escape his fate through means both supernatural and terrifying—our protagonist discovers that some bargains exact a price far steeper than death itself. In this taut thirty-minute thriller, Suspense masterfully weaves together the claustrophobic dread of a prison death row with metaphysical horror, as listeners are drawn deeper into a moral labyrinth where every escape route leads to a darker corner. The ticking clock becomes more than mere backdrop; it's a character unto itself, reminding us that time is the one force no man can ultimately bargain with. What transpires in those final minutes before midnight will chill your blood and linger long after the final fade-out.

Throughout its two-decade run, Suspense earned its reputation as perhaps the finest thriller anthology ever broadcast, consistently delivering tales that gripped America's living rooms with white-knuckle intensity.
