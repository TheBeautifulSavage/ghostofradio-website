# Mission Completed

Picture yourself huddled near your radio set on a December evening, the winter darkness pressing against your windows as an ominous organ note pierces the quiet of your living room. *Mission Completed* begins with deceptive calm—a soldier's homecoming, the promise of peace after years of distant warfare. But as our protagonist settles back into civilian life, the listener senses something terribly wrong. What appears to be a triumphant return becomes a descent into paranoia and psychological torment, where the greatest enemy may be the one looking back from the mirror. The tight twenty-nine minutes unfold with mounting dread, each domestic detail twisted into something sinister. Was the mission truly completed, or has something far more dangerous followed our hero home?

*Suspense* remained America's premier thriller anthology throughout the 1940s and into the Cold War era, a showcase for Hollywood's finest talent and some of radio's most inventive writers. Broadcasting live from CBS studios, each week brought fresh nightmares to millions of households—tales that exploited the era's very real anxieties about espionage, betrayal, and the psychological scars of global conflict. This particular episode exemplifies the show's mastery of internal terror, preferring the creeping dread of a fractured mind to cheap shock tactics. With the war still fresh in listeners' memories, *Mission Completed* tapped into genuine fears about how veterans might struggle to readjust to ordinary life.

Tune in tonight for a masterclass in psychological suspense. Let the crackling audio transport you back to an age when imagination, not special effects, created genuine terror. *Suspense* awaits—and once you hear that organ's opening chord, you'll understand why this show captivated a nation for two decades.
