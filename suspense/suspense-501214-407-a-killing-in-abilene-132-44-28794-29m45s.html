Suspense, which graced CBS airwaves from 1942 to 1962, became the gold standard of radio thriller programming, earning its place in the pantheon of American broadcasting through relentless commitment to psychological terror and human drama. Hosted by the legendary announcer who set the tone with his spine-tingling introduction, each episode was a carefully crafted descent into darkness, featuring some of radio's finest actors in stories that preferred the shadow to the spotlight. "A Killing in Abilene" exemplifies the show's genius for taking ordinary American settings—small towns, everyday conflicts, domestic betrayal—and transforming them into crucibles of suspense where the listener's imagination becomes complicit in the mystery.
