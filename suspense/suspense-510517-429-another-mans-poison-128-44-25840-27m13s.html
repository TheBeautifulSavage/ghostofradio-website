# Another Man's Poison

As darkness falls and the familiar Suspense theme creeps across the airwaves, listeners settle in for a tale of deadly deception and poisoned trust. "Another Man's Poison" weaves a labyrinthine plot where nothing is quite as it seems—where a simple act of kindness becomes a weapon, and where the most dangerous toxin may not be the one in a glass. In this taut twenty-seven-minute thriller, the line between victim and villain blurs with each revelation, and the listener must navigate a maze of motives, secrets, and simmering resentments. The superb cast brings each twist to life with understated precision, their voices betraying barely concealed anxieties and carefully constructed lies. You'll find yourself leaning closer to the radio, certain you've solved the mystery—only to discover the poisoner's true identity lurking in an entirely unexpected corner.

Throughout its twenty-year run from 1942 to 1962, Suspense became the gold standard of American radio drama, presenting over two thousand tales of psychological terror and moral ambiguity. Each episode was meticulously crafted to exploit radio's unique power: the listener's imagination. Without visual distractions, audiences inhabited every shadow, heard every accusation, felt every heartbeat of suspense. "Another Man's Poison" exemplifies the show's mastery of this intimate medium—a closed-circle mystery where backstory and character psychology matter as much as plot mechanics. The writers understood that true suspense isn't about what happens, but about the dread of not knowing what will happen next.

Don't miss your opportunity to experience a masterwork of mid-century radio craftsmanship. Tune in for "Another Man's Poison" and discover why audiences huddled around their receivers night after night, surrendering themselves completely to Suspense. One sip could prove fatal—will you dare to listen?
