Step into the shadows with this gripping installment of CBS's legendary Suspense, where the comfortable veneer of ordinary life conceals something far more sinister. In "The Truth About Jerry Baxter," a seemingly straightforward story about a man named Jerry unravels into something far darker—a psychological thriller that questions whether the person sitting across from you is truly who they claim to be. As the drama unfolds across thirty haunting minutes, the tension mounts with each revelation, building toward a conclusion that will leave listeners questioning the reliability of their own perceptions. The experthand-crafted sound design—footsteps echoing through empty corridors, doors creaking open to reveal uncomfortable truths, the subtle manipulation of silence itself—creates an atmosphere of creeping dread that only the golden age of radio could achieve.

Suspense became America's premier anthology series precisely because it understood that the most terrifying stories are those rooted in recognizable reality. Running from 1942 to 1962, the program earned its reputation by transforming everyday anxieties into nail-biting drama, featuring some of Hollywood's finest talent both before and behind the microphone. These episodes represent the sophisticated storytelling that captivated millions of listeners huddled around their radios in the 1940s, when radio dramas dominated American entertainment and proved that what the imagination conjures is far more frightening than anything seen on a screen.

Don't miss this masterclass in psychological suspense. Tune in and discover why Suspense remains the gold standard of vintage radio drama—where the truth about Jerry Baxter may be far more disturbing than anyone anticipated.
