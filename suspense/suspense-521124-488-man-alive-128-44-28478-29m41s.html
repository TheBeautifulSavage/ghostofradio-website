# Man Alive

Step into the shadowed streets of a city gripped by fear as an ordinary man discovers he's caught in an extraordinary nightmare. In this chilling installment of *Suspense*, a protagonist finds himself entangled in circumstances so bizarre and terrifying that he begins to question the very nature of his existence. Is he truly alive, or has he stumbled into something far more sinister? The taut twenty-eight minutes that follow crackle with mounting dread, punctuated by Wilbur Budd Hulick's masterful sound design—footsteps echoing down empty corridors, the scratch of a needle on vinyl, whispered accusations that seem to come from everywhere and nowhere. This is the kind of psychological thriller that makes you check the locks on your doors long after the final dramatic sting has faded.

*Suspense* reigned as American radio's premier thriller program throughout the 1940s and 1950s, a time when families gathered around their sets for tales that would burrow into the imagination and stay there. CBS invested heavily in the show, recruiting Hollywood's finest talent—from established stars to rising talents looking to hone their craft in live performance. Each episode was a carefully orchestrated production, a live broadcast where a single missed cue or flubbed line could derail the entire dramatic arc. "Man Alive" exemplifies the show's particular genius: taking the everyday and twisting it into something profoundly unsettling, exploring the thin line between sanity and delusion, reality and nightmare.

If you haven't yet experienced the raw power of *Suspense*, there's no better time than now. Dial in, dim the lights, and prepare yourself for twenty-eight minutes of first-rate suspense that showcases why this program became a cultural institution. Your imagination will do the rest.
