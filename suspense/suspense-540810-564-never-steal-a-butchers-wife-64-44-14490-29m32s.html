# Suspense: Never Steal A Butcher's Wife

Picture yourself huddled near the radio on a fog-laden evening, the amber glow of the dial the only light in the darkened room. As the iconic *Suspense* theme crashes through your speakers—that jarring orchestral sting that made millions of 1940s listeners jump from their seats—you're pulled into a tale of passion, jealousy, and the lengths a man will go to protect what he believes is his. In "Never Steal A Butcher's Wife," an ordinary neighborhood butcher becomes something far more dangerous when desire and possessiveness collide. The atmosphere crackles with menace as relationships tangle and deception unfolds, each revelation tightening the noose around the characters' fates. This is radio drama at its most intimate—no special effects, just the raw power of voices, sound effects, and orchestration to conjure a world of genuine moral peril.

*Suspense* was America's premier thriller series, broadcasting live from CBS studios for two decades and earning a devoted following that rivaled even the most popular variety programs. Each week, host William Holden (and later others) would introduce a new tale designed to unsettle and disturb, drawing from literature, original scripts, and real crime. The show pioneered the concept of psychological horror for mass audiences, proving that what listeners *imagined* while hearing talented actors perform was infinitely more terrifying than anything visible on stage or screen. By the 1940s, when "Never Steal A Butcher's Wife" aired, *Suspense* had perfected its formula—economical storytelling, expert pacing, and a keen understanding of human weakness.

Don't miss this mesmerizing slice of radio's Golden Age. Tune in and discover why listeners made *Suspense* appointment listening for over twenty years.
