# The Man With The Steel Teeth

Picture this: a fog-shrouded street corner in the dead of night, the distant wail of a siren, and the unmistakable click-click-click of metal teeth snapping in the darkness. "The Man With The Steel Teeth" pulls listeners into a nightmare of urban terror where an ordinary evening becomes a descent into primal horror. A mysterious stranger with a grotesque dental affliction preys upon the unsuspecting, and our protagonist finds themselves caught in a web of mounting dread and impossible choices. The episode's masterly sound design—those chilling metallic clicks punctuating every moment of tension—transforms what could have been a simple tale into something deeply unsettling. You'll find yourself gripping your radio dial as the suspense tightens, wondering if anyone can escape the grip of this predator who hides in plain sight.

*Suspense*, which aired on CBS from 1942 to 1962, became the gold standard of radio thriller programming, commanding millions of listeners with its motto: "Suspense—none of your old radio favorites, but real stories of real people caught in circumstances that challenge their courage, their love, their sanity." This episode represents the show at its finest—a lean, efficient thriller that understands that the most frightening monsters are those who wear a human face. The writers and producers crafted tales that exploited the unique power of radio itself: the listener's imagination becomes the most potent special effect, far more terrifying than any visual could ever be.

Don't miss your chance to experience *Suspense* as audiences did nearly eighty years ago—settle in, dim the lights, and let this masterwork of audio terror remind you why radio drama remains unmatched in its ability to burrow into the listener's mind and stay there, long after the final fade-out.
