# The Whole Town's Sleeping

On a sultry summer evening, the comfortable rhythms of small-town America mask something sinister lurking just beyond the streetlights. When a dangerous killer escapes from a nearby institution, the peaceful citizens of a quiet Midwestern town find their world transformed into a landscape of fear and suspicion. This is *Suspense*, and in "The Whole Town's Sleeping," you'll experience the creeping dread of an ordinary community gripped by extraordinary terror. As darkness falls and the town locks its doors, three young women discover that safety is an illusion—and that the predator they fear may be closer than anyone dares imagine. The episode unfolds with the deliberate pacing and psychological precision that made *Suspense* radio's most celebrated thriller, building tension through shadowed alleys, whispered conversations, and the terrible knowledge that danger walks among neighbors once thought trustworthy.

Broadcast during the golden age of American radio drama, *Suspense* dominated CBS airwaves for two decades, earning its place as one of the most influential thriller programs ever produced. This particular episode exemplifies the show's genius for finding horror in the familiar, transforming small-town settings into chambers of anxiety. The writers understood that true fear doesn't come from monsters or elaborate plot mechanics—it emerges from the violation of safety, from the realization that evil doesn't announce itself with thunder and lightning but arrives quietly, wearing an ordinary face.

Settle into your listening chair, dim the lights if you dare, and prepare yourself for a masterclass in radio suspense. "The Whole Town's Sleeping" remains a haunting reminder of why millions of Americans tuned in each week to experience *Suspense*—where every shadow hides a secret, and nowhere is truly safe.
