# The Twelfth Rose

As darkness falls and the familiar Suspense theme pierces the evening air, listeners settle into their favorite chairs for a tale of obsession and dread. In "The Twelfth Rose," a sinister mystery unfolds around a seemingly innocent romantic gesture—but each bloom carries with it a mounting sense of terror. Someone is leaving roses, and with each delivery comes a coded message, a confession, a warning. The tension mounts with every knock at the door, every rustling of petals, as the protagonist realizes that these aren't tokens of affection, but harbingers of something far more sinister. Will they decipher the pattern before the twelfth rose arrives? The master storytellers of Suspense craft an atmosphere thick with dread, where even flowers become instruments of psychological torment.

For over two decades, Suspense earned its place as radio's finest thriller program, attracting millions of listeners who craved sophisticated entertainment that relied entirely on sound, voice, and imagination. CBS's commitment to quality drama meant that each episode featured talented performers and writers at the height of their craft. "The Twelfth Rose" exemplifies the show's genius: taking an everyday occurrence and twisting it into something genuinely nightmarish. Unlike the pulp horror of lesser programs, Suspense dealt in psychological terror and human darkness—the kind that lingers long after the final fade-out.

If you haven't yet experienced the masterful command of suspense and storytelling that made this program legendary, "The Twelfth Rose" offers the perfect entry point. Tune in, dim the lights, and prepare yourself for nearly thirty minutes of expertly crafted radio drama that proves the most terrifying things are often those we can only imagine.
