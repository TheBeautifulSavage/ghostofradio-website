# A Matter of Timing

When the clock strikes in the dead of night, a man realizes his carefully laid plans have unraveled by mere minutes—and now there's no turning back. In this taut episode of *Suspense*, the seemingly simple matter of synchronizing watches becomes a matter of life and death. As tension mounts with each passing second, listeners will find themselves gripped by the psychological machinery of a thriller where timing is everything and fate hangs on the swing of a pendulum. The cast delivers performances crackling with desperation and cunning, their voices painting a world where one wrong second can mean the difference between freedom and ruin.

For over two decades, *Suspense* became America's appointment with terror—the show that transformed the radio into a portal of psychological dread and nail-biting drama. Premiering in 1942 on CBS, it became legendary for its commitment to craft, featuring some of Hollywood's finest actors in tales that proved the human imagination could terrify far more effectively than any visual effect. The program's golden age in the 1940s represents the medium at its artistic peak, when a creaking floorboard, a whispered confession, or the tick of a clock could paralyze an entire nation of listeners. Each episode was meticulously constructed to exploit the unique power of radio—forcing audiences to construct their own nightmares from pure suggestion and sound.
