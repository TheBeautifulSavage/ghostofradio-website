# The Tramp

Picture this: a desolate highway at dusk, a woman alone in her car, and the unmistakable sound of footsteps on gravel growing steadily closer. In "The Tramp," *Suspense* delivers one of radio's most primal terrors—the vulnerability of isolation and the menace lurking just beyond the reach of headlights and locked doors. As our protagonist encounters a mysterious wanderer on a lonely stretch of road, the line between Good Samaritan and fatal mistake becomes razor-thin. The radio crackles with tension: every pause in dialogue hangs heavy with dread, every rustle of fabric or distant train whistle amplifies the mounting sense of danger. Within thirty minutes, listeners would discover that sometimes the most dangerous threats wear a human face, and that chance encounters can spiral into nightmare. This is *Suspense* at its finest—intimate, visceral, and utterly inescapable.

For two decades, *Suspense* reigned as CBS's crown jewel of terror, captivating millions of listeners in darkened living rooms across America. Debuting in 1942 and running through the early 1960s, the show pioneered the art of psychological horror on radio, proving that the most frightening images were those conjured in the listener's imagination. With a rotating ensemble of Hollywood's finest talent and scripts that ranged from supernatural chills to everyday horrors, *Suspense* became the gold standard of thriller radio drama. "The Tramp" exemplifies the show's genius for finding terror in the ordinary—not in monsters or mad scientists, but in the unexpected encounters and split-second decisions that define human existence.

Don't miss your chance to experience this masterwork of radio drama. Tune in to "The Tramp" and rediscover why, for nearly two decades, *Suspense* kept audiences on the very edge of their seats.
