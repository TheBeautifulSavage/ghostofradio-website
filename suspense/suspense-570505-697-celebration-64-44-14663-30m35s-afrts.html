# Celebration

Picture yourself settling into your favorite chair as evening shadows lengthen across your living room. The orchestra swells with an ominous flourish, and Orson Welles' commanding voice intones those two immortal words: "Suspense..." In this May 1945 broadcast, listeners are drawn into a tale of celebration turned sinister, where festive anticipation curdles into dread. What begins as preparations for a joyous occasion takes a dark and unexpected turn, transforming the intimate details of ordinary life into instruments of psychological terror. The genius of this episode lies in its ability to weaponize normalcy—the very elements that should comfort us instead become harbingers of something far more terrible.

*Suspense* was radio's most celebrated thriller program, a CBS institution that ran for twenty electrifying years and specialized in turning everyday situations into nightmares. In the mid-1940s, when this episode aired, the show had perfected its craft: master sound designers created unsettling soundscapes while writers crafted twist endings that left listeners breathless. The program's willingness to venture into psychological horror rather than relying solely on monsters or mayhem set it apart from its competitors. Each episode was a self-contained masterwork, often adapted from published short stories, that proved radio drama could be as sophisticated and unsettling as any thriller found in literature.

Whether you're a longtime devotee of classic radio or discovering the golden age of broadcasting for the first time, this episode exemplifies everything that made *Suspense* legendary. Dim the lights, adjust the dial, and prepare yourself for thirty minutes of pure, unrelenting tension. Some thrills never fade—they only grow more chilling with time.
