# Suspense: Flood on the Goodwins

When the storm surge rises over the Goodwin Sands—those treacherous shallows off the English coast where countless ships have met their doom—desperate men find themselves trapped between the merciless sea and an impossible choice. In this taut 1940s thriller, listeners are pulled into the suffocating tension of a doomed vessel as the waters climb and the radio distress calls grow more frantic. The sound design crackles with authentic maritime peril: the groan of straining metal, the relentless hiss of rising floodwater, and voices strained to their breaking point as survival becomes a question of who will live and who will be left behind. This is *Suspense* at its finest—intimate, claustrophobic terror playing out in real time as the clock and the tides work against our protagonists.

For over two decades, *Suspense* reigned as America's premier thriller program, and episodes like "Flood on the Goodwins" exemplify why CBS's anthology series captivated millions of listeners huddled around their radio sets. Each week brought a standalone nightmare, expertly crafted to exploit the listener's imagination in ways television never could. The show's writers understood that radio's greatest power lay not in what could be shown, but in what listeners would conjure in their minds—making every dripping pipe and distant scream exponentially more terrifying. During the 1940s, when these episodes aired, *Suspense* offered respite from the real horrors of wartime, channeling anxieties into carefully controlled narratives where tension could be resolved in thirty minutes.

Step aboard this sinking vessel and experience the golden age of radio drama. "Flood on the Goodwins" awaits—will you answer the call?
