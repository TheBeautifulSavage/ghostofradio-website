High in the Colorado Rockies, where mountain passes cut through howling winds and isolation breeds madness, a terrible secret waits to be unearthed. When a man arrives at a remote lodge claiming his wife has vanished without a trace, the other guests find themselves trapped—not just by the merciless winter storm raging outside, but by suspicion and dread that creeps through every shadowed corridor. Is she truly lost in the snow, or has something far more sinister unfolded within these walls? As the mystery deepens and motives emerge from the darkness, listeners will find themselves gripping their radio dials, wondering who among this assembled cast of characters harbors a murderous truth.

Suspense, which commanded American radio audiences from 1942 through 1962, became the gold standard of dramatic thrills, with some 3,000 episodes crafted to ambush listeners' peace of mind during their evening routines. Hosted by the velvet-voiced announcer who promised that "in the world of drama, there is no shadow of a premise too far-fetched," the program assembled Hollywood's finest talent—from established stars to rising performers—to breathe life into tales of betrayal, danger, and the macabre. Each episode was a masterwork of sonic storytelling: the creak of floorboards, the howl of wind, and perfectly timed silences all conspired to transform a living room into a chamber of horrors. "Affair at Loveland Pass" exemplifies the show's genius for domestic dread, proving that the most terrifying mysteries often lurk closest to home.

Don't miss this chilling journey into the Colorado night. Tune in and discover whether truth or treachery waits at Loveland Pass.
