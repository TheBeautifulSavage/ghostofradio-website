# Star Over Hong Kong

As the familiar *Suspense* theme wails through your radio speaker and the scene-setting sound effects crackle to life, you're transported to the fog-shrouded streets of wartime Hong Kong—a city of intrigue, danger, and moral ambiguity. A mysterious star becomes the focal point of desperation as characters caught between empires scheme, betray, and fight for survival in a port city teetering on the edge of chaos. The tension mounts with each passing moment: whispered conversations in shadowy alleys, the threat of discovery, and the constant reminder that in such a place, trust is the most dangerous commodity of all. William N. Robson's masterful direction guides listeners through this maze of espionage and human conflict, where every footstep and door slam carries the weight of life-or-death consequences.

*Suspense* was America's premier thriller program, and by the 1940s it had perfected the art of psychological terror and sophisticated storytelling. Rather than relying on supernatural gimmicks, the show's genius lay in exploring the darkest corners of human nature—greed, fear, betrayal, and the desperate choices ordinary people make under extraordinary circumstances. "Star Over Hong Kong" exemplifies this approach perfectly, grounding its drama in the very real anxieties of World War II, when international intrigue, espionage, and the fog of war were genuine terrors in listeners' minds. The episode showcases why CBS's *Suspense* commanded millions of devoted listeners who trusted the program to deliver intelligent, adult entertainment.
