# Deep, Deep Is My Love

When you tune in to this chilling April evening broadcast, prepare yourself for a descent into obsession and madness that lurks beneath the surface of ordinary love. "Deep, Deep Is My Love" pulls listeners into the suffocating world of a passion so consuming it threatens to drown everything in its path. As the orchestra swells with unsettling strings and the narrator's voice cuts through the darkness, you'll find yourself trapped in a psychological labyrinth where devotion curdles into something sinister. The intimate setting—perhaps a parlor, a bedroom, a place meant for tenderness—becomes a pressure cooker of jealousy and desperation. By the time the final twist arrives in these sixteen minutes and forty-six seconds, you'll understand that some loves are beautiful precisely because they're dangerous.

This episode represents *Suspense* at the height of its remarkable run on CBS, a program that redefined what radio drama could achieve during the Golden Age. Premiering in 1942, the series made its reputation by exploring the psychological horror lurking in everyday life, proving that the most terrifying threats often come from within the human heart rather than from monsters or murderers. With accomplished writers, seasoned voice actors, and a production team that understood how to manipulate silence and sound for maximum impact, *Suspense* became appointment listening for millions of Americans. These weren't gimmicky shocks—they were carefully constructed narratives that understood human nature's darkest corners.

Whether you're a devoted fan of classic radio or discovering *Suspense* for the first time, "Deep, Deep Is My Love" offers everything that made this series legendary: superb storytelling, expert performances, and a premise that will haunt you long after the final fadeout.
