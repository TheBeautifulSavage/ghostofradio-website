# The Country Of The Blind

In this chilling installment of *Suspense*, listeners are transported to a fog-shrouded landscape where sight becomes a liability rather than a gift. A sighted traveler stumbles into a remote community where blindness is not a handicap but the norm—and his ability to see marks him as a dangerous outsider. As the townspeople close in with their own particular logic and prejudices, the tension mounts unbearably. What begins as a curious tale of isolation transforms into a nightmarish descent where the protagonist discovers that being different in a closed society can be more terrifying than any supernatural threat. The crackling sound effects of footsteps on stone and whispered conversations in darkness create an atmosphere of creeping dread that will keep you gripping your armchair.

Adapted from H.G. Wells' classic dystopian tale, this episode represents *Suspense* at its finest—taking a timeless literary concept and transforming it into intimate, immediate drama through the power of radio. Throughout its twenty-year run, *Suspense* earned its reputation as "the finest thriller program on the air," delivering sophisticated psychological horror to millions of Americans gathered around their receivers. Rather than relying on cheap scares, episodes like this one explored the darker corners of human nature, social conformity, and the fragility of reason itself. With a runtime of nearly twenty-five minutes, there's ample time for the story to burrow under your skin and settle there.

Tonight, dim the lights, tune in, and prepare yourself for a journey into a world where the greatest danger lies not in what you cannot see, but in what others refuse to acknowledge. *Suspense* awaits—and once it grabs hold, there's no turning back.
