Picture this: the crackle of your radio at half-past nine on a February evening, the announcer's silken voice cutting through the darkness with those two words—Suspense—that sent a delicious chill down millions of spines. Tonight, you're about to experience a story that bends the very fabric of time itself. A man discovers the impossible: the ability to travel backward through history. His noble mission? To prevent Abraham Lincoln's assassination at Ford's Theatre. But as our protagonist races against fate itself, he begins to understand a terrifying truth—that some events in history are locked in place, immovable and inevitable. The question becomes not whether he can save Lincoln, but whether the universe will destroy him for trying. Written with the sophisticated sci-fi sensibility that Suspense wielded so masterfully, this episode crackles with temporal dread and existential horror, building to a climax that will leave you questioning the nature of destiny itself.

For two decades, CBS's Suspense stood as the gold standard of American radio drama, delivering anthology thrills that proved the medium's unparalleled ability to burrow into the listener's imagination. Where television would rely on spectacle, radio gave you only voices, sound effects, and orchestral stabs—leaving everything to your mind's eye, which inevitably conjured something far more frightening. This episode exemplifies the show's willingness to grapple with high-concept science fiction premises while maintaining the intimate, personal horror that made Suspense legendary.

Tune in and step into that timeless world where danger lurks in every shadow, where the ordinary becomes extraordinary, and where a single decision could unravel the course of history itself.
