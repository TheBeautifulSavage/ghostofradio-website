# Texas Rangers Episode Page

As darkness falls across the badlands of West Texas, Rangers Clay Morgan and Skip King find themselves pursuing a cunning outlaw who strikes only under cover of night—a phantom killer the locals have dubbed "Nighthawk." With nothing but scattered eyewitness accounts and a trail that vanishes like smoke, our heroes must use every ounce of frontier cunning to track their quarry through the treacherous terrain where Texas lawmen carved order from chaos. This March 1952 broadcast crackles with tension as the rangers close in, piecing together clues while the listening audience is kept in suspense about whether justice will prevail before another innocent falls victim to the night.

*Tales of the Texas Rangers*, which graced NBC's airways from 1950 to 1952, stands as one of the finest examples of the western crime drama that captivated post-war America. The show drew its narrative power from actual case files of the legendary Texas Rangers themselves, lending an authentic grit that separated it from purely fictional adventures. Starring Joel McCrea as Morgan and Harry Lauter as King, the program balanced genuine historical detail with compelling storytelling, appealing equally to those who loved traditional westerns and those who craved detective-style mysteries. Each episode represented real dangers faced by real lawmen, reminding listeners that the American frontier's greatest champions were more investigator than gunslinger.
