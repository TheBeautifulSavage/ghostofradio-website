# The Big Show - Episode 9: Vivian Blaine & José Ferrer

Picture yourself settling into your favorite chair on a Saturday evening in 1951, the living room bathed in the warm glow of your radio set's dial light as The Big Show comes crackling through the speakers. Tonight, the stage at NBC's Studio 8-H vibrates with electric anticipation as Vivian Blaine, the vivacious star of Guys and Dolls fresh from her Broadway triumph, takes center stage alongside the masterful José Ferrer, whose commanding presence has captivated audiences in both comedic and dramatic roles. What unfolds is a masterclass in golden-age entertainment—expect sparkling repartee, unexpected comedy bits that showcase Ferrer's impeccable timing, and musical interludes that showcase Blaine's remarkable vocal range. The energy crackles with the unpredictability that made live radio so thrilling, where anything could happen and often did.

The Big Show itself was NBC's answer to the waning days of network radio's dominance, a lavish Sunday evening spectacular that assembled the brightest stars of stage, screen, and the emerging medium of television. With Tallulah Bankhead as its glamorous host and Meredith Willson orchestrating a full symphony, the program represented radio's last great gasp as a mass entertainment medium—a golden thread connecting listeners to Broadway's brightest lights. Episodes like this one captured a fleeting moment when radio still commanded the nation's attention, before television would fundamentally reshape American entertainment.

Don your headphones or gather the family around the console set, and experience why millions tuned in faithfully each week. Vivian Blaine's charm and José Ferrer's theatrical brilliance combine to create an unforgettable evening of pure broadcast magic. This is The Big Show at its finest.
