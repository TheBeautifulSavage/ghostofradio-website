# The Big Show - Episode 26: Jimmy Durante & Milton Berle

Step into the NBC studios as the orchestra strikes up that unmistakable brass fanfare—*The Big Show* is on the air! This week, two titans of American comedy collide in a spectacular hour of vaudeville-tinged mayhem. Jimmy Durante, the man with the legendary schnoz and the heart of gold, pairs up with Milton Berle, already establishing himself as television's future "Mr. Television," for a virtuoso display of rapid-fire gags, elaborate sketches, and the kind of spontaneous comic genius that kept millions glued to their radio sets. Expect Durante's trademark fractured English, his rambling asides that somehow always circle back to brilliant punchlines, and Berle's relentless energy as the two comedians try to one-up each other across a dizzying array of musical numbers and comedic bits. The supporting cast and house orchestra fuel the proceedings with the kind of live, anything-can-happen electricity that made radio's Golden Age truly golden.

*The Big Show* represents the apotheosis of radio variety entertainment—a lavish, star-studded hour that cost NBC a fortune but delivered unparalleled talent directly into America's living rooms. Produced by the legendary Goodman Ace, the show assembled Broadway's brightest lights and Hollywood's biggest names in a weekly cavalcade of comedy, drama, and song. This 1950-1952 series represents radio's final golden sunset before television claimed the variety show format entirely, making it a precious artifact of the medium's most ambitious era.

Tune in now and experience the uproarious magic that made America laugh before the television age took hold. These performances crackle with an irreplaceable spontaneity and charm—radio at its most spectacular and unrepeatable.
