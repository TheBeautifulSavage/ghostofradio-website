# The Clock: "Lively Ghost"

When the stroke of midnight sounds, settle into your chair and prepare for a tale that will make the shadows in your living room seem a touch more sinister. In "Lively Ghost," a seemingly ordinary inheritance becomes a ticket to terror when our protagonist discovers that the charming country manor bequeathed to him comes with an unexpected tenant—one who refuses to accept that their time among the living has ended. What begins as polite, almost humorous manifestations escalates into genuine menace as the ghost's demands grow more insistent and its methods more violent. The talented cast brings desperate urgency to their performances, their voices trembling with fear and uncertainty as daylight hours grow precious and the nights grow increasingly unbearable. You'll find yourself wondering, as our hero does, whether anyone will believe his impossible predicament before it's too late.

*The Clock* was NBC's answer to listeners' insatiable hunger for mystery and the supernatural during radio's golden age. Running from 1946 to 1948, the series positioned itself as a show where "time stops for mystery," offering anthology tales that proved lightning genuinely could strike twice—audiences returned week after week, never knowing if they'd encounter a murderer next door or something far stranger. This particular episode from 1947 exemplifies the show's genius for balancing supernatural dread with grounded human emotion, crafting stories that felt genuinely unsettling rather than merely theatrical.

Don't miss your appointment with *The Clock*. Tune in as the clock strikes, lights dim across the nation, and one man's inheritance becomes his nightmare. The question isn't whether ghosts exist—it's whether he'll survive the answer.
