# The Clock - "Past Or Present" (March 30, 1947)

When the hands of The Clock strike midnight on this chilling installment, listeners will find themselves ensnared in a mystery that blurs the boundary between memory and madness. A man awakens with no recollection of the past five years—or has he? As the investigation unfolds through shadowy interrogations and the protagonist's increasingly unreliable testimony, The Clock's narrator weaves a tale where every revelation seems to contradict the last. Is this a case of genuine amnesia, deliberate deception, or something far more sinister? The measured ticking of that omnipresent timepiece serves as both comfort and warning, marking each moment as the truth dangles tantalizingly out of reach. Expect the kind of psychological tension that made this series a must-listen for mystery devotees—ambiguous, unsettling, and utterly gripping.

The Clock carved its niche in radio's golden age by eschewing the formulaic detective stories that dominated the medium. Instead, each episode presents an original mystery of ordinary life, where the solutions prove as mysterious as the crimes themselves. Broadcast live from Studio 8H at NBC's Radio City studios, these half-hour dramas showcased the remarkable talents of supporting players like Iva Shepard, whose voices became familiar friends to millions of evening listeners. "Past Or Present" exemplifies the show's hallmark sophistication—a narrative puzzle that respects its audience's intelligence, offering no easy answers and precious few certainties.

Step through time with The Clock and discover why critics and devoted fans alike praised it as one of radio's most intellectually stimulating offerings. In an era before streaming and on-demand entertainment, this episode represents genuine appointment listening—the kind of mysterious, meticulously crafted storytelling that kept Americans gathered around their radios long into the night.
