# The Manicurist

When the clock strikes midnight on this sultry July evening, listeners will find themselves in the gilded cage of an upscale beauty salon, where secrets are filed away as carefully as fingernails and nothing is quite as polished as it appears. "The Manicurist" draws us into a world of whispered confidences and hidden motives, where a seemingly innocent appointment becomes the catalyst for a web of deception and danger. As our protagonist settles into that comfortable chair, the gentle scrape of emery board against nail becomes an instrument of suspense—each detail overheard, each casual remark laden with sinister implication. The salon's elegant veneer cracks to reveal the darker impulses lurking beneath, and our visitor discovers that sometimes the most dangerous secrets are filed away in the minds of those we trust most.

*The Clock* arrived on NBC's airwaves as a masterwork of economical storytelling, using a ticking timepiece as its framing device to explore the human dramas that unfold in a single day. Broadcast during radio's golden age, the show exemplified the medium's unique power to create intimate tension through voice and sound design alone, eschewing the need for visual spectacle in favor of psychological depth. Episodes like "The Manicurist" showcase the writers' gift for finding menace in the mundane, transforming everyday locations and professions into stages for moral reckoning and unexpected tragedy. The show's anthology format allowed audiences to explore different corners of human experience without the constraints of continuing characters, making each episode a complete journey into mystery.

Don't miss your appointment with suspense. Tune in for "The Manicurist" and discover that sometimes the most dangerous tools are the ones right before your eyes. *The Clock* awaits—and it's always the right time for a thrilling mystery.
