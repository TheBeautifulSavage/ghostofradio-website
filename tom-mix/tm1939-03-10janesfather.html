When this episode crackles to life through your speaker, you'll find yourself deep in the Arizona Territory as Tom Mix faces one of his most personal challenges yet. Jane, the young ward he's sworn to protect, has just received shocking news—her estranged father, a man she barely remembers, has returned to the territory with a past that reeks of scandal and danger. As thunder rumbles across the desert and the Ralston Straight Shooters rally to Tom's side, listeners are treated to a rare glimpse of emotional vulnerability beneath the fearless frontier hero's weathered exterior. The question haunting this March evening broadcast isn't about outlaws or buried treasure, but about family, loyalty, and whether a man's past sins can ever truly be forgiven. Tom Mix must navigate the minefield of his ward's conflicted heart while uncovering the truth about her father's mysterious return.

For nearly a decade, Tom Mix and the Ralston Straight Shooters had become an American institution, broadcast five days a week to millions of youngsters and their parents. These weren't mere adventure serials—they were moral instruction wrapped in genuine entertainment, sponsored by Ralston Purina and delivered with authentic Western atmosphere and Hollywood production values. The show's 1939 episodes represent the program at its creative peak, balancing exciting action sequences with surprisingly nuanced character development that elevated it above typical children's programming.

Don't miss this stirring episode that reminds us why Tom Mix remained radio's greatest Western hero for seventeen years running. Tune in now and experience the golden age of broadcasting!
