When the howling wind sweeps across the badlands on this December evening, Tom Mix and his Ralston Straight Shooters find themselves at the remote Rimrock Ranch, where a legendary black cat has begun appearing before acts of sabotage and violence. Is it merely superstition, or does this mysterious creature hold the key to unmasking a dangerous criminal syndicate operating in the territory? With his trusted companions Wash and Pecos by his side, Tom must navigate a maze of false leads, sinister strangers, and mounting danger to separate fact from folklore. Every creaking floorboard, every shadow cast by lantern light becomes suspect in this tense mystery that builds toward a climactic revelation. The question that drives this thrilling adventure is deceptively simple: who commands the black cat, and what do they stand to gain from the chaos they've unleashed?

For nearly a decade, Tom Mix's program had captured the imaginations of millions of American youngsters, skillfully weaving genuine western lore with the excitement of modern detective work. By 1941, with the nation edging toward global conflict, the show's emphasis on honesty, courage, and civic duty resonated deeply with audiences seeking heroes worthy of emulation. The Ralston Straight Shooters membership—reaching nearly a million enrolled listeners—represented more than a fan club; it was a movement toward character education wrapped in the thrilling package of adventure serials. Tom's unwavering moral code and problem-solving intelligence made him an ideal role model for an anxious era.

Settle in by the radio dial and prepare yourself for an evening of genuine suspense. Whether you're a seasoned devotee of the Straight Shooters or encountering Tom Mix for the first time, "The Mystery of the Black Cat" showcases the program at its finest—perfectly balancing action, atmosphere, and the timeless appeal of good triumphing over evil in the American West.
