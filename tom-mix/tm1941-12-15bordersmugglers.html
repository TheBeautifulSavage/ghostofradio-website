Picture yourself huddled around the radio set on a cold December evening, the amber glow of the dial illuminating your living room as the familiar strains of "When It's Roundup Time in Texas" crackle through the speaker. Tom Mix's steady voice cuts through the static to warn of danger lurking along the Mexican border, where a ruthless smuggling ring has been moving contraband across American soil. With his trusty horse Tony and his band of Straight Shooters at his side, Mix must infiltrate the criminal operation, posed as an outlaw, to expose the ringleaders. Every coded message, every midnight rendezvous, every moment of mistaken identity crackles with suspense as Tom navigates a web of deception and violence—all while maintaining the quick wit and unwavering moral compass that made him radio's greatest hero.

Broadcasting just eight days after Pearl Harbor, this episode captures the Tom Mix program at its zenith—a show that had captivated American audiences for nearly a decade with tales of frontier justice and honest heroism. Sponsored by Ralston-Purina, the show blended thrilling adventure with wholesome values, and Tom Mix's character embodied the resourcefulness and integrity that Americans desperately needed during uncertain times. The program's massive popularity spawned millions of Straight Shooter ring premiums, making it not merely entertainment but a cultural touchstone for young listeners and families across the nation.

Tune in now and experience the drama, danger, and daring that kept millions of Americans on the edge of their seats. In this episode, the stakes are personal and the villains are cunning—but Tom Mix has never backed down from a challenge. Can you hear that distant whistle of Tony's neigh? Justice is riding to the border.
