Picture yourself huddled around the radio on a June evening in 1944, the world still locked in war, when Tom Mix's unmistakable drawl crackles through the speaker announcing a mystery most peculiar—The Green Man. In this thrilling installment, our heroes face an adversary shrouded in emerald shadow, a figure whose strange appearance and sinister intentions threaten to upset the peace of the frontier. The Straight Shooters find themselves entangled in a web of deception where nothing is quite what it seems, and even Tom's legendary courage is tested against an enemy whose origins remain maddeningly obscure. Radio listeners of the day would have sat on the edge of their seats as sound effects—the thunder of hoofbeats, the crack of gunfire, mysterious footsteps in the darkness—transported them straight into the Arizona Territory.

For nearly a decade by 1944, Tom Mix and his trusted friends had captivated American audiences through the magic of radio, transforming the genuine cowboy hero into a legend even larger than life. What made the Ralston Straight Shooters special wasn't just the pulse-pounding adventure; it was the show's authentic connection to its sponsor's values, promoting honesty, courage, and good citizenship while offering young listeners an escape from the anxieties of wartime. Tom Mix himself, the real-life silent film star, lent his name and prestige to the program, while his supporting cast brought the frontier to vibrant life with brilliant voice acting and imaginative storytelling.

Tune in now to experience this classic broadcast preserved from radio's golden age—a testament to when storytelling knew no bounds and the American imagination was our greatest resource.
