Picture yourself huddled around the radio on a warm August evening in 1945, the war news heavy in everyone's heart, when suddenly the familiar gallop of a horse cuts through the static and Tom Mix's unmistakable drawl welcomes you back to the Arizona Territory. Tonight brings "The Vanishing Village," a mystery that will keep you guessing through the night: an entire settlement has disappeared without a trace, leaving behind only dust, empty buildings, and a trail of questions that only Tom Mix and his trusty horse Tony can unravel. As our hero investigates the ghost town, strange shadows move between the abandoned storefronts, cryptic messages appear on weathered walls, and the locals in nearby Silver Creek speak in hushed whispers about something sinister—something they're afraid to name. Part One plants the seeds of intrigue that will bloom throughout the week, with sound effects that place you right there in those creaking buildings, the wind howling through empty streets, and danger lurking around every corner.

For over a decade, the Tom Mix Ralston Straight Shooters had captivated millions of American families, bringing the excitement of the Old West directly into living rooms across the nation. Mixed with genuine cowboy lore and Mix's larger-than-life persona—the real Tom Mix had been a genuine Wild West showman and silent film star—the program offered escapism and adventure to Depression-weary and now war-weary listeners. Each episode was a masterclass in radio drama, combining action, mystery, and moral clarity at a time when Americans desperately needed heroes.

Don't miss this thrilling two-part mystery. Tune in tomorrow night at the same time to discover what became of the Vanishing Village and whether Tom Mix can restore order to the lawless frontier.
