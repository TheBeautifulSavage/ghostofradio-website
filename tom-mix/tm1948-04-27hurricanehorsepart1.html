Step into the dusty trails of the Arizona Territory as Tom Mix faces his most formidable challenge yet: a wild mustang so fierce and unpredictable that even the toughest wranglers have learned to fear it. Broadcast on April 27th, 1948, this thrilling two-part adventure opens with thunder rolling across the desert as Tom discovers a mysterious horse that seems to embody the very fury of nature itself. What follows is a gripping tale of determination, danger, and the unbreakable bond between man and beast. With Ralston Straight Shooters—Tom's loyal band of young radio companions—gathered around the campfire, listeners will experience the crack of the whip, the pounding of hooves, and Tom's calm, measured voice cutting through the chaos. The episode crackles with authentic Western atmosphere, from the creaking of saddle leather to the distant howl of coyotes, building suspense with every moment as our hero attempts the seemingly impossible task of breaking this wild animal's spirit without losing his own.

For fifteen years, Tom Mix and the Ralston Straight Shooters had captivated American audiences with wholesome adventure and genuine thrills. By 1948, the program had become an institution of Saturday morning radio, sponsored by Ralston Purina and beloved by millions of young listeners who dreamed of the frontier. Tom Mix himself—a real-life rodeo star and silent film cowboy—lent authentic credibility to every tale, while the show's commitment to moral lessons and clean entertainment made it a staple in American homes throughout the Depression and World War II.

Don't miss the first part of this extraordinary saga. Tune in to hear Tom Mix confront "Hurricane Horse" and discover whether courage and compassion can tame what nature has left wild and dangerous.
