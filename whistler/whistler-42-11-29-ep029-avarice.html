Step into the shadow-draped world of small-town America where a seemingly respectable man's hunger for wealth transforms him into something monstrous. In this November 1942 episode, "Avarice," The Whistler invites you to witness the corrosive power of greed as it consumes a soul. An inheritance, a trusted associate, and a darkening moral landscape converge in a taut 30 minutes of radio drama that crackles with tension and menace. The mysterious Whistler himself—that eerie, omniscient narrator—guides listeners through the moral labyrinth with his characteristic sardonic wit, while behind the microphone, seasoned actors bring desperate ambition to vivid life. You'll hear the scratch of a pen on paper, the tense murmur of hushed conversations, and the ever-present danger that one more reckless decision might seal a character's fate forever.

The Whistler aired during the golden age of radio drama, when Americans gathered around their receivers for escapism and moral instruction wrapped in thrilling entertainment. This particular series, which ran on CBS from 1942 to 1955, became a benchmark for the noir mystery genre—influencing countless films and television shows that would follow. Each episode served as a cautionary tale, probing the darker impulses hidden within ordinary people. "Avarice" exemplifies what made The Whistler essential listening: sophisticated writing, atmospheric sound design, and performances that understood that evil often wears a familiar face.

If you've never experienced The Whistler, or if you're revisiting this classic, "Avarice" is an ideal entry point into radio drama's most compelling anthology series. Tune in, turn off the lights, and let that distinctive whistle transport you to a world where greed extracts its terrible price.
