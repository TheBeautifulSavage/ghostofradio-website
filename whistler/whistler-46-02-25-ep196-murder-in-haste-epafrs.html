# Murder in Haste

Picture this: it's a fog-shrouded evening in the city, and somewhere in the darkness, a man makes a decision he'll regret for the rest of his life. In "Murder in Haste," *The Whistler* pulls you into a world where passion overrides reason and a moment of blind rage becomes an irreversible tragedy. This episode delivers everything the show's devoted listeners craved—a taut, morality-tinged narrative that unfolds with the inexorable pull of fate. As the mysterious Whistler himself observes the unfolding drama with detached certainty, you'll find yourself caught between sympathy and condemnation for a desperate man whose impulsive act spirals into consequences far darker than he ever imagined. The tension builds steadily, punctuated by superb sound design—the crack of violence, the nervous rustle of escape, the relentless closing in of justice.

*The Whistler* was the rare radio program that understood noir before most Americans could even name the genre. Airing five nights a week on CBS throughout the 1940s and early 1950s, it built a devoted following by refusing easy morality. Unlike the clear-cut heroes of typical adventure serials, this anthology presented ordinary people facing extraordinary moral crossroads, with the unseen Whistler serving as a sardonic Greek chorus. Each standalone episode featured the show's signature blend of sophisticated writing, sterling character work, and that distinctive—often haunting—whistled theme. Recorded in 1946, this particular episode showcases the show at the height of its powers, when production values and storytelling had reached their full maturity.

Don't miss "Murder in Haste." Settle in, dim the lights, and let *The Whistler* remind you why radio drama remains the most intimate form of suspense ever broadcast. The answer to every mystery is waiting in the darkness.
