# The Whistler: "Bird of Prey"

March 31, 1948—a spring evening when the sun dips below the horizon and shadow pools gather in every corner of the city. *The Whistler* beckons you into the darkness with his eerie, unforgettable theme, and tonight brings a tale of predation most foul. In "Bird of Prey," listeners will find themselves ensnared in a web of obsession and cruelty, where a seemingly respectable man masks something far more sinister beneath his tailored suit. As the Whistler's cryptic narration unfolds, the line between hunter and hunted blurs—and you'll discover that the most dangerous creatures are often those that walk on two legs. The tension crackles through the airwaves like electricity, punctuated by dramatic silences and the masterful sound design that made CBS's premier mystery program the gold standard of suspense radio.

*The Whistler* ran from 1942 to 1955, becoming one of the most beloved and influential noir programs in radio history. Unlike the detective shows that dominated the medium, *The Whistler* presented self-contained morality plays where the unseen host served as fate's instrument—judge, jury, and cosmic observer. By 1948, the show had perfected its formula: impeccable writing, performances brimming with desperation and dread, and a production team that understood that what listeners *imagined* terrified them far more than anything heard aloud. This particular episode exemplifies the show's genius for exploring the darkness lurking within ordinary lives.

Don your headphones and dim the lights. *The Whistler* awaits—mysterious, unflinching, and utterly captivating. This is radio at its most hypnotic.
