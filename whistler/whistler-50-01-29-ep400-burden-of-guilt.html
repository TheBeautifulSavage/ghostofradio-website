# The Whistler: "Burden of Guilt" — January 29, 1950

As the mysterious Whistler's haunting theme melody drifts through your radio speaker, settle in for a tale of conscience and consequence that will grip you until the final, devastating twist. In this episode, a man's past returns to haunt him with a vengeance—a secret he thought buried surfaces just as his respectable life reaches its zenith, forcing him into an impossible moral reckoning. The Whistler weaves together the sordid details of his transgression with mounting tension, each revelation pulling our protagonist deeper into a web of his own making. You'll hear the crackle of desperation, the cold logic of blackmail, and the creeping realization that some debts can never truly be paid. By the time that eerie whistle returns to close the tale, the weight of guilt will settle upon you like a thick fog over a noir-lit street.

*The Whistler* stands as one of radio's finest achievements in psychological suspense, a show that eschewed the boisterous sound effects and melodramatic shouting of its contemporaries. Instead, it relied on the power of a single, unforgettable voice—the unseen narrator who speaks directly to listeners as our guide through morally shadowed landscapes. Running from 1942 through 1955 on CBS, the program became legendary for its intimate, almost confessional storytelling, making each listener complicit in the unfolding dramas. Episode 400 represents the show at its peak, a masterclass in atmospheric radio drama from an era when imagination was the most powerful special effect.

Don't miss this sterling example of classic radio at its finest. Tune in and discover why *The Whistler* captivated millions—and why its tales of human weakness and moral corruption still resonate today. The Whistler is waiting.
