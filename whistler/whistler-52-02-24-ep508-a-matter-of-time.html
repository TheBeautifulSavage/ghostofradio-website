# A Matter of Time

As the mysterious Whistler's distinctive theme pierces the darkness of your living room, you're drawn into a tale where seconds become currency and fate hangs by a thread. In this February 1952 episode, a man discovers that time itself has become his most dangerous enemy—each passing moment pulling him deeper into a web of circumstance he never saw coming. The Whistler's calm, knowing narration guides you through shadow and suspicion as ordinary choices cascade into extraordinary consequences. With only the crackling intimacy of a radio speaker between you and the mystery, you'll find yourself leaning closer, trying to anticipate the twist that always seems to arrive just when you think you've understood the game.

The Whistler stands as one of radio's finest achievements in psychological suspense, a show that understood that the most terrifying threats aren't always visible. From 1942 through the golden age of radio, CBS built a devoted audience by crafting stories where circumstance, not violence, became the true antagonist. These episodes emerged during an era when Americans gathered around their radios seeking escape and sophistication—programs that treated listeners as intelligent adults capable of appreciating subtle menace and moral complexity. The Whistler's anonymous narrator became an iconic figure in the noir tradition, a guide through the darker aspects of human nature without ever needing to show his face.

If you appreciate classic mystery that relies on atmosphere, plotting, and the power of suggestion rather than sensationalism, "A Matter of Time" exemplifies everything that made The Whistler essential listening. Tune in and discover why, nearly seventy years later, this episode still possesses the power to unsettle and captivate.
