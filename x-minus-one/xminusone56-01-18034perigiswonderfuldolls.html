# "Perigi's Wonderful Dolls"

Step into a world where the line between plaything and menace blurs into shadow. In this haunting episode of *X Minus One*, listeners encounter Perigi's Wonderful Dolls—mechanical playthings that arrive mysteriously in toy shops across America, each with an unsettling perfection that troubles the adults who observe them. As children clutch these dolls to their chests with unusual devotion, something altogether sinister begins to unfold. What is the true purpose of these automatons? Are they truly innocent amusements, or are they instruments of an alien intelligence with designs far beyond our understanding? The episode crackles with the kind of creeping dread that made science fiction radio so potent—the terror that something manufactured, something we've invited into our homes, might harbor intentions we cannot fathom.

*X Minus One*, which aired on NBC from 1955 to 1958, represented the golden age of science fiction radio drama, where writers could explore anxieties about technology, invasion, and conformity through the intimate medium of sound. Though the date on this particular episode suggests an earlier broadcast, it exemplifies the show's commitment to literary quality—many episodes were adapted from stories by genre masters like Isaac Asimov and Frederik Pohl. The program arrived at a moment when Americans were simultaneously enchanted by and suspicious of technological progress, when flying saucers seemed plausible and automated invasion felt disturbingly real. Each episode offered sophisticated storytelling wrapped in the immediacy of live performance.

Join the millions who huddled around their radio sets to experience this electrifying drama. Tune in and discover what dark purpose awaits in Perigi's Wonderful Dolls—a tale that will remind you why, in the age of atomic dreams, even childhood's most cherished comforts could harbor the unknown.
